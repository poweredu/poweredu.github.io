Firstly, parents tend to have a more authoritative and guiding role in times of trouble. They have more life experience and usually have a better understanding of the consequences of certain actions. They also have a more protective role, and may take steps to keep their children safe and out of harm's way.

On the other hand, friends tend to have a more supportive and empathetic role in times of trouble. They may also be going through similar struggles, so they can offer a perspective of someone who understands and can empathize with their friend.

Another difference is that parents have a long-term relationship with their children, and they have a vested interest in the well-being of their children, whereas friends' relationships can be more temporary and may change over time.

In conclusion, parents tend to have a more authoritative and protective role, while friends tend to have a more supportive and empathetic role in moments of trouble.
