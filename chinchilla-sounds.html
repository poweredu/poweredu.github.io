8. is it possible to produce an albino rabbit in the second generation of a cross between a heterozgynous agouti rabbit (Cc) and a heterozgynous chinchilla rabbit (cchch)? How? Prove your answer.​


Yes, if the heterozygous cross in both the agouti rabbit and the chinchilla rabbit is an albino rabbit. In other words, if the agouti and chinchilla rabbits carry recessive albino alleles.

You will have a 25 percent chance of an albino rabbit. You do not need to prove it with a test cross You prove by having lots of crosses of the two rabbits. One breeding alone might produce an albino and it might not, but the more offspring produced by the cross the greater the chance of the numbers reflecting the theoretical percentages.

9. How are the results of the crosses differ if the black allele was dominant over the chinchilla Allele? Explain both the f1 and f2 generations ​


According to the law of dominance, a trait is represented by two contrasting factors of a gene in a heterozygous individual while the homozygous individual carries two copies of same factors. Thus, the genotype of heterozygous rabbit will be Bb and that of homozygous recessive will be bb. Law of segregation states that the two factors for a trait, present together in a heterozygous individual (Bb), do not get mixed and are separated and delivered singly during gametogenesis. Thus, the heterozygous individual produces two types of gametes: 50% gametes with “B” and 50% with “b”. The homozygous recessive individual will make all gametes with “b”

When viewed from the influence of genes on the phenotype.Alleles are members of a pair of genes that have opposite effects. So alleles are genes located at the same locus and have the same or almost the same jobIn homozygous individuals, the two allele pairs have the exact same symbol; e.g. BB, MM. While the heterozygous genotypes of the two allele pairs have different symbols, for example, Bb, Mm. However, Bm and bM are not alleles.Multiple Alleles (Multiple Aleomorphy)

If there is more than one pair of alleles at one locus, it is called multiple alleles. For example, the color of fur in rabbits and blood group A BO system in humans.

Multiple alleles in rabbit fur are the presence of 4 alleles that both affect coat color, and are at the same locus. The coat color of rabbits with the C gene has several consecutive alleles from the dominant one:
