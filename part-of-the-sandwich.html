I'm happy to help you label the parts of a sandwich and provide a brief description of each:

Bread: The foundation of the sandwich. It can be sliced bread, buns, rolls, baguettes, or any other type of bread you prefer.

Spread: A condiment that's typically applied to one or both slices of bread to add flavor and moisture to the sandwich. Examples of spreads include mayo, mustard, hummus, pesto, and butter.

Protein: The filling of the sandwich that provides sustenance. It can be meat (ham, turkey, chicken, beef, etc.), fish (tuna, salmon), tofu, eggs, or cheese.

Vegetables: Fresh produce that adds texture, flavor, and nutrition to the sandwich. Common vegetables used in sandwiches are lettuce, tomato, cucumber, onion, bell pepper, avocado, and sprouts.

Extras: Optional ingredients that add additional flavor or texture to the sandwich. Examples of extras include bacon, pickles, olives, jalapenos, roasted red peppers, and sun-dried tomatoes.

Top bread: The final piece of bread that completes the sandwich.

15. Fe shared her sandwich to April. Lovely ate 1/4 of the sandwich while May ate 2/3 of the sandwich.Which part of the sandwich was eaten by the two friends?​


Addition of Dissimilar Fractions

Addition of fractions is adding the numerators for similar fractions and copying the denominator. Express the answer in lowest term.  For dissimilar fractions, addition can only be done when changed to similar. This is done by using the least common denominator of the given fractions to be added. Answers are expressed in lowest form.

Answer:[tex]\frac{11}{12}[/tex]Solutions:

Given: [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] - part of the sandwich that Lovely had eaten.

           [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] - part of the sandwich that May have eaten

Asked: The amount of sandwich eaten by Lovely and May.

Number Sentence: [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] = N

Operation(s): addition

Solution and Answer:  [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] = N

Using the least common denominator of 4 and 3, express the two fractions as similar.

[tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] → [tex]\frac{3}{12}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] → [tex]\frac{8}{12}[/tex]

Proceed with the addition.

[tex]\frac{3}{12}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{8}{12}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{11}{12}[/tex]

Therefore, Lovely and May have eaten [tex]\frac{11}{12}[/tex] of the sandwich.
