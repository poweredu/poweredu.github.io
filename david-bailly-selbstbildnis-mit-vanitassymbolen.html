1. Learning TaskLook at the painting below by artist David Bailly entitled'selbstbildnis mit Vanitassymbolen" "(Self-Portrait with VanitasSymbols). Write an assumption about what the painting means in thespace below.​


Answer

The Skull symbolizes death. The objects found on the table are the desires of David Bailly. We can see objects like alcohol, jewelry, roses, and other items for the rich. It may suggest that "Self-Portrait with Vanitas Symbols" painting's Message is that seeking luxury is a futile goal since we will eventually die.

Explanation

We should understand what is the meaning of Vanitas:

Vanitas is a painting genre that contains a collection of objects symbolizing the inevitability of death. It is also a symbol of vanity and that earthly achievements and pleasures are only temporary. In other words, a Vanitas painting is like a message to repent because life is too short.

If you look closely, behind the tall glass, you can see her face emerging from the darkness like a shadow. It has also been suggested that this ghostly presence represents the muse of painting.The table on which the tall glass rests is strewn with all manner of objects. The letter in the bottom right-hand corner that hangs over the table edge leaves us in no doubt as to the subject of this painting. The maxim ‘Vanitas vanitatum et omnia vanitas’ comes from the Old Testament book of Ecclesiastes and translates as: vanity of vanities; all is vanity. The objects in this painting all point to the finiteness of human existence. Life on earth is as brief and transitory as an extinguished candle, a fragile bubble, a toppled glass or a faded flower. The hourglass and skull are reminders of death, which is always close by. And once you’re dead, any riches are insignificant, regardless of how many strings of pearls or gold coins you may own. In this painting, Bailly is linking this universal wisdom to the finiteness of his own existence.

29. what is the features of the MITS Altair? ​


Answer:

The Altair 8800 computer was a break-even sale for MITS. They needed to sell additional memory boards, I/O boards and other options to make a profit. The system came with a "1024 word" (1024 byte) memory board populated with 256 bytes.

Answer:Even though MITS developed additional and more advanced models, and shipped thousands of Altair computer systems a month, the company was sold to Pertec in 1976.
