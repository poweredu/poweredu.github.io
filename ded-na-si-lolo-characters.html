2. Ano ang aral sa pelikulang ded na si lolo


Bukod sa mga pamahiin na nagbukas sa aking kaisipan ng mga tradisyon ng mga Pilipino noon pa man at patuloy parin na ginagawa ng mga Pilipino sa ilang piling lugar sa ngayon, natutunan ko rin ang pagpapahalaga at pagbibigay importansya sa pamilya. Maaaring may mga pagkakaiba iba tayo ngunit kung lalawakan natin ang pang-unawa natin, at mas isipin ang kapakanan ng pamilya sa kahit anong kagustuhan mo. Maraming porblema ang kakaharapin ng ating pamilya ngunit kung mananatili tayong nagkakaisa at nagdadamayan mapagtatagumpayan natin ang mga ito. Natutunan ko rin na iba ang klase ng pagmamahal na mabibigay ng ating pamilya, hindi ito mababayaran ng kahit anong yaman sa mundo. Maaring napakalungkot na yugto ng pagkamatay sa pamilya, ngunit it rin ang panahon para mas mapagtibay ang relasyon ng bawat isa.

The strong family ties

Being familiar with one's family is a key component of a successful society. The foundation of our existence is our family. We provide them with the best possible world for the purpose of our own sincere affection. They represent one of our key investments. A father strives for excellence in order to achieve and provide for his family. A mother also contributes to the family's increased income. The Manilyn family could see this specific scenario of close ties within the family. They were able to pursue their life's ultimate objective despite their poverty. The movie's opening sequence left me feeling incredibly moved. During mealtime, the ideal family environment was projected.

To share the grace that comes from the Lord's abundant hands, the family came together. Each of them developed into a powerful force for love in everyone's hearts. The family members greatly value their imposing presence. We never learned how to love and care for our family from anyone. Everyone has this instinct from birth. The only distinction is that Filipinos have a strong tendency to defend their families when they are under attack.

The Lamayan

The second is the "lamay" in the home where Manilyn's family, including their father who doubles as their mother, grew up. One learns from this lamayan experience that, depending on the viewpoint adopted, several interpretations may be attached to it. In our culture, we honor the memory of our loved ones who have passed away. We believe in an afterlife and observe elaborate and colorful funeral customs.

Family members are required to wear black mourning pins. They must wear this as a sign of melancholy since it denotes that they are very grieved. The limos should be placed in a container. Given the high cost of dying, this is beneficial for funeral expenses. The funds raised will be used to pay for food, a coffin, and other costs. At the very least, this will reduce the demand for cash. You should refrain from bathing and combing your hair near the burial. Rainer bathed inside their home, and his mother quickly allowed him to leave and bathe outside.

The truth that the Filipino, regardless of his financial situation, whether rich or poor, is always willing to come to the aid of his fellowmen in their hour of need—whether they be his friends or strangers—becomes clear to us as a result of the realities revealed in Lamayan's experience about the true personal character of the Filipinos. This gentleman's understanding of death and his realization that, as a human, he could never escape the awful experience of someone passing away in his own family are the sources of his gentleness and compassion.
