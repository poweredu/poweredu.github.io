4. Mrs. Dela Vega sold 4 towels at Php. 150. 50 each and several necklaces at Php. 250. 75 each to her avid costumer. If she gave Php. 145.75 change from a thousand and 500 peso bills, how many bracelets did she sell?​


Answer:

Let's start by calculating the total amount Mrs. Dela Vega received from selling the towels and necklaces:

Total amount from selling 4 towels = 4 x Php. 150.50 = Php. 602.00

Total amount from selling necklaces = Let's call the number of necklaces she sold "x"

Total amount from selling x necklaces = x x Php. 250.75 = Php. 250.75x

So the total amount Mrs. Dela Vega received is:

Total amount = Php. 602.00 + Php. 250.75x

Now let's calculate how much the customer paid:

Amount paid = Php. 1000.00 + Php. 500.00 = Php. 1500.00

So the change Mrs. Dela Vega gave to the customer is:

Change = Php. 1500.00 - (Php. 602.00 + Php. 250.75x) = Php. 897.00 - Php. 250.75x

We know that the change she gave is Php. 145.75, so we can set up an equation:

Php. 145.75 = Php. 897.00 - Php. 250.75x

Solving for "x", we get:

Php. 250.75x = Php. 751.25

x = 3

Therefore, Mrs. Dela Vega sold 3 necklaces to the customer. We don't have any information about bracelets, so we cannot answer the question about how many bracelets she sold.
