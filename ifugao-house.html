9. ano ang ifugao?saan matatagpuan ang ifugao?​


Answer:

Ifugao (Ilocano: Probinsia ti Ifugao; Tagalog: Lalawigan ng Ifugao) is a landlocked province of the Philippines in the Cordillera Administrative Region in Luzon. Its capital is Lagawe and it borders Benguet to the west, Mountain Province to the north, Isabela to the east, and Nueva Vizcaya to the south.

24. Research on the cultural similarities and differences of Ifugaos and Maranaos find out specifically details on their houses and utensils. Compare the 2 cultural groups using a Venn diagram. ​


ANSWER←⁠(⁠>⁠▽⁠<⁠)⁠ﾉ:

====================================

Maranao House:

The Maranaos' home. They are made of hard woods with posts made of whole tree trunks and are known for intricately carved panolong (beam ends) with okir designs.

====================================

Ifugao House:

The Ifugao House is made of wood and thatch. It is a one-room dwelling that is square in plan, elevated by four wooden posts made of tree trunks, and crowned by a pyramidal thatched roof that extends beyond the windowless walls.

====================================

The similarities between them is that both of the houses are made of woods while the difference are that the Maranao House use okir to style/ design the house these were meant to drive away evil spirits.

====================================

Ifugao Utensil:

The Ifugao spoon is made of wood it has utilitarian art, with a wonderful rich patina and smoothness. These spoons are kept by individuals and are used by them during their daily meals.

====================================

Maranao Utensil:

"Gadur" or brass serving trays is of cast brass inlaid with silver. Gadur jars were used to hold the ceremonial gifts for the bride's family given by the groom's relatives during wedding festivals.

What are Ifugao people known for?

The Ifuago are known for their rich oral literary traditions of hudhud and the alim. In 2001, the Hudhud Chants of the Ifugao was chosen as one of the 11 Masterpieces of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity. It was then formally inscribed as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2008.
