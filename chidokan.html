Answer:

Explanation:

Chitin is the most abundant aminopolysaccharide polymer occurring in nature, and is the building material that gives strength to the exoskeletons of crustaceans, insects, and the cell walls of fungi. 16. What are the agricultural applications of Chitosan NPs?​


Answer:

In food postharvest applications, chitin increases the ability of fruits to resist diseases and is used as a postharvest biocontrol treatment. Chitin also increases carbohydrate production in the plant, and improves plant's frost tolerance. Due to its antibacterial and antifugal properties, chitosan is successfully used in plant protection, as elicitor, growth promoter, enhancer of secondary metabolites production, and in soil correction. Application can be done by different ways : in the seed, in the soil or by foliar spraying.
