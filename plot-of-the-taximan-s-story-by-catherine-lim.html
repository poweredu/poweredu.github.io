1. What is the problem faced by the taximan in the story " The Taximan's Story " By: Catherine Lim


Answer: The taximan found out that her shy and studious daughter moonlights and possibly looks for tourists to go out at night. Like the other children who throws in a sexy outfit at night once they go out of school, the taximan's daughter is no exemption. This is what drove the taximan angry.

Explanation:

In this story by Catherine Lim, we see the taximan telling his passenger who is a teacher the ills of the generations today. He goes on to compare the discipline set forth in their generation (e.g. they are caned) as opposed to the liberated nature of the youth today. He also gives the teacher some information about the changes in Singapore.

Style:

The storyteller uses a monologue technique for readers to focus only on the taximan. Readers are aware about the presence of a passenger but her voice was never drawn out. Readers know that the passenger asks questions but the focus is on the taximan's reply.

4. sino si Catherine lim? ​


Catherine Lim Poh Imm (Chinese: 林宝音; pinyin: Lín Bǎoyīn, born 21 March 1942) is a Singaporean fiction author known for writing about Singapore society and of themes of traditional Chinese culture. Hailed as the "doyenne of Singapore writers",[1] Lim has published nine collections of short stories, five novels, two poetry collections, and numerous political commentaries to date.[2] Her social commentary in 1994, titled The PAP and the people - A Great Affective Divide[3][4] and published in The Straits Times criticised the ruling political party's agendas.

9. talambuhay ni catherine lim


Answer:

Talambuhay ni Catherine Lim (Tagalog)

Siya ay ipinanganak sa Kulim, Malaysia. Nag-aral siya sa kumbento ng Banal na Sanggol na si Jesus. Natapos niya and digring Bachelor of Arts sa University of Malaya noong taong 1963. Noong 1967, nagpunta siya sa Singapore, nagtrabaho roon habang ipinagpapatuloy ang digring post graduate sa University of Singapore. Noong 1998, natapos niya ang digring Ph.D sa Applied Linguistics sa National University ng Singapore. Siya ay naging Fullbright scholar din sa Colombia University at University of California sa Berkeley noong 1990.

Siya ay naging isang guro, mamamahayag, direktor ng proyekto sa Curriculum Development Institute sa Singapore at isa ring espesyalista at lektyurer sa Regional English Language Center (RELC) at nagturo rin ng socio-linguistics at literatura. Taong 1992 nag-full time siya sa pagsusulat ng mga akdang pampanitikan. Ginawaran siya ng Knight of Order ng Arts and Letter ng pamahalaan ng Pransiya noong 2003. Naging Ambassador siya ng Hans Christian Anderson Foundation sa Copenhagen noong 2005. Ginawaran din siya bilang isang Honorary Doctorate sa Literatura ng Murdoch University at ng Southeast Asia Award.

Inilathala ni Lim ang unang koleksyon niya na may pamagat na “Little Ironies: Stories of Singapore” (1978). Sa koleksyon niyang ito, puno ng pagpapatawa ang estilo niya sa pagsasalaysay ng mga buhay-buhay sa lipunan ng Singapore. Ang iba pa niyang kuwento ay inilathala noong 1980. Isa sa mga  koleksyon niya ang “O Singapore: Stories in Celebration” mula pa noong 1988.

Ang pangunahing tema sa kanyang isinulat na mga kuwento ay ang papel ng kababaihan sa tradisyonal na lipunan at kulturang Tsino. Noong 1998 ginawaran si Lim ng Montblanc-NUS Center for the Arts Literary Award at noong 1990 natanggap niya ang SEA Write Award.

The story of the Taxi man tells the story of a father, a taxi driver and a family man. Who drives his cab for a living. It tells the story of him driving a passenger and telling her stories about his life. He tells a story about how he became a cab driver, how things have changed over the years, and how hard life has become. How he struggles every day to keep his family sustained, all of this hard work of his paid off when some of his children managed to finish their studies and became professionals. Except for one, who is still currently studying, her youngest daughter. Whom he was so proud of, with her being a part of the achievers of her school. His boasting soon turned into ramblings, when he remembered the kind of youth that is around the society nowadays. He was angry at how the young people of today would choose to sell their dignity to foreigners, especially the Westerners. He would see these young girls meeting up with foreigners, mostly those who are aged. Where they would go to hotels and do inappropriate stuff just for money, in order for them to satisfy their wants. The material things such as designer brand bags, phones, and other luxury items could be bought just by selling their bodies to the Westerners.

The taxi driver then goes back to tell the story of his brilliant daughter, whom he recently found out that she was not what he thought she would be. It was during one day, where he was making rounds on his usual route that he saw his daughter together with her friends. In which, they went into an establishment to meet up with some foreigners, it enraged the taxi driver and he went after his daughter. He dragged her out of there and humiliated her in public, he then drove her home and proceeded to beat her there and locked her up. He told the passenger that it was him being disappointed and to discipline his disobedient daughter who brought him shame, by doing the thing that he hated most out of the youth today. He was clearly disappointed and felt betrayed by his daughter, whom he had high hopes with and whom he trusted. The taxi driver then drops off his passenger and is seemingly in a hurry to pick-up his booked passengers who are students with their foreign boyfriends.

The Taxi Man's Story plot:

The Taxi man and his passenger engage in a conversation of different generations and cultures. He has a rather one-sided conversation with a passenger known as "Madame."
