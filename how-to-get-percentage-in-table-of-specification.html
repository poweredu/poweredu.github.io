Formulas:

Base = Percentage/Rate

Percentage = Base x Rate

Rate = Percentage/Base

To round off numbers, identify first the rounding digit. Then, look at the number at its right. If the number beside it is 5 and above, round up by adding 1 to the rounding digit and replace the remaining numbers to its right to 0. If it is 4 and below, round down. The rounding digit will remain the same but the numbers to its right will be replaced by 0.

Example:

225 - rounded off to nearest hundreds

Rounding digit = 2

Number next to it = 2

Round down.

Rounded number = 200

9. how to get a percentage by volume and mass...and example


Example,
A solution is prepared by dissolving 90 milliliters of pure hydrogen peroxide in enough water to make 3000 milliliters of solution.The concentration of the hydrogen peroxide solution is,

SOLUTION:
Given data:
Volume of solute= 90 mL
Volume of solution=3000 mL

Substitute the values in the corresponding formula,

Volume Percent = volume of solute/Volume of solution= x100 PERCENT.
Volume percent = 90 mL/3000 mL = x100 PERCENT.
Volume percent = 3 PERCENT.

Therefore,the concentration of the hydrogen peroxide solution is 3 PERCENT.

2ND,
Calculate the volume/volume percent solution formed by combining 25 milliliters of ethanol with enough water to make 200 milliliters of solution.

SOLUTION:

Given data:
Volume of solute = 25 mL
Volume of solution = 200 mL

Substitute,the values in the corresponding formula,

Volume percent = volume of solute/volume of solution = x100 PERCENT.
Volume percent = 25 mL/200 mL = x100 PERCENT.
Volume percent = 12.5 percent.

Therefore,the concentration of ethanol solution is 12.5 percent.

15. How to get percentage in stratified random sampling?


Answer:

To implement stratified sampling, first find the total number of members in the population, and then the number of members of each stratum. For each stratum, divide the number of members by the total number in the entire population to get the percentage of the population represented by that stratum.

19. how to get bakers percentage​


Answer:

comment me if it helps

Explanation:

When writing a recipe (formula), Baker’s Percentage, Baker's Percent, Flour Weight, or Baker’s Math is a way to express the ratio of ingredients to one another by weight. Baker’s % is internationally used to express formulas for baked products such as bread, cookies, cakes, scones, and most any product where flour is the primary ingredient.

SARAH SAYS: Bakers often talk about the percent hydration of a dough or water Baker's %; that is the amount of water (liquid) in a formula in ratio to the amount of flour.

Each ingredient in a formula is expressed as a percentage of the largest ingredient, usually the flour weight, always expressed as 100%.

The advantage of this system is that it allows for the baker to easily convert their recipe into different weight indicators, such as pounds, ounces, kilograms, or grams. And once all of the weights of the ingredients in the recipe are calculated, it easy to scale, or multiple or divide the recipe. It also leads to greater consistency in recipes, because it is always based on weight (pounds or kilograms). By looking at percentages, it is easier to tell if one recipe is drier, sweeter, saltier, etc. than another recipe. It also makes predicting what the final product will look like easier. Baker’s % can be used to quickly and easily convert between batch sizes as well.

24. how do you get the percentage of a decimal


Converting decimals to percentage:


Multiply the decimal by 100 or move the decimal point two places to the right. Then, put the percentage symbol (%).

Examples:

0.123 = 12.3%

1.54 = 154 %

To convert percent to decimal, move the decimal point two places to the left.

Ex.

25% -> .25

To convert decimal to percent, move the decimal point 2 places to the right then write the percent sign.

Ex.

.25 -> 25%
