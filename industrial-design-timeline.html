2. Create your own timeline about the Industrial Revolution and Explain it.


Answer:

1760 – James Hargreaves invents the Spinning Jenny, revolutionizing the process of spinning yarn.

1769 – James Watt patents his steam engine, marking the dawn of the industrial revolution.

1776 – Adam Smith publishes The Wealth of Nations, introducing the concept of laissez-faire economics.

1793 – Eli Whitney invents the cotton gin, allowing for the mass production of cotton.

1811 – The Luddite movement begins in England, protesting the advent of new mechanized technologies.

1820 – The first labor union, the National Union of Textile Workers, is formed in England.

1830 – The first passenger railroad in the world is opened in England.

1848 – The Communist Manifesto is published by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels.

1876 – Alexander Graham Bell invents the telephone, paving the way for future communication technologies.

1886 – The first automobile is built by Karl Benz in Germany.

1909 – Henry Ford introduces the Model T, ushering in the age of mass consumerism.

The Industrial Revolution was a period of rapid industrialization and technological advancement that began in Europe during the late 18th century and spread to the rest of the world by the early 20th century. It was marked by innovations such as the Spinning Jenny, the steam engine, the cotton gin, the railroad, and the telephone. The Industrial Revolution also saw the rise of new economic theories such as laissez-faire economics and the formation of labor unions. It also ushered in the age of mass consumerism with the introduction of the Model T automobile.

Sociology is the study of groups and group interactions, societies and social interactions, from small and personal groups to very large groups. A group of people who live in a defined geographic area, who interact with one another, and who share a common culture is what sociologists call a society. Sociologists study all aspects and levels of society. Sociologists working from the micro-level study small groups and individual interactions, while those using macro-level analysis look at trends among and between large groups and societies. For example, a micro-level study might look at the accepted rules of conversation in various groups such as among teenagers or business professionals. In contrast, a macro-level analysis might research the ways that language use has changed over time or in social media outlets.

The term culture refers to the group’s shared practices, values, and beliefs. Culture encompasses a group’s way of life, from routine, everyday interactions to the most important parts of group members' lives. It includes everything produced by a society, including all of the social rules. Sociologists often study culture using the sociological imagination, which pioneer sociologist C. Wright Mills described as an awareness of the relationship between a person’s behavior and experience and the wider culture that shaped the person’s choices and perceptions. It’s a way of seeing our own and other people’s behavior in relationship to history and social structure (1959).

One illustration of this is a person’s decision to marry. In the United States, this choice is heavily influenced by individual feelings; however, the social acceptability of marriage relative to the person’s circumstances also plays a part. Remember, though, that culture is a product of the people in a society; sociologists take care not to treat the concept of “culture” as though it were alive in its own right. Reification is an error of treating an abstract concept as though it has a real, material existence (Sahn 2013).

17. ACTIVITY TIMELINE Directions: Write the history timeline of origami, from the start to present. Timeline


Answer:

Origami, which is the art of paper folding, is one of the most iconic cultural contributions of Japan. When did it get its start? Where did it get its start? How did it get its start? These are all questions answered in this lesson.

Historians are unable to pinpoint an exact date for the beginnings of origami, but it most likely began soon after paper was invented by the Chinese nearly 2,000 years ago, sometime around the year 100 CE. Approximately 500 years later, Buddhist monks brought paper to Japan. Historians speculate that the practice of paper folding is most likely as old as paper itself, so the Chinese probably practiced some form of paper folding before the Japanese.

The name ''origami'' is fairly new compared to the art form's long history. In 1880, the art of Japanese paper folding was changed from orikata, which means ''folded shapes,'' to origami, from the Japanese oru (meaning ''to fold'') and kami (meaning ''paper'').

Pre-colonial Period: This period refers to the time before the arrival of Spanish colonizers in the Philippines. During this time, literature was primarily oral and consisted of folk tales, epics, and poems that were passed down from generation to generation. These works often dealt with themes of heroism, love, and spirituality, and were used to impart cultural values and beliefs to the next generation.

Spanish Period: This period began in 1521 with the arrival of Spanish colonizers in the Philippines and lasted until 1898. During this time, literature was heavily influenced by Spanish culture, with many works written in Spanish. The most notable literary works from this period include the "Cuadros de Costumbres" (Scenes of Life), which depict daily life in the Philippines during Spanish rule, and the "Narciso Reyes" poems, which deal with themes of love and patriotism.

American Period: This period began in 1898 with the end of Spanish rule in the Philippines and lasted until 1946. During this time, English became the dominant language of education and literature in the Philippines, and American cultural influence became more pronounced. Notable literary works from this period include Jose Garcia Villa's "Doveglion: Collected Poems," which is considered a seminal work of Philippine poetry, and N.V.M. Gonzalez's "The Bread of Salt," a coming-of-age story that explores themes of poverty, love, and identity.

Contemporary Period: This period is characterized by a diverse range of literary styles and themes, reflecting the complex and rapidly changing social, political, and cultural landscape of the Philippines. Notable works from this period include Nick Joaquin's "May Day Eve," a short story that explores themes of love, temptation, and the supernatural, and Jun Cruz Reyes' "Ilustrado," a novel that deals with the complexities of Philippine history and identity.
