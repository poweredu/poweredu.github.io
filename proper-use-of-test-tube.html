1. what is the proper technique in heating a liquid in a test tube?​


Answer:

Here are some safety rules and tips for working with test tubes.  They are all very important.  If you want to learn to work with test tubes, start with plain water.  Learn to heat the water without "bumping" before you progress to chemical experiments.  Please be advised, however, that dissolved or undissolved additives can change how the liquid behaves with regard to "bumping".

1.)  Wear safety goggles!

2.)  Make sure the mouth of the test tube is pointed away from yourself and from anyone else.  

3.)  Heat gradually and slowly

4.)  Limit the amount of liquid.

5.)  Never heat a plugged or stoppered tube

6.) Do not put the test tube directly into the flame.

2. give ways of proper handling and care of test tube​


Explanation:

Handling and Storing

Dry test tubes, culture tubes, flasks, and other labware by hanging them on wooden pegs, placing them in baskets with their mouths downward and allowing them to dry in the air, or placing them in baskets to dry in an oven. Drying temperatures should not exceed 140 °C.

23. what is the description and uses of test tube​


Answer:

A test tube is a glass container used in a scientific laboratory. Scientists (and mad scientists) use test tubes to hold chemicals during experiments. If you take a chemistry class, you might use a test tube to handle corrosive chemicals, or to mix two substances safely. Chemical reaction, Test tubes are widely used by chemists to handle chemicals, especially for qualitative experiments and assays. Their spherical bottom and vertical sides reduce mass loss when pouring, make them easier to wash out, and allow convenient monitoring of the contents.
