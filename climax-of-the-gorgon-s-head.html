1. climax of the gorgons head


The Climax of The Gorgon's Head

The climax of the Gorgon's Head is the part when Perseus arrived at the Gorgon's and was able to successfully behead Medusa.

The story of the Gorgon's Head revolves around the importance of the Gorgon's head to the characters (https://brainly.ph/question/195179). First, for Perseus, the Gorgon's head (https://brainly.ph/question/641031) is very important to free his mother for King Polydectes' slavery. Same importance applies to the character Danae, the mother of Perseus. In King Polydectes' case, the Gorgon's head is important to retain Perseus' mother in his slavery (https://brainly.ph/question/161142).

From these, we can infer that the climax of the story is the beheading of the Gorgon by Perseus. Perseus' beheading of the Gorgon affected every character, Perseus, Perseus' mother, and King Polydectes.

21. The gorgon's head exposition, rising action, climax, falling action and resolution?​


This is plot story of The Gorgon's Head.

Exposition

Serphos Island's ruler, King Polydectes, is in love with Danae. Danae's son Perseus is a brash, poor, strong, and brave soldier. The king connives with Perseus to obtain a gorgon's head for him as a wedding present. As soon as Perseus is gone, the monarch can wed Danae.

Rising action

Perseus looks for Medusa and her sisters for a very long time, but they are nowhere to be found. Perseus is instructed by Athene to locate the gray ones—the gorgons' sisters. The gray ones instruct Perseus to visit the large cave in a valley in the region of the Hyperboreans and gaze into her eyes.

Climax

By wielding the sword and shield of Athene, Perseus defeats Medusa.

Falling action

When Perseus gets home, he discovers that his mother has wed the king and that he has had her work all day. He presents the head to King Polydectes, who gazes into it and turns into a statue.

Resolution

Dictys is installed as king. Perseus departs the island and finally ascends to the throne of Mycenae. He delivered the head to Athene, who incorporated it into her shield-armor. The garden is where King Polydectes is placed.

The Gorgon's head of Medusa is a representation of utter dread in Greek mythology. It directs its piercing, wide-eyed glare squarely at the observer while baring sharp teeth and a tongue that extends far from its gaping jaw. Snakes that are deadly and ready to strike wrap around the head in place of hair. Anyone who stares at it while they are mortal turns to stone. Greek hero Perseus is ordered to fetch back Medusa's severed head by his enemy King Polydectes in order to secure his demise. But the goddess of wisdom Athena intervenes and bestows a silvery, reflecting shield upon him.

This gave Perseus the opportunity to sever Medusa's hideous head off while she slept by using the shield as a mirror. He carried the Gorgon head to Athena triumphantly, and she assumed ownership of it from that point on. New demons and monsters, including a winged horse named Pegasus, the emblem of poetry, emerged from the blood of Medusa. So the story goes.
