What are the examples of strategic information? Give an example.​

Strategic Information System include developing a long-term vision, defining company objectives, creating targets, and taking measures to achieve those goals while addressing organizational difficulties. The Strategic Information System helps the company stay focused on its vision and reinvent itself. It is used to offer information about a person's, group's, or organization's present position. It is a conceptual structure that aids in the comprehension of an individual's or organization's current and future surroundings. A strategic information system (SIS) is a type of business information system (BIS) that has the following characteristics: systematic approach to data collection, storage, and retrieval; integration with other systems.

1) Creating entry barriers for competitors: In this approach, a firm employs information systems to produce products or services that are difficult to duplicate or are employed to serve highly specialized markets. This discourages rivals from entering since the expense of adopting a comparable technique is prohibitively expensive. (strategic information)

2) Creating databases to boost marketing: An information system also gives businesses a competitive advantage by creating databases to better their sales and marketing tactics. Existing information is viewed as a resource in such systems. For example, a company may utilize its databases to track consumer purchases, identify distinct market groups, and so on. 26. explain the core competencies and strategic work system design​


Core competencies refer to the unique strengths or capabilities that a company possesses that give it a competitive advantage in the market. These can include things like proprietary technology, specialized expertise, a strong brand, or efficient internal processes.Strategic work system design refers to the process of creating an organizational structure and set of processes that align with a company's core competencies and overall business strategy. This can include things like creating cross-functional teams, streamlining communication and decision-making processes, and implementing performance metrics that align with the company's goals. The goal of strategic work system design is to create a work environment that is efficient, effective, and conducive to achieving the company's strategic objectives.
