1. What particular instrument used to serenade in southern Peru, it has a

resemblance to ukulele but smaller version of mandolin.

a. Rasp

b. charango

c. siku

d. ocarina

-  The instrument charango is widespread throughout the Andean regions of Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, northern Chile and northwestern Argentina, where it is a popular musical instrument that exists in many variant forms. The charango is a small Andean stringed instrument of the lute family, which probably originated in the Quechua and Aymara populations in the territory of the Altiplano in post-Colonial times

2. A percussion instrument that used to communicate between other villages since it sends sounds with meaning.

a. Slit drum

b. atingting kon

c. Djembe

d. Shekere

- Atingting kon or Slit gongs used to communicate between villages. Curved out of wood to resemble ancestors and had a "slit opening" at the bottom. Carry sound for miles through the forest and even across water to neighboring islands.

3. Clapping hands, stomping feet, slapping thighs and pounding their upper arms  on chests produces sounds, this sound called as:

a. Balafon

b. body percussion

c. lamellaphone

d. panpipes

- Percussion instruments produce their sound when a player hits, scrapes, rubs or shakes them to produce vibrations. These techniques can also be applied to the human body.

Stomping: Striking left, right, or both feet against the floor or other resonant surface  , Patting: Patting either the left, right, or both thighs or cheeks with hands  , Clapping hands together  , Snapping fingers

4. All musical instruments that produce sound by means of blowing are good  example of

a. Membranophone

b. aerophone

c. idiophone

d. chordophone

- A blown idiophone is one of the categories of musical instruments found in the Hornbostel-Sachs system of musical instrument classification. These idiophones produce sound when stimulated by moving air. For example, the aeolsklavier features sticks while the piano chanteur features plaques.

5. Musical instruments that produces a resonating sounds brought about by flickin  the strings belong to the family of:

a. Idiophone

b. chordophones

c. membranophones

d. aerophone​

- A chordophone is a musical instrument that makes sound by way of a vibrating string or strings stretched between two points. It is one of the four main divisions of instruments in the original Hornbostel-Sachs scheme of musical instrument classification.
