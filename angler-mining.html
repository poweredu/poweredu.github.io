Angler and angle h are also vertical angles. D. Both a and c​


Answer:

1. a. 101°

2. d. both a and c

Step-by-step explanation:

1. supplementary angles measure 180°, if one angle measures 79°, then the measure of the other angle will be computated by subtracting 79 from 180,

180°-79°= 101°

2. supplementary angles measure 180°, therefore their sum is not equal to 90°. whilst vertical angles are always congruent, which means that they are equal, they are a pair of opposite angles formed by intersecting line, and for that reason they are not always supplementary.

11. angler4. A 40-m electric wire is to be cut into two segments whose ratio is 3:5. What arethe lengths of the two cut electric wires?​


Answer:

15 m and 25 m

#NO TO COPY PASTE SOLUTION!

2 Original Solutions Provided:

Algebraic Solution:

Ratio = 3:5

Wire1 = 3x

Wire2 = 5x

Total Wire = 40m

so we can say,

3x + 5x = 40

8x = 40

x = 40/8

x = 5

solve for the lengths of the 2 wires,

wire1 = 3x = 3(5) = 15 m

wire2 = 5x = 5(5) = 25 m

Logic Solution:

Ratios are simply fractions showing part of a whole.

So for 3:5 ratio, we can say that the 2 ratio in fraction form is,

3/8 and 5/8, since 8 is the whole of 3 and 5.

Now we simply multiply the fractions to the total Length of wire of 40 m.

Wire1 = 3/8 (40) = 15 m

Wire2 = 5/8 (40) = 25 m

Therefore the lengths of the two cut electric wires are 15 m and 25 m respectively.

14. similarities about surface mining and uderground mining?​


Surface mining is suitable for large, low-grade ore deposits which occur below a thin layer of rock, or alluvial deposits found in sand and riverbeds. Underground mining is used for small, high-grade deposits covered with a thick overburden (soil and rock above the ore), or for deep and sloping deposits.

RECOGNIZE THE PRINCIPLE

Image of the fish will be drawn using the Snell's law. As light ray is travelling from water to air (medium of higher index of refraction to medium of lower index of refraction), angle of refraction will be greater than angle of incidence. Light rays reaching the angler will be extrapolated to form image of the fish and then distance of image will be compared to distance of fish from the shore
