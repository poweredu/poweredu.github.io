1. what is art?art appreciation and humanities?


ART APPRECIATION AND HUMANITIES

The humanities are commonly thought to include the arts. Visual arts such as painting and sculpture, as well as performing arts such as theatre and dance, as well as literature, fall into this category. Other humanities, such as language, are frequently referred to as the language arts, for example. The term "humanities" is derived from the Latin word "humanitas" and refers to art, literature, music, architecture, dance, and theatre that highlight human subjectivity and dramatize individual expression. The examination and analysis of the art forms that we are exposed to, on the other hand, is referred to as art appreciation. It can be very subjective, dependent on a person's particular tastes and preferences, or it can be based on a variety of factors, such as the piece's design features and mastery.
