(TRUE or FALSE )1.lines are the most basic of the design elements.2. color is an element that is used to attract attention as well as to represent emotion and mood.3. value refers to how dark or light an area of the design looks.4. icon symbols and dingbats are also considered shapes, and they add interest to a design.5. alignment refers to lining up the elements of a design along the top, bottom, center, or sides of the elements6. red blue and yellow are primary colors.7. positive space or space surrounding the subject8. repetition means object in the design are visually connected so that nothing out of place or disconnected.9. elements of design are the materials you build design with.10 textures refers to what the surface of an object looks and feels like.​


(TRUE or FALSE )

TRUE 1.lines are the most basic of the design elements.

Basically lines can be divided into 4 types: horizontal, vertical, diagonal and curved. Each type can represent a message and a certain feeling for the eyes that see it in the form of a photo

TRUE 2. Color is an element that is used to attract attention as well as to represent emotions and moods.

If you want to make an attractive design, of course you have to consider choosing the right color for the design. Color is often referred to as a crucial part of a graphic design work. The reason is, color can affect people's perceptions when they see it.

TRUE 3. value refers to how dark or light an area of the design looks.

The value is the lightness or darkness of the color of an object, background, or text. A color to be lightened

TRUE 4. icon symbols and dingbats are also considered shapes, and they add interest to a design.

Icon is the display of an object or image that represents the function of the object itself. In this case, icons are made to make it easier for users to access certain objects. This is due to the presence of visual aids that help users become familiar with the icon object

TRUE 5. alignment refers to lining up the elements of a design along the top, bottom, center, or sides of the elements

TRUE 6. red blue and yellow are the primary colors.

Primary colors are the main colors consisting of blue, red and yellow which are also known as Hue. These three basic colors are colors that can be combined and produce other derived colors.

TRUE 7. positive space or space surrounding the subject

In art, negative space is the space around and between the subject of an image. Positive space is the subject or object of the image.

FALSE 8. Repetition means that objects in the design are visually connected so that nothing is out of place or disconnected.

Repetition is repeating one element over and over in a design. Elements are the foundation of design, from line, shape, texture, space, size, to color.

TRUE 9. elements of design are the materials you build designs with.

Design elements have 4 formal elements, namely line, shape, color, and texture. The point is the smallest part of a line, where the point is usually identified by its round shape. A line is formed from many points, or it can also be seen as an elongated point.

TRUE 10 textures refer to what the surface of an object looks and feels like.

Covering the image object using one color gives the impression that a thick color will produce an image that looks thick and close.
