1. Examples of this dance are the dances of the mountain peoples of the cordilleras, dances of the ethnic groups in the cagayan valley region and the ethnic dances in the mindanao regions.


ETHNIC DANCES CORDILLERAN DANCES

Cordilleran Dances are used for rituals, wedding dances, or a mating dance.

Cordillera has six provinces and in each province, they have their own ethnic dances which are unique from each others.

BENGUET: Bendian, a Mountain Province Benguet circular dance, is reenacted while remaining true to the dance's context and meaning. The Bendayan, long known as a dance to celebrate the arrival of successful headhunters, has taken on a new look.MT. PROVINCE: Tayaw is a dance in which arms are extended and accompanied by gongs usually held by men called tayaw. KALINGA: Banga is a dance which takes great skill and body control in which the women have stacked pots on their heads up to ten levels. This symbolizes the strength of Kalinga women in farming and agriculture. CAGAYAN VALLEY REGION

Cagayan proudly displays their dances as courting and wardances.

SAMBALI DANCE: The Sambali dance, a war dance of the Itawis tribe of Cagayan that was converted to Christianity through the intercession of the Lady of Piat, inspired the SAMBALI FESTIVAL.MINDANAO REGION

Mindanao Region is diverse as well.

SINGKIL: Singkil is a traditional Mindanao folk dance. Lake Lanao is where it all began. Singkil is a Mindanao folk dance based on the story in the Darangen, the pre-Islamic Maranao interpretation of the ancient Hindu Indian epic, the Ramayana.
