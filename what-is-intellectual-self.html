30. example of intellectual self​


Examples of Intellectual Intelligence

Here are some examples of a person's intellectual intelligence:

Linguistic Intelligence

This intelligence is usually more dominantly owned by a journalist, lawyer, and poet. Linguistic intelligence is a person's ability in the scope of language to process words well in audio or speech form, as well as in written form.

Mathematical or Logical Intelligence

As the name implies, someone with this intelligence is usually good at making classifications and categorizations, thinking with patterns, understanding cause and effect, and good at making hypotheses. Examples of this intellectual intelligence are often found in scientists, accountants, and programmers.

Spatial Intelligence

Is a complex intelligence that combines sharp sensitivity to visuals, understands the balance of color, line, and shape of space, and makes clear sketches of ideas. People who have spatial intelligence include architects, photographers, designers, pilots, and engineers.

Kinesthetic and Physical Intelligence

This example of intellectual intelligence is dominantly owned by an athlete, craftsman, tailor, and mechanic. A person with this ability has the intelligence to express ideas and feelings and also likes activities that involve a lot of physical activity.

Musical Intelligence

People with musical intelligence have the ability to develop expression and also enjoy music and sound. Singers, musicians, musical instrument players, and songwriters are examples of people who have musical intelligence and generally have the ability to memorize songs quickly even if it's the first time they hear them.

Interpersonal Intelligence

Someone who has a sensitivity to the feelings of others cares, motivates, and understands the character of others. Interpersonal intelligence covers one's life by seeing how the attitude is shown to fellow living beings. An example of this intellectual intelligence is owned by a networker, negotiator, and teacher.

Intrapersonal Intelligence

This intelligence is the opposite of interpersonal intelligence, namely having an understanding of intellectual intelligence and knowledge of oneself. This intelligence can make someone prefer to work alone and seem indifferent. People with this trait are usually found by a counselor and theologians.

Existential Intelligence

Existential intelligence involves abilities in the basic elements of life. People with this intelligence have a high curiosity about the meaning of life, death, and the reality of life.

Naturalist Intelligence

A person's ability to recognize the natural surroundings and care for fellow living beings. People who have this intelligence are able to understand and enjoy nature by using it productively and developing their knowledge of nature.
