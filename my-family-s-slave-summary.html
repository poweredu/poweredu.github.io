Identifying idiomatic expressions

Identifying the meaning of the underlined idiomatic expression.

1. The main reason why he cannot concentrate on his study is because he is a total slave of mobile legend.

Explanation: This idiomatic expression means that someone is totally under the influence of something or someone that he spent most of his time for it.

2. I have been slaving away at this research for almost a month now.

Explanation: This idiomatic expression means ‘working continuously’ or constantly being occupied working at something.

3. My mother has been slaving over an oven for almost five hours in order to bake a cake.

Explanation: This idiomatic expression means spending a lot of time and effort in prepping and cooking a meal.

4. I have been working like a slave, yet I receive a very low salary.

Explanation: This idiomatic expression means working intensely for a very long time yet the work actually does not require much skill thus it does not offer a decent salary.

5. I'd rather put up my own business than to work with a slave driver.

Explanation: This idiomatic expression means a person with very high expectations that it's become unreasonable, towards another person’s effort and skills. They usually push people to work harder than they should.

14. Do you agree with the writer's thesis statement? Why or why not about my family slave​


If you had an emotional response to an essay in The Atlantic this week, you aren't alone.

The piece, written by the late Alex Tizon, tells the story of Eudocia Tomas Pulido, a woman who worked for his family and raised him. But Tizon reveals this stunning truth: For 56 years, Pulido, known to him as "Lola" — grandmother in Tagalog — was never paid for her work. She was his family's slave.

Some hailed Tizon for his writing and for telling the story in the first place. Then came the fury. In comments online, many people asked, why didn't he do more to free her, especially once he became an adult? They asked, why do we need to hear this story from Tizon, the oppressor shaping the narrative of the oppressed? Some comments said the remaining family members should be jailed or made to pay reparations to Pulido's remaining family members.
