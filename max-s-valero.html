11. a car speeds down the road at 20m/s if the cars max is 5000 kg what is the momentum? what is its kinetic energy?


The momentum of an object is equal to the mass of the object multiplied by its velocity. In this case, the momentum of the car would be 20 m/s * 5000 kg, or 100,000 kg*m/s.

The kinetic energy of an object is equal to one half of the object's mass multiplied by the square of its velocity. In this case, the kinetic energy of the car would be 0.5 * 5000 kg * (20 m/s)^2, or 200,000 Joules.

"Banal" is a Tagalog term that means "holy" or "sacred". Max Scheler was a German philosopher who wrote extensively on ethics and values. He did not write a work specifically titled "The Holy" or "Banal", but some of his works dealt with the concept of the sacred and the religious experience. For example, in his book "Ressentiment", Scheler discusses the role of religion in providing a framework for moral values and the experience of the sacred in religious practices.

Max Scheler was not a particularly spiritual thinker in the sense of being concerned with religious or mystical experiences. He was more interested in the nature of values and the role they play in human life. However, Scheler did argue that there is a fundamental spiritual dimension to human existence that goes beyond the material and biological aspects of life. In his view, this spiritual dimension is what gives human beings the capacity for ethical action and the ability to strive for higher ideals.

Max Scheler was born in Munich, Germany in 1874 and died in Frankfurt in 1928. He was a prolific writer and influential philosopher, best known for his work in phenomenology and ethics. Scheler studied under Edmund Husserl and was one of the early members of the phenomenological movement. His major works include "Formalism in Ethics and Non-Formal Ethics of Values", "On the Eternal in Man", and "The Nature of Sympathy". Scheler's ideas have been influential in a wide range of fields, including philosophy, psychology, sociology, and theology.
