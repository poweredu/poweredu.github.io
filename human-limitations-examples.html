18. 1. Can humans be limited factors? Explain using specific example from the reading 2. Do limiting population always decrease population? Explain using the term competition in your answer.


Answer:

2.Limiting factors, by definition, are biotic or abiotic factors that prevent a population from increasing exponentially. Thus limiting factors alone always decrease a population, because they invoke competition among individuals of the population. Since there is competition, some individuals will be better at overcoming a given limiting factor than other, and are more likely to out-survive the less well-adapted individuals. The demise of less well-adapted individuals leads to a decrease in population size.

22. Examples of sex-limited trait and sex-influenced trait on human


Answer:

Sex Limited Traits

Sex limited traits are traits that are only present in one sex of a species. These are controlled by sex limited genes.

Examples:Cattle Milk Secretion - Although both sexes carry genes that control milk, only female cattle can secrete milk.Beard Growth - Although the gene for beard growth is only present in human males, females that are related to males with this gene can pass the gene to their sons.Sex Influence Traits

Sex influence traits are expressed based on the influence of the sex. Sex influence traits are traits that are dominant in one sex but recessive in the other.

Examples:Overall Facial Hair - Both sexes have a facial hair, however females have soft facial hair while males have coarse facial hair.Baldness - This happens more frequently in males due to males generally having larger amounts of testosterone than females.
