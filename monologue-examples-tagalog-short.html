28. Teenage Pregnancy monologue (short)​


Answer:

Here is a short monologue on the topic of teenage pregnancy:

"I never expected to get pregnant at 16. I was just a kid myself, with my whole life ahead of me. But now, everything has changed. I'm scared and overwhelmed, and I don't know how I'm going to handle being a mother.

I know I made a mistake, and I wish I could go back and change things. But it's too late for that. All I can do now is try to make the best of a difficult situation.

I'm not sure how my parents are going to react, or how my friends will treat me. I'm worried about how I'm going to finish high school and pursue my dreams while raising a child.

But I know that I can't give up. I have to be strong for my baby and do everything I can to give them the best life possible. It won't be easy, but I'm determined to make it work."

Explanation:

A monologue is a speech or piece of writing in which a character speaks to themselves or to an audience, either directly or indirectly. Monologues are often used in plays, films, and television shows to reveal a character's thoughts, emotions, or motivations. They can also be used as a literary device in novels and other written works. Monologues can be dramatic, comedic, or somewhere in between, and they can take many different forms, including soliloquies, monologues, and inner monologues.

30. Is dramatic monologue/monologue considered acting?​


Yes

What is a dramatic monologue?

Dramatic monologue

refers to a type of poetry. These poems are dramatic in the sense that they have a theatrical quality; that is, the poem is meant to be read to an audience. To say that the poem is a monologue means that these are the words of one solitary speaker with no dialogue coming from any other characters. Think of one person standing alone on a stage speaking to an audience. Certainly, you are part of that audience, but the poem usually implies that the speaker is mainly talking to a specific person(s).
