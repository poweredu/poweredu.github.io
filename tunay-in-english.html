The English mathematical terms of the following are:

pantuos - calculatorlundayan/centro/sentro - center ikanan (ikot plus kanan) - clockwisetinatlo - cubed or raised to threelugar sa sinampu - decimal placeantas - degreebantod - diameterdako - directionhinahati- divisiontumbasan - equationpaulit ulit - exponentialpulutong - groupnumerong buo - whole numbertuwid - linearlogulit (symbol log)- logarithmdalub matematika - mathematicianmagparami -multiplyhatlibo -one thousandthnumerong tunay - real numberisahati - fractionlutas - solution

These Filipino mathematical words are quite difficult to understand since the Philippines uses English in teaching mathematics. However, if you are going to look at the etymology of the words, you will be able to understand why those are the terms for such.

Etymology of Some Filipino Mathematical Terms

Pantuos or calculator is a combination of a prefix 'pan' and an action word 'tuos'. The word 'pan' refers to usage and 'tuos' means to calculate. Numerong buo or whole number is a direct translation. Numero means number while buo means whole. It is also the same for numerong tunay or real number. The Filipino word for real is tunay but sometimes we use the word totoo in another context.lutas or solution is also a direct translation from Filipino to English. There are lots of other synonyms for lutas. Other Filipino words that can describe solution are solusyon, paraan, and more.Tumbasan or equation makes so much sense since the word 'tumbas' in Filipino means equal or equivalent. When we add the suffix '-an' the word translates to having equal parts.

BA-INGLES

Ang Ba-Ingles ay isa sa mga folk dance ng Pilipinas. Ito ay sinasayaw na ng matagal na panahon sa bansa, partikular na sa Ilocos Sur. Noong 1962, inilahad ni Ginang Francisca Reyes Aquino ang Ba-Ingles sa Stanford University. Sinundan ito ni Bernardo Pedere noong 1978 sa Stockton.

Ang Ba-Ingles ay galing sa CABUGAO, ILOCOS SUR.  Ang Ba-Ingles ay hango sa mga salitang BAILE at Ingles.  Ito ay sinasabing dinala ng mga mangangalakal na galing ng INGLATERA. Ang pag-curtsy sa iyong partner at tagapanood ay tinatawag na BOW o SALUDO. Ang KUMINTANG sa Ba-ingles ay ang pagikot ng kamay o galanggalangan.
