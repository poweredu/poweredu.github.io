7. Brianna’s parents built a swimming pool in the backyard. Brianna says that the distance around the pool is 120 feet. Is she correct? Explain your reasoning, or show your work.


Answer:

No, Brianna is not correct.  The distance around the pool is 131.4 ft.

Step-by-step explanation:

The distance around the pool are:

1) Semi circle with a diameter equal to the side opposite it, 20 ft.

Find the length of half the circle:

Circumference  of semi-circle = (1/2)(πd)

Where:

π = 3.14

d = 20 ft.

Circumference of semi-circle = (1/2)(3.14)(20 ft) = 31.4 ft.

2) Vertical lengths from the endpoint  of semi-circle to the endpoints of horizontal lengths, 40 ft each ⇒ 2 × 40 ft. = 80 ft.

3.) Horizontal length at 20 ft.

Add the semi-circle, vertical, and horizontal lengths:

Distance around the pool = 31.4 ft + 80 ft. + 20 ft.

Distance around the pool = 131.4 ft.

9. Brianna pushes a 20 kg box with a force of 50N to achieve an acceleration of 2.5 m/s/s. In order to push a 30 kg box at the same acceleration, how much force will Brianna need to apply?


FORCE

Problem:

๑ Brianna pushes a 20 kg box with a force of 50N to achieve an acceleration of 2.5 m/s/s. In order to push a 30 kg box at the same acceleration, how much force will Brianna need to apply?

Answer:She will need to apply 75 Newtons of force.

[tex] {\orange{ \underline{ \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad \quad \quad \quad \quad \quad\quad\quad \quad \quad }}}[/tex]

Formula:[tex] \underline{ \boxed{ \tt \: F = m \times a}}[/tex]

To calculate the net Force, multiply the given mass and acceleration in order to get answer.

Solution:

Based on the problem, the given mass is 30 kg and the acceleration is 2.5 m/s².

so,

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt \color{grey}{Force = mass \times acceleration} \\ \tt \: F = m \times a \\ \tt \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = 30 \: kg \times 2.5 \: m/s² \\ \: \: \: = \green{\tt \: 75 \: N}[/tex]

Therefore, the force is 75 N.
