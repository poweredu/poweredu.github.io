29. His greatest contribution to chemistry of amadeo avogrado


Answer:

Amedeo Avogadro's (1776-1856) principal contribution to chemistry was a paper in which he advanced two hypotheses: (1) that equal volumes of gas contain equal numbers of molecules and (2) that elementary gases such as hydrogen, nitrogen, and oxygen were composed of two atoms.

Amadeo Avogadro is best known for his Avogadro's Law, which states that the volume of a gas is directly proportional to the number of molecules present. This law is important because it provides the basis for the modern understanding of the relationship between the volume of a gas and its moles, and it is essential for understanding the behavior of gases.
