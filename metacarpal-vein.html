1. What is metacarpalneed long explanation​


Answer:

The metacarpals are long bones within the hand that are connected to the carpals, or wrist bones, and to the phalanges, or finger bones. The metacarpals together are referred to as the ‘metacarpus.’ The tops of the metacarpals form the knuckles where they join to the wrist. On the palm side, they are covered with connective tissue. You can feel and see the metacarpals on the back of your hand, through your skin. The five metacarpals are called thumb metacarpal, index metacarpal, middle metacarpal, ring metacarpal, and small metacarpal. Ten percent of all fractures that occur are those to the metacarpals and phalanges, the most common injuries being from car accidents, sports injuries, and work-related injuries. The goal in repairing these injuries is to do so while maintaining strength of hand grip and no residual pain upon using the hand. Boxers tend to have high incidence of fracture to metacarpals, hence the term ‘Boxers Fracture.’

19. A.how does the blood in the pulmonary vein differ from that in other veins


Answer:

the only difference between pulmonary veins and other veins is that pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood (rich oxygen, carbon dioxide poor)towards heart while other veins carry deoxygenated blood towards heart.

Explanation:

-there are three basic types of blood vessels there are arteries veins and capillaries.

-arteries deliver oxygen rich blood to the cell away from the heart however an exception is the pulmonary artery which carries deoxygenated or oxygen-poor blood from the heart to the lungs.

-veins return oxygen-poor blood from the cells back to the heart. however, an exception is the pulmonary vein which carries oxygenated or oxygen-rich blood from the lungs to the heart.

-capillaries are small blood vessels that connect arteries and veins. this can penetrate the inner portion of different organs and facilitate the transfer of oxygen, carbon dioxide, and dissolved substances in and out of your blood.
