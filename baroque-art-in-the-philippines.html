27. what are the different churches influenced by baroque arts in the philippines?​


Answer:

During the Spanish colonial period, the Catholic Church played a significant role in shaping the artistic landscape of the Philippines. The Baroque style, which originated in Italy in the 17th century, was one of the artistic movements that influenced the architecture and art of Philippine churches during this period. Some of the churches that showcase the influence of Baroque art include:

1.    San Agustin Church - Located in Intramuros, Manila, San Agustin Church is a fine example of Baroque architecture. The church was built in the 16th century and features intricate carvings, paintings, and sculptures that reflect the Baroque style.

2.    Nuestra Señora de la Asuncion Church - Located in Santa Maria, Ilocos Sur, Nuestra Señora de la Asuncion Church is another example of Baroque architecture in the Philippines. The church was built in the 18th century and features a façade adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures.

3.    Santo Domingo Church - Located in Quezon City, Santo Domingo Church is one of the largest churches in the Philippines. The church was built in the 18th century and features ornate decorations that reflect the Baroque style.

4.    Loboc Church - Located in Bohol, Loboc Church is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the best-preserved examples of Baroque architecture in the Philippines. The church was built in the 18th century and features intricate carvings, paintings, and sculptures.
