19. Krypton The first scale of electronegativity was developed by Linus Pauling and on his scale krypton has a value of 3.00 on a scale running from from about 0.7 (an estimate for francium) to 2.20 (for hydrogen) to 3.98 (fluorine).​


Answer:

Explanation:

Krypton, an element in the periodic table with the symbol Kr and atomic number 36, has a value of 3.00 on the electronegativity scale developed by Linus Pauling. This scale was created to measure the ability of an atom to attract electrons towards itself when it is part of a molecule. The scale ranges from 0.7, an estimated value for francium, to 2.20 for hydrogen, to 3.98 for fluorine. Krypton falls in the middle of the scale, indicating that it has moderate electronegativity. This property is important in chemical reactions and bonding, as atoms with higher electronegativity tend to attract electrons more strongly, creating more polar bonds.
