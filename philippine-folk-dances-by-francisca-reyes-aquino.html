Francisca Reyes Aquino was a Filipino folk dancer, choreographer, and researcher who is known for her extensive documentation and preservation of Philippine folk dances. Her works on Philippine folk dances are compiled in five volumes, each focusing on different regions of the country.

In Volume I, Aquino collected and documented folk dances from the northern region of the Philippines. The dances included in this volume were mainly influenced by Spanish, American, and Chinese cultures. The dances are lively and energetic, showcasing the rich culture and heritage of the Ilocanos, Pangasinenses, and other northern tribes.

Volume II contains folk dances from the central region of the Philippines. These dances have a more pronounced Malay influence and are characterized by graceful movements and elaborate costumes. Some of the dances included in this volume are the Binanog, Habanera Capiz, and the popular Tinikling dance.

Volume III focuses on folk dances from the southern region of the Philippines. The dances in this volume are influenced by the Muslim culture and are characterized by their intricate hand movements and colorful costumes. Some of the dances included in this volume are the Pangalay, Singkil, and Kappa Malong-Malong.

Volume IV contains folk dances from the western region of the Philippines. The dances in this volume are a fusion of various cultures, including Malay, Chinese, and Spanish. The dances are characterized by their lively and upbeat music and playful movements. Some of the dances included in this volume are the Maglalatik, Itik-Itik, and Binasuan.

Lastly, Volume V features dances from the eastern region of the Philippines, which includes the Visayas and Mindanao. The dances in this volume are a fusion of Spanish, Malay, and indigenous cultures. The dances are characterized by their graceful movements and colorful costumes. Some of the dances included in this volume are the Kuratsa, Sayaw sa Bangko, and the Pangalay sa Tapis.

Overall, Francisca Reyes Aquino's Philippine Folk Dances, Volumes I to V, serves as a valuable resource for those interested in Philippine culture and heritage. Answer:

Francisca Reyes Aquino was a renowned Filipino researcher and choreographer who made significant contributions to the preservation and promotion of traditional Philippine folk dances. In 1949, she traveled to various countries across Asia and Europe to study foreign folk dances and bring them back to the Philippines.

During her travels, Reyes Aquino observed and documented a wide range of folk dances from different cultures, including the Kung Fu dance from China, the Tinikling from Indonesia, the Saludo dance from Spain, and the Tarantella from Italy. She also learned about the traditional dances of Japan, India, and Greece, among others.

Upon her return to the Philippines, Reyes Aquino incorporated the elements and techniques she had learned from foreign folk dances into her own choreography, creating new and innovative styles that showcased the rich cultural diversity of the Philippines. Her work helped to revitalize traditional Philippine folk dances and inspire new generations to appreciate and embrace their cultural heritage.

Today, the legacy of Francisca Reyes Aquino lives on through the many dance companies and schools that continue to perform and teach her choreography, as well as through the ongoing efforts to preserve and promote traditional Philippine folk dances as a vital part of the country's cultural identity.
