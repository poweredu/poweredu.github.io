14. how does an ofw become an agent of the globalization process?​


Overseas Filipino Worker (OFW) is an agent of globalization by eliminating national boundaries and is defined as the movement of people from one country to another with the aim of getting a job. There are several motives underlying the movement of workers between countries or international migration.

EXPLANATION:

Globalization has removed national boundaries and also removed barriers between high moving costs, regional topography and transportation. Disappearance of barriers between it encourages people to do international migration. In other words, globalization has removed barriers for people to migrate between countries and even between continents.

OFW International Migration is defined as the movement of people from one country to another with the aim of finding work. Currently, it is estimated that tens of millions of people work in countries other than their country of birth. However, although many efforts have been made to protect these migrant workers, many of them experience vulnerability and face serious risks during the migration process.

Migration between countries in the era of globalization is something that cannot be prevented or avoided. The motives underlying the movement of labor between countries or international migration. The first motive, they work abroad with the aim of selling their labor, skills or intelligence. Usually the main flow of this motive labor flow comes from developing countries to developed countries, or from poor countries to rich countries, or from countries with a surplus of labor to countries experiencing a shortage of labor.

The second motive is that they work abroad in connection with technology sales or investment. The main flow of this second motive is generally from developed countries to developing countries. Globalization opens people to move with various reasons they have. This migration problem is also not only a problem for receiving countries, but also for sending countries where after these migrants return to their country of origin it will be a problem that must be resolved.

Refinement and improvement of the education and training system for prospective migrant workers is a priority so that they have high quality and competitiveness and know their rights as migrant workers. As citizens of the world, Philippines migrant workers are expected to contribute to progress and prosperity at the national and global levels.

Perhaps the greatest gift that God has given man is nothing else but the gift of life, plus the gift of freedom. God knows very well that giving us life alone won't make us truly happy. It is through the gift of freedom that we become autonomous and independent creatures. However, human freedom can either be used or abused. And for most of us, it turns out that we usually abused our freedom to the point that we become unpleasing in the eyes of God.

Good  thing, this freedom comes with the Gift of WILL. As human beings we are all endowed with a "will" and our actions and decisions in life are dependent on our will.
