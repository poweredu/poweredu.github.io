1. How did Gregorio Del Pilar defended Tirad pass?​


Answer:

Gregorio del Pilar, also known as Goyo, was a Filipino revolutionary general during the Philippine-American War. He is best known for his defense of Tirad Pass, a mountain pass in the Cordillera mountain range in the Philippines.

In December 1899, American forces, led by General Samuel M. Whitside, were advancing towards Tirad Pass with the goal of capturing Emilio Aguinaldo, the President of the Philippines. Goyo, who was just 22 years old at the time, was tasked with defending the pass.

Goyo, who was in charge of about 120 men, set up defensive positions at the narrowest point of the pass, which was only about 3 meters wide. He used the natural terrain to his advantage and placed sharpshooters in strategic positions to slow down the American advance.

On December 2, 1899, American forces attacked the pass but were met with heavy resistance from Goyo's men. Goyo personally led several counterattacks, but the Americans were able to outflank his position. Despite being heavily outnumbered, Goyo and his men fought bravely and were able to hold off the American advance for several days.

On December 9, Goyo ordered his men to retreat and covered their withdrawal, fighting until the last possible moment. He was able to successfully delay the American advance, allowing Aguinaldo and his government to escape. However, Goyo himself was killed in the battle.

Tirad Pass is now considered as one of Philippines's heroic historical site due to the bravery and leadership of Goyo Del Pilar.


2. What is the factual meaning of "The battle of Tirad Pass" painting by General Gregorio del Pilar


Answer:

The Battle of Tirad Pass (Tirad); (Spanish: Batalla de Paso Tirad; Tagalog: Labanan sa Pasong Tirad; Ilocano: Gubat ti Paso), sometimes referred to as the "Philippine Thermopylae",[4] was a battle in the Philippine–American War fought on December 2, 1899, in northern Luzon in the Philippines, in which a 60-man Filipino rear guard commanded by Brigadier General Gregorio del Pilar succumbed to more than 500 Americans, mostly of the 33rd Volunteer Infantry Regiment under Major Peyton C. March, while delaying the American advance to ensure that President Emilio Aguinaldo and his troops escaped.[5]

7. bakit tinaguriang bayani ng pasong tirad si gregorio del pilar​


Sagot:

Sa murang edad ay pinangunahan ni Heneral Gregorio del Pilar ang labanan sa Pasong Tirad. Patunay lamang ito na hindi sa edad nakabase ang pagiging bayani o paggawa ng kabutihan para sa bayan.

Narito ang ilan sa mga dahilan kung bakit tinaguriang bayani ng PAsong Tirad ang "Boy General o mas kilala sa tawag na Heneral Gregorio del Pilar.

Si Gregorio Hilario del Pilar y Sempio (Nobyembre 14, 1875 - Disyembre 2, 1899) ay isa sa pinakabatang heneral ng Pilipinas noong panahon ng Rebolusyong Pilipino at Digmaang Pilipino-Amerikano. Kilala siya sa kanyang matagumpay na pag-atake sa mga kuwartel ng Espanyol ng Cazadores sa munisipalidad ng Paombong, sa kanyang tagumpay sa unang yugto ng Labanan ng Quingua at sa kanyang huling paglaban sa Labanan ng Tirad Pass sa panahon ng digmaang Pilipino-Amerikano.

Nang sumiklab ang Rebolusyong Pilipino laban sa Espanyol noong Agosto sa ilalim ng pamumuno ni Andres Bonifacio, sumali si del Pilar sa pag-aalsa. Ipinakilala niya ang kanyang sarili bilang komandante habang nakikipaglaban sa mga pulutong ng Espanya sa Bulacan.

Nakilahok siya sa isang pag-atake sa Kakarong de Sili-Pandi, Bulacan, isang bayang tutol sa Katipunan. Noong Enero 1, 1897, siya ay kabilang sa mga nakipaglaban nang ang mga Espanyol ay sumalakay para makuhang muli ang bayan.

Pinangunahan ni del Pilar ang kanyang mga tropa sa isang matagumpay na laban kay Kumandante Franklin Bell sa unang yugto ng Labanan ng Quingua o Plaridel ngayon noong Abril 23, 1899.
