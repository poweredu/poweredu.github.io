Inductive in data gathering activities 3. 4. Has high output replicability No conclusions formulated 5. 6. Uses large sample size Data analysis is subjective 7. Unstructured or semi structured 8.


Answer:

Dependent on statistical tools

Uses open-ended questions 2. X

Inductive in data gathering activities 3. X

No conclusions formulated 5. X

Uses large sample size X

Data analysis is subjective 7. X

Unstructured or semi structured 8. X

Qualitative research does not use closed-ended questions, but rather relies on open-ended questions to gather rich, detailed data about participants' experiences, opinions, and attitudes.

Qualitative research is inductive, meaning that the research design emerges from the data collection process rather than being predetermined. The researcher may make observations, collect data, and form hypotheses based on what they observe.

Quantitative research has high output replicability because it uses standardized data collection tools and procedures that can be repeated by other researchers to obtain similar results.

Qualitative research does not seek to draw definitive conclusions or make generalizations about a population, but rather to gain an in-depth understanding of a specific phenomenon.

Quantitative research typically uses large sample sizes to ensure statistical validity and representativeness of the population being studied.

Qualitative research involves subjective interpretation of data, as the researcher relies on their own observations and perceptions to make sense of the data they collect.

Qualitative research often uses unstructured or semi-structured data collection techniques, such as interviews or observations, that allow participants to express themselves in their own words and on their own terms.
