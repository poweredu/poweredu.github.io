1. kristen gets home from work 5:34pm kristens dinner is ready at 5:51pm how many seconds after kristen gets home is hher dinner ready?


Answer:

1020 seconds

Step-by-step explanation:

In this problem, we are simply asked to find the length or duration of a certain time period, and convert it into a specific unit stated by the question.

First, we need to find the difference between the two time stamps in the problem. Since they happened within the same hour, we can disregard the hours part, and easily just subtract the minutes part.

51 minutes - 34 minutes = 17 minutes

Now, we need to convert 17 minutes into seconds. To do this, we first need to understand the relationship of seconds to minutes and how bigger one is from the other.

We know that there are 60 seconds per 1 minute. Thus, we can just multiply this conversion by the difference a while ago.

(17 min) (60 sec / 1 min) = 1020 sec

Therefore, Kristen waits 1020 seconds after arriving home for her dinner.

Hope this helps!

2. Kristen gets home from work at 5:04 p.m Kristen's dinner is ready at 5:40 p.m how many seconds after Kristen gets home is her dinner ready?​


Answer:

2160 seconds

Step-by-step explanation:

From 5:04 PM to 5:40 PM, 36 minutes have passed. However, in this problem, we are asked for the number of seconds. We have to convert the answer "36 minutes" first.

Remember that 1 minute is equal to 60 seconds. Thus, to find the final answer, we just have to multiply 36 minutes by 60 seconds per minute.

36 min x 60 sec / min = 36 x 60 sec = 2160 sec

Therefore, it would take 2160 seconds after Kristen gets home before her dinner is ready.

29. Kristen wants to have Php2,000,000 for retirement in 45 years. She invests in a mutual fund and pays 8.5% each year, compounded quarterly. How much should she deposit into the mutual fund initially? Patingin din po ng solution salamat


Answer:

Compound Interest

Kristen wants to have Php2,000,000 for retirement in 45 years. She invests in a mutual fund and pays 8.5% each year, compounded quarterly. How much should she deposit into the mutual fund initially?

Solution:

Since we need to find the initial amount Kristen needs to deposit, we need to cancel out (1 + r/n)^nt both sides, to obtain the formula for computing the principal.

[tex]\implies{\sf{A= (1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\sf{\frac{A}{P (1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}} = \frac{P \cancel{(1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}}}{\cancel{(1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\sf{\frac{A}{(1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}} = P}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\implies{\sf{P = \frac{A}{ (1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\: [/tex]

Getting the PrincipalGiven: A = Php2,000,000t = 45 yearsr = 8.5% or 0.085n = 4 periods per year (compounded quarterly)P = ?

[tex]\implies{\tt{P = \frac{A}{P (1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\tt{P = \frac{Php \: 2,000,000}{ (1+\frac{0.085}{4})^{4(45)}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\tt{P = \frac{Php \: 2,000,000}{ (1+0.02125)^{180}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\tt{P = \frac{Php \: 2,000,000}{ (1.02125)^{180}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\implies{\tt{P = \frac{Php \: 2,000,000}{ 44.03}}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\implies{\tt{P = Php \: 45,423.57 }}}[/tex]

Note: The final answer and all computations are rounded off into nearest hundredths (two decimal places)
