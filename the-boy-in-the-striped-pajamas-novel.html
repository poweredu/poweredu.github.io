9. How is Boy G. manifested in George Lamming’s novel In the Castle of My Skin in a symbolic way?​


In George Lamming's novel In the Castle of My Skin, Boy G. is manifested in a symbolic way as a representation of the destructive impact of colonialism on the Caribbean society.

Boy G. is a character who is introduced in the novel as a restless and rebellious young man who challenges the established order and seeks to break free from the oppressive structures of colonial rule. He is depicted as a charismatic and influential figure who inspires others to resist and fight for their rights.

However, as the novel progresses, Boy G. becomes increasingly radicalized and violent, leading a group of rebels in a bloody uprising against the colonial authorities. His actions ultimately result in the destruction of his community and the loss of many lives, including his own.

In this way, Boy G. symbolizes the danger of unchecked resistance and the devastating consequences of violence in the struggle for liberation. His fate serves as a cautionary tale about the risks of blindly following charismatic leaders and the importance of finding a balance between resistance and reconciliation in the struggle against colonialism.
