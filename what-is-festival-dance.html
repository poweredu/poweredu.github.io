What is Festival?

It is an event where people or communities gather to celebrate something. Mainly it is connected by their religion or culture in a particular day or group of days. Festival may be religious o non-religious festivals.

Religious Festival

It was celebrated in honor of a certain religious icon. For example, Sinulog was celebrated in honor of Sto. Nino.

Non-Religious Festival

This festival doesn't involve religious icons. The main focus is on the culture and heritage aspect of the country or town.  

What is Festival Dance?

It is a cultural dance performed with lively and loud beats of percussion instruments, colorful and talented dancers, and usually celebrate in honor of their patron and thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. Festival dances may be religious or non-religious. Festival dances make you see people's culture by portraying people's way of life through movement, costumes and etc.

Dinandayagan festival

Dinagyang is a Hiligaynon word that means “merrymaking.” Just like the Sinulog and Ati-Atihan Festivals, Dinagyang Festival is one of the religious festivities in the Philippines that celebrates the feast of the Santo Niño and the pact between the Datus and locals.

Every 4th Sunday of January, the festival transforms the Iloilo City into a massive street party with overflowing drinks and food. The most exciting part is that the city hosts a highly competitive Dinagyang Festival dance contest and grand float parades.

Kadayawan festival

Indak-Indak, a local term for dance, is the highlight of the Kadayawan Festival. It's a one-of-a-kind street dance competition featuring wildly colorful costumes. The competition gets dancers moving to thundering drumbeats and performances that depict stories from the diverse tribes and Lumad heritage in Mindanao.

19. ATI-ATIHAN FESTIVAL A feast held annually in January in honor of the Santo Niño (infant Jesus), concluding on the third Sunday, in the island and town of Kalibo, Aklan in the Philippines. The name “Ati-atihan” means “to be like aetas” or make believe Ati’s.” Aetas were the primary settlers in the islands according to history books. They too are the earliest settlers of Panay island where the province of Aklan is situated.

20. ATI-ATIHAN FESTIVAL The festival consists of tribal dance, music, accompanied by indigenous costumes and weapons, and parade along the street. Christians and non-Christians observe this day with religious processions. It has inspired many other Philippine festivals including the Sinulog Festival of Cebu and Dinagyang of Iloilo, both adaptations of the Kalibo Ati-atihan Festival.

21. MORIONES FESTIVAL An annual festival held on Holy Week on the island of Marinduque, Philippines. The “Moriones” are men and women in costumes and masks replicating the garb of biblical Roman soldiers as interpreted by local folks. The Moriones or Moryonan tradition has inspired the creation of other festivals in the Philippines where cultural practices or folk history is truned into street festivals.

27. What is festival dance​


Answer:

Festival dance refers to a type of dance performed during a festival or celebration. Festivals are often marked by cultural or religious events, and dance is a common form of expression during these occasions. Festival dances vary greatly depending on the culture or region in which they are performed. Some festival dances are performed as a way to honor ancestors or deities, while others are intended to entertain and bring people together in celebration. In some cases, festival dances may incorporate traditional costumes, music, and instruments that are specific to the region or culture in which they originated. Overall, festival dance is a way to celebrate important events and to connect people to their cultural heritage.
