- Origin of the word "ideal"

The word "ideal" originated from the Late Latin word "idealis" which means "existing in idea" or "concerned with ideas".

- Meaning of the word "ideal"

The word "ideal" refers to a standard of perfection or excellence that one aspires to achieve or attain.

- Advantages and disadvantages of the word "ideal"

The advantage of having an ideal is that it provides a clear vision or goal to work towards, which can motivate and inspire individuals to improve and achieve their full potential. However, the disadvantage of having an ideal is that it can lead to unrealistic expectations and disappointment if one is unable to meet or attain the ideal standard.

- Example of the word "ideal"

An example of the word "ideal" is a person striving to become the ideal employee by consistently meeting and exceeding their job responsibilities, collaborating well with others, and continually improving their skills and knowledge.

9. 14. In the Ideal Gas Law, R stands for a. Ideal gas constant b. Real gas constant c. Temperature constant d. Ideal gas law​


Answer:

d) Ideal Gas Law

Explanation:

The ideal gas law describes the behavior of an ideal sample of gas, and how that behavior is related to the pressure (P), temperature (T), volume (V), and molarity (n) of the gas sample. In the equation PV=nRT, the term “R” stands for the universal gas constant.

Answer:

D. Ideal gas law​

Explanation:

Ideal gas law

The ideal gas law, also called the general gas equation, is the equation of state of a hypothetical ideal gas. It is a good approximation of the behavior of many gases under many conditions, although it has several limitations. It was first stated by Benoît Paul Émile Clapeyron in 1834 as a combination of the empirical Boyle's law, Charles's law, Avogadro's law, and Gay-Lussac's law.[1] The ideal gas law is often written in an empirical form:

   P V = n R T {\displaystyle PV=nRT} PV=nRT

where P {\displaystyle P} P, V {\displaystyle V} V and T {\displaystyle T} T are the pressure, volume and temperature; n {\displaystyle n} n is the amount of substance; and R {\displaystyle R} R is the ideal gas constant.
