19. a kidnapper demands a 40kg cube of platinum as ransom. what is the dimension of the cube?​


Answer:

12.0035246675 cm on all sides

Explanation:

since Platinum has a density of 21.45 g/cm^3, we must divide the total mass of the cube by the density of the material. i.e. 40 Kg/(21.45 g/cm^3).

express 40 Kg as g. Since a kilogram is 1000 grams, multiply it by 40. Therefore, 40,000 g/(21.45 g/cm^3).

Cancel similar units (g), to get the total volume of the cube, i.e. approximately 1864.8018648 cm^3.

Given that the structure is a cube, its total volume must be one of its side multiplied by itself 3 times. Reverse it and get the cube root of the total volume to get the dimensions of the Platinum cube weighing 40 Kg.

Then, we will get to approximately 12.0035246675 cm.
