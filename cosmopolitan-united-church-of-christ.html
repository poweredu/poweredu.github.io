DOGMATIC CONSTITUTION ON THE CHURCH

LUMEN GENTIUM

SOLEMNLY PROMULGATED BY HIS HOLINESS

POPE PAUL VI

CHAPTER I

THE MYSTERY OF THE CHURCH

1. Christ is the Light of nations. Because this is so, this Sacred Synod gathered together in the Holy Spirit eagerly desires, by proclaiming the Gospel to every creature,(1) to bring the light of Christ to all men, a light brightly visible on the countenance of the Church. Since the Church is in Christ like a sacrament or as a sign and instrument both of a very closely knit union with God and of the unity of the whole human race, it desires now to unfold more fully to the faithful of the Church and to the whole world its own inner nature and universal mission. This it intends to do following faithfully the teaching of previous councils. The present-day conditions of the world add greater urgency to this work of the Church so that all men, joined more closely today by various social, technical and cultural ties, might also attain fuller unity in Christ.

20. how can you make yourself united in the Body of Christ through thr Church?​


Answer:

LAR KIN

what is the calling of Christ the king in the kingly mission of Jesus Christ to the community

The calling of Christ the King refers to Jesus Christ's role as a spiritual and moral leader for Christians. In the Bible, Jesus is often referred to as a king, and his mission on Earth is seen as a kingly one. This means that he came to Earth to serve as an example for his followers and to show them how to live their lives in a way that is pleasing to God. As Christ the King, Jesus was also tasked with bringing God's message of love and salvation to the world, and with establishing the Kingdom of God on Earth.

LAR KIN

how can you make yourself united in the Body of Christ through the Church?

One way to become united with the Body of Christ through the Church is to regularly participate in the sacraments, particularly the Eucharist. The Eucharist, also known as Holy Communion, is a central part of Christian worship in which believers eat bread and drink wine to symbolize the body and blood of Christ. By participating in the Eucharist, believers are able to experience a deeper sense of unity with Christ and with other members of the Church.

Another way to become united with the Body of Christ through the Church is to actively engage in the life of your local church community. This can involve attending worship services regularly, participating in small groups or Bible studies, and serving in various ministry roles. By building relationships with other believers and supporting the work of the Church, you can become more deeply connected to the Body of Christ.

It is also important to pray regularly and seek a personal relationship with Christ. Through prayer, you can draw closer to God and become more aware of his presence in your life. This can help you to better understand your role within the Body of Christ and how you can contribute to the Church's mission.
