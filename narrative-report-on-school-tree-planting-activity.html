15. Threes are to be planted in three schools A, B, C. Thirty percent of the trees are to be planted in school The rest are B and A. to be planted in school C in the ratio of 5:2 respectively. If & fewer trees will be planted in school C than B, now trees are to many be planted in school A.​


Let's use algebra to solve the problem.

Let x be the total number of trees to be planted. Then, 30% of x will be planted in school A and (100% - 30%) = 70% of x will be planted in schools B and C.

So, the number of trees to be planted in schools B and C is 0.7x. Let y be the number of trees to be planted in school C in the ratio of 5:2. Then, y/7 will be the number of trees to be planted in school C for every 1 part of school B.

Since there will be 4 parts in total (2 for school C and 2 for school B), the total number of trees to be planted in school C is (y/7) x 2 = (2y/7) and the total number of trees to be planted in school B is (y/7) x 2 = (2y/7).

The problem states that the number of trees to be planted in school C will be 300 fewer than the number of trees to be planted in school B, so we can set up the equation:

(2y/7) + 300 = (0.7x) x 0.7

Simplifying this equation, we get:

2y/7 + 300 = 0.49x

To find the number of trees to be planted in school A, we can use the fact that 30% of x will be planted there:

0.3x = x - (2y/7) - (2y/7)

0.3x = x - (4y/7)

Simplifying this equation, we get:

4y/7 = 0.7x - 0.3x

4y/7 = 0.4x

y = (0.4x) x (7/4)

y = 1.75x

Now we can substitute y into the first equation:

(2(1.75x)/7) + 300 = 0.49x

0.5x + 300 = 0.49x

0.01x = 300

x = 30,000

Therefore, 30,000 trees will be planted in school A, 10,500 trees will be planted in school B, and 4,375 trees will be planted in school C.

University of Maryland/NFPA

Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem Symposium

As the world continues to evolve and transform, there are both new and lingering challenges that impact fire and life safety; many examples that point to gaps, cracks and weaknesses in our safety system that otherwise should protect communities. The NFPA Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem identifies the components that must work together to minimize these risks and help prevent loss, injuries and death from fire, electrical and other hazards.

In September 2022, the UMD Department of Fire Protection Engineering and the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) hosted their inaugural symposium to discuss the principles of the NFPA Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem and its application to address today's fire safety issues. The symposium brought together top researchers, academics, risk managers, code officials, engineers, architects, fire service personnel, public policy makers and urban planners who presented, collaborated and applied learnings of the Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem to the modern-day built environment. Future symposia will seek to do the same:

OBJECTIVES:

Bring together leaders from across the fire and life safety spectrum to learn about a full safety systems approach and encourage action-oriented collaboration between industry professionals using the principles of the Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem

Build consensus for the development of a multi-disciplinary group at the University of Maryland and universities in the Baltimore-Washington area to collaborate on research and educational programming for university students and working professionals associated with the Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem

Initiate discussion of subsequent symposia that focus on the partner activities related to the Fire & Life Safety Ecosystem

Fire safety is the set of practices intended to reduce destruction caused by fire. Fire safety measures include those that are intended to prevent the ignition of an uncontrolled fire and those that are used to limit the spread and impact of a fire.

Fire safety measures include those that are planned during the construction of a building or implemented in structures that are already standing and those that are taught to occupants of the building.

Threats to fire safety are commonly referred to as fire hazards. A fire hazard may include a situation that increases the likelihood of a fire or may impede escape in the event a fire occurs.

Fire safety is often a component of building safety. Those who inspect buildings for violations of the Fire Code and go into schools to educate children on fire safety topics are fire department members known as Fire Prevention Officers. The Chief Fire Prevention Officer or Chief of Fire Prevention will normally train newcomers to the Fire Prevention Division and may also conduct inspections or make presentations.

19. Trees are to be planted in three schools,A,BC. Thirty percent of the trees are to be planted in school A. The rest are to be planted in school B and C in the ratio of 5:2 respectively. If 6 fewer trees will be planted in school C than B, how many trees are to be planted in school A.


The number of trees planted in school A is 6.

Explanation:

From the problem, we know that 30% of the trees will be planted in school A, which is 0.3x.School B and School C will each receive a 5:2 split of the remaining trees.Give the number of trees planted in school B a representation of 5 and that of school C a representation of 2 in each case.

In light of the fact that school C will have 6 fewer trees planted than school B, so:

[tex]2y = 5y - 6[/tex]

Solving for y, we get:

[tex]y = 2[/tex]

Therefore, the number of trees planted in school B is:

[tex]5y = 5(2) = 10[/tex]

And the number of trees planted in school C is:

[tex]2y = 2(2) = 4[/tex]

So the total number of trees planted is:

[tex]0.3x + 10 + 4 = x[/tex]

Simplifying this equation, we get:

[tex]0.3x - x = -14[/tex]

[tex]-0.7x = -14[/tex]

[tex]x = 20[/tex]

Therefore, the number of trees planted in school A is:

[tex]0.3x = 0.3(20) = 6[/tex]

Hence, 6 trees will be planted in school A.
