7. How will you describe the effect of collision between:continental- continental* oceanic-oceaniccontinental - oceanic​


Explanation:

- the effect of continental to continental boundary, when they smash together. it creates mountain ranges. The example of it is himalaya mountains that result of convergent boundary.

- When a convergent boundary occurs between two oceanic plates (oceanic to oceanic), one of those plates will subduct beneath the other. Normally the older plate will subduct because of its higher density.

- When continental and oceanic plates collide, the thinner and more dense oceanic plate is overridden by the thicker and less dense continental plate. The oceanic plate is forced down into the mantle in a process known as "subduction."

In reality, any macroscopic collision between objects will convert some kinetic energy to internal energy and other forms of energy, so no large-scale impacts are perfectly elastic. However, some problems are sufficiently close to perfectly elastic that they can be approximated as such.


21. What Type Of Collision Resulted To The Formation Of Gravitational Waves Detected By LIGO?a. Elasticb. Inelasticc. Perfectly Inelasticd. Elastic Then Perfectly Inelastic ​


Answer:

B. Inelastic

Explanation:

The gravitational waves detected by LIGO were generated by the collision of two black holes, which is an example of an inelastic collision. In an inelastic collision, the objects involved stick together and lose some kinetic energy as they deform or break apart. In the case of the black holes, the collision was so violent that it created ripples in the fabric of spacetime, which were detected as gravitational waves by LIGO.
