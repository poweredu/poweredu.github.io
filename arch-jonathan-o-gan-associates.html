14. An arch over an entrance is semicircular with an opening that is 5 feet wide. Write an equation that models that arch.


Answer:

If the opening is 5 feet wide, I assume you mean from the center point of the straight edge to the top of the arch. Thus the radius of the arch is 5 feet. Since it is on top, I assume the arch points upward and so the equation is y = √(25-x2)

This equation can be found from the equation of a circle (x-h)2+(y-k)2=r2. The point (h,k) is the center of the circle (here we defined the center to be (0,0)) and r=5 so r2=25. Thus x2+y2=25. Then you solve for y but only use the positive answer since we're talking about the top semicircle.

Answer:

Romanesque architecture is an architectural style of medieval Europe characterized by semi-circular arches. There is no consensus for the beginning date of the Romanesque style, with proposals ranging from the 6th to the 11th century, this later date being the most commonly held. In the 12th century it developed into the Gothic style, marked by pointed arches. Examples of Romanesque architecture can be found across the continent, making it the first pan-European architectural style since Imperial Roman architecture. The Romanesque style in England is traditionally referred to as Norman architecture.
