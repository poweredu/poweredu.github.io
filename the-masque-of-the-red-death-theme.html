2. The Masque of the red death by Edgar Allan Poe What is the 21st century theme and why?​


Answer:

The 21st century theme of The Masque of the Red Death is that of inequality and social injustice. This is seen in the story when Prince Prospero attempts to insulate himself from the plague that is ravaging the land by throwing a lavish masquerade ball in his castle, attended by only the wealthy and privileged. This theme resonates in the 21st century as there is still a large amount of social and economic inequality present in the world, with some people having access to resources and opportunities that others do not.

The 'Red Death' had long devastated the country. No pestilence had ever been so fatal, or so hideous.''

This quote is from ''The Masque of the Red Death'' by Edgar Allen Poe, which is a story about how some wealthy folks thought they could cheat the spread of a horrible disease, and therefore, death.

In the story, Prince Prospero invites 1,000 of his closest friends to stay with him in his castle while the terrible disease runs rampant across his country. After about six months, the prince decides to throw an unusual masked ball to entertain them.

However, the party, which takes place in a suite of seven color-themed rooms, doesn't go as planned. A strange and horrifying masked figure with the face of a corpse suddenly appears at midnight and starts to stalk around the rooms. The prince confronts the figure, but when he gets close to it, Prospero abruptly falls to the floor and dies. All of the other guests in the castle face the same fate when they come in contact with the figure, who Poe reveals as the Red Death.

14. The Masque of the red death by Edgar Allan Poe What is the Literary Approach?and Why​


Answer:

The "Masque of the Red Death" is a short story by Edgar Allan Poe in which a prince named Prospero attempts to avoid a deadly plague, known as the Red Death, by hiding in his castle with his friends. The literary approach to this story would be the way in which Poe uses various literary techniques to convey the story's themes and messages. Some examples of literary techniques used in the story include symbolism, imagery, and personification.

Answer:

The literary approach to The Masque of the Red Death is to examine the story from a thematic perspective. This focuses on the idea of how death is inevitable and how it is impossible to outrun mortality. Throughout the story, Poe uses the metaphor of a red death to symbolize the inevitability of death and how it can affect all social classes. He examines how people attempt to escape their fate, and ultimately how futile it is to do so. He also uses visual motifs such as the seven coloured rooms to emphasize the idea of death and its presence in life.
