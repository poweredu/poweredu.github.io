2. about sangyaw festival


The Sangyaw Festival is a religious, social and local celebration held in Tacloban City on June 29. Sangyaw means "proclaiming news" in the Waray Language. It was made in the 80s by Imelda Marcos, the first woman before. Her nephew and former Mayor Alfred Romualdez resurrected the festival in 2008. The resurrected celebration turned into a major achievement, and social contingents took an interest in contending on this side of the region from various performing gatherings of various celebrations in the nation.

Parade of Lights is made up of floats, merrymakers, local drums and bugle corp. from various Tacloban City schools and barangays. Part of the fiesta celebration's highlight is a dazzling nighttime Parade of Lights that will shine with the moon and stars heralded and accented by inventive music and rhythm, energetic street dancing, merrymaking, an imaginative costume show and much more.

28. Why did the sangyaw festival stop in 1986​


Answer:

It was stopped after the People Power Revolution in 1986, which ended the Marcos regime. Sangyaw's revival forced the provincial government to move the parade schedule to June 27 from the original June 29, the fiesta eve to give way for the Sangyaw Festival presentation.
