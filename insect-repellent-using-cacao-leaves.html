28. environmental impact of insect repellents?


Answer:

Toxicity to non-target organisms: Insect repellents contain active ingredients that can be toxic to non-target organisms such as bees, butterflies, and other beneficial insects. When these insects come into contact with the insect repellent, they may die or suffer other negative impacts.

Contamination of soil and water: The active ingredients in insect repellents can contaminate soil and water when they are sprayed or applied. This contamination can affect the health of plants, animals, and humans who come into contact with these resources.

Air pollution: Aerosol insect repellents can release volatile organic compounds (VOCs) into the air, which can contribute to air pollution and negatively impact air quality.

Plastic waste: Many insect repellents come in plastic packaging that can contribute to plastic waste, which can harm wildlife and ecosystems.

Negative impact on biodiversity: The use of insect repellents can disrupt the natural balance of ecosystems by killing insects that play important roles in pollination, pest control, and other ecological functions.
