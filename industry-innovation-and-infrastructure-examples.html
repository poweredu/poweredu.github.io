What are the challenges , goal and innovations to the Philippine government to improve the technology infrastructure of the country? ​

Inadequate infrastructure has been a significant impediment to economic progress and poverty alleviation in the Philippines. Despite the fact that the country has relatively high levels of access to water, sanitation, and power, service levels have not kept up with increasing population expansion and urbanization. The paucity of energy and space to run supercomputers has been a fundamental problem in increasing computer processing capacity. IT administrators have always been on the search for better and quicker technologies that would aid in the speedier processing of today's massive volumes of data. The Inclusive Innovation Industrial Strategy (i3S) intends to expand creative and internationally competitive manufacturing, agriculture, and services while increasing their links to local and global value chains, with innovation (infrastructure) at the heart of the country's strategic policies and initiatives. It argues that, in order to alleviate infrastructure limitations, the Philippines should gradually boost infrastructure investments to at least 5% of GDP, as well as enhance expenditure efficiency. Furthermore, it is strongly suggested that the path forward for long-term infrastructure development requires instituting a rigorous fiscal reform program; pursuing ongoing reforms in key sectors—particularly power, roads, and water—to improve cost recovery, competition, and institutional credibility; and sharply reducing corruption. Sustainable infrastructure incorporates all four components of sustainability: social, environmental, economic, and institutional. 23. Steam engine is termed as the defining innovation of the _____ industrial revolution​


Answer:

Steam engine is termed as the defining innovation of the First industrial revolution

Explanation:

A steam engine is a machine that uses heat and steam pressure to create mechanical motion. The steam engine was the first machine capable of converting thermal energy to mechanical energy while being convenient and practical. Historically, the steam engine is associated with the Industrial Revolution, a period when new manufacturing processes were developed in Britain, continental Europe, and the United States. Additionally, the steam engine was widely used in industry and transportation throughout the 19th century.
