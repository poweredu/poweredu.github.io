6. Analyze the Poem entitled, "In my desire to be nude” by Jose Garcia Villa.


Answer:

"In my desire to be nude" is a poem by Jose Garcia Villa that explores the speaker's desire to strip away the societal constraints and expectations that confine them. The poem is filled with rich imagery and metaphors that convey the speaker's yearning to be free and authentic.

The poem is divided into three stanzas, each of which explores a different aspect of the speaker's desire to be nude. In the first stanza, the speaker expresses their desire to "be naked of clothes," suggesting that they want to shed the trappings of societal expectations and conventions. They long to be free of the constraints that clothing represents, and to exist in a more natural and authentic state.

In the second stanza, the speaker goes further, expressing a desire to be "naked of skin." This line is a metaphor for the speaker's desire to strip away even deeper layers of social conditioning and self-consciousness. They want to be truly vulnerable and open, without any protective barriers. This vulnerability is a prerequisite for true intimacy and connection with others.

In the final stanza, the speaker takes their desire to be nude to its logical conclusion: they want to be "naked of bone." This is the most extreme expression of the speaker's desire to be free and authentic. By shedding even their physical skeleton, the speaker is expressing a desire to be completely unencumbered, to exist in a state of pure being.

Overall, "In my desire to be nude" is a powerful exploration of the human desire for authenticity and freedom. The poem's rich imagery and metaphors invite the reader to consider their own desires for connection and vulnerability, and to contemplate the barriers that stand in the way of achieving these goals.

8. does the poem Sonnet 1 by Jose Garcia Villa have rhythm and meter?​


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

This lyric poem has both rhythm and meter.  

For rhythm, simply identify the stressed and unstressed syllables in each line.

For meter, identify or count the number of stressed syllables.

e.g.

In the line, "It must be slender as a bell" -- the bolded words are stressed

The overall meter of the poem is iambic trimeter with a rhyme scheme of rhyming couplets (AA, BB, CC, etc.)

First, a poem must be magical,

Then musical as sea gull.

It must be a brightness moving

And hold secret a bird’s flowering.

It must be slender as a bell,

And it must hold fire as well.

It must have the wisdom of bows

And it must kneel like a rose.

It must be able to hear

The luminance of dove and deer.

It must be able to hide

What it seeks, like a bride.

And over all I would like to hover

God, smiling from the poem’s cover.

27. Write a critique paper about Jose Garcia villas poem "if my sun set in the west". It should be 250 words.​


Answer:Jose Garcia Villa's poem "If My Sun Set in the West" is a beautiful and thought-provoking piece of literature. The poem is a part of Villa's collection of poems titled "Doveglion: Collected Poems" [1]. The poem is a reflection on love and the inevitability of its end. The speaker in the poem is contemplating the end of a relationship and the pain that comes with it. The first stanza of the poem sets the tone for the rest of the piece. The speaker starts by saying, "If my sun set in the west, / I'd never look to the east." This line suggests that the speaker is willing to let go of the past and move on with their life. However, the second stanza presents a different perspective. The speaker says, "But my heart is never a sun / And my love, never a west." Here, the speaker suggests that their love is not like the sun that sets in the west
