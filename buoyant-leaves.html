10. define buoyant force.


Answer:

Buoyancy, or upthrust, is an upward force exerted by a fluid that opposes the weight of a partially or fully immersed object. In a column of fluid, pressure increases with depth as a result of the weight of the overlying fluid

Formula

Archimedes' principle

F_{b}=-\rho g V

F_{b} = buoyant force

\rho = fluid density

g = acceleration due to gravity

V = fluid volume

27. 1. Which is NOT TRUE about weight and buoyant force? A. When object's weight is greater than the buoyant force, the object will sink. B. When object's weight is smaller than the buoyant force, the object will float. C. When object's weight is smaller than the buoyant force, the object will neither sink or float. D. When object's weight is greater than the buoyant force, the object will neither sink or float.PLEASE ANSWER THIS AND EXPLAIN BECAUSE I NEED THIS​​


Answer:

D. When object's weight is greater than the buoyant force, the object will neither sink or float.

This statement is not true because when the weight of an object is greater than the buoyant force, the object will sink. The buoyant force is a force exerted on an object submerged in a fluid that acts in an upward direction and opposes the weight of the object. If the weight of an object is greater than the buoyant force, the object will sink. On the other hand, if the weight of an object is smaller than the buoyant force, the object will float.

29. an object is buoyant if it _______​


Answer:

Two forces act on an object when it enters water: a downward force called gravity and an upward force called buoyancy. An object's weight measures the downward force of gravity that acts on it.

Explanation:

An object will float if the buoyancy force exerted on it by the fluid balances its weight. But the Archimedes principle states that the buoyant force is the weight of the fluid displaced. So, for a floating object on a liquid, the weight of the displaced liquid is the weight of the object.
