7. Rate of Reaction Student A investigated the rate of reaction between calcium carbonate (marble chips) and hydrochloric acid. The student collected 10.0 cm3 of gas produced after 15 seconds. Student B investigated the rate of reaction between magnesium and hydrochloric acid. The student collected 19.5 cm3 of gas produced after 10 seconds. Which student’s investigation had a faster rate of reaction?


Given:

[tex]V_{\text{A}} = \text{10.0 cm}^{3}[/tex]

[tex]t_{\text{A}} = \text{15 s}[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{B}} = \text{19.5 cm}^{3}[/tex]

[tex]t_{\text{A}} = \text{10 s}[/tex]

Required:

the student's investigation having a faster rate of reaction

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the rate of reaction investigated by Student A.

[tex]rate_{\text{A}} = \frac{V_{\text{A}}}{t_{\text{A}}}[/tex]

[tex]rate_{\text{A}} = \frac{\text{10.0 cm}^{3}}{\text{15 s}}[/tex]

[tex]rate_{\text{A}} = \text{0.667 cm}^{3}/\text{s}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the rate of reaction investigated by Student B.

[tex]rate_{\text{B}} = \frac{V_{\text{B}}}{t_{\text{B}}}[/tex]

[tex]rate_{\text{B}} = \frac{\text{19.5 cm}^{3}}{\text{10 s}}[/tex]

[tex]rate_{\text{B}} = \text{1.95 cm}^{3}/\text{s}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the student's investigation having a faster rate of reaction.

Since Student B's investigated rate of reaction is greater than of Student A,

[tex]\boxed{\therefore \: \text{Student B's investigation has a faster rate of reaction.}}[/tex]

13. If 0.25 g of magnesium metal were to react with an excess amount of hydrochloric acid solution, how many grams of hydrogen gas (H2) would be produced after the reaction?


Answer:

mass H2 = 0.021 g

Explanation:

Writing the balanced chemical equation.

Mg + 2HCl → Mg Cl2 + H2

Solving for the mass of H2 produced.

mass H2 = 0.25 g Mg × (1 mol Mg / 24.31 g Mg) × (1 mol H2 / 1 mol Mg) × (2.016 g H2 / 1 mol H2)

mass H2 = 0.021 g

29. In a reaction with hydrochloric acid, why does powdered magnesium reacts faster than the same mass of magnesium ribbon?a. The powdered magnesium contains more atoms than the magnesium ribbon.b. The powdered magnesium is hotter than the magnesium ribbon.c. The powdered magnesium has a bigger surface area than the magnesium ribbon.d. The powdered magnesium has a smaller surface area than the magnesium ribbon.


Answer:

The powdered magnesium reacts faster than the magnesium ribbon because the powdered magnesium has a bigger surface area than the magnesium ribbon.

Explanation:

Kinetics is the study of the speed of chemical reaction. Some chemical reactions proceeds very fast while others are slow. Depending on what is being observed or needed, Chemists tend to slow down or speed up a chemical reaction. There are  several factors that affect chemical reactions.

Some factors are listed below:

Concentration of reactantsPressure of gaseous reactantsNature of reactantsParticle size of reactantsTemperatureCatalyst

For the situation given, the magnesium compounds in the reaction contain the same mass but differ in particle size. One is powdered and the other is in the form of a ribbon. Particle size will be our basis of our analysis.

Powdered substances have bigger surface areas. Increase in surface area of a solid reactant exposes more of its particle to come in contact with the other reactant. This will result to more collision between reactant particles, hence, speeding up the reaction.
