Media as a source of information

Media are all forms and channels that can be used in a process of presenting information. The presence of the media in the community certainly gives a very l4rge contribution in providing information to the public, both at the local, national and international levels.

The following are various kinds of media as sources of information:

1. Newspapers or print media.

Newspaper or print media is a printed sh33t that contains reports that occur in the community with characteristics, published periodically, general in nature, mass content, actual, about anything and from any where in the world, which contains value for the public to know.

Advantages :

Segmentation of the nature of the reader is clear, flexible, The price is more affordable, The information contained in newspapers can be read repeatedly, Can be used as literature and the presentation is more in-depth and detailed

Disadvantages :

2. Electronic Media

Also called broadcast media (broadcasting media), electronic media is media that presents information in sound (audio) and image (visual) formats using electrical technology.

Advantages :

Electronic media disseminate information to the public quickly, Audio visuals in electronic media make it easier for people to catch news, especially in television electronic media, Electronic media has a wide reach among the general public.

Disadvantages :

You cannot duplicate what is already broadcast when presenting news in electronic media.

3. Online Media

Online media is a means of publishing information through websites on the internet. Online media are also called news sites, cyber media (cyber media), internet media, and digital media.

Advantages :

The process of disseminating information is very fast, Old information or data can be easily reopened at any time, The form of content delivered is very different, namely text, images, audio, video and Can be accessed from any where and anytime as well as practical and flexible to use, Online media users can interact with each other.

Disadvantages :

The information it contains is not always correct and also incomplete, because it prioritizes updates, Users must have a device that supports this and a very stable internet connection, Long-term use of online media can cause eye fatigue and also cause eye health problems.

Information dissemination has been revolutionized in recent years. New information technologies offer a number of attractive features for practitioners who conduct staff training. To illustrate, training DVDs can represent a means of providing relevant information and procedural demonstrations that reduce a practitioner’s time for training staff relative to BST conducted entirely in a face-to-face manner (Macurik et al., 2008). However, caution is also warranted based on what research has shown to represent effective training of staff job skills. Much of the information technologies still rely heavily on dissemination of verbal information, with the addition of performance-based modeling in many cases (Reid et al., in press). Assuming ready access to visual media and technology along with the growing body of research supporting its use—albeit with some inconsistency—it can be helpful for practitioners to consider technological applications in staff training programs. It is recommended, however, that when employing visual media and technology, practitioners ensure that each of the steps of BST are still incorporated within the training. Particular attention is warranted to ensure that the performance practice and feedback steps are implemented to ensure trainee competency. The latter steps may be included, for example, through interactive software involving real time applications or other types of interactive computer training.
