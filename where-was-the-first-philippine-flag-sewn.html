13. write this references or sources where you find the information of the philippine flag​


Answer:

The national flag of the Philippines (Filipino: pambansang watawat ng Pilipinas) is a horizontal bicolor flag with equal bands of royal blue and crimson red, with a white, equilateral triangle at the hoist. In the center of the triangle is a golden-yellow sun with eight primary rays, each representing a province.At each vertex of the triangle is a five-pointed, golden-yellow star, each of which representing one of the country's three main island groups—Luzon, the Visayas (though originally referring to the island of Panay)[b] and Mindanao. The white triangle at the flag represents liberty, equality, and fraternity. A unique feature of this flag is its usage to indicate a state of war if it is displayed with the red side on top, which is effectively achieved by flipping the flag

upside-down.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

The Philippine flag is rectangular.

The area of ​​the Philippine flag that was sewn in 1898 is 4,050 square units.

The length is twice the width.

Question:

What are the dimensions of the Philippine flag?

​Asked:

The first step is to create a linear equation.

The length is twice the width.

p = 2 x l

p = 2l

The area of ​​the Philippine flag that was sewn in 1898 is 4,050 square units.

p x l = L

p x l = 4,050

The second step we substitute p = 2l into the equation p x l = 4.050.

[tex]p~*~l=4,050\\\\2l*l=4,050\\\\2l^2=4,050\\\\l^2=4,050:2\\\\l^2=2025\\\\l=\sqrt{2025} \\\\l=45[/tex]

p = 2l

p = 2 * 45

p = 90

Philippines flag size is 90 units x 45 units.

SECTION 13.

When displayed with another flag, the Philippine flag shall be on the right of the other flag. If there is a line of other flags, the Philippine flag shall be in the middle of the line.

When carried in a parade with flags, which are not national flags, the Philippine flag shall be in front of the center of the line.
