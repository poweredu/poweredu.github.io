30. A 20 ft beam leans against a wall. The beam reaches the wall 13.9 ft above the ground. What is the measure of the angle formed by the beam and the ground


Answer:

44.03 degrees

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked? The measurement of the angle formed by the beam and the groundWhat are given? 20 ft - length of beam13.9 ft - the wall reached by the beamHow to solve? We are dealing with a right triangle by analyzing the problem. We will first denote the certain points and lines of the triangle.

side a = distance between the wall and the base of the beam

side b = the wall

side c = the beam

angle A = angle formed by the wall and the beam

angle B = angle formed the the beam and ground

angle C = angle formed by the wall and ground (90degrees) or the right angle.

We are to find angle B using the known variable which are sides b and c. With these we can use the formula:

sinB= opposite/hypotenuse

The side opposite angle B is the height of wll which is side b and the hypotenus is the height of the beam which is side c. Solution:

sinB=opposite/hypotenuse

sinB=b/c

sinB=13.9ft/20ft

sinB=0.695

B= sin^(-1) 0.695

B=44.03

Conclusion:

Therefore the angle formed by the ground and the beam measures 44.03 degrees.
