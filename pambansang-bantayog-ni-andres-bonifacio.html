3. samala tagpuan ang bantayog ni andres bonifacio​


Answer:

The Andrés Bonifacio Monument, commonly known simply as Bonifacio Monument or Monumento, is a memorial monument in Caloocan, Philippines which was designed by the National Artist Guillermo Tolentino to commemorate Philippine revolutionary Andrés Bonifacio, the founder and Supremo of the Katipunan. Andrés Bonifacio fought for independence from the politically and socially ruthless colonial rule by Spain.[1] The monument 13.7 m (45 ft) in height with symbolic images and other features known as the "Cry of Balintawak" is acclaimed as one of the best monuments in the world.

26. NEED KO PO NGAYON :( Write a description of the Romantic style filipino artwork in terms of its inspiration,subject value and tone 1.Pambansang Bantayog ni Andres Bonifacio 2.University of Philippines Manila 3.Spoliarium CORRECT=BRAINLIEST


1 Pambansang Bantayog ni Andres Bonifacio - The artwork is a national monument located in Caloocan City, Philippines that depicts Andres Bonifacio, a Filipino revolutionary hero and founder of the Katipunan. The inspiration for the artwork is the heroism and bravery of Bonifacio, who fought for the freedom and independence of the Philippines. The subject value is focused on the history and culture of the Philippines, particularly the struggle for independence from colonial rule. The tone of the artwork is patriotic and solemn, emphasizing the importance of remembering the sacrifices of the nation's heroes.

2 University of Philippines Manila - This artwork is a painting that depicts the University of the Philippines Manila campus, featuring its iconic buildings and landmarks. The inspiration for the artwork is the beauty and grandeur of the university, as well as its role in shaping the nation's intellectual and cultural heritage. The subject value is focused on education and learning, highlighting the importance of academic excellence and intellectual growth. The tone of the artwork is nostalgic and uplifting, celebrating the university's rich history and contributions to Philippine society.

3 Spoliarium - This artwork is a painting by Filipino artist Juan Luna, which won a gold medal in the 1884 Madrid Exposition of Fine Arts. It depicts the Roman practice of spoliation, in which gladiators were stripped of their weapons and armor after a fight. The inspiration for the artwork is Luna's experience living in Europe and observing the grandeur of Roman history and culture. The subject value is focused on the beauty and brutality of the human form, as well as the decadence and corruption of imperial power. The tone of the artwork is dark and brooding, emphasizing the intensity and drama of the scene and its historical significance.
