Answer:

Jerome Seymour Bruner (1915-2016) is one of the best known and influential psychologists of the twentieth century. He was one of the key figures in the so called ‘cognitive revolution’ – but it is the field of education that his influence has been especially felt. His books The Process of Education and Towards a Theory of Instruction have been widely read and become recognized as classics, and his work on the social studies programme – Man: A Course of Study (MACOS) – in the mid-1960s is a landmark in curriculum development. More recently Bruner has come to be critical of the ‘cognitive revolution’ and has looked to the building of a cultural psychology that takes proper account of the historical and social context of participants. In his 1996 book The Culture of Education these arguments were developed with respect to schooling (and education more generally). ‘How one conceives of education’, he wrote, ‘we have finally come to recognize, is a function of how one conceives of the culture and its aims, professed and otherwise’ (Bruner 1996: ix-x).

25. Who is saint jerome?


Saint Jerome

Saint Jerome is a known doctor of the church who is also a patron saint of archaeologists, translators, librarians, scholars, and encyclopedists. He is also regarded as the second most voluminous writer in ancient Latin Christianity. Aside from that, Saint Jerome is known for translating the Bible to Latin which was then called the Vulgate. This translation eventually became the official Latin version of the Bible, especially in the 16th century.

Some Important  Points in the Life of Saint Jerome

Saint Jerome was born around 342-345 A.D. as Eusebius Sophronius Hieronymous. Some articles say that he was born in Stridon, Dalmatia - an ancient town that is most probably situated in Croatia or Slovenia. Although, others say he was born in Venice, Italy. Regardless, here are more important facts about the life of Saint Jerome:

He was initially educated at his Christian home by Aelius Donatus and later moved to and studied in Rome at the age of 12.Saint Jerome went on to study philosophy and rhetoric. He also learned languages such as Greek, Latin, Hebrew, and Chaldaic which prepared him for some of his greatest works.He gained ecclesiastical training in Trier, worked as a translator, became a hermit Antioch, eventually a priest, and became a secretary to Pope Damasus in Rome.When Pope Damasus died, he was greatly criticized and eventually escaped to Jerusalem.Saint Jerome, along with his followers, went to Bethlehem and established a monastery, a convent, and a pilgrim hostel.He stayed there until his death on September 30, 420 - which would then become his feast day.Some of his works include: "Concerning Seven Beatings",  "Dialogi contra Pelagianos", and "Book of Interpretation of Hebrew Names".

Jerome Seymour Bruner (October 1, 1915 – June 5, 2016) was an American psychologist who made significant contributions to human cognitive psychology and cognitive learning theory in educational psychology.
