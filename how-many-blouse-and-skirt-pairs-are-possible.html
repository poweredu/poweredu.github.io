   Fundamentals of counting is a rule used to count the entire number of alternative outcomes in a circumstance is known as the fundamental counting principle. It states that if there are n ways to do something and m ways to do something else after that, there are n m nm methods to do both of these acts.    

FOR EXAMPLE:

 The fundamental principles of counting, If there are X ways to do something and Y ways to do something else, then there are X*Y ways to execute both acts, according to the product rule.

21. Assuming, that any skirt can be paired with any blouse, in how many ways can Anna select her outfit? List the possibilities


Answer:

[tex]x * y[/tex] ways

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Let [tex]x[/tex] represents the number of blouses

[tex]y[/tex] the number of skirts.

In every 1 skirt there's [tex]y[/tex] choices of blouses and vice versa, the two are independent of one another. Therefore, to get the number of possible combinations, we will apply the multiplication principle [tex]x * y[/tex] . Multiplication Principle states that for every independent conditions, there are [tex]x * y[/tex] pairs.

Example:

5 skirts and 7 blouses

For every 1 skirt there are 7 blouses to choose from. Therefore, there are 35 ways
