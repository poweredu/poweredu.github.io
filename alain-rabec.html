1. Aldrin and Alain had the same amount of money. Aldrin spent P30 for his snacks Alain spentP12 on Pineapple Juice. After that, the amount of money Alain had left was three times theamount Aldrin had left How much did each have at first?​


Answer:

P39 was the initial money Aldrin and Alain each both have

Step-by-step explanation:

Let

x = Aldrin's Money

y = Alain's Money

We establish first that they both have the same amount of money.

[tex]x = y\\[/tex]

Then, we deduct their food expenses.

[tex]x - 30 = z[/tex]

z= Aldrin's remaining money

[tex]y-12 = w[/tex]

w = Alain's remaining money

Then, we establish the last equation after their expenses

[tex]w = 3z[/tex]

We substitute the values of w and z with x and y

[tex]w = 3z\\y -12 = 3 (x -30)[/tex]

We substitute x with y since the first equation shows that x and y are initially the same amount

[tex]y - 12 = 3 (x-30)\\y - 12 = 3 (y - 30)[/tex]

We solve for the value of y

[tex]y -12 = 3(y-30)\\y - 12 = 3y - 90\\y - 12 + 12 = 3y -90 + 12\\y = 3y - 78\\y - 3y = 3y - 78 - 3y\\-2y = -78\\\\\frac{-2y}{-2} = \frac{-78}{-2} \\ y = 39[/tex]

We now have y = 39

Since x = y, it means that x = 39

Therefore, Aldrin and Alain both have P39 at first.
