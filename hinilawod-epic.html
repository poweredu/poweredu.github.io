11. The oldest and longest epic poem in Panay. a. Hinilawod b. Balitaw c. Maragtas d. Ambahan


Hinilawod

The correct answer among the listed given is letter a. Hinilawod is known as the oldest and longest epic poem in Panay.

History of Hinilawod

Hinilawod is translated generally to be ''Tales From The Mouth of The Halawod River". This is a story of the exploits of the 3 Sulodnon demigod brothers in ancient Panay namely:

Labaw Donggon- the eldest among the 3 brothers, one of the main protagonist HumadapnonDumalapdap

The original form of this epic would take 3 days to perform that's why it is considered as the longest epic poem in Panay.

This is written by one of the Ilongo writers, Dr. Felipe Landa Jocano.

Some other Characters in the epic HinilawodAlunsina -Goddess of the eastern skyKaptan -King of the gods Datu Paubari -A mortal; mighty ruler of halawod Maklium-sa-twan -God of the plainsSuklang Malayon - Known as the Goddess and guardian of happy homes; known as the sister of Alunsina

The information written in this book was originally from the written and oral work of Pedro Alcantara Monteclaro. The Maragtas book contains relevant information on the deep history of Panay from the first inhabitants and the Bornean immigrants. This is known as a debate or dialogue song where a young woman and a young man have a competition for pitching and competing with the best romantic verses.Ambahan - this is a kind of old traditional poetry which is written on bamboo. This came from the Southern Hanunuo Mangyans of Oriental Mindoro. This is a poetic expression of complementing each other.


12. 3. What is the moral from the epic "Hinilawod"?​


Hi! The epic tribal poem of Hinilawod is fascinating. Did you know that it was only discovered by an outsider, anthropologist Felipe Landa Jocano, by accident on the island of Panay? He made sure to tell the poem in tapes to bring back to his office to study. If told continuously, this poem may take three days to finish!

This epic poem was only passed down from one generation to the other by mouth so there have been many changes and different versions over the years. However, the main ideas still remain: Hinilawod or the Tales From The Mouth of The Halawod River tells the story of a goddess who married a mortal and the adventures of her three demigod sons.

The moral stories found throughout the epic are:

True and faithful love shall and always prevail.Deference to your parents and elders will always be rewarded.Greatness is achieved when there is someone to share the load with. Even powerful men need others to survive.A clean conscience and a pure heart will always be rewarded.Greed will eventually lead to your downfall.

15. 4. Give some important information about the Visayan epic Hinilawod.​


Answer:

The Hinilawod epic tells the story of the exploits of the three demigod brothers, Labaw Donggon, Humadapnon and Dumalapdap of Panay.

Hinilawod is an epic poem written by the early inhabitants of Sulod in central Panay who lived by the mouth of the Halawod River. It literally means “Tales From The Mouth of The Halawod River.”

It was first discovered serendipitously in 1955, when F. Landa Jocano, then a young college student—now a renowned anthropologist—traveled the hinterlands of his home province in Iloilo, collecting folksongs, stories and riddles.

Hinilawod is thus considered to be one of the longest epics known—even longer than the “Iliad” which has 15,700 verses.
