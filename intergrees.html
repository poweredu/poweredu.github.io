1. It is anything that interferes with the sender's creating and delivering a message and the receiver interpreting the message. *


Something that interferes the sender's creating and delivering message and the receiver interpretating the message is a distraction. Distraction is separating or distancing ourselves from the reality or problem we need to focus on in the present moment, shifting our attention to another subject that is more worrying or more enjoyable.

Distraction can be a completely mechanical phenomenon, and can be caused by an inability to pay attention, a lack of interest in the object of attention, increased interest or interest in something other than the object of attention, or impaired attention.

In fact, the most sensitive indices to decline in executive function were the distraction capacity index and the process index, which identified working memory and working speed in simple tasks.

Distractions come, as noted above, from external sources, such as those recognized by the senses and physical stimuli, or internal sources such as thoughts, emotions, fantasies, or physical impulses.
