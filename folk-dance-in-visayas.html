Folk Dances of Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao

Folk dances are a type of vernacular dance. It is a recreational activity that displays the past and present culture of a place.

Luzon Folk Dances

1. Aray – This folk dance is described as flirty and uses the pañuelo or a shawl together with tambourines to make graceful moves. ‘Aray’ is a Tagalog word that literally translates to ‘ouch’.

2. Salakot – This folk dance uses the native hat called ‘salakot’ in graceful and flirtatious moves. This involves throwing the hat on the floor and dancing around it.

3. Ragsaksakan – Ragsaksakan translates to the word “joy or merriment”. This dance is performed during a peace pact between two opposing tribes.

4. Banga – This folk dance originated from the people of Kalinga from the Mountain Province. This depicts the grace of the tribe who are also known as great warriors.

5. Idudu – A folk dance that shows the basic structure or the roles of a family from the Itneg or Tinggian people.

Visayas Folk Dances

6. Tinikling – This dance was formed due to the imitation of the tikling birds while they are walking between scattered twigs. Tinikling translates to ‘bamboo dance’ because they use bamboo as the main instrument of the dance.

7. Kuradang –  Also called as ‘Kuratsa’. This dance is done by only one couple at a time. This is what the Waray people like the most.

8. Subli – This folk dance makes it seem like the dancers are lame and crooked. This is because the word subli came from the two words ‘subsub’ or the falling of the head and ‘bali’ or broken.

9. Mananagat – This dance mimics the movement of women who are scooping water while on a boat using a bamboo shell and the movement of men who rows the boat using wooden paddles.

10. Maglalatik – This folk dance shows the fight between the Moros and the Christians. The fight is beacuse of the prize which is the latik from the coconut meat, which was very valuable back in the Spanish rule.

Mindanao Folk Dances

11. Singkil – A dance that originated from the Maranao people and depicts a story of a princess.

12. Moro-moro – This dance depicts the royal families’ many experiences including love and action. This also portrays different creatures like lions, dragons, and giants.

13. Kini-kini – The word Kini has the definition of Royal Walk. This is performed by Maranao women while using scarves.

14. Pangalay – A dance that is native to the Badjao people. This dance is focused on the upper body and its agility. It is usually performed at weddings but can also be performed at different social gatherings.

15. Asik – Usually performed by one lady that is dressed up with makeup, fine beads, and a long head scarf. This dance aims to win the favor of a Sultan.

For Visayas: Tinikling is considered the national folkdance with a pair of dancers hopping between two bamboo poles held just above the ground and stuck together in time to music. ... This dance is in fact a mimic movement of "tikling birds" hopping over trees, grass stems or over bamboo traps set by farmers.

30. how does the folk dance in visayas born​


Answer:

Rice farmers on the Visayan Islands usually set up bamboo traps to protect their fields, yet tikling birds dodged their traps. Locals imitated the birds' movement, and supposedly, that's how this dramatic traditional folk dance was born.
