1. summary of the boy who harnessed the wind ​


Answer:

"The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind" is a book by William Kamkwamba and Bryan Mealer that tells the true story of a young boy named William Kamkwamba who, with his ingenuity and determination, built a windmill in his village in Malawi in order to bring electricity and water to his community during a time of drought and famine.

Despite facing numerous obstacles, including lack of resources and support, William refused to give up on his dream of providing his community with a better life. He taught himself about engineering and electricity by reading books at a local library, scrounged for spare parts in junkyards, and eventually built a windmill from scratch using scrap metal, bicycle parts, and a tractor fan blade.

The windmill proved to be a success, providing electricity and water to the village and improving the lives of its inhabitants. William's achievement gained international attention and he went on to attend school in the United States and become a TED Fellow and a prominent advocate for sustainable development and education in Malawi.

Overall, "The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind" is a powerful and inspiring story of one boy's determination to make a difference in his community and the world, and serves as a reminder of the transformative power of education and innovation in the face of adversity.

In the book "The Boy Who Harnessed the Wind" by William Kamkwamba and Bryan Mealer, William undergoes a significant transformation over time.

11. These are installed in strategic locations to harness the mechanical energy from wind.​


Answer:

Tower, Rotor and Nacelle

Wind turbines have three main components.

The tower supports the nacelle and the rotor. It is generally made of metal and set in a concrete foundation anchored by long piles. Offshore turbines are anchored to the seabed in the same way.

The rotor converts the wind's kinetic energy (or movement) into mechanical energy . It consists of a hub and an impeller comprised of three, or sometimes two, blades. The propeller-like blades are manufactured from composite materials, such as polyester- or carbon-reinforced fiberglass, that are light, rigid and resilient.

The nacelle is located at the top of the tower behind the rotor. It houses the generator that uses the moving rotor's mechanical force to produce electricity. The electricity is transmitted to the ground via electric cables inside the tower.

The nacelle has an installed capacity (power generation capacity) of up to 5 megawatts.

19. Which of the following can be used to harness volcanic energy? A) electricity B) kinetic energy C) steam D) wind ​


C.) steam

Volcanic energy can be harnessed by utilizing the steam that is produced by the volcanic activity. This is done by drilling deep wells into the ground near the volcano, where the hot rocks and magma can heat water and turn it into steam. The steam can then be used to power turbines and generate electricity, which can be used to power homes and businesses.
