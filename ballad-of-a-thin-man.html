13. What are ballads? what are the types of Ballads?


Ballad is a musical genre that originated as an expressive folksong using a narrative text dealing typically about love and affection. The word was derived from the French term "ballade" which means a dancing song. In the contemporary era, ballad is referred to a love song in a rock or pop style. There are three types of Ballads:

1. Blues Ballads- this style is an amalgamation of Anglo-American and Afro-American styles inspired from the 19th century uprisings against the anti-heroes resisting authority. 

2. Pop Standard and Jazz Ballads- this style ballad has a single verse of 16 bars which is followed by a refrain/chorus part of 16 or 32 bars. The Man I Love by George Gershwin is an example of this type of ballad. 

3. Pop and Rock Ballads- this is an emotional love song while having an element of folk music. The Ballad of John and Yoko by the Beatles is one of the examples of Pop and Rock Ballads.

17. differentiate blues ballads and jazz ballads​


differentiate blues ballads and jazz ballads​

The main focus of jazz music is an ensemble's dynamics and improvisations, but blues music is usually focused on a solo guitarist/vocalist and the song's personal lyrical content. The majority of jazz songs are instrumental, whereas blues songs always have lyrics.

Blues music predates jazz and can be considered a subgenre of the latter. Jazz, on the other hand, would not be labeled blues music in and of itself.

What's the difference between jazz and blues?

Guitar, bass, piano, harmonica, percussion, saxophone, vocals, trumpet, trombone, and fiddle are all used in blues. Guitar, piano, bass, saxophone, trumpet, clarinet, drums, and tuba are all used in jazz. Jazz is almost always instrumental, whereas Blues is almost always lyrical.

Answer:

Blue Ballads is used to refer to a specific form of popular music which fused Anglo-American and Afro-American styles from the late 19th century onwards. Early versions combined elements of the European influenced "native American ballad " with the forms of African American music. A jazz ballad, on the other hand, is a love song that is slow, sentimental, and intimate. Often in 4/4 and with a 32-bar form. Examples of classic jazz ballads include “It Might As Well Be Spring,” “My Funny Valentine,” “Some Other Spring,” “The Man I Love,” “In a Sentimental Mood,” “Misty,” and “’Round Midnight.” In popular music, the term ballad has been frequently used to refer to Beatles' songs such as “Yesterday” and “Norwegian Wood.”
