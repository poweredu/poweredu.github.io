6. Resources need to be managed aboard a ship or at sea


Answer:

. MRM is the use and co-ordination of all the skills, knowledge, experience, and resources available to the team to achieve the established goals of safety and efficiency of a voyage or any other safety critical task.

Answer:

Maritime resource management (MRM) or bridge resource management (BRM) is a set of human factors and soft skills training aimed at the maritime industry. The MRM training programme was launched in 1993 – at that time under the name bridge resource management – and aims at preventing accidents at sea caused by human error.

In MRM training it is assumed that there is a strong correlation between the attitudes and behaviours of the seafarers on board a ship and the cultures that these seafarers belong to. The most relevant cultures in this respect being the professional, national, and organizational cultures. Important target groups for MRM training are therefore, besides ships’ officers and crew, all people in shore organisations who have an influence on safety at sea and the work on board a ship.

30. A lighthouse 20m high with the base at sea level is sighted by a ship .If the angle of elevation from the ship to the top of the lighthouse is 12degree, what is the distance from the ship to the lighthouse?


Answer:

94 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?The distance from the ship to the lighthouse.What are given?20 meters - height of the lighthouse12 degrees - angle of elevationHow to solve?First we will assign variables to the important variables (points and lines) of the problem to make it easier to determine.

A = top of the lighthouse

B= location of the ship

C = base of the lighthouse

a = distance between the lighthouse and the ship

b= height of the lighthouse (20 m)

c = the diagonal sight from the ship to the top of the lighthouse

We also have 12 degrees as the measurement of angle ABC. That means the right angle (90 degrees) is angle ACB and angle BAC measures 78 degrees.

Observing from the data, we can form a right triangle. Because of that, we can use the trigonometric functions. With the known values (b and the angles), we will use the formula:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}[/tex], we will use the angle BAC which measures 78 degrees.

Solution:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{b}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{20m}\\a= tan 78 (20m)\\a= (4.7)(20m)\\a= 94 \ meters[/tex]

Answer:

Therefore, the distance from the ship to the lighthouse is 94 meters.
