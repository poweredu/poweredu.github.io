9. Make a reflective essay about the purpose of the physical education during the primitive time and at ancient countries ​


Answer:

       A social and educational process that is an inherent aspect of general upbringing Physical education is aimed to improve one's health and develop the body in a balanced manner. It enhances one's physical qualities and talents, aids in the development and refinement of motor skills required in daily life and work, and finally leads to physical perfection. Physical exercises (specially selected natural motions and sequences of movements, such as those used in gymnastics and track and field), diverse sports, and body hardening are the primary approaches of physical education (using healthful natural forces, such as sun, air, and water). The practice of healthy habits at work and in daily life, as well as the mastery of specialized knowledge and skills for exercising, hardening the body, and maintaining personal and public cleanliness, are also crucial. Class ideology is reflected in the aims, content, organization, and techniques of physical education, which are conditioned by socioeconomic structure.

       Every society has some form of physical education. It existed in prehistoric culture as physical exercise, sports, and competitions based on motions utilized in work, hunting, and combat; these competitions represented numerous ceremonies. Physical education in primitive culture aided in the development of physical strength, endurance, and drive in the young generation. Physical education became a governmental system for preparing the ruling class's young for civil and military duty in slaveholding societies such as the Far East, ancient Greece and Rome, and the nations of Transcaucasia and Central Asia. It was taught at home, as well as in public schools and the army; it was, for example, a major element of Spartan upbringing.

13. what connection can you draw between primitive time and the present time physical education?​


Answer & Explanation:

Physical education has evolved over time, from primitive times to the present day. In primitive times, physical activity was an integral part of daily life and was necessary for survival, such as hunting, gathering, and manual labor. In contrast, physical education in the present day has become more structured and focuses on the development of physical skills, fitness, and health.

However, despite the differences in approach and focus, the underlying goal of physical education remains the same, which is to improve physical health and wellness. Today's physical education programs incorporate modern technology and scientific understanding to provide a comprehensive approach to physical fitness and health. Additionally, physical education in the present day also aims to promote teamwork, sportsmanship, and physical literacy.

In conclusion, there is a clear connection between primitive time and present-day physical education, as the fundamental goal of improving physical health and wellness has remained unchanged throughout history.

25. how does education teach savage primitive countries to be more civilized?​


Education can play a significant role in teaching primitive or underdeveloped countries to be more civilized by providing individuals with knowledge and skills that promote social and economic development.

Education can provide individuals with the knowledge and skills needed to improve their economic status and standard of living. This can include learning basic literacy and numeracy skills, as well as more advanced skills in areas such as science, technology, and business.

Additionally, education can help individuals develop critical thinking skills, which are essential for making informed decisions and solving problems. Critical thinking skills can also help individuals to question and challenge traditional beliefs and practices that may be harmful or outdated.

Education can also promote socialization and the development of social and emotional skills, such as communication, empathy, and cooperation. These skills can promote peaceful and harmonious relationships within communities and help individuals to better understand and appreciate cultural differences.

Moreover, education can promote democratic values and civic engagement, which can help individuals to participate in the political process and advocate for positive social change.

Overall, education can help primitive or underdeveloped countries to be more civilized by providing individuals with the knowledge, skills, and values needed to promote social and economic development, critical thinking, socialization, and civic engagement.
