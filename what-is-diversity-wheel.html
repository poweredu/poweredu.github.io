6. What is Species Diversity? Explain and example What is the importance of Species Diversity? Explain Taglish


Explanation:

Species diversity refers to the number and variety of different species in a given area or ecosystem. It includes both the variety of species present and the abundance of each species.

For example, a forest with many different types of trees, plants, insects, mammals, and birds would have high species diversity, while a field with only one or two types of crops would have low species diversity.

The importance of species diversity lies in the fact that it helps to maintain the overall health and functioning of ecosystems. Each species plays a unique role in the ecosystem, and the more diverse an ecosystem is, the more resilient it is to environmental changes and disturbances.

For example, a diverse ecosystem may have species with different levels of tolerance to drought, disease, or pollution, so if one species is negatively impacted, others may be able to compensate and maintain the overall balance of the ecosystem. In addition, species diversity can provide important benefits to humans, such as food, medicine, and recreation.

12. What is rolling resistance in a wheel and axle? How does it affect the wheel and axle? What is the wheel radius and axle radius on a steering wheel in a normal-sized car? What about a wooden ship?


Answer:

Rolling resistance is the force that resists the motion of a wheel or an axle when it is rolling on a surface. It is caused by the deformation of the surface that the wheel or axle is rolling on and the internal friction in the materials of the wheel and axle.

Rolling resistance can affect the wheel and axle in several ways. It can cause a reduction in the speed and efficiency of the vehicle or machine that uses the wheel and axle. It can also cause heat buildup, which can lead to mechanical failure of the wheel or axle.

The wheel radius and axle radius on a steering wheel in a normal-sized car can vary depending on the make and model of the car. However, in general, the radius of the wheel is typically between 12-18 inches (30-45 cm), while the radius of the axle is usually less than 2 inches (5 cm).

In the case of a wooden ship, the wheel and axle are typically much larger in size than those in a car. The wheel, also known as the ship's helm, can have a radius of several feet or more, while the axle, which is connected to the helm and the rudder, can have a diameter of several inches or more, depending on the size of the ship. Rolling resistance can be a significant factor for a ship's helm and rudder, especially in heavy seas, and can affect the ship's maneuverability and stability.
