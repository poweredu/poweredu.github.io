7. Marie is four times as old as her daughter, Hannah. In three years, Marie will be three times as old as Hannah. How old are they now?


Answer:

I'll solve this one using Long Method

____________

Let's start by assigning variables to the ages of Marie and Hannah.

Let M be Marie's current age and H be Hannah's current age.

From the problem statement, we know that:

M = 4H (Marie is four times as old as her daughter, Hannah)

M + 3 = 3(H + 3) (In three years, Marie will be three times as old as Hannah)

We can simplify the second equation by distributing the 3:

M + 3 = 3H + 9

Now we can substitute the first equation into the second equation to get rid of M:

4H + 3 = 3H + 9

Solving for H, we get:

H = 6

Now we can use the first equation to find Marie's age:

M = 4H = 4(6) = 24

So Marie is 24 years old and Hannah is 6 years old.

9. D= light skin, d=dark skin. Hannah's mother is homozygous dark skin and Hannah's father is heterozygous light skin. What is the probability that Hannah has dark skin? ​


Answer:

50%

Explanation:

Punnett square

-----D-----d

d--Dd---dd

d--Dd----dd

There are two genotypes of Dd and two of dd. Dd is light skin, dd is dark skin. Therefore, the chance of having dark skin is 50%.

Mothers genotype: dd

Fathers genotype: Dd
