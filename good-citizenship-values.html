1. demonstrate good citizenship values​


Answer:

Good citizens are actively involved in their community and in the betterment of their fellow citizens. They take pride in where they live and strive to make it a better place. We all want to be known as a good citizen, and with a little thought and effort, anyone can be one.

FAITH IN GOD – Be God-fearing and live according to His will.

UNITY – Work together and share.

PATRIOTISM – Place the good of the country above one’s own.

WORK – Be diligent and earn an honest living. Do not engage in crime and corruption.

RESPECT FOR LIFE – Recognize the absolute value of human life and the human dignity of every person. Do not inflict harm on others.

TRUTH – Stand up for the truth and avoid intrigue and mudslinging.

JUSTICE – Give everyone their due. Do not oppress or take advantage of anyone.

FREEDOM – Assert your right to be able to do the right things.

LOVE – Look after the good and welfare of one another.

EQUALITY – Treat one another as brothers and sisters being children of one God and one nation.

PEACE – Live and work together in harmony. Avoid violence as a way of settling disputes.

PROMOTION OF THE COMMON GOOD – Put the welfare of the greater number of people over one’s own. Do not be greedy and selfish.

CONCERN FOR FAMILY AND FUTURE GENERATIONS – Look after the welfare of your family and future generations.

CONCERN FOR THE ENVIRONMENT – Keep your surroundings clean and conserve our natural resources.

ORDER – Respect the human rights of one another and comply with your duties and responsibilities.

13. HOW DO YOU DEMONSTRATE THE VALUES OF A GOOD CITIZENSHIP ?​


Answer:

Model the Personal Qualities of Good Citizens.

Explanation:

Honesty – tell the truth.

Integrity – be morally upright.

Responsibility – be accountable for yourself and your actions.

Respectfulness – treat others how you want to be treated.

Compassion – show fellowship with your compatriots who are down on their luck by volunteering and/or making donations to charities.

Kindness – be friendly.

Tolerance – be tolerant of other races and religions.

Courtesy – be considerate of others.

Self-Discipline – have self-control and cultivate the ability to follow through on what you say you’re going to do.

Moral Courage – stand up for what you consider to be wrong and defend those who cannot defend themselves.

Love of Justice – be fair and ask that others be so as well.

Imagine what your country would be like if all its citizens strived to achieve these personal qualities. Start by adopting them yourself.

21. reflect yourself the different classification of good citizenship values based on the 1987 philippine constitution​


An Essay on Good Citizenship Values

Being a good citizen, and more specifically, being a Filipino citizen, is a right we have because we were born in this country. And with that entitlement come a lot of responsibilities and duties, which made our uniqueness responsive with the aim of organizing not only our society but also ourselves. It is our responsibility as Filipinos to uphold harmony and peace in our interactions with the community and its members. We can't separate ourselves from the effects of being Filipino because we share the same culture and nation. Unity is essential if you wish to survive the grip of uncertainty, since no man is an island.

We each need to make the necessary changes in our own lives in order to create a society that is of high quality. As a Filipino, we should uphold strong civic principles that would enable our unique personalities to contribute to the quality of our country from the outset of the so-called transition. The fullness of our obligations as decent Filipino citizens and those of the government were expressed in the Preamble of the Philippine Constitution of 1987. To build and advance a good country governed by the law, we as Filipinos must provide an example of good citizenship by upholding the principles of truth, justice, freedom, love, and peace.

Being patriotic, or Pagka-Makabayan, comes in third. Love your country as well as yourself because it helps to define your identity away from your family. Serve your country as you would God and yourself. Respect the law because it contains information that will free you from unwelcome ignorance. Always pursue justice and equality because they promote fairness and happiness among the people and the country. And as a responsible citizen, use caution when choosing the person who will enforce our laws. They can decide on a deserving candidate since they control the government.

There are also these four important values that signify good citizenship. The first is "Pagkamaka-Diyos," which refers to our unwavering faith in the Almighty God. No matter what religion we practice, we all have a single, supreme Creator whom we sincerely worship and praise. He is our constant source of blessings, love, and protection. And all we need to do is fully rely on Him and honor the life he grants. We ought to respect and love God as we do our own bodies and lives. And in order to further foster self-respect, even in the most basic daily activities, we need to be well-organized. And as a result, the value of being a Maka-Diyos will be strengthened together with respect, faith, and order. Being God-fearing is the most critical value we should cultivate, since it underpins and supports all other positive values that are now in existence.

Maka - Tao is the second value in the case. We should put as much effort into loving others as we do into loving ourselves. Your treatment of and interactions with other people are a reflection of your level of self-love. Fundamentally, you can't love another person until you love yourself.

Being Pagkamaka-Kalikasan comes last. God's inherent endowments should be nurtured because they meet all of our requirements. Nature is a gift from God that we must cherish and preserve, from the luxuries we had to the land we lived on.

From the before mentioned ideals, it follows that good citizenship is essential because it not only benefits the person but also society as a whole.
