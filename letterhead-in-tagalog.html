10. The header is also known as the _____. a. masthead b. letterhead c. headline d. motorhead


The header is also known as the: A. MASTERHEAD.

EXPLANATION:

The masthead is a concept that predates the creation of web pages. Basically, a masthead is a graphic image often found at the top of a printed or electronic page. Mastheads can include a number of different elements designed to grab attention and provide a page with a level of visual appeal. Most designers for mastheads also make an effort to create a final design that is easy for viewers to remember and easy to associate with publications.

The masthead is often considered a very important component in determining the appearance of the front page of a website. Several different elements can be combined to create visually appealing graphics that will entice visitors to the site to stay and explore the content in more detail. For this purpose, a header can contain key elements such as an easily recognizable company logo, a text title in an eye-catching font and color, or some kind of graphic image that helps tell the viewer something important about the purpose and function of the website.

When the overall design of the masthead is effective, website visitors are more likely to continue scanning the web page and hopefully find additional elements of interest. Many online retail businesses understand that a properly designed masthead can cause someone browsing the Internet to stop long enough to develop interest in the site and start browsing items that are offered for sale. For this reason, many online stores will spend a lot of time and energy creating a masthead that will be recognized and accepted by the buying public.

24. when is the right time to use the letterhead?​


Answer:

What is the right time to use the letterhead?

A letterhead, by definition, is a heading on the topmost sheet of your business paper. It carries your company name, address, contact details, and logo. It is meant to be used for all the documents and letters you create and send in your business.

When do we use letterhead?
