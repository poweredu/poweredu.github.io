5. Marvin, Albert, Raff, Jeff, and Ronald want to take a photo in which three or them are lined up in a row. How many different photos are possible pa help poâ€‹


Answer:

10

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming order isn't important (which should not be) then this problem is a concept of combination. There are 5 choices but they are only taken 3 for each photo. So, n = 5 and r = 3.

[tex]nCr = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}=\frac{5!}{3!(5-3)!} = \frac{5!}{3!2!}=10[/tex]

There are 10 possible different photos.
