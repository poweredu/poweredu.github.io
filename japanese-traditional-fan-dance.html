6. japanese traditional drama​


Answer:

Noh and kyogen - Dating back to the 15th century, Noh and kyogen theatre traditions are among the oldest continuous theatre traditions in the world. Both men and women were allowed to perform Kyogen until 1430.Kabuki - First performed by Okunis (Japanese dancer who is credited as being the founder of the Kabuki art form) in 1603. Kabuki has a combination of music, drama, dance, wild costumes, and intense choreography.Bunraku - Bunraku begins in the 16th century with puppets that were used in Japanese Theaters as early as the Noh Plays. The puppets were 3- to 4-foot-tall (0.91 to 1.52 m) and the dolls were manipulated by puppeteers in full view of the audience.Bugaku - Starting at the 7th century (beating the appearance of Noh plays and Bunrakus), is the traditional Japanese dance performed for select elites in the Imperial Court for over 1200 years. It has been known only by the nobility as it is only danced to and for the Imperial Court. Although it has been opened to the public after the World War 2 in 1959.
