9. How are neap tides and spring tides are formed?


Answer:

Spring tides occur twice each lunar month throughout the year, regardless of the season, and the phrase is derived from the idea of the tide "springing forth". When the sun and the moon are at a straight angle to one another, neap tides—which also occurs twice a month—occur.

Explanation:

When the earth, sun, and moon all lined up in a row, the greatest tides, known as spring tides, are created. Every two weeks, at a new moon or full moon, this occurs. When the earth, sun, and moon make a perfect angle, smaller tides are created. These tides are known as neap tides.

11. the type of tide that is higher than the average high tides and lower than the average low tides ​


Answer:

Spring tides have higher high tides and lower low tides whereas neap tides have lower high tides and higher low tides. Hence, the range (difference in water level between high and low tide) is much larger in a spring tide than in a low tide.

28. How a high tide and low tide occur?​


Answer:

High tides and low tides are caused by the moon. The moon's gravitational pull generates something called the tidal force. The tidal force causes Earth—and its water—to bulge out on the side closest to the moon and the side farthest from the moon.

Explanation:

Red tide is a phenomenon caused by algal blood during which algae become so numerous that they discolor coastal waters (hence the name "red tide"). The algal bloom may also deplete oxygen in the waters and/or release toxins that may cause illness in humans and other animals.
