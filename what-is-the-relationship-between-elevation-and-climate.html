Answer:

Usually, as elevation increases, the weather gets colder and the climate becomes harsher (more intense weathering: windier and colder). There is also less air as elevation increases. As elevation decreases on land that most live on, the climate gets warmer as well as more humid.

26. What happens to gravitational potential energy of an object as it is elevated, and what is the relationship between position of an object and its GPE?


Answer:

Since the gravitational potential energy of an object is directly proportional to its height above the zero position, a doubling of the height will result in a doubling of the gravitational potential energy. Gravitational potential energy increases as weight and height increase. Potential energy is energy that is stored in an object or substance. Gravitational potential energy is energy in an object held in a vertical position. The equation for gravitational potential energy is GPE = mgh, where m is the mass in kilograms, g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 on Earth), and h is the height above the ground in meters.
