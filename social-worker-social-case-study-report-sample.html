2. Example of social case study report?​


Answer:

A social case study report typically contains background information, objective data, analysis and recommendations. Typically, the report details the situation of an individual or group of people within a given context, such as a family, school or organization. It outlines the intervention strategy used to improve the social situation, as well as the outcomes of that strategy. The report also provides conclusions and recommendations for further action.

Writing a case study

There are two different approaches to case studies. This guide focuses on the problem oriented method. Always check with your lecturer to confirm if this is the type required.

A successful case study analyses a real life situation where existing problems need to be solved. It should relate the theory to a practical situation; for example, apply the ideas and knowledge discussed in the coursework to the practical situation at hand in the case study.

Identify the problems.Select the major problems in the case.Suggest solutions to these major problems.Recommend the best solution to be implemented.Detail how this solution should be implemented.There are usually eight sections in a case study:1. Synopsis/Executive SummaryOutline the purpose of the case study.Describe the field of research.Outline the issues and findings of the case study without the specific details.Identify the theory that will be used.Note any assumptions made (you may not have all the information you'd like so some assumptions may be necessary e.g.: "It has been assumed that…", "Assuming that it takes half an hour to read one document…").2. FindingsIdentify the problems found in the case by:analysing the problem, supporting your findings with facts given in the case, the relevant theory and course concepts.searching for the underlying problemsThis section is often divided into sub sections.3. DiscussionSummarise the major problem/s.Identify alternative solutions to this/these major problem/s.Briefly outline each alternative solution and evaluate its advantages and disadvantages.There is no need to refer to theory or coursework here.4. ConclusionSum up the main points from the findings and discussion.5. RecommendationsChoose which of the alternative solutions should be adopted.Briefly justify your choice explaining how it will solve the major problem/s.This should be written in a forceful style as this section is intended to be persuasive.Here integration of theory and coursework is appropriate.6. ImplementationExplain what should be done, by whom and by when.If appropriate include a rough estimate of costs (both financial and time).7. ReferencesMake sure all references are cited correctly.8. 6. as a social worker what principles will you apply based on the case? explain substantially with corresponding legal basis​


I do not have access to a specific case or scenario to which I can provide a detailed response. However, as a general guide, here are some principles and legal bases that social workers commonly apply in their practice:

Respect for the dignity and worth of the person: Social workers recognize and respect the inherent value and worth of every person, and strive to promote the well-being and self-determination of individuals, families, and communities. This principle is enshrined in the Code of Ethics of the National Association of Social Workers (NASW) and is also reflected in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and various national and international laws.

Confidentiality: Social workers respect the privacy and confidentiality of their clients, and only share information with others when it is necessary to protect the client or others from harm, or when required by law. Confidentiality is a fundamental principle in social work practice, and is also protected by various laws and regulations, such as the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) in the United States.

Social justice: Social workers recognize and address systemic and institutional inequalities and work to promote social justice and human rights. This principle is based on the understanding that social, economic, and political factors can have a significant impact on the well-being and opportunities of individuals and communities. The principle of social justice is also reflected in various national and international laws and policies, such as the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals.

Empowerment: Social workers support and empower their clients to make informed decisions and take action to improve their lives. This principle is based on the belief that individuals have the right to self-determination and that social workers can serve as advocates and allies to help them achieve their goals. Empowerment is also reflected in various laws and policies, such as the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and the Rehabilitation Act of 1973.

In summary, social workers apply various principles in their practice, such as respect for the dignity and worth of the person, confidentiality, social justice, and empowerment. These principles are based on ethical standards, legal requirements, and a commitment to promoting the well-being and self-determination of individuals, families, and communities.

20. how social media helped social workers discreming information during calameties based on tv​


Explanation:

Social media can be a useful tool for social workers to access and share information during emergencies or calamities. It allows them to stay up-to-date on developments and to connect with other professionals and organizations that can provide assistance. It can also be a way for social workers to disseminate important information to the public, such as emergency response plans or resources for those affected by the disaster.

One challenge that social workers may face when using social media during a disaster is the need to discern accurate information from misinformation or disinformation. It is important for social workers to critically evaluate the sources of information they are accessing and to verify information before sharing it. They can do this by checking multiple sources and cross-referencing information, as well as using reliable sources such as government agencies or reputable news organizations.

Overall, social media can be a valuable resource for social workers during emergencies and calamities, but it is important for them to use it responsibly and to take steps to ensure that they are accessing and sharing accurate and reliable information.
