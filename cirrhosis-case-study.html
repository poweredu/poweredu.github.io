23. The nurse is caring for a client admitted with cirrhosis of the liver. which laboratory results are consistent with the disease process? select all that apply.


Assuming these details:

Potassium 4.0 mEq/L (4.0 mmol/L) Ammonia 96 mg/dL (68.54 mmol/L) Prothrombin time 22 seconds Platelets 75,000 cells/mm3 (75 x 109/L) Amylase 250 units/L (4.18 µkat/L) Albumin 7.2 g/dL (72 g/L)

Answer is:
The client with cirrhosis has liver dysfunction and impaired coagulation and rising ammonia levels. The prothrombin time is prolonged (normal is 10 to 13.0 seconds), and the platelet count is low (normal is 150,000 to 350,000 cells/mm3). A normal ammonia level is 15 to 45 mg/dl (10.71 mmol/L- 32.13 mmol/L), and this client's level is elevated, placing the client at risk for hepatic encephalopathy. A client with cirrhosis typically has hypokalemia because of the diuretic therapy used to treat the fluid retention associated with the disease. Here, the potassium level is within normal limits (3.5 to 5.0 mEq/L or 3.5 to 5.0 mmol/L). In cirrhosis, the albumin level is also typically low (normal is 3.5 to 5.0 g/dl or 35-50 g/L) due to alterations in protein metabolism in the liver. Levels of amylase, a pancreatic enzyme, typically increase with pancreatitis, not cirrhosis (normal level is 27 to 151 units/L or 0.45 mkat/L to 2.52mkat/L).

Early symptoms and signs of cirrhosis include:

Loss of appetite.

Feeling weak or tired.

Nausea.

Fever.

Unexpected weight loss.

As liver function gets worse, other more commonly recognized symptoms of cirrhosis appear including:

Easy bruising and bleeding.

Yellow tint to your skin or the whites of your eyes (jaundice).

Itchy skin.

Swelling (edema) in your legs, feet and ankles.

Fluid buildup in your belly/abdomen (ascites).

Brownish or orange color to your urine.

Light-colored stools.

Confusion, difficulty thinking, memory loss, personality changes.

Blood in your stool.

Redness in the palms of your hands.

Spider-like blood vessels that surround small, red spots on your skin (telangiectasias).

In men: loss of sex drive, enlarged breasts (gynecomastia), shrunken testicles.

Cirrhosis is a condition in which the liver does not function properly due to long-term damage.

Jaundice a medical condition with yellowing of the skin or whites of the eyes, arising from excess of the pigment bilirubin and typically caused by obstruction of the bile duct, by liver disease, or by excessive breakdown of red blood cells.

Answer:

Appendicitis, cirrhosis of the liver, jaundice, heartburn, and hernia are all digestive disorders.

Explanation:

The following are some disorders involving the human digestive system:

Appendicitis is the inflammation of the appendix. The most common symptom of appendicitis includes pain in the lower-right portion of the abdomen which can be accompanied by vomiting and diarrhea with slight fever. Failure to remove the inflamed appendicitis through appendectomy may cause abscess or generalized peritonitis.

Cirrhosis of the liver occurs when the liver produces fibrous tissue in between regenerating cells as a response to the death of some of its cells. As a result, the liver becomes knobbly due to lumps and appears tawny or yellow-brownish in color. Some possible causes of liver cirrhosis include excessive intake of alcohol (leading to alcoholic cirrhosis), viral hepatitis (leading to postnecotic cirrhosis), obstruction in the bile duct (leading to secondary bilary cirrhosis), auto-immune diseases (leading to chronic aggressive hepatitis and primary bilary cirrhosis), chronic heart failure (leading to cardiac cirrhosis). People having liver cirrhosis can experience portal hypertension, ascites (accumulation of fluid in the peritoneal cavity), hepatic encephalopathy), and hepatoma. Liver cirrhosis cannot be cured but its progress can be stopped if the cause is eliminated.

Jaundice is the yellowing of the skin of the white portion of the eyes as a result of the excess bilirubin (bile pigment) in the blood. There are three types of jaundice, each with different treatments.

Obstructive jaundice occurs when the bile from the liver do not reach the intestines due to an obstruction in the bile ducts such as gallstones. Symptoms include dark urine and pale feces. Hepatocellular jaundice is caused by diseased liver cells - e.g. in hepatitis, when the liver is not able to utilize bilirubin so it accumulates in the blood. Haemolytic jaundice occurs when there is excessive destruction of red blood cells. The medical term for this condition is icterus.

Heartburn, also known as pyrosis, occurs when swallowed food is brought up again to the mouth (regurgitation). It is accompanied by pain behind the breastbone (sternum), from abdomen to the throat, and with the sensation of a bitter tasting fluid in the mouth.

Hernia is the protrusion of an organ or tissue out of a body cavity wherein it is normally located. The most common type of hernia is the hiatus hernia wherein the stomach protrudes into the chest cavity through the hole for the esophagus. Complications may include the impossible return of the organ into its normal location, swelling of the organ, and cut-off of blood supply to the organ. Hernias are usually treated surgically.
