16. recommendations bread mold experiment ​


Answer:

Here are some recommendations for a bread mold experiment:

1. Use fresh bread: It is best to use fresh bread for the experiment as it will have the highest chance of growing mold.

2. Label each slice: Label each slice of bread with the date and type of treatment to keep track of which slice has which treatment.

3. Use different treatments: To see how different conditions affect mold growth, use different treatments on different slices of bread. Some possible treatments include exposure to light, moisture, temperature, salt, vinegar, and sugar.

4. Keep slices in sealed bags: To prevent cross-contamination and ensure that the bread is only exposed to the treatment you want, keep each slice in a sealed bag.

5. Observe and record: Check on the bread slices regularly and record observations such as the color, texture, and amount of mold on each slice.

6. Use gloves: When handling the bread, use gloves to prevent any contamination from your hands.

7. Dispose of properly: Once the experiment is over, dispose of the bread slices and gloves properly to prevent the spread of mold.

8. Discuss results: After the experiment, discuss the results and what they indicate about mold growth and the conditions that affect it.

23. what are the sporangia of thebread mold rhizopus?​


Answer:

The black speckles that Rhizopus produces on bread are capsules, called sporangia, that the fungus produces through asexual reproduction. The fungus consists of a network of filaments, called hyphae, that penetrate the bread and absorb nutrients. Sporangia break open, and their haploid spores disseminate.
