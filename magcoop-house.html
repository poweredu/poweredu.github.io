11. Patrick can paint a house by himself in t+4 hours. Karen can paint the same house by herself in t+9 hours. If they work together, they can paint the house in t hours. How much time will it take for Patrick to paint the house by himself?


Answer:

It will take 10 hours for Patrick to paint the house alone.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

The job of painting the house (same) : 1

Patrick's time to finish the job: (t+4)Patrick's work rate: 1/(t+4)

Karen's time to finish the job: (t+9)Karen's work rate: 1(t+9)

Time to finish the painting job if working together: tWork rate when working together:  1/t

Equation:  Add the work rate of Patrick and Karen:

1/(t+4)  +  1/(t+9)  = 1/t

LCD (least common denominator):  (t+4)(t+9)(t)

Multiply each term by their LCD:

[1/(t+4)] (t+4)(t+9)(t) +  [1/(t+9)](t+4)(t+9)(t) =  (1/t)(t+4)(t+9)(t)  

(t² + 9t)  + (t² + 4t) = t² + 13t + 36

Move the equation the left side and equate to 0. Apply Additive Inverse Property:

t² + t² - t² + 9t + 4t - 13t - 36 = 0

2t² - t² + 13t - 13t - 36 = 0

t² - 36 = 0

Factor the difference of two squares product, t² - 36=0.

The factors are sum and difference of two terms.

(t + 6) (t - 6) = 0

t + 6 = 0

t = -6

t - 6 = 0

t = 6

Choose the positive value for time, t = 6 hours.

Substitiute:

Patrick's time to finish the painting job:  t+4 = (6) + 4 = 10 hoursKaren's time to finish the same job: t+9 = (6) + 9 = 15 hoursTime to finish the job if they work together: t = 6 hours

It will take 10 hours for Patrick to paint the house alone.

Work rate is an inverse proportion, the more people working on a job, the fewer hours it will be completed.  Fewer people working on the job, the more hours it will be completed.

Check:

(Patrick's work rate) + (Karen's work rate) = (Working together rate)

(1/10) + (1/15) = 1/6

LCD: 30

3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30

5/30 = 5/30

ΔV (Average Speed) = 2 km/hr  

ΔS (distance) = ? (in km)  

ΔT (time) = 6 hours

Applying the data to the formula:

[tex]\Delta{V} = \dfrac{\Delta{S}}{\Delta{T}}[/tex]

[tex]\Delta{S} = \Delta{V}*\Delta{T}[/tex]

[tex]\Delta{S} = 2\:\frac{km}{\diagup\!\!\!\!\!hr}*6\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!hr[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{\Delta{S} = 12\:km}}\Longleftarrow(distance)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Question :-

It took Ronald 6 hours to walk to Nilo's house at 2 kilometers per hour, how far is Nilo's house?

Answer :-

Nilo's house is 12 kilometers far.

[tex] \blue{ \overline{ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad}}[/tex]

As per the provided information in the given question, we have been given that the speed is 2 km/h. The time is 6 hours. We have been asked to find or calculate the distance.

To calculate the distance, we will use the formula below :-

[tex]\qquad\quad\bigstar \: \: \boxed{\sf{ \: \: \: Distance = Speed \times Time \: \: \:}}[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the above formula and solve for Distance :-

[tex]\sf:\implies{Distance = Speed \times Time }[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Distance = 2 \: km/h \times 6 \: hours }[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies\bold{Distance = 12 \: km}[/tex]

Therefore :-

Nilo's house is 12 kilometers far.

[tex] \\ \sf{}x(x + 8) \bigg ( \frac{3}{x} + \frac{3}{x + 8} = 1 \bigg)[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{} \frac{3 \big(x(x + 8) \big)}{x} + \frac{3 \big(x (x + 8) \big)}{x + 8} = x(x + 8)[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}3(x + 8) + 3x = x(x + 8)[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}3x + 24 + 3x = {x}^{2} + 8x[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}6x + 24 = {x}^{2} + 8x[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}6x + 24- {x}^{2} - 8x = 0[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{} - 2x + 24 - {x}^{2} = 0[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{} {x}^{2} + 2x - 24= 0[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}(x - 4)(x + 6) = 0[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}x - 4 = 0 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: x + 6 = 0[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}x = 4 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: x = - 6 \\ \\ [/tex]
