13. PHOTOS OF CAREGIVING TOOLS. EQUIPMENT and Paraphernalia â€‹


Answer:

1. Wheelchair: A wheelchair is a mobility aid that helps people with physical disabilities move around. It is typically used by people who cannot walk or have difficulty walking due to an injury, illness, or disability.

2. Walker: A walker is a device used to help people with limited mobility maintain balance and stability while walking. It consists of a frame that supports the user's weight and four legs with wheels or rubber tips at the bottom.

3. Transfer Board: A transfer board is a device used to help people with limited mobility transfer from one surface to another, such as from a bed to a wheelchair or from a wheelchair to a car seat. It consists of two flat boards connected by hinges at one end and handles at the other end for easy gripping and maneuvering.

4. Reacher: A reacher is an assistive device designed to help people with limited reach and mobility pick up objects from the floor or other hard-to-reach places without having to bend down or strain their back. It consists of an arm-like handle attached to a long pole with either a claw-like grabber at the end or suction cups for picking up items without having to touch them directly.

5. Bed Rail: Bed rails are assistive devices designed to help people get in and out of bed safely and securely without having to use their hands for support. They consist of two metal bars that attach securely onto the bed frame on either side of the mattress, providing support when getting in and out of bed as well as preventing falls during sleep.
