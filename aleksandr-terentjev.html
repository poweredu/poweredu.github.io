4. In the 1920s, Aleksandr I. Oparin fathered the notion that the origin of life has unfolded based on the physiochemical processes that occur on earth. Select one: a. False b. True


The Origin of Life is a book written by Alexander Ivanovich Oparin, this theory discusses the evolution of living organisms. In the 1920s, Aleksandr (Alexander) officially presented his theory about life that living organism was preceded by a gradual evolution of elements specifically nitrogen and carbon compounds. His influential theory explained that life on Earth was developed through the gradual evolution of carbon-based molecules.

The statement "In the 1920s, Aleksandr I. Oparin fathered the notion that the origin of life has unfolded based on the physicochemical processes that occur on earth." is TRUE.
