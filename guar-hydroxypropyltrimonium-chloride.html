The reaction yields 8.70 grams of silver chloride. sodium chloride (aq) + silver nitrate (aq) silver chloride (s) + sodium nitrate (aq) What is the theoretical yield of silver chloride ? _____ grams What is the percent yield of silver chloride ? _____ %


Answer:

theoretical yield = 10.9 g

percent yield = 79.7%

Explanation:

Writing the balanced chemical equation

NaCl (aq) + AgNO3 (aq) → Ag Cl (s) + NaNO3 (aq)

Calculating the theoretical yield

theoretical yield = 4.45 g NaCl × (1 mol NaCl / 58.443 g NaCl) × (1 mol Ag Cl / 1 mol NaCl) × (143.32 g Ag Cl / 1 mol Ag Cl)

theoretical yield = 10.913 g

theoretical yield = 10.9 g

Calculating the percent yield

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (8.70 g / 10.913 g) × 100

percent yield = 79.7%

8. calculate the percent composition of chloride in NH4Cl(Ammonium Chloride)​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of NH₄Cl.

molar mass = (14.01 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 4) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 53.492 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the percent composition of Cl in NH₄Cl.

[tex]\text{\%Cl} = \frac{\text{35.45 g/mol × 1}}{\text{53.492 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{\%Cl = 66.27\%}}[/tex]

ANSWER :

Sublimation

EXPLANATION :

Sublimation is the conversion between the solid and the gaseous phases of matter, with no intermediate liquid stage. For those of us interested in the water cycle, sublimation is most often used to describe the process of snow and ice changing into water vapor in the air without first melting into water.
