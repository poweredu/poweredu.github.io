10. Are beauty pageants a good idea. (Argumentative Essay) Introduction, Body, and Conclusion​


Introduction:

Beauty pageants are competitions in which participants, usually women, are judged based on their physical appearance and sometimes on their talent or personality. They have been around for decades and are popular in many countries around the world. Some people argue that beauty pageants are a fun and harmless way to celebrate beauty, while others believe that they are harmful and objectify women.

Body:

One argument in favor of beauty pageants is that they can boost confidence and provide a platform for young women to showcase their talents. Many pageant participants claim that the experience has helped them become more confident and outgoing, and has given them the opportunity to pursue their passions and goals.

However, there are also many arguments against beauty pageants. One major concern is that they objectify and sexualize women, reducing them to nothing more than their physical appearance. Pageants often require participants to wear revealing clothing and heavy makeup, which can be seen as promoting unhealthy and unrealistic beauty standards.

Another issue with beauty pageants is that they can be expensive and time-consuming, requiring participants to spend money on dresses, hair and makeup, and coaching. This can be a financial burden, especially for those who cannot afford it.

In addition, beauty pageants often have a narrow definition of beauty that excludes women of different races, sizes, and ages. This can have a negative impact on the self-esteem and confidence of those who do not fit the idealized beauty standards.

Conclusion:

Overall, while beauty pageants may have some positive effects on the confidence and goals of individual participants, they have a number of negative consequences that outweigh these benefits. Beauty pageants objectify and sexualize women, promote unhealthy and unrealistic beauty standards, and exclude those who do not fit a narrow definition of beauty. For these reasons, I believe that beauty pageants are not a good idea.

Every Little Girl Wants to Be a Princess, Right?

FIRST PART:

The Introductory Paragraph

Beauty pageants have become a staple in American culture. Winners of pageants such as Miss America are icons, representations of the ideal woman, and positive role models for young girls. So, society says. More recently, however, a new type of pageant has increased in popularity to the point of being considered a national phenomenon. These pageants are just as glitzy, and the competition is just as fierce; the only difference is that these contestants are the miniature model.

Every last statement in the introductory argument is the thesis statement , arguably the most important component of the intro. It should explain what the essay would be arguing about.

SECOND PART:

The Body/Supporting Paragraphs

They are child beauty queens. The world of child beauty pageants has become a source of fascination, as well as contention, in our society. Networks such as TLC and WE TV have produced hit reality shows featuring the munchkin-sized divas because it sells, but one cannot help but wonder, how can parading children about on stage like show ponies be a positive thing? The truth is it is not. Beauty pageants are not a healthy activity for children because they force young girls to act like little adults, exhibit age-inappropriate sexuality, and have negative body image and mental health problems later in their lives.

The Body and supporting paragraphs includes the evidence and support the claims that arguments about the thesis statement.

LAST PART:

The Conclusion and Transitional Phrases

Children are the future of society, and even those who do not have children should be concerned about the direction of the culture they have to live in. Just because child beauty pageants are socially acceptable does not mean they should be. Our culture needs to eliminate child beauty pageants, at least in their current form. ​

The whole last paragraph will be the conclusion, and the last statement in that paragraph will serve as the transitional phrase. The transitional phrase would unify all the ideas from the top paragraph, to the body, and the conclusion.
