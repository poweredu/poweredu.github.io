8. what is the difference between incomplete dominance and co-dominance?


DOMINANCE

Incomplete dominance occurs when a phenotype is generated by mixing two different genotypes, here neither the dominant genetype can mix with the recessive genotype and generate a new phenotype.

Co-dominance occurs when two unrelated alleles manage to influence the same trait.

Example of incomplete dominance:

A good example of incomplete dominance is the skin color of humans and the fur of dogs and cats.

Example of Co-dominance:

Co-dominance is more complicated to have examples, since most of the genotypes are genotypes linked to the production of non-perceivable intracellular proteins.

Additional information about the topic:

When talking about dominance, it is believed that there is always an allele that will dominate over the other, even in the case of heterozygosity, but it does not always happen that way and there are different types of dominance: complete, incomplete and codominance.

Complete and incomplete dominance:

Complete dominance: in this type of dominance, an allele will suppress the manifestation of another allele when in heterozygosis, being considered the simplest. Thus, the phenotype of the heterozygous individual is the same as the phenotype of a homozygous dominant individual. An example of this is albinism.

Incomplete dominance: the alleles will be expressed in heterozygosity and the phenotype will be intermediate, as there is none that is fully dominant. The phenotype will be different from homozygotes. The lion's mouth plant is an example.

14. explain the difference between incomplete dominance and complete dominance


Answer:

In complete dominance, only one allele in the genotype is seen in the phenotype. In codominance, both alleles in the genotype are seen in the phenotype. In incomplete dominance, a mixture of the alleles in the genotype is seen in the phenotype.

18. Occurs when the dominant allele is not completely dominant​


Answer:

Incomplete dominance is when a dominant allele, or form of a gene, does not completely mask the effects of a recessive allele, and the organism's resulting physical appearance shows a blending of both alleles. It is also called semi-dominance or partial dominance.
