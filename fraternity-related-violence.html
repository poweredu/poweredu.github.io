3. Illegal fraternity related violence meaning


Illegal fraternity-related violence means violence perpetrated by fraternities. This usually refers to hazing. As early as 1995 there has already been laws prohibiting hazing as this results in physical and emotional trauma to those subjected to it. But legal and especially illegal fraternities still conduct hazing as a way to initiate their new members. Below are some information on fraternity related violence.

RA 11053 or The Anti Hazing Act of 2018

Section 2 clearly defines what hazing is which is any act resulting in physical or psychological suffering or harm which is inflicted upon a recurit or a neophyte. This definition covers hazing done in schools and even in military academies. Section 3 specifically prohibits the acts of hazing.

Initiation is Allowed

Initiation is allowed - but not hazing. Any organization who would conduct initiations should inform the school heads in writing indicating the time and place of the initiation and the manner in which they would conduct the initiation. The schools are then obligated to monitor these imitation rites to ensure that no one is hurt or undergoes any psychological trauma.  

Waiver is not valid as it pertains to hazing

Most organization usually get away on their responsibilities through disclaimers and waivers. This does not hold true for hazing. Even if a waiver was signed and consented to by the recruit, this does not hold any legal weight and is considered as null and void as hazing results in violence.  

Penalties

The penalty of reclusion perpetua and a fine of two million pesos would be imposed on those who are principal participants in the conduct of hazing.

14. Claim of value essay about hazing and fraternity violence ​


Answer:

Certainly, here is an essay on the topic of hazing and fraternity violence:

Hazing and fraternity violence are serious issues that have plagued college campuses for years. Hazing is defined as any activity that involves the harassment, humiliation, or abuse of new members of a group, usually in order to initiate them into the group. Fraternity violence, on the other hand, refers to any violent behavior that occurs within or among fraternity groups. Both hazing and fraternity violence have been responsible for numerous injuries, deaths, and other negative consequences.

Hazing is often seen as a rite of passage for new members of a fraternity, but it can have serious consequences. Hazing activities can range from relatively harmless pranks to dangerous and violent rituals that can cause physical and emotional harm. In some cases, hazing has resulted in serious injury or death, as new members are forced to participate in dangerous activities or consume large amounts of alcohol.

Fraternity violence is also a serious issue on college campuses. Fraternity members are often involved in fights, assaults, and other violent behavior that can cause injury or death. Some fraternities have been linked to incidents of sexual assault and other forms of violence against women. In many cases, alcohol and drug use contribute to the problem, as fraternity members engage in reckless behavior while under the influence.

To address the problem of hazing and fraternity violence, colleges and universities have implemented a variety of measures. Many institutions have adopted strict anti-hazing policies that prohibit any form of hazing and impose severe penalties on those who engage in it. Some schools have also taken steps to increase awareness of the dangers of hazing and educate students about the risks involved.

Similarly, many colleges and universities have launched initiatives to prevent fraternity violence. These initiatives often involve training fraternity members on how to recognize and prevent violent behavior, as well as providing resources for victims of violence.

In conclusion, hazing and fraternity violence are serious issues that require attention and action. While there is no one-size-fits-all solution, colleges and universities can take steps to prevent these problems by enforcing strict policies, increasing awareness, and providing resources for victims. It is crucial that we work together to create a safe and inclusive campus environment for all students.
