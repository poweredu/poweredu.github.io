REAL-LIFE PROBLEM GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE

The real-life problem of a geometric sequence:

Assume you have the coronavirus and forgot to cover your mouth when two friends came to visit you in bed while you were sick. They leave, and the next day they are infected with the covid. Assume that each friend spreads the virus to two of their friends the following day using the same droplet. Determine the number of newly infected people on the eighth day.

Solution:

Assuming that this pattern continues and that each sick person infects two other friends, we can represent these events as follows:

Day (n):  No. of newly-infected people

Day 1: 2 Day 2: 4 = 2 × 2Day 3: 8 = 4 × 2  = 2 × 2²Day 4: 16 = 8 × 2 = 2 × 2³so on

The number of newly infected people after n days since you first infected your two friends is represented above

These instances can be expressed as a geometric sequence:

2, 4, 8, 16, ...

As a result, the general formula for a geometric sequence is as follows:

[tex]D_n=ar^{n-1}[/tex]

where:

a = the first term in the sequencer = the common ratio[tex]D_n[/tex] = the number of newly infected people on an nth day.

We will determine the number of newly infected people on the 8th day,

Be Discovered

a = 2r = 4/2 = 2

[tex]D_8[/tex] which can be calculated using the general formula [tex]D_n[/tex].

[tex]D_8=2(2)^{8-1}[/tex][tex]D_8=2(2)^{7}[/tex][tex]D_8=2(128)[/tex][tex]D_8=256[/tex]

Hence, there are 256 newly-infected people with coronavirus.

FURTHER EXPLANATION

to understand the real-life problem above, we will study the concept of geometric sequences.

What is a geometric sequence?

A sequence is a sequence of numbers that follows a pattern or rule. The rule of multiplying or dividing by a constant number (known as the common ratio) each time is called a geometric sequence.

Example 1:

3, 9, 27, 81, ...

In General, if the first term of the sequence is a and the common ratio is r, we could write a geometric sequence like this:

a, ar, ar², ar³, ...

A geometric sequence can be written as a rule:

nth term = ar^(n-1)

where:

a_n = nth terma = the first term of the sequencer = the common ration = [tex]\frac{a_2}{a_1} = \frac{a_3}{a_2} =\frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}}[/tex]
