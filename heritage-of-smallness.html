3. What kind of text is A Heritage of Smallness


What kind of text is A Heritage of Smallness

A Heritage of Smallness is an Hortatory Exposition kind of text. An Hortatory Exposition kind of text, is a text that persuade the readers that something must or must not be the case.

In the essay "A Heritage of Smallness" by Nick Joaquin, he gave emphasis to how the Filipino people can be so much satisfied with all that is little, all that is small and all that is just enough. A child who came from a poor family would probably be poor for the rest of his life. It would be a common situation that they will embrace the way of life of their parents. Instead of finding a way for them to have more money, they will be just satisfied or contented and find fault with whoever the they want to find fault with.

25. Due to the noble " A Heritage of Smallness" by Nick Joaqin, what is your reaction?​


"A Heritage of Smallness" by Nick Joaquin.

In the essay, Joaquin explores the concept of a cultural mindset that values smallness and celebrates the unassuming, rather than embracing ambition and greatness. He argues that this thinking has been ingrained within Philippine society and has hindered the country's growth and development.

Personally, I find Joaquin's analysis to be thought-provoking and insightful. It highlights the importance of examining cultural values and their impact on society, and reminds us of the need to challenge beliefs that may be limiting our potential. Overall, "A Heritage of Smallness" encourages readers to strive for greatness and embrace a growth mindset.
