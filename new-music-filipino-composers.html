The Traditional Music of Philippines

The compositions that have adapted concepts and techniques from twentieth-century art music in the West, as well as the new trends and musical styles in the entertainment industry, are commonly referred to as contemporary music in the Philippines. This short outline focuses solely on the works of art music composers.

Here are some of the famous Traditional Filipino Music Composers: Francisco Santiago is known as "The Father of Kundiman Art Song," as He was the first to create a number of kundiman songs. The National Artist for Music, Lucio D. San Pedro, was an award or title granted to a musician who has contributed greatly to the advancement of Philippine music, as well as our history and traditions. Mr. Ryan Cayabyab - He is recognized as a "Transition Composer" in the music industry as he moved from traditional to contemporary music styles, making a distinction between traditional and 21st century music.

CONCLUSION:  

The Traditional and Contemporary Filipino music must be preserved for future generations because they are evidence of our rich culture and traditions. The government's and cultural institutions' support is critical for the preservation of traditional music.

In the Philippines, a type of love song known as the Kundiman had existed since the early 19th century. But in the early 20th century Kundiman had developed into art song. The term Kundiman comes from the Tagalog phrase “kung hindi man” or “if it were not so”. Written in the Tagalog language, these folksongs were subtly patriotic but typically disguised as love songs. Filipinos, in their long struggle against an oppressive Spanish regime, saw it as a tool that would ultimately unite Filipino revolutionaries to wage war against the Spaniards in 1896 during the Spanish-American War.

Answer:

1.What are the key concepts/ideas of contemporary Philippine music (Traditional to New music?)

Traditional music

⇔ The traditional musics of the Philippines are usually used by woodwind brass, and percussion instruments, and have fewer lyrics, as the tones, dynamics, and melodies are more important than lyrics for their contemporary style. Composers in the Philippines once believed that the tone of the song speaks more than the words of the song or the lyrics of it.

New music

⇔ The new music in the Philippines keeps up with the latest trends and adapts music styles that are popular in the twentieth century until now in the industry of entertainment. Other new musics in the Philippines are usually influenced by the music of the people in the west.

2. How do you describe Kundiman? The melody and the dynamics of Kundiman is soft, gentle, and flows smoothly as the lyrics of the Tagalog language rhymes with the tone and harmony of it. The tempo of the music is calming and refreshing, as the song tells about the love of the man for the woman.

3. Who are the famous Filipino kundiman composers?​The famous composers of the Kundiman song are Francisco Santiago and Nicanor Abelardo, and they were really famous from the timeline of 1893 to 1934.
