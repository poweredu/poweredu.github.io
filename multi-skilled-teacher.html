17. give at least 2 suggestions on how to address the challenges of a being a teacher in multi grade class​


Answer:

1. Introduction

Education is the process of transforming a person into a better individual. It develops one’s personality, provides a physical and mental standard, and transforms people’s living status. Education allows every individual to change into a better life and a better future. Following the Republic Act 9155 (2001), education is free and compulsory for all Filipino children. Thus, the education department mandates the establishment of complete elementary schools in every barangay forming multigrade classes in some remote Barangays. To compensate for teachers’ doubling efforts in the Multigrade classes, they are given a special hardship allowance (D.O. 81, s. 2009). The monetary allowance compensates for the more effort of the multigrade teachers.

In the Philippines, some teachers’ problems in elementary schools, especially in rural and remote areas, are lack of training and insufficient resources and findings (Magno, 2014). Moreover, multigrade schools are situated in remote areas. Multigrade teachers then find ways to serve the students better. They even gave part of their salary to purchase learning materials for their students (Castigador, 2019). They are sharing their resources for the welfare of their learners. During the practice teaching years of elementary teachers, their mentors were trained to be ready in the actual world of teaching, yet this training does not focus on multigrade teaching (Cadosales, 2017). The teachers’ ability to deliver the lesson well is crucial to the students (Cadosales, 2011).

2. Teachers’ practices of multigrade teaching in rural places

Multigrade education is a viable alternative for training for developing and even developed countries (Mulryan-Kyne, 2007). This alternative can be an effective and also cost-effective means of giving quality education to the students. One qualified and dedicated teacher can help many pupils in the far-flung areas regardless of their ages and cultures. Multigrade education is a widespread practice as needed more in rural areas compared to the urban ones in the developing countries (Aksoy, 2008). This practice aims at providing the children the access to universal primary education. Pupils from rural areas also need education even if there are only a few to be incorporated in one grade level. The teacher being the central influencing factor in mediating teaching and learning (Ramrathan & Mzimela, 2016).

Multigrade teaching is brought out because of condition, demand, and the school population that the enrolment of a particular school could not meet the standard number of learners (Joubert, 2010). It is the teaching of different learners where the teacher is the role model in the class of various grade levels, which the learners are consist of two or more grades (Chau, Sibli, & Thanh, 2001).

Multigrade teaching can be experienced as challenging as the teacher needs to vary his/her instructional materials and activities to accommodate the needs of the children of different grade levels and ages (Quail & Smyth, 2014). Lower levels require a set of Instructional materials that is not also applicable to the other level.

Multigrade classes came out because of the needs, demands, and necessity of particular places (Little, 2001). Multigrade teaching was established because of the distance of the location of the sites and the small population.

Even if teaching a multigrade classroom is so challenging, there are still good things that came from it. There are always classroom strategies that are observed. One of them is reducing or capitalizing on the pupil’s heterogeneity (Hyry-Beihammer & Hascher, 2015). In a multigrade classroom, there are different kinds of pupils. Thus, the teacher can use the unique skills and talents as well as the advanced skills of other pupils in helping their other classmates. Some pupils were intellectually superior compared to others. Therefore, teachers allow them to be facilitators and little experts to their other classmates. To acknowledge pupils’ heterogeneity, practices like following a spiral curriculum, having working plans, and peer learning are relevant (Hyry-Beihammer & Hascher, 2015).

One technique in teaching a multigrade classroom is the learning environment, learning process, and learning outcome (LEPO) framework (Msimanga, 2020). In teaching multigrade, it is very important for teachers to prepare well the learning environment. They have to ensure that the there is proper time allotment of the learning process of the students. The students then are given assessment tasks that match their academic competence.
