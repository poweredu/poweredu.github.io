29. Directions: In a separate sheet, make a concept map (example below) of all other fields/disciplines where you think quantitative research can be applied. Explain why quantitative research is important to these fields. journalismanthropologyengineeringhistory​


Answer:

In Economics, data such as statistics, budgets, cost assessments, and cost estimates are frequently used in quantitative analysis. Data analysis is one of the quantitative tools used in economics. Data collection, tabulation, and manipulation.

In Business, it's all about numbers in quantitative research. It employs data and statistical analysis to illuminate key information about your company and market. This type of information, obtained through methods such as multiple-choice questionnaires, can assist you in gauging interest in your firm and its products.

In Education, the quantitative methodology is frequently used in educational research. In education, quantitative research gives numerical data that may be used to confirm or reject a theory, and administrators can readily share the numerical data with other schools and districts.

In Psychology, quantitative psychologists research and create methods for assessing human behavior and other characteristics. Their work entails statistical and mathematical modeling of psychological processes, study design, and data analysis.

In  Health, quantitative approaches' main strength is their ability to produce factual and dependable outcome data. The statistical record of the observed outcomes will provide objective results generalizable to larger populations after the effects of a certain medicine or treatment have been tested on a sample population.
