ppliance energy transformations.

1. Egg beater: The egg beater is a mechanical device that converts electrical energy from the power outlet into mechanical energy, which is then used to turn the beaters. The beaters then transfer the mechanical energy to the eggs, causing them to mix and be transformed into a different texture.

2. Radio: A radio transforms electrical energy into electromagnetic waves, which are then received by the radio's antenna. The electromagnetic waves are then converted back into electrical signals, which are amplified and used to drive a speaker, producing sound energy.

3. Lamp: A lamp transforms electrical energy into light energy and heat energy. The electrical energy is used to heat up a filament or gas in the lamp, causing it to emit visible light and heat. Some lamps may also convert electrical energy into ultraviolet light, which is then transformed into visible light by the lamp's coating or fluorescent material.

17. It takes 3 minutes to cook a hard-boiled egg at 100 degrees c in manila and 4.5 minutes in baguio where water boils at 92 degrees c. what is the activation energy for coagulation of egg albumin protein


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & t_1 = \text{3 min} \\ & t_2 = \text{4.5 min} \\ & T_1 = 100^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{373.15 K} \\ & T_2 = 92^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{365.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the activation energy.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{ln} \: \frac{k_1}{k_2} & = \left(\frac{E_{\text{a}}}{R}\right)\left(\frac{T_1 - T_2}{T_1T_2}\right) \\ \text{ln} \: \frac{t_2}{t_1} & = \left(\frac{E_{\text{a}}}{R}\right)\left(\frac{T_1 - T_2}{T_1T_2}\right) \\ \text{ln} \: \frac{\text{4.5 min}}{\text{3 min}} & = \left(\frac{E_{\text{a}}}{\text{8.3145 J/mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)\left[\frac{\text{373.15 K} - \text{365.15 K}}{\text{(373.15 K)(365.15 K)}}\right] \\ 0.40547 & = E_{\text{a}}(7.0615 \times 10^{-6} \: \text{mol/J}) \\ E_{\text{a}} & = \frac{0.40547}{7.0615 \times 10^{-6} \: \text{mol/J}} \\ & = \text{57420 J/mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{57.42 kJ/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the activation energy for coagulation of egg albumin protein is 57.42 kJ/mol.
