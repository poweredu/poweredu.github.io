As a curricularist, I would start by examining why the restraining forces are present in the first place. I would then look for ways to reduce those forces while still allowing the driving forces to remain intact. This could include a variety of measures, such as increasing flexibility within the curriculum, allowing for more student choice, or providing more resources and support to teachers. Once these changes have been made, I would then assess the impact they have had on the restraining forces, and adjust the driving forces accordingly, if needed.

24. according to lewin, change will be better if the restraining forces shall be decreased, rather than increasing the driving force. as a curricularist, how would you do this?​


Answer:

According to the change management theory of Kurt Lewin, change is more likely to be successful if the restraining forces are decreased rather than increasing the driving forces. Restraining forces are the factors that resist or hinder change, such as fear of the unknown, lack of resources, and resistance from stakeholders. Driving forces, on the other hand, are the factors that drive or push for change, such as a need for improvement or a desire for innovation.
