6. grave of the fireflies what does the movie would like to tell us regarding the human nature, human rights, the value of the human in society​


Answer:

"Grave of the Fireflies" is a 1988 Japanese animated war film directed by Isao Takahata and animated by Studio Ghibli. The movie is based on the semi-autobiographical novel of the same name by Akiyuki Nosaka. The film tells the story of two children, Seita and Setsuko, during the aftermath of the firebombing of Hiroshima in World War II. The movie would like to tell us several things regarding human nature, human rights, and the value of human in society. Some of these things include:

The cruelty and inhumanity of war: The movie depicts the devastating effects of war on civilians, particularly children, and shows how war can turn people into monsters, devoid of empathy and compassion for their fellow human beings.

The resilience and determination of the human spirit: Despite the unimaginable hardship and suffering they endure, Seita and Setsuko are shown to be strong and resilient. They cling to hope and love even in the face of overwhelming despair, which is a message of hope and persistence in the human condition

The importance of family and loved ones: The love between Seita and Setsuko is a central theme in the film, and it shows how family and loved ones can give people the strength to endure even the worst conditions.

The disregard for human lives: The bombing of Hiroshima, the movie portray, show how little regard the authorities had for human lives, especially those of civilians, and how the bombing reduced human lives to mere numbers and statistics, without giving any consideration to the value of individual human lives.

The consequences of neglecting human rights : It also highlights how war can lead to the violation of human rights and the disregard of the most basic needs of civilians, such as food, shelter, and safety.

The impact of the society: Seita and Setsuko's struggle with the society's and their family's lack of understanding, and their rejection due to lack of resources also reflect how society can be cruel and judgmental towards those who are struggling.

In general, "Grave of the Fireflies" is a powerful and moving film that brings attention to the human cost of war, the plight of civilians caught in the middle of it and the basic human rights and the value of human lives that are often neglected in times of conflict.

Lampyridae are a family of insects in the beetle order Coleoptera, with more than 2,000 described species, many of which are light-emitting. They are soft-bodied beetles commonly called fireflies, glowworms, or lightning bugs for their conspicuous production of light, mainly during twilight, to attract mates. Light production in the Lampyridae originated as an honest warning signal that the larvae were distasteful; this was co-opted in evolution as a mating signal in the adults.
