5. A patient is scheduled for a breast biopsy at an ambulatory day-surgery center. an advantage of performing surgery at an ambulatory center is a decreased need for:


Answer:

Laboratory tests and perioperative medications

Ambulatory surgery is usually less expensive and more convenient, generally involving fewer laboratory tests, fewer preoperative and postoperative medications, less psychologic stress, and less susceptibility to hospital-acquired infections. However, the nurse is still responsible for assessing, supporting, and teaching the patient undergoing surgery, regardless of where the surgery is performed.

24. lesson you learn from the story of Bopsy​


The story of Bopsy is a heartwarming tale that teaches us several valuable lessons. Bopsy was a young boy who was terminally ill and was granted his final wish by the Make-A-Wish Foundation to visit Disney World. During his visit, Bopsy met a Disney employee named Ron and shared his dream of becoming a Disney animator. Ron, who was also a Disney animator, took Bopsy under his wing and helped him create his own animation.

One of the lessons we can learn from this story is the power of kindness and compassion. Ron's act of kindness towards Bopsy and his willingness to help him achieve his dream touched the young boy's heart and gave him hope and joy during his final days. It reminds us that small acts of kindness can have a profound impact on someone's life, especially during difficult times.

Another lesson we can learn is the importance of pursuing our dreams and passions. Bopsy's dream of becoming a Disney animator may have seemed impossible, but with the help of Ron, he was able to create his own animation and experience a moment of pure joy and happiness. It teaches us that no dream is too big or impossible to achieve, and that we should never give up on our passions and aspirations.

Overall, the story of Bopsy is a beautiful reminder of the power of kindness, compassion, and pursuing our dreams. It encourages us to make a positive impact in someone's life, and to never give up on our hopes and dreams.
