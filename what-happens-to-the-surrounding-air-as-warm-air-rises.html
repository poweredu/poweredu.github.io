Answer:

When a warm air rises, the air becomes cooler.

The warmth of the air expands the air. Because each molecule uses more space for motion, the air expands and becomes less dense. Therefore, the air expands when heated. When an air becomes warm, its air rises, and when it rises it becomes cooler.
