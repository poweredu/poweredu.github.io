16. Enumerate businesses that are sole proprietorship, partnership and corporation.​


Answer:

The Sole Proprietorship - Nail salons, shops, and small store.

The sole proprietorship is the simplest business form under which one can operate a business. The sole proprietorship is not a legal entity. It simply refers to a natural person who owns the business and is personally responsible for its debts. A sole proprietorship can operate under the name of its owner or it can do business under a fictitious name, such as Nancy's Nail Salon. The fictitious name is simply a trade name--it does not create a legal entity separate from the sole proprietor owner.

The Partnership - Frontrow

A partnership is a business form created automatically when two or more persons engage in a business enterprise for profit. Consider the following language from the Uniform Partnership Act: "The association of two or more persons to carry on as co-owners of a business for profit forms a partnership, whether or not the persons intend to form a partnership." A partnership--in its various forms--offers its multiple owners flexibility and relative simplicity of organization and operation. In limited partnerships and limited liability partnerships, a partnership can even offer a degree of liability protection.

The Limited Liability Company (LLC)- Big companies

The LLC is often described as a hybrid business form. It combines the liability protection of a corporation with the tax treatment and ease of administration of a partnership. As the name suggests, it offers liability protection to its owners for company debts and liabilities.

Additional Knowledge:  

Advantages of a sole proprietorship

1. Easy set up

The process of forming a sole proprietorship in the Philippines is straightforward, particularly as compared to the procedures for forming a partnership or company.

 

2. More streamlined startup

individuals who form a sole proprietorship are not required to go through the lengthy and time-consuming procedure of drafting legal agreements because they will not be doing business with other partners.

 

There is no need for complicated corporate processes, long discussions with the board of directors, or detailed accounts for the company's shares. You simply launch your company right away and hit the ground running.

 

3. Nominal cost

Unlike other business structures that demand high fees and filings to run, sole proprietorships are less expensive to create and retain.

4. Direct control

As previously said, sole proprietorship owners have complete control of all decision making. Instead of sharing power with a company or a board of trustees, the owner is actually the corporation itself, which ensures he or she retains sole control of all business-related decisions. Having full ownership allows sole proprietorship owners to steer the company in whatever way they see fit.

 

5. Hiring flexibility

Sole proprietors will hire others to grow their businesses, resulting in tax advantages. Furthermore, spouses of sole proprietors will work for the corporation without having to declare themselves as employees.

27. compare and contrast corporation and sole proprietorship. ​


Answer:

Sole proprietorships are firms legally owned by only one person. Partnerships are firms legally owned by two or more people. Corporations are firms legally owned by stockholders who have purchased “shares” of the company in the hope that the value of their shares will increase over time and pay dividends.

In a corporation, stockholders are only liable to the extent of their investments to the corporation, no more, no less. In a sole proprietorship, the legal entity of the business and that of the owner is the same, though, they are treated separate for accounting and tax compliance.
