13. fe + h2S04 = fe2(SO4)3 + h2 =​


Good Day

Given:

                       Fe + H₂S0₄  ⇒ Fe₂(SO4)₃  + H₂

The type of reaction is single replacement in which Fe (iron) is more active that H (hydrogen) thus, it replaces the place of Hydrogen. Fe can only replace Hydrogen since they have the same charge. Fe cannot replace the place of SO₄ since they have different charge.

To Balance the equation:

                          2Fe + 3H₂S0₄  ⇒ Fe₂(SO4)₃  + 3H₂                                       ↓                          ↓                                Reactant              Product                                  Fe = 2                    Fe = 2                                  H = 6                     H = 6                                       S0₄ = 3                   S0₄ = 3

We balance the chemical equation since atoms are not destroyed in the process and the mass is conserved. The mass of the reactant is equal to the mass of the product which also means, the number of atoms of elements in both the product and reactant are equal.

15. How many grams of aluminum sulfate: Al2(SO4)3 , would be formed if 250 g of H2SO4 react with aluminum?


Answer:

290.8 grams of aluminium sulfate (Al2(SO4)3) will be produced.

Explanation:

Step 1: Data given

Mass of H2SO4 = 250 grams

Molar mass H2SO4 = 98.08 g/mol

Step 2: The balanced equation

2Al + 3H2SO4 → Al2(SO4)3 + 3H2

Step 3: Calculate moles H2SO4

Moles H2SO4 = mass H2SO4 / molar mass H2SO4

Moles H2SO4 = 250 grams / 98.08 g/mol

Moles H2SO5 = 2.55 moles

Step 4: Calculate moles Al2(SO4)3

For 2 moles Al we need 3 moles H2SO4 to produce 1 mol Al2(SO4)3 and 3 moles H2

For 2.55 moles H2SO4 we'll have 2.55/3 = 0.85 moles Al2(SO4)3

Step 5: Calculate mass Al2(SO4)3

Mass Al2(SO4)3 = moles Al2(SO4)3 * molar mass

Mass Al2(SO4)3 = 0.85 moles * 342.15 g/mol

Mass Al2(SO4)3 = 290.8 grams

290.8 grams of aluminium sulfate (Al2(SO4)3) will be produced.

23. Zn + H2SO4 --> ZnSO4 + H2 A. Combustion B. Single Displacement C. Double D. Decomposition


Zn + H2SO4 --> ZnSO4 + H2 is a (B) single displacement reaction.
.
In a single displacement reaction, an element reacts with a compound and takes the place of the less active element in the compound. For this reaction, zinc (Zn) reacts with sulfuric acid (H2SO4) and takes the place of hydrogen (H) in the compound, forming the products zinc sulfate (ZnSO4) and hydrogen (H2).

30. 3.) A student from section Kaaya-aya of Parang High School mixed 0.20 mol of Fe with 0.25 mol of H2SO4 to produce FeSO4 and H2 gas according to the following balanced equation: Fe + H2SO4 ---> FeSO4 + H2. A. Which is the limiting reactant, and which is the excess reactant?B. What amount of excess reactant will remain unreacted at the end of the reaction?​


Answer:

A. To determine the limiting reactant, we need to compare the moles of Fe and H2SO4 used in the reaction.

Using the balanced equation: 1 mol Fe reacts with 1 mol H2SO4.

The student used 0.20 mol of Fe and 0.25 mol of H2SO4.

Since the stoichiometry of the reaction is 1:1, we can see that H2SO4 is the limiting reactant because there isn't enough of it to react with all of the Fe. Thus, Fe is the excess reactant.

B. To determine the amount of excess reactant that remains unreacted, we need to calculate how much Fe is left over.

From the balanced equation, we know that 1 mol Fe reacts with 1 mol H2SO4 to produce 1 mol H2. Therefore, since all the limiting reactant is consumed, we can assume that 0.20 mol of Fe reacted to produce 0.20 mol of H2.

To find the amount of excess Fe, we need to calculate how much Fe would react with 0.25 mol of H2SO4. Using the mole ratio, we get:

0.25 mol H2SO4 x (1 mol Fe/1 mol H2SO4) = 0.25 mol Fe needed

Since only 0.20 mol of Fe was used, it means that there is an excess of:

0.25 mol Fe needed - 0.20 mol Fe used = 0.05 mol Fe excess

Therefore, 0.05 mol of Fe will remain unreacted at the end of the reaction.
