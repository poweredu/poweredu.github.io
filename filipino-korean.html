18. What are the similarities of korean and filipino?


Similarities between Korea and Philippines Removal of shoes: Korea, as well as Philippines, practice the removal of their shoes or footwear when entering their homes.  Courting and Marriage: Both Koreans and Filipinos give the utmost importance and respect in marriage. As much as possible, they avoid getting in a pre marital situation.  Family oriented: Both Philipines and Korea are family oriented. There's even proper roles given to the men and women of the household Traditional Costumes: Korea has hanbok, whereas Philippines have barong ang saya. These are both used on special occasions Courtesy or being courteous: Koreans show their respect by bowing and using proper pronouns in addressing the older generations. They observe hierarchy among themselves. Same as Koreans, Filipinos also use words such as po and opo to show their respect to other people Food: Koreans like mild using mild spices in their dishes, whereas Filipinos use strong ones. Nevertheless, the use of spices is observed in both communities.  

Differences between Korean and Filipino culture Drinking Culture: Drinking is commonly observed in the Philippines. In Korea, turning down a drink is equivalent to disrespect. Koreans also pour drinks for their companions.  Giving importance to pregnant women: Korean women tend to stay at home when pregnant whereas Filipinos still have to work in order to provide food for the family Paying the Bill: Koreans pay their bills in the counter. It is different from Filipinos who tend to pay on their table Dinner Invitation: Koreans rarely invite their friends on their homes. Filipinos, on the other hand, like inviting their friends in their home.
