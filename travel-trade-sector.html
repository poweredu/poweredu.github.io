10. A route where foreign vessels travel as they trade with the philippines and other countries in asia and its explanation


The Manila Galleons  were Spanish trading ships which for two and a half centuries linked the Philippines with Mexico across the Pacific Ocean, making one or two round-trip voyages per year between the ports of Acapulco and Manila, which were both part of New Spain. The name of the galleon changed to reflect the city that the ship sailed from.The term Manila Galleons is also used to refer to the trade route itself between Acapulco and Manila, which lasted from 1565 to 1815.The Manila Galleon trade route was inaugurated in 1565 after Augustinian friar and navigator Andrés de Urdaneta discovered the tornaviaje or return route from the Philippines to Mexico. The first successful round trips were made by Urdaneta and by Alonso de Arellano that year. The route lasted until 1815 when the Mexican War of Independence broke out. The Manila galleons sailed the Pacific for 250 years, bringing to the Americas cargoes of luxury goods such as spices and porcelain, in exchange for silver. The route also created a cultural exchange that shaped the identities and culture of the countries involved.

Answer:

Regarding the importance of MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions) and the travel trade sectors in the tourism and hospitality industry, especially during a crisis where people are limited to making gatherings and special events, there are a few points to consider:

1. MICE and travel trade sectors form a significant part of the tourism and hospitality industry, contributing significantly to economic growth and job creation. In many destinations, the sector accounts for a notable percentage of overall tourism revenue.

2. Due to their high revenue generation potential, MICE and travel trade sectors are often the focus of destination marketing strategies. They help attract large numbers of domestic and international visitors, leading to increased spending in local economies.

3. During a crisis, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, when people can't make gatherings and special events, the focus shifts to other areas of MICE and the travel trade sector, such as virtual meetings and events, online conferences, and webinars. These alternative formats have seen a rise in popularity in recent times, allowing businesses to continue interactions and discussions despite travel restrictions and social distancing norms.

4. The MICE and travel trade sectors have shown remarkable resilience during the COVID-19 crisis, with businesses adapting to the situation by adopting new technologies and innovative approaches. Employing virtual and hybrid formats and trying to offer solutions to the limitations set to the industry makes them significant assets in the effort of keeping the tourism and hospitality industry thriving.

In summary, the MICE and travel trade sectors form a crucial part of the tourism and hospitality industry and have shown great adaptability during difficult times like the COVID-19 pandemic. Their contribution towards economic growth and job creation can't be ignored and they keep creating a path for constant evolution that helps to consolidate the industry along the years.

30. The capital market is the sector of the financial market where short-term financial instruments issued by corporations and governments trade.


Answer:

Capital markets are where savings and investments are channeled between suppliers—people or institutions with capital to lend or invest—and those in need. Suppliers typically include banks and investors while those who seek capital are businesses, governments, and individuals.

Capital markets are composed of primary and secondary markets. The most common capital markets are the stock market and the bond market.

Capital markets seek to improve transactional efficiencies. These markets bring suppliers together with those seeking capital and provide a place where they can exchange securities.
