17. How would you apply Silence & Good Speech in Music?


Answer:

Imagine a slow, melancholic song that is mainly played on a piano. In the middle of the song, the piano stops playing and there is a moment of silence. Then, a single violin comes in and plays a haunting melody. This use of silence creates a sense of anticipation and draws attention to the violin when it comes in. The violin's melody then represents the "good speech" in this example, providing a contrast to the silence and creating an emotional impact on the listener.

Overall, the use of silence and good speech in music is a powerful tool for creating emotional depth and interest in a song.

24. PLEASE ANSWER. Identify the figure of speech used in the sentences. (All caps) 1. Men sell the wedding bells 2. Speech is silver, but silence is gold. 3. "Every day, every night, in every way, I am getting better and better."


Answer:

1. Metaphor
2. Simile
3. Repetition

Explanation:

1. Men sell the wedding bells - This is a metaphor. A metaphor is a figure of speech that compares two things without using the word "like" or "as." In this case, the speaker is comparing men to wedding bells, suggesting that men have some qualities or characteristics in common with wedding bells.

2. Speech is silver, but silence is gold. - This is a simile. A simile is a figure of speech that compares two things using the words "like" or "as." In this case, the speaker is saying that speech is like silver and silence is like gold, implying that both are valuable in different ways.

3. "Every day, every night, in every way, I am getting better and better." - This is an example of repetition. The phrase "every day, every night, in every way" is repeated for emphasis, and the overall structure of the sentence follows a pattern of repetition as well.
