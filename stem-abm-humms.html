9. In a survey of 300 Junior High School took Senior High School entrance tests the 3 trucks STEM, HUMMS, and GAS. It was found that 34% took STEM, 48% took HUMMS, 42% took for GAS, 18% took STEM and HUMMS, 15% took STEM and GAS, 12% took for HUMMS and GAS, and 6% took all the 3 trucks. a How many of them took exactly one test? b. How many of them did not take any of the test?​


Answer:

A. 52%
B. 15%

Step-by-step explanation:

a. The number of students who took exactly one test can be found by adding the percentages of those who took STEM only, HUMMS only, and GAS only.

STEM only: 34% - 18% (took both STEM and HUMMS) - 15% (took both STEM and GAS) + 6% (took all three) = 7%

HUMMS only: 48% - 18% (took both STEM and HUMMS) - 12% (took both HUMMS and GAS) + 6% (took all three) = 24%

GAS only: 42% - 15% (took both STEM and GAS) - 12% (took both HUMMS and GAS) + 6% (took all three) = 21%

Adding these percentages, we get 7% + 24% + 21% = 52%. Therefore, 52% of the students took exactly one test.

b. The number of students who did not take any of the tests can be found by subtracting the percentage of those who took at least one test from 100%.

100% - (34% + 48% + 42% - 18% - 15% - 12% + 6%) = 100% - 85% = 15%

Therefore, 15% of the students did not take any of the tests.
