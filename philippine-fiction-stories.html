2. Research a fiction and non fiction stories, written works or movies in the Philippines, and provide example in all the three main island which is the Luzon, Visayas, Mindanao. Provide three fiction and non-fiction stories or movies in every island Paki sagot plss


Answer:

Luzon:

Fiction:

1. "Noli Me Tangere" by Jose Rizal - a novel that exposes the corruption and social injustices in the Spanish colonial government during the 19th century.

2. "Smaller and Smaller Circles" by F.H. Batacan - a crime thriller that follows two Jesuit priests as they investigate a series of murders in the slums of Manila.

3. "Ang Larawan" by Nick Joaquin - a play that tells the story of two spinster sisters who refuse to sell their ancestral home and the painting of their father despite their financial struggles.

Non-fiction:

1. "The Philippine Revolution" by Apolinario Mabini - a collection of essays written by the "Brains of the Revolution" that documents the events and struggles of the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule.

2. "The Philippine-American War" by Brian McAllister Linn - a historical account of the conflict between the Philippines and the United States after the former declared independence from Spain.

3. "Maynila: Sa Mga Kuko ng Liwanag" by Edgardo M. Reyes - a novel that depicts the harsh realities of life in Manila's slums during the 1960s.

Visayas:

Fiction:

1. "Sugilanon ni Boccacio" by Jose Garcia Villa - a collection of short stories that showcase the author's unique writing style and experimental approach to literature.

2. "Ang mga Anino sa Lungsod" by William Saroyan - a play that explores the theme of loneliness and isolation in a city setting.

3. "Hinilawod" - an epic poem from the Panay island that tells the story of the hero Labaw Donggon and his battles against supernatural creatures.

Non-fiction:

1. "The Tausug: Violence and Law in a Philippine Moslem Society" by Thomas M. Kiefer - a study on the Tausug people and their culture, particularly their use of violence and law in resolving conflicts.

2. "The Philippine Islands, 1493-1898" by Emma Helen Blair - a comprehensive historical account of the Philippines during the Spanish colonial era.

3. "The Negros Revolution: A Documentary History" by Resil B. Mojares - a collection of primary sources that chronicle the events leading up to the Negros Revolution of 1898, which was one of the first uprisings against Spanish colonial rule.

Mindanao:

Fiction:

1. "The Woman Who Had Two Navels" by Nick Joaquin - a novel that explores the themes of identity, colonialism, and cultural heritage through the story of a woman who believes she has two navels.

2. "Datu Puti" by Amador T. Daguio - a play that tells the story of a Datu who must choose between his love for his daughter and his duty to his tribe.

3. "Talaandig Dreams" by Estrella A. Alfon - a collection of short stories that depict the lives and struggles of the Talaandig people, an indigenous group in Mindanao.

Non-fiction:

1. "Muslims in the Philippines" by Cesar Adib Majul - a historical and cultural study of the Muslim communities in Mindanao and their role in the country's history.

2. "The Lumad in Mindanao: Their Struggle for Cultural and Environmental Survival" by Karl M. Gaspar - a book that highlights the plight of the Lumad people, an indigenous group in Mindanao, and their struggle against displacement and environmental destruction.

3. "The Bangsamoro Struggle for Self-Determination" by Soliman M. Santos Jr. - a legal and political analysis of the Bangsamoro struggle for self-determination and autonomy in Mindanao.
