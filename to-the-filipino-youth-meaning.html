3. what is the meaning in fourth stanza to "To My Filipino Youth"


OVERVIEW

Jose Rizal composed the poem “To the Filipino Youth,” dedicated to the youth of the Philippines. He wanted the Filipino youth to use their capabilities, talents, and skills to stand out not only for their own praise and success but also for the praise and success of their own motherland, the Philippines.

MEANING

Rizal challenges the youth, that in their pursuit of knowledge and wisdom they may humble the hand of Spain, whose proud chin did not look kindly upon the people whom they labeled as "Indios" and whom they treated with contempt.

Answer:

It is difficult to make a blanket statement about the attitudes of all Filipino youth, as people of different ages, backgrounds, and experiences may have varying perspectives. However, it is important to consider the factors that may influence the attitudes and behaviors of young people in the Philippines.

One factor that may contribute to a perception of apathy among some Filipino youth is the lack of opportunities and resources. For example, limited access to education and employment opportunities may leave some young people feeling disheartened and disconnected from the world around them.

Another factor to consider is the level of political and social engagement in the country. In some cases, young people may feel disillusioned with the political system or feel that their voices are not heard, leading to a sense of apathy.

On the other hand, it is also important to recognize that there are many Filipino youth who are actively engaged in their communities and are working towards positive change. It is important not to stereotype or generalize the attitudes of all young people in the Philippines.

In conclusion, while it is not accurate to say that all Filipino youth are apathetic, it is important to consider the various factors that may influence the attitudes and behaviors of young people in the country.
