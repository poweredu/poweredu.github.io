2. 1. These two authors and researchers define communication as the concept of a. Aristotle and Claude Shannon b. Aristotle and Eugene White c. Claude Shannon and Warren Weaver d. Warren Weaver and Eugene White


Answer:

C

Explanation:

The Shannon–Weaver model of communication has been called the "mother of all models." Social Scientists use the term to refer to an integrated model of the concepts of information source, message, transmitter, signal, channel, noise, receiver, information destination, probability of error, encoding, decoding, information rate, channel capacity, etc. However, some consider the name to be misleading, asserting that the most significant ideas were developed by Shannon alone.

What are the similarities of Claude Debussy and Claude Monet?

The impressionist composers are often linked to painters from the same period and with a similar artistic agenda. The music of Debussy is full of symbolism and uses a variety of scale forms and compositional devices that in some ways get closer to improvisation than composers from lets say the earlier Germanic tradition . Debussy loved to find appropriate ways to develop his works which seemed to be on a path of allowing inspiration to be part of the developmental process and not just a regular means of development technique as found in the classical sonata form ( for example) . To compare and link Debussy with Monet is not an easy task as it requires some generalisation . Monet had a love for painting nature scenes along with portraits and general scenery . The standout feature as it occurs to me would be linked to how an impressionist sees and uses light and shade in their pictures . In traditional painting much like photography , an artist would typically depict how light falls on a subject to be as close to photgraphically accurate as possible . In impressionist art , the artist often exaggerates this effect , as by doing so they give more to the viewers imagination , and therby increasing the artistic effect when done tastefully . I am sure this is not the last point that could be made regarding this but its the one point that stands out for me personally . 26. how the entire communication process works based on Claude Shannon's model​


Answer:

The sender encodes the message and sends it to the receiver through a technological channel like telephone and telegraph. The sender converts the message into codes understandable to the machine. The message is sent in codes through a medium.

Shannon and Weaver's original model contains five elements: information source, transmitter, channel, receiver, and destination. The information source is where the information is stored. In order to send the information, the message is encoded into signals, so it can travel to its destination.

Communication models help identify and understand the components and relationship of the communication process being studied. Models represent new ideas and thought on various aspects of communication which helps us to plan for effective communication system.
