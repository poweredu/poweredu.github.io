Pansori

Pansori is a Korean genre of musical storytelling performed by a singer and a drummer. The term pansori is derived from the Korean words pan and sori, the latter of which means "sound." However, pan has multiple meanings, and scholars disagree on the intended meaning when the term is coined.

Khon

Khon is a dance drama genre from Thailand. Khon has been performed since the Ayutthaya Kingdom. It is traditionally performed solely in the royal court by men in masks accompanied by narrators and a traditional piphat ensemble. A variation of this genre with female performers is called khon phu ying.

Sanskrit Theatre

Sanskrit Theatre is part of Sanskrit literature, the classical literature of India, which flourished from about 1500 BC to about AD 1100. The earliest extant critical work on Sanskrit drama is attributed to Bharata, the legendary formulator of dramatic art in India.

Khayal Al-zill

The name Khayal al-zill was an intentionally metaphorical term whose meaning is best translated as "shadows of the imagination" or "shadows of fancy," with implications that reinforced the allegorical nature of the stories.
