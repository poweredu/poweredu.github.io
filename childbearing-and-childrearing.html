11. How does an increase or decrease in the average childbearing age group change the population? why do ""first world"" countries tend to have older childbearing women than ""third world"" countries?


Answer:

An increase in the average childbearing age group can lead to a decrease in the population growth rate. This is because as women have children later in life, there are fewer births overall and the rate of population growth slows.

There are several factors that can contribute to the trend of older childbearing women in "first world" countries compared to "third world" countries. One factor is access to education and employment opportunities for women. In many "first world" countries, women have more opportunities for education and employment, which can lead to them delaying childbearing in order to pursue their goals. Another factor is access to reproductive health care, including contraception and family planning services. In "first world" countries, these services are often more widely available, which can also contribute to the trend of older childbearing.
