18. Principles of gene linkage and example​


Answer:

Linkage explains why certain characteristics are frequently inherited together. For example, genes for hair color and eye color are linked, so certain hair and eye colors tend to be inherited together, such as blonde hair with blue eyes and brown hair with brown eyes.

Identify and explain the importance of linkages and partnership in societal community 4. Demonstrate how thinking process is influence by social network. ​


Answer:

1.) Neural networks are a type of machine learning algorithm that is inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. They are composed of many interconnected neurons, mathematical units that process information and can learn from data. Neural networks can learn complex patterns and relationships in data, making them useful for various applications, such as image and speech recognition, natural language processing, and predictive analytics.

2.) Social networks are online platforms that allow individuals and organizations to connect and communicate with each other. In the 21st century, social networks have become an integral part of our daily lives, providing a wide range of services and capabilities. They allow us to stay connected with friends and family, share content and information, and access various resources. In addition, they have become essential tools for businesses and organizations to promote their products and services and engage with customers.

3.) Linkages and partnerships are essential in a societal community because they help to build relationships and foster collaboration among individuals and organizations. By forming linkages and partnerships, community members can work together to achieve common goals and address shared challenges. These connections can also provide access to a broader range of resources and expertise, allowing for more effective problem-solving and decision-making. In addition, linkages and partnerships can help to build trust and foster a sense of community among members.

4.) Social networks can influence our thinking processes in several ways. First, they can provide access to a wide range of information and perspectives, expanding our knowledge and understanding of the world. Second, they can facilitate communication and collaboration, allowing us to exchange ideas and engage in discussion. Third, they can provide a sense of community and support, which can help to shape our beliefs and attitudes. Overall, social networks can play a significant role in shaping how we think and make decisions.

29. Fill in the missing data in the university hierarchy with the positions below. Note that all positions will be used in the Network Tree. HR Manager Director of Academic Services Accounting Manager Department Chair Director of External Linkages Faculty Payroll Manager HR Supervisor​


Hierarchical or vertical structure

The university hierarchy includes the University President at the top, followed by the Director of External Linkages and Director of Academic Services. The Department Chair, Faculty Payroll Manager, Accounting Manager, HR Manager, and HR Supervisor all report to their respective supervisors. The hierarchy allows for clear lines of authority and accountability within the university.

The hierarchy  of position in the UniversityUniversity PresidentDirector of External LinkagesDirector of Academic ServicesDepartment ChairFaculty Payroll ManagerAccounting ManagerHR ManagerHR SupervisorThis is a hierarchy because the positions are arranged in a hierarchical or vertical structure, where each position has a specific level of authority and responsibility. The University President is at the top of the hierarchy and has overall authority over the university. The Director of External Linkages and Director of Academic Services report directly to the University President and have authority over their respective areas. The Department Chair, Faculty Payroll Manager, Accounting Manager, HR Manager, and HR Supervisor are all in charge of specific functions within the university and report to their respective supervisors. This hierarchy allows for clear lines of authority and accountability within the university.
