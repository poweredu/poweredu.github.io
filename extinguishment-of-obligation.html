13. identify two principal modes of extinguishment of obligation and explain briefly ​


Answer:

The two principal modes of extinguishment of obligation are:

Payment or Performance: This mode of extinguishment of obligation occurs when the debtor performs the obligation by giving or doing what is owed to the creditor. Once the debtor has completed their obligation, the obligation is extinguished, and the creditor cannot demand any further performance or payment.

Novation: This mode of extinguishment of obligation occurs when a new obligation is substituted for an existing obligation. In this mode, the parties to the original obligation enter into a new agreement, which releases the debtor from the original obligation and creates a new obligation. Novation requires the consent of all parties involved and can be done either explicitly or implicitly.

Brief Explanation:

Payment or performance is the most common mode of extinguishment of obligation. It occurs when the debtor performs their obligation by paying the required amount or providing the required goods or services. For example, when a person pays off their loan or mortgage, they are fulfilling their obligation to the lender, and the obligation is extinguished.

Novation, on the other hand, is less common but is still used in certain situations. It involves the substitution of a new obligation for the existing one. For example, if a person owes money to a creditor but cannot pay it, they may enter into a new agreement with the creditor to perform a different obligation instead. Once the new agreement is fulfilled, the original obligation is extinguished.

24. I need your help, please answer advance. Thank you po! Explain and discuss the 9 modes of extinguishment of obligations One paragraph per question. Each paragraph for five sentences.


Answer:

The nine modes of extinguishment of obligations are legal, equitable, voluntary, involuntary, conventional, personal, real, urban and remarkable.

Legal extinguishment of obligations is the process by which the obligations of the obligor to satisfy the creditor can be extinguished through the operation of law alone. This can be either by fulfilling the obligations or when the law declares that the obligations have been extinguished.

Equitable extinguishment of obligations is the process through which creditors may be allowed to release the obligor from the duty of payment due to circumstances such as payment being impossible for the obligor to make.

Voluntary extinguishment of obligations occurs when the creditor and obligor freely and voluntarily enter into a contract that extinguishes a particular obligation. This is often used in debt concession and debt settlements.

Involuntary extinguishment of obligations occurs when due to some outside circumstances, such as the death of the obligor, the obligation that was existing between the two parties automatically ceases to exist.

Conventional extinguishment of obligations involves the creditor and obligor of a debt entering into an agreement to reduce the obligation owed by the former to the latter. This can either be for a total or partial payment, which often involves a form of security.

Personal extinguishment of obligations occurs when the satisfaction of the obligations is dependent upon performances or individual abilities that cannot be debt legally or equitably relieved of.

Real extinguishment of obligations is related to the sale, transfer or assignment of property or rights that have been politically constituted, so that they no longer constitute an obligation of the assignor or transferee.

Urban extinguishment of obligations is when an obligation is extinguished by virtue of a municipal ordinance.

Remarkable extinguishment of obligations is the extinction of certain obligations, due to extraordinary and surprising legal or equitable causes. This cannot be reversed, as the circumstances cannot be duplicated or reproduced.
