18. What are the factors affecting physical growth and development? What does the concept of "nature vs. nurture" mean?


FACTORS AFFECTING PHYSICAL GROWTH AND DEVELOPMENT

Children's growth and development are influenced by both internal and external environmental influences, some of which are beyond our control. Knowing what children require at each stage of their growth and development allows us to properly raise them.

Although the phrases growth and development are used interchangeably, they have distinct biological meanings. Growth refers to gradual changes in physical qualities such as height, weight, size, and so on, whereas development refers to qualitative modifications to growth that end in maturity. Growth and development are mutually beneficial, interconnected, and occur concurrently. Most babies, for example, can weigh 8 to 10 kilos and sit up by the time they are 8 months old.

THE CONCEPT OF NATURE VS. NURTURE

Nature and nurture both contribute to children's growth and development. Although nature's endowment is unchanging, nurture can also make a significant influence. Here are some of the factors that influence children's growth and development.

Heredity

Heredity is the genetic transmission of physical features from parents to children. It has an impact on many areas of physical appearance, including height, weight, body shape, eye color, hair texture, and even intelligence and ability.

Environment

The environment is important in the development of children because it represents the sum total of physical and psychological stimulation received by the child. The physical surrounds and geographical conditions of the child's home, as well as his social environment and interactions with family and friends, are some of the environmental elements impacting early childhood development.
