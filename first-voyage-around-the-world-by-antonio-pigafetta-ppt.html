4. Compare a first voyage around the world by magellan(Antonio Pigafetta and Magellan song by yoyoy villame.​


Explanation:

Magellan is known for leading the first voyage around the world, which took place between 1519 and 1522. He set out with five ships and 270 men, but only one ship and 18 men completed the journey. Along the way, they faced many challenges including harsh weather conditions, mutiny among the crew, attacks from native tribes, and a lack of provisions.

Although the tone and style of Pigafetta's diary and Villame's song are vastly different, both provide a glimpse into the enduring legacy of Magellan's journey and its impact on world history

10. Conclusion about "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" by Antonio Pigafetta.​


"The First Voyage Around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" by Antonio Pigafetta is an important historical account of Magellan's pioneering circumnavigation of the globe. Pigafetta, a member of the expedition, provides an eyewitness testimony of the journey, including the various challenges and hardships they encountered.

One of the main contributions of Pigafetta's account is his documentation of the different cultures and societies that the expedition encountered during their voyage. His descriptions of the customs, beliefs, and ways of life of the people they met, including the inhabitants of South America and the Philippines, are invaluable for understanding the history of these regions.

Additionally, Pigafetta's detailed descriptions of the geography, flora, and fauna of the regions they explored provides a valuable record of the natural world during the Age of Discovery. His writing also highlights the challenges and dangers that the expedition faced, such as extreme weather conditions and hostile encounters with local populations.

Overall, "The First Voyage Around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" is an important historical document that provides insights into the early history of global exploration and the ways in which different cultures interacted during this time period. Pigafetta's account remains a fascinating read for historians and general readers alike.

21. A short summary of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" by Antonio Pigafetta.​


Answer:

Antonio Pigafetta's account of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" is a journal of the famous Portuguese explorer's expedition to the Spice Islands. Pigafetta was one of the few crew members to survive the voyage and his journal provides a detailed account of the journey, including the crew's interactions with the native peoples they encountered.

The expedition left Spain in September 1519 and after a long journey, they arrived in Brazil in December. From there, they sailed down the coast of South America, discovering the Strait of Magellan and passing through it to reach the Pacific Ocean. They encountered various islands in the Pacific and made landfall in the Philippines, where Magellan was killed in a battle with local tribes.

Pigafetta describes the various cultures and customs of the people they met on their journey, including the native peoples of South America, the Pacific Islands, and the Philippines. He also documents the challenges the crew faced, including disease, hunger, and mutiny.

Despite the hardships, the expedition was a significant achievement in the history of exploration, as it was the first circumnavigation of the globe. Pigafetta's account provides a valuable historical record of the voyage and the cultures encountered along the way.

29. what your thoughts about written by Antonio pigafetta first voyage around the world?​


Answer:

On September 8, 1522, the crew of the Victoria cast anchor in the waters off of Seville, Spain, having just completed the first circumnavigation of the world. On board was Antonio Pigafetta, a young Italian nobleman who had joined the expedition three years before, and served as an assistant to Ferdinand Magellan en route to the Molucca Islands. Magellan was dead. The rest of the fleet was gone: the Santiago shipwrecked, the San Antonio overtaken, the Concepcion burned and the Trinidad abandoned. Of the 237 sailors who departed from Seville, eighteen returned on the Victoria. Pigafetta had managed to survive, along with his journal—notes that detailed the discovery of the western route to the Moluccas. And along the way, new land, new peoples: on the far side of the Pacific, the fleet had stumbled across the Marianas archipelago, and some three hundred leagues further west, the Philippines.
