Stem cells are special human cells that are able to develop into many different cell types. This can range from muscle cells to brain cells. In some cases, they can also fix damaged tissues.

In stem cell transplants, stem cells replace cells damaged by chemotherapy or disease or serve as a way for the donor's immune system to fight some types of cancer and blood-related diseases, such as leukemia, lymphoma, neuroblastoma and multiple myeloma. These transplants use adult stem cells or umbilical cord blood.

In some tissues, stem cells play an essential role in regeneration, as they can divide easily to replace dead cells. Scientists believe that knowing how stem cells work may lead to possible treatments for conditions, such as diabetes and heart disease.

12. duties and responsibilities of a STEM student.​


Explanation:

STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) students typically have a variety of duties and responsibilities that include:

Attending and actively participating in classes: This includes arriving on time, being prepared for class, and engaging in class discussions and activities.

Completing coursework and assignments: This includes reading and understanding course materials, completing homework assignments, and studying for exams.

Conducting research: STEM students often conduct research in their field of study, which may include designing and conducting experiments, collecting and analyzing data, and presenting findings in research papers or presentations.

Collaborating with classmates and professors: STEM students often work in teams and may be required to collaborate on projects or assignments with classmates and professors.

Staying current with developments in their field: STEM students should stay up-to-date with the latest research and developments in their field by reading relevant journals and attending conferences and workshops.

Participating in extracurricular activities: STEM students may participate in clubs, organizations, or competitions that are related to their field of study.

Developing problem-solving and critical thinking skills: STEM students should be able to analyze and solve problems, make logical conclusions, and think critically.

Developing technical and laboratory skills: STEM students should become proficient in the technical skills and laboratory techniques required in their field of study.

Communicating effectively: STEM students should be able to communicate their ideas and findings effectively, both verbally and in writing.

Time management and organization: STEM students should be able to manage their time and stay organized in order to balance academic and personal responsibilities.

Answer:

27 students

Step-by-step explanation:

n is 90% of 30

Given:

n = percentage

90% or 0.9 = rate

30 = base

Formula and Solution:

Percentage = Base x Rate

Percentage = 30 x 0.9

Percentage = 27

27 students are not interested in pursuing a career in STEM.
