2. By 7 pm all the party guests had arrived but by 8 pm a third of them had departed. By 9 pm a third of those remaining had also departed. By 10 pm the same happened again and a third of those remaining had also departed. Finally the party ended at 11 pm by which time further third of those remaining had also departed and by 11 pm only 16 guests still remained. How many guests were originally at the party at 7 pm?


Answer:

1296

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the number of guests at the start,

⇒ 7pm: x

⇒ 8pm: x/3

⇒ 9pm: (x/3)/3

⇒ 10pm: ( (x/3) / 3) / 3

⇒ 11pm: ( ( (x/3) / 3) / 3) / 3 = 16

By 11 pm, we can reverse engineer the equation

[tex]\implies \frac{\frac{\frac{\frac{x}{3}}{3}}{3}}{3} = 16[/tex]

[tex]\:[/tex]

First, we do it one by one

[tex] \frac{\frac{x}{3}}{3} = \frac{x}{3}\div 3 = \frac{x}{3}\times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{x}{9}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{\frac{x}{9}}{3}=\frac{x}{9}\div 3=\frac{x}{9}\times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{x}{27}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{\frac{x}{27}}{3}=\frac{x}{27}\div 3 = \frac{x}{27}\times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{x}{81}[/tex]

[tex] \:[/tex]

With the complex fraction simplified, we now have

[tex] \frac{x}{81} = 16[/tex]

[tex]\implies x=16(81) = \fbox{1296}[/tex]
