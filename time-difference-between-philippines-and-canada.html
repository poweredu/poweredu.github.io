5. Why is the philippines much hotter than canada because the Philippines


The Philippines is hotter than Canada because the Philippines is a tropical country while Canada is a cold country.

Detail of explanation:

Climate is the condition or character of the weather in a region or area. Unlike the weather which can change in a short time, climate occurs over a long time and does not change easily.

The climatic conditions in a country are influenced by the geographical location of the country. Each climate gives its own weather characteristics to a country.

Based on its geographical location, there are 4 types of climates in the world:

1. The tropical climate lies between the lines 23.5°N-23.5°S

Tropical countries only have two seasons, namely the rainy season and summer. Tropical countries tend to have hot weather because they are close to the equator. Examples of countries with tropical climates are countries in Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

2. The subtropical climate is between 23.5°N-40°N and 23°S-40°S. Countries with subtropical climates have four seasons, namely summer, spring, winter, and autumn. Examples of countries with subtropical climates are Japan, China, South Australia, the United States, and Turkey.

3. The moderate or temperate climate is between 40°N-66.5°N and 40°S-66.5°S. Countries with a temperate climate, for example, Germany, England, Italy, and others.

4. The cold climate is between 66.5°N-90°N and 66.5°S-90°S. Countries with cold climates are very cold in winter and cool in summer. Countries with cold climates are near the north and south poles. Examples of countries with cold climates are Canada, Finland, and Denmark.

That's why the Philippines is noticeably hotter than Canada. Because the Philippines is a country in Southeast Asia that is located near the equator with a tropical climate. Climate with weather tends to be hotter than other climates. While Canada is a cold climate country where temperatures can reach minus degrees Celsius.
