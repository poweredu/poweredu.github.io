18. What is print and non-print materials? Give some examples.​


Anything that is printed on paper or another physical medium, such as books, newspapers, magazines, brochures, flyers, posters, and pamphlets, is considered a print material. The written word and images are used to convey information to the reader or viewer in these materials.

On the other hand, any materials that do not rely on printed text or images to convey information are considered non-print materials. Videos, films, television shows, audio recordings, podcasts, websites, blogs, and social media are all examples of digital or audiovisual media. In order to convey information to the audience, they make use of a variety of multimedia elements like sound, images, graphics, animation, and interactivity.

Books, textbooks, newspapers, and magazines are examples of print materials, while online news articles, YouTube videos, podcasts, and e-books are examples of non-print materials.

28. examples of disadvantage of non print materials​


Answer:

There are several disadvantages to using non-print materials, such as videos, images, or other multimedia formats, as opposed to traditional print materials like books or articles. Some of these disadvantages include:

Limited accessibility: Non-print materials may not be accessible to everyone, particularly those with disabilities or limited access to technology. For example, videos may not be accessible to people who are blind or have low vision, and interactive materials may not be accessible to people with motor impairments.

Lack of permanence: Non-print materials are often ephemeral, meaning they can be easily lost or deleted. This can make it difficult to refer back to or review the information at a later time, and can make it more difficult to preserve the material for future generations.

Technical difficulties: Non-print materials may require specialized equipment or software to access, which can be a barrier for some users. For example, videos may require a specific player or plugin to view, and interactive materials may require a certain device or operating system to use.

Distractions: Non-print materials can sometimes be more distracting than traditional print materials, as they often incorporate audio, video, or other sensory elements that can draw the reader's attention away from the content. This can make it more difficult to concentrate and understand the material.

Overall, while non-print materials can be a useful and engaging way to present information, they also come with their own set of challenges and limitations. It is important to consider these disadvantages when deciding whether to use non-print materials, and to consider how to make the material as accessible and user-friendly as possible.
