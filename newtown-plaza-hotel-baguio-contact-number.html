10. The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number of rooms at Hotel H. if the total number of rooms at both Hotels is 425, what is the number of rooms at Hotel G?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Define x:

Let the number of rooms in Hotel H be x

Hotel H = x

The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number at Hotel H:

Hotel G = 2x - 10

Given that the total number of rooms is 425:

x + (2x - 10) = 425

x + 2x - 10 = 425

3x - 10 = 425

3x = 435

x = 145

Find the number of rooms in Hotel G:

Hotel G = 2x - 10

Hotel G = 2(145) - 10

Hotel G = 280

Answer: There are 280 rooms in Hotel G.

13. The number of rooms at Hotel G is 10 less twice the number of rooms at Hotel H. if the total number of rooms at both Hotels is 425, what is the number of rooms at Hotel G?


Answer:

Since G is equal to 10 less than twice H, we get:

G = 2H - 10

Since the total number of rooms at G and H is 425, we get:

G + H = 425

H = 425 - G

Substituting H = 425-G into G = 2H - 10, we get:

G = 2(425-G) - 10

G = 850 - 2G - 10

3G = 840

G = 280

18. what are the three laws of Newtowns law of motion?​


Answer:

In first law (inertia), an object will not change its motion unless a force acts on it.

In the second law (acceleration), the force on an object is equal to its mass times its acceleration. In the third law (action, reaction) , when two objects interact, they apply forces to each other of equal magnitude and opposite direction.
