✏️CARDS==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] Joan gave Gigi 1/4 of her cards Gigi gives Larry 1/4 of the card got from Joan, Larry gave Justeen 1/4 of the cards he got from gigi. Justeen received 8 cards how many cards has Joan start with?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] Represent 'n' as the number of cards Joan held before giving fourth of it to Gigi. After giving, Gigi will now have a number of cards of:

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{4} \\ [/tex]

» Now Gigi gave Larry 1/4 of her cards. Thus Larry will now have a number of cards of:

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} \\ [/tex]

» Larry gave 1/4 of his to Justeen. Equal to that Justeen received 8 cards:

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = 8 \\ [/tex]

» Solve the following equation to find 'n' aka the number of cards before Joan gave to Gigi.

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = 8 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{16} \cdot \frac{1}{4} = 8 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{64} = 8 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf n \cdot \frac{1}{\cancel{64}} \cdot \cancel{64} = 8 \cdot 64 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf n = 512 \\ [/tex]

» Checking:

Joan had 512, after giving 1/4 to Gigi, Gigi receives 128 cards. Gigi then gave 1/4 of hers to Larry and Larry now have 32 cards. Larry gave 1/4 to Justeen, Justeen then now receives 8 cards.

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{Joan \: starts \: with \: 512 \: cards}}} [/tex]

21. Elaine and gigi can finish cleaning in 2 hours. If it takes elaine working alone 3 hours longer thain it takes gigi working alone, how many hours will each girl finish the work alone? (With SOLUTION)


Answer:

The answer is for Elaine it takes 6 hours and for Gigi 3 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

Work problem where

1/a + 1/b = 1/t

Where

a is the time taken by first entity

b is the time taken by the second entity

t is the total time the work can be done

Let

Elaine = a

Let Gigi = b

t is the total time which is 2 hours

it takes Elaine working alone 3 hours longer than it takes gigi working alone.

Elaine = Gigi + 3

a = b + 3

Substitute the values to 1/a + 1/b = 1/t

[tex]\frac{1}{b+3} +\frac{1}{ b} = \frac{1}{2} \\[/tex]

In order to not have b on the denominator : Multiply both sides by[tex]\frac{ (b+3)(b)(2)}{b+3} +\frac{ (b+3)(b)(2)}{ b} = \frac{ (b+3)(b)(2)}{2} \\[/tex]

Cancel same terms on the numerator and denominator

2b +(b+3)(2) = (b+3)(b)

Simplifying

[tex]2b + 2b + 6 = b^{2} +3b[/tex]

Combining both terms and transposing

[tex]b^{2} - b - 6 =0[/tex]

Factoring

(b -3 )(b +2) = 0

Equating zero

b = 3

b = -2

Disregarding the value b = -2 since it is negative

The value of b therefore is 3 substiture to

Gigi = b

Gigi = 3 hours

Elaine = Gigi + 3

Elaine = 3+3 = 6 hours

Therefore the answer is for Elaine it takes 6 hours and for Gigi 3 hours

Checking

1/a + 1/b = 1/t

1/6 +1/3 = 1/2
