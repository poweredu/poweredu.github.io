"Pre-Colonial Times" by Christine F. Godinez-Ortega

Christine Godinez-Ortega is a brilliant author, poet, educator, and journalist and is currently a professor at Mindanao State University-Iligan Institute of Technology. She is a founding member, permanent panelist, and director at the Iligan National Writers Workshop. She also works as a Literary Arts Coordinator at Central and Northern Mindanao. She is also a part of the National Commission for Culture and Arts as the Vice Head of the National Literary Arts Committee  We should be looking for her information on Philippine literature during pre-colonial times because she was an expert and has undergone research about the topic and thus provides relevant and reliable data.Godinez-Ortega relies on the expertise of archaeologists, ethnologists, and anthropologists for information on early Filipinos because these are the experts on understanding pre-colonial societies.Information about early Filipinos is recorded in the Spanish language because they are the ones who wrote information about the Philippines in their journals and diaries. Information written by native Filipinos was also burned by the Spaniards in an attempt to make them forget about it and embrace the culture of the Spaniards. Seminal means ‘the seed’. The word is used in the paragraph to explain what could be the reason or origin of something.The most seminal folk speech is ‘bugtong’ or riddles. It is called ‘tigmo’ in Cebuano, ‘bugtong’ in Tagalog, ‘patototdon’ in Bicol, and ‘paktakon’ in Ilongo.‘Tigmo’ is translated into 52 languages.Proverbs or aphorisms are important because they teach the common folks wisdom through simple sentences or phrases that are easily understood by the readers.‘Basahon’ from the root word ‘basa’ or read means literary pieces to be read.Folk songs are songs that were created among a particular group of people or ethnic groups that were popularized and passed down to generations through oral traditions. Some qualities of folksongs are, it was related to national culture, refer to personal and historical events, and it was existing already for several generations. Some examples of folk songs from the Philippines are ‘Magtanim ay ‘di biro’, ‘Paruparong Bukid’, and ‘Ang Pipit’.

Answer:

12.5% -> 150,000

14.0% -> 350,000

Step-by-step explanation:

assumptions:

let x be the money invested at 12.5%

let y be the money invested at 14%

equations:

eq1: x+y=500,000

eq2: 0.125x+0.14y=67,750

use substitution method:

x=500,000-y

0.125(500,000-y)+0.14y=67,750

62,500-0.125y+0.14y=67,750

0.015y=5,250

y=350,000

input answer to first equation:

x+350,000=500,000

x=150,000

[Article in Spanish]

Francisco B Ortega et al. Rev Esp Cardiol. 2005 Aug.

Explanation:

Several studies have demonstrated that physical fitness in childhood and adolescence is related to cardiovascular risk in adulthood. Current data on the physical fitness of Spanish adolescents are not available. Therefore, the aims of this study were: a) to assess the physical fitness of Spanish adolescents and establish reference values for use in health and educational settings as indicators of cardiovascular health, and b) to determine the percentage of Spanish adolescents below the minimum level of aerobic fitness needed to guarantee future cardiovascular health.
