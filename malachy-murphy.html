7. sino si gobernador heneral Frank Murphy?​


Answer:

William Francis Murphy (April 13, 1890 – July 19, 1949) was an American politician, lawyer and jurist from Michigan. He was a Democrat who was named to the Supreme Court of the United States in 1940 after a political career that included serving as United States Attorney General, 35th governor of Michigan, and the mayor of Detroit. Suppose that the amount of petrol in each of the cars, in the beginning, was M.

Now, suppose that they drove for X hours.

In that case, the amount of petrol used by one car in X hours will be = M*X /4

The amount of petrol used by the other car will be = M*X/5

Therefore, the petrol left in one car = M - M*X / 4

Petrol left in the other car = M - M*X / 5

=> X = 15 / 4 = 3 3/4 hours.

20. In your own words, explain elaborately the summary of the 14 principles given by Alexander and Murphy. (a. The knowledge base)


The 14 principles given by Alexander and Murphy form the foundation of their knowledge base for creating healthy, livable spaces. These principles are designed to guide architects and designers in creating spaces that are functional, efficient, and sustainable.

The first principle is "Regard for the existing fabric of the place," which emphasizes the importance of understanding and respecting the existing context of a space, including its history, culture, and natural features.The second principle is "Fit the context," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are in harmony with their surroundings and that respond to the specific needs and desires of the people who will use them.The third principle is "Integrate the landscape," which stresses the importance of designing spaces that are in harmony with the natural environment and that incorporate the natural beauty of the landscape.The fourth principle is "Create a sense of place," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are unique and that reflect the character and identity of the community.The fifth principle is "Provide for a variety of activities," which stresses the importance of creating spaces that are flexible and can accommodate a wide range of activities.The sixth principle is "Provide for diversity," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are inclusive and that cater to the needs of people from different backgrounds and with different abilities.The seventh principle is "Promote community," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that foster social interaction and community engagement.The eighth principle is "Promote a sense of identity," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that reflect the unique identity of a community and that promote a sense of pride and belonging.The ninth principle is "Provide for flexibility," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are adaptable and can be easily modified to suit changing needs and preferences.The tenth principle is "Provide for safety," which stresses the importance of creating spaces that are safe and secure for all users.The eleventh principle is "Provide for efficient movement," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are easy to navigate and that facilitate efficient movement.The twelfth principle is "Provide for accessibility," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are easily accessible to people with disabilities.The thirteenth principle is "Provide for maintenance," which stresses the importance of designing spaces that are easy to maintain and that will last for many years.The final principle is "Provide for sustainability," which encourages architects and designers to create spaces that are environmentally friendly and that promote sustainable living.

Overall, the 14 principles given by Alexander and Murphy provide a comprehensive framework for creating healthy, livable spaces that are functional, efficient, and sustainable. They encourage architects and designers to consider the needs of the community and the environment, and to create spaces that are unique, flexible, and inclusive.
