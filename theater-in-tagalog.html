5. what are the similarities of medivial theater, renaissance theater and baroque theater? ​


Answer:

Medieval Theater

Explanation:

Medieval theatre encompasses theatrical performance in the period between the fall of the Western Roman Empire in the 5th century and the beginning of the Renaissance in approximately the 15th century. Medieval theatre covers all drama produced in Europe over that thousand-year period and refers to a variety of genres, including liturgical drama, mystery plays, morality plays, farces and masques. Beginning with Hrotsvitha of Gandersheim in the 10th century, Medieval drama was for the most part very religious and moral in its themes, staging and traditions. The most famous examples of Medieval plays are the English cycle dramas, the York Mystery Plays, the Chester Mystery Plays, the Wakefield Mystery Plays and the N-Town Plays, as well as the morality play, Everyman. One of the first surviving secular plays in English is The Interlude of the Student and the Girl (c. 1300).

Nineteenth-century engraving of a performance from the Chester mystery play cycle.

Due to a lack of surviving records and texts, a low literacy rate of the general population, and the opposition of the clergy to some types of performance, there are few surviving sources on Medieval drama of the Early and High Medieval periods. However, by the late period, drama and theatre began to become more secularized and a larger number of records survive documenting plays and performances.


6. A classroom has 25 students, 12 are into sports, 10 are into theater and 4 are into both sports and theater. What is the probability of students who are into 1. sports? 2. theater? 3. sports or theater? 4. sports and theater? 5. not into sports or theater?​


Answer:

Probability = number of favorable outcomes / total number of possible outcomes

Probability of students who are into sports:

Number of students who are into sports = 12

Total number of students = 25

Probability of students who are into sports = 12/25 = 0.48 or 48%

Probability of students who are into theater:

Number of students who are into theater = 10

Total number of students = 25

Probability of students who are into theater = 10/25 = 0.4 or 40%

Probability of students who are into sports or theater:

Total number of students who are into sports or theater = 12 + 10 - 4 (since 4 students are into both sports and theater, we need to subtract them to avoid counting them twice)

Total number of students = 25

Probability of students who are into sports or theater = 18/25 = 0.72 or 72%

Probability of students who are into sports and theater:

Number of students who are into both sports and theater = 4

Total number of students = 25

Probability of students who are into sports and theater = 4/25 = 0.16 or 16%

Probability of students who are not into sports or theater:

Number of students who are not into sports or theater = 25 - (12 + 10 - 4) = 7

Total number of students = 25

Probability of students who are not into sports or theater = 7/25 = 0.28 or 28%

The Baroque (UK: /bəˈrɒk/, US: /bəˈroʊk/; French: [baʁɔk]) is a style of architecture, music, dance, painting, sculpture and other arts that flourished in Europe from the early 17th century until the 1740s. In the territories of the Spanish and Portuguese empires including the Iberian Peninsula it continued, together with new styles, until the first decade of the 1800s. It followed Renaissance art and Mannerism and preceded the Rococo (in the past often referred to as "late Baroque") and Neoclassical styles. It was encouraged by the Catholic Church as a means to counter the simplicity and austerity of Protestant architecture, art and music, though Lutheran Baroque art developed in parts of Europe as well.[1]

27. Japan theater and chinese theater and indonesian theater


Answer:

Japan Theater - Kabuki is a traditional Japanese form of theater with roots tracing back to the Edo Period. It is recognized as one of Japan's three major classical theaters along with noh and bunraku, and has been named as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Chinese Theater - Theatre of China has a long and complex history. Traditional Chinese theatre, often called Chinese opera, is musical in nature. Western forms like the spoken drama, western-style opera, and ballet did not arrive in China until the 20th century.

Indonesian Theater - Indonesian theatre is a type of art in the form of drama performances that are staged on a stage, with a distinct Indonesian nuance or background. In other words, theater is a form of visualisation of a drama that is staged on the stage and watched by the audience.
