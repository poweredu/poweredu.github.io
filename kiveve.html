Explanation:

Different materials have different properties. These properties are characteristics that distinguished one material from other materials. Examples of these are malleability, boiling point, solubility, and phase. A change in these properties is referred to as changes of matter. It can be a physical change or a chemical change. Physical change is a change in the form of material without changing the identity and chemical composition of the material. Examples of this are cutting of paper, slicing of bread, bending of wire, and phase change. Chemical change, on the other hand, is a kind of change wherein the composition, structure, or composition changes. There is a chemical reaction involved wherein atoms are rearranged and the bonds between atoms either are broken or formed. In a chemical change, a new substance is formed that is totally different from the original substance. Some of the indicators that a chemical change took place are the formation of precipitate or gas, production of odor, and change of temperature. Examples of chemical change are burning of paper, decomposition, digestion, and cooking.
