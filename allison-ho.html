3. Carla and Allison are sisters. They are on their way from school to home. Carla runs half the time and walks half the time. Allison runs half the distance and walks half the distance. If Carla and Allison walk at the same speed and run at the same speed, which one arrives home first?


If two sisters, Carla and Allison traveled at the same speed while running and walking from their school going to their home and Allison spent half of the distance running and the other half walking while Carla spent half of her time walking and the other running, Carla would arrive first.

Analyzing the problem:

We are asked to see who will arrive first, and this is a matter of time.Carla runs half the time and walks half the time while Allison runs half the distance and walks half the distance. Let us assume that the distance from their house to the school and vice versa is 3 kilometers, their running speed is 5m/s, and their walking speed is 2 m/s.

Solving for Carla's time:

d= 3 km

[tex]s_{walking}[/tex] = 2 m/s

[tex]s_{running}[/tex]= 5 m/s

We know that s= [tex]\frac{d}{t}[/tex]

Therefore, [tex]t_{Carla}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{1}{2} (\frac{3 km}{5m/s})[/tex] - [tex]\frac{1}{2} (\frac{3 km}{2m/s})[/tex] = 7.5 minutes

Solving for Allison's time:

[tex]t_{Allison}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{1.5 km}{5m/s}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{1.5 km}{2m/s}[/tex] = 17.5 minutes

Thus, Carla would arrive first since it will only take her 7.5 minutes, 10 minutes earlier, than Allison.

Question:

Camryn is stuck at the top of a Ferris wheel. Allison is standing 38 feet from the base of the wheel watching her. If the angle of elevation from Allison to Camryn is 73°, how far off the ground is Camryn?

a.118.2 ft.

b. 124.3 ft.

c.120.9 ft

d. 126.5 ft.​

Answer:

b. 124.3 ft.

Solution:

For this scenario, we will use the trigonometric ratio of Tangent,

let x = distance or height of Camryn from the ground

This is the Trigonometric Ratio Equation to solve the distance x,

[tex]\large {\sf \tan\theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}}\\\\\large {\sf tan73^\circ = \frac{x}{38}}\\\\\large {\sf x = 38 (tan73^\circ)}\\\\\large {\sf x = 124.3 \;ft}\\\\[/tex]

[tex]\therefore \;\large\boxed{\text{\sf The correct answer is letter b.}}[/tex]
