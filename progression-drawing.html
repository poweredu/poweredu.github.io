Progressive Tense is use to state that an action is continuing to happen.

When making present progressive tense, follow the format:

am/is/are + verb in -ing form

Example: am/is/are eating

When making past progressive tense, follow the format:

was/were + verb in -ing form

Example: was/were eating

20. How will I draw on my family, peers, and others to track my progress, gather advice and feedback and support my learning?​


Answer:

If you are looking to draw on your family, peers, and others for support, here are some practical ways you can do so:

Share your learning goals with them and ask for their input and advice on how to achieve them.Ask for feedback on your progress and for suggestions on areas where you can improve.Set up regular check-ins with your support network to update them on your progress and get their input.Ask them to share their own experiences and knowledge on topics related to your learning goals.Collaborate on learning projects or activities together, such as working on a group project or attending a workshop or course together.Attend events or join groups related to your learning goals with your support network, such as a book club or a language conversation group.Show appreciation for their support and celebrate milestones and achievements together.

Remember that building a supportive network takes time and effort, but the benefits of having a community to lean on can greatly enhance your learning experience. Be open, honest, and communicative with your support network, and always be willing to reciprocate and support them in return.

22. The _____tenses describe an action that was in progress but was then finished. They are present perfect progressive , past perfect progressive and future perfect progressive.​


Answer:

The past perfect progressive tenses describe an action that was in progress but was then finished.

Explanation:

SOURCE: grammarly.com/blog/past-perfect-continuous-tense/

Unlike the present perfect continuous, which indicates an action that began in the past and continued up to the present, the past perfect continuous is a verb tense that indicates something that began in the past, continued in the past, and also ended at a defined point in the past.
