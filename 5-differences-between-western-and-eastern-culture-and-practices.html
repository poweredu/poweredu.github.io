16. Give me 5 differences between Western and Eastern societies, culture, and individuals


Answer:

1. Language: Western societies tend to speak languages from the Indo-European language family, such as English, Spanish, French, and German, while Eastern societies tend to speak languages from the Sino-Tibetan language family, such as Mandarin, Cantonese, and Japanese.

2. Religion: Western societies tend to be predominantly Christian, while Eastern societies tend to be predominantly Buddhist, Hindu, and Muslim.

3. Values: Western societies tend to emphasize individualism, while Eastern societies tend to emphasize collectivism.

4. Gender Roles: Western societies tend to be more egalitarian in terms of gender roles, while Eastern societies tend to be more traditional and patriarchal.

5. Family Structure: Western societies tend to have nuclear families, while Eastern societies tend to have extended families.
