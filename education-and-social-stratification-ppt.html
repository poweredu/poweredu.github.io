9. 1. What is Social Stratification?2. Explain the different systems of stratification.3. Define social mobility.me​


Answer:

1. Social stratification means the differentiation of a given population into hierarchically superposed classes. Its basis and very essence consist in an unequal distribution of rights and privileges, duties and responsibilities, social values and privations, social power and influences among the members of a society.

2. Systems of stratification vary in their degree of vertical social mobility. The major systems of stratification are slavery, estate systems, caste systems, and class systems. Some Western European nations are not classless but still have much less economic inequality than class societies such as the United States.

3. Social mobility, movement of individuals, families, or groups through a system of social hierarchy or stratification. In modern societies, social mobility is typically measured by career and generational changes in the socioeconomic levels of occupations.

27. Define social stratification​


Answer:

A society's division of its members into groups according to socioeconomic characteristics such as wealth, income, race, education, ethnicity, gender, employment, social position, or derived power is known as social stratification.

Class, status, and power are the three independent variables that Weber introduced to create his theory of stratification hierarchy. Class refers to a person's economic standing in a society, which is based on birth and individual achievement. Social stratification is the division of a given population into hierarchically superposed classes. The existence of higher and lower socioeconomic strata serves as evidence of it.

30. 1. Which of the following defines social stratification? A. Social isolation of people from each other B. System in which everyone is equal but perform very different tasks C. System by which a person's place in society is defined by birth D. None of these 2. Which of the following best describes how social class is related to social stratification? A Each layer has many layers of social stratification B. Each layer of social stratification constitutes a social class C. The terms social stratification and social class mean the same thing D. Social stratification exists in caste systems, whereas social classes only exist in noncaste systems a. 3. What is the key distinction between a caste system and a class system? A. a marriage within a specific group B. occupational prestige C. educational opportunity D. social mobility psagot po pls ​


Answer:

1. D. None of these, because its basis consists in an unequal distribution of rights and privileges, duties and responsibilities, social values and privations, social power, and influences among the members of society.

2. B. Each layer of social stratification constitutes a social class because each layer is based on factors such as level of education, occupation, income, and wealth. Similarly, those within the same class commonly have the same types of jobs or similar levels of income.

3. D. Social mobility, because the social position in a caste system is decided by assignment rather than attainment, meaning there is little to no social mobility. Meanwhile, the class system can be more mobile since the class can be obtained through different factors and not only through birth or marriage.
