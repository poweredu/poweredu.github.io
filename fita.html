3. what type of propaganda is fita​


FITA (Fear, Intimidation, and Threats of Violence) is a type of propaganda that uses fear and threats of violence to further a political agenda. It typically involves playing on people's fears and insecurities for the purpose of spreading hatred and prejudice against a particular group or idea. FITA may involve exaggerating the effects of a policy, misrepresenting facts, or creating an unrealistic sense of urgency. It can also involve attacking individuals based on their beliefs, religion, race, gender, or nationality. In short, FITA is a form of manipulative communication designed to incite fear in order to influence public opinion and advance a certain political message.
