7. the language varieties or lects,that people speak often​


Answer:

The language varieties, or lects, that people speak often serve as the basis for judgment, and even exclusion, from certain social groups, professions, and business organizations. As you study language varieties, keep in mind that they are often based on judgments one group is making in regard to another.

In sociolinguistics, language variety—also called lect—is a general term for any distinctive form of a language or linguistic expression. Linguists commonly use language variety (or simply variety) as a cover term for any of the overlapping subcategories of a language, including dialect, register, jargon, and idiolect.

Types of LectsTypes of LectsIn addition to the distinctions discussed previously, different types of lects also echo the types of language varieties:Types of LectsIn addition to the distinctions discussed previously, different types of lects also echo the types of language varieties:Regional dialect: A variety spoken in a particular region.Sociolect: Also known as a social dialect, a variety of language (or register) used by a socioeconomic class, a profession, an age group, or any other social group.Ethnolect: A lect spoken by a specific ethnic group. For example, Ebonics, the vernacular spoken by some African-Americans, is a type of ethnolect, notes e2f, a language-translation firm.Idiolect: According to e2f, the language or languages spoken by each individual. For example, if you are multilingual and can speak in different registers and styles, your idiolect comprises several languages, each with multiple registers and styles.

STEP 1 - APPLICATION FORM LCC

International University offers Bachelor's and Master's level degree programs. Choose a degree program that you are interested in and fill out an application form.

STEP 2 - English proficiency testLCC

is an international university that provides high quality studies entirely in English. Thus, applicants are required to have good academic English skills. Therefore, we require all applicants to take an English proficiency test. You may take one of the tests at a certified test center in person or online.

We accept:

IELTS Academic

TOEFL iBT

PTE Academic

PTE General Test

CPE or CAE

DUOLINGO English test

As part of your application you also will be asked to write an additional LCC essay.

Step 3 - Interview and required documents

After successfully passing your English proficiency test, your Admissions Counselor will contact you regarding the interview time. You will also be asked to submit required documents to be considered for the chosen degree program.

REQUIREMENTS FOR BA

A picture of you (passport-size picture)

Passport copy - scanned first 2 pages where it has all info about you (passport number, and expiration date)

Valid and final Secondary Education (High school) Certificate* in original language

Official translations of your secondary education (high school) certificate and other documents

One recommendation letter from an organization in home country

Requirements for MA

A picture of you (passport-size picture)

Passport copy - scanned first 2 pages where it has all info about you (passport number, and expiration date)

Personal Statement or Motivational letter

CV that would reflect your relevant work experience and educational background

Two recommendation letters (from people familiar with your character, academic and professional background. Preferably, at least one of the references should come from a university you have attended)

One recommendation letter from an organization in home country

Undergraduate (BA) diploma/certificate* in original language

Official university transcripts* in original language

Official translations of your Undergraduate certificate and other documents

STEP 4 - WAIT FOR ADMISSIONS DECISION

When you complete all the steps above, the Admissions Committee will evaluate your application and academic credentials. Wait for the admissions decision to be made and sent to your email.
