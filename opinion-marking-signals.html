OPINION-MARKING SIGNALS

An opinion is a subjective assessment of a particular subject. It depends on the individual's understanding and how they interpret or perceive something. Certainly, opinions may be accepted or rejected, but what’s important is you were able to voice them.

Words or phrases used to express feelings, emotions, and attitudes toward something are known as opinion-marking signals.Prefer, think, feel, should, and best are some examples.Words or phrases used to express feelings, emotions, and attitudes toward something are known as opinion-marking signals. They are useful cues that come in the shape of words and phrases that help us recognize opinion statements. They typically appear at the start of sentences. They express one's viewpoint. While expressing our opinions, we must also demonstrate that we are listening to, comprehending, and appreciating the views of others. Advice: Always give reasons for your opinions. Instead of simply saying "I agree" or "I disagree," say "I agree because I believe that..." (Justify your choice)," etc.
