4. timeline of philippine literature​


Answer:

A TIMELINE OF PHILIPPINE LITERATURE

I. PRE-COLONIAL ( –BC TO 1564)

A. Characteristics

1. Based on oral traditions

2. Crude on ideology and phraseology

B. Literary Forms

1. Oral Literature

a. Riddles (bugtong)—battle of wits among participants

tigmo—Cebu paktakon—Ilonggo patotdon—Bicol

b. Proverbs (salawikain) —wise saying that contain a metaphor used to teach as a food for thought.

c. Tanaga—a mono-riming heptasyllabic quatrain expressing insights and lessons on life is “more emotionally charged than the terse proverb and thus has affinities with the folk lyric”.

2. Folk Songs—a form of fold lyric which expresses the hopes and aspirations, the people’s lifestyles as well as their loves. These are often repetitive and sonorous, didactic and naïve.

a. Hele or oyayi—lullaby

b. Ambahan (Mangyan) —7-syllable per line oen that are about human relationships and social entertainment.

c. Kalusan (Ivatan) —work songs that depict the livelihood of the people

d. Tagay (Cebuano and Waray) —drinking song

e. Kanogan (Cebuano)—song of lamentation for the dead

3. Folk Tales

a. Myths—explain how the world was created, how certain animals possess certain characteristics, why some places have waterfalls, volcanoes, mountains, flora or fauna.

b. Legends—explain the origin of things

c. Fables—used an animal characters and allegory

d. Fantastic Stories—deal with underworld characters such as “tiyanak” “aswang” “kapre” and others.

13. Learning Tasks 4: What are the Philippine Contemporary Art Forms? Define each in your own words based on the timeline presented. PHILIPPINE ART PERIOD TIMELINE Pre-Colonial Art Period Spanish Colonial Art Period Post War Colonial Art Period American Colonial Art Period Contemporary Art Period ​


Answer:

There are five Philippine contemporary art forms: Pre-Colonial, Spanish Colonial, Post War Colonial, American Colonial, and Contemporary. Each art form has its own unique features and characteristics.

Pre-Colonial art is the earliest form of Philippine art. It is characterized by simple designs and geometric shapes. The most common pre-colonial art forms are pottery and weaving.

Spanish Colonial art is more ornate and elaborate than pre-colonial art. It is often characterized by religious imagery and themes. Common Spanish Colonial art forms include painting, sculpture, and architecture.

Post War Colonial art emerged after the Philippines gained independence from Spain in 1898. This art form is a mix of pre-colonial, Spanish colonial, and American influences. The most common post war colonial art forms are painting and sculpture.

American Colonial art emerged after the United States acquired the Philippines from Spain in 1898. This art form is characterized by realistic depictions of everyday life. Common American Colonial art forms include painting, photography, and architecture.

Contemporary art is the most recent form of Philippine art. It is characterized by abstract or experimental designs. The most common contemporary art forms are installations, performance arts, and digital arts.PA BRAINLY THANKS

Detail Explanation

Art analysis can be defined as an observation of someone's work in the form of a picture or painting. In carrying out this analysis, there are several aspects that are taken into account or analyzed, such as characteristics, elements, and also the principles used by the painter. This analysis must be carried out by people who have an in-depth understanding of the work of painting.

One example of painting analysis that has been carried out is the analysis of the painting on "Ship Hit by a Storm". The analysis includes:

Characteristics, The title of the painting is "Ship in Storms" made by Raden Saleh. This painting was made in 1837 with oil paint on canvas measuring 97 x 74 cm. This painting has a romantic style or theme.The elements, the lines contained in the subject are (a) irregular curved lines on the waves, (b) straight lines on the reef lines and chunks of wrecked ships and ships that are sailing, (c) sharp lines on the ends and some the edge of the reef and at the end of the ship, (d) a regular curved line on the background in the form of a border between black clouds and bright blue clouds. The form shown in the painting takes a figurative form in the form of two ships bobbing in the sea that is experiencing a storm and there is one ship that is hit by waves and then crashes into a rock. The texture in the painting uses virtual textures, namely soft textures on clouds and rough textures seen on ships, waves, and cliffs. The lighting in the painting appears or comes from the upper left that emerges from behind dark clouds, and the light hits the waves and the wrecked ship. The coloring used in the painting is a light blue color behind black clouds which indicates the weather is starting to clear after the arrival of a big storm, and white as light.In principle, the painting is also very calculated regarding the placement of objects with one another, namely the placement of where the light comes from and the presence of steep cliffs, as well as the location of the ships. Ships, oceans, clouds and corals are depicted although they have different shapes and colors but still form a harmony so that they are not fixed on one form. The shape of the ocean waves that vary in the painting produces a curved motion so that the waves appear to move like the original. In the painting, the focus is focused on two ships that are bobbing in the sea during a storm.

Answer:

Arts in the Philippines refer to all the various forms of the arts that have developed and accumulated in the Philippines from the beginning of civilization in the country up to the present era. They reflect the range of artistic influences on the country's culture, including indigenous forms of the arts, and how these influences have honed the country's arts. These arts are divided into two distinct branches, namely, traditional arts and non-traditional arts.Each branch is further divided into various categories with subcategories.

Traditional arts:

•Ethnomedicine – including, but not limited to, the arts of the albularyo, the babaylan, and the manghihilot

•Folk architecture – including, but not limited to, stilt houses, land houses, and aerial houses

•Maritime transport – boat houses, boat-making, and maritime traditions

•Weaving – including, but not limited to, back-strap loom weaving, and other forms of related weaving

•Carving – including, but not limited to, woodcarving and folk non-clay sculpture

•Folk performing arts – including, but not limited to, dances, plays, and dramas

•Folk (oral) literature – including, but not limited to, epics, songs, and myths

•Folk graphic and plastic arts – including, but not limited to, calligraphy, tattooing, folk writing, folk drawing, and folk painting

•Ornament – including, but not limited to, mask-making, accessory-making, ornamental metal crafts

•Textile, or fiber art – including, but not limited to, headgear weaving, basketry, fish-gear arts, and other forms of textile or fiber art

•Pottery – including, but not limited to, ceramic making, clay pot-making, and folk clay sculpture

•Other artistic expressions of traditional culture – including, but not limited to, non-ornamental metal crafts, martial arts, supernatural healing arts, medicinal arts, and constellation traditions

Architecture in the Philippines today is the result of a natural growth enriched with the absorption of varied influences.
