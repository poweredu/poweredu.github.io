11. we ask 30 student on which subject day excel more algebra or geometry 10 student said that they both excel in algebra and in geometry for student said that they excel more in geometry students that they excel more in algebra but not in geometry ​


VENN DIAGRAM PROBLEMS Word Problem:

**Troubleshooted question

We asked 30 students on which subject day excel more: Algebra or Geometry. 10 students said that they both excel in Algebra and in Geometry. Fourteen students said that they excel more in Geometry. How many students said that they excel more in Algebra but not in Geometry?​ Assuming that all of the surveyed students excel in at least one of the mentioned subjects.  

Solution:

This explanation is based on the attached Venn diagram. The first step is to put 10 in the overlapping circles of the diagram since it was stated that 10 of them excelled both in Algebra and in Geometry. The second step is to solve for the total number of students who excelled in Geometry but not in Algebra. Since there are a total of 14 students who excelled in Geometry only, we will be subtracting this by 10 to get the net number of people. Hence, 14 minus 10 is 4. The result is 4.

To compute for the number of students who excelled in Algebra only, we will be subtracting the total number of people who excelled in Geometry plus people who excelled for both subject, from the total number of surveyed students which is 30.

So: 10 + 4 = 14; 30 - 14 = 16.  

Therefore, there are 16 students who excelled in Algebra.
