10. there are 100 bars of blue and green soap in a box.The blue bar weigh 50 g per bar and the green bar 40 g per bar.The total mass of tge soap in tge box is 4.66 kg.How many bars of green soap are in the box?​


Answer:

34 green soaps

Step-by-step explanation:

Equations needed :

40x + 50y = 4660 (Equation 1)x + y = 100 (Equation 2)

Given :

green bars weigh 40gblue bars weigh 50g4.66kg = 4660g

Representations :

x = number of green barsy = number of blue bars

Solution :

Solve for y in Equation 2.

x + y = 100

x + y - x = 100 -x

y = 100 - x

substitute the value of y in Equation 1.

40x + 50y = 4660

40x + 50(100 - x) = 4660

40x + 5000 - 50x = 4660

-10x + 5000 - 5000 = 4660 - 5000

-10x = -340

-10x/-10 = -340/-10

x = 34

Checking :

Solve for the number of blue soaps in Equation 2

x + y = 100

34 + y = 100

34 + y - 34 = 100 - 34

y = 66

Substitute both values of x and y in Equation 1.

40x + 50y = 4660

40(34) + 50(66) = 4660

1360 + 3300 = 4660

4660 = 4660 ✓

Therefore there are 34 green soaps in the box.

14. What happens to the pepper if we use diffrent kind of soap( bar soap, and hand soap)


Answer:

Soap is able to break down the surface tension of water—that’s part of what makes soap a good cleaner. As the soap moves into the water, and the surface tension changes, the pepper no longer floats on top. But the water molecules still want to keep the surface tension going, so they pull back away from the soap, and carry the pepper along with them.


15. cosmetics company that makes small cylindrical bars of soap find the surface area of a bar of soap if the diameter is 4cm and the height is 9cm​


To find the surface area of a cylindrical soap bar, we need to calculate the area of the base and the lateral surface area separately, then add them together.

The diameter of the base is 4 cm, so the radius is half of that or 2 cm. The area of the circular base is given by:

A_base = πr^2 = π(2cm)^2 = 4π cm^2

The lateral surface area is the area of the curved part of the cylinder, which can be found by multiplying the circumference of the base by the height of the cylinder:

C = 2πr = 2π(2cm) = 4π cm

A_lateral = C × h = 4π cm × 9cm = 36π cm^2

Therefore, the total surface area of the soap bar is:

A_total = A_base + A_lateral = 4π cm^2 + 36π cm^2 = 40π cm^2

So the surface area of the soap bar is 40π square centimeters (or approximately 125.66 square centimeters).
