» Determine the Net Force of an object. If it is equal to zero, then the object is in balance. If not then it is unbalanced.

[tex] \boxed{F_{net} = F_1 + F_2} [/tex]

» Set positive force when going right/east/upwards and negative if going left/west/downwards.

[tex] \: [/tex]

#1: Determine the Net Force of the crate.

[tex] F_{net} = 7 \, un. + (\text-10 \, un.) [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 7 \, un. - 10 \, un. [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = \text-3 \, un. [/tex]

» The net force of the crate is [tex] \boxed{\sf unbalanced} [/tex] and its motion is going [tex] \boxed{\sf westward} [/tex].

[tex] \: [/tex]

#2: Determine the Net Force of the box.

[tex] F_{net} = 10 \, un. + 10 \, un. [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 20 \, un. [/tex]

» The net force of the boxes is [tex] \boxed{\sf unbalanced } [/tex] and its motion is going to the [tex] \boxed{\sf right} [/tex].

[tex] \: [/tex]

#3: Determine the Net Force of the hanging pen.

[tex] F_{net} = 2 \, N + (\text-2 \, N) [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 2 \, N - 2 \, N [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 0 \, N [/tex]

» The net force of the hanging pen is [tex] \boxed{\sf balanced} [/tex] and there is [tex] \boxed{\sf no \: direction} [/tex] of the motion.

[tex] \: [/tex]

#4: Determine the Net Force of the box.

[tex] F_{net} = 5 \, N + (\text-5 \, N) [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 5 \, N - 5 \, N [/tex]

[tex] F_{net} = 0 [/tex]

» The net force of the box is [tex] \boxed{\sf balanced } [/tex] and there is [tex] \boxed{\sf no \: direction} [/tex] of the motion.

[tex] \: [/tex]

#5: A boy sitting on a chair.

The gravitational force pulls the boy downwards however, the support of the chair pushes him up as the same force as the gravity.

» Thus, the net force of the sitting boy is [tex] \boxed{\sf balanced} [/tex] and there is [tex] \boxed{\sf no \: direction} [/tex] of the motion.
