27. which of the following atomic model views the electrons as cloud of negative particles?​


Answer:

Where Are the Electrons? Up until about 1920, scientists accepted Niels Bohr's model of the atom. In this model, negative electrons circle the positive nucleus at fixed distances from the nucleus, called energy levels. You can see the model in Figure below for an atom of the element nitrogen.

The modern model is also commonly called the electron cloud model. That's because each orbital around the nucleus of the atom resembles a fuzzy cloud around the nucleus, like the ones shown in the Figure below for a helium atom. The densest area of the cloud is where the electrons have the greatest chances of being.
