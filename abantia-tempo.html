1. In musical terminology, tempo is the speed or pace of a given piece. In classical music, tempo is typically indicated with an instruction at the state of a piece and is usually measured in beats per minute.

2. Most sheet music provides a tempo marking, which is how fast or slow you should sing a song. The marking is located at the very top of the sheet music, just below the composer and arrangers' names and just above the written music.

3. Slow tempo: Lentissimo, Largo, Larghetto and Adagio.
Moderate tempo: Andante, Andantino, Moderato and Allegretto.
Fast tempo: Allegro, Vivace, Vivacissimo and Allegrissimo or Allegro Vivace.
