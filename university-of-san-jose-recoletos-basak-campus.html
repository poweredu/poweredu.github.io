Reduced self-esteem: Discrimination can also impact the self-esteem of students, leading to a lack of confidence in their abilities. This can further impact their academic performance as they may not be willing to take risks or put themselves out there in class.

Limited opportunities: Strand discrimination can limit the opportunities available to students. For instance, if students are not given access to resources or opportunities because of their chosen strand, it can hinder their ability to perform well academically.

Stereotyping: Discrimination can also lead to stereotypes and assumptions about students based on their chosen strand. This can lead to teachers having lower expectations for some students, which can affect their academic performance.

Overall, strand discrimination can have serious negative effects on the academic performance of grade 11 students at Arellano University Jose Rizal Campus.
