10. the clock reads 9:00 what the degree measure of a smaller angle form the hands of the clock​


Answer:

At 9:00, the hour hand is at 9, that is 9*30 = 270 deg from 12 and the minute hand is at 12, or 0 deg from 12.

So the angle between the hour and minute hands is 270 deg which is a reflex angle and the average curve is therefore 360–270 = 90 deg.

15. read each analogue clock and write the time in 12 and 24 hour clock format​


Analog clock time conversion.

I can explain how to read analogue clocks and how to write the time in 12 and 24 hour clock format.

To read an analogue clock, you need to look at the position of the clock hands. The short hand represents the hour, while the long hand represents the minutes. In some clocks, there's a third hand that represents the seconds.

To write the time in 12-hour clock format, you need to specify whether it's morning or afternoon. For example, if the time is 2:30 PM, you would write it as 2:30 PM. If the time is 2:30 AM, you would write it as 2:30 AM.

To write the time in 24-hour clock format, you don't need to specify whether it's morning or afternoon. You simply write the hour and the minute separated by a colon. For example, if the time is 14:30, you would write it as 14:30.

25. The clock reads 4 o’clock. What is the degree measure of the angle formed by the hands of the clock?


Solution:

Let x be the measure of the angle formed by the hands of the clock;

1 turn = 360 degrees
4/12 turn = x degrees

Let's first reduce 4/12 to lowest term and that would be 1/3.

1 : 360 = 1/3 : x
(Cross Multiply)
x = 120 degrees

Answer: The measure of the angle formed by the hands of the clock is 120.
