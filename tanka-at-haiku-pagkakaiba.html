24. pagkakaiba ng haiku at tanka​


Answer:

Haiku and tanka are both traditional forms of Japanese poetry, but they have some differences:

1. Syllable count: Haiku has a syllable count of 5-7-5, while tanka has a syllable count of 5-7-5-7-7.

2. Structure: Haiku is a three-line poem that typically focuses on nature and the changing seasons, while tanka is a five-line poem that can cover a wide range of themes and emotions.

3. Tone: Haiku is often seen as a form of nature poetry, and is usually meditative, contemplative and simple in tone, while tanka is more expressive and can be more emotional, romantic and passionate in tone.

4. Subject matter: Haiku traditionally focuses on nature, while tanka can be more diverse in its subject matter. Tanka can be written about love, nature, personal experiences, and many other topics.

5. Length: Haiku is shorter than tanka, as it is only 17 syllables long, while tanka is 31 syllables long.

28. TANKA (Pagkakaiba)HAIKU (Pagkakaiba)(Pagkakatulad)​


Answer:

Ang tanka at haiku ay mga uri ng panitikan

Ano ang Haiku?

Ito ay maikling tula na gumagamit ng tatlong linya at 5/7/5 na pantig.

Ano Ang Tanka?

Katulad ng Haiku, ang Tanka ay sumusunod sa 5/7/5 na pantig, ngunit, gumagamit ito ng dalawa pang linya para maging 5/7/5/7/7.

Pagkakaiba:

Tanka - May bilang na 31 pantig at may limang taludtod. (May paksang pagbabago ang tanka)

Haiku - May bilang na 17 pantig at may tatlong taludtod

Pagkakapareho:

Parehas na na nagmula sa japan, Parehas na maikling tula, Pumapaksa sa Kalikasan o Pag ibig. Parehas na ginagamitan ng maiikli ngunit matatalinghagang salita.
