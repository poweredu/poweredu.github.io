The following day, I was walking through the hallway, desperately trying to find my classroom, when I heard a voice call out my name. Sarah was my first friend at this new school and soon, I learned to never judge a book by its cover. I'm glad I gave Sarah a chance, or else I would have missed out on a great friendship.


10. On prejudice write about an experience related to this quote "Don't judge a book by it's cover


Answer:

Prejudice is an unfortunate part of human nature, and it often leads us to judge others based on their appearance, race, or gender. However, the truth is that appearances can be deceiving, and we should never judge a book by its cover.

I had a personal experience that taught me this lesson when I was in college. I had just started my first semester and was looking forward to making new friends. One day, I saw a girl walking across the campus, and I immediately formed an opinion about her based on her appearance. She had tattoos and piercings, and her hair was dyed bright pink. I assumed she was a rebellious troublemaker, and I didn't think she would be someone I would want to be friends with.

However, a few days later, I found myself sitting next to her in class. We started talking, and I soon realized that she was one of the kindest and most intelligent people I had ever met. She was studying to be a nurse, and she had a passion for helping others. She was also incredibly artistic and had a talent for drawing and painting. We quickly became friends, and I learned a valuable lesson about not judging people based on their appearance.

This experience taught me that we should never judge others based on their appearance or other superficial qualities. We never know what kind of person someone is until we take the time to get to know them. By judging people based on their appearance, we risk missing out on valuable friendships and connections. We should always strive to be open-minded and accepting of others, regardless of their appearance or background. After all, the most interesting and wonderful people often come in unexpected packages.

15. On prejudice write about an experience related to this quote "Don't judge a book by it's cover


Answer:

Imagine a person who has always lived in a small town where everyone knows each other. This person has grown up with certain beliefs and stereotypes about people who live in big cities. They believe that people from big cities are rude, selfish, and always in a rush.

One day, this person decides to take a trip to a big city for the first time. As they walk around, they notice how crowded and fast-paced everything is. They feel overwhelmed and intimidated by the people rushing by.

However, as they continue to explore the city, they begin to interact with some of the locals. They are surprised to find that many of them are friendly, helpful, and kind. They also realize that they had been wrong to make assumptions about people based on where they live.

This experience teaches them the lesson that they should not judge people based on their appearance, background, or location. Just like how one should not judge a book by its cover, they learned to get to know people for who they are, rather than making assumptions based on stereotypes or preconceived notions.

In conclusion, the quote "Don't judge a book by its cover" is a powerful reminder to approach people with an open mind and heart, and not to let our prejudices and biases cloud our judgment. It is only by getting to know people on a deeper level that we can truly understand and appreciate their unique qualities and experiences.
