5. Problems and challenges can arise and affect scope management from this area: 1. Health and safety 2. Human resources and project stakeholders


Answer:

1. Health and safety:

- Changes in health and safety regulations can impact the scope of a project, especially if it involves physical construction, hazardous materials, or high-risk activities.

- Safety hazards encountered during the project may require additional work or changes in scope to address, such as installing safety equipment or re-designing a space to reduce injury risks.

- Employee sickness, injury or absenteeism due to health and safety concerns can delay or impact project scope, particularly if the employee is a key team member.

2. Human resources and project stakeholders:

- Staffing changes, such as losing key team members or having insufficient resources, can affect the scope of a project by delaying milestones or making it impossible to achieve objectives that require specific expertise or resources.

- Stakeholder expectations and demands for changes to the project scope can cause delays, additional costs and challenges in reconciling competing interests and constraints.

- Conflicts between stakeholders, including disagreements over project goals or priorities, can lead to delays or difficulties in finalizing project scope, particularly if compromises must be made to satisfy differing perspectives.
