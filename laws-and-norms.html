29. How are norms different from laws? Which of the two provides heavier sanctions?


Answer:

Norms are social expectations about how people should act, while laws are rules that have been established by a governing body and are enforced by the police. Norms provide softer sanctions than laws, as they are enforced by social pressure and disapproval rather than by the threat of criminal prosecution. Laws provide heavier sanctions, such as fines or even imprisonment in some cases.
