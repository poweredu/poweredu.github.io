6. Create summary timeline of the development of the ideas and concepts of atom from its conception, structure models and components and reflect of how these contribution affect modern chemistry and humanitymaayos na sagot guys :)​


Answer:

600 BCE - Democritus proposed the concept of the atom: He proposed the idea of the atom as the smallest indivisible piece of matter that could not be further divided.

1808 - John Dalton proposed the atomic theory: This was the first to create a scientific model of the atom. It described the atom as a single indivisible particle made up of positive charge and a small centrally located nucleus surrounded by electrons; the only elements which differed from each other were their different atomic weights.

1897 – J.J Thomson proposed the first model of the atom: This was the first formal atom model. It described the atom as a sphere of positive charge with electrons distributed throughout it randomly.

1911 – Ernest Rutherford proposed his model of the atom: This was the first to propose the presence of the nucleus in the atom. It described the atom as a sphere of dense positive charge with electrons orbiting in a cloud around it.

1913 – Niels Bohr proposed his atomic model: This was the first to explain the structure of the atom in terms of energy levels that the electrons could exist in. This model also provided the initial prediction of the line spectra of atoms, which is an important concept in today's atomic physics.

1924 – Erwin Schrödinger proposed his wave equation: This was the first to explain the behaviour of electrons in terms of wave functions. This equation was the first to be able to describe the structure of the atom in terms of quantum theory.

Modern Atomic Theory - In the modern atomic theory, the structure of the atom consists of either a single proton and neutron, or else a nucleus composed of protons and neutrons. Electrons are believed to form a cloud of negative charge around the nucleus. The phenomenon of quantum mechanics is used to explain the motion of the electrons.

The development of modern atomic theory and understanding of the structure of the atom has had a profound effect on chemistry and humanity. The structure of the atom is used to explain many of the chemical and physical phenomena observed in everyday life. Furthermore, the nuclear aspect of the atom has also enabled us to develop powerful sources of energy, such as nuclear fission and fusion.
