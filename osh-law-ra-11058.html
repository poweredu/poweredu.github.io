27. Description of significance law RA 9211


Answer:

• RA 9211, or the Tobacco Regulation Act of 2003, is a law in the Philippines that regulates the manufacturing, sale, and distribution of tobacco products in the country. The law aims to protect the public, particularly the youth, from the harmful effects of smoking by implementing measures such as increasing taxes on cigarettes, banning smoking in certain public places, and requiring warning labels on cigarette packaging. Additionally, the law also regulates the advertising and promotion of tobacco products, and requires manufacturers to disclose the ingredients in their products. RA 9211 is significant as it is one of the first laws in the Philippines to address the public health issue of smoking and its impact on the population.
