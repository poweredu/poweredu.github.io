8. Create a timeline of the History of Athletics.​


Answer:

796-393AD: First record of organized athletic competition in Greece. Gave warriors the opportunity to test their skills in Olympic type sports: running, jumping, wrestling, throwing, etc. [1]

1896: The first official Olympic games were held, weightlifting was included as an official sport. [2]

1904: At Olympic games held in St. Louis, marathon runner Thomas Hicks won the gold medal. Throughout the course of the race he ingested egg whites off a sponge, three shots of brandy, and strychnine- in order to combat the rigors of the marathon. First documented use of nutrition to aid an athlete. [3]

1930’s: Historians consider the first studies of sport nutrition to be those of carbohydrate and fat metabolism, conducted in Sweden. 3

1950: National Athletic Trainer Association (NATA) was founded to “build and strengthen the profession of athletic training through the exchange of ideas, knowledge, and methods of athletic training.” 4

1955: William E. Newell of Purdue University became the first athletic trainer to be appointed to the position of National Secretary of NATA. [4]

1959: NATA approved first athletic training curriculum model. Required: 8 semesters of biology/ zoology, 6 hours of physics or chemistry, anatomy, physiology of exercise, kinesiology, nutrition and foods, physical science laboratory, and pre-requisite courses for physical therapy school. 4

1960’s: Scandinavian scientists began to study muscle glycogen storage, use, and resynthesis associated with prolonged exercise. 3

1965: Dr. Robert Cade at the University of Florida developed a scientifically formulated beverage for the schools football team, to ensure that players were properly hydrated in the Florida heat—Gatorade. 3

1970: NATA certification committee administered the first certification exam. 4

1990: American Medical Association formally recognized athletic training as an allied health profession. 4

10. 1. What does student athlete mean? 2. What are the training programs of the student athlete?

1.A student athlete (sometimes written student–athlete) is a participant in an organized competitive sport sponsored by the educational institution in which the student is enrolled.
