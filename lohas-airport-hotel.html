1. What is LOHAS? Contrast LOHAS with other market segments identified by the Natural Marketing Institute.​


Answer:

What is the LOHAS market?

Lifestyles of Health and Sustainability (LOHAS) is a demographic defining a particular market segment related to sustainable living, "green" ecological initiatives, and generally composed of a relatively upscale and well-educated population segment.

What are the so called LOHAS factors?

According to a study by the Federal Environment Agency, the LOHAS target group can be identified by the following characteristics: LOHAS Women tend to behave more ecologically than men. Higher education is a positive influencing factor regarding environmentally friendly behavior

What is LOHAS and why are people who follow this lifestyle important?

LOHAS defines people who have interests in increasing personal and their family's health and well-being (Ray and Anderson 2000). They should purchase a wide variety of natural and organic products that enhance physical fitness and its ranges from the foods to the personal care products (e.g., Emerich 2000).

What are the principles of LOHAS consumers?

At their core, LOHAS consumers are looking for honesty, authenticity, and an alignment of principles and values with the brands they buy from. They want things that are real, hence the rapid rise of natural, organic, local, non-GMO, and now even wild foods (see the Power of Wild research), and they want transparency.

18. importance and uses of computer in 1.school2.Airport3.department store4.hospital5.hotel6.bank7.office8.manifacturing company9.home​


1. School: Computers are essential in schools as they are used for research, learning and communication. They are used for online learning, creating presentations, and word processing.

2. Airport: Computers are used in airports for flight scheduling, check-in, baggage tracking, and security systems. They also assist with customer service by providing information and assistance to passengers.

3. Department Store: Computers are used in department stores for inventory management, sales tracking, and financial management. They are also used for customer service, such as online shopping, self-checkout, and credit card processing.

4. Hospital: Computers are used in hospitals for patient records, scheduling appointments, and ordering lab tests. They also assist in the administration of medication and the monitoring of vital signs.

5. Hotel: Computers are used in hotels for room reservations, guest check-in and check-out, and billing. They also assist with inventory management and customer service.

6. Bank: Computers are used in banks for financial transactions, account management, and security systems. They also assist with customer service by providing information and assistance to customers.

7. Office: Computers are used in offices for word processing, spreadsheets, email, and internet access. They also assist with scheduling, financial management, and customer service.

8. Manufacturing Company: Computers are used in manufacturing companies for production scheduling, inventory management, and financial management. They also assist with quality control and the tracking of materials and products.

9. Home: Computers are used in homes for personal and professional use, such as communication, research, and entertainment. They are also used for online shopping, home automation, and other tasks.
