8. cinderella story summary​


Cinderella, as a character, is the embodiment of modesty and diligence. She’s a girl who hasn’t lost her kindness even after her mother’s death or after the cruelty from her stepmother and stepsisters. She tolerated everything patiently. Still, what changed her life was a rebellion, a violation of the rules. Although she was forbidden to going to the ball, she did it with the help of her fairy godmother. At the ball, she met Prince Charming who immediately fell in love with her. She was the only one in the kingdom who could win his heart.

The shoe symbol, which we first associate with Cinderella, tells us that there is only one person we can love. Although we may pretend, the heart always knows the truth. A shoe that fits a single leg is a metaphor for the unique love we can only get from one, special person.

27. COMPARE THE STORY OF CINDERELLA AND SNOW WHITETHEIR THEMETHEIR CONFLICTTHEIR PLOTTHEIR SETTINGTHEIR CHARACTE​


The story of Cinderella and Snow White are both classic fairy tales that have been retold many times in different cultures. Both stories are about young women who are mistreated by their stepmothers and go on to find happiness and fulfillment with the help of magical beings.

The theme of both stories is similar in that they both revolve around the idea of inner beauty and the triumph of good over evil. Cinderella and Snow White are both kind, gentle, and pure of heart, and they are ultimately rewarded for their goodness.

The main conflict in both stories is between the young women and their stepmothers. In Cinderella, the stepmother is cruel and jealous, and she mistreats Cinderella and tries to keep her from attending the royal ball. In Snow White, the stepmother is also jealous and evil, and she plots to kill Snow White out of fear that she will be more beautiful than her.

The plot of both stories follows a similar pattern, with the young women facing hardships and challenges, but ultimately triumphing and finding happiness. In Cinderella, she is forced to do all the household chores and is prevented from going to the ball, but with the help of her fairy godmother, she is able to attend and catch the eye of the prince. In Snow White, she is forced to flee from her stepmother and takes refuge with a group of dwarves, but with the help of a prince, she is able to defeat her stepmother and live happily ever after.

The setting of both stories is a typical fairy tale kingdom, with a castle, a royal court, and a beautiful countryside. In Cinderella, the setting is a wealthy kingdom with a royal ball and a palace, while in Snow White, the setting is a forest filled with magical creatures like dwarves and talking animals.

The main characters in both stories are Cinderella and Snow White, as well as their stepmothers and the princes who help them. Cinderella and Snow White are both kind, gentle, and beautiful, and they are both mistreated by their stepmothers. The stepmothers are evil and jealous, and they both try to harm the young women in order to keep them from finding happiness. The princes in both stories are handsome and brave, and they help the young women to overcome their hardships and find their happily ever after.
