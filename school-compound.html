30. According to my grandfather that school used to be the site of a grave yard . Why Is it a compound preposition? ​


The phrase "according to" is a compound preposition because it is made up of two or more words that work together to show the relationship between the object of the preposition ("my grandfather") and the rest of the sentence.

In this sentence, "according to" indicates that the information being shared is based on the knowledge or opinion of the speaker's grandfather. It functions as a single unit to connect the subject of the sentence ("school") with the information being conveyed ("the school used to be the site of a graveyard").

Other examples of compound prepositions include "in spite of," "out of," "along with," "due to," and "in front of."
