Mathematical operations follow a specific syntax in order to avoid ambiguity (thus the PEMDAS). When these operations are converted into words, the same syntax must then be followed coupled with correct phrasing.

Going over each of the mathematical sentences and converting them into arithmetic symbols:

- The quotient of a number (variable x) and 4, minus 13. Take note of the comma that separates the quotient and the minus thirteen

[tex]\frac{x}{4} - 13[/tex]

- The product of 13 and the quotient of a number(variable x) and 4:

[tex]13(\frac{x}{4})[/tex]

- The difference of 13 and the quotient of a number and 4: When mentioning differences, the order of the numbers matter. Since 13 is mentioned first, normally 13 must be the minuend.

[tex]13 - \frac{x}{4}[/tex]

- Lastly, the sum of a number and four, minus thirteen: It is observed therefore that the comma acts like a parenthesis in the PEMDAS. It separates the operation from a whole another expression.

[tex](x+4)-13[/tex]
