8. Write 5 examples of each common stereotypes Age stereotype Racial StereotypesGender StereotypesSocial Class Stereotypes​


Answer:

Age stereotypes:

1. Older people are forgetful and unable to learn new things.

2. Teenagers are rebellious and irresponsible.

3. Children are naive and innocent.

4. Middle-aged people are boring and unadventurous.

5. Young people are entitled and self-absorbed.

Racial stereotypes:

1. African Americans are lazy and dishonest.

2. Asian Americans are good at math and science.

3. Latinx people are passionate and fiery.

4. Native Americans are lazy and dependent on government assistance.

5. White people are privileged and entitled.

Gender stereotypes:

1. Men are not good at household tasks.

2. Women are emotional and irrational.

3. Girls are passive and delicate.

4. Women are not good at math and science.

5. Men are strong and capable, while women are weak and vulnerable.

Social class stereotypes:

1. Poor people are lazy and do not want to work.

2. Rich people are greedy and selfish.

3. Upper class people are snobbish and pretentious.

4. Working class people are uneducated and uncultured.

5. Middle class people are boring and conformist.

It is important to note that these stereotypes are harmful and not based in reality. Every individual is unique and cannot be accurately judged or categorized based on their age, race, gender, or social class.

Giving a person or an idea a bad label by using an easy to remember pejorative name. This is used to make us reject and condemn a person or idea without examining what the label really means.

Name-calling propaganda is based on putting the other party down. Employing this technique in advertising normally starts brand wars. It can be light-hearted, but sometimes the animosity can get intense.

25. Read and answer. How do you think labels from stereotypes gain popularity.?​


Labels from stereotypes gain popularity through a process of repetition and reinforcement. When people repeatedly associate a behavior or characteristic with a particular group, it becomes embedded in their culture and passes down from generation to generation. Initially, these labels may seem harmless or even amusing, but over time they can lead to prejudice, discrimination and even bigotry. Labels from stereotypes are often spread through media, as well as social interactions and conversations, which can perpetuate stereotypes that are inaccurate and harmful.
