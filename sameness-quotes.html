5. "no man ever steps in the same river twice, for its not the same river and his not the same man". –Heraclitus. How does this quote relate to the statement the other remains infinitely transcendent, infinitely foreign?​


Here is what Heraclitus meant by no man ever steps in the same river twice, for its not the same river and his not the same man and its relation to the statement the other remains infinitely transcendent, infinitely foreign.

No man ever steps in the same river twice means that a person is constantly evolving, whether he realizes it or not. This, in relation to the statement that man infinitely transcends and is infinitely foreign means that a human being is a different version of himself almost all the time.

The same man that Heraclitus meant here is actually just an analogy, and the fact is that the man being talked about is the same individual, but with different way of thinking, feelings, as well as personal preferences and perspectives. More often than not, these things are influenced by one's experiences, and if those prove the man to be a better person, then the second statement about being transcendent and foreign apply.

11. The words of the speaker are given in a different form but with the same meaning.a. Speechb. Direct or Quoted Speechc. Indirect or Reported Speechd. Direct - Indirect Speech​


Answer:

The answer is letter C.

Indirect speech is speech which tells you what someone said, but does not use the person's actual words: for example, 'They said you didn't like it', 'I asked her what her plans were', and ' Citizens complained about the smoke'.

25. Task 8.2 A QUOTE ON QUOTE


The phrase “quote unquote” means speaking a quotation on a word for word basis. You end the quotation with the word “unquote” to indicate that it’s finished. On the one hand, the phrase “quote on quote” is a mispronunciation of “unquote.” The correct phrase is “quote unquote.” This indicates that what you’re saying is a repetition of someone else’s statement.
