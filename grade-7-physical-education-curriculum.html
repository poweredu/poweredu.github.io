2. Research on the legality of physical education in the curriculum...​


Physical education (PE) is generally considered to be a mandatory part of the curriculum in most public schools in the United States. The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) require that students with disabilities be provided with appropriate physical education. However, some states have different requirements for PE. In some states, homeschooled students are not required to participate in physical education. Additionally, there are some private schools that do not include PE in their curriculum. In general, physical education is considered an important part of a well-rounded education, and its inclusion in the curriculum is supported by a wide range of organizations, including the National Association for Sport and Physical Education (NASPE) and the American Heart Association (AHA).

11. enumerate the philosophy of K-12 program in physical education curriculum


Hi Elicahpablo,

The answer to your question lies on the k12 physical education curriculum guide and the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2012.

According to Republic Act No. 10533 (also known as the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2012), The K to 12 curriculum seeks to develop 21st century skills among its learners. This also includes the following:

• Cognitive skills of critical thinking

• Problem-solving and creative thinking

• Interpersonal skills of communication

• Collaboration

• Leadership and cross-cultural skills

• Self-management skills of self-monitoring and self-direction

• Project management skills

• Personal characteristics which are part of;

o Ethics

o Civic responsibility

o Accountability

Contents about Fitness and Movement Education are the core of the physical education philosophy statement. The said philosophy encompasses value, knowledge, skills and experiences in physical activity participation in order to:

1. Achieve and maintain health-related fitness

2. Optimize health.

The philosophy also envisions to embed an understanding of why health-related fitness is important in order to apply it and translate health-related fitness into action.

The K to 12 PE Curriculum prioritizes these standards:

1. Habitual physical activity participation to achieve and maintain health-enhancing levels of fitness.

2. Competence in movement and motor skills requisite to various physical activity performances

3. Valuing physical activities for enjoyment, challenge, social interaction and career opportunities

4. Understanding various movement concepts, principles, and strategies and tactics as they apply to the learning of physical activity

Excerpts from the k to 12 curriculum guide in physical education December 2013

In essence, the k to 12 PE curriculum envisions that learners can appreciate PE as means of developing and optimizing individual health through inclusive approach. It also aims to answer the how’s and why’s of such movements and how it is relevant to the 21st century skills that a student must have.
