9. life cycle of water hyacinth?​


Life cycle of water hyacinth⬆️

Water hyacinth grows from seed and through vegetative reproduction. Seeds are produced in capsules at the base of each flower. Daughter plants are produced by vegetative reproduction, remain attached to the parent plant until they are broken off by wind or other physical damage.

13. who are the characters in the story of echo and narcissus​


Answer:

Echo - Echo is an innocent forest nymph that loved to talk. But there is a constant obstacle that Echo will always face; Hera has put a terrible curse on her. For the rest of her life, she can only echo what others say. She sees the handsome, young nymph, Narcissus wandering through the forest and instantly falls in love. Echo has a dilemma; she isn't able to talk to Narcissus because of the awful curse and she cannot be with Narcissus and she sulks in her heartbreak until only her voice is left behind.  

Narcissus - Narcissus is a vain and handsome young man, with many admirers but uninterested in a romantic relationship. When he meets the nymph, Echo, he likes her but becomes annoyed when he realizes that all she does is copy what he says. Then, Narcissus gets punished for his own vanity and gets a curse of his own. He has fallen in love with someone he can never be with... himself. All day and all night Narcissus stares at his reflection in the river until he turns into a flower.

Zeus/Jupiter/Jove - Zeus, King of the gods, is always in pursuit of a young girl. He never seems to be satisfied with his own wife, Hera, and constantly seduces those of the female species.

Hera/Juno - Hera is the wife of Zeus who constantly gets on her nerves, striking up romances with other women. Echo, a beautiful nymph, falls victim to

her angry power.

30. water hyacinth structure


Answer:

Water hyacinth is a floating waterweed up to 65 cm tall and the leaves are dark green rounded leaves up to 5–10 cm in diameter. Leaf stalks of young plants are swollen into spongy, bulbous structures; mature plants have elongated leaf stalks. An erect stalk supports a single spike of 8 to 15 flowers.
