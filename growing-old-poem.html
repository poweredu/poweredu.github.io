The best of the poem "growing" by bob Wallace is its message of hope. Despite the challenges that come with growing up, the speaker remains optimistic about the future. This optimism is contagious, and it's one of the things that makes this poem so unique. We all face challenges in our lives, but it's important to remember that there's always hope for a better tomorrow.

Growing up is a process that happens gradually over time. It's a journey that starts with taking our first steps and ends with us becoming adults. Growing old, on the other hand, is something that happens to us whether we like it or not. It's a process that can't be stopped and eventually leads to us dying.

Wallace begins the poem "growing" by describing how our bodies change as we get older. We lose muscle mass and bone density. Our skin becomes thin and dry. Our hair starts to fall out. In the end, Wallace reminds us that growing old is a natural part of life and that we should embrace it.
