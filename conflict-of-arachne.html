3. what is the nature of the conflict in arachne?


Stories such as myths are made interesting and teach lessons through conflicts. In particular, what is the nature of the conflict in Arachne (a mythological story)? Basically, the nature of the conflict can be classified as “man versus self” despite the presence of another character, who was a key element in the story. This conflict is demonstrated by the main character’s arrogance, a characteristic trait that was clearly shown by Arachne.

For me Arachne’s climax, which can be seen on this link https://brainly.ph/question/812774, also addresses the primary conflict of the story. Her weaving competition against a goddess is a metaphorical take on how she manages her innate boisterousness and high confidence. Meanwhile, the theme of the story is about the natural proudness of humans. Moreover, the story talks about limitation which was best demonstrated by the punishment of Arachne by the goddess, whom she defeated.
