8. A girl is sitting 50 m away from the stage of a concert. if she is 10 m above the level of the stage, what is the angle of depression is she watching the concert? use 'degrees' to denote the angle of depression.


Answer:

The angle of depression is the angle between the line of sight from the girl to the stage and the horizontal. We can use the tangent function to find this angle:

tan(theta) = opposite/adjacent

In this case, the opposite side is the height of the girl above the stage (10 m) and the adjacent side is the distance from the girl to the stage (50 m). So:

tan(theta) = 10/50

tan(theta) = 0.2

To find the angle itself, we need to take the inverse tangent (or arctangent) of both sides:

theta = arctan(0.2)

Using a calculator, we find:

theta ≈ 11.31 degrees

So the angle of depression is approximately 11.31 degrees.

29. conservation of aesthetic value​


Answer:

Aesthetic value is the value that an object, event, or state of affairs (most paradigmatically an artwork or the natural environment) possesses in virtue of its capacity to elicit pleasure (positive value) or displeasure (negative value) when appreciated or experienced aesthetically.
