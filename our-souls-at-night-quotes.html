3. Do you agree or disagree with the quote "Brevity is the soul of wit" Why?


Answer:

It is one of the countless phrases coined by William Shakespeare. It appears in his play, Hamlet, in the second act, where Polonius says, “Since brevity is the soul of wit / And tediousness the limbs and outward flourishes, I will be brief…” However, doubt about the creation of this phrase lurks among literary circles. Some critics argue that, by using this proverb in his play, Shakespeare made it popular, though the phrase was already in use. On the other hand, some doubt the veracity of this argument

21. What is the meaning of the quote “I am the master of my fate, I am the captain of my soul.” By: William Shakespeare


Answer:

In the poem 'Invictus,' the speaker says at the end, "I am the master of my fate,/ I am the captain of my soul." The first line of this quote means that the speaker is fully responsible for whatever happens to him. The reason for this is that he has complete control over his destiny. So one's fate is in one's own hands. It all depends on how you make it work for you. The speaker then says in the final line that he is the captain of his soul. The person in command of a ship is known as the captain. The ship represents the speaker's soul in this context. So, the direction of the soul is determined by one's actions, not by predestination.

24. Is supernatural virtues The divine life of our soul???​


Answer:

Supernatural virtues can be understood as virtues that go beyond our natural abilities and inclinations and are infused into our souls by God's grace. These virtues include faith, hope, and charity (also known as love), as well as the four cardinal virtues of prudence, justice, fortitude, and temperance.

The divine life of our soul, on the other hand, refers to the spiritual life that we receive from God through faith in Jesus Christ. This divine life is characterized by our participation in God's own life and nature, and it transforms us from within, enabling us to grow in holiness and to love God and others more deeply.

So while supernatural virtues are certainly a part of the divine life of our soul, they are not the whole of it. Rather, they are one aspect of the spiritual transformation that takes place in us when we receive God's grace and participate in His divine life.
