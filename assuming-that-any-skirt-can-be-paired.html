3. Assuming, that any skirt can be paired with any blouse, in how many ways can Anna select her outfit? List the possibilities


Answer:

[tex]x * y[/tex] ways

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Let [tex]x[/tex] represents the number of blouses

[tex]y[/tex] the number of skirts.

In every 1 skirt there's [tex]y[/tex] choices of blouses and vice versa, the two are independent of one another. Therefore, to get the number of possible combinations, we will apply the multiplication principle [tex]x * y[/tex] . Multiplication Principle states that for every independent conditions, there are [tex]x * y[/tex] pairs.

Example:

5 skirts and 7 blouses

For every 1 skirt there are 7 blouses to choose from. Therefore, there are 35 ways
