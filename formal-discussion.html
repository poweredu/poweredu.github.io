6. what is a formal discussion done by a selected group of experts?


Answer:

A panel discussion is a specific format used in a meeting, conference or convention. It is a live or virtual discussion about a specific topic amongst a selected group of panelists who share differing perspectives in front of a large audience.

The panel is typically facilitated by a “moderator” who guides the panel and the audience through the event. The panel, typically 3-4 experts or practitioners in the field, shares facts, offers opinions and responds to audience questions either through questions curated by the moderator or taken from the audience directly. The panel session typically lasts for 60-90 minutes.

19. define and discuss the definition of formal writing?​


ANSWER;

Formal writing is that form of writing which is used for the business, legal, academic or professional purpose. On the other hand, informal writing is one which is used for personal or casual purpose. Formal writing must use a professional tone, whereas a personal and emotional tone can be found in informal writing.

Definition of Formal Writing

A formal piece of writing is used when we do not have any idea of the person, or when we know the person but haven’t exchanged words, or we are not having familiar terms with the person who receives the letter. Here, we use formal language which indicates dignified and deferential regard for the reader. It is used when writing for academic, professional and legal purposes.

20. ESSAY Discuss some forces that influence the formality of strategic management systems.


Explanation:

The formality of strategic management systems is influenced by several forces, including the size and complexity of the organization, the level of competition in the industry, the regulatory environment, and the organization's culture and leadership style. Larger and more complex organizations tend to have more formal strategic management systems, with clearly defined processes and structures. In highly competitive industries, formal strategic management systems may be necessary to ensure a company's survival. The regulatory environment may also require formal strategic management systems to ensure compliance with legal and ethical standards. Finally, the organization's culture and leadership style can influence the formality of strategic management systems, with more hierarchical and centralized organizations typically having more formal systems than those with more decentralized and collaborative cultures

26. Discuss Formal Report It is a complex account either written or oral that uses formal and structured language and is usually applied in major projects and organizations.​ ​


Answer:

A formal report is a document that presents information in a clear and organized manner. It is typically used to present research findings, analyze a specific problem or issue, or provide a recommendation for a particular course of action. Formal reports are often used in professional and academic settings and are typically written in a more formal, impersonal tone than other types of writing.

There are several key components that are typically included in a formal report:

1. Title page: The title page includes the title of the report, the author's name, and the date.

2. Executive summary: The executive summary is a brief overview of the report that highlights the main points and conclusions.

3. Table of contents: The table of contents lists the various sections and sub-sections of the report and provides page numbers for easy reference.

4. Introduction: The introduction provides background information on the topic being addressed in the report and explains the purpose of the report.

5. Methodology: The methodology section describes the research methods used to gather data for the report.

6. Results: The results section presents the findings of the research in a clear and organized manner.

7. Discussion: The discussion section interprets the results and puts them into context.

8. Conclusion: The conclusion summarizes the main points of the report and may include recommendations for further action.

9. References: The references section lists the sources that were used in the report.

10. Appendices: The appendices include any supplementary material that is relevant to the report, such as charts, graphs, or tables.
