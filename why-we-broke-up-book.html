Detail of explanation:

Phrasal verbs are verbs combined with other words, usually adverbs, prepositions, or both, that make a new meaning.

For example:  

     Give    +        up            = Give up

     verb    +  preposition   =  Phrasal verb

When standing alone, give means to offer or provide something to someone. But when the word gives combined with the preposition up, the meaning changes to stop doing something before it's finished.

Let us discuss the correct phrasal verb for the three sentences in the question

1. We both       meeting your new girlfriend

(to) Look is the original verb form that has several meanings, but the main one is to turn the eye to something or someone. Look up means trying to find out information by looking in lists, books, etcLook forward to means being happy and excited about something that will happen.

Of the given options, look forward to is the most suitable phrasal verb for the sentence above, so that it means we are so happy and excited to meet your new boyfriend.

3. My car       on the highway today.

Broke down means something (like a machine) suddenly doesn't work.Broke off means to be separated from something by force or to stop doing something for some time.Broke away means to leave someone or something holding you back.

Of the given options, break away is the most suitable phrasal verb for the sentence above, which means my car stopped working on the highway today.

3. I have to        to the finish line and back.

run away means to escape from a certain person, situation, or place.run out of means to spend something so that nothing is left.(to) run is the original verb form that means to move faster than walking towards a person, place, or thing.

Of the given options, run is the most suitable choice for the sentence above.
