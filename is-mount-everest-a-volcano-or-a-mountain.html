Problem:

A mountain climber reached the peak of Mount Everest. After that, he went to the moon and climbed a mountain with the same height as Mountain Everest. Which situation shows more change in potential energy? ​

Answer;

The greatest change in Potential energy is when a mountain climber reached the peak of Mount Everest ( height of 8,848 m).

Note:

The gravitational acceleration on Earth is 9.8 m/s², while the gravitational acceleration in moon is 1.62 m/s².

Explanation:

The formula for Potential energy is P.E. = mgh. Let as assume that the mass of the person is 50 kg. The height of Mount Everest is 8,848 m.

g                        1.62 m/s²                         9.8 m/s²

h                        8,848 m                         8,848 m

P.E,                    716,688 J                  4,335,520 J

You will observe that the Potential energy of the person on Earth is far more greater compared to the moon since the the gravitation acceleration on Earth is higher compared to the moon.
