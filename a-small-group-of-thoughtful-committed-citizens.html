20. Research on violations of social justice committed by secular and religious authorities and citizens.


There have been numerous instances throughout history of violations of social justice committed by both secular and religious authorities and citizens. These violations can take many forms, including discrimination based on race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, or socioeconomic status, as well as acts of violence, exploitation, and abuse.

In some cases, these violations are perpetrated by government institutions, such as police forces or judicial systems, who fail to protect the rights and safety of marginalized communities. In other cases, they are carried out by individuals or groups who hold prejudiced beliefs or seek to maintain power and privilege at the expense of others.

Examples of social justice violations committed by secular and religious authorities and citizens include slavery and forced labor, genocide and ethnic cleansing, apartheid and segregation, police brutality and extrajudicial killings, gender-based violence and sexual assault, human trafficking and exploitation, and systemic discrimination in education, employment, and housing.

Efforts to address these violations of social justice have taken many forms, including legal reforms, grassroots activism, and international human rights movements. However, much work remains to be done to ensure that all people are treated with dignity, respect, and equity in their communities and societies.

25. write your pledge of commitment on how you can become a productive citizen


As a citizen, I will stand up for justice, freedom, and human rights. I will take part in home evangelism and food programs as a citizen. As a citizen, I will refrain from using violence to resolve conflicts. I will assist and look for justice for those in need as a citizen.

'Productive,' which is synonymous with 'useful,' is the key word here. Being one therefore constitutes the bare minimum of what it means to be a good citizen. One who is useful to the society to which they belong is a productive member of that society. The people who contribute favorably to the nation are they.

To be a good neighbor and fellow citizen, one must live in peace and harmony. He has a responsibility to uphold his nation's institutions. A good citizen should never compromise their commitment to upholding the law and should not put up with criminals or other undesirables. In order to protect the nation, he must be watchful.

Become a part of the democratic process. Respect and follow all applicable laws, both national and local. Observe others' rights, convictions, and opinions with respect. Get involved in your neighborhood.

More than only performing patriotic obligations, being a productive member of society also entails paying taxes, offering assistance to those in need, and gaining sufficient knowledge of society's operations to enable informed and responsible voting as well as intellectual contribution.
