9. what is the potential of the 30kg object at the height of 30 meters? a. 900 joules b. 450 joules c. 3550 joules d. 2970 joules​


Solution:

The potential energy of an object is

PE = mgh

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

g = 9.81 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²

h = height

Listing the values

m = 30 kg

h = 3 m

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (30 kg)(10 m/s²)(3 m)

PE = 900 J

Answer:

A. 900 joules

18. tell the words derived from the eponyms below.1.Allesandro volta2.James watt3.Isaac newton4.Georg Simon ohm5.James Prescott joule​


Answer:

1. Volta, Alessandro

The volt, a unit of the electromotive force that drives current, was named in his honour in 1881.

2. Watts or Steam watts

James Watt FRS FRSE was a Scottish inventor, mechanical engineer, and chemist who improved on Thomas Newcomen's 1712 Newcomen steam engine with his Watt steam engine in 1776, which was fundamental to the changes brought by the Industrial Revolution in both his native Great Britain and the rest of the world.

3. Newton's law of universal gravitation and laws of motion

Sir Isaac Newton, an English physicist, mathematician, and astronomer. ... Newton used universal gravitation, along with his laws of motion, to substantiate Kepler's laws of planetary motion.

4. Georg Ohm

The law was named after the German physicist Georg Ohm, who, in a treatise published in 1827, described measurements of applied voltage and current through simple electrical circuits containing various lengths of wire.

5. The Joule

Joule studied the nature of heat, and discovered its relationship to mechanical work (see energy). This led to the law of conservation of energy, which in turn led to the development of the first law of thermodynamics. The SI derived unit of energy, the joule, is named after him.


19. A car has a kinetic energy of 1.9 × 103 joules. If the velocity of the car is decreased by half, what is its kinetic energy? A. 2.2 × 102 joules B. 4.7 × 102 joules C. 5.2 × 102 joules D. 5.8 × 103 joules


Car's kinetic energy : B. 4.7x10² J

Further explanation  

Energy is the ability to do work  

Energy because its motion is expressed as Kinetic energy (KE) which can be formulated as:  

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {KE = \frac {1} {2} mv ^ 2}}}[/tex]

So for two objects that have the same speed, the greater the mass of the object, the greater the kinetic energy  

A car has a kinetic energy of 1.9 × 10³ joules

so :

[tex]\rm KE=\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2\\\\1.9.10^3=\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2\\\\mv^2=3.8.10^3[/tex]

The velocity of the car is decreased by half, then :

[tex]\rm KE=\dfrac{1}{2}m(\dfrac{1}{2}v)^2\\\\KE=\dfrac{1}{2}m.\dfrac{1}{4}v^2\\\\KE=\dfrac{1}{8}mv^2\\\\KE=\dfrac{1}{8}\times 3.8\times 10^3\\\\KE=\boxed{\bold{4.75.10^2~J}}[/tex]

22. 28. A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. what is the kinetic energy of the object?A. 12 JoulesB. 36 JoulesC. 18 JoulesD. 6 Joules​


PROBLEM:

A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. What is the kinetic energy of the object?

A. 12 Joules

B. 36 Joules

C. 18 Joules

D. 6 Joules

FORMULA:

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} [/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = velocity

SOLUTION:

Substitute the given velocity and mass, simplify the following.

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(6m/s {)}^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(36m/s {)} \\ \\ KE = \frac{2}{2} kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 1kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 36 \: J[/tex]

ANSWER:

The kinetic energy is 36 Joules.

Letter B is the answer.
