Answer: Filipino values are social constructs within Filipino culture that define that which is socially considered to be desirable. The Filipino value system describes "the commonly shared and traditionally established system of values underlying Filipino behavior" within the context of the more extensive Filipino cultural system.[1] These relate to the unique assemblage of consistent ideologies, moral codes, ethical practices, etiquette, and personal and cultural values promoted by Filipino society.[citation needed]

The formal study of Filipino values has been made difficult by the historical context of the literature in the field. The early scholarship about the Filipino value system lacked clear definitions and organizational frameworks and was mostly written by foreigners during the Philippines' American colonial period.[1][2] The latter half of the 20th century saw efforts to develop clearer definitions and properly contextualized frameworks, but many aspects of the scholarship require further clarification and consensus.[1][2]

The different value system of Filipinos has generally been described as rooted primarily in personal alliance systems, especially those based on kinship, obligation, friendship, religion (particularly Christianity), and commercial relationships.[3]
