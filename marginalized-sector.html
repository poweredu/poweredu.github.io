1. what is Marginalized sector?


Answer:

Marginalized sector refers to a portion of society or economy that do not receive proper treatment and services that is offered by the government.

Explanation:

There are people in every country who do not benefit from the services that should be free for all and provided by the government. Due to not getting proper treatment and services, they have far less advantages in life compared to the ordinary citizen.

They are called the marginalized or are part of the marginalized sector because they are at the margins or very edge of society. They are not at the center of society receiving help.

There may be several reasons why they are at the margins of society:

They are too poor to have received proper education.They are not aware of their rights.They often ignored because people do not deem them worthy or valuable.They live too far away to receive help.They are most often taken advantage of.

In the Philippines, these are some of the marginalized people:

beggars and street childrenunemployed peopleindigenous people who are taken advantage of such as the Lumads and Aetasfarmers who are often targeted by loan sharksresidents of slum areasvictims of domestic violence and sexual assaultpeople living below poverty level

2. ano ang marginalized sector​


Answer:

MARGINALIZED SECTORS

The marginalized sectors include the self employed or those working in family workshops, jeepney drivers, rural workers like fisherfolks and farmers. The nature and operation of their work are beyond the scope and reach of government legislation and regulations on labor as there are no employer-employee relationships.

17. what can Marginalized sectors do to help informal settlers?


Answer:

1. Advocate for better housing policy. Marginalized sectors can engage with policymakers to ensure that the needs of informal settlers are taken into account when crafting and implementing housing policies.

2. Provide resources and medical care. Marginalized sectors can provide food, water, and medical care to informal settlers to help them manage their current situations and prepare them for long-term sustainability.

3. Increase access to education. Marginalized sectors can provide access to literacy classes, job training, and other educational resources to informal settlers in order to provide them with the tools needed to secure better housing and employment.

4. Partner with grassroots organizations. Marginalized sectors can partner with grassroots organizations and NGOs working on housing issues to help create better local solutions for informal settlers.

5. Facilitate dialogue with government. Marginalized sectors can work with the government to create effective strategies for relocating informal settlers to areas with greater access to resources, decent housing, and job opportunities.
