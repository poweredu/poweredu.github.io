2. Which two types of the abacus were directly derived from the Chinese abacus?


Answer:

Explanation:

#Slay

Answer:

The two types of abacus that were directly derived from the Chinese abacus are the Japanese Soroban and the Korean Suanpan.

The Soroban is a type of abacus that was introduced to Japan from China in the 14th century. It is similar in design to the Chinese abacus, but typically has fewer beads and is used for more advanced calculations. The Soroban is still widely used in Japan for educational and business purposes.

The Suanpan is a type of abacus that was introduced to Korea from China during the Joseon Dynasty (1392–1910). It is similar in design to the Chinese abacus, with two decks of beads, one for the upper deck and one for the lower deck. The Suanpan is still widely used in Korea for educational and business purposes.
