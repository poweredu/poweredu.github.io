Further Explanation

The cycle in a thermal power plant uses a variety of parts. The thermal power plant's primary components are given below.

Boiler

In a thermal power plant, the boiler is used to transform the chemical energy in coal into thermal energy, also known as heat energy. The boiler's inside reaches a high temperature while the coal burns. This temperature can cause water to vaporize and become steam.

Turbine

The boiler receives the high pressure, high temperature steam. The turbine blade is being struck by the superheated steam. The turbine then begins to turn. The steam heat energy is transformed into rotational energy or kinetic energy by a mechanical mechanism called a turbine.

Super-heater

Superheated steam rotates the turbine in a steam turbine. The super-heater receives the moist, saturated steam. And it is a machine that transforms it into dry, extremely hot steam.

The temperature of the steam produced by the boiler is raised using the superheater. The steam's thermal energy will rise as a result.

Condenser

The operating pressure is reduced by the condenser. Thus, the vacuum is made stronger. Additionally, this will increase the amount of steam produced, which increases the amount of work that the turbine can do. And as a result, as the production of the turbines increases, so will the plant's efficiency.

Economizer

The economizer is a heat exchanger tool designed to cut down on energy usage. Flue gases are released into the atmosphere from the boiler. The temperature of these gases is high. As a result, the economizer heats the water using flue gas heat.

Feedwater pump

Water is fed into the boiler using a feedwater pump. Freshwater or condenser water may be used. The water is pressured by this pump. The feedwater pump typically uses centrifugal or positive displacement pumps.

Alternator

On the same shaft, the alternator and turbine are connected. Along with the flow of steam, the turbine also rotates. The alternator's rotor spins while producing electricity. As a result, the alternator is a mechanism that transforms rotational or kinetic energy into electrical energy.

Chimney

Coal is the fuel of choice for the majority of thermal power plants. In the boiler, flue gases are produced during coal burning. The chimney offers a conduit for the exhaust and flue gas to reach the atmosphere. The stack effect and natural draft are what make the chimney work. The hot air rises because it is light in weight. The chimney has a tall height. The amount of draft or draught created increases with height.

Cooling tower

The cooling tower's purpose is to reject waste heat from the atmosphere, as its name suggests. In the cooling tower, many heat transmission techniques are applied. The atmosphere is heated by the water's evaporation. And the cycle continues with the use of the chilly water.

Steam was turned into water via the condenser. Additionally, the cooling tower receives water from the condenser. In the thermal power plant, forced flow cooling towers are typically used. And the tower's bottom to top are both ventilated.

26. Advantages of Reheat Cycle in Thermal Power Plant​


Advantages:

The advantages of using a reheat cycle are higher thermal efficiency, reduced feed water pump power, smaller condenser, smaller boiler, long life of the turbine and less handling of the fuel and firing requirement.

Disadvantages:

Disadvantages of Reheating:

1- Reheating requires more maintenance.

2- The increase in thermal efficiency is not appreciable in comparison to the expenditure incurred in reheating.

What is the importance of reheating cycle in steam power plant?

Reheat not only effectively reduced the penalty of the latent heat of vaporization in steam discharged from the low-pressure end of the turbine cycle, but it also improved the quality of the steam at the low-pressure end of the turbines by reducing condensation and the formation of water droplets within the turbine.
