25. 3.When you do not worry,you do not have stress in your life.Is this statement right or wrong? a.Right,because stress is always associated with worrying. b.Wrong, because both sad and happy tension like worries and excitenments can cause stress. c.Right,because it is only when you worry that you will be stressed. d.Wrong,because worrying has nothing to do with stress​


Answer:

B.

Explanation:

The statement "When you do not worry, you do not have stress in your life" is generally wrong, and option (b) is the most accurate explanation for this. While worrying can certainly contribute to stress, there are many other factors that can cause stress, such as work pressure, relationship issues, financial difficulties, and health problems. Furthermore, not all worry leads to stress, and not all stress is caused by worry. For example, someone may experience stress due to a deadline at work, even if they are not particularly worried about it. Therefore, it is important to manage stress through various coping mechanisms, rather than simply trying to eliminate worry.

Answer:

The statement "When you do not worry, you do not have stress in your life" is incorrect. The correct answer is option (b) "Wrong, because both sad and happy tension like worries and excitements can cause stress."

Stress is a physical and emotional response to a variety of factors, including both positive and negative experiences. While worrying can certainly contribute to stress, it is not the only factor. Excitement, anticipation, and other positive emotions can also cause stress in the body.

Furthermore, stress can also be caused by external factors such as work pressure, financial problems, health issues, and relationship difficulties, which may not necessarily be related to worrying.

In summary, stress can be caused by a variety of factors, including both positive and negative emotions, as well as external circumstances. Therefore, it is not accurate to say that not worrying automatically eliminates stress from a person's life.
