18. Describe how the frictional force affects the movement of: Math teacher uses the whiteboard and marker to solve mathematical problems.


Answer:

Frictional force affects the movement of a math teacher using the whiteboard and marker in the following ways:

Writing on the whiteboard: Frictional force between the marker and the whiteboard surface affects the movement of the teacher's hand as they write. A higher level of friction between the marker and the whiteboard will make it more difficult for the teacher to write smoothly and make precise markings.

Erasing mistakes: Frictional force also affects the movement of the teacher's hand when erasing mistakes on the whiteboard. If there is too much friction between the eraser and the whiteboard, it will make it harder for the teacher to erase effectively.

Holding the marker: Frictional force between the teacher's hand and the marker affects the ability of the teacher to hold and control the marker. If there is too much friction, the teacher may have difficulty holding the marker securely, which can affect their ability to write or draw on the whiteboard.

Movement of marker: Frictional force between the marker tip and the whiteboard surface can affect the ink flow of the marker, and may make it harder for the teacher to write legibly.

Overall, frictional force plays a crucial role in the movement of the math teacher using the whiteboard and marker. It affects the teacher's ability to write, erase and control the marker, which in turn affects their ability to effectively communicate and teach mathematical problems
