2. From the story of ORPHEUS. Determine the Tone,Mood,Technique and purpose of the author in writing the text.


The story of Orpheus revolves around the son of Apollo who is named Orpheus and his wife, the wood nymph, Eurydice. The story is one of the greatest stories of love in Greek Mythology along with:

Pgymalion and Galatea, Cupid and Psyche, and Pyramus and Thisbe.

It continues to inspire the modern Greek Mythology and was translated into other versions of the original myth.

Tone

The tone of the story of Orpheus is serious. The story of Orpheus focuses on the sincerity of love and the passion that encompasses it, making the literature serious. Because of its serious tone, the readers are able to see the author's approach to the parts of the story much clearer.

Mood

The mood of the story is sad and tragic. The story of Orpheus did not end happily because he was not successful in bringing his wife, Eurydice back to the land of the living.

Technique

The story was uncomplicated and therefore, easy to understand by people of all ages. The writer had made it the technique so that the story of Orpheus can be understood even through light reading.

Purpose

The purpose in writing the text is to dwell into the love stories found in Greek Mythology. Greek Mythology already consists of many heroic stories about demigods, however, there are only a few stories of love that have depth.

"I Have a Dream" is a famous speech delivered by Martin Luther King Jr. on 28 August 1963 during the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom. The speech is characterized by an inspiring and hopeful tone, as King urges listeners to join the movement for equality and justice. The mood of the speech is optimistic and joyful, conveying King's determination and resolve. To drive home his message, King employs various rhetorical techniques such as repetition, alliteration, metaphor, and parallelism. The purpose of the speech was to rally the American public in support of the Civil Rights Movement and push for the passage of laws to end racial discrimination and segregation. King's ultimate goal was to achieve equality and justice for all people, regardless of race.
