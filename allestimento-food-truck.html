9. Andrea bought tacos from a food truck and left a 25% tip of $2.00. What was the price of Andrea's tacos, before tip?


Using the given information, the word problem was solved below. Here are the steps and answer regarding the given question of what was the price of Andrea's tacos before the tip.

Answer:

The price of Andrea's tacos before the tip is $8.00.

Step-by-step explanation:

We will use the concept of percentage in answering the formula.

Remember that A × B% = C.  We were given the value of B and C so we are to determine the value of A. A × 25% = $2.00. This is also equivalent to A × 0.25 = $2.00. Let us divide 0.25 to both sides so that 0.25 will cancel out on the left side. A = $2.00 ÷ 0.25 = $8.00. Therefore, the price of Andrea's tacos before the tip is $8.00.

We could recheck by computing $8.00 × 25%, and this is indeed equivalent to $2.00.

Those are the steps and answer regarding the given question of what was the price of Andrea's tacos before the tip.

10. PROBLEM SOLVING1. Consider a 28,000 kg truck travelling at 8 m/s towards another stationary truck. After the two truck collide, they couple together and move along at 5 m/s. What is the mass of the second truck?2. A Blue Truck and Green Truck are identical truck that are on a flat, horizontal smooth surface. The blue truck is motionless, while the green truck is moving 3.5m/s to the left and collides to blue truck. the toy trucks have a mass of 25g. After the collision, the blue truck is moving at 3m/s to the left. What is the final velocity of the green truck?================================NONSENSE/INCOMPLETE = REPORTCORRECT ANSWER = BRAINLEIST================================​


1.) Given:

m1 = 28,000 kgs.

v1 = 8 m/s

v2 = 0 m/s (stationary)

Vt = 5 m/s

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t} [/tex]

[tex](28000 \times 8) + ( m_{2} \times 0) = (28000 + m_{2})(5) [/tex]

[tex]224000 + 0 = 140000 + 5 m_{2}[/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = \frac{224000 - 140000}{5} [/tex]

[tex] m_{2} = 16800 \: kgs.[/tex]

2.) Given:

m1 & m2 = 25 kgs.

v1 = 0 m/s (motionless)

v2 = 3.5 m/s (to the left)

vt1 = 3 m/s (to the left)

Solution:

[tex] (mv)_{1} + (mv)_{2} = (mv)_{t1} + (mv)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex](25 \times 0) + (25)( - 3.5) = (25)( - 3) + (25) (v)_{t2}[/tex]

[tex]0 - 87.5 = - 75 + 25 v_{t2}[/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = \frac{ - 87.5 + 75}{25} [/tex]

[tex] v_{t2} = - 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} \: [/tex]

or

[tex] v_{t2} = 0.5 \: \frac{m}{s} (to \: the \: left)[/tex]

13. B. Differentiate the following food business ideas provided using the concept map below. Food cartFood truck Buffet Coffee shop ​


A food cart is a mobile kitchen set up on the street to prepare and sell street food to passers-by. Food carts are often found in cities worldwide selling food of every kind. Food carts come in two basic styles. One allows the vendor to sit or stand inside and serve food through a window.

A food truck is a large motorized vehicle or trailer, equipped to cook, prepare, serve, and/or sell food. Some, including ice cream trucks, sell frozen or prepackaged food; others have on-board kitchens and prepare food from scratch, or they heat up food that was prepared in a brick and mortar commercial kitchen.

A buffet is a meal consisting of several dishes from which guest serve themselves.

A coffeehouse, coffee shop, or café is an establishment that primarily serves coffee of various types, e.g. espresso, latte, and cappuccino. Some coffeehouses may serve cold drinks, such as iced coffee, iced tea, and other non-caffeinated beverages. In continental Europe, cafés serve alcoholic drinks. A coffeehouse may also serve food, such as light snacks, sandwiches, muffins, fruit, or pastries.
