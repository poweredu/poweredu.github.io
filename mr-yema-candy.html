7. Mrs. Bailey had 15 pieces of candy. She gave away some candy and has 3 pieces left. What is the percent decrease of candy?


Answer:

80 percent

Step-by-step explanation:

First: work out the difference (decrease) between the two numbers you are comparing. Then: divide the decrease by the original number and multiply the answer by 100. If your answer is a negative number then this is a percentage increase.

Answer:

80%

Step-by-step explanation:

Start by dividing 15 by 3: 15 / 3 = 5

Then divide 100 / 5 = 20

This means for every 3 pieces of candy, she loses 20% of her candy.

Now subtract 15 - 3 = 12

This shows the number of candies she lost.

Divide 12 / 3 = 4

Multiply 4 * 20 = 80

Answer:

80%

29. There are betweeb 30 and 40 yema candies in a packet. If the yema cannbe shared among 5 or 7 children, how many yema are there in the packet?


Answer:

35

Step-by-step explanation:

In order for the yema to be distributed equally and without remainder among 5 or 7 children, its number must be divisible by 5 and 7. Since its given that 30 < n < 40, where n is the no. of yema, we can tell that 35 is the only answer.

^_^

Answer:

There are 35 yema candies in the packet.

Step-by-step explanation:

Find the least common multiple (LCM) of 5 and 7 between 30 and 40:

5 = 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40

7 = 7, 14, 21, 28, 35, 42

30 < 35 < 40

Therefore, there are 35 yema candies in the packet.
