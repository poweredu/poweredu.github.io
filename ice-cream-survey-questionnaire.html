PROBLEM:

One gallon of ice cream is equal to______.

A. 4 liters of ice cream.

B. 1 liter of ice cream.

C. 3.8 liters of ice cream.

D. 2 liters of ice cream

ANSWER:

One gallon is equivalent to an approximate value of 3.785 liters or 3.8 liters when rounded off to the nearest tenths.

Therefore, the answer is letter C. One gallon of ice cream is equal to 3.8 liters of ice cream.

ADDITIONAL INFO:Gallon is a unit of measurement in terms of volume, in the imperial system and United States customary while liters is a metric unit of volume and capacity.

13. A random survey of 250 students was conducted at a middle school to determine which flavor of ice cream students prefer. The results of the survey are recorded in the table below. Student Ice Cream Preferences Vanilla,75 Chocolate,25 Butter Pecan,10 Strawberry,9 Chocolate Chip Mint,8 Neapolitan,11 Other,112 Based on these results, what is the probability that a student selected at random does NOT prefer Butter Pecan ice cream? 10% 96% 4% 90%


Answer:

24/25 or 96%

Step-by-step explanation:

Probability is defined as the likeliness of an event to occur,

Probability = # of favored outcomes / # of total outcomes

Here, our favored outcomes is everything EXCEPT those who prefer Butter Pecan ice cream. The total outcomes, on the other hand, is the total number of everything. Since the total is already given as 250, it would be easier to represent it as follows:

Instead of,

P (Not prefer Butter Pecan) = (75 + 25 + 9 + 8 + 11 + 112) / 250

We can show,

P (Not prefer Butter Pecan) = (250 - 10) / 250

= 240 / 250

= 24 / 25

= 96%
