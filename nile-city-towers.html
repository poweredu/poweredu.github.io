Answer:

Egypt is called the "gift of the Nile" because the Nile River provided fertile soil and a reliable source of water for irrigation, which allowed ancient Egyptians to grow crops and sustain a civilization. The Nile River is approximately 4,135 miles long and runs through 11 countries in northeastern Africa. It has several physical features, including its delta, which is the largest in the world, and its cataracts, which are series of rapids that made navigation difficult.
