15. Which functional group is responsible for the visible positive result in the following tests? a. Biuret test b. Ninhydrin test c. Xantoproteic test d. Millon's test e. Hopkins-Cole test f. Sakaguchi test g. Nitroprusside test h. Fohl's test i. Test for Amide j. Pauly's test


Millon's Test is the functional group that is responsible for the visible positive result.

The development of a red or pink colored precipitate during the Millon's test serves as evidence of a favorable outcome. This suggests that tyrosine or a protein containing tyrosine is present.

Using Millon's test, an analytical technique, one may identify tyrosine, the only amino acid with the phenol group. Millon's test selectively identifies tyrosine, but proteins are not recognized since it also picks up the phenolic group, which can be found in other substances. As a result, additional tests such the Biuret and Ninhydrin tests must be performed in addition to the Millon's test. The test is useful in identifying proteins because tyrosine is a common component of them. The test was developed by French scientist Auguste Nicolas Eugene Millon, who received credit for its creation.

The premise of Millon's test is that complexes with heavy metals like mercury are formed as a result of nitrification of the phenol group in tyrosine. The test ingredient, known as Millon's reagent, is composed of mercuric and mercurous nitrates that have been thoroughly mixed with strong nitric acid. The phenol group on the tyrosine molecule is nitrated by the reagent's nitric acid during the test. The nitrated tyrosine is then mixed with the mercury ions in the solution to produce a red precipitate or solution. Some proteins that include tyrosine react initially with mercuric nitrate to form a white or yellow precipitate.
