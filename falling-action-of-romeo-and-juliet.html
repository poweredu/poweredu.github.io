1. romeo and juliet falling action?​


Answer:

The falling action of Romeo and Juliet begins after the tragic deaths of the titular characters. The Capulets and Montagues reconcile after realizing the tragedy that their feud has caused. The Prince delivers a speech condemning the feud and warning that anyone who continues it will face severe punishment. Finally, the play ends with the Capulets and Montagues agreeing to end their feud and erecting statues of Romeo and Juliet as a symbol of their love and sacrifice.

2. What is falling action in romeo and juliet


ANSWER:

The falling action in "Romeo and Juliet" starts to happen in Act III, Scene ii,- after Romeo kills Tybalt. Juliet becomes confused as to what to feel because her new secret husband is now banished from Verona while her beloved cousin is dead by Romeo's hand. This strengthens the pair's assumptions that they are never to be together in a relationship approved by their families.

10. Romeo and juliet climax?, Rising action?​


Answer:

The climax of Romeo and Juliet is when Romeo and Juliet both die in the Capulet tomb, and their families finally reconcile and end their feud.

The rising action includes:

- Romeo and Juliet meeting and falling in love despite their families' feud.

- Romeo and Juliet secretly marrying.

- Tybalt killing Mercutio and Romeo killing Tybalt in revenge.

- Romeo being banished from Verona

- Romeo being banished from Verona - Juliet faking her own death with a potion to avoid marrying Paris.

- Romeo hearing of Juliet's death and rushing back to Verona to kill himself beside her in the Capulet tomb.

12. Romeo and Juliet climax, exposition, rising action, conflict, falling action, resolution


Answer:

Climax: Romeo and Juliet die

Exposition: Romeo and Juliet are from rival families

Rising action: They fall in love and secretly marry

Conflict: Their parents forbid the marriage

Falling action: They take desperate measures to be together

Resolution: They die together, thus ending the feud between their families.
