Answer:

Rules are standard in school and at home. Children have little input in the creation of rules.

Guidance focuses on agreements that are different from rules. Agreements are ideas that groups of people create through trial and error and are in place to provide a baseline that guides our actions.

13. Homeroom Guidance Quarter 1 – Module 2: Discovering the Road to Self-Discipline


Answer:

Self-discipline is the ability to control one's impulses, emotions, and actions to achieve a particular goal or set of goals. It's an essential quality to develop if you want to be successful in any area of life. However, self-discipline is not something that comes naturally to everyone. It requires effort and practice to develop.

Here are some steps you can take to discover the road to self-discipline:

Define your goals: The first step in developing self-discipline is to define your goals clearly. What do you want to achieve, and why is it important to you? Write down your goals and keep them visible to remind you of what you're working towards.

Create a plan: Once you've defined your goals, create a plan of action. Break down your goals into smaller, achievable steps, and create a timeline for completing them. Having a plan will help you stay focused and motivated.

Establish habits: Habits are the building blocks of self-discipline. Identify the habits that will help you achieve your goals and work to establish them. For example, if your goal is to get in shape, establish a habit of exercising every day.

Stay accountable: Accountability is critical to developing self-discipline. Find someone who can hold you accountable for your actions and progress. This could be a friend, family member, or coach.

Practice self-care: Self-discipline requires mental and emotional energy. Taking care of yourself is essential to building and maintaining the energy and focus needed for self-discipline. Make sure to get enough sleep, exercise regularly, and eat a healthy diet.

Celebrate your successes: Celebrating your successes along the way is important to stay motivated and maintain momentum. When you achieve a goal, take the time to acknowledge your hard work and progress.

Developing self-discipline takes time and effort, but the rewards are worth it. With self-discipline, you can achieve your goals and create the life you want. Remember to be patient with yourself, stay focused, and keep moving forward
