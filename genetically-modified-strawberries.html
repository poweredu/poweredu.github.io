Answer:

GENETIC ENGINEERING

---------------------------------------------------------------

METHODS TO MODIFY GENES

1. Mutation of existing genes

2. Replacement of genes ( recombination )

3. Insert new genes

4. Removal of genes

---------------------------------------------------------------

CREATES GENETICALLY MODIFIED ORGANISMS (GMOs)

USE OF GM BACTERIA

To produce hydrocarbons, fuels, plastics, drugs/medication.

USE OF GM PLANTS

To produce disease and insect-resistant crops, edible vaccines, larger crops.

USE OF GM ANIMALS

To track protein production, for disease detection, to produce larger animal as food source.

9. genetically modified organisms examples


Genetically Modified Organisms

Popularly called as GMOs, they are animals, plants or microorganisms that had its DNA altered with the various techniques used in genetic engineering.

While the existence of these GMOs are controversial, they also play a heplful and beneficial role for both humanity and the world:

Examples:

1. Modified soybeans and corns

From being typical vegetables, these crops were genetically altered to have herbicide tolerance and insect resistance. In this way, farmers can conveniently grow them without much trouble. 40% of the total livestock in the United States consisting of soybeans and corn

2. Modified cotton

This kind of cotton can kill microorganisms and pests by producing internal insecticide which does not pose harm to humans. It is an ideal material for out lasting clothing. The use of cotton pesticides around the globe was approximately reduced to 90% after the creation of this cotton.

3. Papaya

The papaya industry was almost wiped out the United States in the late 20th century after the papaya ringspot virus spread like wild fire starting from Hawaii. With that in mind, a genetically engineered papaya, called the rainbow papaya, was developed and designed to resist the virus. Its seeds were distributed commercially to farmers. In just 10 years, 90% of papaya production in the States came from the rainbow papaya roots.

4. Hypoallergenic Cows

To reduce the risk of a number of people getting allergic reactions from milk, a cow named Daisy in New Zealand was genetically engineered to produce milk without the very protein which causes the allergy. This shows how humans can actually alter nutritional content of substances for a healthy cause, although the milk is still not for sale for the public.

5. Insulin

During the 1900s, insulin is being extracted from animals inorder to treat diabetes. However, since animal insulin isn't exactly compatible for humans, it sometimes give off side effects such as rashes. 70 years later, insulin was created from a human protein. From there, it had been more safe using them for medical treatment.

18. what is the modified trait of a genetically modified insulin?​


Answer:

The gene for making insulin is cut from a length of human DNA using restriction enzymes. it is inserted into a plasmid using ligase enzymes. the plasmid goes into a bacterial cell. the transgenic bacterium reproduces, resulting in millions of identical bacteria that produce human insulin.

25. Genetically Modified Organisms are plants, animals, bacteria, or fungi whose ________ have been modified by genetic manipulation.


Answer:

DNA

Explanation:

Genetically Modified Organisms are plants, animals, bacteria, or fungi whose __DNA__ have been modified by genetic manipulation.

The exact definition of a genetically modified organism and what constitutes genetic engineering varies, with the most common being an organism altered in a way that "does not occur naturally by mating and/or natural recombination". A wide variety of organisms have been genetically modified (GM), from animals to plants and microorganisms. Genes have been transferred within the same species, across species (creating transgenic organisms), and even across kingdoms. New genes can be introduced, or endogenous genes can be enhanced, altered, or knocked out.
