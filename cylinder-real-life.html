15. Can you give me some examples of Real-life Volume Questions, Necessary dimensions , Formula for Volume for Square pyramid , Cylinder , Cone , Sphere


Answer:

"A reservoir is shaped like a square prism. If the area of its base is 255 square centimeter, how many liters of water will it hold?"

Answer: 3.375 L

Step-by-step explanation:

For square pyramid:

V = s^3

A = s^2

Given:

A = 255 cm^2

Sol'n:

[tex]s^{2} = A\\ \\s = \sqrt{A} \\\\s = \sqrt{255}\\ \\s = 15 cm[/tex]

Thus, volume is:

V = s^3

V = (15 cm)^3

V = 3375 cm^3

note: 1000 cm^3 = 1 L

[tex]V = 3375 cm^{3} *\frac{1 L}{1000 cm^{3} }[/tex]

V = 3.375 L

28. Cylinders A and B have the same radius but different heights. Which statement correctly compares cylinder A and cylinder B? A. The radius of cylinder B is 4 times the radius of cylinder A. B. The volume of cylinder B is 4 times the volume of cylinder A. C. The volume of cylinder B is 5 times the volume of cylinder A. D. The heights of cylinder A and B are the same.


Answer:

C. The volume of cylinder B is 5 times the volume of cylinder A.

Explanation:

By inspection of the diagram we can see that the radius of both cylinders are the same and the heights are different, so we can automatically discount answer options A and D.

Solve for the volume of cylinders A and B:

ㅤ

FORMULA

V = ⫪r²h

Cylinder A

[tex]\sf{V=3.14(25cm)\times4cm}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V=3.14(100cm)}[/tex]

[tex]\large{\boxed{\sf{V=314^{2}}}}[/tex]

Cylinder B

[tex]\sf{V=3.14(5cm)^{2}\times20cm}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V=3.14(25cm)\times20cm}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V=3.14(500cm)}[/tex]

[tex]\large{\boxed{\sf{V=1570^{2}}}}[/tex]

ㅤ

Divide the volume of cylinder B by the volume of cylinder A.
