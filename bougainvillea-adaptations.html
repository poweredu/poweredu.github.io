15. what is the structure of bougainvillea ​


Answer:

Bougainvillea flowers are trumpet-shaped, white or yellow-white, waxy and less than 1 inch long. They typically develop in a triangularly arranged group of three flowers on a single short stem. Two of these three flowers usually open at the same time, with the third opening slightly later.

20. What is the characteristics of bougainvillea


The leaves are large and ovate, with rippling along the edges and hairs on the underside. The bracts are red, dark pink, or purple, while the small flowers are cream colored. Its thorns are large and may be curved. The growth habit is dense, and the colorful bracts appear up and down the branches.

26. Common parts of bougainvillea


Answer:

Bracts

Bougainvillea blooms come in shades of red, pink, gold, orange and white, but they are not flowers. Instead, they are modified leaves called bracts. The bracts surround the actual flowers, which are tiny, yellow or white, and visually insignificant.
