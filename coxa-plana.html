1. sino si carmen planas?


Siya ang unang naging babaeng nahalal na konsehal ng Maynila.

2. carmen planas achivements on philippines


 Carmen Planas known as "Manila’s Darling", was the first woman to be elected to the Manila City Council, winning a seat in the Capital City Municipal Board by general suffrage.

3. ano ang nagawa ni Carmen Planas



Siya ang pinakauna-unahang babae na nailuklok o naihalal sa konseho ng lungsod ng Manila.

Binansagan siyang bilang "Manila's Darling" at "Manila's Sweetheart".

Nanalo siya sa Spanish Declamation contest.

Gumawa din siya ng undercover work, at gumawad siya ng walang katulad na serbisyo sa mga gerilya (guerillas). Palagi siyang nagdadala ng mga pagkain at iba pang porma ng tulong (aid) sa mga ospital at mga kabahayan ng mga nasugatang mga ex-servicemen.

Matapos ang giyera, nagsilbi siya sa magkakaibang posisyon sa pamahalaan.

Naging gobernador siya at sekretarya ng Philippine red Cross.

Naging Legal Adviser ng Philippine Association of Women Doctors, ng Filipino Youth Symphony Organization, at ganundin ng Women's International League.

Ipinadala siya ng Philippine red Cross bilang nag-iisang delegado sa Convention of red Cross governors sa Oslo, Norway.

Siya rin ang naging delegado ng Philippine Lawyers Association para sa Lawyers International Conference sa Monte Carlo, sa bansang Monaco.

17. ano ang kaugnayan ni carmen planas sa bagong republika?


Carmen Planas was the daughter of Concepcion Lim and Illuminado Planas. Her siblings include Attorney Charito Lim Planas (Former Vice-Mayor of Quezon City), SocialiteAdela Planas-Paterno (former Miss Visayas) & businessman Severino L. Planas. She was a consistent class valedictorian from grade school through college.[1] At the Zaragosa Elementary School she was top pupil in her fourth grade. In the seventh grade she transferred to Collegia de Sta. Rosa where she was also a top student. She attended high school at the Holy Ghost College (now known as the Holy Spirit College).She enrolled in the prelaw course at the University of the Philippines, where she became a scholar. Her oratical and debating ability and zeal earned her gold medals in the U.P. College of Law. Once her debating ability was tested on the issue of women suffrage. She was assigned to take the affirmative side, and advocated it brilliantly. Then she was assigned to argue the negative side on the same issue, she defended it with even more convincing eloquence. This display of rare talent earned her two medals. She also won the Spanish declamation contest.
