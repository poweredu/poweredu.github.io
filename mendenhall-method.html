10. Activity 5: Try It Find the first quartile (Q1), second quartile (Q2), and the third quartile (Q3), given the scores of 10 students in their Mathematics activity using MENDENHALL AND SINCICH METHOD 4 9 7 14 10 8 12 15 6 11


Answer:

The first quartile (Q1) is 7 , the second quartile (Q2) is 9.5 and the third quartile (Q3) is 12.

Finding the quartiles is dividing the set of data into 4 equal parts. The first quartile shows the lower 25% of the set or lower quartile. The second quartile shows 50% of the set or the median. The third quartile shows 75% below of the set or the upper quartile.

Step-by-step explanation:

First is to arrange the data set from smallest to biggest or ascending order. After doing so, we will have:

4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 14, 15

Afterwards, we can solve for the the different quartiles using the following formulas(Mendenhall & Sincich Method):

Lower quartile: Q1 = [tex]\frac{n+1}{4}[/tex]

Median: Q2 = [tex]\frac{n+1}{2}[/tex]

Upper quartile: Q3 = [tex]\frac{3(n+1)}{4}[/tex]

where n is equal to the number of elements in the data set. For our example, we have 10 scores of the students.

Lower Quartile

Q1 = [tex]\frac{10+1}{4}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{11}{4}[/tex] = 2.75 ≈ 3

This means that the third element in the set will be the lower quartile. Therefore, Q1 is 7.

Median

Q2 = [tex]\frac{10+1}{2}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{11}{2}[/tex] = 5.5

This means that the median will be in the middle of the 5th and 6th element/ To get this value, we need to find the average of the elements of the 5th and 6th elements in the set. Putting together 9(5th) and 10(6th) then dividing the sum by two, we will get 9.5. Therefore, Q2 is 9.5.

Upper Quartile

Q3 = [tex]\frac{3(10+1)}{4}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{3(11)}{4}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{33}{4}[/tex] = 8.25 ≈ 8

This means that the eighth element in the set will be the upper quartile. Therefore, Q3 is 12.
