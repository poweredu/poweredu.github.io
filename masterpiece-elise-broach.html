8. what concept music of Für Elise​


Fur Elise is generally considered a bagatelle , a term that translates literally as "a thing of little value." In musical terms, however, a bagatelle is a short piece. Despite its short length, Fur Elise is arguably as recognizable even to casual listeners of classical music, as Beethoven's Fifth and Ninth symphonies.

10. Explain the concept of Elision in poetic verses.​


In poetry, elision is the omission of one or more sounds within a word or phrase. It is a common technique used in poetry to create a particular rhythm or meter, or to fit the words into a specific structure such as a rhyme scheme. Elision is often used in conjunction with other poetic devices, such as assonance and alliteration, to create a pleasing sound and enhance the overall musicality of the poem.

For example, in the phrase "I am going," the word "going" could be elided to "goin'" in order to create a specific rhythm or to fit the word into a rhyme scheme. This technique can be used with any type of word, including nouns, verbs, and adjectives. Elision is particularly common in languages that have many silent letters, such as English, as it allows poets to create more compact and rhythmic lines of verse.

15. What is the setting of fur elise


Answer:

"Für Elise" (German for For Elise) is the common name of the "Bagatelle in A minor", written by Ludwig van Beethoven in 1810. The song is thought to be written for Therese, a woman that Beethoven wanted to marry in 1810, however his handwriting was misspelt undergoing transcription, allowing the piece to be known as Fur Elise rather the Fur Therese. Therese did not want to marry him.

The piece of music, which was written for the piano, was not published until 1865, a long time after Beethoven’s death. It is one of the most well-known pieces of piano music in the world.
