11. What is the difference between elicited and commanded acts ? ​


Answer:

An elicited act is an act that you were "persuaded" to do. The person has a choice whether they want to do it or not.

A commanded act is an act that someone is "ordered" to do. The person doesn't have a choice, they must do it or face consequences.
