4. summary of olorun the creator​


Olorun

Olorun is the most potent and smartest divinity according to Yoruba mythology from West Africa. The all-knowing deity actively participates in both heavenly and terrestrial events. Olorun, the head of the Yoruba pantheon, is also referred to as Olodumare, Olofin-Orun (Lord of Heaven), and Oba-Orun (King of the Sky) (Almighty).

Olorun was supposedly one of the two initial creation gods in Yoruba mythology. The goddess Olokun was the other. The cosmos was first made up merely of the sky and an amorphous mass of marshy water. Olokun governed the huge marshy waters below, while Olorun dominated the sky. There were countless other gods, but none possessed Olorun's level of wisdom or power.

Obatal4 went to Olorun's eldest son, Orunmil4, and enquired as to his next course of action. Obatal4 was instructed by Orunmil4 to gather gold in order to create a chain that could be dropped from the heavens to the ocean below. After the chain was complete, Orunmil4 handed Obatal4 a palm nut, a white hen, a bl4ck cat, and a snail's shell filled with sand. Obatal4 sprinkled the sand over the seas as he lowered himself onto the chain. The hen was then let go, scattering sand every where as it cl4wed at it. Dry ground was created wherever the sand fell. As soon as he set foot on the property, which is referred to as Ife, Obatal4 constructed a home, used palm nuts to grow palm trees, and shared his home with a bl4ck cat.

L4ter, when Obatal4 was lonely, he created cl4y sculptures. Olorun gave these objects life, transforming them into people. Olorun instructed the gods who came to reside on earth to pay attention to the prayers of people and defend them.

Olokun, the water goddess, was not happy with these acts of creation and attempted to flood the country to recl4im the lost territory. But Orunmil4 utilized his abilities to cause the waves to subside. Olokun challenged Olorun to a weaving match to see who was the better skilled weaver in response to his fury about the sky god's son's victory over her.

Olokun was a weaver of-unsurpassed talent and wisdom, but whenever she created a stunning piece of cloth, Agemo the chameleon—who delivered messages for Olorun—changed its skin tone to match the fabric. Olokun conceded defeat and acknowledged Olorun as the greatest deity when she discovered that even Olorun's messenger could replicate her best clothing.
