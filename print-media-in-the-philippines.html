An aged photograph hanging on the wall of the Chinese Embassy library has often interested visitors, which has captured the historic scene of Premier Zhou Enlai and the then Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos signing the Joint Communiqué on the Establishment of Diplomatic Relations between the People's Republic of China and the Republic of the Philippines, when our two countries formally started diplomatic ties 46 years ago.

Ever since the ancient times, many Chinese have come to the Philippines, where they settled down, raised families and made friends, and even fought shoulder to shoulder with local Filipinos against foreign aggression to protect the common homeland. 30. What can you say about the print media and digital media in the Philippines?​


Answer:

The print media and digital media in the Philippines both have their own strengths and weaknesses.

Print media, such as newspapers and magazines, have been the primary source of news and information in the country for decades. They have a large and established readership, especially among older generations who are used to reading physical copies of these publications. Print media also has a sense of credibility and trustworthiness associated with them, as they have been around for a long time and have a reputation for quality journalism.

However, with the rise of digital media, the print media industry has experienced a decline in readership and revenue. Many people now prefer to consume news and information online, where they can access it quickly and easily on their mobile devices. This shift in consumer behavior has forced many print media companies to adapt to the digital age by creating their own online platforms or partnering with digital media companies.

Digital media, which includes news websites, social media platforms, and online video streaming services, has become increasingly popular in the Philippines in recent years. It offers a wider range of content and is more interactive than print media. Digital media also has the ability to reach a larger and more diverse audience, including younger generations who are more tech-savvy and prefer to consume information online.

However, digital media also faces challenges such as fake news and disinformation, which can spread quickly and easily online. It can also be difficult to verify the accuracy and reliability of online sources, and there is a risk of online harassment and bullying.
