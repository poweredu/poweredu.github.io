9. B. How will you store the following: 1. newly laundered towel- 2. newly sterilized cuticle nipper- 3. colored nail polish - 4. unused cling wrap - 5. nail polish remover-​


Answer:

Proper storage of the following materials:

1. newly laundered towel- to properly store this, it should be folded first. After folding, it can be placed inside the drawer with other towel or to a designated storage bin.

2. newly sterilized cuticle nipper- this should be put together with other sterilized nail cleaning tools. It can be stored in a mini organizer.

3. colored nail polish - before storing the nail polish, it should be checked first if the caps are closed tightly to avoid spillage. Then, it can be placed in a pouch together with other colored nail polish that are not directly placed under the sun.

4. unused cling wrap- It should be covered first before putting inside the cupboard/ storage cabinets in the kitchen are.

5.  nail polish remover-​ before storing the nail polish remover, it should be checked first if the caps are closed tightly to avoid spillage.  Then, it can be placed in a pouch together with other nail polish cleaners/removers that are not directly placed under the sun.

26. why do clients hands should be wrapped with plastic film/cling wrap and mittens after applying paraffin wax?​


Answer:

Plastic wrap, cling film, Saran wrap, cling wrap or food wrap is a thin plastic film typically used for sealing food items in containers to keep them fresh over a longer period of time. Plastic wrap, typically sold on rolls in boxes with a cutting edge, clings to many smooth surfaces and can thus remain tight over the opening of a container without adhesive. Common plastic wrap is roughly 0.0005 inches (12.7 μm) thick.[1][2] The trend has been to produce thinner plastic wrap, particularly for household use (where very little stretch is needed), so now the majority of brands on shelves around the world are 8, 9 or 10 μm thick.
