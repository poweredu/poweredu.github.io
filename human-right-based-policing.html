The police and the military are the only organizations responsible for making sure Filipinos have their human rights respected and protected. True or false? Explain​


Answer:

1. False. Every human being has human rights. No nationality or race is deprived from exercising these rights because, everyone is a human being that deserves to exercise these rights.

2. True. Every right has its limitations and everyone should make it to a point that not only his or her rights are being respected but also the people around him or her.

3. False. Even criminals have human rights despite the crimes they have committed because, they are human beings after all. One example of these rights is the rights of the accused.

4. False. People may have the right to freedom of speech, but this right also comes with limitations. Yes, you are allowed to express yourself freely in whatever media you feel the most comfortable in, but you should make sure that the opinions you express does not harm others.

5. False. The police and the military are not the only organizations that ensure the human rights of the Filipinos. One example of another organization is the Commission of Human Rights.


28. explain the statement " human right is at the very core of policing"​


Explanation:

Human rights are the basic rights and freedoms that belong to every person in the world, from birth until death.

They apply regardless of where you are from, what you believe or how you choose to live your life.

They can never be taken away, although they can sometimes be restricted – for example if a person breaks the law, or in the interests of national security.

These basic rights are based on shared values like dignity, fairness, equality, respect and independence.

These values are defined and protected by law.
