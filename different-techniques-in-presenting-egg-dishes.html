21. why is it important to know the different between techniques in cooking egg dishes​


Answer:

Eggs are spectacularly versatile as an ingredient. They contain a tremendous diversity of proteins, more so than meat, and thus can be boiled, poached, fried, scrambled, whipped and baked to produce a tremendous range of textures. Every one of these cooking techniques has its own nuances, and it begins with understanding the structure of an egg. The whites and yolk are made from different kinds of proteins and cook at different temperatures. The whites become translucent first and then opaque as more heat is applied while the yolk sets into a gelatinous texture and then turns crumbly as more heat is applied.

A few things to remember: adding salt to eggs 15 mins ahead of time will yield you a softer and creamier omelette, likewise using frozen butter added straight to the pan gets you a creamier omelette. Eggs will continue to cook well after you take them off the heat, so you will adjust for that appropriately. Poaching is generally considered tricky with the internet recommending a ton of methods from adding vinegar to creating vortices in the water. In my experience, it’s temperature (just short of bubbling) and the freshness and quality of the egg that matters. If the whites are too watery, your poached egg will be messily stringy.
