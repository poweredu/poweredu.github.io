Answer:

contrast

Electromagnetic waves differ from mechanical waves in that they do not require a medium to propagate. This means that electromagnetic waves can travel not only through air and solid materials, but also through the vacuum of space.

compare

They can both be measured by their amplitude, wavelength, and frequency. When they interact with matter, they can be reflected, transmitted, absorbed, refracted, or diffracted.

Waves are disturbances that propagate through space and time.

Mechanical waves are waves that require a medium to travel through, such as sound waves, water waves, and seismic waves.

Electromagnetic waves are waves that do not require a medium and can travel through a vacuum, such as radio waves, microwaves, and light waves.

Three examples of mechanical waves are :

ocean waves, ultrasound waves, and seismic waves

Three examples of electromagnetic waves are :

Radio waves, X-rays, and gamma rays. While waves are a fascinating topic, their potential harm to living organisms cannot be ignored.
