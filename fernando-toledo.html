12. Analyze the poem Even light should be described By joel toledo ​


The poem "Even light should be described" by Joel Toledo is a brief but powerful work that uses language in a unique way to reflect on the nature of perception and observation. The poem can be analyzed in several ways:

Structure and Form:

The poem consists of four short stanzas with varying line lengths. The structure and form of the poem is simple, but the varying line lengths and enjambments give it a feeling of fluidity and movement. The poem is free verse and lacks rhyme, but it has a distinct rhythm that is created by the repetition of certain phrases.

Theme:

The theme of the poem is the importance of observation and description in understanding the world around us. The title of the poem suggests that even something as basic as light needs to be described in order to be fully understood. The poem suggests that we must be attentive to the world around us, that we must observe and describe the things we see in order to truly understand them.

Language and Imagery:

The language of the poem is simple and direct, but it is also highly evocative. The poem is full of sensory details that paint a vivid picture of the world being described. The poet uses metaphor and simile to create powerful images that reinforce the theme of the poem. For example, the line "the light is a car alarm" creates a jarring image that suggests that the light is disruptive and chaotic.

Tone:

The tone of the poem is contemplative and reflective. The poet is not making any specific argument or trying to persuade the reader of anything. Instead, the poem encourages the reader to reflect on their own experiences of observation and perception. The poem is full of wonder and curiosity, suggesting that the act of observation is itself a kind of marvel.

Overall, "Even light should be described" is a poem that uses language in a unique way to explore the importance of observation and description in understanding the world around us. Through its use of metaphor and sensory detail, the poem encourages the reader to pay attention to the world and to engage in the act of observation with a sense of wonder and curiosity.
