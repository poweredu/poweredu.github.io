24. Pahelp po, paki answer po with solutions po sana. Brainlist ko po tayo ty Lee needs P70,000 for her business expansion. She is willing to pay the interest of P9,625 if she borrows the said amount from the bank. If she plans to pay within 30 months, what must be the interest rate of her loan? Valerie wishes to have P22,700 after 2 years and 3 months for her dream vacation. How much should she invest in her account today if the interests rate is 6%?


Answer:

1.  Lee's loan is 4.5%

2. Invest today is 18,813.98

Step-by-step explanation:

Interest = (Principal x Rate x Time) / 100

Given that:

Principal = P70,000

Interest = P9,625

Time = 30 months

9,625 = (70,000 x Rate x 30) / 100

Rate = 9,625 x 100 / (70,000 x 30)

Rate = 9,625 x 100 / 2,100,000

Rate = 0.045 or 4.5%

So the interest rate of Lee's loan is 4.5%

To calculate the amount that Valerie needs to invest today, we can use the formula:

Future Value = Principal x (1 + Rate/n)^(nt)

Given that:

Future Value = P22,700

Time = 2 years and 3 months (converted to 27 months)

Rate = 6%

22,700 = Principal x (1 + 0.06/12)^(12 x 27)

To solve for the principal, we need to divide both sides of the equation by (1 + 0.06/12)^(12 x 27)

Principal = 22,700 / (1 + 0.06/12)^(12 x 27)

So the amount that Valerie needs to invest today is 18,813.98
