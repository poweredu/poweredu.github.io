Hanunuo, as one of the twelve tribes of Mangyan, is know for it's embroidery that is based on the cross shape on the back of their traditional blouses called pakudos. What can be the best characterization of this trademark embellishment of the Hanunuo Mangyan's?​

The Hanunuo Mangyan people of Mindoro in the Philippines employ a pakudos as a visual motif. Pakudos are distinguished by their symmetrical, artistic, and ordered use of lines and space, as well as their equal use of vertical and horizontal composition.[1] The word pakudos is derived from cruz, the Spanish word for cross. The pakudos motif appears frequently in Mangyan embroidery and crafts, as well as on the official seal of San Jose, Occidental Mindoro pakudos. A group of Mangyans living in Southern Mindoro refers to themselves as Hanunuo Mangyans, which means "true," "pure," or "genuine," a term they use to emphasize their strict adherence to ancestral tradition and practices.

21. A tradition poetry of the hanunuo mangyans in mindoro


Answer:

The ambahan is the traditional poetry of the Hanunuo Mangyans of Oriental Mindoro. It is usually written on bamboo in the Surat Mangyan, a centuries-old pre-Spanish script. The syllabic script and the ambahan poetry have complemented each other, contributing to their continued existence today.
