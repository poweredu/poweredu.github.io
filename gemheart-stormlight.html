2. How does the deflection of starlight happens?​


Answer:

Starlight that passes near the Sun's edge on its route to Earth is deflected, changing the appearance of those stars by a little amount. If you look at it from a mile and a quarter away, the amount of deflection is about the width of a dime.

29. A car travelling 60 km/hr took 120 seconds to reach the stoplight, what is the car’s acceleration?


Given:

[tex]Speed \: or \: Velocity (v) \\ = 120 \: \frac{km}{hr} \times \frac{1 \: hr}{3600 \: s} \times \frac{1000 \: m}{1 \: km} \\ = 33 \times \frac{1}{3} \: \frac{m}{s} \: or \: 33.33 \: \frac{m}{s} [/tex]

[tex]time (t)= 120 \: s[/tex]

Unknown:

Acceleration (a)

Formula:

[tex]a = \frac{v}{t} [/tex]

Solution:

[tex]a = \frac{v}{t} = \frac{33.33 \: \frac{m}{s} }{120 \: s} = 0.2778 \: \frac{m}{ {s}^{2} } [/tex]
