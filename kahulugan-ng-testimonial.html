2. Ipaliwanag ang kahulugan ng mga salitang sumusunod:Glittering GeneralitiesName-CallingTestimonialTransferCard Stacking Plain Folks Bandwagon ​


Answer:

Ang mga salitang sumusunod ay tumutukoy sa mga uri ng propaganda:

Glittering Generalities - mga salitang nagbibigay ng positibong kahulugan at bumubuo ng magandang imahe sa produkto o kandidato nang walang konkretong ebidensya o detalye upang suportahan ito. Halimbawa: "makabago," "mahusay," "malinis," atbp.

Name-Calling - pagbibigay ng mga pangalan o tawag na may negatibong konotasyon sa mga kalaban o sa kanilang mga ideya o paninindigan. Halimbawa: "manloloko," "trapo," "mapanlinlang," atbp.

Testimonial - paggamit ng mga personalidad o kilalang tao upang magbigay ng suporta o rekomendasyon sa produkto o kandidato. Halimbawa: "Ang gamit ko ay ang produktong ito at sobrang nakatulong ito sa aking pang-araw-araw na gawain."

Transfer - paglipat ng magandang imahen mula sa isang bagay patungo sa produkto o kandidato. Halimbawa: "Ang ating bayan ay kilala sa mga malinis at tapat na lider, kaya naman iboboto ko si Candidate A dahil siya rin ay malinis at tapat sa kanyang panunungkulan."

Card Stacking - pagpapakita ng mga lamang ng isang bagay at pagkukulang ng kalaban. Halimbawa: "Ang kandidatong ito ay may malinis na rekord sa paglilingkod, hindi tulad ng kalaban nating nakasuhan ng katiwalian."

Plain Folks - pagpapakita na ang isang kandidato ay isa rin sa mga ordinaryong tao at nakaka-relate sa mga pang-araw-araw na karanasan ng mga botante. Halimbawa: "Kandidato A ay isang magsasaka katulad ninyo, kaya alam niya ang mga pangangailangan ng mga magsasaka tulad ninyo."

7. What is Testimonial ​


What is Testimonial?

A testimonial is a statement or recommendation made by a person or an organization expressing their satisfaction with a product, service, or experience. Testimonials often include a description of how the product or service helped them solve a problem or meet a need, and they may be used to promote the product or service to others.

Testimonials can take many forms, including written statements, videos, audio recordings, social media posts, and reviews. They are often used in marketing and advertising to build trust with potential customers and to demonstrate the value of a product or service.

Testimonials can be powerful tools for businesses because they provide social proof that their products or services are effective and valuable. When potential customers see testimonials from satisfied customers, they are more likely to trust the business and to be willing to try the product or service themselves.

11. evaluate propaganda testimonial​


1. •NAME CALLING or STEREOTYPING:  Giving a person or an idea a bad label by using an easy to remember pejorative name.  This is used to make us reject and condemn a person or idea without examining what the label really means.  Examples: "Republican", "Tree-Hugger", "Nazi", "Environmentalist", "Special-Interest Group".

2. •VIRTUE WORDS or GLITTERING GENERALITY: These words are used to dupe us into accepting and approving of things without examining the evidence carefully.  Examples: "Natural", "Democratic", "Organic", "Scientific", "Ecological", "Sustainable".

3. •DEIFICATION:  This is when an idea is made to appear holy, sacred, or very special and therefore above all law.  Any alternative or opposite points of view are thereby given the appearance of treason or blasphemy.  Examples: "God-given right to...", "Mother Earth", "Gia".

4.  •TRANSFER:  Transfer is when a symbol that carries respect, authority, sanction, and prestige is used along with and idea or argument to make it look more acceptable.  Examples: American Flag, University Seal, Medical Association Symbol (or something that looks like it).  This method is also called GUILT- or VIRTUE-BY-ASSOCIATION.

5.  •TESTIMONIAL:  When some respected celebrity (or alternatively someone generally hated) claims that an idea or product is good (or bad).  This technique is used to convince us without examining the facts more carefully.

6.  •PLAIN FOLKS:  This is a way that a speaker convinces an audience that an idea is good because they are the same ideas of the vast majority of people like yourself.  Examples: "This is the will of the People", "Most Americans...".  Another example would be when the speaker tells a story about a family or people that are "just like you" to reinforce the speaker's point of view.

17. What is testimonial propaganda


Answer:

Propaganda is a form of communication used to influence people in regard to a particular position, cause, or agenda. Ways of transmitting propaganda messages include pamphlets, books, movies, radio, television, posters, news reports, and the Internet. Testimonial propaganda is a type of propaganda that is used when someone famous says something good about a certain issue, product, etc. The viewer will say, Ooh, if they like it, then why won't I? It is basically basing their decisions off of someone famous.

Explanation:

Testimonials are quotations, in or out of context, especially cited to support or reject a given policy, action, program, or personality. The reputation or the role (expert, respected public figure, etc.) of the individual giving the statement is exploited. The testimonial places the official sanction of a respected person or authority on a propaganda message. This is done in an effort to cause the target audience to identify itself with the authority or to accept the authority's opinions and beliefs as its own.

30. what is testimony appeal​


Answer:

Testimony is a sworn statement by a witness in a court trial, that is affirmed to be true and correct. It cannot be hearsay- in other words, relayed from someone else's version of events. To appeal testimony is to claim it is not the above- true and correct, seen first hand, or otherwise reliable and unbiased.
