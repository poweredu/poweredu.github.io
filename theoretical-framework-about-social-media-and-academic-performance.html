8. What is the Theoretical framework of the effect of mass media to moral development of grade 11 student​


Answer:

The theoretical framework for studying the effect of mass media on the moral development of grade 11 students can draw upon several theories from different fields, such as psychology, sociology, and media studies. Some of the theoretical frameworks that could be used to investigate this topic include:

Social Learning Theory: This theory suggests that people learn by observing and imitating the behavior of others. In the context of media, this means that students may learn moral values from the characters they see on TV shows, movies, and social media platforms.

Cognitive Development Theory: This theory emphasizes the role of cognitive processes in shaping moral development. It posits that as students mature, they develop increasingly complex ways of understanding moral concepts and making moral judgments. Exposure to media can provide opportunities for students to engage in moral reasoning and reflect on their own values.

Cultivation Theory: This theory suggests that media exposure shapes individuals' perceptions of reality over time. Exposure to media that presents certain values or behaviors as normative can lead individuals to adopt those values or behaviors as their own. In the context of moral development, exposure to media that portrays certain behaviors as acceptable or desirable could influence students' own moral values and decision-making.

Uses and Gratifications Theory: This theory focuses on the ways in which individuals use media to fulfill their own needs and desires. In the context of moral development, students may seek out media that aligns with their own moral values, or they may use media to explore different perspectives on moral issues.

Media Ecology Theory: This theory emphasizes the ways in which media and technology shape social interactions and cultural norms. In the context of moral development, media exposure can influence the cultural values and norms that students are exposed to, potentially shaping their own moral beliefs and behaviors.

These are just a few examples of theoretical frameworks that could be used to study the effect of mass media on the moral development of grade 11 students. The specific framework chosen would depend on the research question and the hypotheses being tested.

14. theoretical framework?​


A theoretical framework is a set of concepts, assumptions, and principles that form a foundation for understanding a particular phenomenon or topic. It provides a way to organize ideas, concepts, and information and helps to explain the relationships between them.

Theoretical frameworks are used in many fields, including social sciences, natural sciences, humanities, and business. They can be used to guide research, develop hypotheses, and interpret findings.

A theoretical framework typically includes a set of assumptions, concepts, and variables that are linked together to form a logical and coherent explanation of a phenomenon. It may draw on existing theories, models, or frameworks, or it may be developed specifically for a particular study or research question.

In summary, a theoretical framework is a conceptual structure that provides a framework for understanding a phenomenon, and it helps researchers to identify key variables, make predictions, and interpret their findings.

17. What is the Theoretical framework of the effect of mass media to moral development of grade 11 student​


Answer:

The theoretical framework for the effect of mass media on moral development of grade 11 students can be based on several theories.

1. Social Learning Theory: This theory suggests that individuals learn from the observations of others in their social environment. In the case of mass media, students may observe and learn from the behavior and values portrayed in media content, which can influence their moral development.

2. Cultivation Theory: This theory suggests that exposure to media content can shape an individual's perception of reality. In the case of mass media, students may develop their moral values based on the messages and themes presented in media content.

3. Cognitive Development Theory: This theory suggests that moral development is a process that occurs through cognitive growth and reasoning. In the case of mass media, students may engage in critical thinking and moral reasoning when processing media content, which can influence their moral development.

4. Uses and Gratification Theory: This theory suggests that individuals use media content to fulfill their personal needs and desires. In the case of mass media, students may seek out media content that aligns with their moral values and beliefs, which can reinforce and strengthen their moral development.

Overall, the theoretical framework for the effect of mass media on moral development of grade 11 students can be based on the interplay of these theories, as students are exposed to media content, process it through cognitive and social learning processes, and use it to fulfill their personal needs and desires.
