6. solving routine and non routine problems involving area of composite figures grade 6​


Answer:

To solve routine and non-routine problems involving area of composite figures, follow these steps:

1. Identify the composite figure: Determine if the figure is composed of simple shapes such as rectangles, triangles, or circles.

2. Break down the figure: Divide the composite figure into simpler shapes. For example, a figure may be divided into rectangles, triangles, or circles.

3. Find the area of each individual shape: Use the appropriate formula to find the area of each individual shape.

4. Add up the areas: Add up the areas of all the simpler shapes to get the total area of the composite figure.

5. Solve the problem: Use the total area of the composite figure to solve the problem.

For example, if the problem asks for the area of a composite figure made up of a rectangle and a triangle, you would use the formula for the area of a rectangle and the formula for the area of a triangle to find the area of each individual shape. Then, you would add those areas together to get the total area of the composite figure. Once you have the total area, you can use it to solve the problem.

Non-routine problems may require creative problem-solving skills, but the basic steps remain the same. It is important to first identify the composition of the figure and then break it down into simpler shapes to facilitate the calculation of the individual areas.
