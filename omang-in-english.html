1. Theme:

Identification: Theme is the central idea or message that the author conveys through a literary work. It is usually an abstract concept that is explored through the characters, plot, setting, or symbolism.

Analysis: To analyze the theme of a literary work, readers must consider how the various elements work together to create meaning. They may examine how the theme is developed, how it contributes to the overall impact of the work, and how it relates to broader issues or themes in society.

2. Narrative Structure:

Identification: Narrative structure refers to the organization and flow of a literary work. It includes elements such as plot, point of view, character development, and pacing.

Analysis: To analyze the narrative structure of a literary work, readers must examine how the story is told and how the various elements work together to create a cohesive whole. They may consider the use of techniques such as foreshadowing, flashbacks, or multiple narrators, and how these contribute to the overall effect of the work.

3. Distinctive Voice, Style , and Intimate Point of View:

Identification: Distinctive voice, style, and intimate point of view refer to the unique way that an author writes and tells a story. It includes elements such as tone, language, syntax, and perspective.

Analysis: To analyze the distinctive voice, style, and intimate point of view of a literary work, readers must consider how the author's writing choices contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine the use of figurative language, dialogue, or sensory details, and how these elements create a particular tone or mood. They may also consider the author's perspective and how it shapes the reader's understanding of the story.

4. Detail and Description:

Identification: Detail and description refer to the specific language and imagery that an author uses to create a vivid and immersive setting or scene.

Analysis: To analyze the use of detail and description in a literary work, readers must consider how the author's language choices contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine the use of sensory details, figurative language, or symbolism, and how these elements create a particular mood or atmosphere. They may also consider how the author's descriptions of characters, settings, or objects contribute to the reader's understanding of the story.

5. Scene and Summary:

Identification: Scene and summary refer to the way that an author tells a story by either depicting events in real time or summarizing them.

Analysis: To analyze the use of scene and summary in a literary work, readers must consider how the author's choices contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine how the use of scene creates a sense of immediacy and vividness, or how the use of summary creates a sense of distance or detachment. They may also consider how the author's use of scene and summary affects the pacing and structure of the story.

6. Poetic Devices- Figurative Language:

Identification: Poetic devices, such as figurative language, refer to the use of language in a literary work to create a particular effect or meaning.

Analysis: To analyze the use of poetic devices in a literary work, readers must consider how the author's language choices contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine the use of metaphors, similes, personification, or other figurative language, and how these elements create a particular tone or mood. They may also consider how the author's use of poetic devices contributes to the reader's understanding of the story.

7. Personal Reflection:

Identification: Personal reflection refers to the way that an author's personal experiences or perspectives are reflected in a literary work.

Analysis: To analyze the use of personal reflection in a literary work, readers must consider how the author's experiences or perspectives contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine how the author's personal experiences shape the themes or characters of the story, or how the author's perspective on a particular issue is reflected in the story. They may also consider how the author's use of personal reflection affects the reader's understanding of the story.

8. Word Choice / Diction:

Identification: Word choice or diction refers to the specific language that an author uses in a literary work.

Analysis: To analyze the use of word choice or diction in a literary work, readers must consider how the author's language choices contribute to the overall effect of the work. They may examine the use of specific words or phrases, and how these elements create a particular tone or mood. They may also consider how the author's use of word choice or diction affects the pacing and structure of the story.
