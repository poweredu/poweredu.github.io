8. A measure of the warmth of an object or substance ?​


Answer:

A measure of the warmth of an object or substance is TEMPERATURE.

Explanation:

Temperature is the measure of the warmth of an object or substance, temperature also means the average kinetic movement of atoms or molecules in a system.

Further readings:

The higher its temperature, the faster its molecular energy hence the more it will do heat transfer. Always remember that heat moves from Hot object to Cold object, not the other way around. It is proven in the zeroth law of thermodynamics, that satiates "No energy transfer will happen between two system if the thermal equilibrium has been achieved."

10. rubbing both hands together for warmth​


Answer:

When you rub your hands together, the action of scraping the surface of your skin back and forth against each other causes the molecules in your skin to move a little faster. The faster that molecules move, the higher the temperature. So the friction of rubbing your hands together makes them feel warmer.
