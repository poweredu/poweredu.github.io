I wonder what lies ahead for me as a literature major after graduation. I already face the real fact that I might be suffering from under employment here in the Philippines and my last option resorts to go abroad, find a stable job, find a nice life and seeing myself settling to a new country, abiding from their new laws and becoming a citizen. After ten years, I’ll go home with my big baggage, big sunglasses, jackets and all. Inside my baggage will be everything that I brought from the country I embrace letting my relatives see how lucky I am and making them feel that going abroad is the only way you can get rich. Isn’t it ironic? I get paid per hour of working, what’s the difference? I earn dollar and I spend dollar. In our conversations, I’ll start comparing everything. That’s the usual scene; even I wonder why they change? I mean my cousins who went to California just because they were petitioned by my aunts and uncles. I saw them change even in the way they speak and not only that I saw it in my own brother. I thought if I go out, will I change?

The essay “Brasing for balikbayan by Conrado de Quiros” gave me the realizations that yes indeed, everything about their attitude changed. In contrast, we the relatives closest to them almost kiss everything about them when they arrive not knowing that we are compared and unenlightened to social realities. The title seems so ironic for me because when we say bracing it means more like making yourself refreshing or invigorating. Therefore, in correlation the word bracing would turn out like the new and refresh balikbayans and so we can tell that there was change giving totally a great significance to the story as a whole for it tells about things that have been change but the representation was made in an irony but the title never some up the main events in the story. The virtue of nationalism change when they embrace a new culture that they boast to have. The author uses contrast of either two different situations or scenarios and after that he would eventually end up putting his own idea in an ironic way that you yourself would eventually open your minds to reality. In my own opinion, the general truth about the essay is that people would somehow change from what they acquire but eventually they would search to what they call home. I like the essay because it opens the minds of people of their difference from what they idolize so much and the way they think of themselves as a Filipino. This is an opening for us in the sense of nationalism and of embracing what we don’t have and be contented. Yes, I agree we are way behind, socially unenlightened to some other aspect and facts but unlike our Caucasian friends, we won’t have remorse after all because we live the way we are, a life full of happiness and time worth of its purpose.
