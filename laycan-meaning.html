12. How will you differentiate the layman's term of work from its scientific meaning?


Answer:

The layman's term for work is described as anything done that exerts effort or energy. It can also be something that has to be done. The scientific meaning of work, on the other hand, is the energy transferred to or from an object as one applies a force that causes the object to move. Thus, work is the product of the amount of force and displacement (W=Fd).

In physics, there are some criteria to be considered for it to be considered as work. These are:

1. force must be applied to the object,

2. the object must have a displacement, and

3. the displacement is caused by the force applied, thus, the direction of the displacement must be a component of the force.

EXPLANATION

To illustrate the scientific definition of work, let us look at this example: When one lifts a box, work is done because force is applied on the object, the box moved (has displacement), and the direction of the displacement (upward) is a component of the force being applied. However, if the person walks across the room while carrying the box, we can say there is no work being done. Force has been exerted as the person lifts the box and there is displacement as the box moves across the room, however, the force did not cause the displacement. The direction of the displacement is not a component of force.

In the example of carrying the box across the room, we can say work has been done in layman's definition of work. It is because there is an energy or effort that has been exerted to carry the box across the room.

Another example to show this is if a person pushes a wall and the wall did not move. In layman's definition, we can say that work has been done since the person exerts effort and energy. In fact, the person may get tired of pushing the wall. However, based on scientific definition, we can say that no work is being done. This is because it did not meet the second and third criteria. The wall did not move, thus, there is no displacement.  

Therefore, it is important to note that for something to be considered work scientifically, it should meet the three criteria mentioned above.
