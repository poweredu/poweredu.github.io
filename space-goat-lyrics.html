25. the ratio of the numver of goats in farm X to the number of goats in farm Y was 9:4. After 35 goats were transferred from farm X to farm Y, there was an equal number of goats in each farm. How many goats were there in each farm at first?


The original number of goats in each farm:
Farm X:  9a
Farm Y:  4a

After transferring goats from Farm X to Farm Y:
Farm X: 9a - 35
Farm Y: 4a + 35
Ratio ⇒  1:1

Equation:

9a - 35 = 4a + 35
9a - 4a = 35 + 35
5a = 70
5a/5 = 70/5
a = 14

Substitute, a = 14:
Original number of goats in each farm:
Farm X:  9x = 9(14) = 126 goats
Farm Y:  4x = 4(14) =  56 goats

ANSWER:  There were 126 goats in Farm X and 56 goats in Farm Y.

Check:
Original ratio:

126/56 = 9/6
9/6 = 9/6  (True)

The new number of goats, equal ratio 1:1:
Farm X: 126-35 = 91 goats
Farm Y: 56 + 35 = 91 goats
