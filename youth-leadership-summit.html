Here are the answers to the questions about independent and dependent clauses.

In English, there are two types of clauses: independent and dependent. Independent clause is the one clause that does need another clause in order to be taken as a complete clause, so in other words it could stand on its own. On the contrary, dependent clauses couldn't, so it requires an additional clause so readers would understand what is meant by the clause. Here are the examples:

Dependent clause because we need continuation of the clause.Independent clause because this clause can be understood clearly just fine.Independent clause due to the fact that it doesn't need additional clauses to explain itself.Independent clause for the same reason above.Independent clause because it can be understood just by reading that clause.

The differences between the two clauses can be tricky, but with enough exercises you will be able to pick them up eventually.

28. difference between youth civic engagement and leadership civic engagement​


Answer:

Youth civic engagement refers to the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities and decision-making processes that affect their communities. This can include volunteering, participating in community projects, or becoming involved in political campaigns.

Leadership civic engagement, on the other hand, refers to the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement. This can include taking on leadership roles in community organizations, running for office, or leading grassroots movements. The focus is more on developing leadership skills and taking initiative in creating positive change in the community.

Explanation:

In summary, youth civic engagement focuses on the participation and involvement of young people in civic activities, while leadership civic engagement focuses on the development and demonstration of leadership skills in the context of civic engagement.
