6. Kinilaw history through the years


Answer:

Kinilaw is native to the Philippines. The Balangay archaeological excavation site in Butuan (dated c. 10th to 13th century AD) has uncovered remains of halved tabon-tabon fruits and fish bones cut in a manner suggesting that they were cubed, thus indicating that the cooking process is at least a thousand years old.

Answer:

=Origins of Kinilaw

Filipino ceviche using vinegar and citrus, kinilaw, actually predates the Spanish arrival. Physical evidence was found in an excavation site dating back to the 10th century. Archaeologists discovered the remains of citrus fruits and fish bones that showed markings of cubed cutting.

30. what spices or condiments needed in tuna to be able to create the Kinilaw. explain​


10 ounces fresh or fresh frozen ahi tuna, cubed

Juice from 3 pieces lemon

6 pieces Thai chili chopped

1/4 teaspoon garlic powder

1/2 teaspoon ginger powder

1/4 teaspoon granulated white sugar

1/4 teaspoon salt

A dash of ground black pepper

Combine lemon juice, salt, garlic powder, ginger powder, and sugar. Mix well.

Place the cubed ahi tuna in a large bowl. Pour-in the lemon mixture and gently toss until all the ingredients are well blended.

Add the chopped chili and ground black pepper. Gently toss. Cover and place inside the fridge for at least 1 hour.

Serve chilled. Share and enjoy!
