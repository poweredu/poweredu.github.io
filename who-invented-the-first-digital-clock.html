1. First clock invented by who?


Answer:

The first clock is believed to have been invented by the ancient Egyptians around 1500 BCE. These early clocks were called sundials and were used to measure the passage of time by tracking the movement of the sun across the sky.

The ancient Greeks and Romans also made significant contributions to the development of clocks. They designed water clocks, which used the flow of water to mark the passage of time, and hourglasses, which used sand to measure the passage of an hour.

It wasn't until the 14th century, however, that the first mechanical clock was invented. This clock was created by a German monk named Johannes de Sacrobosco, and it used a system of weights and gears to power a set of mechanical hands that indicated the time.

3. Who invented the first digital camera?


Answer:

Steven Sasson invented the self-contained digital camera at Eastman Kodak in 1975. It weighed 8 pounds (3.6 kg) and had only 100 × 100 resolution (0.01 megapixels). The image was recorded onto a cassette tape and this process took 23 seconds.

14. When was the first digital adding machine invented?​


Answer:

1642

Explanation:

In 1642, at the age of 18, Pascal invented and build the first digital calculator as a means of helping his father perform tedious tax accounting. Pascal’s father was the tax collector for the township of Rouen.

The device was called Pascal’s calculator or the Pascaline or the Arithmetique. Pascal continued to make improvements to his design through the next decade and built fifty Pascaline machines in total.
