21. Because of beach tourism, businesses like restaurants and specialty shops start opening near the coastline. Would you agree that it is a good idea to allow these structures to be built so close to the shoreline? Explain further.


Allowing businesses like restaurants and specialty shops to be built near the coastline can have both advantages and disadvantages.

On one hand, beach tourism can boost the local economy by creating job opportunities and generating revenue for the community. Businesses like restaurants and specialty shops can provide amenities and services that enhance the visitor experience, making the area more attractive to tourists. This can ultimately lead to increased tourism and a stronger local economy.

On the other hand, allowing structures to be built so close to the shoreline can have negative consequences. Coastal ecosystems are fragile and vulnerable to human activities. Development near the coastline can disrupt the natural flow of water and sand, leading to erosion and the destruction of habitats for marine life. It can also lead to increased pollution from runoff and waste, further damaging the coastal ecosystem.

In addition, the risk of damage and destruction from natural disasters, such as storms and hurricanes, is increased when structures are built close to the shoreline. These structures may also contribute to the loss of public access to the beach, as private businesses may restrict access to certain areas or charge for access.

Therefore, it is important to carefully consider the potential impacts before allowing businesses to be built so close to the shoreline. Proper planning and regulations can help to minimize the negative impacts on the coastal ecosystem, while still allowing for economic benefits from tourism. This may include requiring businesses to use environmentally sustainable practices, limiting the size and number of structures, and preserving public access to the beach.

In conclusion, while beach tourism can bring economic benefits to a community, it is important to weigh the potential negative impacts on the coastal ecosystem and public access to the beach. Proper planning and regulations can help to strike a balance between economic development and environmental preservation.
