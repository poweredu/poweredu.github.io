12. 1. What are the necessary requirements for the teachers and administrators to implement a standards-based curriculum?


Answer:

First — the standards must be rational and stated understandably.

Second — the standards must be possible.

Third — the standards must be aligned with the curriculum.

Fourth — the standards must be known and understood and accepted.

Fifth — administrators must have been teachers; otherwise they are useless and should not be involved.

Sixth — standards must never be used as a tool of evaluation.

Seventh — teachers and administrators must accept that standards based education is of value. This is called the buy-in concept. All the latest data show that buy-in is the single most important issue for a successful program.

20. 1. As a pre-service teacher, how do you plan, manage and implement developmentally sequenced teaching and learning process to meet curriculum requirement in varied teaching context?


PRE-SERVICE TEACHER

A teacher uses a range of teaching methods and materials to educate young minds about a particular subject area, providing knowledge on a specific topic. Teachers put a lot of effort into working full-time, especially in preparing the lessons, administering tests, and providing engaging learning environments to support students' capacity.

The responsibilities are valued depending on the level they are teaching at. A good teacher will support students individually as well as provide lessons in life that would motivate them to strive hard.

HOW TO PLAN, MANAGE, AND IMPLEMENT THE LEARNING PROCESS:Measure and determine what is need to be addressed in the learning process.Take note, not all learners learn the same way some are quick and some are not. But as a teacher, you must set achievable goals for the students to be more motivated.some learners have different learning styles. Thus, as a teacher, you must facilitate the learning process by letting them choose how they will learn. A variety of strategies makes them easy to learn.  Assessment is crucial because it's a window for the learner's progress. Identifying learners' progress tests can be one way of identifying them. Evaluate their capacity and design some interventions in which they are applicable in their comfort. Reflection is an aid for a teacher to indicate his role and assess what are the key ideas for learners to perform well in the class.

Good lessons must be planned. It helps make your lessons clear and time-bounded, meaning students learn actively or they are interested in your topic. However, effective planning includes other built-in flexibility so that teachers can respond to any dilemmas that occur in the student's learning as they teach along the way.

The curriculum itself should be patterned and planned to facilitate learning for the learner. It includes the aims, objectives in teaching content, strategies, and intervention. In short, it is an overall package to care a total package of student's experiences that transpire in the educational process.
