1. Robert runs 30 m north then turns and runs 45 m east. What is Robert distance and displacement? solution in 7 science​


Answer:

Robert's distance is 54.06 m and his displacement is 46.80 m.

Explanation:

Robert's distance is the total length of the path he ran. We can use the Pythagorean theorem to find this distance:

Distance = sqrt((30 m)^2 + (45 m)^2)

Distance = sqrt(900 + 2025)

Distance = sqrt(2925)

Distance = 54.06 m (rounded to two decimal places)

Robert's displacement is the straight-line distance and direction from his starting point to his ending point. We can use basic trigonometry to find the displacement:

Displacement = sqrt((30 m)^2 + (45 m)^2) * sin(theta)

where theta is the angle between Robert's path and the north direction. We can find this angle using trigonometry:

tan(theta) = opposite/adjacent = 30 m / 45 m

theta = arctan(30/45)

theta = 36.87 degrees

Now we can substitute this angle into the equation for displacement:

Displacement = sqrt((30 m)^2 + (45 m)^2) * sin(36.87 degrees)

Displacement = sqrt(2925) * 0.6

Displacement = 46.80 m (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, Robert's distance is 54.06 m and his displacement is 46.80 m.

Answer:

Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance: An Inquiry into Values (ZAMM) is a book by Robert M. Pirsig first published in 1974. It is a work of fictionalized autobiography, and is the first of Pirsig's texts in which he explores his "Metaphysics of Quality".

Explanation:

Pirsig received 126 rejections before an editor finally accepted the book for publication—and he did so thinking it would never generate a profit. It was subsequently featured on best-seller lists for decades, with initial sales of at least 5 million copies worldwide.[2] The title is an apparent play on the title of the 1948 book Zen in the Art of Archery by Eugen Herrigel. In its introduction, Pirsig explains that, despite its title, "it should in no way be associated with that great body of factual information relating to orthodox Zen Buddhist practice. It's not very factual on motorcycles, either."

In a 1974 interview with National Public Radio, Pirsig stated that the book took him four years to write. During two of these years, Pirsig continued working at his job of writing computer manuals. This caused him to fall into an unorthodox schedule, waking up very early and writing Zen from 2 a.m. until 6 a.m., then eating and going to his day job. He would sleep during his lunch break and then go to bed around 6 in the evening. Pirsig joked that his co-workers noticed that he was "a lot less perky" than everyone else

21. 2.)robert needs to part the surface of a cylinder cal tank whose high 24 m What is the area of the surface that he needs to paint.answer correctly ​


Answer:

911.16 square meters of surface

Explaination:

To calculate the surface area of a cylinder, we need to add the areas of its top and bottom circles, and the lateral surface area.

The formula for the lateral surface area of a cylinder is:

Lateral surface area = height x circumference of the base

And the formula for the area of a circle is:

Area of circle = π x radius^2

Since we only have the height of the cylinder, we need to assume that we also have the radius of the cylinder. Let's assume the radius is 5 meters.

So, the circumference of the base is:

Circumference = 2 x π x radius

Circumference = 2 x π x 5

Circumference = 31.42 meters

The lateral surface area is:

Lateral surface area = height x circumference of the base

Lateral surface area = 24 x 31.42

Lateral surface area = 754.08 square meters

The area of each circular base is:

Area of circle = π x radius^2

Area of circle = π x 5^2

Area of circle = 78.54 square meters

Since there are two circular bases, the total area of both bases is:

Total area of both bases = 2 x Area of circle

Total area of both bases = 2 x 78.54

Total area of both bases = 157.08 square meters

Therefore, the total surface area that Robert needs to paint is:

Total surface area = Lateral surface area + Total area of both bases

Total surface area = 754.08 + 157.08

Total surface area = 911.16 square meters

So, Robert needs to paint approximately 911.16 square meters of surface.
