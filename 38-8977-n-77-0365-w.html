8. Given: F1 = 20 N, 25 deg F2 = 35 N, 120 deg. Find the magnitude and direction of the resultant force. scale: 5 N = 1 cm.a. 68.77 N 89.07 degb. 28.77 N 59.07 degc. 38. 77 N 89.07 deg​


The magnitude and direction of the resultant force : c. 38. 77 N ,89.07°

Further explanation

Each vector can be described in two perpendicular vectors  

The first vector is a vector component on the x-axis (Fx) and the second vector is a vector component on the y-axis (Fy)  

Using the principle rules of cosines and sines in triangles then:  

Fx = F cos θ  

Fy = F sin θ  

Known

[tex]\tt F_1=20~N, \theta=25^o\\\\F_2=35~N,\theta=120^o[/tex]

[tex]\tt F_{1x}=20~cos~25=18.126\\\\F_{1y}=20~sin~25=8.452\\\\F_{2x}=35~cos~120=-17.5\\\\F_{2y}=35~sin~120=30.310\\\\\sum F_x=F_{1x}+F_{2x}\\\\\sum F_x=18.126-17.5=\boxed{\bold{0.626}}\\\\\sum F_y=F_{1y}+F_{2y}\\\\\sum F_y=8.452+30.310=\boxed{\bold{38.762}}[/tex]

The resultant force :

[tex]\tt F=\sqrt{F_x^2+F_y^2}\\\\F=\sqrt{0.626^2+38.762^2}\\\\F=\boxed{\bold{38.767~N}}[/tex]

The direction :

[tex]\tt tan~\theta=\dfrac{\sum F_y}{\sum F_x}\\\\tan~\theta=\dfrac{38.762}{0.626}\\\\\theta=\boxed{\bold{89.075}}[/tex]
