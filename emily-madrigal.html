11. Ann has 120 roses, while Emily has 160 roses. How many roses should Emily give to Ann so that Emily will have 3 times less roses than Ann will have?


Answer:

90 roses

Computation:

Given,

[tex]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}Ann:&120&roses\\Emily:&160&roses\end{array}\right]\\\to Total=\:\:280\:\:\:\:roses[/tex]

Determination of the required number of roses for each person.

Let x and 3x represent the number roses that Emily and Ann should have respectively.

[tex]x+3x=280\\4x=280\\x=280/4=70[/tex]

Let n represent the number of roses that Emily should give to Ann so that she will have three times less roses than Ann.

[tex]160-n=70\\-n=70-160\\-n=-90\longrightarrow \boxed{n=90}[/tex]


12. composition of madrigal​


Answer:

A madrigal is a secular vocal music composition of the Renaissance (15th–16th c.) and early Baroque (1600–1750) eras. The polyphonic madrigal is unaccompanied, and the number of voices varies from two to eight, but usually features three to six voices, whilst the metre of the madrigal varied between two or three tercets, followed by one or two couplets. Unlike the verse-repeating strophic forms sung to the same music, most madrigals were through-composed, featuring different music for each stanza of lyrics, whereby the composer expresses the emotions contained in each line and in single words of the poem being sung.

15. Emily and Ella both love reading books. 2/3 of Emily's books is equal to 1/8 of Ella's books. What is the ratio of Emily's to Ella's books? Express your answer in the simplest form


Answer:

Let's use the variable "E" to represent the number of books Emily has, and "A" to represent the number of books Ella has.

According to the problem, we know that:

2/3 E = 1/8 A

We can simplify this equation by multiplying both sides by the least common multiple of the denominators, which is 24.

2/3 E * 24 = 1/8 A * 24

Simplifying:

16E = 3A

Now we can find the ratio of E to A by dividing both sides by 3:

16E/3 = A

So the ratio of Emily's to Ella's books is:

E:A = E/(16E/3) = 3/16

Therefore, the ratio of Emily's to Ella's books is 3:16.
