8. compare and contrast a restaurant review from a movie review


COMPARISON A RESTAURANT REVIEW FROM A MOVIE REVIEW:

The restaurant review consists of foods/drinks, feasibility of dining room and amenities, service and payment method, accessibility and price. While the movie review consists of orientation, interpretation, evaluation and conclusion.

EXPLANATION:

The restaurant review:

1. Food/drink

This aspect describes not only the taste but also the many variations of the menu, matching food/drinks (no-gluten, vegetarian, suitable for children) and plating of food/beverages.

2. Feasibility of dining room and amenities

In food/beverage shops that carry order and go, without chairs, tables and plugs, this aspect alone contains a minus value. In this aspect the explanation is quite a lot, namely narrow or wide, indoor and outdoor, AC and non-AC, high, low or sitting tables, music, wifi and plugs, prayer rooms, toilets, sinks, smoking areas and parking and other aspects that make visitors feel at home.

3. Service and payment method

Aspects of the two can be combined because there are interrelationships. This includes cashier services, waitresses, cooks (as well as cashiers at lower-middle class food/beverage shops), delivery people (if any) and parking guards and payment methods (cash, credit card or barcode scan).

4. Accessibility

This aspect includes access to the location and the way in and out of the location. Seen in the first example, there is a sentence "only the layout must be improved again, because it is confusing with the flow of the road."

5. Price

This aspect describes how worth it the price is and its comparison with the taste of food, service and place. This explains the positive reviews by comparing the price and taste of the food.

The movie review:

1. Orientation

The orientation contains some general information about a film related to the Film Name, year, director, screenwriter, and main actor. Orientation also contains "background" for the reader on what will be reviewed.

2. Interpretation

Contains a summary of the story, contains a detailed description of a work (film) being reviewed, for example parts of the work, uniqueness, excellence, quality, and so on.

3. Evaluation

Contains the views of reviewers regarding the work reviewed. This is done after doing a sufficient interpretation of the results of the work. Contains an assessment related to (strengths) or parts that are less valuable (lack) of a work (films and dramas).

4. Conclusion

Contains conclusions from a review of a work (film and drama). This section also contains the author's comments whether the work is of value/quality or not to be watched/witnessed. Who deserves to watch this movie.

14. 2paragraph 10 sentences about American movies vs Filipino movies using compare ang contrast​


Answer:

American movies and Filipino movies are comparable in that they both tell stories via visual and audio methods. However, there are also some noteworthy differences between the two.

One of the primary differences is the intended audience. American movies are often aimed for a global audience, whereas Filipino movies are typically designed for a domestic audience. This might result in various styles of storytelling and diverse cultural references in the two sorts of films.

Another difference is the funds and resources accessible to the filmmakers. American movies frequently have far larger budgets, allowing for more sophisticated special effects and higher production values. In contrast, Filipino movies may have more low budgets, resulting to a more raw and personal sense to the storyline.

23. Compare and Contrast of Youtubers and Movie actors/Actress ​


Answer:

Youtubers and movie actors/actresses are both in the entertainment industry, but they have distinct differences in terms of their medium, audience, and career paths. Youtubers primarily use the internet and YouTube to share their content and interact with their audience, while movie actors and actresses typically work in the film industry and showcase their talents on the big screen. Youtubers have more control over their content and the distribution of their videos, while movie actors and actresses have to go through a series of auditions and casting processes to get a role. Both have a large audience, but their connection with the audience is different, Youtubers having a more direct and personal connection with their audience.
