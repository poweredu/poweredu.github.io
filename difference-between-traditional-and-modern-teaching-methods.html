4. What is the difference between traditional and modern chinese music?


Answer:

Traditional Chinese music has a history of more than 8,000 years. Music in China was very much a part of the Confucian tradition, which used music for performing rituals. Some instruments dug up from archaeological sites of the Shang Dynasty including panpines, bronze bells and stone chimes date back to the 1766-1027 B.C. By the time that the Han Dynasty was established, the imperial government has created a Music Bureau tasked with collecting and editing the earliest melodies and folk songs. Contacts with Central Asia have led to the growth and development of traditional Chinese music.

Traditional Chinese music emphasizes proper inflection and articulation of each tone. Melody and tone are very important aspects of Traditional Chinese music. Much of Chinese music is based on the five-tone and seven-tone scale. Traditional Chinese musical instruments produce unique sounds that affect the variations in tone quality, beat, rhythm and embellishments that are very different from Western music. Although these instruments can also be classified into percussion, woodwind, plucked strings and bowed strings just like Western musical instruments, their distinct make, the materials used in their creation and the manner in which they are played are what make them different.

Modern Chinese music is similar to modern Western music. Modern Chinese music more frequently employs music lyric to evoke feelings. Like Modern Western music that can be classified according to genre, namely, classical music, country music, pop music, etc., modern Chinese music follows this classification. Variations stem from the uniqueness of the traditional instruments that Chinese music incorporates into modern music. Aside from the difference in language that make a Chinese song distinctive, the utilization of traditional Chinese musical instruments, their incorporation with Western musical instruments and the adoption of western styles and conventions in singing are what make modern Chinese music unique.

8. describe the difference between an traditional and modern societies


Traditional societies are the way communities are ruled by the predominant norms and practices. Whereas, modern societies refer to the present world we are living. Major changes in traditional society were political, economical, and social, which led to revolutionize society, to become a modern society.

Traditional societies and modern societies differ in values, social structures, technologies, and economic systems. Traditional societies prioritize traditional values, cultural practices, and community ties, while modern societies prioritize individualism, personal freedom, and self-expression. Social structures in traditional societies are often rigid and hierarchical, while modern societies prioritize equality and equal rights. Traditional societies use simple technologies and traditional methods, while modern societies have advanced technology and complex systems. Finally, traditional societies rely on subsistence farming and bartering, while modern societies have complex market economies and global trade networks.

26. Distinguish the difference between traditional and modern penology


Answer:

Traditional penology focuses on criminal law and criminality that has emphasized punishing and correcting individual offenders. On the contrary, the new or modern penology adopts an actuarial approach in which specialists assess the risks of specific criminal subpopulations.

Explanation:

Penology, also known as Penal Science, is the division of criminology that concerns the philosophy and practice of society in its efforts to repress criminal activities. As the term implies (from Latin poena, “pain,” or “suffering”), traditional penology stands for the policy of inflicting punishment on the offender as a consequence of his wrongdoing; but it may reasonably be extended to cover other policies, not punitive in character, such as probation, medical treatment, and education, aimed at the cure or rehabilitation of the offender; and this is, in fact, the accepted present sense of the term.

Modern penology has represented a school of doctrine, born of the new humanitarian impulse of the 18th century, with which Voltaire, Jean-Jacques Rousseau, and Montesquieu in France and Jeremy Bentham in England were associated. This, which came afterwards to be known as the classical school, assumed every criminal act to be a deliberate choice determined by a calculation of the prospective pleasures and pains of the act contemplated. All that was needed to overcome the criminal purpose was to provide for each and every crime a penalty adequate to overbalance its assumed advantages. Excessive penalties, like death, were unjust and unnecessary.

To make the mental study of the criminal an essential part of diagnosis, a fact that has given the psychologist and, particularly, the psychiatrist a leading place in the development of modern penological theory. From studies such as these, criminologists discovered that there was no single formula that accounted for all violators of the penal code, while the policy of the individualization of punishment took on the form of individualization of treatment.

28. Difference between modern and traditional literature


Differences Between Traditional and Modern Literature

Traditional and modern literature vary in that the former is more likely to follow a certain style and genre, to have archetypal characters, to ask the reader to suspend disbelief, and to convey a moral message. The terms "traditional" and "modern" literature are interchangeable. Whereas many people must favor reading modern literature, there are also many who advocate for traditional literature. The literary style is distinct in both traditional and modern literature. The first covers more urban and influential topics, while the second is about people and their way of life. There are several sub-categories of traditional and modern literature.

Modern Literature

The modernist epoch, which spanned the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, is referred to as "modern literature." It was during this period that literature was written, discussed, and spoken. It was divided to reveal both prose and verse's meaning. Modern writers are more prone to experimentation and taking chances in order to produce something fresh and fashionable. Their work isn't just about revolution and passion. They've spoken about the environment, the future, art, matter, passion, sorrow, and a variety of other topics. They use their work and imagination to try to persuade others. It's all about teaching people how to communicate with one another and how to stay current in society.

Traditional Literature

The emphasis of classic or traditional literature was on the inner self and vision, with the author's stream of consciousness being the only thing to be found in his or her work. Their practice is more focused on influencing citizens through conflicts, urbanization, and revolt. Since the writing was the best way to clarify the current situation, it was delivered in a rather conversational tone. To describe the storyline, they used characters that were solid and dramatic. Furthermore, the majority of classical literature academics used poetry to express their ideas.

As a general reference, consider the following: In traditional literature, characters are typically straightforward. They are portrayed without nuanced character arcs, making their plight easy to recognize. Characters in modern literature, on the other hand, are frequently more nuanced. Their problems are often so complex that readers have a hard time deciding which decisions the character can make. Readers are frequently unable to determine how a problem can be resolved when characters are immersed in dynamic scenarios. Plots in traditional literature are often simple; this was important because they were often communicated to an illiterate community by an oral culture. Simple plots allowed these tales to be passed down over the centuries. In modern literature, on the other hand, complex plots with several subplots are common. It is no longer possible to keep the story straightforward in a more literate world, and readers are engaged in several disputes that occur at the same time.
