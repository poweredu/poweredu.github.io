1. The EF of ascorbic acid is C₃H₄O₃. Based from an experimental data, the molecular mass of ascorbic acid is 180 g/mole. Determine the molecular formula of ascorbic acid.​


Solution:

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{3}\text{H}_{4}\text{O}_{3})_{n}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

empirical mass = (12 g/mol × 3) + (1 g/mol × 4) + (16 g/mol × 3)

empirical mass = 88 g/mol

Step 3: Divide the molecular mass by the empirical mass.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molecular mass}}{\text{empirical mass}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{180 g/mol}}{\text{88 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 2[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{3}\text{H}_{4}\text{O}_{3})_{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} = \text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{8}\text{O}_{6}}[/tex]

9. Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) contains 40.92% C, 4.58% H, and 54.50% O by mass. What is the empirical formula of ascorbic acid?​


Answer:

empirical formula = C₃H₄O₃

Explanation:

Assuming that the mass of the compound is 100 g

mass C = 40.92 g

mass H = 4.58 g

mass O = 54.50 g

Calculating the number of moles of each element

n C = 40.92 g × (1 mol / 12.0107 g) = 3.40696 mol

n H = 4.58 g × (1 mol / 1.00794 g) = 4.54392 mol

n O = 54.50 g × (1 mol / 15.9994 g) = 3.40638 mol

Determining the empirical formula

Representing the empirical formula

empirical formula = CxHyOz

Dividing the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles

x = 3.40696 mol / 3.40638 mol = 1 × 3 = 3

y = 4.54392 mol / 3.40638 mol = 1.33 × 3 = 4

z = 3.40638 mol / 3.40638 mol = 1 × 3 = 3

Writing the empirical formula

empirical formula = C₃H₄O₃

26. Vitamin C (ascorbic acid) contains 40.92 % C, 4.58% H, 54.50 % O, by mass. The experimentally determined molecular mass is 176 amu. What are the empirical and chemical formulas for the ascorbic acid?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

For empirical formula

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass C} = \text{40.92 g} \\ & \text{mass H} = \text{4.58 g} \\ & \text{mass O} = \text{54.50 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n \: \text{C} = 40.92 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{12.01 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{3.41 mol} \\ & n \: \text{C} = 4.58 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{1.008 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{4.54 mol} \\ & n \: \text{O} = 54.50 \: \cancel{\text{g}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{16.00 \: \cancel{\text{g}}} = \text{3.41 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_x\text{H}_y\text{O}_z[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles to get the subscripts of each element.

Since the smallest number of moles is 3.41 mol, we divide each number of moles by 3.41 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{3.41 mol}}{\text{3.41 mol}} = 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{4.54 mol}}{\text{3.41 mol}} = 1.33 \\ & z = \frac{\text{3.41 mol}}{\text{3.41 mol}} = 1 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Multiply the subscripts obtained by a certain number to become whole number.

Since the decimal part in 1.33 is 0.33, we need to multiply all the subscripts by 3. Therefore,

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = 1 \times 3 = 3 \\ & y = 1.33 \times 3 = 4 \\ & z = 1 \times 3 = 3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_3\text{H}_4\text{O}_3}[/tex]

Therefore, the empirical formula of ascorbic acid is C₃H₄O₃.

--------------------------------------------------------

For molecular formula

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_3\text{H}_4\text{O}_3)_n[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{empirical mass} & = \text{3(12.01 amu) + 4(1.008 amu) + 3(16.00 amu)} \\ & = \text{88.06 amu} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Divide the molecular mass by the empirical mass to find the value of n.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{molecular mass}}{\text{empirical mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{176 amu}}{\text{88.06 amu}} \\ & = 2 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molecular formula} & = (\text{C}_3\text{H}_4\text{O}_3)_2 \\ & = \boxed{\text{C}_6\text{H}_8\text{O}_6} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molecular formula of ascorbic acid is C₆H₈O₆.
