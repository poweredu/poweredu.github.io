13. The following are evidences for continental drift theory EXCEPT: A. Fit of the continents B. Seafloor Spreading C. Evidence from rocks and mountains D. The distribution of ancient fossils


Answer:

B

Explanation:

In the early part of the 20th century, scientists began to put together evidence that the continents could move around on Earth's surface. The evidence for continental drift included the fit of the continents; the distribution of ancient fossils, rocks, and mountain ranges; and the locations of ancient climatic zones

→ You can see the concept map in the image below.

Alfred Wegener

The "Continental Drift Theory," an earlier formulation of plate tectonic theory, was put out by Alfred Wegener. The seven current continents were previously united before separating to form a single land mass known as "Pangaea," in accordance with the continental drift theory. According to this idea, the seven continents are where they are today due to the movement or drift of the crustal plates.

The following evidence is presented to support the continental drift theory:

Jigsaw puzzle: The shape and edge of the continents indicate that they were once united.Evidence from Fossils - Some fossils that are found in South America is also found in Africa, indicating that the two continents were formerly connected.Despite the fact that there is no longer any plant life in Antarctica, coal deposits have been found there, indicating that the continent is not only located far from the south pole but also in a region capable of supporting both plant and animal life.Rocks provide evidence that South American and African rock formations are related.
