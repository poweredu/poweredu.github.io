7. What is the climax of the singa


Answer:

When the prince Sang Nila Utama and his men were sailing and suddenly a storm came to them.

Explanation:

Climax is an element of a story that signal the rising and falling action of the story. It is the point in story where something relevant happens like for example in the story of Singa. The climax happened when they were sailing in the sea and a storm suddenly came across and the only thing that he needs to do is to throw his crown onto the sea to return it to his ancestor who became the Sea-king.  

After climax comes the resolution or the ending of the story. This is the part of the story where mystery have been solved, questions have been answered and finally the end of the story.

11. Ano ang ibig sabihin ng Singa-pura?


Answer:

The English language name Singapore comes from its Malay name Singapura, which is believed to have been derived from Sanskrit meaning "Lion City". Singa comes from the Sanskrit word siṃha (सिंह), which means "lion", and pūra (पुर) means "city" in Sanskrit and is a common suffix in many Indian place names.

14. the story of the singa​


Story of Singa

Once there was a Malay Prince from Srivijaya named Sang Nila Utama. Together with other people they sailed the seas near the Temasik Island. Then a great storm stopped their peaceful sailing, to avoid being in a more crucial condition Sang Nila Utama looked for something that may weigh them down. There he threw his crown overboard, then the storm passed and they were back to their peaceful sailing.

When they landed on the Temasik Island Sang Nila Utama went hunting with the others in the jungle. Being the Temasik a small trading port means that animals still resides freely in it. Then, they spotted a lion in the jungle this became the new name of the Temasik Island when the Malay Prince Sang Nila Utama took over and lead the Island which is now called Singapura or Lion City.

Few facts about Singapura or Lion City:

There was somehow a mistake when the Prince Sang Nila Utama saw a lion when it was indeed a tiger Singapore is an independent state It inflicts much stricter rules than just banning chewing gums They mostly changed their time zones

Singapura befalls in the category of strict and well-developed people and places. It has clean street all over and well-disciplined people. These things is what makes Singapore unique and unlike other countries in the South East Asia.

20. Introduction of the story Singa?


Answer:

This summary is uplifted from the blogspot FOLKLORE LOVER. Here is the summary:

There is an adventurous king named Sang Nila Utama. He was restless by nature and wanted to travel to far away places.

He loves to hunt wild animals. Upon hearing that there were stags in the jungles of Tanjong Bentam, he took him a great fleet of ships to head to the forest.

Upon arriving on the island, he had an expedition which lasted for several hours. He slayed many wild and savage beasts, but no stags. Sang was unsatisfied.

Suddenly, a large stag came out of a bush in front of Sang, shocking him. He drew his dagger and hurled it at the stag, only grazing the animal. The stag ran and Sang followed it.

The stag ran through the jungle and darted up a knoll. Sang followed the stag up the hill. Upon reaching the summit, it was nowhere to be seen. There was a large rock which Sang climed. He saw the land and sea spread out around him. In the distance, he saw a stretch of white sand – an island.

The king was fascinated by the sight of the island. He asked the subjects what it is and the subject replied that it is Temasek.

29. what is the rising action in the singa


Answer:

Explanation:

The King was out for a hunt when the King saw an island. He immediately told his people that they will go to the island.

At first the weather is quite good, there is no sign of any storm but when they are already near the island the clouds started to form, the wind blew stronger and the waves became unsteady.

The storm almost made the ship shrink. Fortunately, the King remembered about the legend about their ancestors who became the King of the sea and the only way to make it through is by throwing his crown into the sea.

After the King throw his crown, the storm subside and they safely reach the island of Singa.
