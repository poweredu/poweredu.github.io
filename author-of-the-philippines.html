3. philippines statistics authority​


Answer: PSA

Explanation:

Answer:

The Philippine Statistics Authority (Filipino: Pangasiwaan ng Estadistika ng Pilipinas), abbreviated as PSA, is the central statistical authority of the Philippine government that collects, compiles, analyzes and publishes statistical information on economic, social, demographic, political affairs and general affairs of the people of the Philippines and enforces the civil registration functions in the country.

Answer: Yes, because if you know many languages, you are able to communicate with other people who have different languages. You are also likely to have access to a wider choice of jobs. 19. what is background of the author in philippines history?​


Answer:

Teodoro A. Agoncillo is considered one of the most important historians of our time. His new brand of historiography did away with conventional ways of writing the history of Philippines—through the eyes of foreigners—and introduced a more Filipino-centric style, seeing the events of the Philippines unfold through the eyes of Filipinos. Today, his works are considered essential to the study of Philippine history, and have also transcended to the realm of classic literature.

In 1985 Agoncillo was included in the roster of the Order of National Scientists by President Ferdinand E. Marcos for his contributions to Philippine History. His notable works include The History of the Filipino People; Malolos: The Crisis of the Republic; The Writings and Trials of Bonifacio; and Revolt of the Masses.

To celebrate his birth centennial, we are publishing two essays from the book History and Culture, Language and Literature: Selected Essays of Teodoro A. Agoncillo edited by Dr. Bernardita Reyes Churchill.

27. background of the author in the 1898 declaration of philippine independence​


Answer:

Ambrosio Rianzares Bautista (December 7, 1830 - December 4, 1903), also known as Don Bosyong, was a lawyer and author of the Declaration of Philippine Independence. A distant relative of the Rizal family, Bautista frequently provided advice to Philippine national hero José Rizal during his school days in Manila.

Other names: Don Bosyong

Known for: Author of the Declaration of Philippine Independence

Born: 7 December 1830; Biñan, Laguna, Captaincy General of the Philippines

Died: 4 December 1903 (aged 72)
