Hunter-gatherer, also called forager, any person who depends primarily on wild foods for subsistence. Until about 12,000 to 11,000 years ago, when agriculture and animal domestication emerged in southwest Asia and in Mesoamerica, all peoples were hunter-gatherers. 6. COMPARE AND CONTRAST THE HUMAN SOCIETIES HUNTING AND FOOD GATHERING SOCIETIES INDUSTRIAL SOCIETIES POST INDUSTRIAL SOCIETIES SUBJECT: UCSP


Hunting and Food Gathering Societies:

These societies were primarily based on hunting, fishing, and gathering food from the surrounding environment.They had a small population and were nomadic in nature, moving from one place to another in search of food and resources.Social organization was usually based on kinship ties, with families and extended families forming the basic social units.Division of labor was based on gender, with men responsible for hunting and women responsible for gathering food and taking care of children.These societies had a simple technology, usually consisting of stone tools and weapons.

Industrial Societies:

These societies emerged with the advent of the Industrial Revolution in the 18th century and were characterized by the development of large-scale production and manufacturing.They had a much larger population and were primarily urban in nature, with people moving from rural areas to cities in search of work.Social organization was based on a complex division of labor and formal institutions like governments and corporations.Division of labor was based on skills and education, with people specializing in different fields and professions.These societies had advanced technology, machinery, and factory systems for production.

Post-Industrial Societies:

These societies emerged in the late 20th century and were characterized by a shift away from manufacturing and towards service-based industries.They are primarily urban and have a high degree of technology and automation in their economies.Social organization is based on knowledge and information, with a highly educated workforce and a focus on innovation and creativity.Division of labor is highly specialized and diverse, with people working in a variety of fields and industries.These societies are characterized by a high degree of globalization and interconnectedness, with a focus on information and communication technologies.

Summary:

In summary, hunting and food gathering societies were small-scale, nomadic, and based on kinship ties, while industrial and post-industrial societies were much larger, urban, and based on a complex division of labor and formal institutions. Industrial societies had advanced technology and machinery, while post-industrial societies have a high degree of automation and a focus on knowledge and information.

16. COMPARE AND CONTRAST THE HUMAN SOCIETIES HUNTING AND FOOD GATHERING SOCIETIES INDUSTRIAL SOCIETIES POST INDUSTRIAL SOCIETIES SUBJECT: UCSP


Answer:

Hunting and Gathering Societies: These societies are also known as “foraging societies” and are typically small-scale societies which rely on hunting wild animals and gathering wild plants for food. These societies are typically mobile, as they need to move from place to place in order to find food. Hunting and gathering societies are the oldest form of human societies, and are found in environments where other forms of subsistence (such as farming and herding) are not possible.

Industrial Societies: These societies are characterized by the widespread use of machines and technology to produce goods and services. Industrial societies are typically large-scale societies with a high level of specialization and division of labor. Industrial societies also tend to be highly urbanized, with large populations living in cities.

Post-Industrial Societies: These societies are characterized by a shift away from the traditional industrial economy. Post-industrial societies rely less on manufacturing, and more on services and knowledge-based industries. Post-industrial societies also tend to have higher levels of education and complex communication networks, as well as greater economic and political stability.
