15. please answer my question its about the beak of the finch​


Beak of the finch

You can separate them according to the shapes of their beaks. Some have longer, pointier beaks whilst others have flat and short beaks. This is because they eat different things: seeds vs worms and insects (their beak changes when the bird developed different taste for worms, insects, seeds, fruits, etc...)

This means that the shape of their beak is an adaptation (it enables their survival) because it makes it easier for them to consume food. This is known as occupying different niches in the habitat, because even if they all live in one area, they will eat different things, meaning that they will always have enough food to survive.

22. Describe the role of variations in the adaptation and speciation of the Galapagos Island finches.


Answer:

Galalpagos Island finches have different shaped beaks which can consume different foods. The more food sources a number of species can tap into, the less scarcity they have to deal with within one--less competition, more food overall...and if that certain genetic trait helps a finch survive, that trait is going to be perpetuated by its offspring.
