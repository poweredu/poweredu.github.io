Answer:

food safety and sanitation are crucial elements for TVL students in SHS. By prioritizing these practices, they can ensure that the food they serve is safe and of high quality, which can lead to success in the food industry and the satisfaction of their customers.

Answer:

Food safety and sanitation are critical components of any food-related industry, including the TVL strand in Senior High School. As students learn the skills required to become future food handlers, it's essential to emphasize the importance of maintaining high standards of food safety and sanitation.

The consequences of failing to maintain food safety and sanitation practices can be severe. Foodborne illnesses can result in serious health issues, and outbreaks can damage a school's reputation, leading to decreased enrollment and revenue.

By ensuring that students in the TVL strand receive education and training on proper food safety and sanitation practices, they can develop habits that will not only protect themselves and others but also help them succeed in the food industry. They will learn how to handle food safely, prevent cross-contamination, maintain proper temperatures, and clean and sanitize kitchen equipment.

Overall, emphasizing food safety and sanitation practices in the TVL strand is crucial to ensure the safety of the students, the quality of the food produced, and the success of the food industry
