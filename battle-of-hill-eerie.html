1. What happened at the Battle of Bunker Hill


It happened on June 15, 1775. It was fought during the early stages of the American Revolutionary War. Over 2,400 American Troops and 3,000 British Troops participated in the battle and it was a Pyrrhic victory for the British.


2. How was losing the Battle of Bunker Hill just as important for the colonists as winning?


√QUESTION√

»How was losing the Battle of Bunker Hill just as important for the colonists as winning?

√ANSWER√

»When news of the battle spread through the colonies, it was reported as a colonial loss, as the ground had been taken by the enemy, and significant casualties were incurred. George Washington, who was on his way to Boston as the new commander of the Continental Army, received news of the battle while in New York City.
