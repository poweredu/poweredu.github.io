A. DIRECTION: Indicate whether the statement is TRUE or FALSE

1. The closer the probablity of an event is to 1, the less likely it is to happen.

→TRUE

2. If the probability is equal to zero, that means it can never happen.

→TRUE

3. The probability of dependent events means that the result of the first event affects the probability of the second event.

→TRUE

4. A tree diagram is a helpful tool to determine the number of outcomes in a sample space.

→TRUE

5. The fundamental counting principle helps determine the number of outcomes.

→TRUE

Explanation:

1. Probability is a number that can be assigned to outcomes and events. It always is greater than or equal to zero, and less than or equal to one.

2. An event with a probability of zero [P(E) = 0] will never occur (an impossible event). An event with a probability of one [P(E) = 1] means the event must occur (a certain event).

3. Dependent events: Two events are dependent when the outcome of the first event influences the outcome of the second event. The probability of two dependent events is the product of the probability of X and the probability of Y AFTER X occurs.

4. A tree diagram is a drawing with "line segments" pointing out all of the different possible "paths" for the outcomes. In addition to helping determine the number of outcomes in a sample space, the tree diagram can be used to determine the probability of individual outcomes within the sample space.

5. Fundamental Counting Principle Definition.

The Fundamental Counting Principle (also called the counting rule) is a way to figure out the number of outcomes in a probability problem. Basically, you multiply the events together to get the total number of outcomes.

D-The fundamental counting principle is a rule used to count the total number of possible outcomes in a situation. It states that if there are n ways of doing something, and m ways of doing another thing after that, then there are n × m n\times m n×m ways to perform both of these actions.
