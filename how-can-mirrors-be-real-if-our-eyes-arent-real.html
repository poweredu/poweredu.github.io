Concave Mirrors

The picture is reversed and, in the center, when the object is far from the mirror. Light truly focuses at the place of the image, therefore it is real.

The image is at infinity when the focal point is that object. The picture behind the mirror becomes virtual and upright as the object travels nearer the mirror inside the focal point.

At first, the virtual picture is much larger and farther away than the actual thing, but as the object gets closer to the mirror, the virtual image becomes closer as well and gets smaller.

Convex Mirrors

Convex mirrors always provide a virtual picture that is situated behind the mirror. The reflection is erect and in the center when the object is far from the mirror.

The picture increases until it is the same height as the object as it moves toward the mirror with the object.

29. A concave mirror can produce a real or a virtual image, depending on the position of the object. Give one difference between a real image and a virtual image.


Explanation:

We all know that a concave mirror can produce a real or a virtual image, depending on the position of the object. 

So, the difference between the real image and virtual image is that:

The virtual image is the ray of light where after the reflection from the mirror the image does not penetrate through the mirror but it get reflected. The reflected rays of light is diverging , it expand as it proceed to the surface. The image appears to be located at the point of apparent divergence. Because the rays never really converge, so this image cannot be projected onto a screen.

The real image, as we all know it is exist in our world. It is formed when the rays of light after reflection or refraction actually meet at some point. Further information that  real image is formed when light rays originating from a point on one side of a lens (the object) are refracted by the lens so that they focus  to a point on the other side of the lens at the image location.
