12. Your city is having a sneaker convention! A pair of Ground Morgans is 20% less expensive than a pair of Elite Butterflies, Elite Butterflies are 30% more expensive than Cloud Trainers, and Cloud Trainers are 50% less expensive than Floating Jumps. What percent of the cost of a pair of Floating Jumps is a pair of Ground Morgans?


Answer:

52%

Step-by-step explanation:

1. Given: Let's first cite the given and cite each of them:

w= a pair of Ground Morgans

x= a pair of Elite Butterflies

y= a pair of Cloud Trainers

z = a pair of Floating Jumps

2. Interpretation: Considering all the conditions of the problem, we will have the following values, (note that the percentage are converted into decimal to simplify terms)

[tex]w=x-0.2x=0.8x\\x=y+0.3y=1.3y\\y=z-0.5z=0.5z[/tex]

3. Problem: What percent of the cost of a pair of Floating Jumps is a pair of Ground Morgans?

4. Solving: The method I will be using will be more simplified. It literally means simplifying z (Floating Jumps) in terms of w (Ground Morgans).

Using all of the data we had from interpretation, we will have;

w=0.8x

w=0.8(1.3y)

w=1.04y, finally substitute y with z

w=1.04(0.5z)

w=0.52z

w is 52% of z

5. Conclusion:

Therefore Ground Morgans is 52% of the cost of Floating Jumps.

28. These are floating plastic lanes marker used to separate the lanes turbulence and serves as a guide for the swimmers to swim in a straight line​


The floating plastic lanes marker you are referring to are commonly known as lane ropes. They are an essential part of swimming competitions and training as they serve as a guide for swimmers to swim in a straight line and separate the lanes to minimize turbulence. Lane ropes are typically made of a series of plastic floats connected by a flexible cable, which allows them to be easily adjusted for different pool sizes and configurations. The floats are usually colored in alternating patterns to help swimmers identify their lane and maintain their course during a race or training session. Overall, lane ropes play an important role in ensuring fair and safe swimming conditions for athletes.
