8. The author of being and nothingness, a book which mostly tackles transcendence


Jean-Paul Sartre

Jean-Paul Charles Aymard Sartre was a French dramatist, novelist, screenwriter, political activist, biographer, and literary critic, as well as a significant character in 20th-century French philosophy and Marxism. He was also one of the most influential personalities in existentialism.

The author of the book Being and Nothingness is Jean-Paul Sartre. He was a French philosopher who impacted literature, sociology, critical theory, and postcolonial theory.

Being and Nothingness continues to define the existentialist "movement," and de Beauvoir's works from the same period.

Nothingness is the fundamental quality of the for itself, according to Sartre. A tree cannot alter or create its being. In contrast, man creates himself via his actions in the world. Instead of merely existing, as the object-in-itself does, man, as an object-for-itself, must act upon his existence.

Some existentialists view Nothingness that impeding the existence of every object. But, given that there is something rather than nothing, any such annihilating force must have been thwarted.
