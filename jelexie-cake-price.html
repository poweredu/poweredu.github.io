6. Belle is planning to buy a cake and a dress as a gift for Alice. The price of the cake is Php40.00 more than half the price of the dress. The total cost of the cake and the dress is Php670.00. How much is the cake?


We know that the total cost of the cake and the dress is Php670.00, so we can set up an equation:

x + (0.5x + 40) = 670

Now, we can solve for x:

1.5x + 40 = 670

1.5x = 630

x = 420

So, the price of the dress is Php420.00.

To find the price of the cake, we can substitute the value of x into our expression for the cake's price:

0.5x + 40 = 0.5(420) + 40

= 210 + 40

= 250

Therefore, the price of the cake is Php250.00.

Answer:

From the problem, we know that the price of the cake is Php40.00 more than half the price of the dress. We can write this as:

y = 0.5x + 40

We also know that the total cost of the cake and the dress is Php670.00. We can write this as:

x + y = 670

Now we can use the first equation to solve for y in terms of x, and substitute it into the second equation to solve for x:

y = 0.5x + 40

x + y = 670

Substituting y = 0.5x + 40 into the second equation:

x + (0.5x + 40) = 670

Combining like terms:

1.5x + 40 = 670

Subtracting 40 from both sides:

1.5x = 630

Dividing both sides by 1.5:

x = 420

So the price of the dress is Php420.00.

To find the price of the cake, we can substitute x = 420 into the equation y = 0.5x + 40:

y = 0.5(420) + 40

y = 210 + 40

y = 250

Therefore, the price of the cake is Php250.00.
