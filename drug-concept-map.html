14. Fill in the 3 concept map on drugs that are commonly used in the philippines and 3 their effects.


Here are three concepts and their corresponding effects that could be included in a concept map on drugs commonly used in the Philippines:

1. Analgesics (painkillers): Analgesics are drugs that are used to relieve pain. Some common examples of analgesics include acetaminophen, ibuprofen, and aspirin. These drugs work by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, which are chemicals that contribute to inflammation and pain.

Effects: Relief of pain, reduced inflammation, reduced fever.

2. Antidepressants: Antidepressants are drugs that are used to treat depression and other mood disorders. Some common examples of antidepressants include selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and tricyclic antidepressants. These drugs work by increasing the levels of neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and norepinephrine, in the brain.

Effects: Improvement of mood, reduction of anxiety, improved sleep.

3. Antibiotics: Antibiotics are drugs that are used to treat bacterial infections. Some common examples of antibiotics include penicillins, tetracyclines, and cephalosporins. These drugs work by inhibiting the growth and reproduction of bacteria or by killing them outright.

Effects: Reduction of bacterial infection, prevention of infection, improved health.

Substance abuse isn't something you should take lightly. It occurs when you use alcohol, prescription medicine, and other legal and illegal substances too much or in the wrong way.

25. Drug Concept Map ADD INFORMATION TO COMPLETE THE MAP​


Six classification of drugsGateway Drugs - legal drugs that a non-drug user might try (ex. cigarette, alcohol)Depressant Drugs - drugs that slow down a person's central nervous system and relax muscles and nerves (ex. alcohol, tranquilizers)Stimulant Drugs - drugs that speed up a person's central nervous system and make a person's energy high (ex. caffeine, cocaine, shabu)Narcotics - drugs that relieve pain and induce sleepiness (ex. heroin, marijuana)Hallucinogens - drugs that distorts reality and facts (ex. psilocybin)Inhalants - drugs that are found in household chemical products and anesthetics (ex. acetone, rugby)
