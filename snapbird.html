Biotic components are all the living things in an ecosystem. They are the animals, the plants and the microorganisms. Biotic components also include the waste from living things and dead organisms.

When people move to the music in a way that has structure and pattern, this is called "dance." It is a composition, which means that the parts are put together in a certain way.

2) (A) Canao

The native mountain people of Northern Luzon celebrate or hold a ceremony called Canao or Kanyaw. As part of a religious ceremony, chickens, pigs, or carabaos are killed and eaten as a sacrifice.

The TAGBANWA people of Palawan believe that three goddesses come down from heaven every 13th moon to bless the planting of rice.

10. In an experiment, a shearwater (a seabird) was taken from its nest, flown 5100 km away, and released. The bird found its way back to its nest 12.4 days after release. If we place the origin at the nest and extend the +x-axis to the release point, what was the bird’s average velocity in m/s (a) for the return flight and (b) for the whole episode, from leaving the nest to returning?


Answer:

     (0,0)                                                       (5100,0)

Origin (Nest)                                           Release Point

Conversion:

[tex]5100 km \times \frac{1000m}{1km}=5,100,000m[/tex]

[tex]12.4days\times\frac{24hours}{1day}\times\frac{60mins}{1hour}\times\frac{60sec}{1min}=1,071,360s[/tex]

a. Since the bird flies from +5100 to 0 (right to left), its velocity will be negative.

[tex]V=\frac{displacement}{time}=-\frac{5,100,000m}{1,071,360s}\\\\V=-4.760 \frac{m}{s}[/tex]

b. Since the total displacement of leaving the nest to returning is 0, there is no velocity.

[tex]V=\frac{0}{1,071,360s}=0[/tex]
