15. What is your reflection (introduction, body and conclusion) of the poem "First inspiration" by Jose rizal​


Answer:

Introduction:

"First Inspiration" is a poem written by the Philippine national hero, Jose Rizal, during his adolescence. The poem reflects his deep passion for writing and his strong desire to express himself through poetry.

Body:

The poem begins with Rizal describing his initial encounter with the world of poetry. He talks about how he was first inspired to write by the beauty of nature and the sound of a bird's song. He also talks about how he was initially hesitant to pursue his passion for poetry, but eventually found the courage to express himself through his writing.

Throughout the poem, Rizal emphasizes the importance of perseverance and determination in achieving one's goals. He encourages the reader to follow their dreams and not be discouraged by obstacles or setbacks. Rizal also highlights the transformative power of art and how it can inspire and uplift the human spirit.

In the final stanza, Rizal expresses his hope that his poetry will inspire others to pursue their own passions and find their own "first inspiration." He also acknowledges that his journey as a poet is just beginning and that he has much to learn and explore.

Conclusion:

"First Inspiration" is a powerful and inspiring poem that reflects Jose Rizal's passion for writing and his belief in the transformative power of art. Through his words, he encourages us to pursue our dreams and not be afraid to express ourselves through our passions. The poem is a testament to the enduring legacy of Rizal as a poet, writer, and national hero of the Philippines.

24. what is the contribution of jose rizal in the philippines that is still present today? how was his works contributed and inspired the filipino youths?


Answer:

what is the contribution of jose rizal in the philippines that is still present today?

— Jose Rizal's contribution to the Philippines that is still present today is his role as a national hero and his advocacy for freedom, education, and nationalism. His writings and ideas inspired the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonialism and continue to influence the country's political and social development. Rizal's values and principles are reflected in the Philippine Constitution, national symbols, and education system. His legacy remains a source of inspiration for many Filipinos today.

how was his works contributed and inspired the filipino youths?

— Jose Rizal's works, particularly his novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," were significant in inspiring and contributing to the Filipino youths' nationalistic consciousness and desire for independence during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonization. Rizal's writings exposed the injustices and abuses committed by the Spanish colonial government and clergy, and advocated for reforms and social equality. His works inspired Filipinos to unite and fight for their rights, and continue to serve as a source of inspiration for the Filipino youth to this day.
