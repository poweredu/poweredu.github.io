15. research about the different elements used in a musical play contributes in communicating meaning​


Answer:

Story line, Setting, Score, Flow, Format

Explanation:

Musical play or theater is a combination of song, dance, action and spoken dialogue to express and communicate story to its audience.

To be able to do this, here are the basic elements of a musical play:

1. Story line. This contains the plot, theme or idea, dialogues and characters. This is commonly called the Script which is considered the heart of the theatrical play.

2. Score. This contains the lyrics and the music that will be played in the musical.

3. Flow. This is where story-build up happens. First Act, Middle Act, Final Act.

4. Format. This is where how many acts should comprise the play is decided.

5. Setting. This includes the background design, props, costumes, accessories.

23. true or falseMusic of Mindanao Doe's not have symbolic meanings to the community​


Answer:

False

Explanation:

The music of Mindanao in the Philippines often holds symbolic meaning for its communities. Mindanao is home to a diverse array of ethnic groups, each with their own unique musical traditions and cultural heritage. The music is often used to convey important messages, celebrate cultural heritage, and to express emotions and experiences.

For example, the kulintang music, a traditional ensemble music that originated from the Maranao people in Mindanao, is considered to be a cultural symbol of the Maranao community. The music is performed during important social and religious events and is also used to mark milestones in the lives of individuals and communities.

Similarly, the various indigenous music of the Lumad people of Mindanao, such as the B’laan and T’boli, are considered to be vital cultural expressions of their communities, embodying the history, traditions, and values of these groups.

Overall, the music of Mindanao plays a significant role in the cultural and social lives of its communities, providing a powerful means of expression and a deep connection to their heritage and identity.
