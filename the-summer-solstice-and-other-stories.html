27. what is your opinion about the story the summer solstice and the reason for liking it or disliking it?​


"The Summer Solstice" is a short story written by Filipino author Nick Joaquin. The story explores the cultural and social dynamics of Filipino families during the summer solstice festival, which originated from the pagan beliefs of celebrating the longest day of the year. The story revolves around the matriarchal figure of the Manangu, who performs a ritual dance with her male grandchildren to appease the god of fertility.

One of the reasons for liking the story is its ability to depict the complexity of Filipino culture and tradition. The story shows how modernization and Westernization have affected the Filipino way of life, yet the essence of the culture still remains through its beliefs and practices.

Another reason is its narrative style, which engages the reader with vivid descriptions and symbolism. The use of contrasting images, such as the dark and light, and the symbols of the snakes and lizards, provides a deeper layer of meaning to the story.

On the other hand, some readers may dislike the story for its portrayal of gender roles and stereotypes. The story highlights the patriarchal mindset of the male characters and their attitudes towards women, especially towards the Manangu, who is both respected and feared.

In conclusion, the story "The Summer Solstice" is a compelling piece of literature that presents a snapshot of Filipino culture and tradition. It has its strengths and weaknesses, and its appreciation can depend on one's personal and cultural background.

29. Describe the difference between the summer and winter solstice ​


Answer:

The summer solstice, also known as estival solstice or midsummer, occurs when one of the Earth's poles (North pole) has its maximum tilt toward the Sun. The winter solstice, or hibernal solstice occurs when one of the Earth's poles (North Pole) has its maximum tilt away from the Sun.

| SUMMER AND WINTER SOLSTICE |

The summer solstice, also known as estival solstice or midsummer, occurs when one of the Earth's poles (North pole) has its maximum tilt toward the Sun.

The winter solstice, or hibernal solstice occurs when one of the Earth's poles (North Pole) has its maximum tilt away from the Sun.

Winter solstice is the shortest day of the year (falls in December every year) while the Summer solstice is the longest day of the year (falls in June every year).
