1. A trapezoid has bases b1=9 inches and b2= 3inches and height h = 4inches.


AREA OF TRAPEZOID

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

A trapezoid has bases b₁=9 inches and b₂= 3 inches and height h = 4 inches.

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

The area of a trapezoid can be found using the formula

[tex]\boxed{A = \dfrac{1}{2} (b_1 + b_2) h}[/tex]

Where:

[tex]{b_1 \rm{(The \: 1st \: base \: of \: the \: trapezoid/ The \; wider\: base) = 9 \: inches}}[/tex][tex]{b_2 \rm{(The \: 2nd \: base \: of \: the \: trapezoid/ The \; smaller\: base) = 3\: inches}}[/tex][tex]{h \rm{(The \: height\: of \: the \: trapezoid) = 4 \: inches}}[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the formula, then solve for area:

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (b_1 + b_2) h[/tex]

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (9 + 3) (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (12) (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = 6 (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = \green{24}[/tex]

Hence, the area of the trapezoid is 24 inches.

17. How many triangles that has a base and height of 1 inch can you fit in a 1inch by a 6-inch rectangle​


Answer:

2 triangles

Step-by-step explanation:

First, find the area of the rectangle. The area of the rectangle can be determined by multiplying the width and length, so 6 x 1 = 6in^2

then determine the area of the triangle so (b x h)/2, which will be equivalent to 3in^2.

divide the area of the rectangle by the area of the triangle. (6/3) which will be equivalent to 2! 2 triangles can fit in the rectangle.

30. 1. SSS-3inches-4-inches-5inches congruence triangles ​


Answer:

Pythagorean Triple.

Step-by-step explanation:

Any triangle whose sides are in the ratio 3:4:5 is known as Pythagorean Triple. so this means if you multiply a constant number to the ratio, the resulting triangle or sides measurement will be congruent.

e.g. 3:4:5 if multiply by 5, then ratio becomes 15:20:25 which is also a Pythagorean Triple.
