1. Sina Padre Burgos, Padre Gomez, at Padre Zamora ay tinaguriang____?


Answer:

Gomburza

Explanation:

Gomburza, alternatively stylized as GOMBURZA or GomBurZa, refers to three Filipino Catholic priests (Mariano Gómez, José Burgos, and Jacinto Zamora) who were executed by garrote on 17 February 1872 in Bagumbayan, Philippines by Spanish colonial authorities on charges of subversion arising from the 1872 Cavite mutiny. The name is a portmanteau of the priests' surnames.

17. paboritong laro sa baraha ni padre jacinto zamora


Answer:

Jacinto Zamora y del Rosario (14 August 1835 – 17 February 1872) was a Filipino Catholic priest, part of the Gomburza, a trio of priests who were falsely accused of mutiny by the Spanish colonial authorities in the Philippines in the 19th century

Zamora had the custom of joining the other priests after their parochial duties in their common pastime of playing card games, particularly manilla and panguingue, from time to time. He was fond of gambling, which was his great passion. he had also a liking for fiesta, the tertulia, and the cockpit. He always invite friends and neighbors whenever he met them to a card party in his house on Fraternidad street. One day Zamora received an invitation from his friend, Fr.Pedro Duran, of the parish of San Anton to play a game of cards with a big group of individuals in a Sampaloc convent. The invitation was worded thus: "Grand reunion. Come without fail. Our frinds will will be there and will be provided with bullets (bala) and powder. The affair was set for January 20, 1872, the day of fiesta of San Anton. Coincidentally, the Cavite Mutiny broke the same day as the fiesta of San Anton."
