7. How to fix overheating portable mini table fan? (step by step)


Answer:

1. Unplug the fan: Before attempting to fix the overheating issue, unplug the portable mini table fan from its power source.

2. Clean the fan blades: Overheating can be caused by a build-up of dust and debris on the fan blades. Use a soft cloth or a brush to gently clean the blades, taking care not to damage them.

3. Check the motor: Overheating can also be caused by a faulty motor. Check to see if the motor is working properly by turning the fan on and listening for any unusual sounds or vibrations.

4. Oil the motor: If the motor is making unusual sounds, it may be due to a lack of lubrication. Use a few drops of lubricating oil to lubricate the motor.

5. Check the bearings: Overheating can also be caused by worn-out bearings. Check the bearings by rotating the blades with your hand. If the blades do not rotate smoothly, it may be time to replace the bearings.

6. Replace the motor: If the motor is faulty and cannot be fixed by oiling or replacing the bearings, it may be time to replace the motor entirely. Contact the manufacturer or a professional technician for help with this.

7. Check the power source: Finally, if the fan is still overheating after performing the above steps, check the power source. Make sure that the voltage and amperage are correct for the fan and that the power cord is not damaged.

14. how to maintain portable electrical drill​


Answer:

1.Using a standard metal lubricating oil, spread the oil on the inside of the chuck where the drill bits would go (make sure the chuck teeth are completely open).

2.Tighten and loosen the chuck three to four times to evenly spread the oil throughout the inside of the chuck.

3.Using a rag or shop cloth, apply the oil to the outer surface of the chuck. This will ensure oil is spread thoroughly between the chuck and the hand grip.

4.If the chuck is keyed, follow the above steps with the addition of lubricating the vital teeth around the outside of the chuck. This will reduce wear on the teeth of the core and the chuck.
