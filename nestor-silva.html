Given:

Kitty has 2 sisters. Nestor has double that number of sisters. Juana has double Nestor's number of sisters. How can you find the number of sisters Juana has?

Solution:

Let:

K = number of Kitty's sisters

N = number of Nestor's sisters

J = number of Juana's sisters

K = 2

N = 2(2) = 4

J = 2N = 2(4) = 8

Juana has 8 sisters.

30. If the Silva family has 6 children and the Oliveira family has 4, what is the probability that all the Silva children will be girls and all the Oliveira children will be boys? a) 1/512 b) 1/682 c) 1/921 d) 1/1754 e) 1/1024​


✒️[tex]\large{\mathcal{ANSWER}}[/tex]

[tex]======================[/tex]

The probability that all Silva children are girls and all Oliveira children are boys is 1/1024,

➪Alternative E.

The chances of any couple having a boy or girl is a fil 50%, represented as a fraction is 1/2.

[tex]------------------[/tex]

The probability that all of the Silva family's children are girls is;

[tex]( \frac{1}{2} )^{6} = \frac{1}{64} [/tex]

The probability that all the children of the Oliveira family are boys is;

[tex]( \frac{1}{2} ) ^{4} = \frac{1}{16} [/tex]

Therefore, the probability that all children of the Silva family are girls and all children of the Oliveira family are boys is:

[tex] \frac{1}{64} \times \frac{1}{16} = \frac{1}{1024} [/tex]

Therefore, the correct alternative is letter E [tex]\frac{1}{1024} [/tex]
