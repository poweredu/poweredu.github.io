2. How long do we prescribe antibiotics for children after extraction of teeth


Dentists routinely administer antibiotics to children at the time of the extraction as a precaution to stop infection from starting in the first place. This might not be necessary and could have unintended consequences.

A medical operation called tooth extraction leaves a wound in the mouth that may contract an infection. Infection can cause swelling, discomfort, pus formation, fever, and "dry socket" (where the tooth socket is not filled by a blood clot, and there is severe pain and bad odour). These unpleasant side effects can make it difficult for patients to eat, speak, or clean their teeth, and they may even force them to miss days of work or school. Infections may usually be treated easily by draining the infection from the incision and giving the patient antibiotics.

The bacteria that cause infections are either eliminated by antibiotics or have their growth slowed. Some infections do, however, resolve on their own. Unnecessary use of antibiotics may prevent them from working successfully in the future. Antimicrobial resistance is a global issue that is getting worse. Additionally, antibiotics may have undesirable side effects like nausea and diarrhea. Antibiotics may cause adverse reactions in some people, and they may interact poorly with other medications.

Braces are dental tools that help correct problems with your teeth, like crowding, crooked teeth, or teeth that are out of alignment. Many people get braces when they’re teenagers, but adults get them too. As you wear them, braces slowly straighten and align your teeth so you have a normal bite. Some people get braces to adjust their smile.

If you have crooked teeth and/or a misaligned bite (an underbite or overbite), there are a variety of treatments that can help straighten teeth, including braces and retainers, custom-made, removable or fixed tools that cover the outside of your teeth and help keep them in position.

Many general dentists do basic alignment and treat other tooth problems, but orthodontists specialize in correcting issues with your teeth.
