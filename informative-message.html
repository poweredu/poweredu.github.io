16. The source of the information or message​


Sources of information are all things that can be used by someone so that they know about new things, and have characteristics, namely, (1) can be seen, read, and studied, (2) researched, studied and analyzed (3) utilized and developed in activities - educational, research, laboratory activities, (4) are transformed to other people. According to some opinions, learning resources have meanings, namely; Learning resources include anything that can be used to help everyone learn and display their competence, learning resources include messages, people, materials, tools, techniques, and settings.

Sources of information have a very large influence on learning resources. Sources of information provide everything that is useful as a means to learn everything that might be new. The completeness and correctness of information greatly affects learning outcomes, because information is the main source of learning, besides that completeness of information can facilitate the implementation of the learning process so that the expected amount of information, knowledge, experience, and skills will be obtained. Given the very vital influence of information sources on learning resources, filtering information is the main thing that must be done, this is expected to prevent misplaced information sources for learners, due to the different learning needs of each individual.

Types of Information Sources

The following are the types of information sources, namely:

1. Visual

Is a source of information that can be seen by the sense of sight, can be in the form of writing and pictures.

Example: books, journals, papers

2. Audio

Is a source of information that can only be obtained through the sense of hearing, because it is only in the form of sound.

Example: Radio

3. Audiovisual

Is a source of information that can be obtained both through the senses of sight and hearing.

Example: television, experts/experts, cell phones, internet

Conveying a message to other people clearly might sound simple, but it's a process that is often fraught with error.

Many people struggle to communicate effectively – verbally and in writing. They impart thoughts and ideas that don't reflect their intended meaning, and that their recipients misinterpret or misunderstand.
