20. if the width of diorama is represented as x and length as x + 3, find the widths and lengths of the diorama if the given area is 108 square inches.


A diorama is a rectangle. Area of a rectangle is:

Area = Length x Width

108 = (x)(x+3)

x^2 + 3x = 108

x^2 + 3x -108 =0

(x+12)(x-9)=0

x = -12 (width cannot be negative, so ignore this)

x = 9 in (this is the width)

Length is x + 3

Length = 9+3 = 12 in

check: length x width = 12 x 9 = 108 sq in

1. Remove the Wire

The first step will be to cut the wire into multiple evenly sized sections.

The size will be determined by the size of your tree.

Anything between 13 and 15 inches, though, should suffice.

You'll need 15 to 20 wire pieces to build a single tree.

2. Wires should be bundled together.

Once you've cut all of the wire pieces needed for one tree, securely and evenly bundle them together.

Next, fold the wire bundle in half and twist the bottom of the result.
