30. neyceptsA sustainable environment is well-balanced and rich in naturalresources. This means that the environment can provide the needs of theorganisms living there and can still provide these resources to futuregenerations.However, the environment can only support a limited population. Thisis referred to as the environment's carrying capacity. What are humanactivities that can affect an environment's carrying capacity? What mayhappen when the ecosystem has reached its maximum carrying capacity?Maintaining balance is important for the environment to become lifesustaining. Earth's resources are classified into two categories: renewable andnonrenewable.Renewable resources are those that can be used readily and producedor regenerated repeatedly (in one or two human lifetime) by nature's recyclingprocess and sound management practices of human beings. The air webreathe, the freshwater we drink, the light from the sun, the plants andanimals that we use as food are all examples of renewable resources. They areinfinite or inexhaustible when used and managed properly.Nonrenewable resources are those that have finite existence, that is,they cannot be replaced or regenerated easily (in one or two human lifetime);or the rate by which the regeneration process occur is so slow. Fossil fuels -coals, oil and natural gas and minerals from the soil are examples ofnonrenewable resources.​


What are human activities that can affect an environment's carrying capacity?

While food and water supply, habitat space, and competition with other species are some of the limiting factors affecting the carrying capacity of a given environment, in human populations, other variables such as sanitation, diseases, and medical care are also at play.

What may happen when the ecosystem has reached its maximum carrying capacity?

If a population exceeds carrying capacity, the ecosystem may become unsuitable for the species to survive. If the population exceeds the carrying capacity for a long period of time, resources may be completely depleted. Populations may die off if all of the resources are exhausted.
