7. 36. How is the plant cell similar to an animal cell? a. Both have nuclei, chloroplast and cytoplast b. Both have cell wall, nuclei and cell membrane c. Both have nuclei, cytoplasm and cell membrane d. Both have cell wall, chloroplast and cell membrane 37. What part of the plant cell controls substances from entering and leaving the cell?​


36. The answer is (c) Both have nuclei, cytoplasm, and cell membrane.

Both plant and animal cells share some basic structures such as a nucleus, cytoplasm, and cell membrane. The nucleus is the control center of the cell that contains genetic material, and the cytoplasm is a jelly-like substance that contains organelles, such as mitochondria, that are involved in various cellular functions. The cell membrane, also known as the plasma membrane, is a thin, flexible layer that encloses the cell and regulates the movement of substances in and out of the cell.

However, plant cells also have some unique features such as chloroplasts and a cell wall that are not present in animal cells. Chloroplasts are organelles that are responsible for photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert sunlight into energy. The cell wall is a rigid structure that provides support and protection to the cell.

37. The cell membrane, is also called the plasma membrane.

The cell membrane controls substances from entering and leaving the plant cell. The cell membrane is a selectively permeable membrane that regulates the movement of substances in and out of the cell. It allows some substances to pass through freely, while it prevents others from passing through at all, or only allows them to pass through with the help of specific transport proteins. This selective permeability is essential for maintaining the internal environment of the plant cell and for allowing the cell to carry out its functions properly.

Answer:

36.C-Structurally, plant and animal cells are very similar because they are both eukaryotic cells. They both contain membrane-bound organelles such as the nucleus, mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, golgi apparatus, lysosomes, and peroxisomes. Both also contain similar membranes, cytosol, and cytoskeletal elements

37.The cell membrane-, also called the plasma membrane, is found in all cells and separates the interior of the cell from the outside environment. The cell membrane consists of a lipid bilayer that is semipermeable. The cell membrane regulates the transport of materials entering and exiting the cell.
