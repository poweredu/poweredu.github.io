The school is situated in a far-flung area and 95% of the students belong to the indigenous people. With the students in mind, apply what you have learned in Educ 223 course based on the specific questions below. 1. What should you consider in teaching students in this community? (10 Points) 2. What teaching strategies should you employ to ensure inclusive education in this group? Explain 2 strategies from your answer. (10 points) 3. Should you consider as well the assessment, learning resources, and instructional processes? Why? (10 Points)​


Answer:

1. When teaching students in this community, it is important to consider the unique backgrounds and experiences of the students, as well as their needs and the conditions of the community. Moreover, it is essential to create a safe and supportive environment that is inclusive and respectful of the different cultures, languages, and beliefs. Furthermore, teachers should also consider the availability and access of resources, such as books and instructional materials, as this is often a challenge in far-flung areas.

2. To ensure inclusive education in the group, teachers should employ teaching strategies that support learning for all students. Firstly, teachers should use differentiated instruction and ensure that instructional materials are tailored to the students' unique backgrounds and experiences. In this way, teachers can meet the specific needs of each student. Secondly, teachers should also promote peer-to-peer learning, in which students work together to complete tasks and help one another understand challenging content. This encourages social cohesion and respect for diversity among students.

3. Yes, teachers should consider the assessment, learning resources, and instructional processes when teaching students in this community. The assessment should be developmentally appropriate and fair for all students and should be adjusted to students’ diverse backgrounds and experiences. Furthermore, learning resources should be carefully selected to meet the individual needs of the students and allow them to learn effectively. Lastly, instructional processes should be engaging and interactive, allowing students to be actively involved in the learning process.

Answer:

Indigenous peoples, also referred to as First people, Aboriginal people, Native people, or autochthonous people (from the Ancient Greek αὐτός autós meaning self/own, and χθών chthon meaning Earth), are ethnic groups who are native to a particular place. Indigenous first emerged as a way for Europeans to differentiate enslaved black people from the indigenous peoples of the Americas, being first used in its modern context in 1646 by Sir Thomas Browne, who stated "Although... there be... swarms of Negroes serving the Spaniard, yet they were all transported from Africa... and are not indigenous or proper natives of America.

Peoples are usually described as Indigenous when they maintain traditions or other aspects of an early culture that is associated with a given region. Not all Indigenous peoples share this characteristic, as many have adopted substantial elements of a colonizing culture, such as dress, religion, or language. Indigenous peoples may be settled in a given region (sedentary) or exhibit a nomadic lifestyle across a large territory, but they are generally historically associated with a specific territory on which they depend. Indigenous societies are found in every inhabited climate zone and continent of the world except Antarctica. It is estimated that there are approximately five thousand Indigenous nations throughout the world.
