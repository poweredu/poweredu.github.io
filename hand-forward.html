Answer:

The bump

Explanation:

Dig or ‘Bump’

The bump is the basic pass in volleyball. It is used when receiving a serve or reacting to an opponent’s attack.

The player should be holding his arms away from the body at a 90-degree angle with his hands together.

Knees should be bent. The ball comes in contact with the lower forearms just above the wrist. After contact,

the arms should point toward the target. Over-swinging the arms is a common mistake. The first bump of a

volley should be aimed at a setter so they can "set" up the volleyball for the spike.


27. Which is not an example of an appropriate action reaction force?A. When the car brakes, the body is pushed forwardB. A person who swims forward, pushing the water backwards with his handsC. When the hand pushes against the wall, the hand hurtsD. When the ball is thrown against the wall, the ball will bounce back​


[tex] \huge{ \rm{Answer:}}[/tex]

which is not an example of an appropriate action reaction force is C. When your hand pushes against a wall, your hand hurts.

[tex] \huge{ \rm{Explanation:}}[/tex]

The law of action and reaction is based on Newton's 3rd law, namely:

"When an object is given a force, the object exerts a force equal in magnitude and opposite in direction to the applied force."

~In conclusion, if an object is given a force, it will return or give a force in the opposite direction.

A.

Option A. includes reaction action, because when the car is braked (backward force) the body will be pushed forward (forward force).

B.

Option B. includes reaction action, as the swimmer's hand will push back (backward force) and exert a forward force (forward move).

C.

Option C. does not include reaction action, because the sore hand has nothing to do with the opposing force.

D.

Option D. includes reaction action, because the ball pushed forward (forward force) will bounce back towards us (applying a backward force).
