15. 5 similarities and 5differences of jose rizal and modern filipino heroes​


Jose Rizal was a hero like any other. For some reason, he was automatically the most popular among them. Jose Rizal was well-known. America did not force him to become a national hero. He was already the most popular of the ilustrados as late as the 1900s, so they just made it official.

How he is similar to other hero:

1. Andres Bonifacio and Apolinario Mabini were members of Jose Rizal's La Liga Filipina. La Liga Filipina, led by Marcelo Del Pilar, is the Philippine equivalent of La Solidaridad in Europe.

2. Los Cuerpos de Compromisarios was created by conservative factions of La Liga Filipina led by Apolinario Mabini to organize financing for La Solidaridad's reform drive in Europe. The Katipunan was created by radical forces led by Andres Bonifacio to achieve independence through war.

3. Emilio Aguinaldo was a Katipunan member.

4. All of this began in 1872 with Father Burgos' creole insurgencies. Fathers Gomez and Zamora were executed alongside him.

5. Rizal was never intended to be the greatest. He had no intention of being any different from the other revolutionaries. He was simply the most popular of them all.

How he was different:

1) He was a well-known illustrator. He was a doctor with a high level of education. 2) He was a novelist and a writer. 3) He never fought, though. And 4) he was never a member of the Katipunan, the group that initiated the insurrection against Spain.

In addition, 5) most Filipino heroes gained their titles by fighting genuine battles against foreign invaders. That was not done by Jose Rizal. He supported for liberty by peaceful methods rather than through violent revolution, primarily through political changes. He also opposed Philippine independence. I believe he thought the Philippines weren't ready to rule themselves at the time.
