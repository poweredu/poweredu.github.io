1. determine the weight of a 2007 honda accord with a mass of 1,450,000 grams


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

Determine the weight of a 2007 honda accord with a mass of 1,450,000 grams.

Given Data:

m (mass) = 1,450,000 g

g (gravity) = 9.8 m/s²

w (weight) = ? unknown

Solution:

We will convert first the mass in grams (g) to kilogram (kg).

Conversion factor (1 kg = 1000 g)

1,450,000 g × 1 kg/1000 g = 1,450 kg

To solve for weight, we can use the formula w = mg since weight = force:

w = mg

   = 1,450 kg × 9.8 m/s²

   =  14, 210 kg.m/s²

   = 14, 210 N

Answer:

weight is equal to 14, 210 kg.m/s²

Weight is the same as force and the unit for both weight and force is Newton (N). Weight on earth may not be different on other planet since weight on Earth depends on the force of gravity on Earth (9.8 m/s²) which is different from other planet.

17. Which of the following is NOT a capital component when calculating the weighted average cost of capital (WACC) for use in capital budgeting? a. Accounts payable. b. Common stock "raised" by reinvesting earnings. c. Common stock raised by new issues. d. Preferred stock. e. Long-term debt.


Weighted Average Capital Cost

The expense of each financial institution (debt and equity) is multiplied by the appropriate weight by market value, and the products are then added to get the total cost of capital, or WACC. The discount rate for potential future cash flows in discounted cash flow analysis is also WACC.

a. When calculating the WACC for capital budgeting, accounts payable is excluded

Accounts payable is the amount of money a business owes its suppliers, whereas accounts receivable is the amount of money owing to the business, generally by clients. When two businesses deal on credit, one of them records an entry to accounts payable while the other documents an entry to collections receivable in their books.

The calculation of WACC includes preferred stock, long-term debt, common stock "raised" through fresh issuance, and common stock "raised" through the reinvestment of earnings.

The expense of each financial institution (debt and equity) is multiplied by the appropriate weight, and the products are then added together to determine the WACC. In the formula above, E/V stands for the percentage of equity-based financing, and D/V for the percentage of debt-based financing.

28. The realized return on a stock portfolio is the weighted average of the expected returns on the stocks in the portfolio.


False, the realized return on a stock portofolio is the weighted average of the expected returns on the stocks portofolio.

Explanation :

The realized return on a stock portfolio and the expected return on a stock portfolio are two different things that are not interrelated. The difference between the two lies in the time at which transactions in one's portfolio take place.

Realized returns refer to returns achieved in the past, and expected returns refer to returns that are anticipated over future periods. The required return is the minimum expected rate of return an investor would need to invest in an asset over a given period, given the risk of the asset.
