Explanation:

The Cultural Center of the Philippines (or CCP) is located in Metro Manila, the Philippines, and was opened in 1969 to promote and preserve Filipino arts and culture. It hopes to become the mecca of culture and the arts in Asia.

3. the main theater of the cultural center of the Philippines and hall of the up college of music are named after him


Answer:

Nicanor Abelardo

Explanation:

The main theater of the Cultural Center of the Philippines is named after him, and it is called the "Tanghalang Nicanor Abelardo." Meanwhile, the concert hall of the UP College of Music is also named after him, and it is called the "Abelardo Hall." Nicanor Abelardo was a renowned composer and musician who is known for his contributions to the development of Filipino classical music. Some of his most famous works include "Mutya ng Pasig," "Nasaan Ka Irog," and "Magbalik Ka Hirang."

The main theater of the Cultural Center of the Philippines and the Hall of the University of the Philippines College of Music are named after National Artist for Music, Dr. Felipe Padilla de Leon. He is a renowned composer and conductor who has contributed significantly to the development of music in the Philippines. Some of his notable works include the opera Noli Me Tangere and the Mass for Peace. De Leon received numerous awards and recognitions for his outstanding contributions to Philippine music and culture, including the National Artist Award in 1997.
