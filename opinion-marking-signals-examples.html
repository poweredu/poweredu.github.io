What are opinion marking signals?

Words or phrases that convey views regarding something are known as opinion-marking signals. They are valuable cues in the shape of words and phrases that help us recognize opinion assertions.

Words that serve as opinion marking signals are those that start an opinionated sentence. When you want to share your personal opinion, you use them. Or if you want to make it clear that this is just your opinion and not a fact or a statement made by an authority figure.

Anything you say before expressing your opinion can serve as an opinion marking signal. Even if it isn't formally recognized as such, it is a marking signal as long as it conveys your need to communicate your opinion.

It is rather simple to recognize opinion marking signals. One is that they do it immediately after offering opinions, but that is too evident. They also start phrases, which is another bonus.

Understanding the idea of opinions will help you recognize these signs. There is no way to establish that an opinion is a fact. It's simply one person's way of thinking. Opinion marking signals are actually useful for indicating that the next line will only be a mere opinion and not a reality.
