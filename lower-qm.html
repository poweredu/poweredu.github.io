11. Quarter 3: Week 7 Activity 1: Name Me! Directions: Identify the parts of a circle. 1. O 2. OP 3. QM 4. MN 5. QN​


Here are the answers1. Center/Centre2. Radius3. Chord4. Diameter5. Segment

Explanation

A CIRCLE is a closed plane figure made up of points which are the same distance from a point called CENTER.

A DIAMETER is a line segment that passes through the center of the circle, and its endpoints lie on the circle.

A RADIUS is a line segment from the center to any point on the circle. Its length in one-half the length of the diameter, and this length is also called the radius of the circle.

A CHORD is a line segment whose endpoints are point on the circle.

An ARC of a circle is a part of a circle consisting of two endpoints and all the points on the circle between these endpoints. An arc is classified as major and minor.

A MINOR ARC is an arc whose degree measure is less than 180˚.

A MAJOR ARC is an arc whose degree measure is more than 180˚.

A CENTRAL ANGLE is an angle whose vertex is the center of the circle.

An INSCRIBED ANGLE is an angle whose vertices a point on the circle and those sides are chords of the circle.

A TANGENT LINE is a line that intersects the circle at exactly one point. This point is called the POINT OF TANGENCY. The tangent line is perpendicular to a radius at the point of tangency.

A SECANT LINE is a line that intersects the circle at two points.
