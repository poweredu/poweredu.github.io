7. Ms. Claro went to a glass shop to buy tempered glass for her two square tabletops. One is 10 cm wider than the other. If the sales attendant estimated that Ms. Claro would need 1.81 sqm of glass, what is the dimension of her smaller tabletop in cm?


Answer: 90x90 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: We let "x" as the measure of the side of the smaller tabletop. Since larger tabletop's side measures 10 cm wider, it can be represented as "x+10".

smaller tabletop: x

larger tabletop: x+10

Since, the formula for finding the area of square is: side x side, we can let:

area of smaller tabletop: x²

area of larger tabletop: (x+10)²

Then, the total area is 1.81 square meters. We need to convert it first to square centimeters because the units used in the sides of two tabletops is expressed in centimeters. Using the conversion factor:

1 square meter is equal to 10,000 square centimeters

So, 1.81 sq. meters multiplied by 10,000 is equivalent to 18,100 sq. cm.

Formulating the equation:

x² + (x+10)² = 18,100 (Expand the equation)

x² + (x²+20x+100) = 18,100 (Transpose 18,100 to the other side of equation)

2x² + 20x + 100 – 18,100 = 0

2x² + 20x – 18,000 = 0 (Divide the whole equation to 2 to simplify)

x² + 10x – 9,000 = 0

Using quadratic formula to solve for x:

x = [–b±√(b²+4ac)]/2a

where:

a = 1

b = 10

c = –9,000

Substitute the values of a, b and c to the quadratic formula.

x = [–10±√(10²+4(1)(–9,000)]/2(1)

x = [–10±√(100+36,000)]/2

x = [–10±√36,100]/2

x = [–10±190]/2

x1 = [–10+190]/2 x2 = [–10–190]/2

x1 = 180/2 x2 = –200/2

x1 = 90 x2 = –100

Since we have two values of x, we need to consider the positive value of x because the real-life measurement can

never be negative. So x = 90.

Therefore, the dimension of the smaller tabletop is 90x90 cm.

29. What is the index of refraction of the tempered glass if the speed of light has changed from 3.0x108 m/s to 1.84x108  m/s?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \: \text{m/s} \\ & v = 1.84 \times 10^8 \: \text{m/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the index of refraction.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{c}{v} \\ & = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8 \: \text{m/s}}{1.84 \times 10^8 \: \text{m/s}} \\ & = \boxed{1.63} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the index of refraction of tempered glass is 1.63.


30. What rock-forming mineral is found in tempered glass? in ceramic glass? in marble toys? in river rocks?​


Answer:

Marble is a metamorphic rock formed when limestone is subjected to high pressure or heat. In its pure form, marble is a white stone with a crystalline and sugary appearance, consisting of calcium carbonate (CaCO3). Usually, marble contains other minerals, including quartz, graphite, pyrite, and iron oxides. These minerals can give marble a pink, brown, gray, green, or variegated coloration. While true marble forms from limestone, there is also dolomitic marble, which forms when dolomite [CaMg(CO3)2] undergoes metamorphosis.
