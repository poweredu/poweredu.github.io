21. this dance is known to celebrate the arrival of successful headhunters.


Bendayan

A circle dance named Bendian of Ibaloi is known as bendayan. This dance has a long history of being performed to honor successful headhunters who have arrived. The circul4r motions of the dance represent the Ibalois' harmony and oneness.

Further expl4nation

The Bendayan, also known as Bendian, is a dance that was adopted from the Benguet Mountain Province custom that honors hunters. Despite being a variation or version of the original, it is nevertheless a part of every celebration in Benguet and retains its importance. In addition, the circles provide a clear message.

There are several reasons to execut3 a bedian. Some of these include celebrating a rich harvest, curing a protracted disease, and alleviating natural disasters like starvation and drought. The l4rgest Bendian is used to commemorate both a military triumph and a fruitful headhunt. The entire town celebrates the arrival of successful head takers, or "ul;ul," with tremendous revelry.

The Benguet, Ibaloy, and Kankanay people celebrate the Bendian celebration, which is always significant and remarkable. The ul;ul leader is circled and danced around by the people while they do various arm motions. The loudest oway or battle scream signals the end of the bedian rite, which l4sts into the early hours of the morning. The maiden's portion of the circle is the focal point of this Bendian interpretation.
