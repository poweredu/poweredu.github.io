1. Seryu’s age is 10 less than twice the age of Hak. How old is Hak if the sum of their ages is 62?


✏️AGES

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

Seryu’s age is 10 less than twice the age of Hak. How old is Hak if the sum of their ages is 62?

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\Large\rm» \:\: \green{Hak \: is \: 24 \: years \: old}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

- Represent [tex]a[/tex] and [tex]b[/tex] as the ages of Seryu and Hak respectively. Formulated equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10& \red{(eq. \: 1)} \\ a + b = 62& \red{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute a from the first equation to the second equation in terms of b which is the age of Hak.

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\(2b - 10) + b = 62 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\2b - 10 + b = 62 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\3b - 10 = 62 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\3b = 62 + 10 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\3b = 72 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\ \begin{gathered} \frac{ \cancel3b}{ \cancel3} = \frac{72}{3} \end{gathered} \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}a = 2b - 10 \\b = 24 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] Hak is 24 years old.

22. Differences between Li Po and Tu Fu/ Li bai and Du fu poets


Answer:

With the unique aspects and images these poets write about, they distinguish the similarities between themselves and contain different intensities in their poetry. While Li Po has a more relaxed tone to his poetry, Tu Fu deals with the serious aspects of life such as war, poverty, and suffering.

Answer:During the Tang Dynasty, Li Po and Tu Fu have reigned the literary world with their poetry. Their writing techniques and themes in their poetry allow them to stand out amongst other poets at the time. With the unique aspects and images these poets write about, they distinguish the similarities between themselves and contain different intensities in their poetry. While Li Po has a more relaxed tone to his poetry, Tu Fu deals with the serious aspects of life such as war, poverty, and suffering.

Li Po's writing style is conventional and contains no new innovations. Much of his poetry contains older styles such as lu-shih, chueh-chu, and especially the ku-shih. Li Po also tends to look upon the past more than the future. In his poem, " At Su Terrace Viewing the Past", Li Po states, "Old gardens, a ruined terrace, willow trees new…and now there is only the west river moon, that shone once on a lady in the palace of the king of Wu." Throughout this poem, Li Po shows a longing for the past, the longing of that lady who lived in that palace. Now he is looking back upon the past and the wonderful experiences he had.  Although Li Po suffered through exile and complicated political connections, he rarely expresses his grief through his poetry. Usually, his poetry contains the happier aspects of life and avoids being too personal and less revealing on his inner emotions.
