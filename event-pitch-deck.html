9. One card is chosen at random from a deck of a 52 cards. Find the event the number on the chosen is divisible by 3


Answer:

The probability of choosing a card from a deck of cards which is divisible by 3 is 12 out of 52 or  [tex]\frac{12}{52}[/tex]  or [tex]\frac{3}{13}[/tex].

Step-by-step explanation:

In a regular deck of cards, there are 13 possible cards (i.e.2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10,Jack,Queen, King and Ace) having 4 different suits (i.e. clovers, clubs, hearts, diamonds) that can be picked.

Among the different cards, only 3,6, and 9 are divisible by 3. We also need to take note that for each kind of card, there are 4 possible suits it can appear as. Therefore, the possible events that a card which is divisible by 3 can be drawn from the deck is 12.

Since there are a total of 52 cards, the probability of this event from happenings is 3 is 12 out of 52 or  [tex]\frac{12}{52}[/tex]  or [tex]\frac{3}{13}[/tex].
