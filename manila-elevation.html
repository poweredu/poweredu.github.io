2. How does the elevation of manila affects its climate?​


The elevation of Manila affects its climate in a number of ways. Manila is located near sea level, with an elevation of only around 2 meters above sea level. This means that Manila has a tropical climate that is influenced by its proximity to the ocean. The warm, moist air from the ocean brings rainfall to the city, making it relatively humid.

However, the low elevation of Manila also makes it susceptible to flooding, especially during typhoons or heavy rainfall events. Additionally, the city's location on the western coast of Luzon Island puts it in the path of typhoons that frequently affect the Philippines during the rainy season, which can bring strong winds, heavy rains, and storm surges that can cause significant damage and loss of life.

Overall, the low elevation of Manila and its location on the coast make it vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, including sea-level rise and more frequent and severe weather events.

The elevation of Manila, being a coastal city, has a significant impact on its climate. The city has an elevation of approximately 2 meters above sea level, making it highly vulnerable to the effects of sea-level rise and storm surges. The combination of its low elevation and its location in a tropical region results in a warm and humid climate with an average temperature of 27 degrees Celsius.

The city's low elevation also makes it prone to flooding, especially during the rainy season. Heavy rainfall can cause rivers to overflow, resulting in inundation of low-lying areas. In recent years, climate change has exacerbated the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events such as typhoons, floods, and landslides, posing a significant threat to the city's infrastructure, economy, and population.

In summary, Manila's low elevation, combined with its tropical location and rapid urbanization, results in a warm and humid climate that is vulnerable to flooding and extreme weather events. The city's climate situation poses a significant threat to the health, safety, and well-being of its population, highlighting the urgent need for climate adaptation and mitigation measures.

12. A building has two elevators P and Q. The time needed for elevator P to rise one floor is (3x+8) seconds. Elevator Q is (x-4) seconds faster than elevator P. How long will elevator Q to reach 3rd floor?​


Answer:

(12x+12) seconds

Step-by-step explanation:

Elevator Q = Elevator P + (x-4)

Elevator Q = (3x+8) + (x-4)

Elevator Q = 4x+4

Now , Elevator Q will took 4x+4 seconds to reach one floor. So , if it reaches the third floor , we will just multiply it by 3

Time = 3 foors × Elevator Q

Time = 3 × (4x+4)

Time = 12x+12

Therefore , the time reaches the 3rd floor is (12x+12) seconds
