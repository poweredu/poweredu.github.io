11. A space shuttle weighs 15,000 N on Earth,what will be the equivalent weight of thespace shuttle on the surface of the moon?​


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

A space shuttle weighs 15,000 N on Earth,  what will be the equivalent weight of the  space shuttle on the surface of the moon?​

Note:

We must first find the mass of the space shuttle on Earth before we can solve its weight  on the surface of the moon.

Moon has a gravity of 1.62 m/s².

Given Data: ( mass on Earth )

a ( acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

F ( Force ) = 15,000 N or 15,000  kg·m/s²

m ( mass ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using the Formula F = m × a, and to find m, we use m = F ÷ a .

m = F ÷ a

   = 15,000  kg·m/s² ÷ 9.8 m/s²

   = 1,530.61 kg

SOLVING FOR WEIGHT ON MOON

Given Data:

a ( acceleration ) = 1.62 m/s²

m ( mass ) = 1,530.61 kg

F ( Force ) = ? unknown

Solution:

F = m × a

   = 1,530.61 kg ×  1.62 m/s²

   = 2,479.59 kg·m/s² or 2,479.59 N

Answer:

The equivalent weight of the  space shuttle on the surface of the moon is 2,479.59 kg·m/s² or 2,479.59 N.

15. How do space shuttles land


At First the shuttle is at the orbit of an altitude of more than 200 mile or (322 km). The crew members and others will begin to configure and control the onboard computers for the Entry, with others such as the hydraulic system that will power the shuttle's “aerosurfaces" and its wing, flaps and the rudder. Two hours before landing the astronauts change their orange spacesuits while they strap and held themselves into their seats. After an hour, the shuttle cruises into position at 15 miles which equivalent of 282 km above the Earth. At the soaring speed of 16,465 mph or 26,498 kph, it undergoes several process of deorbit burns which lit like a falling fireball, and flip around with the firing of engines which is against its direction of motion towards the Earth, and slow the pace of atmosphere reentry. 30 minutes, the shuttle itself hits the atmosphere at an altitude of 76 miles or 122 km, the engines will be off for reentry and rely on its steering jets and later on to its aerosurfaces that control the airflow to the shuttle. The shuttle then performs a four steep banks, which then rolls over as much as 80° to one side, but despite the effort to decrease the deceleration, it was still faster than the speed of sound. At exactly 5 minutes, it will slow past at the speed of 768 minutes and drag its parachute and the shuttle land safely.

The Space Shuttle was a partially reusable low Earth orbital spacecraft system operated from 1981 to 2011 by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) as part of the Space Shuttle program. Its official program name was Space Transportation System (STS), taken from a 1969 plan for a system of reusable spacecraft where it was the only item funded for development.[4] The first of four orbital test flights occurred in 1981, leading to operational flights beginning in 1982.
