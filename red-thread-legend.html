6. What is the "red threadrunning through God'screation, according to no.1? What does this tell aboutGod's plan of creation?​


Answer:

The answer is God's love.

Explanation:

What does the red thread running through God's creation signify?

In the Bible, there is only one instance where a red or scarlet thread was mentioned. It can be found in the second chapter of the book of Joshua.

Two Israelites were sent by Joshua to the city of Jericho to spy on the land and the people. These two men were somehow found out and the king ordered to have them captured and possibly killed.

Rahab, a beautiful woman who sells her body, heard about the two Israelites. She gathered the two and let them hide out at her house. She helped them escaped by tying a red thread or rope down her window and directed them to hide out in the mountains for at least three days.

In some ancient cities, selling sexual services was a noble profession because sex was part of worship and was a service sought by people who were respected. We can deduce that Rahab is among those kinds of women because she had her own home and was privy to news coming from other kingdoms. She has heard of the God of Israelites who have saved them many times from slavery, from enemies, from hunger and death.  

Even if Rahab was not an Israelite, she wanted to serve the One and True God Jehovah. By helping the Israelites spies, she was given mercy and was ordered to keep the red thread on her window and gather her family in her home. The red thread was to be taken as a sign that righteous and God-fearing people live in that home.

So when the Israelites marched to Jericho and blew their horns, the walls of the tower of Jericho miraculously collapsed but leaving the home of Rahab safe and standing. Rahab and her family was saved.

That is why when the red thread runs through, it means that God's love and mercy will save you.
