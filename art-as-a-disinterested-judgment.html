29. evaluative judgment and nonevaluative judgment​


Explanation:

Evaluative judgments involve making a value judgment or evaluation about something, while nonevaluative judgments are simply statements of fact or descriptions of something without any evaluation or value judgment. Evaluative judgments involve determining the worth, quality, or value of something, while nonevaluative judgments do not.
