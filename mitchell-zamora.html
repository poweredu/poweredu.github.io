23. Mitchell took a math exam today. If he got 63 out of 84 questions correct, what percentage did Mitchell get wrong? (answer using a percent sign) A) 20% B) 21% C) 25% D) 63%


Answer:

C. 25%

Step-by-step explanation:

Given that:

84 questions 63 questions were answered correctly

find:

a. the number of incorrect answers

b. rate of the incorrect answers

Solution:

a. In finding the number of incorrect answers, subtract the number of questions answered correctly to the number of questions.

     thus, 84 - 63= 21

     therefore, 21 is the number of incorrect answers.

b. to get the percentage of 21 incorrect answers over the number of questions which is 84, derive first the formula in finding the rate from the formula of finding the percentage.

      Percentage formula is P= BxR

      Since you are going to find as to how much percent is 21 in 84, you are going to use the formula R= P/B.

     Why is this now the formula?

     because, P= 21, B= 84, and R=?

     Next, substitute the terms in the formula

         R= P/B

         R= 21/84

         R= 0.25

      Change the decimal number to % by moving the decimal point two places to the right.

      So, R= 25%

     Therefore, the answer is letter C.
