4. 13. Nakatuklas na umiikot ang daigdig sa araw habang umiikot sa sariling nitong axis A. Erastosthenes B. Aristarchus C. Euclid D. Herophilus​


NAKATUKLAS UMIIKOT ANG DAIGDIG  SA ARAW

Answer:

B. Aristarchus

Explanation:

B. Aristarchus- Nakatuklas na umiikot ang daigdig sa araw habang umiikot sa sariling nitong axis.

in English,

Aristarchus of Samos was a Greek astronomer who believed that the Earth rotated on its axis and revolved around the Sun. He was born about 310 BCE and died around 230 BCE. On this basis, Cleanthes the Stoic declared in his Against Aristarchus that Aristarchus should be charged with impiety "for setting the hearth of the universe in motion."

Although Aristarchus' book on the motion of the Earth has perished, his ideas have been preserved because to references by Greek mathematician Archimedes, Greek historian Plutarch, and Greek philosopher Sextus Empiricus. In his Sand-Reckoner, Archimedes claimed that Aristarchus had suggested a new theory that, if true, would make the universe far greater than previously thought. (Unless the stars are really far away, a moving Earth should induce a parallax, or annual change, in the apparent positions of the fixed stars.)

Answer:

Si Fernão de Magalhães (pinakamalapit na bigkas /fekh·néw ji ma·ga·lyáysh/) (1480–Abril 27, 1521; Fernando de Magallanes sa Kastila, Ferdinand Magellan[2] sa Ingles) ay isang eksplorador na Portuges na naglayag para sa Espanya. Siya ang kauna-unahang nakapaglayag mula sa Europa pakanluran patungong Asya, ang unang Europeo na nakatawid ng Karagatang Pasipiko, at ang namuno ng unang ekspedisyon para sa sirkumnabegasyon ng daigdig. Bagaman nasawi siya sa Pilipinas at di nakabalik sa Europa, 18 sa kanyang mga tripulante at isang barko ang nakabalik sa Espanya noong 1522, at natupad ang pangarap na paglibot sa buong mundo.
