10. Difference of graft and corruption


As a result of the pervasive culture of graft and corruption, laymen are usually misled into thinking that the terms "graft" and "corruption" mean the same thing. However, there is a subtle difference between the two in that "corruption" is the broader term that encompass dishonest behaviors or unethical conduct of persons occupying high position of power and authority. Graft is only one of the many types of corruption. It is a specific and political type of corruption where a politician uses his position and power to enrich himself, his family, or his cronies - usually at the expense of the public and the taxes they pay. In this case, the political intentionally misdirects the use of public funds. An example of graft is when a politician unduly uses his insider knowledge (gained by virtue of his position) for his own unjust enrichment (ex: knowing that the government needs a certain area of land for its project, the politician purchases the said area of land at a low cost and then sell it to the government at a much higher price to make a hefty profit).

Extortion

This is done by government officials against their clients by demanding money, valuable items, or services from ordinary citizens who transact business with them or with their office. This is rampant in agencies issuing clearances and other documents, those involved in the recruitment of personnel, or those performing services that directly favor ordinary citizens.

“Tong” or Protection Money

This is a form of bribery which is done by citizens performing illegal activities and operations. They deliver huge amount of money to government officials particularly to those in-charge of enforcing the law in exchange for unhampered illegal operation. The law enforcement officer who receives the money will be duty-bound to protect the citizen concerned together with his illegal activities from other law enforcement authorities. This is practiced mostly by gambling lords and those engaged in business without the necessary permits.

The “Lagay” system or Bribery

The “lagay” system or the act of citizens to bribe government officials occupying sensitive positions in government is perpetuated due to bureaucratic red tape. It is with great concern that I am writing this open letter to address the issue of bribery and corruption in our society. We cannot deny that this problem is deeply embedded in our culture and is a significant obstacle to our progress and development as a nation.

Funds that should be earmarked for the common good are instead being pocketed by corrupt officials, leaving the poor and marginalized without the basic services they need.

20. solution of graft and corruption


1. Punish the vote sellers and vote brokers

to decrease the number of vote sellers in the Philippines, the COMELEC and other concerned agencies should do their best to educate and warn all voters across the country. Informing people through the Internet and social media isn’t enough since 2/3 of our population don’t have a good access to the Internet yet. Our government have to increase efforts to reach, educate and warn voters in the rural areas.


2. Annual assessment of all public officials by the BIR

Majority of politicians in the Philippines are rich and generating income from businesses. Even the poorest mayor in Metro Manila has a net worth of P3.31 million. Therefore, the BIR should not only focus on examining small business owners and professionals, but should also concentrate on investigating the politicians and the companies that are fully or partially owned by them.


3. A more effective and efficient tax system

Creating a better tax system is a job that must be initiated by our congressmen and senators – the lawmakers. Thus, they have to consult not only with the BIR, Department of Finance and DTI, but also with various independent tax experts, the business sector, and other taxpayers who are affected by our tax system.


4. Regular surprise examination of all government offices

Government employees can also commit corruption. If they don’t do their job to serve the public well or if they don’t work for the hours the government is paying them, that’s already a form of corruption. Thus, the CSC should ensure that all government personnel are doing their job well and complying with the Anti-red tape law and other applicable laws in our country. They should obey the no noon breaks policy, should work beyond 5pm when necessary, and should not commit fixing or connive with fixers.


5. Timely governmental audit by COA or by an Independent auditing firm

The Commission on Audit is the government agency responsible for examining, preventing and reporting frauds happening in the government. But are they doing their job efficiently? Scams are already uncovered and reported to the public after 3 years or more. Just consider the current Pork Barrel scam.

27. kahulugan ng graft and corruption


Ano ang Graft and Corruption?

Ang graft and corruption ay ang pang-aabuso sa posisyon ng isang opisyal ng gobyerno at ang pagnanakaw sa kaban ng bayan. Ito ang nagiging paratang sa mga opisyal ng gobyerno na ginagamit ang pampublikong pondo para sa pansariling interes.

Mga uri ng korupsiyon sa Pilipinas

1. Pagtakas sa pagbabayad ng buwis

2. Mga ghost project at pasahod

3. Pag-iwas sa pagsusubasta sa publiko sa pagkakaloob ng mga kontrata

4. Pagpasa ng mga kontrata mula sa isang kontraktor sa isa pa

5. Nepotismo at paboritismo

6. Pangingikil

7. Suhol o Lagay

Mga halimbawa ng Korupsiyon Coco Levy Fund Scam - scam na kinasangkutan ni Ferdinand Marcos at iba pa. Fertilizer Fund scam - scam na kinasangkutan ni Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo at iba pa 2011 Eskandalong Korupsiyon sa Sandatahang Lakas ng Pilipinas - Eskandalong kinasangkutan ng mga kasapi ng Sandatahang Lakas ng Pilipinas NBN-ZTE Scandal - eskandalong kinasangkutan ni Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo at asawa nitong si Mike Arroyo Pork barrel scam- scam na kinasangkutan ng ilang mga senador at kinatawan ng Pilipinas. Ito ay nilantad ng whistleblower na si Benhur Luy noong Hulyo 2013. Ito ay tinatawag sa media na "ina ng lahat ng mga scam".

Mga ahensiyang nilikha upang sugpuin ang korupsiyon sa Pilipinas

1. Office of the Ombudsman (OMB)

-nag-iimbestiga at kumikilos sa mga reklamong inihain laban sa mga opisyal at empleyadong pampubliko at nagisislbi bilang mga “people’s watchdog” ng pamahalaan.  

2. Civil Service Commission (CSC)

-sentral na ahensiya ng tauhan ng pamahalaan na inatasang magtatag ng isang serbisyong karera at magtaguyod ng moral, kaigihan, integridad, pagtugon, pagsulong at kagandahang loob sa serbisyong sibil.  

3. Commission on Audit (COA)

-bantay ng mga operasyong pangsalapi ng pamahalaan. Ito ay binigyang kapangyarihan upang siyasatin, tasahin o iaduit at bayaran ang lahat ng mga account na nauukol sa kinita o nalikom na buwis, mga resibo at mga gastos o paggamit ng mga pondo at ari-arian sa ilalim ng kustodiya ng mga ahensiya ng pamahalaan at mga instrumentalidad.

4. Sandiganbayan

-isang hukumang anti-graft sa Pilipinas. Ito ay may hurisdiksiyon sa mga kasong sibil at kriminal na kinasasangkutan ng mga kasanayang graft at corrupt at ibang gayong mga paglabag na ginawa ng mga opiser at empleyadong pampubliko.

Mga sagabal sa pagsugpo ng korupsiyon sa Pilipinas

1. Ang spesipikong kultura ng mga Pilipino nagpapalakas ng paglaganap ng graft at korupsiyon.

2. Ang kulturang Pilipino ng pagbibigay regalo ay nagbibigay katwiran sa paglalagay o panunuhol at pangingikil na gumagawang mahirap para sa pagpapatupad ng batas at mga ahensiyang anti-korupsiyon na sugpuin ang problema.

3. Ang mga ahensiya sa Pilipinas na inatasan na labanan ang graft at korupsiyon ay hindi mahusay na pinopondohan ng pamahalaan.

4. Ang transparency o pagiging bukas ay hindi sinusunod partikular na sa mga transaksiyon ng pamahalaan.  

5. Ang epektibong pagmomonitor ng mga programa at proyekto ng pamahalaan gayundin ang mga gastusin ay hind seryosong isinasagawa ng mga ahensiya o tauhan na inatasang magmonitor ng mga ito.

6. Ang mga Statement of Assets, Liabilities and Net Worth o SALN na mekanismo sa pagsugpo ng graft at korupsiyon ay isinusumite ng lahat ng mga opisyal pampubliko.

Graft and Corruption

Epekto ng Graft and Corruption sa ating Bansa

Lubos na paghihirap ng mga mamamayan dahil napupunta sa bulsa ng mga namumuno ang kaban at pondo ng pamahalaan na dapat para sa mga mamamayan at para sa pag-unlad ng bayan.
