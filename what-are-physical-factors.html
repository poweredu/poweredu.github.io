27. what is the factors of physical weathering​


Answer:

Physical weathering refers to the breakdown of rocks and minerals into smaller pieces through mechanical processes. The factors that can contribute to physical weathering include:

1. Temperature changes - Rocks that are exposed to frequent temperature changes, especially freeze-thaw cycles, can experience physical weathering. When water gets into cracks and crevices in rocks, it can freeze and expand, putting pressure on the rock and causing it to break apart.

2. Abrasion - Abrasion occurs when rocks are physically worn away by friction, such as when rocks rub against each other due to water flow, wind, or other forces.

3. Pressure - Pressure can contribute to physical weathering when rocks are buried deep underground and experience intense pressure from the weight oflying over rocks.

4. Plant and animal activity - The growth of plant roots and the burrowing of animals can also contribute to physical weathering. As roots grow and exert pressure on rocks, they can cause cracks and crevices to form, while burrowing animals can move soil and rocks around, changing the landscape over time.

5. Water - Water can contribute to physical weathering through erosion, which is the gradual wearing away of rocks and minerals due to the movement of water over time. Water can also cause rocks to become saturated and weaken, making them more susceptible to physical weathering.
