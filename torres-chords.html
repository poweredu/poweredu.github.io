4. Which chords are major chords and minor chords ​


ANSWER

MAJOR CHORDS V.S MINOR CHORDS

•A major chord has what's called a “natural third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective major scale.

•A minor chord has what's called either a “minor third” or a “flat third.” It's the third degree of the chord's respective minor scale.

11. a steel cylinder contains a mixture of nitrogen gas at 410 torr oxygen gas at 110 torr and helium gas at 232 torr what is the total pressure of the gas mixture?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_{\text{N}_2} = \text{410 torr} \\ & P_{\text{O}_2} = \text{110 torr} \\ & P_{\text{He}} = \text{232 torr} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the total pressure by using Dalton's law of partial pressures.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_{\text{T}} & = P_{\text{N}_2} + P_{\text{O}_2} + P_{\text{He}} \\ & = \text{410 torr + 110 torr + 232 torr} \\ & = \boxed{\text{752 torr}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the total pressure of the gas mixture is 752 torr.

16. one standard atmosphere atm is equivelent to 75.0 torrtrue or wrong the 75.0 torr?​


Answer:

The statement "one standard atmosphere (atm) is equivalent to 75.0 torr" is false.

Explanation:

One standard atmosphere (atm) is defined as the pressure exerted by a column of mercury 760 millimeters (mm) in height at 0°C at standard gravity (9.80665 meters per second squared). This is equivalent to 101,325 Pascals (Pa) or 101.325 kilopascals (kPa).

On the other hand, 1 torr is defined as 1/760th of a standard atmosphere or 133.32 Pascals (Pa).

Therefore, 75.0 torr is equivalent to 0.0989 atm or 9,890 Pa. It is not equivalent to one standard atmosphere (atm).

27. What volume will 40 ml of a gas at 710 Torrs occupy if the pressure is increased to 760 Torrs?


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{710 torr}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{40 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{760 torr}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{40 mL} × \frac{\text{710 torr}}{\text{760 torr}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{37 mL}}[/tex]
