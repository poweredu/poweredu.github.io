23. What causes Rapid Population Growth in the Philippines?​


Answer:

1. High fertility rates: The Philippines has a high fertility rate, with an average of 2.7 children per woman. This is partly due to cultural and religious beliefs that encourage large families.

2. Lack of access to family planning: Many Filipinos do not have access to or do not use modern contraceptive methods, leading to unintended pregnancies and larger families.

3. Poverty: Poverty is a major factor in rapid population growth, as families in poverty often have more children in order to increase their chances of having support in old age and to help with household chores and income.

4. Low levels of education: Women with lower levels of education tend to have more children, as they may not have access to information about family planning or may not have the means to access it.

5. Cultural and religious beliefs: In some parts of the Philippines, large families are seen as a sign of wealth and status, and there is a cultural and religious emphasis on having children.

Overall, addressing these factors through education, access to family planning, and poverty reduction programs can help slow down population growth in the Philippines.
