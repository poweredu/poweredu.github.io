What is the role of the mango tree growing in the backyard of the Mendoza house? 2.At first, how Kikay react to Tony’s reminder that they are secretly engaged to be married? 3.What makes her change her mind about the engagement? 4.When does Kikay drop her New York affectations and put on her Tondo characteristics? 5.What happens to the engagement of Nena and Tony? That of Kikay and Tony? Why? 6.How does Kikay show that she has been cured of her New York affectations? 7.What is your impression of Kikay? What gives you this impression? (New yorker in Tondo)


1. The mango tree growing in the backyard of the Mendoza house symbolizes growth, nourishment, and renewal. It serves as a reminder of Kikay's roots and her connection to her hometown. It also represents the natural cycle of life and the importance of staying grounded.

2. At first, Kikay reacts defensively to Tony's reminder that they are secretly engaged, insisting that she is not ready for marriage and that their arrangement was merely a joke.

3. Kikay changes her mind about the engagement after a conversation with her mother, who advises her to think about what she really wants in life and not just what others expect of her.

4. Kikay drops her New York affectations and puts on her Tondo characteristics when she realizes the importance of embracing her identity and heritage. This happens after she has a conversation with Tony's mother, who reminds her that she will always be an outsider in New York and that she should never forget where she comes from.

5. Nena and Tony's engagement is cancelled because Nena's family cannot afford the wedding expenses. Kikay and Tony's engagement is also cancelled because Kikay realizes that she is not ready for marriage and wants to pursue her dreams of becoming a fashion designer in New York.

6. Kikay shows that she has been cured of her New York affectations when she speaks with her friends in Tondo and realizes that she can still relate to them and their experiences despite her time away.

7. My impression of Kikay as a character is mixed. While I admire her determination and strength in pursuing her dreams, I also found her sometimes self-centered and insensitive to the feelings of those around her. However, I appreciated her growth as a character and her eventual realization of the importance of staying true to oneself.

Answer:

1. In "New Yorker in Tondo," the mango tree growing in the backyard of the Mendoza house symbolizes the connection of the characters to their home and roots.

2. At first, Kikay reacts defensively and angrily to Tony's reminder that they are secretly engaged to be married.

3. Kikay changes her mind about the engagement when she realizes that Tony is a successful businessman in New York and that marrying him would allow her to achieve her dreams of living a luxurious life.

4. Kikay drops her New York affectations and puts on her Tondo characteristics when she realizes that her behavior is not well-received by her friends and family.

5. The engagement of Nena and Tony is called off when Tony realizes that he still has feelings for Kikay.

6. Kikay shows that she has been cured of her New York affectations when she reverts to speaking in a Tondo accent and behaving in a more familiar and genuine manner.

7. Kikay comes across as a conflicted and superficial character, driven by her desires for wealth and status but also torn by her sense of loyalty and connection to her roots.
