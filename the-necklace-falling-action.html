15. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.

Answer:

"The Necklace" by Guy de Maupassant. Here is a breakdown of the story based on your prompts:

SETTING:

Place: Paris, France

Time: Late 19th century

Mood/Atmosphere: Somber, melancholic

PLOT:

Exposition: Madame Loisel, a woman born into a lower-middle-class family, is unhappy with her life and dreams of a more luxurious existence.

Rising Action: Madame Loisel borrows a necklace from a wealthy friend to wear to a party, but loses it on her way home.

Climax: Madame Loisel and her husband spend ten years working to repay the debt incurred from buying a replacement necklace.

Falling Action: After ten years, Madame Loisel encounters the friend who loaned her the original necklace and confesses the truth about the lost necklace.

Denouement/Resolution: It is revealed that the original necklace was a fake, rendering Madame Loisel's decade of suffering pointless.

CHARACTERS:

Madame Mathilde Loisel - protagonist

Monsieur Loisel, Madame Loisel's husband

Madame Forestier, Madame Loisel's wealthy friend who loans her the necklace

CONFLICT:

External: Madame Loisel's desire for a luxurious lifestyle conflicts with her lower-middle-class upbringing and financial means.

Internal: Madame Loisel's dissatisfaction with her life and appearance causes her to make poor decisions and suffer the consequences.

POINT OF VIEW:

The story is told from a third-person omniscient point of view.

CHARACTERS/CHARACTERIZATION:

Protagonist: Madame Mathilde Loisel, a dissatisfied and unhappy woman who dreams of a more luxurious life.

Antagonist: There is no clear antagonist in the story, but Madame Loisel's own dissatisfaction and poor decision-making could be considered a form of self-antagonism.

THEME:

The themes of the story include the dangers of pride and materialism, the consequences of poor decision-making, and the idea that appearances can be deceiving. Another possible theme is the idea that the pursuit of happiness through material possessions is ultimately unsatisfying.

22. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.
