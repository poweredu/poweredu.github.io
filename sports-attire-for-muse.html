1. winning connects good feelings with the sports? (for intramural muse please)


Answer:

Winning in sports can definitely lead to good feelings for both the athletes and the fans who support them. When a team or athlete wins a competition, it can bring about a sense of accomplishment, pride, and joy. Winning can also boost self-esteem and confidence, which can translate to improved performance in future competitions.

For fans, winning can bring a sense of community and shared celebration with fellow supporters. It can also create a sense of pride and connection to the team or athlete, especially if they have a personal connection or investment in the sport.

However, it's important to note that winning isn't the only factor that connects good feelings with sports. The act of participating in sports and physical activity itself can also bring about positive emotions such as happiness, stress relief, and a sense of achievement. Additionally, the social and supportive aspects of sports, such as teamwork and camaraderie, can also contribute to good feelings.

Ultimately, while winning can certainly be a source of positive emotions in sports, it's not the only one. The overall experience of participating in sports, whether as an athlete or a fan, can lead to a wide range of positive emotions and connections.

Tips For Labeling Cables

Unless you have one of the more expensive label makers that use the self laminating wire labels, use the cable flag type label. You can also try wrapping the label with some clear shipping tape to help it stay on longer and possibly clean the cable with some alcohol first.It's good to label cables on both ends but not necessary to label every cable. For instance you likely only have one monitor cable. It's obvious what it looks like and where it goes.Don't place the label all the way up to the connector. Leave it back a few inches where it's easier to see. If you're constantly pulling cables out of the back of your computer it doesn't help you much if the label is tucked away out of sight.Try to keep labels out of regular sight for things you don't use daily. It's fun to label but you don't want to create label clutter. Find a happy medium between hidden and convenient.
