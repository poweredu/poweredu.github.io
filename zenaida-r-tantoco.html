20. Zenaida is making a vegetable farm in her back yard. The vegetable farm will be rectangular and have an area of (64x³-27y³) ft². If the length of the vegetable farm is (4x - 3y) ft, what is its width?​


Answer:

[tex] {16x}^{2} + 12xy + {9y}^{2} [/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

The solution to this problem is to divide the area and the length to find the width.

[tex] \frac{( {64x}^{3} - {27y}^{3} )}{4x - 3y} [/tex]

Using the pattern of Sum/Difference of Two Cubes, we can factor the numerator.

[tex] \frac{(4x - 3y)( {16x}^{2} + 12xy + {9y}^{2} ) }{4x - 3y} [/tex]

4x - 3y cancels out, therefore the width of the vegetable farm is...

[tex]{16x}^{2} + 12xy + {9y}^{2}[/tex]
