11. where globalization , modernization, westernization ,and Americanization have an influence ​


Answer:

Globalization, modernization, westernization, and Americanization have an influence on various aspects of society, including culture, economics, politics, and technology, among others. In terms of culture, the global spread of popular culture and media has led to the diffusion of Western values, fashion, and lifestyles in many parts of the world. In economics, globalization has increased international trade and investment, resulting in the integration of markets and economies across the globe. Modernization has brought technological advancements and improvements in living standards, while also leading to changes in traditional social structures and norms. Westernization has influenced political systems and governance models, with many countries adopting democratic ideals and institutions. Americanization, in particular, has had a significant impact on popular culture, consumerism, and the spread of the English language. Overall, these forces have shaped the world in various ways, and their influence continues to be felt today.

22. If cultural globalization merely entails the spread of a Western monoculture, what explains the prevalence of regional cultural trends? ​


Answer:

Cultural Trends has been providing in-depth analysis of cultural sector statistics since 1989. It focuses on key trends within the fields of material culture, media, performing arts and the historic environment, and it includes coverage of issues which impact on the sector as a whole, such as the internet, poverty and access to the arts, and funding. Cultural Trends is based on the assumption that cultural policy should be based on empirical evidence and it champions the need for better statistical information on the cultural sector. It aims to: stimulate analysis and understanding of the arts and wider cultural sector based on relevant and reliable statistical data; provide a critique of the empirical evidence upon which arts and wider cultural policy may be formed, implemented, evaluated and developed; examine the soundness of measures of the performance of government and public sector bodies in the arts and wider cultural sector; and encourage improvements in the coverage, timeliness and accessibility of statistical information on the arts and wider cultural sector.
