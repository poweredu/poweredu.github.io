19. Divide 50 in the ratio of 2:3


A ratio is a relation between two quantities where whatever number the other is multiplied, the other one will also follow.

To able to solve this, we will add a variable to represent the missing number with a variable on both quantities of the ratio. Next, we will add the two quantities then equate it with the given total to able to find the value of the variable. Finally, whatever the value of the variable will be, we will substitute it to the two ratio quantities. To check, the sum of the rations multiplied each with the variable must be equal to the total given.

Given Ratio: 2:3

Equation: 2:3 = 50

Let the ratio 2:3 become [tex]2x:3x[/tex]

Equation: [tex]2x+ 3x=50[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]2x+ 3x=50[/tex]

[tex]5x=50[/tex]

[tex]\frac{5x}{5}= \frac{50}{5}[/tex]

[tex]x=10[/tex]

Substitute:

[tex]2x = 2(10) =20[/tex]

[tex]3x=3(10)=30[/tex]

Checking:

[tex]20+30 = 50[/tex]

[tex]50=50[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]20:30[/tex]

20. Divide 50 pens between Sohan and Meera in the ratio 2:3.


Answer:

Sohan's number of pens is 20.Meera's number of pens is 30.

Step-by-step explanation:

Dividing a Quantity in a Given Ratio

Given:

total number of pens - 50

ratio of Sohan's share to Meera's share - 2:3

Solution:

Ratio of Sohan's share to Meera's share - 2:3

This means that if Sohan gets 2 parts, Meera should get 3 parts.

Sum of the ratio terms - 2 + 3 = 5

We need to get the sum of the terms. The sum of the terms means that there are 5 equal parts.

Sohan's

Sohan should get 2 parts out of these 5 equal parts.

= ⅖ of the total number of pens

= ⅖ of 50

= ⅖ × 50

= 20 pens

Sohan gets 20 pens.

Meera's

Similarly, Meera gets 3 parts out of 5 equal parts.

= ⅗ of the total number of pens

= ⅗ of 50

= ⅗ × 50

= 30 pens

Meera gets 30 pens.

Final Answer:

2:3 = 20:30
