The piano or saxophone was usually the principal instrument in the early rock and roll forms. In the mid to late 1950s, guitar frequently replaced or augmented these instruments. Rock & roll had a divisive impact on culture, dress, attitudes, and language. It is frequently portrayed in films, fan publications, and on television. Terminology

Greg Kot defines "rock and roll" in Encyclopedia Britannica as "music that emerged in the mid-1950s and eventually expanded into the more inclusive worldwide style known as rock music."
