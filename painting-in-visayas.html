Ethnocentrism

Ethnocentrism is described as a culture's beliefs that feel they are superior to others because of their race, ethnicity, or nationality. Ethnocentrists may attempt to convert individuals from other cultures to their own, which may be demeaning and lead to horrific events such as genocide and war.

Cultural relativism

Cultural relativism, on the other hand, is described as examining a culture's beliefs, values, and behaviors through the eyes of the culture being studied. It will examine the context of each culture and recognize that each culture is unique and is better understood by those who are a part of that culture.

Ethnocentrism and cultural relativism demonstrate the many different perspectives on other cultures. While their world points of view differ, they are both approaches to evaluating culture. It bases culture on the research and thorough examination of other cultures, which makes these two belief systems seem comparable.
