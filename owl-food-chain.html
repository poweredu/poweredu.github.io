29. What food chain does occur in the forest ecosystem? A. grass, mouse, birds B. grass, caterpillar, snake C. tress, snake, lion D. tress, rabbit, owl


FOOD CHAIN THAT OCCUR IN THE FOREST

The food chain depicts the process by which energy is transferred from one living organism to another via food. It is critical that we understand how the food chain works so that we can identify the important living organisms that comprise the food chain and how the ecology is balanced. The food chain describes how energy and nutrients circulate in an ecosystem. Plants produce energy at the most basic level, and then it progresses to higher-level organisms such as herbivores. When herbivores eat carnivores, energy is transferred from one to the other. Energy is transferred from one living organism to another in the form of food along the food chain.

ANSWER: B. Grass, caterpillar, snake
