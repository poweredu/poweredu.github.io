21. Applying the brakes on the car causes it to slow down because the brakes


Answer:

When you apply the brakes on a car, the brake pads or shoes press against the rotating brake rotor or drum, which creates friction and slows down the car. This happens due to the conversion of kinetic energy (the energy of motion) into thermal energy (heat) through the process of friction.

The brake system of a car typically includes a brake pedal, a master cylinder, brake lines, brake calipers, and brake pads or shoes. When you press the brake pedal, it pushes a piston in the master cylinder, which pressurizes the brake fluid. This pressure is transmitted through the brake lines to the brake calipers or wheel cylinders, which then apply pressure to the brake pads or shoes, creating friction against the brake rotor or drum and causing the car to slow down or stop.

In summary, the application of brakes on a car slows it down by converting kinetic energy into heat energy through the process of friction.

23. A fish weighs 4 kg when the elevator is at rest, it is then placed on a weighing scale as shown in the diagram. When the elevator goes up, the reading becomes higher. When the elevator goes down, the reading becomes lower. (Explain the readings)​


IT DEPENDS ON THE ACCELERATION OF THE ELEVATORIt is determined if the elevator is accelerating (and in which direction) or travelling at a steady speed.The scale would measure the same weight if it was moving at a steady pace.If the elevator accelerates, you will also accelerate. Therefore, in order for you to accelerate, there must be a net external force acting on you. And the only forces at work on you when you're in the elevator areThe earth's gravitational force, and The normal reaction of the elevator's surface. The earth's gravitational attraction to you would scarcely alter. To generate the desired acceleration, the normal force must increase or decrease This typical response is what most weighing scales measure. This normal is equivalent to the gravitational force while at rest (or in uniform motion). However, as previously established, it would be greater or less than the gravitational force as you accelerate. As a result, the weight measured by the scale would differ.However, the gravitational force remains constant. It is still the same. The natural reaction is what changes. When we are in a speeding elevator, we clearly feel our weight change. This leads us to another realization. The weight we experience in our own bodies is caused by the ground's regular response rather than gravity.

28. Readings in philippine history book otis proclamation


Answer:

Drawing on arguments found in international relations theory, this chapter explores Philippine nationalist resistance against imperial occupation in the late nineteenth century leading up to the Philippine–American War at the turn of the twentieth century. In particular, the chapter explores the transnational diffusion of liberal ideas among Philippine revolutionaries developed during the period of Spanish colonial rule. In hopes of securing their independence after the Spanish–American War, Filipino leaders quickly developed a constitution based on republican ideals, a legislative body, and a declaration of independence and national self-determination. Paradoxically, such ideas and actions remained unpersuasive to American policymakers. With the Treaty of Paris formally ending the war with Spain in 1898, the United States acquired its first overseas empire. In response, Filipinos resorted to guerilla warfare, drawing the United States into its “first Vietnam.”
