A multitester, often known as a volt/ohm meter or VOM, is a type of electronic measuring equipment that integrates numerous measurement operations into a single unit. A standard multimeter may have capabilities such as measuring voltage, current, and resistance.

1) B. Pointer

This is the needle-shaped rod that moves over the meter scale. The moving coil is mechanically attached to the needle pointer. The value over which the cursor is hovering indicates the value being measured by the multitester.

2) C. Scale

This is how the value being measured is read. This is a set of semicircle markings on an analog multitester. Depending on the sophistication of your multimeter, you can read numerous variations on the device screen and scale section, such as voltage, current, and resistance.

3) A. Zero-ohm adjusting knob

When measuring the resistance of an object, the zero-ohm adjustment knob is used to calibrate the multitester.

4) A. Range Selector Knob

This component is also referred to as a selector switch. A range selector knob allows you to fine-tune the multitester's parameters (range selector knobs are also present on the best insulation resistance testers).

5) A. Adjustment screw

This component, often known as the dial or infinity knob, allows you to set the scale's pointer to the zero position. This is normally done with the assistance of a flathead screwdriver.
