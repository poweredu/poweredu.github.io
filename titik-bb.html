14. 5. Two parents with brown eyes have a child with blue eyes. What must their genotypes be? A. They both have Bb. C. One has Bb, one has bb. B. They both have BB. D. One has BB, one has bb​


Answer:

A. They both have Bb

Explanation:

The gene is the basic unit of inheritance. They are passed on from one generation to the next. They are described as phenotype and genotype. A phenotype is an individual's observable traits such as eye color. The genetic make-up of organisms is called the genotype. They are made up of alleles. Different versions or forms of a gene are called alleles. Genotype can either be homozygous or heterozygous. Homozygous genotype is made up of the same type of alleles while heterozygous genotype is made up of different alleles. Alleles can be either dominant or recessive.

Dominant alleles show their effect in a homozygous genotype and even if the individual has a heterozygous genotype. The heterozygous gene has one copy of the allele. For example, in the above situation, the allele for brown eyes is dominant. Therefore, both homozygous dominant genotype (BB) and heterozygous genotype (Bb) will produce brown eyes. It is a dominant gene thus you only need one copy of the 'brown eye' allele to have brown eyes. Recessive alleles only show their effect if the individual has a homozygous recessive genotype. Homozygous genotype has two copies of the allele. For example, the allele for blue eyes is recessive, therefore to have blue eyes you need to have two copies of the 'blue eye' allele (bb).

a. Bb x Bb

If both parents have brown eyes with heterozygous genotype (Bb), it will produce 75% chance of brown with homozygous genotype (BB) and 25% chance of blue eyes (bb).

b. BB x BB

If both have brown eyes with homozygous dominant (BB), there will be 100% chance that the offspring will have brown eyes with homozygous dominant (BB) genotype.

c. Bb x bb

If one of the parents' genotype is Bb (brown eyes) and the other one is bb (blue eyes), there will be 50% chance of brown eyes with heterozygous genotype (Bb) and 50% chance to have blue eyes (bb).

d. BB x bb

If one of the parents has brown eyes with homozygous dominant genotype (BB) and the other has blue eyes with homozygous recessive genotype (bb), It will produce 100% chance of having brown eyes with heterozygous genotype (Bb).

Among all the crosses above, only Bb x Bb cross satisfies the description (both parents have brown eyes and the has offspring of blue eyes)

30. What would be the phenotypic percentage of the offspring, if the parents have Bb x bb alleles? ​


Answer:

In the cross BB X Bb, what is the probability of having an offspring with the dominant phenotype?

There is a near 100% chance of offspring expressing the dominant phenotype.

Genetic crosses of single gene combinations (monohybrid inheritance) are best examined using Punnett squares. In this example, ‘B’ is being used to represent the dominant allele and ‘b’ to represent the recessive allele.

If present, a dominant allele will always be expressed, even if it is only found on one of the chromosomes. As you can see from the diagram above, all possible outcomes involve ‘B’ so the dominant phenotype will always be expressed in offspring.
