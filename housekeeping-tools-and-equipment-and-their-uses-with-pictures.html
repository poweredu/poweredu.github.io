28. What are the differences among housekeeping tool,equipment and material?​


Answer:

Abrasives: Sharpening stones, grit paper

Brushes: Handheld flat brushes

Trolley: Often found in hotels and used to store all the housekeeping tools – you can create one for the household you work at, if there is enough space to move the trolley around in.

Dustbins: Make sure they are big enough to store all the rubbish, and that the close properly. You don’t want any rubbish-smells coming out when the Principal and family are home!

Dusting Cloths: These are soft cloths especially for dusting – make sure you do not use these for any other cleaning or wet surfaces.

Dustpans: Handy to collect dust and dirt by hand from the floor – but not for deep cleaning.

Mops: String mops, dust mops, synthetic mops. Different mops will have different absorbing properties and it is important that you keep them clean after every use. Also make sure they dry properly.

Spray Mop: Trigger spray gives a fine mist spray of cleaning solution to the floor in front of the mop head, this can be handy for quick touch ups.

Mop Wringer: Bucket allowing for the mop to be wrung without the hands getting dirty. Again, make sure this is cleaned after every use and make dry – do not leave water residue in the bucket!

Spray Bottles: Handy for home made cleaning products – they should be clearly labelled and make sure you do not mix different products in one bottle.

Ironing Board and Accessories: Make sure it is high / low enough so you can stand upright during the ironing, without having to bend forward and potentially hurt your back.

Small brush/ Toothbrush: These are handy for cleaning hard to reach areas, such as around the base of taps and small areas such as grout between tiles

30. Cleaning materials, tools and equipment in housekeeping with definition


These are the common cleaning materials, tools, and equipment used in housekeeping:

Vacuum cleaner this a device that uses an air pump to create a partial vacuum to suck up dust and dirt. A broom is a cleaning tool consisting of usually stiff fibers, plastic, hair or corn husks. Dustpan a small shovel usually with a short handle into which dirt or debris from the floor is swept. It is used to scope the dirt. Glove a garment that use to cover the whole hand of a person whenever it performs household services. Waste Container use temporarily for strong waste. MOP A device consisting of absorbent material, like a sponge, fastened to a handle and used especially for washing floors. Sponge a piece of any of various absorbent materials, such as rubber or cellulose, that are soft when wet and use for washing plate or kitchen utensils and etc. Bleach a chemical that makes clothes white, remove stains. Squeegee a tool made of a handle and usually use to remove water from windows after washing.  Scourer a small metal pad used for scouring a surface. Bucket a round deep container use to store water. Duster a cleaning tool with a handle and feathers used for wiping dust. Garbage o trash bag a large bag for collecting household garbage or waste.  Floor Polisher used to clean and maintain non-carpeted floors. Toilet bowl brush used to clean the toilet bowl and upper area.  Ceiling broom used to remove cobwebs and dirt in high areas. Boots protect the foot and ankle from water and provide ankle support while doing strenuous activities just like cleaning. Polishing Cloths or towels used to wipe away spilled liquids or dirt on surfaces.   Mask to protect yourself from dust. Hand Brushes use to clean tile and difficult stain.
