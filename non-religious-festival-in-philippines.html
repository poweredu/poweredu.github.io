9. What are the example of non religious festival in the philippines???


Here are some of the non-religious festivals in the Philippines:

1. Pangisdaan Festival in Navotas
Navotas, found on the north of Manila, is considered as the Fishing Capital of the Philippines. Pangisdaan Festival is one of the highlights of the Navotas Day Celebration every 16th of January. This is to pay tribute to their main source of income, which is fishing.

2. Panagbenga Festival in Baguio
Baguio holds its biggest festival, the Panagbenga Festival, every February. The event showcases floral floats and native dances. Panagbenga is a term which means "a season of blooming." The event also known as "Baguio Flower Festival."

3. Maskara Festival in Bacolod
Maskara came from two words: English word "mass" and Spanish word "kara" or "face." This started in early 1980s when Bacolod, the county's sugar capital, was hit by major crisis. To lift the people's spirit up, Maskara Festival was done. This celebration happens every third week of October.

17. List of non religious festivals in the philippines


Here are some festivals celebrated in the Philippines that are not in any way religious in nature.
1. Panagbenga Festival
This is celebrated in the northern part of the Philippines during the month of February. During this festival, people flock to Baguio City to see a parade and exhibition of big and beautiful flowers. It is done to celebrate the blooming of the flowers that are grown in the province.
2. Maskara Festival
This is celebrated in the southern part of the Philippines during the month of October. During this festival, visitors go to Bacolod to see a pageantry of costumes, accessories and headgears. This festival is observed to uphold the values of the locals in continuing to smile amidst life challenges.
3. Kadayawan Festival
This is celebrated in the southern part of the Philippines during the month of August. This is a time for Davaoenos to celebrate thanksgiving for a great crop harvest, peaceful life and other fortunes. 

18. non religious festivals in the philippines


Non-religious festivals in the Philippines are those festivals not usually associated with a patron saint or a religious event. While many of our festivals are religious in nature, there are also many non-religious festivals. Many of these are also new festivals that are meant to promote a particular place, its tourism and culture. Different types of non-religious festivals in the Philippines include food festivals, flower festivals and even agriculture-related festivals. Below are some examples of these festivals in the Philippines

Davao - Kadayawan Festival

Kadayawan is a festival meant to celebrate a good and bountiful harvest. The festivities are held on the third week of August. The word "kadayawan" was an derivation from "madayaw" which means a something fortunate, a celebration, a thanksgiving or even wealth of both culture and harvest. Floats of flowers and fruits, along with street dancing are the usual sights during the Kadayawan Festival.  

Baguio - Panagbenga or Panagbanga Festival

Closely similar to Kadayawan, this festival celebrates the blooming of flowers. It also serves another social purpose, that of forgetting the deadly 1990 earthquake that left Baguio in a helpless state. The festivities are held during the month of February and features floral floats and native dances.

 

Dagupan - Bangus Festival

One example of a food / fishing festival is the Bangus Festival in Dagupan, Pangasinan. This festival is held every third of April and until the end of the month. This celebrates the city's abundant aquaculture with a focus on bangus or milk fish. Cooking contests as well as food bazaars featuring various ways to cook bangus can be observed during this non-religious festival.  

Catanduanes - Abaca Festival

This celebration is heald every 4th week of May in Virac, Catanduanes. It is an opportunity to take cognizance of abaca or Manila hemp, a major product of the province.  

Other Festivals:

21. Directions: Give the characteristics of the following Philippine Festivals. Religious Festivals Non-Religious Festivals​


Characteristics:

Philippine festivals are town fiestas celebrating moments in history that defined identity, polity and locality.

Non-Religious Festival

Dagupan - Bangus Festival

Characteristics:

The festival is held in the beautiful city of Dagupan in the province of Pangasinan. The festival is held in celebration of the city's thriving aquaculture, specializing in bangus or milkfish. Every year, tourists from all walks of life make their way to the city to tantalize their taste buds with this unique delicacy.

Religious Festival

Sinulog Festival

Characteristics:

This is by far the most celebrated festival in the Philippines. Locals and tourists fly to Cebu to commemorate the feast of Santo Niño de Cebu, a Roman Catholic icon given to the islanders by the Spanish upon their arrival in 1521.

Every Feast Day of Santo Niño (or the third Sunday of January), the weekend event is marked by the Sinulog Fluvial Parade on Saturday and the all-day Santo Niño Procession right after Sunday Mass. Don’t mistake this procession for a mundane trot down the street — it’s more like a Philippine Mardi Gras filled with merriment, dancing and elaborate costumes.
