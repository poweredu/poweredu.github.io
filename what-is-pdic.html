2. What are the roles of the PDIC in the banks?​


Answer:

PDIC is a government instrumentality created in 1963 by virtue of Republic Act 3591 to insure the deposits of all banks. PDIC exists to protect depositors by providing deposit insurance coverage for the depositing public and help promote financial stability.

8. 2. Assessment at Collectionbangko upang maseguro ang kanilang deposit liabilities. Ibinibigay ng PDIC ang assessment na dapat bayaran ng bangko. Ang PDIC din ang nangongolekta ng assessment mula sa mga bangko. Ang halaga ng assessment na kinokolekta ng PDIC ay katumbas sa 1/5 ng 1 * 0/o ng kabuuang deposit liabilities ng bangko.basahin po tapos gumawa po ng explanation tungkol dito kung ano po talaga yung sinasabi like ieexplain mo. pls​


Answer:

Ang "assessment" ay isang bayarin na kinakailangan bayaran ng mga bangko sa PDIC o Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation bilang bahagi ng pagiging miyembro nila ng nasabing korporasyon. Ang PDIC ay isang ahensiya ng gobyerno na nagbibigay ng insurance protection sa deposito ng mga indibidwal at korporasyon sa mga bangko sa Pilipinas.

Ang assessment ay isang paraan upang matiyak na may sapat na pondo ang PDIC upang matugunan ang mga posibleng pagbabayad ng mga kaso ng pagkawala ng deposito ng bangko. Sa madaling salita, ito ay isang uri ng "premium" na binabayaran ng mga bangko upang magkaroon ng proteksyon ang kanilang mga depositante.

Ayon sa paliwanag, ang halaga ng assessment na kinokolekta ng PDIC ay katumbas ng 1/5 ng 1% ng kabuuang deposit liabilities ng bangko. Ibig sabihin, kung halimbawa ay mayroong isang bangko na mayroong kabuuang deposit liabilities na P1 bilyon, ang assessment na dapat nilang bayaran sa PDIC ay P2 milyon (P1 bilyon x 0.002).

Ang assessment na ito ay isa sa mga responsibilidad ng mga bangko bilang miyembro ng PDIC. Sa kabila nito, ito ay isang mahalagang aspeto ng pagiging miyembro ng PDIC dahil magbibigay ito ng proteksyon sa kanilang depositante at magbibigay ng kumpiyansa sa publiko sa kanilang banko.

24. 1. Salapi 2. BSP 3. Kooperatiba 4. Bangko 5. GSIS 6. SSS 7. PDIC 8. SEC 9. Hindi Bangko 10. Landbank


Answer:

The given list appears to be a mix of different financial institutions and regulatory bodies in the Philippines. Here's a brief description of each:

Salapi - Salapi is the Filipino word for money, which is a medium of exchange used in transactions of goods and services.

BSP - BSP or the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas is the central bank of the Philippines, which is responsible for implementing monetary policy, regulating banks and financial institutions, and issuing Philippine currency.

Kooperatiba - Kooperatiba is the Filipino term for a cooperative, which is an organization formed and owned by its members to provide economic and social benefits to the members.

Bangko - Bangko is the Filipino term for a bank, which is a financial institution that accepts deposits, lends money, and provides other financial services to individuals, businesses, and organizations.

GSIS - GSIS or the Government Service Insurance System is a social insurance institution in the Philippines that provides retirement, disability, survivorship, and other benefits to government employees.

SSS - SSS or the Social Security System is a government agency in the Philippines that provides social security protection to private sector employees, self-employed individuals, and voluntary members.

PDIC - PDIC or the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation is a government agency that provides deposit insurance to depositors in banks and other financial institutions in the Philippines.

SEC - SEC or the Securities and Exchange Commission is a government agency that regulates the securities industry in the Philippines, including the registration of securities and the enforcement of securities laws.

Hindi Bangko - Hindi Bangko is a Filipino term that means "not a bank," which is not a specific financial institution but rather a category of entities that are not licensed to operate as a bank.

Landbank - Landbank or Land Bank of the Philippines is a government-owned financial institution in the Philippines that provides banking and financial services to farmers, fishermen, and other sectors of the economy
