3. what is gravimetric analysis and how is done?​


Answer:

Gravimetric analysis describes a set of methods used in analytical chemistry for the quantitative determination of an analyte based on its mass.

General Procedure:

Weigh the sample to be analysed.

Dissolve the sample in a suitable solvent, eg, water.

Add an excess of the precipitating reagent to precipitate the analyte.

Filter the mixture to separate the precipitate from the solution. ...

Wash the precipitate to remove any impurities.

7. devise a plan on how you will perform gravimetric analysis by giving the fundamental step and analytical process​


Explanation:

Gravimetric analysis, a method of quantitative chemical analysis in which the constituent sought is converted into a substance (of known composition) that can be separated from the sample and weighed. The steps commonly followed in gravimetric analysis are preparation of a solution containing a known weight of the sample,separation of the desired constituent, weighing the isolated constituent, and computation of the amount of the particular constituent in the sample from the observed weight of the isolated substance.

Why they still use gravimetric method?

The gravimetric method is the most extensively used method for determining the amount of PM suspended in the air. The weight difference of filters pre- and post-sampling is used to calculate PM concentration in this method. When samples are gathered by gravimetric sampling, an extra analytical method is always required to examine them. Two preweighted filters are stacked one on top of the other in a single filter holder in this technique. The sampling filter is the first filter in front of the collector, while the control filter is the second. On the sample filter, PM is collected. After sampling, both filters are weighed, and any changes in the control filter are used as a corrective factor for weighing the sampling filter. The gravimetric analysis' precision is dependent on the elimination of electrostatic influences.

30. If AD is an angle bisector of LAB and mZDAB= 49°, what is m/LAB? (Sample) answer: 10 degrees)​


If AD is an angle bisector of ∠LAB and m∠DAB = 49°, what is m∠LAB?

The angle bisector divides the angle into two equal parts. That means, in this problem, m∠DAB is equal to m∠LAD.

m∠DAB = m∠LADm∠DAB = 49°m∠LAD = 49°

m∠LAB is the sum of m∠DAB and m∠LAD. Let's put it like this:

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LAD

Substitute the value/measure of ∠DAB and ∠LAD to the equation above. Solve to find the answer to the original problem.

m∠LAB = m∠DAB + m∠LADm∠LAB = 49° + 49°m∠LAB = 98°

Thus, the answer to the question: “what is m∠LAB?”, is 98°.
