22. sanitation standard operating procedure example​


Answer:

Sanitation Standard Operating Procedure

1. Ensure that all employees in the workplace are aware of the sanitation guidelines.

2. Establish and maintain a cleaning schedule for all areas of the workplace.

3. Utilize the appropriate cleaning products for each area, based on safety and effectiveness.

4. Ensure that all cleaning tools and equipment are appropriately stored and maintained.

5. Disinfect all surfaces and equipment that are in contact with food products.

6. Provide adequate hand-washing facilities and ensure that they are kept clean and stocked with soap and disposable towels.

7. Educate employees on proper hand-washing protocol and ensure they are following it.

8. Sanitize all food-contact surfaces, utensils, and equipment after each use.

9. Store food properly in designated areas to prevent cross-contamination.

10. Dispose of any expired food products in accordance with local regulations.

11. Monitor and maintain the temperature of food products to prevent spoilage.

12. Ensure all areas of the workplace are maintained in a clean and sanitary condition.

13. Document all sanitation activities and corrective actions taken.

14. Follow all applicable government regulations on sanitation and food safety.
