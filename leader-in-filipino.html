Answer:

(1) The early Filipino society had a major impact on the way of life of the early Filipinos. The traditional Filipino culture was based on a strong sense of family and community, with a focus on respect for elders and ancestors. This was reflected in the way that people interacted with each other, as well as in their religious beliefs and practices. The traditional Filipino lifestyle also emphasized hard work, cooperation, and hospitality. These values were passed down from generation to generation, helping to shape the Filipino culture that exists today.

(2) In the early Filipino society, laws were used to promote peace and order in the barangays. These laws were based on the customs and traditions of the people, and they were enforced by the local leaders. Laws regulated behavior, such as prohibiting fighting or stealing, and they also established punishments for those who broke them. These laws helped to ensure that everyone in the barangay respected each other's rights and followed accepted norms of behavior. This helped to create a peaceful and orderly environment in which people could live together harmoniously.

(3) The system of laws and the judicial process of the early Filipinos was based on a combination of indigenous customs and traditions, as well as Spanish colonial law. The judicial process was largely informal, with disputes being settled by village elders or local leaders. In contrast, the present system of laws and judicial process is based on a modern legal code that is largely derived from Spanish civil law. The judicial process is now formalized, with cases being heard in courts of law presided over by professional judges.

(4) At present, the qualities that should be considered in choosing a leader include: integrity, intelligence, communication skills, empathy, vision, decisiveness, resilience, and the ability to inspire and motivate others. Additionally, a leader should have a strong moral compass and be able to make difficult decisions in difficult times. They should also have a good understanding of the current political and economic climate and be able to think strategically. Finally, they should have a commitment to social justice and equity.

(5) The early Filipino society was largely based on communal living, with a strong emphasis on family and community. People were more closely connected to their environment and relied heavily on subsistence farming and fishing for their livelihoods. They also had a strong sense of identity and pride in their culture, which was expressed through art, music, dance, and storytelling.

In contrast, the present Filipino society is much more modernized and industrialized. People are more likely to live in cities or urban areas, with access to technology and modern amenities. There is also a greater emphasis on individualism and consumerism, as well as a growing divide between the wealthy and the poor. Despite this, Filipinos still maintain a strong sense of identity and pride in their culture.
