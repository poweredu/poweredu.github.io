3. give a long reflection to the pixels 2015 movie ​


The 2015 movie "Pixels" is a science fiction action-comedy film directed by Chris Columbus and starring Adam Sandler, Kevin James, and Josh Gad. The movie centers around a group of video game experts who are recruited by the US government to fight an alien invasion that takes the form of classic video game characters.

One of the most notable things about the movie is its nostalgia factor. For those who grew up playing classic video games like Pac-Man and Space Invaders, seeing these beloved characters come to life on the big screen is an exciting and entertaining experience. The movie effectively captures the retro aesthetic of the 80s and 90s video game era, with vibrant colors, pixelated graphics, and an overall fun and lighthearted tone.

However, while the movie certainly has its entertaining moments, it falls short in a number of areas. The humor is often crude and juvenile, relying on tired stereotypes and predictable punchlines. The plot is also quite thin, with a lack of depth and character development that makes it difficult to care about the fate of the main characters.

Furthermore, the movie has been criticized for its treatment of female characters, who are largely sidelined and reduced to love interests for the male leads. This is especially disappointing given the growing demand for more diverse and inclusive representation in movies and other forms of media.

In conclusion, while "Pixels" may be a fun and nostalgic trip down memory lane for fans of classic video games, it ultimately falls short in terms of its humor, plot, and representation of diverse characters. While it may provide some entertainment value, it ultimately fails to deliver a memorable and well-crafted movie experience.

Answer:

What is a pixel?

The pixel -- a word invented from picture element -- is the basic unit of programmable color on a computer display or in a computer image. Think of it as a logical -- rather than a physical -- unit.

Pixels are the smallest unit in a digital display. Up to millions of pixels make up an image or video on a device's screen. Each pixel comprises a subpixel that emits a red, green and blue (RGB) color, which displays at different intensities. The RGB color components make up the gamut of different colors that appear on a display or computer monitor.

When referencing the resolution of a display, numbers like 1920 x 1080 refer to the number of pixels.
