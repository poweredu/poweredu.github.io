3. Function media of Soviet Media​


Answer:

Soviet media might be seen to have three phases. In the first period, from 1986 to 1990, the Soviet leadership encouraged criticism from the news media, emphasising the constructive nature of such activity rather than its perception as `dissidence'. However, the state and Party structures were still the parameters within which `glasnost' was developed. The second phase started in August 1990 with the passing of legislation which ended the Party's monopoly control of the media, opening up opportunities for criticism of the whole political system and reflecting the setting up of new political parties. It made it difficult for opponents of reform to halt the process of change in the Soviet Union. The third phase of `media reconstruction' after 1991 was in the context of Russian society coming to terms with the break up of the certainties of Communist authoritarian rule in the USSR, the emergence of virulent nationalisms, and the definition of the role of the media in the development of a capitalist, democratic Russia.
