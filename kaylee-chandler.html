5. Kaylee and Rory have a circular swimming pool. The pool has a cover that fits snuggly over the top of it. If the radius of the pool is 11 ft, what is the area of the cover?​


SURFACE AREA

==================================

Problem

Kaylee and Rory have a circular swimming pool. The pool has a cover that fits snuggly over the top of it. If the radius of the pool is 11 ft, what is the area of the cover?

Solution

'Solve this problem using the formula of the surface area of a circle, then the substitution method.

[tex]\bold{Formula\:||\:Surface\:Area=4πr²}[/tex][tex]\tt{S_a = 4( 3.14 ) ( 11 \:feet)²}[/tex]

'Now Multiply the radius two times in itself then, multiply again the answer in Pi ( π = 3.14 ).

[tex]\tt{S_a = 4πr²}[/tex][tex]\tt{S_a = 4( 3.14 ) (11\:feet)²}[/tex][tex]\tt{S_a = 4(3.14) (121\:feet²) }[/tex][tex]\tt{S_a = 4( 379.94) }[/tex][tex]\tt{\green{1,519.76 \:feet²}= Surface\:Area}[/tex]

Final Answer

Hence, if the radius of the pool is 11ft then the area of the cover is 1,519.76 feet²
