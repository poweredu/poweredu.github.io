11. Is the athletes/ or someone undergoing coaching accountable to a desirable coaching outcome? Or is it only to the coach solely? Why?​


Answer:

Both the athletes and the coach are accountable for a desirable coaching outcome.

The coach is responsible for creating and implementing effective training plans, providing feedback, and motivating the athletes. They are also responsible for creating a positive and supportive team environment where athletes feel encouraged to reach their full potential.

On the other hand, athletes are responsible for putting in the effort, following the training plans, and communicating effectively with the coach. They are also responsible for taking ownership of their own development and progress towards their goals.

Ultimately, the success of coaching is a collaborative effort between the coach and the athletes. Both parties must work together, communicate effectively, and hold each other accountable to achieve the desired outcomes.

19. four students in the class weighed themselves. Carter was 15 kilograms lighter than adrain. Gray was twice as heavy as Carter anf Jeremy was seven kilograms heavier than Gary. If Jeremy weighed 71 kilograms what was Adrian's weight?


Answer:

Algebraic Equations

The weight of Adrian is 47 kilograms.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Carter’s weight – he is 15 kilograms lighter than Adrian Gary’s weight – he is twice as heavy as Carter Jeremy’s weight – he is 7 kilograms heavier than Gary

Find:

The weight of Adrian in kilograms

In order to find the answer to this question, the information given should be translated into an algebraic expression.

Let x be the weight of Adrian. Therefore:

Carter’s weight = x – 15

Gary’s weight = 2 (x – 15)

Jeremy’s weight = 2 (x - 15)

Therefore, the equation should be written as:

2 (x - 15) + 7 = 71

Solution:

2 (x - 15) + 7 = 71

2x - 30 + 7 = 71

2x - 23 = 71

2x = 71 + 23

2x = 94

2x ÷ 2 = 94 ÷ 2

x = 47

Therefore, Adrian's weight is 47 kilograms.
