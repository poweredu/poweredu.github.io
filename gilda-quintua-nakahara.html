14. summary of the story the eye of a needleby gilda cordero?pahelp po di po kasi ako marunong mag summary.​


Answer:

"The Eye of a Needle" is a short story by Gilda Cordero-Fernando, set in a small village in the Philippines. The story revolves around a poor couple, Gaudencio and Jacinta, who dream of owning a sewing machine so they can start a business and improve their lives.

One day, they receive an unexpected gift of a brand new sewing machine from a wealthy couple, Don Vicente and his wife, as a gesture of gratitude for the kindness they had shown to them in the past. However, the couple's joy is short-lived when they discover that the sewing machine is missing its needle, and they cannot afford to buy a replacement.

Gaudencio, determined to find a needle for the sewing machine, embarks on a journey to the nearest town, but he faces many challenges along the way, including hunger, exhaustion, and danger. He finally finds a needle, but he must sell his most prized possession, a rooster, to pay for it.

When Gaudencio returns home with the needle, he discovers that Jacinta had sold the sewing machine to buy food for their children. Disappointed, Gaudencio decides to leave his family and start a new life, but he eventually realizes that he cannot leave his responsibilities behind.

In the end, Gaudencio and Jacinta reconcile, and they start a sewing business with the help of the sewing machine and the needle. The story highlights the struggles of the poor and the power of determination and perseverance in the face of adversity.

16. Help me guys ! Summary of “Lundagin mo baby” (go for it) by Gilda Cordero-Fernando.


“Lundagin Mo Baby, (Go For It)” by Gilda Cordero-Fernando Summary

When times get rough, there are several motivational quotes that uplift the dampened spirits. “Lundagin Mo Baby” is not just another new colloquial term but a phrase that expresses encouragement. Reaching new heights can only be attained if you aim to jump for it. There is no perfect time to say it but in every moment where feelings of desperation and hopelessness are at their peak. If there’s another phrase that best matches it, it would be this: “aim high.”
