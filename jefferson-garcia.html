11. Do you think Thomas Jefferson was a good or bad person and why?


Explanation:

Thomas Jefferson was a complex individual who contributed greatly to the development of the United States, but also had personal flaws and contradictions.

On the one hand, Thomas Jefferson played a significant role in the American Revolution, and was a champion of individual rights and liberties. He was one of the primary authors of the Declaration of Independence, which proclaimed the principles of equality, freedom, and self-government. He also advocated for limited government, separation of church and state, and the abolition of slavery.

On the other hand, Jefferson was a slave owner who maintained the institution of slavery throughout his life, despite his professed opposition to it. He also held views that are considered racist and sexist by modern standards. His personal life was marked by scandals, including his relationship with Sally Hemings, a slave whom he owned and had children with.

Therefore, the assessment of Jefferson's character and legacy is a matter of interpretation and perspective. Some may view him as a hero and visionary who helped shape the course of American history, while others may view him as a flawed and hypocritical figure who fell short of his own ideals. Ultimately, the judgment of Jefferson's legacy is left to historians and the public to decide.

13. Hamilton and Jefferson and their views on the military


Answer:

Alexander Hamilton and Thomas Jefferson had different views on military spending. Hamilton believed that a strong military was necessary for national security and that government should be able to raise taxes to support it. Jefferson, on the other hand, was more skeptical of a standing military and preferred diplomacy and economic sanctions as a means of protecting the nation. Jefferson argued that a standing military was a threat to liberty, while Hamilton argued that it was necessary to protect the nation.
