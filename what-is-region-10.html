What is the area of the shaded region if the diameter of the circle is 10 cm long?

To get the area of the shaded refion, I will subtract the area of the circle from the area of the square. That is,

Area of the shaded region = Area of square - Area of circle

Area of the shaded region = (s²) - (π×r²)

Area of the shaded region = (10 cm)² - π × (5 cm)²

Area of the shaded region = 100 sq. cm - 3.14 × (25 sq. cm)

Area of the shaded region = 100 - 78.5 sq. cm

23. what is the area of the region between the circle and the square is inscribed in a circle whose circumference is 10 square root of 2 and pi?​


Answer:

50π - 100

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's start with the given first:

Circle's circumference = 10√2 π

The problem can be represented in this diagram (in dar man.png):

We needed to solve for the BLUE AREA,  by subtracting the area of the CIRCLE to the area of the SQUARE.

We can observe in the figure that the diagonal drawn to the square is also A DIAMETER of the circle.

Since circumference of the circle is diameter * π or πD;

10√2 π = πD (Divide both sides by π)

10√2 π / π = πD/π

10√2 = D

The circle's diameter (and the square's diagonal) is 10√2.

Going back to the figure (in dar man.png), the diagonal in the square formed a RIGHT TRIANGLE, therefore, we can use the Pythagorean Theorem (hypotenuse^2 = leg^2 + other leg^2, with the solved diagonal (being the HYPOTENUSE, the longest side), and the square's sides as the legs) to find the side of the square;

hypotenuse^2 = leg^2 + other leg^2

(10√2)^2 = leg^2 + other leg^2

(10√2)^2 = 2(leg^2) (The legs were added together because they just have the same length)

200 = 2(leg^2) (The left side was simplified)

200 / 2 = 2(leg^2) / 2 (Divide 2 to both sides)

100 = leg^2

√(100) = √(leg^2) (Find the square root of both sides)

10 = leg (Simplify)

Based from the solution, the leg (or the side of the square was 10).

Now, to find the circle's area, WE MUST FIRST FIND ITS RADIUS.

We now that the diameter of the circle is 10√2, we have to divide 10√2 by 2, to find the radius, which is: 10√2 / 2 or 5√2.

Now, for the final part of the problem, we can now solve for the area's of the 2 polygons (circle and square):

For the area of the circle (with 5√2 as the radius (r));

Area = πr^2  

Area = π(5√2)^2

Area = 50π

The area of the circle is 50π.

For the area of the square (with 10 as the side (s));

Area = s^2

Area = (10)^2

Area = 100

The area of the square is 100.

We have to subtract the areas of the circle and square to find the area of the blue region (the one we needed for the problem), which is equal to:
