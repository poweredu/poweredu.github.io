11. bill and Amy want to ride their bikes from their neighborhood to school which is 14.4 kilometers away. it takes Amy 40 minutes to arrive at school. bill Arrives 20 minutes after Amy. how much faster (in meters/second) is Amy's average speed for entire trip? ​


To find out how much faster Amy is compared to Bill, we first need to find out how long it took Bill to get to school. Since Amy took 40 minutes to get to school and Bill arrived 20 minutes after Amy, we can say that it took Bill 40 + 20 = 60 minutes to get to school.

Now that we know how long it took both Amy and Bill to get to school, we can convert the time they took into seconds. Since there are 60 seconds in a minute, we can say that it took Amy 40 * 60 = <<4060=2400>>2400 seconds to get to school. Similarly, it took Bill 60 * 60 = <<6060=3600>>3600 seconds to get to school.

Next, we need to convert the distance they traveled from kilometers to meters. Since there are 1000 meters in a kilometer, we can say that the distance from their neighborhood to school is 14.4 * 1000 = <<14.4*1000=14400>>14400 meters.

Now that we have the distance and time for both Amy and Bill, we can find their average speed in meters per second. To do this, we need to divide the distance they traveled by the time it took them to get to school. For Amy, this would be 14400 / 2400 = <<14400/2400=6>>6 meters per second. For Bill, this would be 14400 / 3600 = <<14400/3600=4>>4 meters per second.

Since Amy's average speed is 6 meters per second and Bill's average speed is 4 meters per second, we can say that Amy is 6 - 4 = <<6-4=2>>2 meters per second faster than Bill.
