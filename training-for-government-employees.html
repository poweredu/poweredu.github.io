What are transitional devices?

Cohesive devices are another name for transitional words and phrases. They improve the writing's flow and make the connections between ideas, phrases, and paragraphs more clear. Transitional elements act as links between different sections of your paper. The reader can interpret the concepts that a paper develops with the use of these cues. Words or phrases that serve as transitional devices help move thought from one sentence to the next, from one idea to another, or from one paragraph to the next.

Conjunctive adverbs and conjunctions are the two main categories of transitions. A referent is the name given to another form of transition. The relationships between sentences are made clearer by the use of transitional words and phrases. Depending on their purposes, transitions can be categorized into several classes.
