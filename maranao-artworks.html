1. why are the artworks of maranao considered their arts as sophisticated​


Answer:

Okir or okil is the term for rectilinear and curvilinear plant-based designs and folk motifs that can be usually found among the Moro and Lumad peoples of the Southern Philippines, as well as parts of Sabah. It is particularly associated with the artwork of the Maranao and Sama (Badjao) people, although it can also be found to a lesser extent among neighboring Maguindanao, Iranun, Tausug, Yakan, and Lumad groups. The design elements vary among these ethnic groups, with the greatest refinement being found among the Maranao.[

4. How does the traditions/history of Maranao is being reflected in their artworks particularly the Sarimanok?​


The Sarimanok is a legendary bird of the Maranao people and is an important symbol in their art and culture. It is used to represent wealth, prosperity, and good luck. The Sarimanok is an ornately decorated bird carved out of wood or crafted out of fabric. It is typically displayed in festivals, ceremonies, and celebrations. The Sarimanok is thought to represent courage, strength, and fertility, and is a reminder of the Maranao people’s strong connection to their ancestors. The elaborate designs of the symbols carved into the Sarimanok also reflect the Maranao people’s rich cultural heritage.

Answer:

The Maranao people of the Philippines have a rich history and cultural heritage that is reflected in their traditional artworks, including the Sarimanok.

The Sarimanok is a bird in Maranao mythology that is said to symbolize good fortune and divine protection. In Maranao art, the Sarimanok is often depicted as a colorful and elaborate bird with a long, curved beak, feathered wings, and a long, flowing tail. The bird is often shown in a graceful and dynamic pose, with intricate patterns and designs that reflect the Maranao's love for ornate and intricate details.

The Sarimanok is not just a decorative piece but also has cultural and spiritual significance. It is often used in important Maranao celebrations and rituals, such as weddings, harvest festivals, and other important occasions. In these contexts, the Sarimanok serves as a symbol of the Maranao people's connection to their history, culture, and spirituality.

In summary, the Sarimanok and other traditional Maranao artworks reflect the rich cultural and spiritual traditions of the Maranao people. These artworks are not only decorative but also serve as important symbols of identity, spirituality, and cultural heritage.

9. this pattern is the distuishing marks of the maranao arts and muslim influenced artworks in southern philippines​


Answer:

Okkir or okkil (sometimes spelled as okir)refers to a geometric and flowing designs based on leaf and vine patterns. This pattern is the distinguishing marks of the Maranao arts and Muslim influenced artworks in southern Philippines and even in other southeast Asia.
