5. Jamie and Lynn each worked a different number of days, but each earned the same amount of money. Jamie earned ₱150 per day and Lynn earned ₱100 per day. Lynn worked 5 more days than Jamie. How much did each earn?


Answer:

Jamie and Lynn each earned 1500 pesos.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's assume Jamie worked for x number of days.

Therefore, Lynn worked for x+5 days.

Jamie earned 150 pesos per day, so his total earnings would be:

150x

Lynn earned 100 pesos per day, so her total earnings would be:

100(x+5)

Since they both earned the same amount of money, we can set these two expressions equal to each other and solve for x:

150x = 100(x+5)

150x = 100x + 500

50x = 500

x = 10

So Jamie worked for 10 days, and Lynn worked for 15 days (5 more than Jamie).

Jamie earned 150 pesos per day for 10 days, so his total earnings are:

150 x 10 = 1500 pesos

Lynn earned 100 pesos per day for 15 days, so her total earnings are:

100 x 15 = 1500 pesos

Therefore, Jamie and Lynn each earned 1500 pesos.

8. In the barn, the ratio of chickens to sheep is 12:7. If there are 49 sheep, how many animals are in the barn?'


Let's say that x = # of chickens, and y = # of pigs

If there are 13 animals altogether, that means that

x + y = 13

If there are 40 legs together, we know that chickens have 2 legs, while pigs have 4, so the total number of legs would be:

2x + 4y = 40

We now have two different equations, with the same two variables, so we can rearrange and substitute one equation into the other to solve. We can rearrange the first equation as:

x + y = 13

x = 13 - y

Let's plug this expression for "x" into the second equation:

2(13-y) + 4y = 40

Now we have an equation with only one variable, so we can use algebra to solve for y:

26 - 2y + 4y = 40

2y = 14

y = 7, there must be 7 pigs.

From the first equation that we rearranged,

x = 13 - y

Now that we know the numerical value of "y", we can plug that in:

x = 13 - 7

x = 6, there must be 6 chickens

15. lynne jogs daily around a circular track of diameter 25 m for a total distance of 942 m in 5.0 minutes. (a) How many times did lynne jog around the track? (b) what is Lynne's average angular speed in radian/s?


(a) Lynne jogged about 12 times around the track

(b) Lynne's average angular speed is about 0.25 rad/s

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Further explanation

Given:

Diameter of circular track = d = 25 m

Total Distance = D = 942 m

Time Taken = t = 5.0 minutes = 300 seconds

Asked:

(a) N = ?

(b) ω = ?

Solution:

Question (a):

[tex]N = \texttt{Total Distance} \div \texttt{Circumference of Track}[/tex]

[tex]N = D \div ( \pi d )[/tex]

[tex]N = 942 \div ( \pi \times 25 )[/tex]

[tex]N = 37.68 \div \pi[/tex]

[tex]N \approx 12[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Question (b):

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div T[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div ( \frac{t}{N} )[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi N \div t[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi(12) \div 300[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 24\pi \div 300[/tex]

[tex]\omega = 2\pi \div 25[/tex]

[tex]\omega \approx 0.25 \texttt{ rad/s}[/tex]

24. What problem does Lynn and the others are facing in "Poison"?​


Answer:

To put it simply, willful ignorance. Lynn demonstrates willful ignorance multiple times in the short story. The first instance occurs when Lynn makes the decision to stay in Cape Town and get drunk two nights in a row and “left it terribly late, despite all the warnings.” Following this, she neglects a ride out of town in a taxi in favor of waiting for rescue. Even at the very end of the story Lynn continues to believe she will be rescued and does not attempt to escape on the bike at the petrol station. Instead, she chooses to literally turn her back to the problem and wait. She makes a conscious decision ignore her environment, despite it getting progressively worse and posing more harm to her.
