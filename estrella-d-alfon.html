5. "Summary the story of Servant Girl by Estrella D. Alfon"​


SUMMARY THE STORY OF SERVANT GIRL

Servant girl story tells about a housemaid. She is a beautiful and humble woman. Her fate in this story is to be physically and verbally abused by an obnoxious mistress.

Rosa fantasizes about a cochero which she names "Angel" who becomes known as Pedro. From her imagination, Pedro is a figure who is authoritative, gallant but gentle to herself and becomes her life savior from this slavery.

Her admirer, Sancho, on the other hand is rude and abusive towards her and violently hurts her. She ran from her employer's house and Sancho's rough arms. She is eager to find her "Angel" who will become known as Pedro. She imagined him to be different from other men, manlier, gentler, and her savior from her miserable life with mistress. Angel becomes the hope and embodiment of Rosa's desire to be free from slavery.

Rosa has an admirer named Sancho, one day she gets harsh and painful treatment from Sancho which makes her run away from her master's house. She hoped that a savior "angel" would come to save him.

But the angel never existed. It was just Rosa's imagination. Finally Rosa still had to accept the fate of being a miserable slave.

14. what is my indipendent critique of the story servant girl by estrella alfon​


Answer:

"Servant Girl" by Estrella Alfon.

One possible critique of the story is that it perpetuates gender inequality and reinforces the idea of women being subordinate to men. Rosa's character is portrayed as submissive and helpless, and her fate is determined by the men in her life. Tony, the man who promises to rescue her, ultimately betrays her, leaving her alone and vulnerable. Meanwhile, the male employers in her former household have the power to offer her forgiveness and assistance, highlighting the patriarchal nature of the society in which the story is set.

Another critique is that the story may be too simplistic and one-dimensional in its portrayal of social issues such as poverty and class differences. The characters are either rich or poor, and their interactions are primarily based on their economic status. The story does not delve deeper into the social and political structures that perpetuate poverty and inequality, nor does it provide a nuanced portrayal of the characters' motivations and struggles.

Overall, while "Servant Girl" is a compelling and emotionally charged story, it may be limited in its scope and portrayal of complex social issues.

24. give me 1 example of the moralist of the story of servant girl by estrella alfon​


Answer:

The story "Servant Girl" by Estrella Alfon is about a young woman named Rosa who works as a servant in a wealthy household. She is mistreated by her employers and longs for a better life. She meets a man named Tony who promises to take her away from her miserable life and marry her.

However, Rosa's dreams are shattered when Tony abandons her, leaving her alone and pregnant. She is forced to return to her former employers and ask for their forgiveness and help. They offer her assistance, but the shame and humiliation of her situation weigh heavily on her.

The moral lesson of the story is that we should not be blinded by our desires and dreams to the point that we lose sight of what is truly important. Rosa's desire for a better life and escape from her circumstances led her to make choices that ultimately caused her great pain and suffering. The story serves as a cautionary tale about the consequences of pursuing our dreams without considering the possible outcomes and the people we may hurt along the way.
