1. How can workstation locations foster teamwork and open communication​


Answer:

Teamwork is an essential part of a successful and nurturing work environment. When staff learns to work together -- and work together well -- they not only get more accomplished but also come up with innovative ways of doing things as they spin ideas off one another. However, there are certain tips to consider if you are trying to foster a better sense of teamwork in your workspace.

Assign teams instead of individuals to projects. These projects tend to be more successful because no one person is afraid of "messing up" on the project because the team shares in the responsibility. Moreover, projects can be completed faster because several people are attacking them versus putting all of the responsibility on one person.

Hold regular brainstorming sessions to approach new projects. This teaches employees how to work well together and bounce ideas off one another. Encourage everyone in the room to speak up and contribute at least one idea, no matter how zany it may seem. Sometimes, the most creative ideas end up being viable approaches to professional tasks.

5. clarity is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended messagetrue or false​


Answer:

To answer the question simply, this statement is false.

"Clarity is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended message."

The statement above is not correct. Clarity is the quality or characteristic of being understandable and coherent. It is not an act but it is the goal of good communication.

Instead of clarity, clarification is the right word to use. The statement is going to be true if it instead said: "Clarification is any communicative act that must be fostered as it will facilitate better understanding of the intended message."
