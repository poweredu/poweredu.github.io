3. what is athmosphere?​

An atmosphere is a layer or a set of layers of gases surrounding a planet or other material body, that is held in place by the gravity of that body. An atmosphere is more likely to be retained if the gravity it is subject to is high and the temperature of the atmosphere is low.

5. what are the layers of the athmosphere​


Answer:

troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere and thermosphere.

Layers Of The Athmosphere​:

TROPOSPHERE

It extends from 0 to 10 km high. It is the densest layer of the atmosphere. It contains 75% of the gases of the atmosphere. Dust and water vapor are found in this layer Weather phenomena occurs in this layer. Its upper boundary is the tropopause.

STRATOSPHERE

It extends from 10-30 km high. Less dense and dry layer. Ozone layer is present in this layer which absorbs the ultraviolet radiation and turns into heat. Its upper boundary is the stratopause.

MESOSPHERE

It extends about 30-50 km. The temperature is very low. Its base has a  temperature of -3ᵒC and drops at -85ᵒC at the  top. Its upper boundary is the mesopause.

THERMOSPHERE

It extends about 50 to 400 km high. The temperature in this layer increases throughout  the layer. Atmospheric gases are very thin in this layer. Lonosphere plays important role in radio and  telecommunication of the Earth.Layer that is located on its lower region is ionosphere. The ionosphere acts as a wall that reflects waves. Aurora borealis and Aurora Australis are  present in this area. Its upper boundary is the thermopause.

EXOSPHERE

The outermost region of a planet's atmosphere. Atmospheric gases are very thin. Temperature and pressure are very low. It is the boundary of the Earth and the outer  space.

The asthenosphere is the highly viscous, mechanically weak and ductile region of the upper mantle of the Earth. It lies below the lithosphere, at depths between approximately 80 and 200 km below the surface. The lithosphere–asthenosphere boundary is usually referred to as LAB.
