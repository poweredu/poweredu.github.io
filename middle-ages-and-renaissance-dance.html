German's damson, which means to stretch or drag, is where the term dance originates. It evolved as a pure manifestation of synchronized feeling and action. Because it reacts to historical and religious events as well as social and political remarks, dance is seen as the mirror of society. People still dance primarily for four reasons: (a) to appease gods; (b) to appease others; (c) to appease themselves; and (d) to foster community within an ethnic group or social interaction, despite the vast comparative variations in time and culture.

During the Prehistoric Period, dance was a significant form of social expression and religious rite in primitive culture. It is founded on superstition and imbued with magic, and was utilized as a means of expression and to strengthen tribe unity and power. Lead dancers known as shamans served as the tribes' healers, spiritual authorities, and guardians.
