1. Where was the last poem written by Jose Rizal hidden?


Answer:

The manuscript appears to have been folded into four parts and was hidden inside an alcohol stove or burner, a gift from Trinidad H. Pardo de Tavera, which Rizal used from travels abroad till his last days in Fort Santiago.

QUESTION

Where was the last poem written by Jose Rizal hidden?

ANSWER

Mi último adiós

"Mi último adiós" (English: "My Last Farewell") is a poem written by Filipino propagandist and writer Dr. José Rizal before his execution by firing squad on December 30, 1896. The piece was one of the last notes he wrote before his death. Another that he had written was found in his shoe, but because the text was illegible, its contents remain a mystery.

5. What is your reflection (introduction, body and conclusion) to the poem "My last thought" by Jose rizal​


Answer:

"My Last Thought" is a poignant poem that reflects the speaker's contemplation on the fleeting nature of life and the inevitability of death. The poem is divided into two stanzas, each with four lines, and it follows a simple and straightforward structure.

In the first stanza, the speaker reflects on the brevity of life and the fact that everything in this world is temporary. He compares life to a fleeting dream and describes the beauty and transience of nature.

In the second stanza, the speaker contemplates his own mortality and expresses his desire to be remembered after his death. He hopes that his legacy will live on and that his memory will continue to inspire others to strive for greatness.

Overall, "My Last Thought" is a powerful reflection on the nature of life and death. The poem is a reminder that life is short and that we must make the most of the time we have. It is also a call to action, encouraging us to leave a lasting legacy that will continue to inspire future generations. The poem is a testament to Rizal's enduring influence and his commitment to inspiring his fellow Filipinos to greatness.
