The standard definition of political instability is the propensity of a government collapse either because of conflicts or rampant competition between various political parties. Also, the occurrence of a government change increases the likelihood of subsequent changes. Political instability tends to be persistent.

Economic growth and political stability are deeply interconnected. On the one hand, the uncertainty associated with an unstable political environment may reduce investment and the pace of economic development. 16. what are some of the political policies made by the Spanish colonial administration that are still evident in the Philippines today?


Some of the political policies made by the Spanish colonial administration that are still evident in the Philippines today include the following :

1. The Spanish colonial administration established a centralized form of government in the Philippines, which is still evident in the country's current political system.

2. The Spanish colonial administration also implemented the policy of "divide and rule" in the Philippines, which has contributed to the country's current social and political divisions.

3. The Spanish colonial administration also implemented a policy of religious intolerance, which has resulted in the current situation in the Philippines where there is a high degree of religious intolerance and discrimination.
