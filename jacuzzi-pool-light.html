10. A light wave traveling in air passes into the water in a swimming pool at an angle of incidence of 35˚. Calculate the angle of refraction of the light in water.​


Answer:

According to Snell's law of refraction, the ratio of the sines of the angles of incidence and refraction is equal to the ratio of the velocities of light in the two media:

sin(incidence angle) / sin(refraction angle) = velocity of light in air / velocity of light in water

Assuming the velocity of light in air is approximately equal to its value in a vacuum, which is 3 x 10^8 m/s, and the velocity of light in water is 2.25 x 10^8 m/s, we can plug in the values and solve for the angle of refraction:

sin(35˚) / sin(refraction angle) = (3 x 10^8 m/s) / (2.25 x 10^8 m/s)

sin(refraction angle) = sin(35˚) x (2.25 x 10^8 m/s) / (3 x 10^8 m/s)

sin(refraction angle) = 0.47

refraction angle = sin^-1(0.47)

refraction angle ≈ 28.4˚

Therefore, the angle of refraction of the light in water is approximately 28.4˚.
