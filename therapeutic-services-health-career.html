13. therapeutic careers maze answer key


Therapeutic Careers maze

Careers in the Therapeutic Services pathway are focused primarily on changing the health status of the patient over time. Health professionals in this pathway work directly with patients; they may provide care, treatment, counseling and health education information.

Based on an aging population, and a retiring workforce, the demand for health care workers will remain high. As the role of Therapeutic careers changes, professionals in this pathway will find increased opportunities to work independently. Additionally, an increasing number of career opportunities are becoming available outside of the traditional hospital setting The Therapeutic Services Career Path involves occupations focused on directly changing the health status of patients over time through direct care, treatment, counselling, or health education information. Health professionals in this pathway work directly with patients in healthcare and hospital settings and deal with both in-patients and out-patients. Healthcare practitioners and technical occupations are varied and include Audiologists, Chiropractors, Clinical Nurse Specialists, Dietitians & Nutritionists, Music Therapists, Opticians, Orthodontists, Pharmacy Technicians, and Podiatrists.

26. PERSONAL HEALTH CAREER PLANCAREER EXPLORATIONHealth Careers-engineer-police-medtech-nursingDescription Of The Health Career----​


Certainly! Here are brief descriptions of the health careers for engineer, police, medtech, and nursing:

1. Health Careers for Engineer: Biomedical Engineer

Biomedical engineering combines biology, medicine, and engineering to create new technologies and develop innovative applications that improve human health, such as prosthetics, biomedical imaging, and medical devices.

2. Health Careers for Police: Forensic Pathologist

Forensic pathologists are medical doctors who specialize in investigating the cause of death in cases of sudden, unexpected, or violent deaths. They work closely with law enforcement agencies to help solve crimes by conducting autopsies and analyzing evidence.

3. Health Careers for Medtech: Medical Technologist

Medical technologists are responsible for running complex medical tests and analyzing the results. They work in hospitals, clinics, and laboratories, using specialized equipment to study cells, tissues, and bodily fluids to diagnose diseases and monitor treatment.

4. Health Careers for Nursing: Nurse Practitioner

A nurse practitioner is a registered nurse who holds an advanced degree in nursing and is licensed to diagnose and treat patients. They work in a variety of healthcare settings, from primary care clinics to hospitals to nursing homes, and provide a range of services, including physical exams, medication management, and patient education.
