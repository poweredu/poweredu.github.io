1. song analysis choose one contemporary and traditional composition written by a filipino contemporary composer ​


Explanation:

Contemporary Composition: "Tatsulok" by Bamboo Mañalac

"Tatsulok" is a song by Filipino rock band Bamboo, composed by the band's lead vocalist, Bamboo Mañalac. It was released in 2005 as part of their self-titled debut album.

The song's lyrics revolve around the themes of social inequality and the struggle for justice. The title "Tatsulok" means "triangle" in English, which is a metaphor for the unequal distribution of power and resources in Philippine society. The lyrics describe how the rich and powerful exploit and oppress the poor and powerless, creating an unjust and unequal society.

The song starts with a haunting guitar riff and gradually builds up to a powerful and energetic chorus, with Bamboo's powerful vocals driving the song forward. The music perfectly complements the lyrics, creating a sense of urgency and intensity that reflects the song's message.

Overall, "Tatsulok" is a powerful and socially conscious song that tackles important issues in Philippine society. Its catchy melody and driving rhythm make it a memorable and effective piece of social commentary.

Traditional Composition: "Pamulinawen" by Felipe Padilla de Leon

"Pamulinawen" is a traditional Ilocano folk song composed by Filipino composer Felipe Padilla de Leon. It is a love song that expresses the singer's deep affection for a woman named Mulinawen.

The song is known for its beautiful melody and poetic lyrics. The opening lines, "Pamulinawen, pusok imdengam man / Toy umas-asug agrayo ita sadiam," describe the singer's yearning for Mulinawen, comparing her to a beautiful flower that he longs to possess.

The song's melody is simple and sweet, with a gentle rhythm that perfectly captures the song's romantic and nostalgic mood. The use of traditional Ilocano instruments, such as the kudyapi and the banduria, further adds to the song's traditional flavor.

Overall, "Pamulinawen" is a timeless and beautiful love song that has been enjoyed by generations of Filipinos. Its simple melody and poetic lyrics make it a classic example of traditional Filipino music.

Contemporary Filipino Artists

Here are some of the contemporary Filipino artists:

Ernest Concepcion (1977-present)

Intense emotion, deconstructing images in paintings, sculptures, and installations are the focus of Concepcion's works. He creates art like recording a music album, where each painting is from a series of nine.

Ronald Ventura (1973-present)

Ventura’s work is known to consist of multiple layers, using imagery that focuses on the human form.

Oscar Villamiel (1953-present)

Villamiel is a multimedia artist known for his large-scale installations consisting of objects found in local communities.

Dex Fernandez (1984-present)

Dexter practices a variety of mediums ranging from painting to street art and animation. His work is influenced by pop culture, graffiti, children’s drawings, and tattoos creating pieces that challenge people’s views on fine art. He is known locally for his on-going series of Garapata street art.

Neil Pasilan (1971-present)

Pasilan has moulded clay figures for most of his life and continues to use this in his work. He has become known for his paintings that hold multiple layers, using different mediums to expose new forms.

Kawayan de Guia (1979-present)

He illogically arranges texts and icons to compose a painting, depicting the human form in new ways. He also creates sculptures and massive art installations.

Leeroy New (1986-present)

Leeroy’s work blends theatre, fashion, film, production design, and public art.

25. contemporary Filipino culture​


Answer:

The culture of the Philippines, an island country in the South-East Asia, represents a huge range of very outstanding and fascinating traditions. Nevertheless, for anyone who would like to discuss the issue of the Filipino contemporary theatre, the difficult and very complicated postcolonial history of the country appears as one of the main challenges. The Philippines were first colonized by Spain (1565-1898) and later (in the 20th century) by the USA.
