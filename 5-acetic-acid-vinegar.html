2. A popular household vinegar contains 5% by volume acetic acid , how much acetic acid is in 200 ml of the vinegar ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \%(v/v) = 5\% \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{200 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solute (acetic acid).

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(v/v)}{100\%} \times V_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{5\%}{100\%} \times \text{200 mL} \\ & = \boxed{\text{10 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of acetic acid in the vinegar is 10 mL.

8. Vinegar contains 5.0 g acetic acid, CH3COOH, in 100.0 mL of solution. Calculate the molarity of acetic acid.​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (acetic acid).

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 4) + (16.00 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 60.052 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 5.0 g acetic acid} × \frac{\text{1 mol acetic acid}}{\text{60.052 g acetic acid}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.083 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.083 mol}}{\text{0.1000 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 0.83 M}}[/tex]

29. a vinegar brand contains 4% acetic acid by volume. if the density of vinegar is 1.05g/ml , how many grams of acetic acid are in a 250.0 ml of vinegar​


Given:

[tex]\text{volume}_{solution} = \text{250.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = 4\%[/tex]

[tex]\text{density}_{solution} = \text{1.05 g/mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]\text{mass}_{solute}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the volume of solute (acetic acid).

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume}_{solute}}{\text{volume}_{solution}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume}_{solute} = \frac{\text{percent by volume}}{100} × \text{volume}_{solution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume}_{solute} = \frac{4}{100} × \text{250.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume}_{solute} = \text{10.0 mL}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solvent (H₂O).

[tex]\text{volume}_{solvent} = \text{volume}_{solution} - \text{volume}_{solute}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume}_{solvent} = \text{250.0 mL} - \text{10.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume}_{solvent} = \text{240.0 mL}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solution.

[tex]\text{mass}_{solution} = \text{density}_{solution} × \text{volume}_{solution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass}_{solution} = \text{1.05 g/mL} × \text{250.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass}_{solution} = \text{262.5 g}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mass of solvent.

[tex]\text{mass}_{solvent} = \text{density}_{solvent} × \text{volume}_{solvent}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass}_{solvent} = \text{1.00 g/mL} × \text{240.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass}_{solvent} = \text{240.0 g}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\text{mass}_{solute} = \text{mass}_{solution} - \text{mass}_{solvent}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass}_{solute} = \text{262.5 g} - \text{240.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass}_{solute} = \text{22.5 g}}[/tex]

30. 1. The vinegar we used in cooking or in making sauce is made using acetic acid in water, usually 5% by mass. How many grams of pure acetic acid are in 1000 grams of vinegar?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solution}} = \text{1000 g} \\ & \%(m/m) = 5\% \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(m/m)}{100\%} \times mass_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{5\%}{100\%} \times \text{1000 g} \\ & = \boxed{\text{50 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 50 g of pure acetic acid in 1000 g of vinegar.
