27. The height of a right cylinder is 3 times the radius of the base. The volume of the cylinder is 24π cubic units. What is the height of the cylinder?


Volume of right cylinder = (π d² h) /4

Where: 
d = 2r   (diameter = 2 × radius)

Given:
Volume = 24π  cubic units
height = 3r  (thrice the radius)

Substitute the given values/expression to solve for radius:

24π = [π (2r)² (3r)] / 4

24π = [π (4r²)(3r)] / 4

24π = 12πr³ / 4

24π = 3πr³

24π/3π = 3πr³/3π

r³ = 8

∛r³ = ∛8

r = 2 units  ⇒  radius of the base

If height is three times the radius, then:

height = 3(2 units)

ANSWER:  height = 6 units

Check:

Volume =  (π d² h) /4

24π = [π (2×2)² (3×2)] / 4

24π = [π (16) (6)]/4

24π =  π(4)(6)

24π = 24π  (True)
