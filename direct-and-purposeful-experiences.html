8. Directions: Reflect on the importance of kinds of sentences according to purpose in your daily life. Share an experience that shows the “KSAP”.​


Answer:

There are different kinds of sentences. Each helps us to convey the meaning. It is also helping to make the communication easier and to be understood by the sender and the receiver.

Explanation:

There are four types of sentences, classified by their purpose:

Declarative sentence (statement) - It tells us something.  They give us information, and they normally end with a full-stop/period.  The usual word order for the declarative sentence is:  subject + verb. Declarative sentences can be positive or negative. Example: John likes Joy. Interrogative sentence (question) - It asks us something. They want information, and they always end with a question mark.  The usual word order for the interrogative sentence is:  (wh-word +) auxiliary + subject + verb. Example: Does Joy likes John? Imperative sentence (command) - It tells us to do something. They end with a full-stop/period (.) or exclamation mark/point (!).  The usual word order for the imperative sentence is:  base verb. Example: Stop! Close the window. Exclamative sentence (exclamation) - It expresses surprise. Example: What a funny story!
