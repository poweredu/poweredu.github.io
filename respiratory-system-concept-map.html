3. Concept map. Create a concept map on the relationship of Reproductive, Digestive, Respiratory, and Circulatory system.


Answer:

The reproductive system is responsible for producing and delivering gametes, or sex cells, to enable fertilization and reproduction. The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients that can be absorbed and used by the body. The respiratory system brings oxygen into the body and removes carbon dioxide. The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, and hormones throughout the body and removes waste products.

The reproductive system relies on the circulatory system to deliver hormones and nutrients necessary for gamete production and transport. The digestive system provides nutrients necessary for the production of gametes and hormones. The respiratory system provides oxygen necessary for energy production and tissue repair, which is essential for the development and maintenance of reproductive organs.

The circulatory system also plays a crucial role in transporting hormones and nutrients necessary for digestion and respiration. The digestive system relies on the circulatory system to transport nutrients from the small intestine to the liver for processing and distribution. The respiratory system relies on the circulatory system to deliver oxygen to the body's tissues and remove carbon dioxide.

Overall, the reproductive, digestive, respiratory, and circulatory systems are interconnected and work together to maintain homeostasis in the body. Each system relies on the others to function properly, and any disruption in one system can affect the others.

CIGARETTE SMOKING

Smoking causes cancer, heart disease, stroke, lung diseases, diabetes, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), which includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis. Smoking also increases risk for tuberculosis, certain eye diseases, and problems of the immune system, including rheumatoid arthritis.

CIRCULATORY SYSTEM

The system that contains the heart and the blood vessels and moves blood throughout the body. This system helps tissues get enough oxygen and nutrients, and it helps them get rid of waste products.

RESPIRATORY SYSTEM

The respiratory system is the network of organs and tissues that help you breathe. It includes your airways, lungs and blood vessels. The muscles that power your lungs are also part of the respiratory system. These parts work together to move oxygen throughout the body and clean out waste gases like carbon dioxide.

27. the human respiratory and circulatory system concept map​


The Human Respiratory and Circulatory System

The heart and blood vessels are part of the circulatory system, which also transports blood throughout the body. This system aids tissues in obtaining the necessary amounts of oxygen, nutrients, and waste removal. The system circulates blood throughout the body and houses the heart and blood arteries. The only organ of your circulatory system is your heart. To receive oxygen, blood travels from the heart to the lungs. A component of the respiratory system is the lungs. The remainder of your body receives oxygenated blood afterward through arteries.

A respiratory system is a group of interconnected organs and tissues that facilitates breathing. Your blood vessels, lungs, and airways make up this system. The muscles that drive your lungs are a part of the respiratory system as well. Together, these elements aid in the body's oxygen circulation and waste gas elimination (including carbon dioxide). When you inhale, air enters your lungs and oxygen goes to your blood. In addition, a waste gas called carbon dioxide moves simultaneously from your blood to your lungs before being inhaled. Life-sustaining processes include a gas exchange.
