Answer:

debakan - The debakan is a single-headed drum, the only non-gong percussion instrument in the kulintang ensemble. It is struck with two bamboo or rattan sticks. The body of this example consists of hollowed out coconut or jackfruit wood. The drumhead or tympanum is made of goat skin stretched by four cloth-wrapped bamboo rings.

gandingan - The gandingan is a set of four large hanging gongs which is a part. of the gong and drum ensemble known as kulintang among the. Magindanaon. Concentrated in the central western part of Mindanao island. Magindanaons are the largest Muslim minority of the Philippines with

17. what is polyphony? what is oneat? what is seruling? what is debakan? what is rebab? what is pinpeat?


Polyphony, in music, the simultaneous combination of two or more tones or melodic lines (the term derives from the Greek word for “many sounds”). Thus, even a single interval made up of two simultaneous tones or a chord of three simultaneous tones is rudimentarily polyphonic.

The Oneat is a low-pitched xylophone used in the Khmer classical music of Cambodia. It is built in the shape of a curved, rectangular shaped boat. It has twenty-one thick bamboo or hard wood bars that are suspended from strings attached to the two walls.

A suling or Seruling is a Southeast Asian bamboo ring flute especially in Brunei, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines and Singapore. It is used in gamelan ensembles. Depending on the regional genre, a suling can be tuned into different scales.

The dabakan is a single-headed Philippine drum, primarily used as a supportive instrument in the kulintang ensemble.

Rebab is a two-stringed fiddle. This instrument are found in the Middle East, North Africa and South Asia. So, it is generally being considered as an foreign instruments in Indonesian gamelan. Rebab has an almost heart-shaped body made of wood. The body is covered with a thin layer of skin taken from the intestine or bladder of a buffalo.

The pinpeat orchestra or musical ensemble performs the ceremonial music of the royal courts and temples of Cambodia. The orchestra consists of approximately nine or ten instruments, mainly wind and percussion.
