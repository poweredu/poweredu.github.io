-Mussel. Mussel (/ˈmʌsəl/) is the common name used for members of several families of bivalve molluscs, from saltwater and freshwater habitats. The answer is the first statement. A population of mussels was observed in a particular ecosystem.

Here are the explanation.

An ecosystem can simply be defined as collecting  all living and non-living components in a given environment.  The underlying living components  are known as biotic factors.  Biotic factors include  plants, micro-organisms and animals.  The surrounding non-living components  are known as abiotic factors.  Factors of abiotics include things like  rocks, water , soil, sun, rocks etc.

The ecosystem idea is related to the idea that all organisms in the environment are  engaged in relationships in that environment with every other aspect (like resources and other organisms).  Ecosystems manage resources and the flow  of nutrients through a system / community.  For instance, an ecosystem could be described as a household or a  university, and a city or state could be described as a larger ecosystem.

Ecosystems are  variable in diversity.  With many plants and animals some ecosystems may be very diverse;  whereas with fewer animals and plants other ecosystems may be less diverse.  For example, tropical rainforests could be classified as a highly diverse ecosystem; while  temperate rainforests could be classified as an ecosystem with lower diversity than tropical rainforests.
