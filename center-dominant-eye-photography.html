1. Characteristics of Filipino-Centered Photography


The pioneers of photography in the Philippines were Western photographers, mostly from Europe. The practice of taking photographs and the opening of the first photo studios in Spanish Philippines, from the 1840s to the 1890s, were driven by the following reasons: photographs were used as a medium of news and information about the colony, as a tool for tourism, as an instrument for anthropology, as a means for asserting social status, as an implement for historical documentation, as a device for communication, as materials for propaganda, and as a source of ideas for illustrations and engravings. The practice of photography in the Philippines was not without the influence and influx of Western-art concepts into the colonized archipelago.[1]

24. This technique of photography is applicable into tiny subject if you want to enlarge it. This is to emphasize details that are invisible to the human eye


Answer

In photography, there are three terms used to define the capturing of tiny photo subjects to enlarge it. These are Micro Photography, Macro Photography, and Close-up Photography, which are differentiated by a photography term called Magnification Ratio.

What is Magnification Ratio?

Magnification Ratio simply refers to how big the subject appears in the camera's sensor. For example, a macro shot has a 1:1 ratio. Meaning, the camera sensor is processing the image in its actual size. If it goes below 1:2, 1:3, 1:100, and so on, the camera is enlarging the subject 2x, 3x, 100x it's actual size. Shots below 1:1 are called close-up. Micro Photographyhave a ration of 20:1 and below, like using an actual microscope for a lens.

To be specific, the answer you might be looking for is Macro Photography
