1. Difference between truth and opinion


Truth is a statement in the form of a real situation or an event that occurred. Facts contain something that really exists and the statement of a point is usually difficult for anyone to refute.

Meanwhile, opinion is an attitude or opinion of someone about a situation that has or has not happened. The opinion is strongly influenced by feelings, thoughts, perspectives, desires, attitudes, experiences, understandings, and beliefs of each individual.

Fact features:

Have Accurate Data

In fact sentences, there is clear data in an event. In a sentence, the data can be in the form of statistical numbers, the date and time of the incident, or other things that have been verified.

Objective

What is meant by objective in the sentence of fact is that the statements contained in it are general in nature and have been acknowledged to be true by many parties, especially by official bodies or institutions.

Really happened

A sentence can be considered a fact if the statement in it describes a situation that actually happened. It actually happened means that someone could see it with their own eyes or hear the news report from an authorized person.

Opinion features:

Contain Personal Opinion

The opinion, the sentence contains many opinions from yourself or from others. In some cases, opinions are found in statements from well-known people so that they seem to be facts. In fact, that person's words are still only opinions that cannot be proven true.

Subjective

Almost the same as the first characteristic, the second characteristic of opinion sentences is that the statements presented in the sentence tend to be subjective. This means that the things stated are only according to one party so they cannot be said to be neutral.

Having a Relative Adjective

In opinion sentences, someone will tend to find words that are relative. The relative meaning here is that the word or phrase tends to change depending on who says it. Words that are relative include most, more, somewhat, or usually.

6. how can an understanding of the difference between truth and opinion lead us to wisdom?​


Answer:

One of the original and most fundamental searches of theory has been to distinguish appearance from reality. One of the terrific and ongoing outcomes of this search is to recognize how simplistic this distinction is.

The concept of truth has undergone powerful complication over the centuries well beyond any comparison with opinion. In the comparison you pose, the concept of true belief—belief in what actually is—would contrast with opinion, a posit of what there actually might be, or what might be the case. But the concept of reality, of what actually is, has also undergone complication. It is far more subtle now in reflective thought than it is in common sense. The finding in quantum research that our observing reality changes it in fact, is spectacularly revolutionizing even to our complex and subtle understanding of the ideas involved.

The truth can be conceived abstractly and hypothetically, apart from any form of belief. A sense of it, a kind of truth, can also be attributed to certain kinds of knowing. You can know the truth, or you can know reality in some sense (not all), which could make some things you say about it descriptive interpretations as compared to subjective ones, part-projections.

Reflecting on all these matters, the subtle distinctions involved, can indeed lead to wisdom. They can change your confidence in knowing. They can help one apply the same subtleties and processes of generating them to other areas of thought. They can make mince meat out of the rule-of-thumb distinction we use between objective and subjective. This process started big time perhaps with critical theory, Immanuel Kant, nothing that all our perceptions and conceptions are conditioned on (limited and shaped by) our capacities to perceive and conceive. 24. What is the importance of being able to tell the difference between truth and opinion? ​


Answer:

A fact is a statement that can be proven true or false. An opinion is an expression of a

person’s feelings that cannot be proven. Opinions can be based on facts or emotions and

sometimes they are meant to deliberately mislead others. Therefore, it is important to be

aware of the author’s purpose and choice of language. Sometimes, the author lets the

facts speak for themselves.

The following is an example of a fact:

• With fewer cars on the road, there would be less air pollution and traffic noise;

therefore, the use of mass transportation should be encouraged.

Sometimes the author may use descriptive language to appeal to your emotions and sway

your thinking. The following is an example of an opinion:

• Do you like looking at a smoggy view from a congested highway? How do you

feel about fighting road hugs and bumper to bumper traffic everyday? Mass

transportation is the solution to all these problems.

Emotional language is neither right nor wrong, but the way in which it is used can be

positive or negative; it is up to you to make reasonable judgement about the material you

are reading and to draw your own conclusion.

Therefore, when you read, it is important to judge facts and opinions carefully in order to

come to the right conclusion. Ask yourself, “are the facts reliable?” or “are the opinions

based on the facts?” Once you answer these questions, you may be on the right track for

finding and sticking to the facts; you be the judge.
