4. Which of the following shows a chemical reaction in which magnesium and hydrogen sulfide are the reactants and magnesium sulfide and hydrogen are the products? A. magnesium sulfide + hydrogen → magnesium + hydrogen sulfide B. magnesium + magnesium sulfide → hydrogen sulfide + hydrogen C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen D. magnesium + hydrogen → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen sulfide


Answer:  C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen

In chemical reactions, it always involve both reactants and products. Reactants are the substances that start a chemical reaction, and products are substances that are produced in the reaction. Reactants are usually at the left of the tail of the arrow (→ ) and products are at the right or tip of the arrow (→) if it is forward reaction. And if it is backward reaction, it is the reverse of the forward reaction.

Given: Reactants: magnesium, hydrogen sulfide

           Products: magnesium sulfide, hydrogen

Answer: magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen

17. What is the mass of 7.75 x 10²² formula units of lead (II) sulfide, PbS?Please show your complete solution. ​


Answer:

mass = 30.8 g

Explanation:

Calculating the molar mass of PbS

molar mass = (207.2 × 1) + (32.06 × 1)

molar mass = 239.3 g

Calculating the mass of PbS

mass = 7.75 × 10²² formula units × (1 mol / 6.022 × 10²³ formula units) × (239.3 g / 1 mol)

mass = 30.8 g
