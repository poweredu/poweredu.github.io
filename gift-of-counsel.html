1. why do we need the gift of counsel


With the gift of counsel, the Holy Spirit speaks, as it were, to the heart and in an instant enlightens a person what to do. It corresponds to the promise made by Christ to his followers, "When they hand you over, do not worry about how to speak or what to say; what you are to say will be given to you when the time comes; because it is not you who will be speaking; the Spirit of your Father will be speaking in you"

Counsel refers primarily to prudent conduct in one's own case, and only secondarily in favor of others. Enlightened by the Spirit, a person learn what to do in a specific case and what advice to give when consulted or command to make if he is in authority.

28. Discuss the following areas of specialization where councelors work1. Child development and counseling2. Adolescent development and counseling3. Gerontological counseling4. Marital or relationship counseling5. Health6. Phobia counseling​


Child development and counseling: Counselors who specialize in child development and counseling work with children who are experiencing emotional, behavioral, or social problems. They help children develop coping mechanisms and strategies to manage their emotions and behaviors, as well as provide guidance to parents and caregivers on how to support their child's development.

Adolescent development and counseling: Counselors who specialize in adolescent development and counseling work with teenagers who are experiencing a wide range of issues, including peer pressure, identity development, academic challenges, and mental health concerns. They help adolescents navigate these challenges and develop skills to manage stress, anxiety, and other mental health issues.

Gerontological counseling: Gerontological counselors work with older adults and their families to address issues related to aging, such as physical health concerns, loss of independence, and end-of-life decisions. They help seniors maintain their independence, cope with chronic health conditions, and address issues related to social isolation and loneliness.

Marital or relationship counseling: Counselors who specialize in marital or relationship counseling work with couples to improve their communication skills, resolve conflicts, and strengthen their relationships. They may also help couples navigate difficult life transitions, such as divorce or the loss of a partner.

Health counseling: Health counselors work with individuals who are dealing with chronic illnesses, injuries, or other health concerns. They help clients manage their conditions, develop healthy habits, and address any emotional or psychological issues related to their health.

Phobia counseling: Phobia counselors work with individuals who are experiencing intense fear or anxiety related to a specific object, situation, or activity. They help clients identify the root causes of their phobias and develop coping mechanisms to manage their symptoms. Treatment may involve exposure therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy, or other specialized interventions.
