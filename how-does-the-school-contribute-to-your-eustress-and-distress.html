As an individual, I have experienced both eustress and distress within my community. One way in which my community has contributed to my eustress is through social support. I have found that having a strong social support system has helped me cope with stress and provided me with a sense of belonging and connection. My community offers various activities, events, and groups that have allowed me to connect with others who share similar interests and experiences, which has contributed to my eustress.

Another way in which my community has contributed to my eustress is through positive feedback. When I receive recognition and positive feedback from my community, it boosts my self-esteem and motivation. This has led me to continue to strive towards achieving my goals and contributing to my community.

Furthermore, my community has offered opportunities for growth, such as training programs and mentorship. These opportunities have provided me with a sense of purpose and direction, which has contributed to my eustress.

However, my community has also contributed to my distress. For instance, social isolation has led to feelings of loneliness and isolation, which has contributed to my distress and mental health problems. Discrimination and prejudice within my community have also created a sense of fear, anxiety, and insecurity, leading to distress. Additionally, the lack of resources such as healthcare, education, and employment opportunities has created a sense of hopelessness and despair, which has contributed to my distress.

Overall, my community has had both positive and negative impacts on my eustress and distress. Seeking out positive social connections and support, as well as advocating for equitable access to resources and opportunities, can help to mitigate the negative effects of community-related distress.

18. how does your school contribute your eustress


Answer:

Eustress is good stress

Explanation:

The school contributes to eustress, or good stress by allowing you to be pressured on working towards a goal. How is that achieved?

A school calendar is usually composed of semesters or quarters and there are deadlines for term papers, projects, assignments that are distributed within that time frame. Specific goals are set and clear guidelines are also given. When this happens, as a student you are obligated to work on your assignments as you have guidance.

If, however, there is no clear goal and no clear timeline and yet you school expects you to do your projects and assignments, then distress, or bad stress may happen if you are not prepared. Take the example of a pop quiz for example. If you did not study, then you will experience distress.

One thing to remember about stress is that it can be managed and it should be managed. You do not want to burn you body out with all the stress hormones as it could lead to various stress-related diseases like hypertension. So, being zen about what happens at school or at home can help in decreasing your stress levels.

Here are some ways in which each of these factors can contribute to eustress (positive stress) and distress (negative stress):

Family: Positive interactions with family members, such as feelings of love and support, can contribute to eustress. However, negative interactions such as conflict, criticism or neglect can lead to distress.

School: Positive experiences in school, such as feelings of accomplishment, achievement, and social connections can contribute to eustress. However, negative experiences such as high academic pressure, bullying, and social isolation can lead to distress.

Peer: Positive social interactions with peers, such as friendship, acceptance, and support can contribute to eustress. However, negative interactions such as peer pressure, bullying, and rejection can lead to distress.

Friends: Positive interactions with friends, such as feelings of trust, loyalty, and companionship can contribute to eustress. However, negative interactions such as betrayal, conflict, and social isolation can lead to distress.

It is important to recognize the impact of these factors on our overall well-being and to take steps to manage any negative stress that may arise. This can include seeking support from positive relationships, practicing stress management techniques, and seeking professional help if necessary.

EUSTRESS is a moderate or normal psychological stress interpreted as being beneficial for the experiencer.

DISTRESS is an extreme anxiety, sorrow, or pain.
