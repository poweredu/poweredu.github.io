21. Assertion and opinion


Answer:

An assertion is a statement of fact or belief that can be proven or disproven using evidence. It is a statement that is presented as true and can be supported by evidence.

An opinion is a personal view or judgment about something, not necessarily based on fact or knowledge. It is a statement that reflects someone's beliefs, feelings or attitudes about a particular subject and it can't be proven or disproven by evidence.

In simple terms, an assertion is a statement that can be proven to be true or false, while an opinion is a statement that reflects someone's personal feelings, beliefs or attitudes about a particular subject.

23. explain what is assertion of fact, assertion of convention, assertion of opinion,and assertion of preference ​


Answer:

Assertion of Fact: An assertion of fact is a statement that can be proven to be true or false. It is based on evidence and can be verified. Examples include: The sky is blue, Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

Assertion of Convention: An assertion of convention is a statement that reflects the accepted behavior or practice in a particular society or culture. Examples include: It is polite to say please and thank you, In the United States, people drive on the right side of the road.

Assertion of Opinion: An assertion of opinion is a statement that expresses an individual's beliefs or feelings about something. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: Chocolate ice cream is the best flavor, I think rap music is underrated.

Assertion of Preference: An assertion of preference is a statement that expresses an individual's preference for something over another option. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: I prefer coffee over tea, I like apples more than oranges.

Before Galileo and NEwton, many people thought objects slowed down because they had a natural built in tendency to do so. But those people weren't taking onto account the many forces - eg friction, gravity and air resistance- here on Earth that cause objects to change their velocity. If we could observe the motion of an object in deep interstellar space, we would be able to observe the natural tendencies of an object's motion free from any external influences. In deep interstellar space, we would observe that if an object had a velocity, it would continue moving with that velocity until there was some force to cause change in the motion.
