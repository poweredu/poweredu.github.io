6. 1 John and his friends were out for some long drive with his new car. Misfortune occurs and John and her friends find themselves getting a workout. They applied a cummulative force of 1320 N to push the car 310 m to the nearest gas station. Determine the work done to the car2. Lamar Grant, U.S. powerlifting star, became the first man to deadlift five times his own body weight in 1985. Deadlifting involves raising a loaded barbell from the floor to the position above the head with outstretched arms. Determine the work done by Lamar in deadlifting 300 kg to a height of 0.90 m above the ground. 3. A 70 kg skydiver is about to jump out of an airplane at an altitude of 900 m above the ground. How much potential energy does the skydiver posses?4. Suppose the 70 kg skydiver already jumps and is now gliding at a speed of 70 m/s, determine its kinetic energy.​please help


Answer:

1. 409200 joules

2. 2648.7 joules

3. 618030 joules

4. 171500 joules

Explanation:

1. Work = Force*distance

w = 1320N*310m

w = 409200Nm = 409200J

2. w = f*d

w = 300kg*(9.81m/s²)*0.90m, [since the direction of work is upwards, acceleration due to gravity is present]

w = 2648.7J [1J = 1kgm²/s²]

3. potential energy

=mass*acceleration due to gravity*height

p.e. = 70kg*(9.81m/s²)*(900m)

p.e. = 618030J

4. kinetic energy = 1/2*mass*velocity²

k.e. = 1/2*(70kg)*(70m/s)²

k.e. = 171500J

Modified TRUE or FALSE (Give the CORRECT answer when the statement is FALSE) The object in projectile motion is moving horizontally at a constant accelerated motion. (1 Point) TRUE FALSE: constant motion FALSE: changing motion Somewhat true


Answer:

FALSE: contanst motion

Explanation:

In projectile motion, an object is launched and then moves under the influence of gravity. The horizontal motion of the object is constant and does not change, but the vertical motion of the object is accelerated, as it is subject to the constant downward force of gravity. Therefore, the statement that the object in projectile motion is moving horizontally at a constant accelerated motion is false.
