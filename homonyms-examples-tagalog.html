20. homonyms and polysemy examples with meaning ​


Answer:

Homonyms are words that are pronounced the same but have different meanings. Here are some examples:

Bat - a flying mammal / a piece of sports equipment used to hit a ball

Rose - a type of flower / past tense of "rise"

Bank - a financial institution / the edge of a river or lake

Bark - the outer covering of a tree / the sound a dog makes

Polysemy refers to words that have multiple related meanings. Here are some examples:

Foot - the lower part of the leg / a unit of measurement

Watch - a device used to tell time / to observe or monitor

Rock - a type of mineral / to sway back and forth

Ball - a spherical object used in sports / a formal social event

In both cases, the meaning of the word is determined by the context in which it is used

The word "homonym" comes from the prefix "homo-," which means the same, and the suffix "-nym," which means name. Therefore, a homonym is a word that has the same name as another word, meaning that the two words look and sound exactly alike.

5 examples of homonyms

Break-Brake Saw-Saw Ruler-Ruler Duck-Duck Meet-Meat

Explanation:

1. Brake as a verb means to stop, to halt, to pause a running object.  

Example: The driver braked smoothly as it stops near pedestrian line.

   Break as a verb means to shatter, to convert whole into smaller pieces, to destroy or to demolish.

Example: The wicked twins planned to break the China vase.

2. Saw as a verb means to see in the past tense.

Example: She saw a flying disk in the sky.

   Saw as a noun refers to the object that cuts bigger objects or materials into half, like woods or plastic wares.

 Example: Jordan used the saw to cut the log that fell down.

3. Ruler as a noun could refer to the measuring device usually made of plastic or wood that is used to determine the length of an object. The surface of the ruler usually is printed or painted with numbers and lines.

Example: Leia used the ruler to measure how long the plant has gotten.

   Ruler as a noun could also mean a person governing a  land or area.

Example: Vlad is the ruler of the kingdom of Lustria.

4. Duck as a verb means to lower the head or body to avoid being hit by an object.

Example: Duck, Cover, and Hold.

   Duck as a noun means the waterbird that is usually seen in freshwaters characterized by having a blunt bill, short legs and webbed feet.

Example: Carrie fed the ducks in the park.

5. Meet as a verb means to get acquainted with someone in person.

Example: Meet the brothers of Gracie.

   Meat as a noun means flesh of an animal as food.

Example: Please tell Anita to tenderize the meat before cooking.

"My aunt has an ant farm in their house."

Homophones are words that are pronounced the same as the other but differs in meaning. A homophone may also differ in spelling.

Homographs are words that share the same written form as another word but has a different meaning.
