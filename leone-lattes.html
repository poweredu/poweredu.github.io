16. Application of One-Sample T-test Problem 1: A coffee shop relocates to Sto Tomas and wants to make sure that all lattes are consistent. They believe that each latte has an average of 4 oz of espresso. If this is not the case, they must increase or decrease the amount. A random sample of 25 lattes shows a mean of 4.6 oz of espresso and a standard deviation of 2.2 oz. Use 0.05 as the level of significance. 4 OZ. 4.6 oz.​


Answer:

To determine whether the average amount of espresso in a latte is significantly different from the desired amount of 4 oz, we can use a one-sample t-test.

The null hypothesis is that the true mean of the espresso in a latte is equal to 4 oz, while the alternative hypothesis is that it is not equal to 4 oz.

We can use the formula for the t-test, which is:

t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n)

where:

x̄ = sample mean (4.6 oz)

μ = population mean (4 oz)

s = sample standard deviation (2.2 oz)

n = sample size (25)

Plugging in the values, we get:

t = (4.6 - 4) / (2.2 / √25)

t = 1.818

Using a t-distribution table with 24 degrees of freedom (n - 1), and a level of significance of 0.05, the critical t-value is ±2.064.

Since our calculated t-value of 1.818 is less than the critical t-value of 2.064, we fail to reject the null hypothesis. This means that we do not have enough evidence to conclude that the average amount of espresso in a latte is significantly different from 4 oz. Therefore, the coffee shop does not need to increase or decrease the amount of espresso in their lattes.

25. A mocha latte sells at a regular price of P180. Because the coffee shop has an anniversary treat today, they sell the latte for only P80. Find the percent of the decrease in the price.​


Answer:

The percent of the decrease in the price is 55.56%.

Step-by-step explanation:

Markdown = original price - new price

= 180 - 80

Markdown = 100

Markdown Rate = (Markdown ÷ Orig. Price) × 100

= ( 100 ÷ 180) × 100

Markdown Rate = 55.56%

28. what is the difference between cappuccino and caffe latte?​


Answer:

A cappuccino has an even distribution of espresso, steamed milk, and foamed milk. A latte has way more steamed milk and a light layer of foam. A cappuccino is distinctly layered, while in a latte the espresso and steamed milk are blended together.
