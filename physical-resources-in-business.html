1. Physical resources definition in business


Answer:

Physical resources are tangible items that are used in the operation of the business. Other resources are available to support the actions of the company. Some of the most common physical resources include raw materials, buildings and facilities, machinery, energy, and supplies

Explanation:

Physical resources are tangible that are used in operation the business. ...Other resources are available to support the actions of the company. Some of the most common physical resources include raw materials, buildings and facilities, machinery,energy and supplies.


2. Which of the following best describes a balance sheet? A. It is a statement of the assets, liabilities, and capital of a business or organization at a particular point in time, detailing the balance of income and expenditure over the preceding period. B. It involves harnessing human, money,and physical resources from well-selected suppliers. C. It is one of the essential parts of a business plan that generates capital investment and sources of funding for the operation of the business. D. It is a set of choices and actions geared toward gaining a sustainable competitive advantage.


Answer:

A. It is a statement of the assets, liabilities, and capital of a business or organization at a particular point in time, detailing the balance of income and expenditure over the preceding period.

Explanation:

A balance sheet is a financial statement that provides a snapshot of a business or organization's financial position at a specific point in time, typically the end of a reporting period. It presents the company's assets (what it owns), liabilities (what it owes), and capital (the residual interest in the assets after deducting liabilities) at that particular moment. It does not involve harnessing resources from suppliers, generate capital investment or sources of funding, or pertain to choices and actions for gaining a competitive advantage, as described in the other options.
