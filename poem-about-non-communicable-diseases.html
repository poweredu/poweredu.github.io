5. write an essay about non-communicable diseases


Answer:

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) are a group of chronic illnesses that cannot be transmitted from person to person. These conditions are often caused by lifestyle factors like unhealthy diets, lack of physical activity, tobacco use, and alcohol consumption. NCDs are responsible for a significant portion of the global disease burden, with over 41 million deaths each year. This essay will explore the causes, consequences, and potential solutions to the NCD epidemic.

One of the primary causes of NCDs is an unhealthy lifestyle. Poor dietary habits, such as excessive consumption of processed foods, sugar, and saturated and trans fats, are major contributors to obesity, type 2 diabetes, and cardiovascular disease. Lack of physical activity is also a significant factor, with sedentary lifestyles increasing the risk of NCDs like heart disease, stroke, and cancer. Tobacco use and alcohol consumption are additional lifestyle factors that contribute to NCDs, with smoking increasing the risk of lung cancer and heart disease, and alcohol consumption leading to liver disease and other health problems.

The consequences of NCDs are severe, both for individuals and society at large. NCDs can lead to premature death, disability, and reduced quality of life. These conditions also place a significant burden on healthcare systems, with NCDs accounting for a large proportion of healthcare spending. The economic impact of NCDs is also significant, with lost productivity and increased healthcare costs affecting economies at the national and global level.

To address the NCD epidemic, there are several solutions that can be implemented. One approach is to promote healthy lifestyle habits through education and awareness campaigns. This can include initiatives to encourage healthy eating, regular physical activity, and smoking cessation. Governments can also implement policies to reduce the consumption of unhealthy foods, such as taxes on sugary drinks and restrictions on advertising unhealthy foods to children.

Another approach is to improve access to healthcare services for NCD prevention and treatment. This can include increasing funding for NCD research, improving access to screening and early detection services, and providing affordable medication and treatment options.

Finally, a comprehensive approach to addressing NCDs must include a focus on social determinants of health, such as poverty and inequality. These factors can contribute to unhealthy lifestyle habits and increase the risk of NCDs. Addressing social determinants of health through policies that promote equity and social justice can be an effective way to reduce the burden of NCDs.

In conclusion, NCDs are a significant global health challenge that requires a comprehensive and multifaceted approach. By addressing the underlying causes of NCDs, promoting healthy lifestyle habits, improving access to healthcare services, and addressing social determinants of health, we can work towards reducing the burden of NCDs and improving health outcomes for individuals and communities worldwide.

9. characteristic of communicable disease and non communicable disease​


Answer:

A communicable disease is one that is spread from one person to another through a variety of ways that include: contact with blood and bodily fluids; breathing in an airborne virus; or by being bitten by an insect.

A non-communicable disease (NCD) is a disease that is not transmissible directly from one person to another. NCDs include Parkinson's disease, autoimmune diseases, strokes, most heart diseases, most cancers, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, osteoarthritis, osteoporosis, Alzheimer's disease, cataracts, and others.

21. What is communicable disease and non communicable disease?


COMMUNICABLE VS NON-COMMUNICABLE DISEASESWhat is a Communicable Disease?Communicable diseases are a type of disease that can be transferred between one host to another.They utilize a carrier, which can be anything that has the ability to transfer the disease from one host to another.An example of a carrier may be bodily fluids (e.g. saliva, blood, sweat, etc.)

Examples of Communicable DiseasesFiliarasisIs a parasitic disease that is caused by roundwormsThey spread throught mosquito bites, wherein a mosquito infected by the disease can transfer the disease when they feed on the human host's blood.

MalariaIt's a fatal disease that targets the Red Blood Cells (erythrocytes) of the infected host, which is one of the main components of our blood.Like the example stated above, this disease also spreads through mosquito bites.

What is a Non-communicable Disease?These are diseases that have no ability to transfer between one host and another.Unlike communicable diseases, they usually develop inside the host's body, rather than invading it.This is most often due to the internal malfunction of the body and any of its constituent organs.

Examples of Non-communicable Diseases

PsoriasisThis is a disease that is caused due to the speedy mass production  of skin in a person's body.A person infected by this disease will notice symptoms of having red and scaly patches in their skin.This is mainly caused by genetics, and cannot be spread.

DiabetesIs a disease that is caused due to high amounts of glucose levels (blood sugar levels).This happens when the body's ability to give insulin, a hormone responsible for the distribution of glucose throughout the body, fails.
