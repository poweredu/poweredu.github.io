10. A scientist is a person who is an expert in any field of science and conducts scientific investigation. Scientist not only possess the scientific inquiry skills they also have scientific attitude or the values and habits of the mind in science these scientific attitudes are the following


Answer and Explanation:

A scientific attitude is one that seeks knowledge and understanding, challenges all claims, looks for facts and their meaning, looks for confirmation, and weighs the pros and drawbacks.

These attitudes include curiosity, honesty in the recording and validation of data, flexibility, persistence, open-mindedness, willingness to tolerate uncertainty, and an acceptance of the provisional nature of scientific explanation. These are the features that characterize scientific thinking.

Science is all around us and everything in our lives is closely related to it. Scientists are curious  about everything. Everything around us is related to science. For us to further obtain answers from our questions and  curiosity, we should conduct research or experiment by way of scientific investigations. Scientific  investigations are composed of scientific components.

Scientists came up with a scientific way in conducting experiments or research with minimized  errors and increased confidence in the result. This procedure or steps followed by scientists is known as  scientific method or scientific investigation.

27. List down (at least six) different scientific attitudes, values and skills.


Explanation:

Curiousity

- A scientist shows interest and pays particular attentions to objects or events. He asks questions and seeks answers.

Honesty

- A scientist gives a truthful report of observations. He does not withhold important information just to please himself or others.

Open-Mindedness

- A scientist listens to and respects the ideas of others. He accepts criticism and changes his mind if reliable evidence contrdicts his believes.

Skepticism

- A scientist bases suggestions and conclusions on evidences. When in doubt, he questions the veracity of a statement in relation to the evidences presented.

Creativity

- A scientist can generate new and original ideas.
