13. we should abolish zoos and ocean parks.​


Answer:

The abolishment of zoo and ocean parks might not be the best for the welfare of land and marine animals. In today's era, the wild has been inhospitable to many animal and marine creatures as pollution, overhunting, fewer food sources, calamities such as forest fires and competition with humans make it harder for wildlife to survive on their own. The most significant factor among these is overhunting. In the wild, animals are prone to overhunting by humans for food, parts (such as rhino horn, ivory tusks, or whale's ambergris and blubber) that it would be safer for animals to be taken cared of by more responsible humans than be left in the wild.

A better compromise would be safaris. In a safari setting, land animals are not necessary caged for the viewing pleasure of humans, The land area is vast enough to permit the animals to roam. A safari is modeled to be as exact as the animals' habitat as much as possible, minus the threat of predators and human poachers. In this setting, trained rangers, biologists, and veterinarians are also on call to ensure the well being on the animals. While technically still on display, animals in a Safari are well fed, well taken cared of, and are more protected that their counterparts in the outdoors.

What isa fever?

Your body temperature fluctuates slightly throughout the day. A high temperature is also known as a fever. Generally, a fever is a rise of body temperature above the normal daily variation. Normal temperature varies depending on the person, the body site where temperature is measured, and the time of day.

What temperature is a fever?

The actual temperature causing a fever varies between different people and depends on what their usual temperature is. It is usually agreed that a person with a temperature of 38°C or higher has a fever. However, a temperature over 37.5°C can often be considered to be a fever in many children. Fevers can occur in children and adults and can be very common. Fevers are often accompanied by sweating.
