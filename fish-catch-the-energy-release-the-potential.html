Mechanical Energy

There are one form of energy that is Mechanical energy. Mechanical energy is energy that acquired by objects upon which work is done. There are two kinds mechanical energy or the combination of energy that is Potential Energy and Kinetic Energy.

Potential Energy

1. Potential Energy is energy that is stored. It has two kinds of;

a. Gravitational Potential Energy is the energy owner by an object because of its location or position.

Examples : A book on top of the table, car parked on a ramp.

b. Elastic Potential Energy is the energy stored in a stretched or compressed elastic material such as spring.

Examples : The spring on the handle of a pinball machine has more energy when compressed than when in arelaxed position.

Kinetic Energy

2. Kinetic Energy is the energy of motion.

Examples : A bullet in motion, stream of flowing water, rock falling off a cliff, roller coaster.

28. Which event illustrates the direct transformation of potential to kinetic energy? a.) a basketball player catches a flying ball b.) a kalesa moves from the rest c.) kathy's arrow is released from its bow d.) the spring mechanism of a toy is rotated until it locked


The event that illustrates the direct transformation of potential to kinetic energy is letter C.) Kathy's arrow is released from its bow. This is because the potential energy of the arrow, against the bow is transformed to motion when released. The said motion is kinetic energy. Potential energy refers to the energy of an object in relation to other objects. Meanwhile, kinetic energy refers to energy of an object in motion. More is discussed below.  

Potential Energy

Other examples of potential energy include the gravitational pull of the Earth on the mass of various objects. For example, a ball resting on a table has that potential energy. When that same ball is pushed towards the ground, the energy changes from potential to kinetic because of the downward motion.  

Kinetic Energy

When an object is in motions, it is said to have kinetic energy. THat object would remain in motion unless stopped by an outisde force. The object will likewise maintain that kinetic energy unless the speed changes.

Energy Transformation

Energy can not be created nor destroyed and it can only be transferred or conserved. Hence, as with the examples above, it can only changed from potential to kinetic and from chemical to kinetic. There are various ways in which energy can be transformed, the most useful perhaps is when we convert energy for electrical use--that eventually powers many of our gadgets and machines.
