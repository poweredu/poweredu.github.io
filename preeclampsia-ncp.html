22. 4. What nursing measures would you implement for a client with mild preeclampsia? Pa help po plsss


Pre-eclampsia is a pregnancy hypertensive disease that occurs with three classic symptoms including edema, proteinuria, and hypertension. The nursing intervention for this said disease is actually based on its severity.

If the mother experience a mild preeclampsia (BP is not over 160/100mmHg) the following are the Nurse's interventions:

Promote bed rest

Rationale: Bed rest is the best method for aiding increased evacuation of sodium and encourage diuresis of edema fluid. The mother should be in a lateral recumbent position to avoid uterine pressure and prevent supine hypotension.

Monitor Antiplatelet Therapy

Rationale: to delay the development of pre-eclampsia. The common antiplatelet agent given to a mother with a mild pre-eclampsia is a low dosage of aspirin. Educate the client that high dose of aspirin can cause maternal bleeding.

Provide a support system

Rationale: Remember that pre-eclampsia is a serious disease for a pregnant woman and they might experience such anxiety and find it difficult to understand what's happening within. It is important to provide emotional support so that the mother will cooperate and will be given a proper and precise care.

Promote good nutrition

Rationale: the mother needs to continue her usual pregnancy nutrition even if during bed rest to ensure that she gets the proper nutrients. This is also essential in monitoring her blood pressure.
