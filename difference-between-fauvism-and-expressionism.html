15. describe each following in one word:*cubism*dadaism*expressionism*fauvism*surrealism*abstractionism​


Answer:

CUBISM - a movement in the early 20th Century characterized by the depiction of natural forms as geometric structures of planes.

DADAISM - a movement in art and literature based on deliberate irrationality and negation of traditional artistic values.

EXPRESSIONISM - a movement in art characterized by visible brush strokes, ordinary subject matters, and an emphasis on light and its changing qualities.

FAUVISM - a movement of the last part of the 19th century which emphasized spontaneity and the use of extremely bright colors.

SURREALISM - a movement and an aesthetic philosophy that aims for the liberation of the mind by emphasizing the critical and imaginative powers of the subconscious.

ABSTRACTIONISM - a movement that breaks away from drawing art as it is represented in real life. There are many movements and artists that are classified as being part of abstract art.

19. difference between impressionism and expressionism


Impressionism, which developed after the camera came along and (at least ostensibly) relieved artists from the strictures of realism, asks an artist to give an "impression" of something, rather than a realistic rendering.

Expressionism intends exactly what the word indicates. Its primary intention is the expression of feelings or spirit and it therefore - in effect -gives an artist permission to distort image or even dispense with image altogether, in order to better convey emotions or spirit.

28. describe the expressionism(neoprimitism,fauvism,dadaism,surrealism,social realism​


Answer:

Expressionism refers to art in which the image of reality is distorted in order to make it expressive of the artist's inner feelings or ideas. Wassily Kandinsky.

Neo-Primitivism is a style-label employed by the Muscovite avant-garde in the early twentieth century to describe forms of visual art and poetry that were tendentiously crude in style and socially and politically contentious in terms of subject

Fauvism is the name applied to the work produced by a group of artists (which included Henri Matisse and André Derain) from around 1905 to 1910, which is characterised by strong colours and fierce brushwork.

Dadaism a movement in art and literature based on deliberate irrationality and negation of traditional artistic values … artists of the day who were influenced by contemporary European art movements like Dadaism and Futurism …

Surrealism a style of art and literature developed principally in the 20th century, stressing the subconscious or nonrational significance of imagery arrived at by automatism or the exploitation of chance effects, unexpected juxtapositions, etc.

Social realism is the term used for work produced by painters, printmakers, photographers, writers and filmmakers that aims to draw attention to the real socio-political conditions of the working class as a means to critique the power structures behind these conditions.
