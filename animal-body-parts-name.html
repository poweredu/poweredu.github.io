Animal body parts and their uses for survival.

1. Fish.

Nostrils: Nostrils in fish function to recognize the smell of prey and predators that want to eat it so that they can protect themselves.Fins and tail: They function for swimming, balance guards, and control the movement of fish when moving, like turning when chased by predators.

2. Turtles.

Shell: It functions to cover the turtle's upper back and as a place to insert the head, tail, and other body parts when threatened by enemies.Feet: On the turtle's feet there is a membrane that serves to help the turtle swim quickly in the water and run away from enemies.

3. Deer.

Horns: To defend themselves and their territory from predators who want to attack.Feet: To run fast when being chased by predators.

4. Snake.

Mouth: The snake's mouth has teeth, a tongue, and a small sac filled with venom. When threatened, the snake will bite its prey and release its venom to defend itself.Body: The snake's body is for moving and some snakes can wrap their bodies around their enemies when threatened.

5. Eagle.

Feet: On the eagle's feet there are strong and sharp claws that serve to grip prey.Beaks: Eagles have sharp beaks for tearing their enemies and prey to pieces.Wings: With its wings, the eagle can fly very fast to avoid enemies.
