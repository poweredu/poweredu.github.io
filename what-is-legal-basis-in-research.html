28. what is the legal basis of bullying in the philiipines?​


Answer:

The legal basis for identifying and establishing bullying in the Philipines is the Republic of Philipines Act No. 10627.

It is also known as the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013. It comprises 10 sections which speak the following:

The definition of acts that constitute bullying;

Anti-bullying policies and who should adopt them and how they ought to be adopted; This section - Section 3, also speaks about actions which having being defined are prohibited.

Section 4 stipulates the methods for addressing bullying

Section 5 - the reporting requirement;

Section 6 speaks about the repercussions of non-compliance

7 through 10 are relatively generic sections comprising: How Rules must be implemented, Separability Clause, Repealing Clause, and Effectivity.

It is important to note that the Anti-Bullying Act of 2013 was designed for the elementary and secondary schools in the Philipines. It not only gives direction on what to do if a bullying incident occurred within a school, but it also references the steps to take if the act is carried out by the student of one school to another.
