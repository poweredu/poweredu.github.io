4. Direction: Say something or Describe how these Filipino Character Traits can be shown - give two tothree sentences in each character traitsA. RespectB. Strong family tiesC. Generosity and helpfulnessD. Religious​


A. Respect

Respect has been a great part of every Filipino's life, likewise, it is widely known that they do their tradition "pagmamano" for elderly family members or even some other people. The use of "po" and "opo" while having a conversation with elderly people is also their way to show some sort of respect.

B. Strong Family Ties

In terms of devotion to family, Filipino people never failed to amaze us with their traits towards their family members, they always prioritize having a strong bond and good harmony to their family.Some were still living in their old tradition which is staying in a single house with several family extensions.

C. Generosity and Helpfulness

they always been generous and caring to other people even though they are completely strangers. Several foreigners as tourists recognized Filipino hospitality.They tend to help each other in times of need as they treated everyone as their beloved family.

D. Religious

Most of Filipinos are considered Christian-Catholic, their traits and devotion towards their beliefs are too strong and somehow spectacular, I guess, that's the reason why many churches are established and still functioning in this country. They have saints, mass, altar for prayers, no wonder that their religious spirit has continue and will be continued to ignite for long runs.

The first is the veneration of the Buddha or other buddhas, bodhisattvas, or saints, which involves showing respect, meditating on the qualities of the Buddha, or giving gifts. Emily: A busy college student who is struggling to finish her assignments

Tom: Emily's classmate who notices that she's having a hard time keeping up

Mrs. Johnson: An elderly woman who is struggling to carry her groceries

Scene:

Emily is sitting in the college library, surrounded by textbooks and notes. She looks stressed and overwhelmed, trying to finish her assignments before the deadline. Tom walks in and notices that she's struggling.

Tom: Hey Emily, how's it going?

Emily: Oh hey Tom, I'm just trying to finish these assignments. I'm so behind on everything.

Tom: I noticed that you've been working hard lately. Do you need any help?

Emily: That would be great, actually. I'm really struggling to keep up.

Tom: No problem, I'm happy to help. Let's work on these assignments together.

As Emily and Tom work on their assignments together, they hear a commotion outside. They look out the window and see Mrs. Johnson struggling to carry her groceries. They both get up to help her.

Emily: Excuse me, ma'am, can we help you with those groceries?

Mrs. Johnson: Oh, bless your hearts! That would be wonderful.

Tom: Here, let me carry these bags for you.

Emily: And I'll help you to your car. Don't worry, we've got you.

Mrs. Johnson: Thank you, thank you so much. You two are so kind.

After helping Mrs. Johnson, Emily and Tom return to their work with a sense of accomplishment and happiness.

Emily: You know, it feels good to help people.

Tom: I agree. Generosity and helpfulness are important values to live by.

Emily: Yeah, and it's amazing how just a little bit of help can make such a big difference in someone's life.

Tom: That's why it's important to always be kind and helpful, even in the little things.

Emily: I couldn't have said it better myself. Thanks for your help today, Tom. You're a great friend.

Tom: Anytime, Emily. That's what friends are for. As Emily and Tom continued to work on their assignments, they felt a sense of happiness and contentment that came with helping others. They knew that they were making a difference in the world, even if it was just in small ways.

Later that day, as Emily was walking home, she saw an elderly man struggling to cross the street. Without hesitating, she ran over to him and offered to help him across.

Elderly man: Thank you, dear. You're so kind to help an old man like me.

Emily: Of course, sir. It's important to help those in need.

As she walked away, Emily couldn't help but smile. She realized that generosity and helpfulness were not only good for others, but they were also good for her soul. By helping others, she was making the world a better place, one small act of kindness at a time.

From that day forward, Emily made it her mission to live a life of generosity and helpfulness. She volunteered at local charities, helped her friends and family whenever they needed it, and went out of her way to make a positive difference in the world.

And as she looked back on her life many years later, she knew that the world was a better place because of her efforts. She had made a difference, and that was all that mattered.

Characters:

John - a neighbor who needs helpSarah - a generous and helpful neighbor

John: Hi, Sarah. Yeah, I'm trying to fix my lawnmower, but I can't seem to get it to work. I have so much to do in my yard, and I don't know how I'll be able to finish everything.
