We can't subtract two fractions with different denominators. So you need to get a common denominator. To do this, you'll multiply the denominators times each other... but the numerators have to change, too. They get multiplied by the other term's denominator.

24. Jay and Rey are comparing their heights. If Jay's height is 120³∕₄ cm and Rey's height is 96¹∕₃ cm. What is the difference in their heights?


Answer:

24 and 5/12 centimeters

Step-by-step explanation:

ASKED:

WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE IN THEIR HEIGHTS?

GIVEN:

Jay's height = 120¾ cm

Rey's height = 96⅓ cm

OPERATION/S TO BE USED:

SUBTRACTION and FINDING THE LCD OF FRACTIONS,

LCD means "Lowest Common Denominator"

SOLUTION:EXPRESS THE GIVEN AS BINOMIALS

120¾ ---> 120+¾

96⅓ ---> 96+⅓

FIND THE LCD OF THE FRACTIONS (2nd term of the binomials)

Find their LCD to make them "Similar Fractions". That way, it would be easier to

subtract them.

120+¾ ---> 120 + 9/12

96+⅓ ---> 96 + 4/12

To get the LCD, multiply the numerator and denominator of each fraction by the

denominator of the other fraction.

3 = 3•3 = 9 or 9/12

4 4•3 12

1 = 1•4 = 4 or 4/12

3 3•4 12

SUBTRACT

120 + 9/12

- ( 96 + 4/12 ) ---> Subtract the numerators

24 + 5/12 of the fractions.

ANSWER:

The difference in their heights is 24 and 5/12 centimeters.
