Answers: 1.The tone of "The Raven" is desperate, as the speaker turns to a raven for comfort in the loss of his beloved Lenore. The mood is eerie, as the poem utilizes dark and foreboding word choice and intentional literary devices which generate unsettled feelings in the reader.2.The tone of this novel is a tragic one. Throughout the novel Hemingway foreshadows Catherine's death. When Catherine is brought into the delivering room, the doctor tells her he has concerns about her narrow hips. Therefore, they had to get a caesarean, and the baby dies. Explanations:1.ToneThe tone of the poem "The Raven" is sorrowful and despondent. The speaker of the poem has lost his love, Lenore.MoodIn the poem, the speaker moves from melancholy to outright despair. His initial sorrow looks to have been caused by Lenore's death; however, by the end of the poem, his unhappiness is caused by the realization that his grief is eternal. In the poem, the Raven's words "Nevermore" is significant

2.ToneThe two main tones Hemingway uses in this novel are somber and detached. American's were disillusioned by the aftermath of WWI which explained the somber tone. The narrator was a veteran of WWI and had a detached tone for most of the novel because of the horrors he dealt with in the war.

MoodIn literature, mood is a literary device that reveals to the reader the overall feeling or attitude of a story. Mood is changeable; it does not have to be the same throughout the entire plot. Mood is communicated through the descriptions of places, characters, and situations in a story and how those elements connect to the plot. For example, a detective story might have a mood of mystery and foreboding, while a romance might have a mood of whimsy and excitement.
