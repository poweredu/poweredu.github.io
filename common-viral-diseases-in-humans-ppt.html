14. Can a viral disease turn into a bacterial disease?​


Answer: A viral disease cannot turn into a bacterial disease, but a body weakened by a viral infection can possibly develop a bacterial infection.

Explanation: Viruses are different from bacteria. Viruses are pieces of RNA or DNA wrapped in a shell of protein. Bacteria are single-celled organisms. They are much larger than viruses.

Viral diseases do not turn into bacterial diseases. But a viral infection can put the body's immune system under a lot of stress, which makes the immune system less capable of protecting the body from secondary infections. Because the immune system is weakened, the body is more vulnerable to getting a bacterial infection. When that happens, it is known as an opportunistic bacterial infection.
