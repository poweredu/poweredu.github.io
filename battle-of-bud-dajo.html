6. 10. Which of the following is a NOT an active volcano in the Philippines?A. Apo in Davao B. Bud Dajo in Sulu C. Isarog in Camarines SurD. Kar​


Answer:

Identify the Volcano that is not Active

Out of all the choices, Mount Apo in Davao is the one that is currently not considered active. Its current status as of 2022 is a potentially active volcano. The correct answer is letter A.

Explanation:

Mount Apo is a stratovolcano located in the borders of Davao del Sur and North Cotabato. It is the highest mountain in the Philippines. So far, its status is potentially active because there are no historical or Holocene eruptions recorded, but solfataric activities are strong all over the volcano. Bud Dajo, in Sulu, is a cinder cone, and it is considered active by PHIVOLCS since it has clues that the eruption has happened in recent centuries. Isarog in Camarines Sur was elevated into active status after the discovery of charcoal deposits indicating an eruption around 3500 BC, within the bounds of the Holocene. Kanlaon in Negros Occidental has historical eruptions, making it active.

Budding in Yeast

Yeast cells reproduce asexually by an asymmetric division process called budding. In yeast, budding usually occurs during the abundant supply of nutrition. In this process of reproduction, a small bud arises as an outgrowth of the parent body. ... The newly created bud divides and grows into a new cell.


11. which of the following is an active volcano in the philippines ?a.apo in davaob. bud dajo in sulo c. isarog in camarines surd. kanlaon in negros oriental​


Answer:

Selecting an Active Volcano

There are three answers to this question: Bud Dajo, Isarog, and Kanlaon. These three volcanoes from Sulu, Camarines Sur, and Negros, respectively, are all active volcanoes as per PHIVOLCS’s official website.

Explanation:

Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines, and it is actually a volcano. However, it doesn’t fall under the category of active volcanoes. Instead, it is classified as a potentially active volcano. This is because there are no known historical records of Apo’s eruption, and all of the geological samples obtained from Mount Apo did not yield an age of 10,000 years old. Nevertheless, solfataric activity is still present around the volcano, which makes it a potentially active one.

Answer:

1.Budding is a form of asexual reproduction that results from the outgrowth of a part of the body leading to a separation of the “bud” from the original organism and the formation of two individuals, one smaller than the other.

2.Bacteria, yeast, corals, flatworms, Jellyfish, and sea anemones are several animal species which reproduce through budding.

3.In the adult, or medusa, stage of a jellyfish, they can reproduce sexually by releasing sperm and eggs into the water, forming a planula.

15. Some simple invertebrates reproduce by budding What is budding​


Answer:

Budding is a form of asexual reproduction that results from the outgrowth of a part of a cell or body region leading to a separation from the original organism into two individuals. Budding occurs commonly in some invertebrate animals such as corals and hydras.
