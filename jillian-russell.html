9. Who is Charles Taze Russel?​


Answer:

Charles Taze Russell, or Pastor Russell, was an American Christian restorationist minister from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, and founder of what is now known as the Bible Student movement. After his death, Jehovah's Witnesses and numerous independent Bible Student groups developed from this base.

Answer

Charles Taze Russel, also known as Pastor Russel, is the founder of the  International Bible Students Association in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, U.S. The growth of the International Bible Students Association paved the way for the foundation Jehovah's Witnesses.

Additional Information

Charles Taze Russel was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, U.S. on the 16th of February 1852. He died in Pampa, Texas, U.S. on 31st of October 1916.

During his twenties, Pastor Russel couldn't accept the idea that the Merciful God has prepared an eternal hell for sinners. He didn't understand that God is both a God of Love and a God of Judgement because God is both omniscience and omnipotence. So, Pastor Russel left the Presbyterianism and the Congregationalism movement and became skeptical until, he had an encounter with William Miller, the founder of the Adventist Movement.

24. How do you solve russels' paradox?


Zermelo's solution to Russell's paradox was to replace the axiom "for every formula A(x) there is a set y = {x: A(x)}" by the axiom "for every formula A(x) and every set b there is a set y = {x: x is in b and A(x)}."

25. How did Russell connect belief with truth?​


Answer:

In the chapter "Truth and Falsehood" in his Problems of Philosophy,[2] Russell advances the "correspondence" theory of truth. On this theory, truth is understood in terms of the way reality is described by our beliefs. ... In order for our beliefs to be true, our beliefs must agree with what is real.
