12. he was the primary exponent of the impressionist movement and the focal point for other impressionist composers


Answer:

Claude Debussy is regarded as the primary exponent of the impressionist movement.

Explanation:

Achille Claude Debussy or referred to as Claude Debussy was a French composer born in August 1862 and died on March 1918. Claude Debussy is considered as one of the most influential composers of the late 19th and early 20th centuries. He is often regarded to as the first Impressionist composer, although he vigorously rejected the term.  

Claude Debussy’s works

Here is a list of some of Debussy’s works:

La Mer (1905) - La Mer is Debussy's most popular and widely performed concert work. Deux Arabesques- one of the very early impressionistic pieces of music, following the French visual art form, and portrays evocative scenes through music. Suite Bergamasque is a four-movement suite – ‘Prélude’, ‘Menuet’, ‘Clair De Lune’ and ‘Passepied’ for piano.   String Quartet In G Minor-it combines alluring melodies with exhilarating rhythmic freedom  Prélude à L’Après-Midi D’Un Faune – it is considered as one of the most popular pieces of music of all time  Pelléas Et Mélisande – it is the only opera Debussy every completed and is considered a landmark in 20th-century music
