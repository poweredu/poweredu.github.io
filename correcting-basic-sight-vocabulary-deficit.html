14. 1. Prosody refers to the patterns of language. a. stress and intonation b. vocabulary and meaning c. grammar and structure d. sight and sound in a​


Explanation:

a. stress and intonation.

Prosody refers to the patterns of stress, intonation, and rhythm in language. It includes variations in pitch, loudness, and duration of sounds, which can convey emotions, attitudes, and other meanings beyond the literal meaning of words. Prosody is an important aspect of spoken communication as it helps to convey the speaker's intended message and emotions.

Answer:

A. stress and intonation

Explanation:

Stress and intonation are two important aspects of prosody in language.

Stress refers to the emphasis or prominence given to certain syllables or words in a sentence. In English, stress is often marked by a change in pitch, volume, or duration of the stressed syllable. For example, in the word "banana," the stress is on the second syllable, so it is pronounced as "buh-NA-na."

Intonation, on the other hand, refers to the rise and fall of pitch in a sentence. It can convey different meanings or emotions, such as a question, a statement, or sarcasm. For example, a rising intonation at the end of a sentence can indicate a question, while a falling intonation can indicate a statement or a command.

Together, stress and intonation help convey meaning and nuance in spoken language, and can greatly affect how a message is perceived by the listener.

26. what could the BSP do to correct a budget deficit situation​


Answer:

here are some possible actions that the BSP (Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas) could take to correct a budget deficit situation:

1. Increase interest rates - This would encourage people to save more money rather than spend it, which could help reduce inflation and increase government revenue.

2. Implement fiscal reforms - This could involve reducing government spending, increasing taxes, or both. This would help balance the budget and reduce the deficit.

3. Sell government assets - The BSP could sell government assets to generate revenue and reduce the budget deficit.

4. Increase borrowing - The BSP could borrow money from other countries or international organizations to finance government spending and reduce the budget deficit.

5. Increase money supply - The BSP could increase the money supply to stimulate economic growth and generate more revenue for the government. However, this could also lead to inflation if not managed properly.

30. surplus, abundance :: deficit : ___________ *​

surplus, abundance :: deficit : ___________ *

The correct answer is lack.

Allow me to explain why this word fits the analogy. We can actually tell the answer by analyzing the different words here. Let us take a look at the first part. When there is surplus, this means that there is an abundance. Therefore, we have to find what exists when there is a deficit. Surplus is the opposite of deficit, while the opposite of abundance is none other than lack.
