7. What is The Golden Ratio And Golden Rectangle


  In the golden ratio, a + b is to a as a is to b.

The Golden ratio is a special number found by dividing a line into two parts so that the longer part divided by the smaller part is also equal to the whole length divided by the longer part. It is often symbolized using phi, after the 21st letter of the Greek alphabet. In an equation form, it looks like this:

a/b = (a+b)/a = 1.6180339887498948420 …Golden Ratio, is the ratio of the length to the width of what is said to be one of the most aesthetically pleasing rectangular shapes. This rectangle, called the Golden Rectangle, appears in nature and is used by humans in both art and architecture.

11. list three false notions about the golden age martial law and explain each false notions


FALSE NOTION

The Philippines' Martial Law period, which ran from 1972 to 1981, was characterized by totalitarian government, the repression of civil freedoms, and a profusion of violations of human rights.

Sadly, there are still certain misconceptions regarding this time period that are still prevalent. Here are three of them along with the explanations that go with them:

Martial Law improved the economy.This is a typical fallacy that proponents of martial law frequently use as justification for its application. Although the economy did expand at this time, the benefits were not fairly distributed. While the poor got poorer, the affluent got richer. The dictatorship also made extensive use of foreign loans, which ultimately resulted in a financial crisis in the 1980s. Additionally, the Marcos administration was known for its corruption, and crony capitalism played a significant role in the country's economic expansion.Corruption was nonexistent when martial law was in place.Contrary to popular belief, there was a significant corruption problem under Martial Law. Through bribery and corruption, the Marcos family and its allies gained vast fortune. As a result of the totalitarian regime's lack of openness and accountability, several public officials engaged in corrupt activities.During the time of martial law, neither resistance nor opposition existed.This is a perilous myth that downplays the sacrifices and hardships made by the courageous men and women who battled the Marcos dictatorship. There were many different types of resistance and dissent throughout the Martial Law era, including as student demonstrations, labor strikes, and armed conflict. For resisting the dictatorship, many activists, thinkers, and common people were detained, tortured, and murdered. The heroism and perseverance of individuals who opposed the dictatorship are a monument to the fact that democracy was finally restored in the Philippines.
