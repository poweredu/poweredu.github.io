The problem that the President of the United States of America poses in movies can vary depending on the plot and genre of the film. However, some common themes include:

Political Intrigue: In some movies, the President may be the target of political intrigue, either from within their own government or from external forces. This could be due to their policies, personal beliefs, or actions that have made them enemies.Assassination or Kidnapping: The President is a high-value target for those who wish to cause chaos or make a political statement. In some movies, the President may be the target of assassination attempts or kidnapping plots.Global Crisis: In disaster or apocalyptic movies, the President may be responsible for leading the country through a global crisis, such as an alien invasion, natural disaster, or war.Moral Dilemma: In some movies, the President may be faced with a moral dilemma that could have far-reaching consequences for the country. This could be a decision to go to war, approve a controversial law, or negotiate with a foreign government.

Ultimately, the problem that the President poses in movies is often a reflection of the concerns and fears of the society in which the movie was made. These films can provide a window into the political and cultural landscape of their time, and can spark conversations about the role of leadership and the responsibility of those in power.
