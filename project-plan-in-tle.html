2. What are the parts of a project plan in TLE?


Answer:

1. Project Statement: An overview of the project’s purpose, scope and objectives.

2. Stakeholder Analysis: A list of people involved in the project and their roles.

3. Project Scope: A detailed description of the project goals, tasks, deliverables, resources, timeline and budget.

4. Risk Management: Identification of potential risks and strategies for mitigating them.

5.

Answer:

A project plan in Technology, Livelihood Education (TLE) typically includes the following parts:

1. Project Title: The title of the project that clearly and concisely describes the main objective of the project.

2. Project Description: A brief overview of the project, including the problem or need that the project is addressing, the main goal and objectives of the project, and the expected outcomes or benefits.

3. Background: A description of the context in which the project is being implemented, including relevant information about the community or organization where the project will be implemented.

4. Scope and Limitations: A clear definition of the project's boundaries, including what is and is not included in the project, and any constraints or limitations that may affect the project's implementation.

5.Project Timeline: A schedule that outlines the major tasks and milestones of the project, including the start and end dates, and the estimated time required for each task.

6. Budget: An estimate of the financial resources required for the project, including the costs of materials, equipment, and labor, as well as any contingencies.

7. Human Resource Plan: A description of the project team and their roles and responsibilities, including the project manager, team members, and any external consultants or contractors.

8. Monitoring and Evaluation: A description of the methods and tools that will be used to monitor and evaluate the progress and success of the project.

9. Risk Management: An identification and assessment of potential risks or challenges that may arise during the project, and strategies for addressing or mitigating those risks.

1p Conclusion and Recommendations: A summary of the project's main findings and recommendations for future actions or improvements.
