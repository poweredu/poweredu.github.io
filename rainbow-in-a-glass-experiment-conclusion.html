27. why do we see a rainbow of colours when light passes through a glass prism?


When light passes through a glass prism, it is refracted, or bent, due to the change in the light's speed as it passes through the different mediums (in this case, air and glass). Different wavelengths of light (corresponding to different colors) are refracted at different angles, causing the light to spread out and create a spectrum of colors. This is why we see a rainbow of colors when light passes through a glass prism.

The colors of the rainbow can be remembered with the acronym "Roy G. Biv," which stands for red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. These colors are arranged in the spectrum in the order in which they appear when light is refracted through a prism.
