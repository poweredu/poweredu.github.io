27. similarities of Philippines and Korea​


Similarities of Philippines and Korea​

Asian and Western cultures have affected both cultures. Korea and the Philippines culture, like most Asian countries, were attacked by Japan during WWII. Interaction with foreign culture, technology, and the innate instinct of self-preservation have all shaped Korean culture, as has Filipino culture. The Philippines and South Korea enjoy a strong relationship since the two countries have always been and continue to be close diplomatic and military allies. In terms of commerce, immigration, and tourism, they are also important economic partners. The political histories of South Korea and the Philippines are startlingly similar. First and foremost, both countries were colonized. Korea was a Japanese colony from 1910 to 1945, while the Philippines was a US colony from 1899 to 1946 and a Spanish colony or culture for over three centuries before that.
