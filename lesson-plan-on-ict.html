9. What do you think are the problems that you might encounter in planning an ICT project? ​


Answer:

There are several potential problems that one might encounter when planning an ICT (Information and Communications Technology) project. Here are some examples:

1. Unclear goals and requirements: If the goals and requirements of an ICT project are not clearly defined, it can lead to confusion and disagreements among team members. It's important to have a clear understanding of what the project is trying to achieve and what features and functionality are necessary to meet those goals.

2. Limited budget and resources: ICT projects can be expensive, and there may be limitations on the budget and resources available to complete the project. This can lead to the need to prioritize certain features or to find creative solutions to achieve the desired outcomes with limited resources.

3. Technical challenges: ICT projects often involve complex technical systems, and there may be technical challenges that arise during the planning phase. These challenges can range from issues with compatibility and integration to problems with scalability and security.

4. Stakeholder buy-in: ICT projects often involve multiple stakeholders with different priorities and perspectives. Getting buy-in from all stakeholders and keeping them engaged throughout the project can be a challenge, especially if there are conflicting priorities or a lack of understanding about the project's goals and benefits.

5. Time constraints: Like any project, ICT projects are often subject to time constraints. There may be deadlines that need to be met, and delays can have consequences for the project's success. It's important to carefully plan and manage the project timeline to ensure that the project stays on track.

Overall, planning an ICT project requires careful consideration of a wide range of factors. By anticipating potential problems and taking steps to mitigate them, project managers can increase their chances of success.

30. enumerate five things that should be accomplished or considered during the planning of an ICT project​


Five (5) Things That Should Be Considered During Planning of an ICT Project

Planning is the first stage in creating an ICT Project. It includes specification of software requirements. It means, software requirements must be identified based on the business operations. Problems in the current system should be determined and addressed in the planning stage to know what are the improvements needed to include in the new system or project. Here are some things to consider in planning an ICT Project:

1. Timeline - in planning the project, always take a look at the time of the project development. Of course, the longer the project to develop, the higher the cost it will be.  

2. Manpower - to shorten the time of development and testing of the project, adding of right number of manpower is a must.  

3. Cost - a project without money involved is hard to pursue. Always consider the resources and cost of the project.  

4. Users - the number of users should be included in the plan because this project should benefit the company in the long run. The ICT project head should foresee how many users needed for the project.

5. Process - repetitive and manual processes should be remove and replaced by computerized or automated process to help the business save cost and time.
