2. Do animals really have a sixth sense?


So having a "sixth sense" doesn't literally mean sense number six, it instead means a sense that normal humans don't have, or a sense that is much better than what normal humans have, allowing the being to achieve feats that a normal human is not capable of.

Under that definition, it's kind of obvious that animals have sixth senses.

Some birds and insects can see magnetic fields (helps with migration); some fish can sense electrical activity; various birds, fish and insect can see colours outside the human range, including the ability to see polarized light; dogs can smell things that humans can't, loads of animals can hear things outside the human range.

This quote by Malcolm Bradbury implies that culture is a way to make sense of the world and to make it more understandable. He suggests that when we create cultural norms, values and beliefs, we can put a framework around our understanding of the world. In doing so, we attempt to make the world more predictable and easier to navigate. It also allows us to share our experiences and knowledge with others, as well as to identify what is important to us.

Answer:

The quote "Culture is a way of coping with the world by defining it in detail" by Malcolm Bradbury suggests that culture is a tool that human beings use to make sense of and navigate their environment. Culture provides a framework for understanding the world, and it helps us to define and categorize different aspects of our lives.

Bradbury argues that culture is a means of coping with the complexity and uncertainty of the world. It helps us to understand the world by providing a set of shared beliefs, values, and practices that shape our perceptions and actions. Culture is a way of providing order and meaning in a chaotic and unpredictable world.

The quote also implies that culture is not a fixed or static concept. It is a constantly evolving and adapting process, shaped by the experiences and interactions of different individuals and groups. Culture is defined in detail, meaning that it is constantly being refined, changed, and expanded upon through the actions and experiences of people.

In summary, Malcolm Bradbury's quote suggests that culture is a means of coping with the world. It provides a framework for understanding and navigating the complexity of the world. Culture is a constantly evolving and adapting process that is defined in detail by the actions and experiences of people.

Pigeons. Pigeons have a sixth sense called magnetoreception.
