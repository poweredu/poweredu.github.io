23. Between the broadcasting legislation of the Philippines and South Africa which is more effective and why?


Answer:

Both the Philippines and South Africa have broadcasting legislation that aims to regulate and ensure that their broadcast media outlets operate fairly, equitably, and sustainably.

In the Philippines, the National Telecommunications Commission (NTC) regulates media content and licensing requirements. The country's constitution guarantees freedom of the press, but the government may intervene in cases where the media violates the law. The Philippines has faced criticism for the inconsistent implementation of its broadcasting regulations.

On the other hand, South Africa's broadcast industry is governed by the Independent Communications Authority of South Africa (ICASA). The country has a relatively robust media regulatory system that promotes diversity of voices and protects the editorial independence of broadcasters. South Africa's broadcasting legislation has been lauded for providing a level playing field for all players in the broadcast industry.

Overall, determining which country's broadcasting legislation is more effective is subjective and depends on different factors, such as the context, goals, and priorities of the government and society.

25. who is the father of Philippine broadcaster?and his biography?


The Father of Philippines Broadcaster is James Lindenberg, his birthdate is December 20, 1921. He was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvannia. He moved to the Philippines in the 1940s. He is credited for his founding of Bolinao Electronics Corporation (BEC), the precursor of ABS-CBN Corporation, and dubbed as the Father of Philippine Television. He died in April 28, 2009 at Makati, Philippines.
