15. 1. A pressure vessel that has a volume of 10 m³ is used to store high pressure air for operating a supersonic wind tunnel. If the air pressure and temperature inside the vessel are 20 atm and 300 K, respectively. What is the mass of air stored in the vessel (in kilograms)?


Answer:

The mass of air stored in the vessel is 1.206 * 10^4 kg.

Step-by-step explanation:

The mass of air stored in the vessel can be calculated using the ideal gas law:

PV = nRT

Where:

P = pressure (in atm)

V = volume (in m³)

n = number of moles of gas

R = universal gas constant

T = temperature (in Kelvin)

Therefore, the mass of air stored in the vessel can be calculated as follows:

m = nM

Where:

m = mass of air (in kg)

n = number of moles of gas

M = molar mass of air

Using the ideal gas law, we can calculate the number of moles of gas as follows:

n = PV/RT

Therefore, the mass of air stored in the vessel can be calculated as:

m = PV/RT * M

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

m = (20 * 10^3) / (8.314 * 300) * 28.964

m = 1.206 * 10^4 kg

Sacred Tradition is the inspired and unwritten truths of the deposit of faith that were passed orally from Christ to the Apostles, then to the early believers, and so forth. Sacred tradition eventually developed sacred Scripture.
