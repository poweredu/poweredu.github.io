4. essay about value and impact of being a media and information literate individual to the global community


Answer:

Media and information literacy enables people to interpret and make informed judgments as users of information and media, as well as to become skillful creators and producers of informationgiphy (4) and media messages in their own right. MIL combines media literacy and information literacy under one term, and includes a combination of competencies. Media literacy emphasizes the ability to understand media functions, evaluate how they are performed and use them for self-expression, while information literacy emphasizes the importance of access to information and the evaluation and ethical use of such information.It recognizes the primary role of information and media in our everyday lives. It focuses on the  freedom of expression and information since it empowers citizens to understand the functions of media and other information providers, to evaluate their content, and to make informed criticism as users and producer of information and media content .

Information Literacy and Media Literacy are traditionally seen as separate fields. It is the strategy  that  brings together these two fields as a combined sets of competencies (knowledge, skills and attitude) necessary for life and work today. MIL considers all forms of media and other information providers such as libraries, archive, museums, Internet and other technology used.

The importance of being a media and information literate person is very crucial nowadays because we are living in the 21st century which revolves us with rapid growth of technologies such as television, computers, cellphones, radio’s and other medium for information. Some other benefits are to improve the quality of life, improved learning environment and more cohesive learning units.

According to: Nordic Information Center for Media and Communication Research, Media and information literacy (MIL) is considered a crucial competence in today’s logga-eng_0society. MIL equips citizens with the competencies they need to fully benefit from media and information channels and to enjoy fundamental human rights, such as the right to freedom of opinion and expression stated in Article 19 of the Universal  Declaration of Human Rights:

Everyone has the right to freedom of opinion and expression; this right includes freedom to hold opinions without interference and to seek, receive and impart information and ideas through any media and regardless of frontiers.

We can gain  inspiration to become an  future innovators to help our country to develop technologically and economically. Lastly we can promote our country and its rich in culture literature so that we gain more attention in the global community. To become a media literate is not to memorize facts or statistics about the media but rather to learn, to raise the right questions about what you are watching, reading, or listening to and to be Information Literate a person must be able to recognize when information is needed & have the ability to locate, evaluate and use effectively the needed information.

Title: The Importance of Media and Information Literacy in Today's World

Introduction:

In the digital age, we are constantly inundated with information from various sources such as social media, news outlets, and blogs. While the abundance of information may seem like a blessing, it can also be overwhelming and challenging to navigate. Therefore, being a media and information-literate individual is more important than ever before. In this essay, I will discuss the value of being a media and information-literate individual, and provide two examples to support my argument.

Body Paragraph:

One of the key values of being a media and information-literate individual is that it helps us make informed decisions. For example, let's say you are trying to purchase a new laptop. You do some research and find two laptops with similar specifications and prices, but one has significantly better reviews than the other. By being media and information literate, you can make an informed decision and choose the laptop with better reviews. Similarly, when it comes to political decisions, being media and information-literate can help you identify credible sources of information and make informed decisions based on facts rather than rumors or propaganda.

Another value of being a media and information-literate individual is that it helps us avoid falling prey to misinformation and fake news. In today's digital world, anyone can create and spread fake news and misinformation, which can lead to harmful consequences. By being media and information-literate, we are better equipped to identify misinformation and fact-check the information we consume. This, in turn, helps us avoid making decisions based on false information.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, being a media and information-literate individual is crucial in today's world. It helps us make informed decisions, avoid falling prey to misinformation, and navigate the overwhelming amount of information available to us. Therefore, it is essential that we develop our media and information literacy skills and become responsible and informed consumers of information. By doing so, we can ensure that we make informed decisions that are based on facts, rather than opinions or false information.
