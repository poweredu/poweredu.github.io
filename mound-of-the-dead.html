5. termite mounds biotic or abiotic ​


Answer:

Biotic

Explanation:

A termite mound is a complex biotic system.  It is has a large and complex set of microbes living within it, moderated by the termites themselves.  

Though dirt constitutes a fair bit of the mass, it is held together with saliva and feces and lots of stored (half chewed) food.

19. What is the role of termite mounds in ecosystem? ​


Answer:

Termites help plants grow by enriching the soil with nutrients and digging tunnels that improve water flow to roots. As a result, ecosystems with termite mounds may be more resistant to losing vegetation, especially during droughts.

Termite mounds retain more water than the surrounding soil. The retained moisture
results in more vegetation than would otherwise grow in this climate, reducing the chances of desertification. Termites bring organic material to their mounds, concentrating nutrients and fertilizing the soil on and around mounds.

21. do all volcanoes have mounds?​


Answer:

Not all volcanoes have mounds, but many do. The term "mound" can refer to different features of a volcano, such as the cone-shaped hill that forms when volcanic materials, such as lava and ash, build up over time.

Volcanoes that are formed from explosive eruptions, like stratovolcanoes, are the most likely to have mounds. These volcanoes typically have a steep, cone-shaped profile and can grow to significant heights. Some famous examples of stratovolcanoes with prominent mounds include Mount Fuji in Japan, Mount St. Helens in the United States, and Mount Pinatubo in the Philippines.

Other types of volcanoes, such as shield volcanoes and cinder cones, may not have prominent mounds. Shield volcanoes are typically characterized by a broad, gently sloping profile and are formed by relatively mild eruptions, while cinder cones are smaller, steep-sided volcanoes that are formed from explosive eruptions of volcanic ash and rocks.

So while many volcanoes have mounds, it ultimately depends on the type of volcano and the specific circumstances of its formation.

30. What is the Termite mound​


Answer:

Mound-building termites are a group of termite species that live in mounds. These termites live in Africa, Australia and South America. The mounds sometimes have a diameter of 30 metres. Most of the mounds are in well-drained areas. Termite mounds usually outlive the colonies themselves.
