Explanation:

the opposite of the benefit that would have been gained had an action, not taken, been taken—the missed opportunity. This is a concept used in economics. Applied to a business decision, the opportunity cost might refer to the profit a company could have earned from its capital, equipment, and real estate if these assets had been used in a different way. The concept of opportunity cost may be applied to many different situations. It should be considered whenever circumstances are such that scarcity necessitates the election of one option over another. Opportunity cost is usually defined in terms of money, but it may also be considered in terms of time, person-hours, mechanical output, or any other finite resource.

Although opportunity costs are not generally considered by accountants—financial statements only include explicit costs, or actual outlays—they should be considered by managers. Most business owners do consider opportunity costs whenever they make a decision about which of two possible actions to take. Small businesses factor in opportunity costs when computing their operating expenses in order to provide a bid or estimate on the price of a job. For example, a landscaping firm may be bidding on two jobs each of which will use half of its equipment during a particular period of time. As a result, they will forgo other job opportunities some of which may be large and potentially profitable. Opportunity costs increase the cost of doing business, and thus should be recovered whenever possible as a portion of the overhead expense charged to every job.

SOCIAL MEDIA

Social media can take various forms of technology-enabled activities. This includes photo sharing, blogging, social gaming, social networks, video sharing, business networks, virtual worlds, and several other activities. Social media refers to websites and programs emphasizing communication, community-based input, engagement, content sharing, and collaboration. People utilize social media to maintain relationships with friends, family, and communities.

BOOKMARKING

Bookmarking is the process of labeling a website page with a browser-based application to revisit it in the future readily. Instead of bookmarking social media postings in your browser's favorites, you can use the bookmarking functionality of specific platforms. Social bookmarking Websites allow users to save and share links to other websites or intriguing content. This is an excellent method for storing and organizing connections that are essential to us. Most bookmarks are marked, allowing later users to search for them by subject.

SOCIAL NEWS

Social news is the combination of internet news with a sort of social engagement, and it is fully dependent on social media websites. It may include sharing, voting, or user input, and what is provided is based on historical trends of how users interacted with comparable content.

MICROBLOGGING

Microblogging is a form of blogging consisting of small blog posts intended for quick and direct audience engagement. These brief blogs are disseminated via social media platforms such as Tw1tter and 1nsta9ram, and they may incorporate voice, text, photos, and video.

BLOG AND FORUMS

A blog is a constantly updated web page or website where the information appears in reverse chronological order. In contrast, a forum is an online discussion board where users publish messages to exchange ideas, thoughts, or assistance. Author-created content and comments are organized into categories in blogs. The sub-forums of a forum are filled with topics and posts produced by its members.
