15. In the parking lot of a building there are 56 cars, five-sevenths have 5 doors and three-fourths of these cars have a diesel engine. Calculate how many five-door cars have a diesel engine?​


30 five-door cars have a diesel engine.

Step by step explanation:

We are told that 5/7 of 56 cars have 5 doors.

To calculate a fraction of a quantity we have to multiply the fraction by the quantity:

5/7 of 56 cars = 5/7 × 56 = 5×56/7 = 280/7 = 40 5-door cars

We are told that 3/4 of these cars have a diesel engine:

3/4 of 40 cars = 3/4 × 40 = 3 × 40/4 = 120/4 = 30 diesel cars

Answer: 30 five-door cars have a diesel engine.

30. You are traveling in a car that is moving at a velocity of 20 m/s. Suddenly, a car 10 meters in front of you slams on it’s brakes. At that moment, you also slam on your brakes and slow to 5 m/s. Calculate the acceleration if it took 2 seconds to slow your car down. NOTE: It must have explanation


Answer:

Given:

Initial Velocity  = 20m/s

Distance of another car from our car = 10m

Final velocity of our car = 5m/s

find

The acceleration of our car to slow down in 2 seconds.

Using the equation v = u+at

where v = 5m/s

u = 20m/s

t = 2s

On substituting the values in the equation, we get:

5 = 20 + 2a

2a= 15

a= 7.5m/s^2

the acceleration of our car to slow down in 2 seconds is 7.5m/s^2.
