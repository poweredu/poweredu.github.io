Three Things you need to remember during debate

Debate competition may be tricky for some reasons. Sometimes we feel certain motions can be undebatable since we are not in the favour side of the house and it makes us less engage to the debate. Somehow, the debaters need to be actively engaged during the debate so the adjudicator can credit them more. These three things will help you win the debate even when you are not really knowing how the motion supposedly works.

During the debate, first, make sure that you and your team fulfill the speakers’ role. In Asian Parliamentary debate, there are 3 speakers which have various crucial responsibilities which need to be fulfilled. As the first speaker, we really need to settle the definition of the motion by contextualizing the real problem of the motion. Defining motion based on a dictionary won’t help the adjudicator know what your team’s stance on the debate is. As a second speaker, giving rebuttal will help the team a lot because it means your team can engage actively in the debate. Third speaker will need more cases or examples of the case to extend the whole team argument. Second of all, giving responses through POI (Point of Information) actively will make the adjudicator think that your team can contribute actively to the debate. Last but not least, construct your argument based on AREL (Assertion, Reasoning, Evidence, Linkback) structure. By having a clear segment on the argument, it makes it easier in delivering the argument, on the other hand it will help the adjudicator to write down your argument easily.

Fulfilling the speaker's role, actively engaging the debate through POI, and constructing arguments with AREL structure are basic things for debaters which shouldn’t be ignored in every debate round. Winning or losing may happen in a competition, but if you remember these three things during debate it will help increase the winning probability.
