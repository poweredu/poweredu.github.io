18. How Edwin Sutherland describe a person committing crimes and development of criminal behavior on his Differential Association Theory?​


Edwin Sutherland's Differential Association Theory states that a person committing crimes develops criminal behavior through interactions with others in their social environment who have engaged in criminal behavior. Criminal behavior is learned through socialization and imitation of others and is not innate. The development of criminal behavior is a gradual process that occurs over time through a series of interactions with others.
