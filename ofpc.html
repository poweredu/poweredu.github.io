4. give a question using a fpc and permutation and answer that question​


Question: How many ways can a committee of 4 people be formed from a group of 10 individuals?

Answer: To answer this question, we can use the formula for permutations, which is nPr = n! / (n - r)!, where n represents the total number of individuals and r represents the number of individuals to be selected for the committee. In this case, we have n = 10 and r = 4, so the number of ways to form the committee is:

10P4 = 10! / (10 - 4)! = 10! / 6! = 10 x 9 x 8 x 7 = 5,040

Therefore, there are 5,040 ways to form a committee of 4 people from a group of 10 individuals.

25. WHAT IS THE MEANING OF the difference ofP and Q


Answer:

The set of elements in P but not in Q.

Answer:

Let P and Q be two propositions. In some logic texts, they say that P→Q is a new proposition, also written ¬P∨Q. But P⟹Q is a relation between the two propositions, not a proposition itself.

Maybe an analogy will help. Let x and y be two real numbers. Then x+y is a new real number. But x≤y is a relation between the two real numbers, and is not itself a real number.

The confusion is that in logic, we talk about some objects called "propositions", but in the language we are using we may also think that we are writing propositions. So you have to keep these two levels separate somehow in your mind.
