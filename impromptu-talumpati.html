10. The similarities of impromptu and extemporaneous


Answer:

Explanation:They are both one of the major types of speech.

Both should have normal delivery style.

Like most speeches, both require an introduction, body, and conclusion. Furthermore, the delivery of both speeches is also fundamental to their effectiveness. Tone, pitch, and projection all need to be considered when delivering either speeches. What is more, techniques such as body language, posture, and eye contact cannot go unrecognised in either an impromptu o extemporaneous speech.

26. What is an impromptu speech?


This is the on-the-spot speech or debate that involves speaking within 5 to 8 minutes with little or no preparation at all after the topic has been given.  No rehearsal takes place in this kind of speech, and no organized references or materials to be used.  This style of speaking involves thinking on your own, and  the speaker just rely only with the stored knowledge he/she have.

 

Although this type of speech seems difficult, in reality, most speech in a person’s daily life is impromptu.  Every conversation does not need to be researched or prepare ahead before speaking to a friend.  The key difference is that, impromptu speech usually is given to a large audience, depending on the moderator of the program.
