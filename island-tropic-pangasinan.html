29. why tropical Island vulnerable to tourism​


Answer:

The growth of tourism in many small tropical and sub-tropical islands in the last two to three decades has been accompanied by the preparation of an increasing number of tourism development plans. The need to plan for tourism on islands is especially crucial given their limited size and resource base. Smallness and insularity often mean that the resource base of islands offers limited possibilities for development and their physical environment, society and economy may be particularly vulnerable to the changes which tourism and other external influences may bring (Conlin and Baum, 1995; Brigulio et al., 1996; Lockhart and Drakakis-Smith, 1997). Planning can help make the best use of the limited resources available, for example investment capital, land and labour, while at the same time mimimizing the adverse impacts which tourism may generate. Furthermore, the general increase in island tourism, whether in the Pacific, Caribbean or Indian Ocean, means individual island destinations are facing growing competition from other islands offering similar products and drawing on the same markets. Planning can also contribute to a better marketing positioning to face up to this competition.
