9. GUIDE QUESTIONS: 1. What are the impacts of climate change? 2. What does EL NINO mean? La NINA mean? 3. Why do we have more precipitation in the Phil during LA NINAP Less precipitation during EL NINO? How does trade winds affect the temperature and air pressure in the east part of the Phil.? 4. 5. Why do we have strong upwelling during LA NINA? Weak upwelling during EL NINO? pasagot den po ito


Answer:

1. Climate change has a huge range of impacts on the environment and society, from hotter and drier conditions, to more extreme weather like floods,G droughts, and higher storm intensity. It also leads to more frequent and intense heat waves, changes to ocean temperatures and sea levels, and more soil erosion, among other impacts.

2. El Nino is a naturally occurring meteorological phenomenon in which the sea surface temperatures of the Pacific Ocean rise, leading to changes in global weather patterns. La Nina is the opposite, a naturally occurring meteorological phenomenon in which the sea surface temperatures of the Pacific Ocean decrease, also leading to changes in global weather patterns.

3. La Nina is associated with increased precipitation in the Philippines due to the introduction of moist air from the Indian Ocean and the increased instability associated with La Nina. Conversely, El Nino is associated with decreased precipitation in the Philippines due to the increased stability in the atmosphere and the presence of drier air from the Pacific Ocean.

4. The trade winds in the east part of the Philippines can affect the temperature and air pressure because these winds play an important role in transporting air from one region to another and so can affect the local temperature and pressure. These winds also carry moisture, which can contribute to precipitation in the area, and also cause upwelling in the eastern Indian Ocean.

5. During La Nina, the trade winds that converge in the eastern Indian Ocean are strong, and so can cause upwelling of cold water to the surface, leading to an increase in rainfall. Conversely, during El Nino, the trade winds weaken and so cause less upwelling and a decrease in rainfall.

Answer:

Explanation:

El Nino means Little Boy, or Christ Child in Spanish. South American fishermen first noticed periods of unusually warm water in the Pacific Ocean in the 1600s. The full name they used was El Nino de Navidad, because El Nino typically peaks around December.

The Ati-Atihan festival is a Philippine festival held annually in January in honor of the Santo Niño (Holy Child or Infant Jesus) in several towns of the province of Aklan, Panay Island. The biggest celebration is held during the third Sunday of January in the town of Kalibo, the province's capital.
