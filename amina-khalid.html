30. Khalid and Jesse took different overnight trains. The graph shows the relationship between the total distance Khalid travelled and the time in hours. The distance that Jesse travelled after x hours is given by the equation y = 150x. Khalids Travel graph of a diagonal line on a coordinate plane going up and to the right with Time in hours on the x axis and Distance in miles on the y axis. The line begins at the origin and passes through the point 1 comma 140.


Since the graph is a diagonal line, the distance travelled by Khalid is directly proportional to the time. Thus, the equation of the line representing Khalid's travel is of the form y = kx, where k is the constant of proportionality.

Substituting the coordinates of the point that the line passes through, we get 140 = k(1). Solving for k, we get k = 140. Hence, the equation of the line representing Khalid's travel is y = 140x.

To compare the distance travelled by Khalid and Jesse, we substitute the same value of x in both equations. For example, if x = 3 hours, then the distance travelled by Khalid is 140x = 140(3) = 420 miles, and the distance travelled by Jesse is 150x = 150(3) = 450 miles. Since 450 > 420, Jesse travelled a greater distance than Khalid in 3 hours.

Therefore, Jesse travelled a greater distance than Khalid in all time intervals.
