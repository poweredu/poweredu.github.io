1. sayaw sa bangko costume​


SAYAW SA BANGKO DANCE COSTUME

The costume for the Sayaw sa Bangko dance are the following:

Women wear a patadyong (Pre-spanish clothing item or loose skirt) Men wear a camisa chino with handkerchief tied around their neck and rolled up trousers

The Sayaw sa Bangko is danced by a couple or a pair with barefoot on a narrow wooden bench. The couple or the pair moves backwards, forwards, hopping, twisting and switching places without falling off. Sayaw sa bangko has lively movements and festive music.

6. Background of sayaw sa bangko?​


Answer:

The Philippine folk dance Sayaw sa Bangko (literally means Bench Dance) originated in the Lingayen and Pangasinan of the Pangapisan Tribe, nowadays it is mostly performed during Town Fiestas. The dance step is quite simple but making sure both dance partners don`t fall down is the hard part, as they perform the dance on a narrow bench, hopping and inching and exchanging places from one end of the bench to the other side. It was originally performed by newlyweds during their wedding feast for their guests, the dance showcase both bride and groom and how they complement and help each other with the intricate steps making sure they don`t fall from the bench.

1. Tinikling

The Tinikling is the national dance of the Philippines and is a traditional folk dance that originates from the Spanish colonial era. The dance imitates the movement of the tikling birds as they dodge bamboo traps set by rice farmers. From this action, the Tinikling was created

Tagalog

2. Sayaw sa Bangko

English

Sayaw Sa Bangko is a famous Filipino folk dance where the dancers perform to show their incredible acrobatics skills and teamwork by dancing on a narrow bench. Usually performed by two persons, this particular dance requires the dancers to jumping and constantly switch places with their partners. This dance is commonly found in town fiestas.

15. Ano ang Sayaw sa Bangko?​


Answer:

Sayaw sa Bangko is a famous Pilipino folk dance in which the dancers must use good skill and balance as they dance on top of a narrow bench. This dance dates back to before the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, which means it is a purely Pilipino Ancestral dance. Dancers showcase their skills as they dance atop a narrow bench, while constantly trading places. As opposed to competing with each other to stay on the bench, dancers must complement each other to ensure that they do not fall. This dance is usually performed in town fiestas.

16. what is the steps of sayaw sa bangko?​


Explanation:

Do these steps outside the bamboo poles:

1. Hop on your right foot.

2. Hop another time on the right foot.

Perform these steps between both poles:

3. Step on the left foot.

4. Step on the right foot.

Do these steps outside the poles:

5. Hop on the left foot outside the poles.

6. Hop again on the left foot outside both poles.

Return to between the poles for these steps:

7. Step on your right foot.

8. Step on your left foot.

Doubles Tinikling Dance

For the foot work for these Tinikling steps, both dancers should start to the right side by the bamboo poles.

Do these steps outside the bamboo poles:

1. Hop on both feet.

2. Hop another hop on both feet.

Hop between the poles for these steps:

3. Hop on both feet.

4. Do another hop using both feet.

Straddle the poles as you jump outside them for these steps:

5. Hop on both feet.

6. Again, hop on both feet.

Go between the poles again to finish the dance:

7. Hop on both feet.

8. Do another hop on both feet.

These are the basics of the Tinikling, though the dance can be embellished with other steps for both singles and doubles, including arm movements.

18. props and costumes of santacruzan​


Santacruzan Flores de Mayo Festival Philippines Santacruzan is held annually in the warm month of May and is considered to be the Queen of Filipino Festivals. It features elaborately dressed teenage queens of Flores de Mayo as biblical and allegorical figures their consorts and the crowning of that years Reyna Elena.

Flores de Mayo (Spanish for "flowers of May") is a festival held in the Philippines in the month of May. It is one of the May devotions to the Blessed Virgin Mary and lasts for the entire year.The Santacruzan (from the Spanish santa cruz, "holy cross") is the ritual pageant held on the last day of the Flores de Mayo. It honors the finding of the True Cross by Helena of Constantinople (known as Reyna Elena) and Constantine the Great. Its connection with May stems from the May 3 date of Roodmas, which Pope John XXIII deleted in 1960 due to the trend at the time to abolish holy days. The Feast of the Exaltation of the Cross on September 14, which commemorates the recovery of the relic by Emperor Heraclius from the Persians instead of the finding by Saint Helen was all that was retained in the calendar of the New Order.

26. Folk dance Characteristics/ Description Origin Costume Props 1.Tinkling 2.Itik-Itik 3 Sayaw sa Bargk 4. Maglalatik 5.Cariñosa​


Answer:

Tinkling:

Tinkling is a Philippine folk dance that originated in the Visayan Islands. It is characterized by the use of bamboo poles, which are clapped together by the dancers in a specific rhythm while they move around them. The dance is often performed during celebrations and festivities, and it represents the joyful spirit of the Filipino people. The costume for this dance typically consists of traditional clothing such as the barong tagalog for men and the baro't saya for women.

Itik-Itik:

Itik-Itik is another Philippine folk dance that originated in Surigao del Norte. The dance is known for its graceful movements that imitate the movements of ducks. Legend has it that the dance was created by a young girl who was asked to dance by the village chief, but she did not know any formal dances. So, she mimicked the movements of a duck and created the Itik-Itik dance. The costume for this dance typically consists of a colorful dress for women and a barong tagalog for men.

Sayaw sa Bargk:

Sayaw sa Bargk is a folk dance that originated in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is often performed during religious celebrations and represents the cultural heritage of the region. The dance is characterized by the use of bamboo poles, which are used to create intricate patterns and movements. The costume for this dance typically consists of traditional clothing such as the barong tagalog for men and the baro't saya for women.

Maglalatik:

Maglalatik is a Filipino folk dance that originated in the province of Laguna. It is a mock-war dance that depicts the fight between the Moros (Muslims) and the Christians over the prized latik or coconut meat during the Spanish colonial era. The dance is characterized by the use of coconut shells as props, which are worn by the dancers on their hands and on their bodies. The costume for this dance typically consists of traditional clothing such as the barong tagalog for men and the baro't saya for women.

Cariñosa:

Cariñosa is a Philippine folk dance that originated in Panay Island. It is known for its romantic and flirtatious movements, which are reminiscent of courtship dances during the Spanish colonial era. The dance is often performed during weddings and other formal occasions. The costume for this dance typically consists of a colorful dress for women and a barong tagalog for men. Props such as handkerchiefs are often used to accentuate the graceful movements of the dancers.
