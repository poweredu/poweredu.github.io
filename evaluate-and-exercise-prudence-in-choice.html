To evaluate and exercise prudence in choices means to be careful before completely making up one’s mind about an important decision. Getting a tattoo on the face or in other places that can prevent you from getting a job. Investing everything you have in one thing.  Going through cosmetic surgery.

The choices we make today will take us to our future so it is important to make wise choices.

13. why should the virtues of frugaluty and prudence be exercised toward nature?


A Wise and good decision making is called prudent. In prudent concept, a person does not overestimate the amount of revenues recognized or underestimate the amount of expenses. Here the traditional method of keeping track of the amount of assets is required but not underestimate liabilities. The end result should be conventionally-stated financial statements


A frugal person always finds a way to minimize the monetary cost of their action while trying to keep it lower than their useful needs. For example: The person decides to buy rice and eggs, he buys it in bulk to minimize the cost at the same time he gains nutrition. The level of consumption of a frugal person is lower than that of a typical consumer. Thus the person is less subjected to hidden environmental cost.

29. Illustrate a situation wherein you exercise prudence when you make a choice ​.


Explanation:

One situation where I exercised prudence when making a choice was when I was considering whether to accept a job offer. I had received an offer from a company that seemed like a great opportunity, but I was also being offered a position at another company that I was very interested in. I knew that I needed to make a decision quickly, but I also wanted to make sure that I made the right choice.

To exercise prudence in this situation, I took the time to carefully weigh the pros and cons of each offer. I considered factors such as the job duties, the company culture, the location, the benefits and compensation package, and the potential for growth and advancement. I also sought out advice from people I trusted, such as my family and friends, and I did some research online to learn more about the companies and their reputations.

Ultimately, I made the decision to accept the job offer that I felt was the best fit for me based on my values, goals, and priorities. I felt that I had made a careful and thoughtful choice, and I was confident that I had exercised prudence in the decision-making process.
