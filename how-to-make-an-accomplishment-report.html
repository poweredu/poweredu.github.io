Later in life, keeping promises will help you be professional at work. Most jobs have deadlines and project termination dates. If you are good at keeping promises, you would be able to come up with a service or a product before the deadline. Your employment relies heavily upon your performance, and meeting deadlines is just the start of it. 6 It must be said that a promise is, first and foremost, a choice. It is a personal decision to fulfill a promise, and as such, it is also a personal decision to break it. The reason why people fail to keep their promises sometimes is rooted here. According to psychologist and writer Farouk Radwan, you should consider three things when you are about to make a promise: 7 The first is self-understanding. You should be able to tell what things you are likely to enjoy doing, which ones are chores for you, and those which you absolutely cannot abide. “For example,” he writes, “If you don’t know that working under pressure is the last thing you would want to do in life you might promise your manager to finish all of your work in few hours, [then you might start feeling the pressure].” According to Radwan, if you understand how you work, you will not make promises you cannot keep. 8 Related to the first is Ability If you do not know your own limitations, you might take on projects that you are not able to finish. For instance, if you offer to have all 3000 pages of your report photocopied, and you find that you’re exhausted at the 1500-paper mark, then you have just discovered your personal photocopying limit (to the detriment of your group). 9 Finally, there is practicality Some things just cannot be accomplished, given the time frame. And when you keep a promise, in effect, you are telling the other person that they are important to you. The Seven Communicative Strategies:

Nomination Strategy
When someone is presenting a particular topic, or saying what he thinks is relevant, he is using the nomination strategy.
Restriction
With the support of enough set of categories that are to be constrained by a particular response or reaction, someone is using a restriction strategy.
Turn-taking
A person knows when to speak or respond because he knows when is the right time to speak or his turn. That uis when someone applies the turn-taking strategy.
Topic control
Someone is using the topic control strategy by making sure that the interaction is ongoing. The use of topic control strategy requires techniques to keep the conversation going.
Topic shifting
The process of topic shifting is continuing a communication or interaction by asking relevant questions or elaborating what someone said.
Repair
When the communication is not in good shape, a repair strategy is being done to overcome communication breakdown.
Termination
It uses verbal or nonverbal signals to end communication or interaction.
