3. In the story of the gift of the magi, What features or elements of the story surprised me?​.


Answer:

The features of the story that surprised me was the irony of the two couples. It manifest the futility of the couples' coincidental sacrifices that give both nothing but frustration and realization about their love for each other. Although, it is notable that they are poor and have little money, they are willing to sacrifice anything to make each other happy. Specifically their most prized possessions. The couple's predicament not only is bittersweet and ironic but shows their sincerity and selflessness.

It sways the readers at the reminder that gifts given out of love are far more wiser, sincere, and pure as things can't always be materialistic to show someone how much you love them. By giving gifts with sentimental value, then you have given them the most valuable gift, anyone can have.

Answer:

Time, place and the details that make up time and place are what contribute to the setting. “The Gift of the Magi” takes place in a furnished flat in New York City, the day before Christmas. The time of this story is important because it explains why Della and Jim need a present for each other.


6. The gift of the magi How will you relate this story to the account on the three magi in the story of the birth of jesus​


Answer:

The magi are also well-known as the Three Wise Men.  According to the story of the magi, these men traveled far and long in order to find Jesus and give their gifts to Him.  They were also a contrast to the poverty into which Jesus was born. However, the gifts and the long journey were great sacrifices for these three men.  They symbolized the deep love they had for Jesus.  Likewise, Della and Jim each sacrificed their most prized possessions—Jim’s watch and Della’s beautiful hair.

12. What features or elements of the story surprised me?(the gift of the magi​


The parts of the story that surprised me were the good details and the ending, which I didn't see coming.

The story's title is an allusion to the wise men in the Bible, who were called "magi." When Jim sees that Della's hair is unkempt, he acts in a way that is hard to understand, which scares Della. At the end of the story, there is suspense and mystery. The name of the book shows that there is a secret and a thrill at the end. The way the author wrote it is really surprising to read because it starts out with a predictable flow and ends with a big mystery.

Setting

New York, December 24, 1906. Around this time, inflation had taken away many people's money and homes. Della wanted to buy her husband Jim a gift, but they couldn't afford it because of inflation. She had been looking for money for Christmas for months, but she could only find $1.87. Also, because of inflation, the letters in their mailbox have worn away, so they no longer get mail. They lived in a small apartment that seemed a little worn and old.

Point of view

3rd person limited. The narrator tells the reader everything that happens in the story. He also describes how the characters look and where they live.

CharactersJim Dillingham is Della Dillingham's husband. He works hard and can be counted on. He also treats his wife Della well. He is a full and interesting person.Della Dillingham was a devoted wife who would do anything to make her husband, Jim, happy. She is a full and interesting person.Madame Sofronie runs a hair shop where she buys and sells wigs. She is a flat and unchanging person.

Themes and SymbolsDella's love for Jim is shown by the watch chain, and Jim's love for Della is shown by the expensive combs.Their most valuable things are Jim's watch and Della's hair.The feeling of unconditional love was what the author was going for because when Della cut off her hair to buy Jim a gift, she and the readers thought Jim would be mad at her for doing that and wouldn't love her as much. But Jim surprised the readers when he said he loved Della the same with or without her hair.
