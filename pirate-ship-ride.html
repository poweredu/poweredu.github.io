26. A cartoon of 20 compact disks contains 5 pirate and 15 original disks. In how many ways can an inspector choose 3 of the compact disks and a. get one that is pirated? b. get two of the pirated disks? c. none of the disks is pirated?


a. To get one pirated disk, we have to choose 1 disk from the 5 pirated disks and 2 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,1) * C(15,2) ways (where C(n,r) denotes the combination of n items taken r at a time). So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk is:

C(5,1) * C(15,2) = 5 * (15 choose 2) = 5 * 105 = 525

Therefore, there are 525 ways to choose 3 disks with one pirated disk.

b. To get two pirated disks, we have to choose 2 disks from the 5 pirated disks and 1 disk from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(5,2) * C(15,1) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks is:

C(5,2) * C(15,1) = (5 choose 2) * 15 = 10 * 15 = 150

Therefore, there are 150 ways to choose 3 disks with two pirated disks.

c. To choose none of the disks as pirated, we have to choose 3 disks from the 15 original disks. This can be done in C(15,3) ways. So, the total number of ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated is:

C(15,3) = (15 choose 3) = 455

Therefore, there are 455 ways to choose 3 disks with none of the disks as pirated.
