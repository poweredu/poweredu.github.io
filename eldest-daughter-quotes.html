AGE WORD PROBLEMPROBLEM:

Mrs. Co is 45 years old, and her eldest daughter is 21 years old. When will Mrs. Co be 4 less than twice as old as her eldest daughter?

ANSWER

Finding the age of Mrs. Co's eldest daughter:

In the given problem, we must subtract 21 from 45 so we could get the present age of Mrs. Co's eldest daughter. Hence, 45 minus 21 is equal to 22. Therefore, the age of her eldest daughter is 22.

Representation:

Before we get the equation and the solution for the problem, let's represent their ages!

Let ...

'x' represents the number of years(x + 45) - Mrs. Co's age after 'the number of years' (in x years)(x + 21) - eldest daughter's age also in x years.

Equation:

Mrs. Co's age after 'the number of years' (x + 45) could be 4 years less than twice as old as her eldest daughter. So, the equation will be made this exactly:

Equation: x + 45 = 2(x + 21) - 4

in which the left side is the age of Mrs. Co (x + 45) and the right side is the 4 years less than twice as old as her eldest daughter.

Solution:

Now that there already have an equation for the given problem, let us solve the number of years represented as an 'x'.

Solution:

[tex]\begin{aligned}x + 45 & = 2(x + 21) - 4\\x + 45 & = 2x + 42 - 4\\x + 45 & = 2x + 38\\x - x + 45 & = 2x - x + 38\\45 & = x + 38\\45 - 38 & = x + 38 - 38\\7 & = x; \boxed{x = 7}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Answer:

In 7 years, Mrs. Co's age will be 4 less than twice as old as her eldest daughter.

Checking through Substitution:

Mrs. Co's age in seven years:

[tex]x + 45; x = 7\\7 + 45 = 52[/tex]

Eldest daughter's age seven years:

[tex]x + 21; x = 7\\7 + 21 = 28[/tex]

Check:

[tex]52 = 2(28) - 4\\52 = 56 - 4\\52 \: \rm (TRUE)[/tex]

Therefore, Mrs. Co will be 52 years old while her eldest daughter will be 28 years old.
