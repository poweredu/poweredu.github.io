18. What caused Arachne's fears and failures?


ARAKHNE (or Arachne) was an arrogant girl from the Greek town of Kolophon in Lydia who once dared challenge the goddess Athena to a contest in weaving. In the match Athena portrayed the gods seated in glory upon high thrones, while Arakhne represented them in the guise of animals chasing after mortal girls. Athena was furious at her impiety and beat the girl with her shuttle, driving Arakhne to hang herself in despair. The goddess then transformed her into a spider (Greek arakhnês)

27. What caused Arachne's fears and failures?


Greek Mythology: Arachne

Answer:

Arachne was a Greek Mythology that teaches moral lesson along the way as the story progresses. She was a beautiful maiden with exceptional talent of weaving; many people praised her for her handy-works. And she was the best among all in her eyes, no one dare compared to her talent. She said that no one taught her to weave, and that by her effort and inspiration she was able to make such wonderful and enchanting cloths.  

But then, one moment when people crowds over her works a person implied that the Goddess Athena must have taught her this but Arachne denied and boasted that Athena couldn’t even weave in the same level as her. Amidst the exchange of words a woman wrapped in a cloak appeared. It was Athena, she challenged Arachne who weaves best and in return whoever loses shall forfeit weaving forever.  

Arachne’s fears were:

She couldn’t win against Athena If she losts she would never weave ever again Her life, will that of no purpose

Her fears were the result of the challenge, for whoever loses must never touch a loom or a spindle. It was Arachne’s livelihood, her hobby, her passion. And if it was taken away from her, she finds no reason anymore to live.

Arachne’s Failures:

She failed to be a better weaver She failed to watch her words

Arachne was a woman with a talent that can impress even the heavens, but due to her carelessness she provoked a challenge she can’t win no matter what. She failed as a weaver who spoke greatness yet produced something unworthy. Yet, she honored Athena’s consequence and by that she gained the Goddess’ pity. She was turned into something that can weave as long as she wants without breaking the promise.
