The repeating decimal of 7/11 is 0.636363636363636363636364

The repeating decimal of 2/9 is 0.222222222222222222222222

The terminating decimal of 5/8 is 0.625

NOTE:

The first and second items are repeating decimals.

Explanation:

A terminating decimal is usually defined as a decimal number that contains a finite number of digits after the decimal point.

A repeating decimal is a decimal number whose digits are periodic and the infinitely repeated portion is not zero. The infinitely repeated sequence is called repetend. To express a repeating decimal, simply put a bar above the first 2 decimals of the number.

Example:

Writ 0.22 and draw a bar above 22. That's how you write a repeating decimal.
