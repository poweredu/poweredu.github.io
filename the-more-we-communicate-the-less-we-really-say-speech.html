When you exercise and your muscles work harder, your body uses more oxygen and produces more carbon dioxide. To cope with this extra demand, your breathing has to increase from about 15 times a minute (12 litres of air) when you are resting, up to about 40–60 times a minute (100 litres of air) during exercise.

Good day everyone,

Today we are gathered here to discuss whether or not rich people should pay more taxes. As the speaker who opposes this proposal, I firmly believe that this idea is not a constructive way to address income inequality and the financial struggles that our society faces.

Firstly, it is important to understand that a progressive tax system is already in place. In most countries, the more money you earn, the higher percentage of taxes you pay. This system is in place to ensure that those who have more resources contribute more to society. Therefore, I do not see the need to impose additional taxes on the wealthy.

Furthermore, we should consider the negative consequences of increasing taxes for the rich. Many people who earn a high income are entrepreneurs, business owners, or investors. By raising their taxes, we risk discouraging them from investing their money in our economy. They may choose to invest in other countries that have more favorable tax policies, leading to a loss of job opportunities and economic growth in our own country.

Moreover, it is unfair to single out the wealthy as the sole source of funds for our public services. Everyone should contribute to the betterment of our society according to their means, not just the rich. It is essential that we promote a culture of shared responsibility and encourage everyone to do their part, regardless of their income.

In conclusion, imposing more taxes on the rich is not the solution to address income inequality or financial struggles. It is essential to create a fair and equitable tax system that benefits all members of society, not just a select few. Let us work together to promote a culture of shared responsibility and find more constructive ways to address the issues that we face as a society. Thank you.
