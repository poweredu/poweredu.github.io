THINGS I LEARNED FROM DIGITAL PAINTING

The process of painting on a tablet and making artwork digitally on a computer is known as digital painting. This is accomplished by using painting software that mimics the conventional method of painting by using brushes.

In the area of animation, illustration, and 2D game art, many artists opt to digital painting because it's less expensive in the long run (few materials) and it's a requirement.

Because computers are now so much faster and more powerful, it's also a lot easier to get into digital painting. As a result, they are able to handle big files with minimal lag time, as well as brush strokes that are extremely quick.

You can also save time by using digital layers in your work with modern painting tools.

With traditional mediums, this isn't always possible.

Layers are a term used to describe the process of painting (or drawing) on many fictitious layers that are then stacked to form the finished piece. To change something, simply erase that layer and try again.

The concept of digital painting is quite basic at its core: you paint digitally!
