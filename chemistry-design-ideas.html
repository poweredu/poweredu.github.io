1. explain chemistry of fire own idea​


Answer:

Fire is a chemical reaction that happens when heat, fuel, and oxygen come together. The heat causes the fuel to start burning, and the oxygen helps the fuel to keep burning. This creates light, heat, and smoke. Fire needs all three things to start and keep burning, so if you take away one of them, the fire will stop. This is why we use things like fire extinguishers to take away the oxygen, or fire blankets to take away the heat.

20. Describe the ideas of the Ancient Greeks on the atom, the ideas of the Ancient Greeks on the elements, and the contributions of the alchemists to the science of chemistry.​


Answer:

According to ancient Greek belief, all matter can be further split until it is no longer divided. "Atom" is derived from the Greek word "atomos," which meaning "indivisible." When a stone is sliced in half, the two halves retain the same qualities, leading to the conclusion that if objects are repeatedly cut into smaller pieces, the object will eventually become too small to cut up.

These types of indivisible substance, according to thinkers like Leucippus and Democritus, are too little to be seen but absolutely solid, immutable, and indestructible. They also thought that physical characteristics like color and taste were made up of atoms.
