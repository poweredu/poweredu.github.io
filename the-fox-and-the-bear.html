2. story of the fox and the bear​


Answer:

Sure, here's an analysis of "The Story of the Fox and the Bear" according to the guide questions:

1. Characters: The characters in the story are the Fox and the Bear. They are both animals who are trying to find food during the winter season. The Fox is cunning, while the Bear is naive and easily fooled. The Fox tricks the Bear into giving him his food by pretending to be his friend.

2. Setting: The story takes place in the forest during the winter season. The environment is described as being cold and barren, with little food available for the animals to eat.

3. Conflict: The main conflict in the story is between the Fox and the Bear. The Fox wants the Bear's food, but the Bear is reluctant to share it. The Fox uses his cunning and trickery to manipulate the Bear into giving him the food.

4. Plot: The story begins with the Fox and the Bear both searching for food in the forest. The Fox approaches the Bear and pretends to be his friend, offering to help him find food. The Bear trusts the Fox and shows him where his food is hidden. The Fox then tricks the Bear into leaving the food unguarded, and steals it for himself. The story ends with the Bear realizing that he has been tricked and feeling foolish for trusting the Fox.

5. Tone: The author's attitude towards the subject is one of caution and warning. The story is meant to teach a lesson about the dangers of trusting others too easily and the importance of being cautious and aware of one's surroundings.

6. Point of view: The story is told from a third-person point of view, with an omniscient narrator describing the actions and thoughts of both the Fox and the Bear.

7. Theme: The lesson that the author wants the reader to learn from the story is the importance of being cautious and not trusting others too easily. The story also teaches that cunning and trickery can be used to take advantage of others, and that it is important to be aware of these tactics and protect oneself from them.
