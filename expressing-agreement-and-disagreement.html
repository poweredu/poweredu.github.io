6. How do signs of agreement and disagreement help in expressing an opinion


Answer:

Using signs of agreement and disagreement can be a useful way to show your position on a topic and engage in discussions with others.

Signs of agreement can include:

• Saying "yes" or "I agree"

• Nodding your head

• Saying "exactly" or "precisely"

• Using phrases like "I see what you mean" or "I couldn't agree more"

Signs of disagreement can include:

• Saying "no" or "I disagree"

• Shaking your head

• Saying "I see it differently" or "I don't agree"

•Using phrases like "I don't think that's true" or "I see things differently"

Using these signs can help to clearly communicate your position on a topic and show that you are actively listening to and engaging with others. It's important to use these signs in a respectful and non-confrontational manner, and to be willing to listen to and consider the perspectives of others, even if you disagree with them.

13. Give your insight about the importance of expressing your agreement and disagreement to social growth.​


Answer:

For a society to grow, it is incredibly important for people to have the ability to express their agreement and disagreement regarding different themes and issues. Whenever this right is oppressed, there will be plenty of negative outcomes that the future generations will witness.

Before anything else, I think we should all acknowledge the fact that nothing is perfect. When it comes to any proposal, there will always be advantages and disadvantages. If we want to discuss an issue thoroughly, it is important to hear from people who support it and those who criticize something.

The free expression of agreement is important and valuable in any society. This shows that people support the idea and believe that it is feasible and will have a good outcome. However, blind support is not going to be helpful. The people should be equipped with critical thinking skills and an understanding of the issue at hand to arrive to properly analyze the matter.

A society where disagreement or criticism is not allowed or considered taboo will find it hard to understand what is truly best for the people. It is wrong to think that because someone expresses disagreement, they are not supportive of progress. Constructive criticism is important since it can be used to further improve an idea, a project, or even social matters.

Democracies are based on the idea that the constituents can decide what will happen to the society for themselves. The concept is that the power lies in the people. This involves the expression of both agreement and disagreement without any fear for their safety. Everyone has a valid opinion, but the goal is to arrive at something that works for the greater good. This refers to the decision that is not only going to benefit certain members of the society but the highest number of people possible.

If we want a progressive society, we need to give the people the tools they need to make informed and educated choices about their future. We should invest in the people to make this happen. Once this has been accomplished, the people will be able to decide what is best for them. The freedom to express agreement and disagreement is important because every member of society is important. A society that listens to its members and considers their opinion will naturally experience growth and progress that will be beneficial both its current constituents and the future generation.
