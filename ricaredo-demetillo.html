1. What is the paraphrase of My Neighbor By Ricaredo Demetillo


The question “what is the paraphrase of My Neighbor by Ricaredo Demetillo” may be asking for a summary of the My Neighbor poem. For the appropriate use of word paraphrase, you may click on this link: https://brainly.ph/question/215375. The poem narrates how a boy got too curious of his neighbor, who built a high wall around his house to possibly prevent any intruders. The boy was also likely excited to know what more is inside the walls and what else could it be hiding.


Other Ricaredo Demetillo poems are entitled the “The Lover’s Death,” “The Pasig River,” and plays like the “The Heart of Emptiness is Black,” and many others. Ricardo Demetillo’s biography is peppered with great works and notable contributions to the Filipino culture. He was not only a poet but also a prolific essayist and playwright (know more about this term here: https://brainly.ph/question/638843), who won various awards here and abroad. He was also a recipient of a Carlos Palanca award, to know more about this award, you can click this link: https://brainly.ph/question/955705.


Apart from being a literary genius, Demetillo was also able to academically excel. He finished his Master’s Degree in Fine Arts in English and Creative Writing at Iowa State University. He was also a Rockefeller scholar at Indiana University.
