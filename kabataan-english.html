Answer:

Youth now and then have undergone significant changes in terms of their attitudes, values, and beliefs. The youth of today are vastly different from the youth of the past in many ways.

Firstly, the youth of today are much more connected to the world than ever before. With the advent of the internet and social media, young people have access to a wealth of information and can connect with others from all over the globe. This has allowed them to become more informed and aware of the world around them, as well as more politically and socially active. In contrast, the youth of the past were much more isolated and had limited access to information and opportunities to connect with others.

Secondly, the youth of today have more opportunities than ever before. With the rise of technology, there are many new fields and industries that have emerged, creating more job opportunities for young people. Additionally, young people now have access to a wider range of education and training opportunities, which has helped to break down traditional barriers to success. In the past, opportunities for young people were much more limited, and their options were often determined by their social and economic background.

Thirdly, the youth of today also have a more diverse set of values and beliefs than the youth of the past. Today's young people are more accepting of diversity and more open to different lifestyles, cultures, and beliefs. They are also more politically aware and engaged than ever before, and are more likely to take action on issues that they feel passionately about.

However, it's not all positive, the constant use of technology also has its downsides as it has led to an increase in mental health issues such as depression, anxiety and addiction.

Another major change that has occurred in the youth of today is their approach to mental health and well-being. In the past, mental health was not widely discussed or understood, and young people were often expected to simply "tough it out" when faced with emotional or psychological difficulties. Today, however, mental health is recognized as a crucial aspect of overall well-being, and young people are encouraged to seek help and support when needed.

Furthermore, the youth of today are more open and expressive about their emotions and feelings than the youth of the past. They are also more likely to engage in self-care and mindfulness practices, such as meditation and yoga, as a means of maintaining their mental and emotional well-being.

In conclusion, the youth of today are vastly different from the youth of the past in many ways. They are more connected to the world, have more opportunities, and hold a more diverse set of values and beliefs. Technology has played a major role in shaping the way that young people communicate and interact with the world, but it has also brought about new challenges in terms of mental health and well-being. Despite these challenges, the youth of today are well-equipped to navigate the rapidly changing world and make a positive impact in society.
