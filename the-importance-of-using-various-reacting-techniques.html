Combustion reactions are reactions with combustible materials with oxidizing agents to produce oxidized products. Oxidizers are chemicals that fuels need to burn, generally oxygen. Consider the example of burning magnesium metal.

2Mg + O2 → 2MgO + Heat

Here, 2 magnesium atoms react with oxygen molecules to produce 2 molecules of magnesium oxide compound which releases heat in the process.

2. Combination Reaction

A combination reaction is a chemical reaction in which two or more reactants combine to form a single product is known as a combination reaction. The form of the equation is as below:

X + Y → XY

Combination reactions are also known as synthesis reactions.

Combination reaction example: 2Na + Cl2 → 2NaCl

3. Decomposition Reaction

Reactions in which one compound breaks down into two or more simpler compounds are known as decomposition reactions. The form of the equation is as below: XY → X + Y

Reactions in which compounds decompose upon heating are known as thermal decomposition reactions. Decomposition reactions are the opposite of combination reactions. An example of a decomposition reaction: CaCO3 → CaO + CO2

A chemical reaction in which a more reactive element displ4ces a less reactive element from a salt solution. The form of the equation is

Displ4cement reactions are also called substitution reactions. An example of a displ4cement reaction: Zn + CuSO4 → ZnSO4 + Cu

23. Why the REACT strategy in teaching is important? Elaborate


The REACT strategy in teaching is a valuable tool for educators because it helps students to become more engaged and active in the learning process. REACT stands for Reflect, Engage, Act, Collaborate, and Transform. This strategy encourages students to look at the material from multiple angles, to think critically and to engage actively with the content.

Reflecting on the material allows students to consider the material from a variety of perspectives. This encourages them to think critically and to gain a deeper understanding of the material.

Engaging with the material encourages students to actively participate in the learning process. This could include discussions, debates, and writing activities.

Acting on the material encourages students to apply their learning to real-world situations. This could include field trips, simulations, and hands-on activities.

Collaborating with other students provides an opportunity for students to work together to solve problems. This encourages teamwork and builds social skills.

Transforming the material encourages students to creatively express what they have learned. This could include creative writing, art, and music.

Overall, the REACT strategy encourages students to think critically, collaborate, and apply their knowledge to real-world situations. It is an effective way to engage students in the learning process and to help them develop the skills they need for success.

Calculating the Formula Mass of a Compound.

Balance the Chemical Equation

Mg + N₂ → Mg₃N₂

Identify the molar mass of every element

Molar mass of Mg = 24.305 g/mol

(24.305)(3) = 72.915 g Mg

Molar Mass of N = 14.0067 g/mol

(14.0067)(2) = 28.0134 g N

Add the total molar mass to find the mass of the compound

m = ∈(molar masses per element)

m = 72.915g + 28.0134

molar mass of Mg₃N₂ = 100.9284 g/mol
