20. how did first anaerobic bacterias developed, and where were they thriving?


Answer:

Aerobic and anaerobic bacteria are important to the ecosystem. The aerobes can survive in areas that are rich in oxygen, whereas the anaerobes do not need oxygen to survive. The aerobes are present in varied places like air, water, soil, food, inside the body, and so on. The ecosystem depends on these bacteria because they participate in a variety of important reactions. Inside soil, they decompose the organic matter into smaller compounds that can be used by plants. Some legumes have roots that harbor various anaerobic bacteria (as the process required an oxygen-free environment) that assimilate nitrogen and return it to the surrounding. They are also present in the gut of ruminants and humans, where they metabolize larger biomolecules and make them easy to digest. The bacteria play an important role in nitrogen, carbon, and sulfur geochemical cycles and help to maintain their composition in the ecosystem.
