The flippers of whale and forelimb of a cat are the homologous structures.

The flipper of a whale, leg of a cat, together with the wing of a bat,  are all very similar to the human arm, most especially to the humerus bone, ulna and radius. These features of these animals appear to be similar  in form. Bats, whales, cats, humans and many other animals have very similar homologous structures which demonstrates that they all had a common ancestor.

What are homologous structures?

Homologous structures are structures that look the same between organisms regardless of their functions. Organs and structures which have a common anatomical plan and similar origin regardless of its function are regarded as homologous.

25. write homologous or analogous for the given anatomical structures1.) human arm and starfish arm2.)forelimb of dog and fin of shark3.)wings of penguins and flipper of dolphins4.) foot of frog and forelimb of horse​


1.Analogous - The human arm and the starfish arm have different structures and functions. The human arm is composed of bones, muscles, and tendons while the starfish arm is composed of a flexible skeletal structure and tube feet used for locomotion and feeding. Although both structures have a similar function in locomotion and grasping, they evolved independently from different ancestral structures.

2.Analogous - The forelimb of a dog and the fin of a shark have different structures and functions. The forelimb of a dog is composed of bones, muscles, and tendons and is used for walking, running, and grasping objects. The fin of a shark is composed of cartilage and is used for swimming and maintaining buoyancy. Although both structures are used for movement, they evolved independently from different ancestral structures.

3.Homologous - The wings of penguins and the flippers of dolphins have similar structures and functions. Both structures are modified forelimbs adapted for swimming and diving. The bones in the wings of penguins are similar in structure to those in the flippers of dolphins, and both structures have a similar shape and range of motion.

4.Homologous - The foot of a frog and the forelimb of a horse have similar structures and functions. Both structures are adapted for locomotion, with the foot of a frog used for jumping and the forelimb of a horse used for walking, running, and grasping objects. Although the structures have different shapes and sizes, they share a common ancestor and have similar skeletal structures.

TRUE

Sea stars\starfish are remarkable for their capacity to regenerate limbs and, in rare cases, complete bodies, in addition to their peculiar appearance. They do this by encasing the majority of their essential organs in their arms. Some starfish species require the core body to be whole in order to regenerate, although a few may produce an entirely new sea star from a part of a severed limb.
