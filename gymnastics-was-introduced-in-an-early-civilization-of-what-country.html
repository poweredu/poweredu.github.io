11. What is significant role of agriculture during the early civilizations?


Answer:

When early humans began farming, they were able to produce enough food that they no longer had to migrate to their food source. This meant they could build permanent structures, and develop villages, towns, and eventually even cities. Closely connected to the rise of settled societies was an increase in population.

As food was grown and stored more efficiently, populations increased and settlements grew larger, creating both the incentive and the means to produce even more food on more land. Agriculture spread at different rates, depending on climate and geography.

15. what important agricultural innovation that crop yield of early civilization?​


Answer:

The history of agriculture records the domestication of plants and animals and the development and dissemination of techniques for raising them productively. Agriculture began independently in different parts of the globe, and included a diverse range of taxa. At least eleven separate regions of the Old and New World were involved as independent centers of origin.

Wild grains were collected and eaten from at least 105,000 years ago.[1] However, domestication did not occur until much later. Starting from around 9500 BC, the eight Neolithic founder crops – emmer wheat, einkorn wheat, hulled barley, peas, lentils, bitter vetch, chick peas, and flax – were cultivated in the Levant.[2] Rye may have been cultivated earlier, but this remains controversial.[3] Rice was domesticated in China by 6200 BC[4] with earliest known cultivation from 5700 BC, followed by mung, soy and azuki beans. Pigs were domesticated in Mesopotamia around 11,000 BC, followed by sheep between 11,000 BC and 9000 BC. Cattle were domesticated from the wild aurochs in the areas of modern Turkey and Pakistan around 8500 BC. Sugarcane and some root vegetables were domesticated in New Guinea around 7000 BC. Sorghum was domesticated in the Sahel region of Africa by 3000 BC. In the Andes of South America, the potato was domesticated between 8000 BC and 5000 BC, along with beans, coca, llamas, alpacas, and guinea pigs. Bananas were cultivated and hybridized in the same period in Papua New Guinea. In Mesoamerica, wild teosinte was domesticated to maize by 4000 BC. Cotton was domesticated in Peru by 3600 BC. Camels were domesticated late, perhaps around 3000 BC.

What are the uniqueness of the fall of the four rivers of the early civilizations?

Despite the fact that each culture was distinct, we can observe common themes across these early civilizations since they were all centered on rivers. These early civilizations were all hydraulic empires, most notably.

The Tigris and Euphrates Valleys, the Nile River Valley, the Indus River Valley, and the Yellow River Valley were the four river valley civilizations. Because rivers gave locations to hunt and fish, civilizations sprang up around them. In addition, when the rivers filled, the surrounding land became productive.

Humans have lived near water since the dawn of time since water is the most essential resource for human survival and growth. Many civilizations were built near rivers, and water resources were managed to limit the impact of floods and droughts.
