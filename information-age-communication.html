29. how did new media technologies in the industrial age affect communication and information?​


Answer:

The industrial age saw a significant shift in communication and information dissemination, thanks to the emergence of new media technologies that revolutionized how people communicated with each other. Some of the new media technologies that emerged during this period include the telegraph, telephone, and radio. Here are some ways in which these technologies impacted communication and information:

1. Increased speed of communication: The telegraph and telephone allowed people to communicate over long distances in real-time, enabling them to share information and conduct business more efficiently.

2. Widened access to information: The telegraph and telephone made it possible to transmit information across great distances, opening up new avenues for news reporting and information sharing.

3. Standardized communication: The telegraph and telephone introduced standardized codes and formats for communication, making it easier for people to understand each other despite linguistic differences.

4. Increased commercialization of communication: The rise of new media technologies paved the way for the commercialization of communication and information dissemination, as businesses realized the potential for profit in these fields.

5. Democratization of information: With the rise of radio broadcasting, information dissemination became more democratic, as it was no longer controlled by a small group of elites.

6. Creation of new forms of media: The industrial age saw the emergence of new media technologies, such as the phonograph, film, and photography, which revolutionized how people recorded and shared information.

Explanation:

Overall, the new media technologies that emerged during the industrial age had a profound impact on communication and information dissemination. They facilitated faster and more efficient communication, widened access to information, democratized information dissemination, and created new forms of media that continue to shape the world we live in today.
