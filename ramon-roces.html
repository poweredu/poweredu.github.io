Balance refers to many things. As toddlers, we learn to balance our bodies. Riding a bicycle requires balancing an object. Even how we organize our time requires a measure of balance.

Lucrecia Roces Kasilag. Born in San Fernando, La Union, in 1918, Lucrecia Roces Kasilag completed her undergraduate studies at the Eastman School of Music before continuing her education at the Philippine Women's University and St. Scholastica's College. She held a prominent place in the Philippines' music community as a composer, teacher, writer, and administrator..Josefino Chino Toledo is a conductor-composer. He is currently a full professor of composition and music theory at the University of the Philippines as well as the executive director of the Miriam College Centre for Applied Music. Toledo was the original music director of the modular music group Grupo 20/21 as well as the Metro-Manila Concert Orchestra (MMCO).Jose Maceda. The musicality of the Filipino people was thoroughly investigated by Jose Maceda, a composer, musicologist, teacher, and performer. Maceda began a lifetime commitment to promoting and understanding traditional music from the Philippines. Through his studies and travels, Maceda has amassed an enormous amount of music recordings from the most distant island communities and mountain villages.Ramon Pagayon Santos. The leading representative of contemporary Filipino music in the nation at the moment is composer, conductor, and musicologist Ramon Pagayon Santos. Santos, a leading player in the second generation of contemporary Filipino composers, has made significant contributions to the search for new musical paths that draw inspiration from non-Western traditions in the Philippines and Southeast Asia.

27. Karanasan ni Joaquin " chino"Roces noong panahon ng batas militar


Answer:

Si Joaquin P. Roces ay kilala sa karamihan ng mga "street parliamentarians" bilang Chino Roces, "Tatang," isang kaswal na bihis, mabait na ginoo na may puting buhok at bigote.

Isa siya sa mga pinaka-maimpluwensyang personalidad sa bansa bago idineklara ng martial law ni Pangulong Marcos, na siyang may-ari at publisher ng Pilipinas na pinakalat na napalaganap at iginagalang mga pahayagan sa panahong iyon. Kasama dito ang Manila Times, Daily Mirror, Taliba, at Weekly Women's Magazine. May-ari din siyang istasyon ng radyo at isang istasyon ng telebisyon.

Nang ideklara ni Marcos ang batas militar noong 1972, si Roces ay kabilang sa una na inutusan niya na inaresto at ikinulong. Siya ay makulong ng dalawang beses pa pagkatapos nito, ngunit tumanggi siyang sumuko. Sa kabila ng kanyang mataas na edad at hindi magandang kalusugan, siya ang nanguna sa maraming mga rali ng protesta, nahaharap sa mga kanyon ng tubig, mga truncheon at mga bomba ng luha sa gas.
