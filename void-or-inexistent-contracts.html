1. example situation of Void contract​


Answer:

Any contract agreement created between two parties for illegal actions is also considered a void contract. For example, a contract between an illegal drug supplier and a drug dealer is unenforceable from the onset due to the illegal nature of the agreed-upon activity.


2. explain the difference between voidable contract and void or in existence contract.​


Answer:

Essentially, the difference between void and voidable contracts is enforceability: a void contract is illegal and unenforceable; a voidable contract is legal and enforceable. A contract that is void is unenforceable, meaning that neither party has legal recourse against the other for a breach.


3. Which of the following contract up sale is void?


Answer:

A void contract is legally invalid from the moment the two parties sign it because it's illegal or violates public policy.. Examples of a void contract include contracts where the parties agree to do something illegal, like committing a crime, or otherwise against the law even if the thing agreed upon isn't a crime.

28. Enumerate flora and fauna that become extinct and the reasons for their inexistence​


Answer:

Extinction of Flora and Fauna

Extinct Flora:

Glossopteris – a plant that used to cover the southern continents, Glossopteris became extinct due to changes in the world’s climate. Cooksonia – these are believed to be the first land plants, but have gone extinct in the Early Devonian period due to a shift in the world’s climate.

Extinct Fauna:

Dinosaurs – dinosaurs used to rule the world during the Mesozoic, from 251 to 65.5 million years ago. However, the asteroid impact 65.5 million years ago ended their reign. Many species of dinosaurs died during the impact and the subsequent chaos that followed. Wooly Mammoth (Mammuthus primigenius) – the wooly mammoth is one of the largest animals to roam the land. Their population started to dwindle during the Last Ice Age because of overhunting by humans. Dodo Bird (Raphus cucullatus) – the dodo bird became extinct during the 17th century as colonists landed in the island of Mauritius. The rats, dogs, and cats that they introduced on the island killed off the birds and destroyed their eggs.
