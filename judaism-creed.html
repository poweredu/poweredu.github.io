4. how is the creed a trinitarian?​


Answer:

The Nicene Creed is considered trinitarian because it affirms the belief in the three persons of the Holy Trinity: the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit. The creed states that the Father is the creator of all things, that Jesus is the Son of God and was born of the Virgin Mary, suffered, died, and rose again, and that the Holy Spirit is the giver of life who proceeds from the Father and the Son. By acknowledging these three distinct persons as one Godhead, the Nicene Creed asserts the central doctrine of the trinitarian belief.

6. difference between rabbinical judaism and karaite judaism​


Answer:

Rabbinic Jews have specific traditions on how the tassels are to be knotted. Karaite Jews, for their part, have certain traditions on the manner of braiding the tassels, although they are not binding. Consequently, the way the Tzitziyot are made usually distinguishes Karaite Tzitziyot from rabbinic Tzitziyot.

why do Catholics pray the creed

The Creed is a summary of the Christian faith and expresses the central truths that the members of the Catholic Church believe.

In Mass, we pray the Nicene Creed, which was written during two of the great councils of the Church, Nicacea in 325 A.D. and Constantinople in 381 A.D.
