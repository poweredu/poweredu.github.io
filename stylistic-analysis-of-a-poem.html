5. a) Explain the significance of style in literary works. b) Critically examine the significance of stylistics in the analysis of literary texts.​


Answer:

a) Style is an essential element of literary works as it serves as a medium through which the author communicates with the reader. The style of a literary work includes the use of language, syntax, tone, and other literary devices that the author employs to convey their message effectively. Style can influence the mood, atmosphere, and meaning of a literary work, making it a crucial element for literary analysis. The style can also reveal information about the author's personality, cultural background, and worldview. A well-crafted style can engage readers, evoke emotions, and make the text more memorable.

b) Stylistics is an approach to literary analysis that examines the linguistic and formal elements of a text to understand its meaning and significance. Stylistic analysis involves studying the author's use of language, grammar, and literary devices to determine how they contribute to the overall message of the text. Stylistics can provide insight into the author's intentions, the cultural and historical context of the text, and the literary techniques employed in the work.

The significance of stylistics lies in its ability to enhance our understanding and appreciation of literary works. By analyzing the style of a text, we can uncover its deeper meanings and appreciate the craft of the author. Stylistics can also help us to identify the unique features of an author's style and their contributions to the literary tradition. Moreover, stylistic analysis can enable us to compare and contrast different literary works and to understand the stylistic evolution of a particular genre or period. However, it is important to note that stylistics should be used in conjunction with other approaches to literary analysis, such as historical and cultural criticism, to gain a holistic understanding of a text.

6. stylistic devices examples


Answer:

1. Adnomination

Repetition of words with the same root. The difference lies in one sound or letter. A nice euphony can be achieved by using this poetic device.

Examples: “Nobody loves no one.” (Chris Isaak). Someone, somewhere, wants something.

2. Allegory

Representation of ideas through a certain form (character, event, etc.). Allegory can convey hidden meanings through symbolic figures, actions, and imagery.

Example: Animal Farm by George Orwell is all about the Russian Revolution. And characters stand for working and upper classes, military forces, and political leaders.

3. Alliteration

The repeated sound of the first consonant in a series of words, or the repetition of the same sounds of the same kind at the beginning of words or in stressed syllables of a phrase.

Examples: A lazy lying lion. Peter picked a peck of pickled peppers. Sally sells seashells by the seashore.

4. Allusion

Reference to a myth, character, literary work, work of art, or an event.

Example: I feel like I’m going down the rabbit hole (an allusion to Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland by Lewis Carroll).

5. Anaphora

Word repetition at the beginnings of sentences in order to give emphasis to them.

Example: “Let freedom ring from the mighty mountains of New York. Let freedom ring from the heightening Alleghenies of Pennsylvania. Let freedom ring from the snow-capped Rockies of Colorado. Let freedom ring from the curvaceous slopes of California.” (Martin Luther King)

Opposite: Epiphora. Word repetition at the end of sentences.

Example: “And that government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth.” (Abraham Lincoln)

13. Kahulugan ng stylistic


Stylistics, a branch of applied linguistics, is the study and interpretation of texts in regard to their linguistic and tonal style. As a discipline, it links literary criticism to linguistics. It does not function as an autonomous domain on its own, and it can be applied to an understanding of literature and journalism as well as linguistics.Sources of study in stylistics may range from canonical works of writing to popular texts, and from advertising copy to news, non-fiction, and popular culture, as well as to political and religious discourse. Indeed, as recent work in critical stylistics,multimodal stylistics and mediated stylistics has made clear, non-literary texts may be of just as much interest to stylisticians as literary ones. Literariness, in other words, is here conceived as 'a point on a cline rather than as an absolute'.

Stylistics as a conceptual discipline may attempt to establish principles capable of explaining particular choices made by individuals and social groups in their use of language, such as in the literary production and reception of genre, the study of folk art, in the study of spoken dialects and registers, and can be applied to areas such as discourse analysis as well as literary criticism.

Common features of style include the use of dialogue, including regional accents and individual dialects (or ideolects), the use of grammar, such as the observation of active voice and passive voice, the distribution of sentence lengths, the use of particular language registers, and so on. In addition, stylistics is a distinctive term that may be used to determine the connections between the form and effects within a particular variety of language. Therefore, stylistics looks at what is 'going on' within the language; what the linguistic associations are that the style of language reveals.

Acrostic STYLITICS

An acrostic is a literary device in which the first letter of every verse consecutively forms a word or message.  An acrostic is mostly applied in poetry, but can also be used in prose or word puzzle. This word or alphabet is often connected to the theme of the poem. It is deliberately inserted to make readers discover the layered message. It also acts as a mnemonic device that can quicken the pace of the memorization process. Acrostic poetry can be written in any meter, or free verse form, with or without a rhyme scheme. However, the most common types of acrostic poems are those in which the initial letter of each line forms a word, and is often capitalized.

18. a) Explain the significance of style in literary works. b) Critically examine the significance of stylistics in the analysis of literary texts.​


Answer:

Significance of Literary

a) Style refers to the unique way in which an author expresses their ideas through language, including the use of syntax, diction, tone, and other literary devices. Style is significant in literary works because it can evoke emotions, create atmosphere, establish a narrative voice, and communicate meaning beyond the literal interpretation of the text. A writer's style can help to distinguish them from other writers and make their work recognizable, influencing the way that readers interpret and engage with their writing.

b) Stylistics is the study of the style of written or spoken language, including literary texts. It involves analyzing the linguistic and literary techniques used by authors to convey meaning, and can provide insights into the author's intentions, the social and cultural context in which the text was written, and the reader's response to the text. Stylistic analysis can help to reveal the underlying themes, symbols, and metaphors within a text, and can provide a more nuanced understanding of the author's perspective.

However, it is important to approach stylistic analysis with a critical eye, as it can be subjective and open to interpretation. Different readers may interpret the same stylistic elements in different ways, and stylistic analysis can also be influenced by the reader's own cultural and literary background. Additionally, a focus on style alone may overlook other important aspects of a text, such as its historical context or the author's biography. Therefore, while stylistic analysis can provide valuable insights into literary texts, it should be used in conjunction with other analytical methods to provide a more comprehensive understanding of the text.

26. a) Explain what is stylistics. b) Explain the significance of style in literary works. c) Explain the significance of stylistics in the analysis of literary texts. ​


a) Stylistics is a branch of linguistics that studies the features of language use in literature and the way they contribute to the meaning and effect of a text. It analyzes the various linguistic choices made by the author, such as diction, syntax, figurative language, and rhetorical devices, to uncover the stylistic patterns and strategies that shape the literary work.

b) Style is an essential aspect of literary works as it contributes to the overall aesthetic and emotional impact of a text. It creates a distinctive voice or tone that can evoke different moods and feelings in readers. Style can also reveal the author's personality, worldview, and cultural background, as well as the historical and social context in which the work was created. Moreover, style can convey different levels of meaning and ambiguity, which can enrich the reader's interpretation and appreciation of the text.

c) Stylistics is significant in the analysis of literary texts because it provides a systematic and objective approach to understanding the stylistic features of a text and their effects on the reader. By examining the linguistic choices made by the author, stylistics can uncover the underlying patterns and structures of a text, which can reveal its meaning, themes, and messages. Stylistics can also help to identify the author's intended audience and purpose, as well as the literary conventions and traditions that influenced the text. Furthermore, stylistic analysis can facilitate a comparative study of different literary works and genres, and help to identify the stylistic innovations and trends that shape literary history.

A. Stylistics is the study of the style of a piece of writing or speech. It looks at how the writer or speaker uses language to create meaning, and how the language choices affect the reader or listener.

B. Style is an important element in any literary work. It is used to convey the author's message, create atmosphere, and evoke emotion in the reader. The author's choice of words, sentence structure, and punctuation all contribute to the overall style of the work.

C. Stylistics is an important tool in the analysis of literary texts. By looking at the language choices made by the author, we can gain insight into the author's intentions and the themes of the work. Stylistics can also help to identify the genre of a text, and to compare different authors or works.
