2. Places (any) in the Philippines where minerals are abundant ​


Answer:

The Philippines is a country rich in mineral resources. Here are some places in the Philippines where minerals are abundant:

Caraga Region - This region in the northeastern part of Mindanao is known for its abundant mineral resources, including gold, copper, nickel, chromite, and iron.

Cordillera Administrative Region - This region in the northern part of Luzon is rich in gold, copper, and silver. The Benguet Corporation's open-pit mine in Itogon, Benguet is one of the largest gold mines in the country.

Masbate - This island province in the Bicol Region is home to the largest gold mine in the country, the Masbate Gold Project.

Surigao del Norte - This province in the Caraga Region is known for its abundant nickel reserves. It is home to several nickel mines, including the Taganito and the Rio Tuba mines.

Zambales - This province in Central Luzon is known for its abundant chromite reserves. The Acoje mine in Sta. Cruz, Zambales is one of the largest chromite mines in the country.

Palawan - This island province in the MIMAROPA Region is known for its abundant nickel deposits. It is also home to the largest copper mine in the country, the Rio Tuba mine in Bataraza.

These are just a few of the many places in the Philippines where minerals are abundant.

Volcanoes are formed when two plates collide or along the convergent boundary. As the plate collide, denser plate usually oceanic plate will subduct beneath the other and moves towards the mantle. As the oceanic plate subduct and moves towards the mantle beneath the other plate, the leading edge of the subducting plate will melt and this melted material will turn into magma and rise up on the surface of the other plate forming a volcano on the surface.

DIDIPIO, Philippines – Before OceanaGold began to systematically remove any trace that Dinkidi Mountain ever existed in northern Luzon’s Sierra Madre range, 100 farmers raised rice and citrus crops on farmland they had cleared in the mountain’s shadow. Didipio was a village of 700 residents, many of whom settled on Dinkidi’s east side in the 1960s and 1970s after walking, with their children in tow, for two days on steep forest trails.

In many ways Didipio was a rare place of hard work and solitude, a village difficult to reach by vehicle or on foot, and distant from the mainstream of Philippine life.

Many of those settlers — a minority in a village that today has 3,000 residents — blame Australian-Canadian mining company OceanaGold for ending Didipio’s era of agrarian calm.

Starting with construction in 2011, it took just two years for the company to replace Dinkidi with an open-pit gold and copper mine nearly a kilometer in diameter and nearly 300 meters (~1,000 feet) deep. Millions of tons of mine waste forms a perimeter of terraced rock around half of the mine. Above that is a green lagoon filled with liquid tailings, spreading over more than 100 hectares (~250 acres) where rice was once cultivated

18. Why do you think are metallic deposits abundant in places where there are trenches or volcanoes?


Mineral deposits are said to be produced at the Earth's active centers. These centers are the location where hydrothermal circulation is present. The presence of hydrothermal circulation relates to the temperature gradient. The higher the temperature gradient, the nearer the magma chamber is to the surface of the earth. 

Trenches and volcanoes are products of plate tectonic movements which creates hydrothermal activity and results to the formation of metallic deposits.
