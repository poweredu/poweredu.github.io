2. Give 4parts of the Proscenium stage ​


Answer:

Proscenium Stage Parts

The four main parts of a Proscenium stage are:

1. Stage: The main performing area where actors and actresses perform. This is usually raised above the audience and surrounded by a large frame called the "proscenium arch."

2. Proscenium Arch: The large frame that surrounds the stage and separates it from the audience. The proscenium arch is an important architectural feature that helps to define the playing space and focus the audience's attention on the performance.

3. Apron: The area of the stage in front of the proscenium arch that extends towards the audience. This is where actors can interact directly with the audience and where important events can take place during a performance.

4. Fly System: A system of ropes, pulleys, and counterweights used to raise and lower backdrops, curtains, and other scenic elements onto and off of the stage. The fly system is a key component of the Proscenium stage and allows for quick and seamless changes in scenery during a performance.

1618–
