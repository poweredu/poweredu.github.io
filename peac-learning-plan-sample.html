8. write down a plan to do acts of justice, love and peace in your family, neighbors and friends. write down ways on how you will carry out your plans​


Answer:

Act of Justice:

Plan: To actively promote fairness and equality within my family, neighbors, and friends by speaking out against discrimination and actively advocating for marginalized groups.

Execution:

Educate myself on issues of discrimination and privilege.

Call out and challenge any discriminatory language or behavior within my immediate community.

Support and amplify the voices of marginalized groups within my community.

Vote and support political candidates and policies that promote equality and fairness for all.

Volunteer and support organizations that work towards justice and equality.

Act of Love:

Plan: To actively show love and kindness towards my family, neighbors, and friends by being a supportive and caring presence in their lives.

Execution:

Make an effort to regularly check in with my loved ones and ask how they are doing.

Offer to help with tasks or errands for my neighbors and friends.

Be a listening ear for anyone who needs to talk or vent.

Show physical acts of affection, like hugs and kisses.

Show love through acts of service and kindness

Act of Peace:

Plan: To actively promote peace and understanding within my family, neighbors, and friends by fostering open communication and empathy.

Execution:

Practice active listening when communicating with others.

Seek to understand and acknowledge the perspectives of others, even if they differ from my own.

Speak up and intervene if I witness harmful or violent behavior within my community.

Encourage and facilitate open and honest conversations about difficult or sensitive topics.

Practice forgiveness and let go of grudges.

It is important to note that these plans are not exhaustive and it is important to continue to reflect and adjust as you go along. It's also important to recognize that change takes time, and be patient with yourself and others as you work towards making a positive impact.

17. Why does Eastern Visayas needs peace and security in their development plan?


Answer:

Officials of the National Capital Region (NCR) Regional Peace and Order Council (RPOC) visited Eastern Visayas on Monday to learn how the region deals with its security, and peace and order concerns, as well as development issues, and interventions carried out after super typhoon Yolanda.

“We wanted to personally hear it from them and also learn several other programs, which we can also adopt in Metro Manila like the Women Peace Table,” said Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG) NCR Regional Director Maria Lourdes Agustin during the RPOC Interfacing between authorities of NCR and Region 8 (Eastern Visayas).

Women Peace Table is the peace council and a DILG program launched early this year in Northern Samar intended to end the exclusivity of negotiating tables for men.

It aims to include women as active players in peacebuilding and not view them as mere “victims” of conflicts and not “actors” to end wars.

It is generally agreed upon that peace is a desirable state for individuals and societies. Peace can be defined as a state of tranquility or quiet, free from disturbance or turmoil. It is often associated with feelings of contentment, happiness, and security.

In contrast, war is a state of armed conflict between different nations or groups. It is characterized by violence, destruction, and often loss of life. War can have a wide range of causes and may be motivated by various factors, such as political, economic, or ideological differences.

It is understandable that many people would prefer peace to war, as peace can provide a sense of stability and security, while war often brings chaos, destruction, and suffering. At the same time, it is important to recognize that peace is not always easy to achieve, and that it requires effort and cooperation from all parties involved. Conflict and disagreement are natural and inevitable parts of human interactions, and finding ways to resolve these conflicts peacefully can be challenging.

Overall, it is important to work towards building a more peaceful world, and to find ways to address conflicts and differences in a constructive and non-violent manner.
