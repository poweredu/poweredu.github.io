7. superstitions related to comets and asteroids with a scientific evidence against it


Answer:

Superstitions about comets and asteroids DO in fact have a scientific basis. The specific sciences in question being psychology and sociology.

Superstitions about comets and asteroids, like all superstitions, are based on patterns and a human desire to explain and understand the world around us. If in your part of the world, the last time a comet showed up there was a drought, the next time your people see one they are likely to prepare for hard times.

People need an explanation for why the drought is happening to them, and the comet becomes a sort of external scapegoat. It remains in the social memory and can in some cases even become part of local myth and spiritual belief.

Comets and asteroids are just left over planetary debris. Most of them are nothing more that water, minerals and metals. At most it's possible some may contain organic material, but there's nothing mystical or special about them. All superstitions based on them are a product of the human mind and social conditioning.
