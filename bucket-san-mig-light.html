20. There are two buckets: A and B. There are 11 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A and 15 kg of water in bucket B. Pour a part of the pure alcohol in bucket A into the bucket B and mixed it with water. Pour part of the mixture in bucket B into bucket A, then the pure alcohol content in bucket A become 62.5% while the pure alcohol content in bucket B will be 25%. In the second mixing, how many kg is the mixture poured from bucket B into bucket A?


Answer:

See below.

Explanation:

When pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the content of bucket B becomes an alcohol solution. After pouring alcohol solution from bucket B to bucket A, the concentration of bucket B has not changed.

However, when pouring pure alcohol from bucket A into bucket B, the 15 kg of water in bucket B turns into a 25% concentration of alcohol solution, and subsequently, the solution in bucket B will be 15 ÷ (1 - 25%) = 20 kg.

So bucket B contains 20 - 15 = 5 kg of pure alcohol. The remaining pure alcohol in bucket A is 11 - 5 = 6 kg.

Let the alcohol solution poured from bucket B to bucket A be x kg, then it contains pure alcohol of 25% of x = x/4. Because 6 kg of pure alcohol in bucket A has an alcohol solution with a concentration of 62.5%,

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{6+\frac{x}{4}}{6+x}=\frac{62.5}{100}\implies x=6\end{aligned}[/tex]

Thus, the mixture poured from bucket B to bucket A is 6 kg on the second time.

23. Migs and Bert have a total of $80. After Migs spent $5.25 on chips, he is left with 4 times as much as Bert. How much does Bert have?


Answer:

$59.8

Step-by-step explanation:

Migs + Bert = $80

Migs spent $5.25 of the $80. Therefore subtract $5.25 from $80. The answer is $74.75

Migs = 1 and Bert = 4. The ratio given is now 1:4.

Add the digits in the ratio. 1 + 4 = 5

$74.75 divided by 5 is equal to $14.95. Migs = $14.95

Bert has 4 times as much as Migs. Therefore, $14.95 times 4. The answer is $59.8.

Therefore, Bert has $59.8.
