6. Beginning inventory is P50,000, net cost of purchases is P125,000, and ending inventory is P35,000. Compute for the cost of goods sold.


Answer:

P140,000

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's identify the given first and give substitution on variables for simplicity.

B = Beginning Inventory  

N = Net Cost of Purchase

E = Ending Inventory

S = Cost of Goods Sold

The Values of our variables are this:

B = P50,000

N = P125,000

E = P35,000

S = ?

Our problem is to find "S", the Cost of Goods Sold.

S = (B+N) - E

Subtitute the Values

S = (P50,000 + P125,000) - P35,000

Add first the values inside the clause.

S = P175,000 - P35, 000

Proceed on Subtracting

S = P140,000

So the total value of Cost of Goods that were sold is P140,000.

28. it is an inventory costing method that assumes that the first items placed in inventory are the first sold​


Answer:

FIFO method

The FIFO method assumes that the first unit in inventory is the first until sold. FIFO gives a more accurate value for ending inventory on the balance sheet. On the other hand, FIFO increases net income and increased net income can increase taxes owed.
