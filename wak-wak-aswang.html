16. A 27k man waks at a park during the afternoon. If the man produce 37J of energy in walking. How fast the man walks?


Answer:

A 27k man waks at a park during the afternoon. If the man produce 37J of energy in walking. How fast the man walks?

Let's identify the given first,

mass = 27 kg

Work = 37N-m or J

The formula for kinetic energy, KE is

[tex]KE=\frac{1}{2}mv^2[/tex]

To solve for the velocity, let's isolate it based on the equation above

[tex]2KE=mv^2\\\\v^2=\frac{2KE}{m}\\\\v=\sqrt{\frac{2KE}{m}\\}[/tex]

Substituting the values, we get

[tex]v=\sqrt{\frac{2(37)}{27}}\\\\v=1.656 \frac{m}{s}[/tex]

The man is walking 1.656 m/s.
