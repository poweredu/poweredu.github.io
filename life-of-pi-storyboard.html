A. Storyboard: Life of Filipinos Abroad

Event 1: Departure - A Filipino family is shown at the airport, bidding farewell to their loved ones as they prepare to leave the Philippines for work abroad. There are tears, but also a sense of hope for better opportunities.

Event 2: Arrival - The family arrives in a foreign country and is shown settling into their new home. They encounter language barriers and cultural differences, but they are determined to adapt and make a new life for themselves.

Event 3: Work Challenges - The family members are shown facing challenges at their respective jobs abroad. They work long hours, face discrimination, and struggle with homesickness. Despite these challenges, they persevere to support their families back in the Philippines.

Event 4: Community Support - The family finds solace in the Filipino community abroad. They attend gatherings, celebrate Filipino traditions, and support each other through the ups and downs of living in a foreign land. They form bonds with fellow Filipinos who become like family.

Event 5: Remittances - The family is shown sending remittances back home to support their families in the Philippines. They make sacrifices and send money to pay for education, healthcare, and other needs of their loved ones.

Event 6: Homesickness - The family experiences moments of homesickness, missing their loved ones, and the familiar surroundings of their home country. They reminisce about their life in the Philippines and yearn for the comfort of home.

Event 7: Achievements - Despite the challenges, the family members are shown achieving their goals abroad. They may have acquired new skills, gained promotions, or established successful businesses. Their hard work and determination pay off.

Event 8: Homecoming - The family is shown returning to the Philippines after a period of time abroad. They are greeted with warmth and joy by their loved ones, who are proud of their achievements and grateful for their sacrifices. The family is reunited, and they share their experiences of living abroad.

B. Author for a Day:

As an author for a day, I would write a fictional story about a young Filipino who goes abroad to pursue their dreams. The protagonist faces challenges, such as language barriers, cultural differences, and homesickness, but with determination and perseverance, they overcome these obstacles and achieve their goals. Along the way, they make new friends, find support in the Filipino community abroad, and send remittances to their family in the Philippines. Ultimately, the protagonist returns home as a successful individual, proud of their achievements, and grateful for the lessons learned during their time abroad. The story would highlight the resilience, perseverance, and achievements of Filipinos living and working abroad, showcasing their contributions to their families and communities.

Life of Pi book cover

MENU

What is the exposition, climax, and resolution in "Life of Pi?"

The exposition of Life of Pi includes Pi telling the story of his childhood growing up at the zoo and in the Indian school system. There might not be a definite climax, since, during his epic struggle for survival onboard the lifeboat, Pi has so many bizarre and intense experiences, but one potential climax is the scene where Pi loses his eyesight and experiences a dark moment where he thinks all hope is lost. After Pi reaches land, he recounts his story to the Japanese men, and this is the resolution.

Exposition refers to the beginning of a story, where the author establishes a setting, introduces the characters, and provides a foundation for all of the coming action and conflict. We get to know the characters a little bit, and are given pertinent background information that will help us to understand their reactions in the face of the succeeding plotline. Given that definition, the exposition in "Life of Pi" is quite a long one, about 1/3 of the entire book. It is when Pi and his family are in India. We learn about Pi, his life, his family, and then, are given a lot of very important information on zoology and religions, that play a crucial role in the coming story, and in the ending of the tale. We learn all about animals, boundaries, training, zoos, respect and safety--all of these things play a role in the lifeboat. We learn of Pi's devotion to three different religions, and how he felt that each of them had really great stories, so loved all of them. That will tie in directly to the ending.
