In the early days, primitive man showed how many animals he owned by placing an equal number of stones in a pile, or sticks in a row. Truly our number system underwent the process of development for hundreds of centuries. 2. why do you think he needed names or words to tell "how many"?​


Answer:

It is likely that primitive man needed names or words for numbers in order to communicate effectively with others about quantities. Without a system of symbols or words to represent numbers, it would have been difficult for people to accurately convey information about the quantities of objects they had, such as the number of animals they owned or the amount of resources they had available. By developing a system of symbols or words to represent numbers, people were able to communicate more effectively with one another and make more accurate decisions about how to allocate resources and manage their possessions.
