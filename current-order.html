26. what is produced by the electric current in order to overcome resistance​


What is produced by the electric current in order to overcome resistance?

When an electric current flows through a material, it encounters resistance, which is the property of the material that opposes the flow of electric charge. To overcome this resistance, the electric current produces heat, light, and other forms of energy. This process is described by Joule's Law, which states that the heat produced by an electric current is proportional to the square of the current and the resistance of the material through which the current is flowing. In other words, the greater the resistance of the material, the more heat will be produced by the electric current in order to overcome that resistance. This is why electrical appliances, such as heaters and lamps, produce heat and light respectively, as they convert the electrical energy into thermal energy and light energy to overcome the resistance of the material and perform their intended function.
