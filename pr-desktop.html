7. ∆PQR where PQ=9,QR=6 and PR=5 Solutions: To find m∠P (QR)²=(PR)²+(PQ)²-2(PR)(PQ) (cosP)


✏️LAW OF COSINES==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] ∆PQR where PQ=9,QR=6 and PR=5

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] To find m∠P

[tex] \small \sf (QR)^2 = (PR)^2 + (PQ)^2 - 2(PR)(PQ)(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf 6^2 = 5^2 + 9^2 - 2(5)(9)(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf 36 = 25 + 81 - 90(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: P = \frac{25 + 81 - 36}{90} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: P = \frac{70}{90} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf m \angle P = cos^{-1} \Big( \frac{70}{90} \Big) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf m \angle P = 38.94 \degree \\ [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{m \angle P = 38.94 \sf \degree}}} [/tex]

30. Q is the midpoint of PR and PR = 38 cm, how long is QR?​


Answer:

QR = 19

Step-by-step explanation:

PR = 38
Imagine a line with endpoints P and R. If you didn't understand what I mean, Imagine a stick with a sticker P at one end and a sticker R at another end.
Since Q is in the midpoint, it means that you are going to place the sticker Q in the middle of the stick.

Now, if the whole stick measure 38cm, that means from sticker P to sticker R is 38, and Q is in the middle. It means that Q is dividing the length from P to R. So the distance from P to Q is and Q to R is 19.
