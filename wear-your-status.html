Further explanation:Unbiased Language

Sometimes our language displays our prejudices. While we may be able to convince our listeners that we are not being disrespectful when speaking, it is much more difficult to do so when writing.

Use language that is straightforward, objective, and devoid of stereotypes to prevent the reader from becoming confused or unnecessarily enraged. When discussing issues of gender, ethnicity, race, sexual orientation, or individuals with disabilities, refrain from using generalizations.

Gender

If a term ends in -man or -woman, see if it accurately describes the person being described. When gender is not crucial to the notion you are trying to convey, choose more gender-neutral alternatives. Use firefighter instead of fireman, a chair instead of chairman, or a salesperson instead of a salesman, as examples.

Ethnicity and race

If a person's race or ethnicity is mentioned in the text, it must be pertinent to the story being told. Deciding the right phrase might be challenging because ethnic and racial labels frequently shift.

Disability

When adjectives are used as collective nouns, labels are frequently produced. Use the terms "people with impairments" and "people with schizophrenia" instead of the terms “the disabled” and "the schizophrenics."

The APA Manual of Style also advises using neutral language when referring to people with impairments, such as "someone with AIDS" rather than "AIDS victim" or "someone with emphysema" rather than "emphysema sufferer" (76).

Sexual Orientation

The labels gay for men and lesbian for women are replacing the term "homosexual" to designate a man or woman, according to Random House's Sensitive Language. Lesbians, homosexual men, bisexual men, or women have favored terminology since they clearly convey the concept.

When discussing any specific group of individuals, it's crucial to be sympathetic, truthful, and objective.

Arguments:

However, many schools have programs to help families obtain uniforms at a low cost or for free. Additionally, over time, uniforms can save money for families who would otherwise have to purchase expensive clothing for school.

c. Some may argue that school uniforms do not address the root causes of student behavior problems and that other solutions should be explored.
