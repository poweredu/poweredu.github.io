1. what is Formalist Criticism? and Deconstructionist Criticisms​


New Criticism was a formalist movement in literary theory that dominated American literary criticism in the middle decades of the 20th century. It emphasized close reading, particularly of poetry, to discover how a work of literature functioned as a self-contained, self-referential aesthetic object.

Deconstructive criticism follows the belief that objects have meaning because that it was it has been defined as through language. Deconstruction uses the concept of binaries in which one object has been given a sort of privilege, the better appeal i.e. good/bad, love/hate, white/black, and male/female.


2. Deffrenetiate formalist Criticism from Deconstructionist Criticism​


As nouns the difference between formalism and deconstruction

is that formalism is strict adherence to a given form of conduct, practice etc while deconstruction is a philosophical theory of textual criticism; a form of critical analysis.

10. Based on the poem above, why is the word "beloved" associated with the words 'tamed', 'wild' and 'love'? Analyze using the deconstructionist approach in literary criticism.​


Answer:

The deconstructionist approach in literary criticism suggests that meaning is not fixed and can be deconstructed or analyzed in multiple ways. In the poem above, the word "beloved" is associated with the words 'tamed', 'wild', and 'love' because of the multiple meanings and interpretations of these words.

The word "beloved" can be interpreted as a term of endearment or affection, suggesting a deep emotional connection between two individuals. The word "tamed" can be interpreted as the act of controlling or domesticating something or someone, suggesting a sense of ownership or possession. The word "wild" can be interpreted as untamed, free, and unrestrained, suggesting a sense of freedom or liberation. Finally, the word "love" can be interpreted as a complex emotion that can be both positive and negative, suggesting a sense of passion, desire, and attachment.

Therefore, the association between these words suggests a complex and nuanced relationship between the speaker and their beloved, one that involves both love and control, freedom and restraint. The deconstructionist approach highlights the multiple meanings and interpretations of words and how they can be used to create complex and layered meanings in poetry.
