5. how is a mercator projection made​


Answer:

In 1537, he proposed constructing a nautical atlas composed of several large-scale sheets in the cylindrical equidistant projection as a way to minimize distortion of directions. If these sheets were brought to the same scale and assembled, they would approximate the Mercator projection.


6. Explain the use of MERCATOR Chart in calculating and measuring distance accurately between two points.​


Answer:

Because a chart is a scaled representation of the real world, it can be used to measure distances between objects. This is useful, among other things, for estimating sailing or motoring time between points. On a Mercator chart, the latitude scale on the sides of the chart serves as the distance scale.

19. describe how the universal transverse mercator grid system references the location of features found on the earth's surface​


The Universal Transverse Mercator (UTM) is a map projection system for assigning coordinates to locations on the surface of the Earth. Like the traditional method of latitude and longitude, it is a horizontal position representation, which means it ignores altitude and treats the earth as a perfect ellipsoid. However, it differs from global latitude/longitude in that it divides earth into 60 zones and projects each to the plane as a basis for its coordinates. Specifying a location means specifying the zone and the x, y coordinate in that plane. The projection from spheroid to a UTM zone is some parameterization of the transverse Mercator projection. The parameters vary by nation or region or mapping system.

22. Ano ang kahulugan ng mercator​


Answer:

Ang Proyekto ng Mercator (/ mərˈkeɪtər /) ay isang proyekto na may cylindrical na mapa na ipinakita ng Flemish geographer at kartographer na si Gerardus Mercator noong 1569. Ito ang naging pamantayang projection ng mapa para sa pag-navigate sapagkat natatangi ito sa kumakatawan sa hilaga ng pataas at timog na pababa saan man habang pinapanatili ang mga lokal na direksyon at mga hugis. Sa gayon ang mapa ay umaayon. Bilang isang epekto, pinalalaki ng Proyekto ng Mercator ang laki ng mga bagay na malayo sa equator. Ang inflation na ito ay napakaliit malapit sa ekwador ngunit nagpapabilis sa pagtaas ng latitude upang maging walang katapusan sa mga poste. Kaya, halimbawa, ang mga landmass tulad ng Greenland at Antarctica ay lilitaw na mas malaki kaysa sa aktwal na kamag-anak ng mga landmass na malapit sa ekwador, tulad ng Central Africa.

26. ano ang pagkatulad ng Mercator Projection at Conical Projection.​


Answer:

In Mercator projections, both latitude and longitude lines are projected as parallel onto a map. In reality, only latitude lines are parallel, so this causes some distortion on a corresponding map. In conic projections, regions of Earth’s surface are projected onto a surrounding cone, and the image generated is flattened to create a map.

Meridians (lines of longitude):

Starting from 10°E and going up to 90°E, with intervals of 10 degrees

Parallels (lines of latitude):

Starting from 10°N and going up to 90°N, with intervals of 10 degrees

Starting from 10°S and going up to 90°S, with intervals of 10 degrees
