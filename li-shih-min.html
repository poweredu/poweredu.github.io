9. ilang months bago manganak ang shih tzu


Tumatagal nang 53 hanggang 68 araw o halos dalawang buwan ang pagbubuntis ng shih tzu at kalimitang nanganganak ito sa ika-63 araw.

Hindi lang para sa mga shih tzu ang naturang tagal ng pagbubuntis dahil ganito rin ang sitwasyon para sa iba pang uri ng aso.

Sa unang linggo ng pagbubuntis, normal pa ang kilos ng shih tzu. Sa ikalawang linggo, unti-unti nang mabubuo ang embriyon (embryo). Pagtuntong ng ikatlong linggo, nagkakaporma na ang fetus na tinatayang isang sentimetro lang ang laki. Sa ganitong panahon, tila mas gutom na ang aso.

Sa ikaapat na linggo, maaari nang makapa ang fetus kaya maaari nang ipatingin sa beterinaryo ang shih tzu para sa pagsusuri. Ito ang pinakamahalagang yugto ng pagbubuntis ng shih tzu dahil bulnerable ang mga fetus. Sa ganitong panahon din bulnerableng malaglagan ng sanggol (miscarriage) ang aso.

Bunsod ng posibilidad ng pagkalaglag ng sanggol, dapat nang bantayang maigi ang shih tzu lalo na sa mga pagkilos nito. Sa ikalimang linggo, lalaki nang lalo ng tiyan ng aso at mas magiging gutumin na. Sa panahong ito, maaari na ring malaman ang kasarian ng mga fetus.

Sa ikaanim na linggo, lalo pang tataba ang shih tzu at kapansin-pansing mangingitim ang utong nito. Sa ikapitong linggo ng pagbubuntis, posibleng malagasan ng balahibo ang aso at walang dapat ipag-alala rito. Kailangang bantayan ang aso sa ikawalong linggo dahil posibleng manganak ito nang wala sa oras (premature).

Sa huling linggo ng pagbubuntis, senyales na malapit nang manganak ang shih tzu kapag mabagal nang kumilos, tahimik, at walang ganang kumain. Bantayan nang maigi ang aso dahil nalalapit na ang paglabas ng kaniyang mga tuta.

10. Ano ang kahulugan ng shih Huang to?



Ang Shih Huang-Ti-ay ang unang ruler upang mapag-isa ang lahat ng China. Sinimulan niya ang out bilang hari ng Ch'in, ang pinaka-militaristic ng 'naglalabanan Unidos'. Sa 221 BC ang kanyang mga hukbo annexed ang anim na mga estado na rivaled Ch'in, at siya ipinapalagay ang pamagat ng kauna-unahang emperador (Shih Huang-Ti) ng isang bagong dinastya - ang Ch'in. Ang emperador pinag Tsina na may malupit lupit at vowed na ang lahat ng Confucian doktrina, na magtakda ng mga limitasyon sa kapangyarihan ng pinuno, ay dapat na mabubura. Inutusan niya ang kanyang kanselor na magsunog ng lahat ng pampanitikan classics ng nakaraan. Shih Huang-Ti itinatag ng isang sentralisadong pangangasiwa at itinayo ng isang network ng mga kalsada at kanal. Siya ay nakipaglaban sa mga bayan kapatagan mula sa hilagang disyerto, at siya ay nagsimulang na napakalawak ng trabaho, ang Great Wall ng Tsina, upang magtakda ng mga limitasyon sa kanilang mga incursions

23. One of the classics of Confucianism that is a collection of 300 poems and songs from the early Chou Dynasty. Select one: a. Ch'un Ching b. Shu Ching c. I Ching d. Shih Ching e. Li Ching


Answer: d. Shih Ching

Shih Ching (translated as "The Book of Songs" or "Classic of Poetry") is one of the classics of Confucianism that is a collection of 300 poems and songs from the early Chou Dynasty.

Sometimes, people call “Shijing Poems”, or “Book of Odes Confucius”.

Experts usually include this as a reference for their old and new Chinese poem analysis, research and studies.

**********

It is the most primitive Chinese repository of verses and it contains 300+ (or 307 to be exact) poems of folk songs and also a lot of court origins.

Court poems are more on ceremonial in its character that are designed to be sung usually at sacrifices.

However, the sacrifices are to accompany the dances, celebrations, and feasts in honour of dynastic ancestors or to adorn occasions like banquets, chases, and archeries.

Well, tradition says that it was in fact edited into its present form by Confucius himself.

They say that the book was actually assembled before, during, and even after Confucius death.

It includes fragments of works as old as Shang Dynasty dated 1766 - 1154 BCE as well as contemporary poems from the Chou feudal states. These were written or spoken by aristocratic court figures and plain folks in society.

If not the majority of the poems as oral folk art, it is clear that from the artistry of the written language, people converted Chou folk songs and court verses into poetry in written Chinese characters.

The characters used to render unpretentious direct lyrical utterances of illiterate peasants and folks that often honour them with such carefully chosen vocabulary. Thus, the heart and the soul of the folk art actually remained present however, literary subtleties were introduced.

It is apparent that the scribes who created Shih Ching were creative writers like poets and of course, not some techie tape recorders.

Hence, they clearly chose those best of what existed that they honoured it with their own style and art.

 

While folk songs are love and romantic lyrics of various kinds, complaints, satires, elegies, and even georgics, majority of the poems were composed in early Chou Dynasty also known as the pre-Confucian period.

Shih Ching predates Confucius by 3 centuries but still every so often being credited with arranging it into its current form since around 520 BC.

Hundreds of verses of poetry illustrated the proper conduct of sovereign and general rules for inculcation of propriety and even righteousness. It is believe that Confucius probably didn’t do much alteration on the original text; his agenda in it ensured that the book could survive. As he described it as “by the Odes that the mind is aroused.”

Stanzas are replete with admonitions and reproaches to the rulers overzealous for material things, power, and glory.

Therefore, the central moral lesson of this collection of poems is that a leader should rule with compassion and moderation.
