Color is often useful, but should not be relied upon. Different minerals may be the same color. Real gold, as seen in figure 2, is very similar in color to the pyrite in figure 1.

Additionally, Some minerals come in many different colors. Quartz, for example, may be clear, white, gray, brown, yellow, pink, red, or orange. 3. Physical properties of color


Answer:

Hue: Hue is the name of a pure color, such as red, blue, or yellow.

Value: Value is the lightness or darkness of a hue (color). The value of a hue can be changed by adding black or white.

Intensity: Intensity is the brightness or dullness of a hue (color). Pure hues are high-intensity colors.

15. If two minerals have the same color and size, what physical property will you use to differentiate them? Explain why you used this physical property.​


Answer:

other physical properties aside from size and color for mineral classification: Streak, Luster, Fracture, Cleavage, Hardness, Crystalline Structure and Specific Gravity.

Explanation:

Streak - color of a mineral powder.

Luster - the way a mineral reflects light.

Hardness - Ability of mineral to resist being scratched.

Fracture - minerals break unevenly or irregularly.

Cleavage - tendency of material to break evenly.

Crystalline Structure - some minerals tend to create or form crystals.
