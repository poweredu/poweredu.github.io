Bring in outdoor plants and other objects that could be blown away by heavy winds. Ensure that larger objects — bicycles, laundry poles, etc. — are secure. Close exterior shutters and, if you don't have them, make sure to close your windows and avoid sleeping near glass

-Remain calm and be alert.

-Stay indoors.

-Monitor the weather updates.

-When local authorities advise the family to evacuate, do so. ...

-Avoid remaining in coastal areas that are prone to floods or storm surge.

What to do after the typhoon

Check your home and property for damage.

Keep clear of damaged buildings, powerlines and trees.

Be aware of road hazards such as floodwater, debris and damaged roads or bridges.
