9. The combined average weight of an okapi and a llama is 450 kilograms. The average weight of 3 llamas is 190 kilograms more than the average weight of one okapi.


Linear equations

Linear equations can be solved by several methods. The methods are graph method, substitution, elimination, and combination (substitution and elimination). By completing the linear equation it can be determined that the average weight of the Okapi is 290 kg and the Ilamas is 160 kg

Further explanation

One variable linear equation is an equation that has a variable and the exponent number is one.

Can be stated in the form:

ax = b  

or

ax + b = c, where a, b, and c are constants, x is a variable

Whereas the two-variable linear equation is a linear equation that has 2 variables and the exponent is one

Can be stated in the form:

ax + by = c  

x, y = variable

The solution to a Two-Variable Linear Equation System can be done in several ways

1. Graph Method

By determining the coordinates of the intersection of the graph of the two lines from the linear equation

a. if the two lines intersect at one point, then the system of equations has a solution

b. if the two lines are parallel, then the system of equations has no solution.

c. if the two lines coincide, then the system of equations has an infinite number of solutions.

2. Substitution Method

Method by substituting the variables.

Substitute one equation to another so that the value of one variable is obtained.

3. Elimination Method

Method by eliminating one of the variables.

4. Elimination-Substitution (Combined) Method

This method combines elimination and substitution methods

Let's take a look at the two equations given

The combined average weight of an okapi(O) and a llamas(I) is 450 kilograms.

⇒O + I = 450

⇒O = 450 - I (eq 1)

3 llamas is 190 kilograms more than the average weight of one Okapi.

⇒3I = 190 + O (eq 2)

by substitution :eq 1 to eq 2

3I = 190 + 450 - I

4I = 640

I = 160 kg

O = 450 - 160

O =290 kg

11. The combined average weight of an okapi and a llama is 450 kilograms. The average weight of 3 llamas is 190 kilograms more than the average weight of one okapi. On average, how much does an okapi weigh, and how much does a llama weigh?


Answer:

Problem Solving

An okapi weigh 270 kgs while a llama weigh 180 kgs.

Step-by-step explanation:

To solve for the weight of both an okapi and llama, allow x to be the weight of an okapi and y be the weight of a llama thus, x + y = 450 kgs since the problem states that the combined average weight of an okapi and a llama is 450 kgs. Given also that 3 llamas is 190 kgs more than the average weight of an okapi then, 3y = 190 + x. Using 3y = 190 + x find the value of x such that x = 3y - 190. Replace x in x + y = 450kgs using x = 3y - 190 then, 3y - 190 + y = 450. Combine the similar terms: 3y + y = 450 + 190. 4y = 640. Divide both sides of the equation by 4 thus, y = 180. Therefore, the weight of a llama is 180 kgs. If a llama weigh 180 kgs then the weigh of an okapi will be 450 kgs - 180 kgs which is equal to 270 kgs.

Llamas spit as a show of dominance over other llamas. Spitting at a lower-ranked juvenile llama in the herd is also a form of hierarchical discipline. It is especially common when llamas are eating and want to keep others away from their food. A female llama can spit to show a male she's not interested in his mating advances, as well.
