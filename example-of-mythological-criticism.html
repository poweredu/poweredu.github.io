4.Ahura Mazda – king of the gods. His name means “Lord of Wisdom”. He was the supreme creator, maker of heaven, earth, animals, humans, and the sacred elements of air, water, earth, and fire which allowed creation to function. After Zoroastrianism's development, he became the only god of the new faith; later known as Ormuzd and Hormuzd.5.Ahurani – wife or daughter of Ahura Mazda, goddess of waters, health, healing energies, wealth, and personal growth/development. Her name means “Belonging to Ahura” and, as ahuranis, is also used in reference to divine spirits (ahuras) who fight against evil forces and protect humanity.

6.Ahuras – divine spirits of light, emanations of Ahura Mazda, protectors and defenders of humanity; also known as ahuranis. Their enemies are the daevas, evil spirits of darkness.

7.Airyaman – god of friendship, healing, reconciliation.

8.Airyaneum Vaejah – also known as Eranvej, the center of the world; the ancient Iranian homeland first ruled by the just king Yima who was favored by the gods before he fell from grace. Outside of mythology, referenced as Ariana/Iran, “land of the Aryans”. It was the first of the seven regions created by Ahura Mazda.9.Aka Manah – daeva (evil spirit) of sensuality and desire; tempter of the prophet Zoroaster who tried to seduce him away from devotion to Ahura Mazda.

10.Al (Hal) – nocturnal predator demons who fed on newborns and could harm or kill women in late-stage pregnancies. They were depicted as old women with long, stringy hair, sharp teeth, and talons. They were a specific type of a larger group of nocturnal predator demons known as Umm Naush.

12. Give atleast 20 examples of gods in greek mythology (includes their power and where they live)​


Answer:

1. Zeus=

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: Olympian king and leader of the Olympic gods, god of lightning, thunder, and the heavens.

Power: He controlled weather, sky, thunder, lightning and storms. He can also use his power to transform to seduce mortal women, other goddesses, and nymphs.

2. Hera

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= wife of Zeus, Olympian queen of Olympus, women, and marriage.

Power: She had the ability to bless and curse marriages. She also called the protector of the Amazons, the group of warrior women.

3. Poseidon

Domain/Place: Ocean, Sea or Mount Olympus

Title: Olympian god of the sea, earth shaker, horses tamer

Power: He could cause violent earthquakes, volcano eruptions, hurricanes, violent big waves and more.

4. Hades=

Domain/Place: the Underworld

Title: god of the dead, king of the underworld and riches

Power: He controlled death and lives. He also possessed the cap of invisibility, which he used to kidnap Persephone, daughter of Demeter.

5. Ares

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus, Thrace, Macedonia, Thebes, Sparta, and Mani

Title: Olympian god of war, courage, and brutal warfare

Power= He had great power when it comes to war due to his powerful and smart strategies.

6. Apollo

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus, Delos (his sacred island)

Title= Twin brother of Artemis, Olympian god of the sun and light, god of music, truth and prophecy.

Power: He controlled the sun which can burn the earth, oceans, and sky if he wanted to. He gives oracles to all his followers.

7. Artemis

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus

Title= twin sister of Apollo, virgin goddess of the moon, hunt, wild animals, wilderness, and virginity

Power= She was strong and great with bow and arrows that no one can compare to her. Although she could heal mortals, she could also bring terrible diseases.

8. Athena=

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: goddess of wisdom and justice, handicrafts, and the strategic side of war

Power: She is known to be an independent, intelligent, and ruthless goddess in battles. She also had a shield called an aegis that depicted the head of Medusa, representing its great protective power.

9. Aphrodite

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of love and beauty, pleasure and procreation

Power: She was beautiful, and possessed a magic girdle called a cestus, that had the power of inspiring love.

10. Demeter

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of the harvest and agriculture

Power= She had power over all living things around the earth, nature, and seasons, especially crops and harvest. She also maintained the circle of life on earth.

11. Hephaestus

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: god of blacksmiths, craftsman, metals, fire and volcanoes

Power: He had strong power that can blow up anything, and he also created countless inventions.

12. Hermes

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: god of commerce, travel, boundaries, and messenger of the gods.

Power: He moves so fast, could deliver countless punches in seconds. He was the fastest among Greek gods. He used his power to charm the gods and heal mortals, and He is the guide to the Underworld and protector of thieves, shepherds, orators, road travelers, and merchants.

13. Dionysus

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= Olympian god of wine, pleasure, and madness

Power= He could turn a rock into water and water into wine with his thyrsos/magic staff or wand.

14. Persephone

Domain/Place: Earth and Underworld (6 months each place)

Title= goddess of the spring and vegetation, kidnapped queen of the underworld.

14. Nike

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of victory

Power: She can control victory in any battles and assumes the role of the divine charioteer, often accompanying Athena and Ares.

15. Hestia

Domain/Place: Not specified. She left Mount Olympus for a quieter place and gave her place to Dionysus as one of the 12 Olympians.

Title: virgin goddess of hearth, home and chastity

Power: She symbolized harmony in the family and city and the hearth fires dedicated to her burn for both day and night.

16. Iris

Place: Not specified.

Title: goddess of the rainbow and also a messenger for the gods to humanity.

Power: She can go to the Underworld to collect water from River Styx, for the swearing of solemn oaths.

17. Eris

Place: Not specified.

Title: goddess of chaos, discord, and strife.

Power: She has absolute authority over chaos, strife, and discord.

18. Nemesis

Place: Not Specified.

Title: goddess of balance, retribution, and vengeance

Power: She had absolute authority over luck, and also can create horrible curses as punishments.

19. Morpheus

Place: Not Specified.

Title: god of dream and sleep

Power: He had divine authority and control over dreams and can make people fall asleep and shape peoples' dreams.

20. Thanatos

Place: the Underworld

Title: god of death (peaceful)

Power: As the god of peaceful death, he is able to keep track of all souls in the Underworld. He keeps the balance between the living and the dead and stops the world from falling into chaos.

PHILIPPINE MYTHOLOGY ARE GOOD GENRE OF LITERATURE TO INSPIRE CREATIVITY AMONG CHILDREN

In the end, understanding mythology helps us put our reality, our literature, and our personal views into context. It is important to consider the relevance of these myths; even a basic degree of research will be helpful.Mythology influenced art in the same way that art influenced mythology. The stories, people, and symbols of mythology provide artists with a wide reservoir of symbolic material due to their infinite reinterpretation possibilities.

USES OF MYTHOLOGIESMyths were frequently produced to explain natural events and frequently featured gods and mythical beings.Myths are tales that are told to instruct people over something essential and valuable. They were frequently used to teach people about occurrences that they could not always comprehend, such as disease and death, or earthquakes and floods. Legends are similar to myths, yet they are not the same.Language and narrative are integral to the human experience.Painting, cinema, and other visual arts may represent the intricacies of the human experience for some, but there is something about literature and language that uniquely capture thinking and feeling. Myths, for example, are instances of the intangible attempting to make sense of the tangible.

Answer:

Zeus was the god of the sky and ruler of the Olympian gods. He overthrew his father, Cronus, and then drew lots with his brothers Poseidon and Hades, in order to decide who would succeed their father on the throne.

Poseidon is the god of the sea and protector of all aquatic features. Brother of Zeus and Hades, after the overthrow of their father, Cronus, he drew lots with them to share the universe. He ended up becoming lord of the sea. He was widely worshipped by seamen.

Hermes was the Greek god of commerce, son of Zeus and Maia. Quick acting and cunning, he was able to move swiftly between the world of man and the world of gods, acting as a messenger of the gods and the link between mortals and the Olympians.

Hades was the brother of Zeus and Poseidon. After the overthrow of their father, Cronus, he drew lots with them to share the universe. He drew poorly, which resulted in becoming lord of the underworld and ruler of the dead. Nevertheless, he was not considered to be death itself, as this was a different god, called Thanatos.

Apollo was the son of Zeus and Leto, twin brother of Artemis. He was the god of music, and he is often depicted playing a golden lyre. He was also known as the Archer, far shooting with a silver bow; the god of healing, giving the science of medicine to man; the god of light; and the god of truth.
