Answer:

A detailed topo map and a compass are still the primary tools for navigation in the wilderness. A compass helps you orient the map, identify land features and locate your position. You may hike for days on a trail and never even use the compass.

Why is compass and map important?

A compass helps you to: Know which direction you are travelling in – this is called your heading. Align or orientate your map with your surroundings – setting the map. Work out which direction an object or destination is from you – its bearing.

16. Give the exact definition of a map and a compass rose.


A map is a representation of a real place. It helps you find which places are located. A compass rose tells directions on a map. East(E), West(W), North(N), and South(S) are the primary directions also called Cardinal Directions. East and West are opposite directions. North and South are opposite directions. The four secondary directions are Northeast, Northwest, Southeast, and Southwest. Secondary direction is also called as intermidiate directions wherein it helps locate places between the cardinal directions.
