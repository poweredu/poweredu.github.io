6. how is data-driven research different from hypothesis-driven research?​


Answer:

With the availability of large datasets and advanced correlation/statistical methods, will we still need to rely on hypotheses in scientific inquiry? Traditionalists argue that in purely data-driven methods, one may not know where to look for those interesting findings if no hypotheses were formed beforehand. Big data advocates, on the other hand, argue that with no prior beliefs, one is not constrained by established ways of thinking or doing, opening the possibilities of breakthrough insights where nobody had looked before. To explore these questions, we examine several fields and describe an historical progression in knowledge production. We believe that in these contexts large scale data collection and analysis represent the next step - going beyond the capabilities of todays simulation models with an empirical data-collection driven approach.

ANTI- THESIS SYNTHESIS​


Answer:

Thesis: I will try my best to offer facts to back up my opinions in such a way that is not condescending, but rather informative and educating. In such sense, I do not disrespect them nor do I insult them.

Anti: I will bombard them with facts so that they will know that they their opinion or argument is wrong. Of course, I will respect their opinion but still if they are wrong they must be held accountable for choosing the wrong belief.

Synthesis: I will use facts to let them know they are wrong, but I will not rub it in their face that they are wrong. Instead, I will explain why their opinion is not correct and will let them understand these facts I have.
