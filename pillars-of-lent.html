1. What are the pillars of Lent?Your answear​


Answer:

Ash Wednesday is fast approaching, and with it the very Catholic anxiety about what we are “giving up for Lent.” Many of us focus so much on what we give up – a practice not made mandatory by the Church – that we neglect the three pillars of Lent; prayer, fasting, and almsgiving.

Prayer

This one should be the easiest of the three. As Christians we are supposed to be praying anyway, so why make it a requirement? Because the sad truth is that many Christians do not pray, at least not with any regularity.

I myself was, for a very long time, a non-praying Christian. Sure I prayed when I went to church and the priest (or preacher, depending on how far back I’m going) said “let us pray,” and whenever I sped past a hidden highway patrolman. But just to give praise to the God who created me, or to adore His son who died for me? No I was too busy for that. It wasn’t like I thought of God and then just brushed Him aside, had I thought of Him I would’ve prayer, it’s just that I was so caught up in living my life that God became more of an afterthought than a savior. He became my divine security blanket, kept in the closet and only brought out when I was scared, or needed a favor.

5. How can we integrate the three pillars of lent in dealing with healthy stress management?


Answer:

Stress is already a part of our daily lives. At work, at school and even at home, we experience stress. If stress is left unmanaged, it can lead to other mental health problems such as anxiety and depression. Knowing so, it is important that each individual will engage oneself in healthy stress management.

As christian, we can always integrate the three pillars of lent in dealing with healthy stress management. How? In times, of stress, the purposive act of cooling off during lenten season which may be through pulling yourself out from common activities that are causing your busy schedule and unhealthy lifestyle is very helpful. The three pillars of lent includes giving, fasting and praying.

As you give alms to the needy, you also give you yourself a reward. This reward is boosting your self-worth and your self-esteem through the feeling of happiness and gratefulness for having more than enough and being blessed. This, in turn will contribute to your psychological well-being. The more grateful and happy you are, the more that you relieve the stress and unnecessary tensions in your mind and body.

Fasting doesn't necessarily mean that you will not eat or you are going to starve yourself. It means one full meal and little food intake during the rest of the days. It also means social or digital detoxification so you will focus your attention in worshiping God and repentance. The one full meal means a healthy food diet within a day  that helps your body sustain the needed energy throughout. Social and digital detoxification allows you to distant yourself from stressful stimulus. Thus, making you stress-free and promotes the feeling of contentment,

When you pray, you talk to the higher being. It allows you to pour out all your pain, your hopes, your fear in life that you cannot share with anyone else. This will give you the feeling of relief.  As such, it will allow the stresses in your body to flow outward. Also, the feeling of having someone up there who listens to you and is ready to help you will give you the sense of security. This act means much more than any other stress therapy. It is a spiritual connection that heals your totality.

6. short description about "Fasting and Abstinence" Pillar of Lent. *


Answer:

Lent is a 40 day season of prayer, fasting, and almsgiving that begins on Ash Wednesday and ends at sundown on Holy Thursday. It's a period of preparation to celebrate the Lord's Resurrection at Easter. During Lent, we seek the Lord in prayer by reading Sacred Scripture; we serve by giving alms; and we practice self-control through fasting. We are called not only to abstain from luxuries during Lent, but to a true inner conversion of heart as we seek to follow Christ's will more faithfully. We recall the waters of baptism in which we were also baptized into Christ's death, died to sin and evil, and began new life in Christ.

11. ano ang employment pillar, workers right pillar, social protection pillar​


ISYU NG PAGGAWA

Mga layunin:Nasusuri ang apat na haligi para sa disente at marangal na paggawaNalalaman ang mga trabaho at kaakibat na suliranin para ditoNakakamungkahi ng mga posibleng solusyon sa mga isyu ng paggawa

Departmen of Labor and Employment (DOLE) - Ang pangunahing ahensya ng gobyerno ng Pilipinas na naglalayong maglaan ng trabaho, linangin ang likas-pantao, at pangalagaan at itaguyod ang mga karapatan ng mga manggagawa.

Ayon sa ulat ng Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE, 2016) upang matiyak ang kaunlarang pang-ekonomiya ng bansa kailangang ingat ang antas ng kalagayan ng mga manggagawang Pilipino tungo sa isang disenteng paggawa (decent work), na naglalayong na magkaroon ng pantay na oportunidad ang bawat isa anumang ang kasarian para sa isang disente at marangal na paggawa.

Apat na haligi para sa isang disente at marangal na paggawa:

Employment Pillar

Tiyakin ang paglikha ng mga sustenableng trabaho, malaya at pantay na oportunidad sa paggawa, at maayos na "workplace" para sa mga maggagawa.

Worker's Right Pillar

Naglalayong palakasin at siguruhin ang paglikha ng mga "batas" para sa paggawa at matapat na pagpapatupad ng mga karapatan ng mga manggagawa.

Social Protection Pillar

Hikayatin ang mga kompanya, pamahalaan, at mga sangkot sa paggawa na lumikha ng mga mekanismo para sa "proteksyon" ng manggagawa, katanggap-tanggap na pasahod, at oportunidad.

Social Dialogue Pillar

Palakasin ang laging "bukas na pagpupulong" sa pagitan ng pamahalaan, mga manggagawa, at kompanya sa pamamagitan ng paglikha ng mga collective bargaining unit

14. What is Lent? How do we prepare spiritually for Lent?​


Answer:

Lent is a season of grace. The joy of the Risen Lord at Easter will depend on how we live out this holy season of Lent. God’s generosity has no limits, but we often fall short in giving God our whole hearts so that He can fill them with His love.

What do we prepare for during Lent?

Traditionally, Catholics spiritually prepare during Lent through prayer, fasting, and almsgiving. We can do all three (pray, fast, and give alms), two of these, or just one.

1. Lent

the period preceding Easter that in the Christian Church is devoted to fasting, abstinence, and penitence in commemoration of Christ's fasting in the wilderness. In the Western Church it runs from Ash Wednesday to Holy Saturday and so includes forty weekdays.

1. What is Lent Lent is a solemn religious observance in the Christian liturgical calendar commemorating the 40 days Jesus spent fasting in the desert, according to the Gospels of Matthew, Mark and Luke, before beginning his public ministry, during which he endured temptation by Satan.

2. Where Does the Word "Lent" Come From?It comes from the Old English word læncte, meaning “lengthening (of daylight hours)” (or, less literally, “spring” or “springtime”). Easter itself is tied to the start of springtime, as it falls on the Sunday following the first full moon after the vernal equinox.

In Western Churches, Lent begins on Ash Wednesday and ends approximately six weeks later; depending on the Christian denomination and local custom, Lent concludes either on the evening of Maundy Thursday
