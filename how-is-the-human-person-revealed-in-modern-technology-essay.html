Heidegger strongly opposes the view that technology is “a means to an end” or “a human activity.” These two approaches, which Heidegger calls, respectively, the “instrumental” and “anthropological” definitions, are indeed “correct”, but do not go deep enough; as he says, they are not yet “true.” Unquestionably, Heidegger points out, technological objects are means for ends, and are built and operated by human beings, but the essence of technology is something else entirely. 11. how does science and technology reveals nature and the human person role in it?​


Answer:

This is an interesting question. On the surface, it is probably axiomatic that technology does not reveal nature because it is not part of it. However, as I think your question implies, there is another level. Technology can tell us how things work in two ways. Technology can be the tools for analyzing nature (example: genomics). Also, technology can mimic (to an extent) natural things and thus give us ways we can study natural mechanisms and analyze them in detail. So, in these ways technology can “reveal” nature.

Then comes the really complex part of your question, “and people’s role in it”. Probably since the first caveman hit the first animal (or human competitor) over the head with a club we have come to love our tools. We have learned to believe that our tools (technologies) will save us - or get rid of our opponents. Our technologies have allowed us to extract ourselves from the risks of the natural world (examples: houses, water purification, sewage systems, etc). So, for a very long time we have pursued utilizing our technologies not to reveal our role in nature but rather to extract ourselves from it (example: cities).

However, as a species we are having an awakening. There are now so many of us, and we consume at such rates that we threaten the entire ecosystem of the planet. So, while we have tried to extract ourselves from the natural risks and while we have utilized our technologies to “control” nature, we now come to a time when we are (very slowly) awakening to the perspective that if we do not preserve the world’s natural ecosystem then it will be the end of us (and many other innocents that we take down with us).

We have had perhaps 100,000 years of believing in tools. Now we have a short time to discover perspectives that are beyond them.
