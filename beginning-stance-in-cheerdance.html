15. definition of cheerdancing ​


Answer: Cheerdancing is a performance that combines cheerleading, dance, and gymnastics elements. It involves a routine that includes jumps, tumbling skills, lifts, and tosses, combined with the shouting of cheers and yells to lead the crowd to cheer for a specific team during a game or sport. Cheerdancing is performed to motivate sports teams, entertain the audience, or for competition. It is a popular activity in schools and universities performed by professional cheerleading teams. Cheerdancing is often associated with American culture but has also gained popularity in other parts of the world.

18. in a group of 50 student,18 took cheerdance,26 took chorus,and 2 took both cheerdance and chorus.how many students in the group are not enrolled in either cheerdance or chorus?


Using the venn diagram,

Subtract 2 from the both number of students in cheerdance and chorus to get the number of the one who only took cheerdance and chorus.

18 - 2= 16 only took cheerdance
26-2 = 24 only took chorus

And 2 who took both. To identify the no. of students that are enrolled,

16+24+2 = 42 are enrolled

Then subtract 42 from 50 to identify those who aren't enrolled.

Therefore, there are 8 students in the group who aren't enrolled.
