10. name and explain two types of prewriting


Answer:

What is prewriting?

Prewriting is the "getting ready to write" stage. In composition, it is any activity that helps writers especially beginners to prepare them to write. The activities are meant to help the writers to discover what they want to know, what they know already and what they need to know. Part of prewriting is to determine a purpose, then think of a topic and lastly prepare to write.

What is the aim of prewriting?

The aim of prewriting is simply to prepare any writers for the actual writing process. More so, when prewriting is done by any writers, it helps them to find good topics, be able to narrow down topics that are too broad. From doing this, they just prepared themselves for the actual writing process, which is the main objective of the prewriting.

Below are types of Prewriting.

Freewriting - In this type of prewriting, the writer has all freedom to write anything. May it be a word, a phrase or a sentence. The words, phrases, or sentences may be guided with a specific topic or none at all. Usually the product of this is unusable. However, the product helps the writer to choose which topic to focus. Moreover, the freedom to write any idea is under a specific period of time. This means that writers may have the freedom to write everything but they do not have the luxury of time to do it since it is timed. A specific period of time is set.Looping - It is another type of prewriting which normally can be done only after doing the freewriting. What happens in looping is that, the writer gets a particular idea or concept from the ideas he or she has written during the freewriting, and then, after getting a specific idea or concept, the writer can now do freewriting again base on the idea or concept he or she has chosen.

26. prewriting necessary step example​


Answer:

During prewriting exercises, it is important to record everything that comes to mind without editing as you write. You can use the various techniques to generate a number of different ideas to choose from to formulate your topic. Brainstorming can help you find where your true interests lie and what part of a topic you might want to delve into further. Freewriting can help you generate new ideas about a topic by writing nonstop, without editing, for a set amount of time. Clustering, or concept mapping, can help you refine your thoughts and narrow the scope of a topic by making a map or diagram of different ideas you associate with a central topic.
