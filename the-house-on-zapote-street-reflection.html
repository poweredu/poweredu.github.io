"The House on Zapote Street" by Nick Joaquin

Marxist Approach:

The Marxist approach focuses on the portrayal of power connections and socioeconomic classes in the plot. In "The House on Zapote Street," the home represents the upper class's power and money, while the street depicts the lower class. As seen in the character of Doa Lupeng, the story depicts how the higher class exploits and dominates the lower class. She is depicted as a wealthy and powerful landowner who wields authority and manipulates those around her, especially the lower-class Aling Biang. The story also emphasizes women's oppression and their restricted opportunities for success within patriarchal society.

Structuralist/Formalist Approach:

The Structuralist/Formalist approach focuses on the story's literary components, such as storyline, character, place, and symbol. In "The House on Zapote Street," the house represents power, riches, and social prestige. The format of the novel, which employs flashbacks and numerous perspectives, emphasizes the complexities of the characters and their relationships. The story's setting, in the old Manila suburb of Quiapo, adds a sense of nostalgia and history to the story's themes of tradition and change.

Reader Response Approach:

The Reader Response approach focuses on the reader's interpretation and reaction to the story. Readers may react differently to the characters and their acts in "The House on Zapote Street," depending on their own experiences and values. Some readers may sympathize with Aling Biang's situation and bemoan Doa Lupeng's arrogance and abuse of authority. Others may understand and sympathize with Doa Lupeng's yearning for prestige and respect. Class and gender themes in the novel may also resonate differently with various readers, based on their own social and cultural origins.

11. Show your solution. 1. Las Piñas Engineering office installed electric posts along the stretch of Alabang-Zapote Road. They used 41 posts which were equally apart. How far apart is each post if the distance from Alabang to Zapote is 16.4 km? r, Michaela​


DIVISION: REAL-LIFE PROBLEM

Problem:

Las Piñas Engineering office installed electric posts along the stretch of Alabang-Zapote Road. They used 41 posts which were equally apart. How far apart is each post if the distance from Alabang to Zapote is 16.4 km?

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

The required data for this word problem is the distance of two posts from each other. The given information are 16.4-kilometer distance from Alabang to Zapote and there were 41 electrical posts to be installed. The operation that we will use here is division.

By dividing 16.4 by 41, we will get a quotient of 0.4 km.

If 0.4 km will be converted to meters, just remember that 1 km is equivalent to 1,000 meters. Multiply 1,000 to 0.4, the result will become 400 meters

Therefore, each post is 0.4 km or 400 m away to the next post.
