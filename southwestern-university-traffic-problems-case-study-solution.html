7. What is indicated in the traffic sign to prohibit accidents and traffic problem?


Answer:

Traffic signs provide valuable information to drivers and other road users. They represent rules that are in place to keep you safe, and help to communicate messages to drivers and pedestrians that can maintain order and reduce accidents. Neglecting them can be dangerous.

Most signs make use of pictures, rather than words, so that they are easy to understand and can be interpreted by people who speak a variety of languages. For this reason, it’s important that you know what each picture represents, and that you use them to inform your driving. Failing to do so could result in a serious accident or a fine.

1. AIR POLLUTION

CAUSE : caused by solid and liquid particles and certain gases that are suspended in the air. These particles and gases can come from car and truck exhaust, factories, dust, pollen, mold spores, volcanoes and wildfires.

EFFECT :Long-term health effects from air pollution include heart disease, lung cancer, and respiratory diseases such as emphysema. Air pollution can also cause long-term damage to people's nerves, brain, kidneys, liver, and other organs. Some scientists suspect air pollutants cause birth defects.

2, FLOODED COMMUNITY

PROBLEM : Some of these factors are shown below; Loss of lives and property: Immediate impacts of flooding include loss of human life, damage to property, destruction of crops, loss of livestock, non-functioning of infrastructure facilities and deterioration of health condition owing to waterborne diseases.

SOLUTION :

6 Ways Your Town Can Prepare for Flooding

1.Encourage residents to be prepared for a flood

2.Have an emergency plan that outlines procedures for every department and agency during a flood

3. Maintain infrastructure that presents flood risks

4.Watch water flow during rainfall, especially on hard surfaces

5.Discourage construction in flood hazard areas…

6.Develop a mitigation plan to take steps now to prepare for future floods.

3. ERODED HOUSE

CAUSE : Urbanization causes erosion as land is converted to pavement and vegetation cover is greatly reduced, increasing rates of wind erosion. Water runoff is increased, and run off often carries pollutants with it which negatively impact the surrounding land.

EFFECT : effects of erosion include increased flooding, increased sedimentation in rivers and streams, loss of soil nutrients' and soil degradation, and, in extreme cases, desertification.
