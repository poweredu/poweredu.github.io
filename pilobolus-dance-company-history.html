6. What is the history or background history of Philippines Folk Dance Tiklos​


Answer:

Tiklos

Tiklos refers to a group of peasants who agree to work for each other one day each week to clear the forest, prepare the soil for planting, or do any odd job on the farm, including building a house. At noontime the peasants gather to eat their lunch together and to rest. During this rest period Tiklos music is played with a flute accompanied by a guitar and the guimbal or tambora (drums). The peasants then dance; thus, the Tiklos has evolved.

Tiklos was introduced at the 1967 University of the Pacific Folk Dance Camp by Bernardo T. Pedere, who was the lead male dancer and dance con- sultant of the Leyte Filipiniana Folk Dance Group which in 1966 toured the United States.

DANCE HISTORY OF AMERICA

Historians commonly place the beginning of American dance in the late 1800s, when indigenous institutions and notable artists began to emerge. Most dancers could only work professionally on the popular stage until the twentieth century, which included music halls, burlesque, and vaudeville.

Dance, like all other forms of artistic expression, reflects the society in which it is performed. Dance forms in the United States are derived from a vast pool of various forms, just as the country's history contains a wide range of complicated influences. Dance in the United States has embraced and combined individual, cultural, and stylistic components in an ever-changing kaleidoscope that draws from and adds to artistic, social, religious, cultural, and even political domains. Dance can serve a variety of goals, including expression, communication, education, therapy, recreation, and entertainment, and it can be used in a variety of settings, including personal, political, social, religious, and spiritual ones. 15. history of Popular dance​


Answer:

History of Popular Dances

Waltz

The Waltz first appeared as a fashionable dance in Bohemia, Austria, and Bavaria, duringdances the late 1700s. Danced in 3/4 time, the recurring even beats send the dancers whirling around the floor with rising and lowering action and sway in the upper body. The Waltz is a smooth, flowing and elegant social dance, in which the couples use both the open and closed position.

Count: 123 123 123 123

Foxtrot

Considered the dance of Fred Astaire and Ginger Rogers, Foxtrot began in 1914 when a young dancer named Harry Fox did a version of the Turkey Trot. As one of the most popular social dances in the world, the Foxtrot is the foundation for many dances that followed.

Count: Slow-quick-quick

Tango

A dramatic dance characterized by a close hold between partners, a low center of gravity, and a stealthy cat-like step. It was born in the brothels of 19th-century Buenos Aires, where diverse ethnic groups would mingle. First shunned by the upper classes, it slowly gained acceptance after the turn of the century. Dance in 2/4 or 4/4 timing.

Count: : Slow-slow-quick-quick-slow

Rumba

Originally a Haitian and African folkloric dance of fertility, Rumba was at first rejected by the upper class due to the suggestive body and hip movements. The steps taken with a slightly bent knee cause the hips to sway in a sensual movement. Considered the dance of love.

Count: Quick-quick-slow-quick-quick-slow

Cha Cha

The Cha Cha was popularized during the 1950s as a combination of Salsa and Swing. The syncopated rhythm of Cha Cha grew out of the danzon, an older Cuban music and was adapted in America to incorporate big-band music. Like all Latin dances it emphasizes hip action and rhythm expressed throughout the body.

Count: 234&1 – 234&1

Swing

Consists of six- and eight-count rhythms usually performed with a single or triple-step action. Easily the most popular of the American dances, it grew out out of the big-band era and is the foundation of most club dances today.

Count: 1&2 3&4 56

Night Club 2-Step

Danced to contemporary romantic ballads, the simple rhythm is easy to follow with its 1 & 2 count. Best distinguished from other ballroom dances by its lack of rigid technique and allows for different style and interpretation.

Count: 1&2 3&4

West Coast Swing

California’s state dance moves in a slot and is danced inn six- and eight-count patterns. The character can range from smooth to bouncy and has a wide range of musical styles, from blues to country and pop.

Count: 12 3&4 5&6

Samba

A lively dance with roots in African rhythms danced on Brazilian plantations. Samba is still the heartbeat of the Brazilian celebration, Carnival. Characterized by rapid foot changes and bouncing hip movement, Samba was first introduced in America at the Worlds Fair in 1939 and gained mainstream popularity in the 1960s. Danced in 2/4 time.

30. origin and history of quaker oats company​


Answer:

This answer is based on www.quakeroats.com

Explanation:

1850

Ferdinand Schumacher founds German Mills American Cereal Company in Akron, OH. During the same period, John Stuart established the North Star Mills Company in Canada.

1877

Quaker Oats registered as the first trademark for a breakfast cereal. The trademark was registered with the U.S. Patent Office as "a figure of a man in 'Quaker garb.'" Both former owners, Henry Seymour and William Heston, claimed to have selected the Quaker name as a symbol of good quality and honest value.

1881

Henry Parsons Crowell buys the bankrupt Quaker Mill in Ravenna, OH, and its most important asset – the brand name Quaker.

1882

Crowell launches the first national magazine advertising program for a breakfast cereal.

1888

Seven of the largest American oat millers--including the Consolidated principals--unite as the American Cereal Company.

1890

The American Cereal Company runs a special all-Quaker Oats train from Cedar Rapids, IA to Portland, OR, and introduces the first ever "trial-size samples." 1/2 oz. sample boxes of Quaker Oats are delivered to every mailbox in Portland, OR.

1891

The Quaker brand becomes the first brand to feature a recipe on its box (Recipe for Oatmeal Bread).

1901

American Cereal changes its name to The Quaker Oats Company.

1908

Oat Cakes become the first oatmeal cookie to appear on the Quaker Oats package.

1911

Quaker acquires Mother's Oats, giving Quaker ownership of half of all milling operations east of the Rocky Mountains.

1915

The familiar round Quaker Oats package is introduced.

1922

Quaker introduces Quaker Quick Oats, one of the first convenience product.

1961

Life® Cereal is introduced in its original flavor.

1966

Quaker Instant Oatmeal is introduced.

1970

The first instant oatmeal flavor is introduced – Maple & Brown Sugar.

1981

Quaker introduces the world to Quaker Chewy granola bars.

1987

Actor Wilford Brimley is the first celebrity to work with The Quaker brand.

1997

The Quaker brand introduces the first food-specific health claim for oatmeal, which is approved by the FDA. The new heart health claim appears on Quaker Oatmeal cereals which qualify. The claim reads: "Soluble fiber from oatmeal as part of a low saturated fat, low cholesterol diet, may reduce the risk of heart disease."

2017

Quaker celebrates our 140 year anniversary.
