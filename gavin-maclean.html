4. the ratio of john's money to gavin was 4:7 at first . After John spent 1/2 of his money and Gavin had twice as much money as John . How much money did John have at first?​


Answer:

John had 8 units of money at first. To solve this problem, we can set up a proportion. Let x = how much money John had at first. Then the ratio of John's money to Gavin's was 4:7, or 4/7. We know that John spent 1/2 of his money, so 4/7 multiplied by 1/2 = 4/14. We also know that after John spent 1/2 of his money, Gavin had twice as much money as John. So John's final amount would be 4/14 of Gavin's final amount. The following proportion can be set up: 4/14 = x/7. Multiply both sides by 7 to find John's original amount: 4/14 * 7 = x. 4 * 7 = 28 and x = 28, so John had 8 units of money at first.

Answer:

Let's use the variable "J" to represent the amount of money that John had at first.

We know that the ratio of John's money to Gavin's money was 4:27 at first. So we can write:

J : 27 = 4 : 27

We can cross-multiply to find that:

27 * 4 = J * 1

J = 108

So John had 108 units of money at first.

Now we know that Johnny is left with half of his original money, which means he has 54 units of money. We also know that Gavin has 360 units of money, which is twice as much as Johnny. So we can write:

360 = 2 * 54

We can solve for Gavin's original amount of money by dividing 360 by 2, which gives us 180 units of money.

Finally, we can check that the ratio of John's original money to Gavin's original money is indeed 4:27:

J : 180 = 4 : 27

108 : 180 = 4 : 27

4/4 = 4/4

So our solution is correct, and John had 108 units of money at first.

21. £940 is divided between Gavin, Jim & Krutika so that Gavin gets twice as much as Jim, and Jim gets three times as much as Krutika. How much does Jim get?


Answer:

£282

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us represent each amount with the following variables:

g = amount of money Gavin gets

j = amount of money Jim gets

k = amount of money Krutika gets

The expression we have so far is

[tex]940 = g + j + k[/tex]

Gavin gets twice as much as Jim. Using our variables, we have

[tex]g = 2j[/tex]

Jim gets three times as much as Krutika. Using our variables, we have

[tex]j = 3k[/tex]

We can substitute the value of j to the second equation to see how Gavin and Krutika's money are related

[tex]g = 2j\\j = 3k\\\\g = 2(3k)\\g = 6k[/tex]

So, we can say that Gavin gets 6 times as much as Krutika has.

Since we expressed j and g in terms of k, we can substitute those values from the original equation.

[tex]g+k+j = 940\\g = 6k\\j = 3k\\\\6k + k + 3k = 940 \\10k = 940\\k = 94\\\\[/tex]

So Krutika has £94.

To find out how much the Gavin and Jim have, we substitute k =94 to their respective equations.

[tex]k = 94\\\\g = 6k \\g = 6(94)\\g = 564\\\\j = 3k\\j = 3(94)\\j = 282[/tex]

Jim has £282 while Gavin has £564.
