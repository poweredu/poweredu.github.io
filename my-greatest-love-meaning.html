Formalism Analysis of "Now I Know" by Jose Paulo Tolentino

A. Literary Devices

Repetition: "seven months", "seven years"

Rhyme: "fears", "years"; "wall", "weight"; "city", "responsibility"; "die", "redeem"; "goal", "soul"

Enjambment: the lines continue without pause or punctuation at the end of each line, creating a sense of flow and continuity

B. Meaning

The poem "Now I Know" expresses the struggles and challenges of growing up and facing the responsibilities of adulthood. The speaker reflects on the past seven months and seven years of their life, and how they have come to realize the heavy weight of responsibility that comes with being an adult in a tough and sometimes mad world. They have learned that many of the things they were taught about love, success, and goals were just lies, and that they now have a hollow soul. Despite this, the speaker acknowledges that they now know the reality of being an adult, and that they must face it head on, even if they don't know where to go from here.
