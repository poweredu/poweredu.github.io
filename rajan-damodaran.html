12. 10. Rajan cut out the figure as shown below. The figure is made ip of 2 identical quarter circles and 2 identical semicircles. Find the area of figure. (Take π = 3.14.)


Answer:

Area = [tex]50.24cm^{2}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Area of semi-circle:

[tex]\frac{\pi r^{2}}{2}[/tex]

diameter = 16/2 = 8

radius = 8/2 = 4

[tex]\frac{\pi 4^{2}}{2} = \frac{628}{25}cm^{2} or 25.12cm^{2}[/tex]

Area of quarter circle:

 [tex]\frac{\pi r^{2}}{4}[/tex]

radius = 16/2 = 8

[tex]\frac{\pi 8^{2}}{4} = \frac{1256}{25}cm^{2} or 50.24cm^{2}[/tex]

Total area = ((Area of quarter-circle) - (area of semi-circle))*2

Total area = (50.24 - 25.12)*2 = [tex]50.24cm^{2}[/tex]
