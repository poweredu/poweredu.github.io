6. which of the following dance step has a step pattern of step close step and counting of 1 2 3 4 5​


Answer:

The waltz

Explanation:

The dance step that has a step pattern of "step, close, step" and counting of "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" is likely to be the Waltz. The Waltz is a ballroom dance that originated in the late 18th century in Austria and Germany. It is a smooth and elegant dance that is danced in 3/4 time and is characterized by a step, close, step pattern and a slow, steady rhythm. The dance is typically counted as "1, 2, 3" with the steps taken on the first, second, and third beats of the music, respectively.

Answer:

The dance has 2 basic steps, the natural and the kinampilan. The natural step is commonly referred to as two steps forward, one step back, while the kinampilan has one step forward with the right foot and one step back with the left foot in place.
