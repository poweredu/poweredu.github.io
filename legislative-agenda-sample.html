Although cruise ships represent a small fraction of the entire shipping industry worldwide, public attention to their environmental impacts comes in part from the fact that cruise ships are highly visible and in part because of the industry’s desire to promote a positive image.

Cruise ships carrying several thousand passengers and crew have been compared to “floating cities,” and the volume of wastes that they produce is comparably large, consisting of sewage; wastewater from sinks, showers, and galleys (graywater); hazardous wastes; solid waste; oily bilge water; ballast water; and air pollution. The waste streams generated by cruise ships are governed by a number of international protocols (especially MARPOL) and U.S. domestic laws (including the Clean Water Act and the Act to Prevent Pollution from Ships), regulations, and standards, but there is no single law or rule. Some cruise ship waste streams appear to be well regulated, such as solid wastes (garbage and plastics) and bilge water. But there is overlap of some areas, and there are gaps in others. Some, such as graywater and ballast water, are not regulated (except in the Great Lakes), and concern is increasing about the impacts of these discharges on public health

Answer:

Policy Proposal: Addressing Youth Unemployment

Introduction:

Youth unemployment is a pressing issue that must be addressed by the government. It is not only a social issue but also a threat to the economic growth of the country. This policy proposal aims to address the problem of youth unemployment and provide recommendations on how to prevent it.

Policy Statement:

The policy proposal aims to establish a comprehensive strategy to prevent youth unemployment through education, training, and job opportunities. The policy proposes the following measures:

Education and Training: The government shall provide free education and training programs to equip the youth with the necessary skills and knowledge to qualify for jobs. The education and training programs shall focus on in-demand skills that are relevant to the job market.

Job Creation: The government shall work with private sector employers to create jobs that cater to the needs of the youth. The government shall provide incentives to companies that hire young people, including tax breaks and grants. The government shall also create public works programs that will provide temporary jobs to the youth.

Entrepreneurship: The government shall provide support to young people who wish to start their businesses. The government shall create business incubators that provide funding, mentorship, and training to young entrepreneurs. The government shall also provide low-interest loans and grants to young entrepreneurs.

Career Guidance: The government shall provide career guidance services to young people to help them make informed decisions about their future careers. The government shall work with schools and universities to provide career guidance services to students. The government shall also create a national career guidance center that provides career advice to young people.

Internship Programs: The government shall create internship programs that provide young people with hands-on work experience. The internship programs shall be open to all students and young people who are interested in gaining work experience.

Conclusion:

The policy proposal aims to prevent youth unemployment through education, training, job creation, entrepreneurship, career guidance, and internship programs. The government shall work with private sector employers, educational institutions, and other stakeholders to implement these measures. The policy proposal recognizes that the prevention of youth unemployment is a complex issue that requires a comprehensive strategy. The government must act now to prevent youth unemployment and ensure that young people have a bright future ahead of them

15. halimbawa ng agenda


Ang AGENDA ay ang listahan ng mga kailangang talakayin at gagawin sa isang pagpupulong o meeting. Maaari itong gamitin sa kahit anong meeting (office meeting, staff meeting, business meeting, PTA Meeting, Homeowner’s Association Meeting, atbp.). Importanted bigyan ng Title o pamagat ang bawat agenda na iyong gagawin. Madalas makikita natin sa agenda ang petsa, oras, lugar kung saan ito gaganapin. Kailangang maipakita rin dito ang inyong objective o layunin kung bakit magkakaroon ng pagpupulong. Idiniditalye rito ang schedule o ang target na oras bawat isyu o paksa. Gumagawa ng agenda para masigurong matatalakay ang mga dapat pag-usapan nang maayos at nasa oras.

28. Who is a local legislator? And how does legislation relates with legislature? Is there any? How?​


A local legislator is a person elected to represent an area of a local government at a state or national level. Local legislators may be part of a state Senate, state House of Representatives, or a city council. The term "legislation" refers to laws made by a legislature, which is a group of people with the power to make laws. The legislature is usually composed of elected representatives from different political parties. Legislation is an act or law made by a legislature in order to govern and regulate society.
