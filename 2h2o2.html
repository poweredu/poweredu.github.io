2H2O2 → 2H2O + O2

40 g / 34.01 g/mol = 1.176 mol of H2O2

From the balanced chemical equation, we can see that for every 2 moles of H2O2, we get 2 moles of H2O and 1 mole of O2. This means that for 1.176 mol of H2O2, we will get:

1.176 mol / 2 mol H2O2 x 2 mol H2O / 1 mol H2O2 = 0.588 mol of H2O

1.176 mol / 2 mol H2O2 x 1 mol O2 / 1 mol H2O2 = 0.588 mol of O2

To convert the moles of water and oxygen to mass, we need to use their respective molar masses:

Molar mass of H2O = 18.015 g/mol

Molar mass of O2 = 32.00 g/mol

Mass of water produced = 0.588 mol x 18.015 g/mol = 10.60 g

Mass of oxygen produced = 0.588 mol x 32.00 g/mol = 18.82 g

Therefore, starting with 40 grams of hydrogen peroxide, we will get 10.60 grams of water and 18.82 grams of oxygen.
