He accompanies a South Vietnamese general and his entourage to the airport as they flee the country during the fall of Saigon.Climax: The narrator is captured and tortured by the South Vietnamese army, and eventually escapes with the help of his communist handlers.Ending of the story: The narrator returns to the United States, but is haunted by his experiences and struggles to reconcile his divided loyalties and identities.Issues:Type of issue: Moral and social issueThe morality of spying and betrayal, and the consequences of political ideology on personal relationships"I had come to believe that the task of the revolutionary was not to remake the world at great cost to others, but to reveal the world as it already was, as it was always becoming, in the hope of giving others the vision and courage to change it for themselves."
