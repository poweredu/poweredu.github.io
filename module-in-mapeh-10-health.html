23. Subject: MAPEH Module-I 1-Enumerate and explain the different existing laws that protect public health.​


Clean Air Act: This federal law regulates air emissions from stationary and mobile sources, in order to protect air quality and reduce the risk of respiratory diseases.

Clean Water Act: This federal law regulates the discharge of pollutants into navigable waters and protects water quality for drinking, fishing, and recreation.

Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act: This federal law regulates the safety of food, drugs, and cosmetics, and ensures that products are safe for human consumption.

Occupational Safety and Health Act: This federal law regulates workplace safety and health, and aims to reduce work-related injuries and illnesses.

Americans with Disabilities Act: This federal law prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities and requires accessibility in public spaces and services, such as healthcare facilities and public transportation.

Emergency Medical Treatment and Active Labor Act: This federal law requires hospitals to provide emergency medical treatment to patients regardless of their ability to pay, in order to prevent medical emergencies from becoming public health crises.

Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act: This federal law expands access to affordable health insurance and healthcare services, and aims to improve public health outcomes by increasing preventive care and reducing healthcare costs.

Vaccine Injury Compensation Program: This federal program provides compensation for individuals who suffer adverse reactions to vaccines, in order to encourage widespread vaccination and prevent the spread of infectious diseases.
