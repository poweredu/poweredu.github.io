2. who invented turbo broiler​


Answer:

Abstract

AbstractA gas-fired broiler which produces infrared heat by means of stainless steel radiants. Stainless steel baffles are located adjacent to and between each pair of radiants which causes the flue air and spent gases to be redirected over the hot radiants, thus superheating the air. The superheated air and the heat from the radiants further heat the baffles. By incorporating the baffles between each pair of radiants to block the direct venting of the air, greater efficiency is achieved. The improved efficiency of the broiler is provided by producing higher cooking temperatures while using the same amount of BTU's.

3. how to clean Turbo broiler?​


Answer:

Cleaning a Turbo broiler can be a little tricky, but it is important to maintain hygiene and ensure the appliance lasts for a long time. Here are the steps you can follow to clean a Turbo broiler:

Allow the Turbo broiler to cool down: Before cleaning the Turbo broiler, make sure it has cooled down completely.

Remove the racks and drip tray: Take out the racks and drip tray from the Turbo broiler.

Soak the racks and drip tray: Fill a sink or basin with warm water and dish soap. Soak the racks and drip tray for a few minutes.

Clean the interior: Using a damp cloth or sponge, wipe the interior of the Turbo broiler to remove any food particles or grease.

Use baking soda paste: Make a paste using baking soda and water. Apply the paste to the interior of the Turbo broiler and let it sit for 30 minutes. Wipe the paste off with a damp cloth or sponge.

Use vinegar solution: Mix equal parts of water and vinegar in a spray bottle. Spray the solution onto the interior of the Turbo broiler and wipe it off with a damp cloth or sponge.

Clean the exterior: Wipe the exterior of the Turbo broiler with a damp cloth or sponge.

Dry the Turbo broiler: Once you have cleaned the Turbo broiler, make sure it is completely dry before using it again.

Reassemble the racks and drip tray: Put the racks and drip tray back into the Turbo broiler.

Store the Turbo broiler: Store the Turbo broiler in a dry and cool place.

By following these steps, you can easily clean a Turbo broiler and keep it in good working condition.

8. Turbo charge differs from supercharger in terms of ​


Answer:

"Supercharger" is the generic term for an air compressor used to increase the pressure or density of air entering an engine, providing more oxygen with which to burn fuel.

A turbocharger is simply a supercharger that is powered instead by a turbine in the exhaust stream.

Supercharger vs Turbo. A supercharger doesn't work off the exhaust gas, it is attached to your engine and spins with the crankshaft. When the crankshaft spins the supercharger, it forces air into the motor. The turbo is more efficient as it doesn't require engine power to spin it, so it makes more power per boost.

No lag: the supercharger's biggest advantage over a turbocharger is that it does not have any lag. Power delivery is immediate because the supercharger is driven by the engine's crankshaft. Low RPM boost: good power at low RPM in comparison with turbochargers. Price: cost effective way of increasing horsepower.

MICROWAVE OVEN

A microwave oven (also known as a microwave) is an electric oven that heats and cooks food by exposing it to microwave-frequency electromagnetic radiation.[1] This causes polar molecules in the food to rotate and produce thermal energy, which is known as dielectric heating. Because excitation is rather consistent in the exterior 25–38 mm (1–1.5 inches) of a homogeneous, high water content food item, microwave ovens heat foods fast and efficiently. Microwave ovens use microwaves, which are a type of electromagnetic energy akin to radio waves, to heat food. Microwaves have three properties that enable them to be employed in cooking: they are reflected by metal, pass through glass, paper, plastic, and other similar materials, and are absorbed by meals. The microwave oven is an extremely flexible kitchen equipment with a wide range of applications. Microwave ovens are useful for reheating and cooking meals, disinfecting kitchen goods, juicing citrus fruits, heating beauty products, roasting garlic, and de-crystallizing honey, among other things.
