4. You have a pure sample of the antiseptic aminacrine, C13H10N2. (a) Calculate the mass in grams of 0.06500 mol aminacrine. (b) Calculate the number of aminacrine molecules in a 0.2480-g sample. (c) Calculate the number of nitrogen atoms in this 0.2480-g sample. (d) Calculate the mass of N in 100. g aminacrine.


(a) SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass.

The molar masses of C, H and N are 12.011 g, 1.008 g and 14.007 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molar mass} & = \text{13(12.011 g) + 10(1.008 g) + 2(14.007 g)} \\ & = \text{194.237 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 & = \text{0.06500 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \times \frac{\text{194.237 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{12.63 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 12.63 g in 0.06500 mol of aminacrine.

------------------------------------------------------------

(b) SOLUTION:

There are 6.02214 × 10²³ molecules in 1 mole of aminacrine.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \: \text{molecules} & = \text{0.2480 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2}{\text{194.237 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \times \frac{6.02214 \times 10^{23} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \: \text{molecules}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \\ & = \boxed{7.689 \times 10^{20} \: \text{molecules}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 7.689 × 10²⁰ molecules present in 0.2480 g of aminacrine.

------------------------------------------------------------

(c) SOLUTION:

There are 2 moles of nitrogen in 1 mole of aminacrine.

There are 6.02214 × 10²³ atoms in 1 mole of nitrogen.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{number of N atoms} & = \text{0.2480 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2}{\text{194.237 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \times \frac{\text{2 mol N}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \times \frac{6.02214 \times 10^{23} \: \text{N atoms}}{\text{1 mol N}} \\ & = \boxed{1.538 \times 10^{21} \: \text{atoms}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 1.538 × 10²¹ atoms of nitrogen present in 0.2480 g of aminacrine.

------------------------------------------------------------

(d) SOLUTION:

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of N} & = \text{100. g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2}{\text{194.237 g} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \times \frac{\text{2 mol N}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{C}_{13}\text{H}_{10}\text{N}_2} \times \frac{\text{14.007 g N}}{\text{1 mol N}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{14.4 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 14.4 g of nitrogen in 100. g of aminacrine.

11. Eugenol (C10H12O2) is known to be found in nutmeg, cinnamon, basil and nutmeg. What is the boiling point of a solution containing 0.144 g of this compound dissolved in 10.0 g of benzene? (Kb= 2.53 °C/m), Bpt(benzene)= 80.10 °C and Molar mass of C10H12O2= 164.2 g/mol ​


Given:

mass of solute = 0.144 g

mass of solvent = 10.0 g = 0.0100 kg

molar mass of solute = 164.2 g/mol

[tex]\text{BP}_{solvent} = \text{80.10°C}[/tex]

[tex]K_{\text{b}} = \text{2.53°C/molal}[/tex]

solute: eugenol

solvent: benzene

Required:

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{0.144 g}}{\text{164.2 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = 8.76979 × 10^{-4} \: \text{mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]m = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]m = \frac{8.76979 × 10^{-4} \: \text{mol}}{\text{0.0100 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]m = \text{0.0876979 molal}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the boiling point elevation.

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = mK_{\text{b}}[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = (\text{0.0876979 molal})(\text{2.53°C/molal})[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = \text{0.222°C}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the boiling point of the solution.

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{BP}_{solvent} + \Delta T_{\text{b}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{80.10°C} + \text{0.222°C}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{80.322°C}}[/tex]

17. Eugenol is the active ingredient in the oil of cloves used to relieve toothache. Calculate the boiling point of a solution in which 33 grams of eugenol, (C10H12O2) is dissolved in 100.0 grams of benzene. (Kb benzene= 2.53 0C/m; Tb(solvent) = 80.100C)​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{33 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{164.204 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{100.0 g = 0.1000 kg} \\ & K_{\text{b}} = 2.53^{\circ}\text{C/}m \\ & T^{\circ}_{\text{b}} = 80.10^{\circ}\text{C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{33 g}}{\text{164.204 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.20097 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.20097 mol}}{\text{0.1000 kg}} \\ & = \text{2.0097 mol/kg} \\ & = 2.0097 \: m \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the boiling-point elevation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \Delta T_{\text{b}} & = K_{\text{b}}m \\ & = (2.53^{\circ}\text{C/}m)(2.0097 \: m) \\ & = 5.08^{\circ}\text{C} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the boiling point of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} T_{\text{b}} & = T^{\circ}_{\text{b}} + \Delta T_{\text{b}} \\ & = 80.10^{\circ}\text{C} + 5.08^{\circ}\text{C} \\ & = \boxed{85.18^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the boiling point of the solution is 85.18°C.
