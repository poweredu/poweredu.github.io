10. Does a coat of vaseline on a leaf make the leaf wilt or help preserve it?


Answer:

Applying Vaseline, in real life experiment it will reduce water loss from upper surface to moderate and from lower surface to minimum ( more stomata is present on lower surface). Gradually the plant will seize its metabolic activity resulting in plant death.

The stomata would be covered and there would be no photosynthesis and transpiration.

Answer: It will make the leaf wilt.

Explanation: We all know that leaves do have numerous pores known as STOMATA. This stomata is located under the leaves and this keep the plants to take up CO2 ( Carbon dioxide) which is useful in the process of food-making or what we called "PHOTOSYNTHESIS". Stomata is responsible for releasing the O2 (Oxygen) made during photosynthesis, without the stomata, plants cannot make food and will die. If you applied the Vaseline coat underneath the leaf where the stomata are located this will blocked the stomata and preventing plants to photosynthesize. When the Vaseline coat os applied on top of the leaf, the stomata is not blocked and it will still be able to do photosynthesis. Therefore, it will live.

24. Is it still possible to use expired vaseline?

If your Vaseline jar has gone past its expiration date but looks and smells fine, it should be safe to use.
