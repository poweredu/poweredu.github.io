Answer:

Rules for Machine Safety

1.Never remove or try to defeat machine safeguards.

2.Don't create new hazards, such as allowing objects to fall into the moving parts or by creating a new pinch point.

3.Report problems with machine safeguards to your supervisor immediately.

4.Never leave machines unattended with parts still moving.

Remove guards only when the machine has been locked out and tagged out.

6. If possible, lubricate machine parts without removing the safeguard; otherwise, turn the machine off and lock it out before lubricating.

7. Operate equipment only when guards are in place and properly adjusted.

8. Do not use unauthorized or damaged guards.

9. Do not wear loose clothing, jewelry, or long hair around machines—these increase the risk of being caught in the machinery.

10. Ask your supervisor if you have any questions about a machine safety or how to work with machine guards safely.

11. Safety measures in handling/using simple machines


Simple machines are tools that make work easier by changing the direction or magnitude of a force. Examples of simple machines include levers, pulleys, inclined planes, wedges, screws, and wheels and axles. While they can be very useful in many applications, they can also be dangerous if not handled or used properly. Here are some safety measures to consider when handling or using simple machines:

1. Read and follow the manufacturer's instructions and warnings carefully.

2. Use personal protective equipment (PPE) such as gloves, safety glasses, or hard hats as appropriate for the task and the machine being used.

3. Inspect the machine before use to ensure that it is in good condition and that all parts are working properly.

4. Use the machine only for its intended purpose and within its specified capacity.

5. Keep your fingers, hair, and loose clothing away from moving parts.

6. Never try to reach into a machine while it is running or attempt to stop it with your hands.

7. Do not modify or alter the machine without proper authorization and training.

8. Do not use a machine if you are not trained or authorized to do so.

9. Keep the work area clean and free of clutter to prevent tripping or falling hazards.

10. Always turn off the machine and disconnect it from its power source before performing maintenance or making adjustments.

By following these safety measures, you can help prevent accidents and injuries when handling or using simple machines. It is important to prioritize safety at all times to ensure that you and those around you are protected.

16. Give some safety measures in handling simple machines.​


Answer:

Never remove or try to defeat machine safeguards.

Don't create new hazards, such as allowing objects to fall into the moving parts or by creating a new pinch point.

Report problems with machine safeguards to your supervisor immediately.

Never leave machines unattended with parts still moving.

Do not wear loose clothing, jewelry, or long hair around machines—these increase the risk of being caught in the machinery.

Operate equipment only when guards are in place and properly adjusted.

Remove guards only when the machine has been locked out and tagged out.

If possible, lubricate machine parts without removing the safeguard; otherwise, turn the machine off and lock it out before lubricating

Do not use unauthorized or damaged guards.

Ask your supervisor if you have any questions about a machine safety or how to work with machine guards safely.
