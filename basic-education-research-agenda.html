23. example of research agenda thats easy to reseach and explain​


Answer:

Here are some examples of research agenda topics that are relatively easy to research and explain:

The effects of social media on mental health: This is a popular and relevant topic that can be explored through surveys, interviews, or existing studies. The research can focus on the impact of social media on self-esteem, body image, and other mental health concerns.The benefits of mindfulness meditation: This topic can be explored through literature reviews or surveys. The research can focus on the impact of mindfulness meditation on stress, anxiety, depression, and overall well-being.The impact of remote work on work-life balance: This topic has become more relevant during the pandemic and can be explored through surveys or interviews. The research can focus on the effects of remote work on employee productivity, job satisfaction, and work-life balance.The effectiveness of online learning: This topic can be explored through surveys or interviews with students and teachers. The research can focus on the effectiveness of online learning compared to traditional in-person learning, as well as the benefits and drawbacks of online learning.The impact of exercise on mental health: This topic can be explored through literature reviews or surveys. The research can focus on the impact of exercise on depression, anxiety, and overall well-being, as well as the optimal duration and frequency of exercise for mental health benefits.

These research agenda topics are relatively easy to research and explain because there is existing literature or data available, and they are relevant and important issues in today's society.

25. RESEARCH AGENDA- Academic Track​


Research Topic

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) Practice among the Maasai Community in Kenya: Contributing Factors

Research Methods: Qualitative or Quantitative?

I would use a qualitative research methodology for this project. The objective of a qualitative method of inquiry is to comprehend a social or human problem (FGM practice) from several angles. The research issue is appropriate for ethnographic research, which examines an intact cultural group (the Maasai) in its natural environment (Kajiado and Narok Counties, Kenya) over a predetermined length of time.

Research Methods

We'll employ a descriptive research design. Structured questionnaires will be used to gather primary data. The study's target population includes young Maasai women, men, and traditional leaders in Kenya's Kajiado and Narok counties who are between the ages of 15 and 20. As recommended by Mugenda & Mugenda, the researcher will utilize stratified random selection to select the sample, taking 30% of each stratum of the target population (2007). To demonstrate the relationship between independent factors and the dependent variable, data will be analyzed using the correlation statistic. Tables and graphs will be used to present the results.

Importance of research to society

Understanding the factors that promote this harmful practice will help community members, national policymakers, policymakers in the counties of Kajiado and Narok, nongovernmental organizations (NGOs), and community-based organizations (CBOs) who are working cooperatively to implement programs, laws, and awareness-raising tactics that discourage FGM.

Abstract

Considers an area of growing importance in marketing research. Small to medium‐sized enterprises (SMEs) are continuing to play an increasing role in the development of western economies. Puts forward the argument that existing approaches to conducting marketing research in SMEs are rooted in the big firm mindset and, therefore, in positivist thinking, tending to focus mainly on survey methods. Examines the various orientations that predominate in and shape the SME context. Develops a research position with a syncretised qualitative research methodology outlined and applied to a research project carried out by one of the authors into 57 small firms in the UK. All of the orientations of the SMEs appeared rooted, to a large extent, in one or more highly influential individuals who fashion the culture and direction of these firms. Argues for an approach to research in SMEs that recognises the various influencing orientations including the impact on marketing research and the role of the entrepreneurial individual.

30. What are the eight (8) themes in the research Agenda?​


There are eight themes that reveal eight aspects of the research agenda, they are systematic research approach, knowledge, rigorous data, evidence-based, reliable sources, influencing decision making, providing useful and practical insights, and collaborative social channels

Further explanation:

Systematic research approach. A deliberate approach to developing a research method is reflected by careful study, a scientific methodology, and time.Knowledge. Organizations define knowledge as the individual's capacity to identify elements in a field of endeavor based on comprehension of context, theory, or both.Rigorous data. Persuasive, persuasion skills, and data reporting are under the umbrella of providing rigorous information that can portray an explanation with truth underlying all the variables associated with the subject.Evidence-based. Possessing the ability to confirm and validate skepticism, which evaluates evidence-based data and seeks to enhance findings, is a quality.Reliable sources. Synthesis of information, preview of previous work, and the fusion of theory and practice are examples of practices of using reliable sources, which should employ acceptable sources connected to the issue.Influencing decision making. The purpose of research in organizations is to examine the impact of educated decision making.Providing useful and beneficial insights. Skills that influence strategy implementation, policy development, and policy implementation offer practical and applicable insights into corporate strategy.Collaborative social channels. Themes for the collaborative social channel necessary to put the findings into practice include channel, communication, social system, collaborative analysis, and collaborative invention.
