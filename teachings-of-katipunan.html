11. in one phrase or sentence, how can you describe the teaching stated in the kartilya ng katipunan?​


Answer:

The Kartilya ng Katipunan is a set of principles that must be followed by the members of the revolutionary group. They are based on laws and on basic human principles. The Kartilla is written to ensure that humanity is still alive amidst the war, colonization, and revolution. The teachings dictate both men and women to respect each other and other lives. They also teach that women are not tools but partners. It is also taught that the members should respect and take care of their parents as they are already getting old and in near death. The kartilla verifies values that must be applied especially those that reflect their vision and mission. These values are deeply rooted in the love for country, God, and people. The kartilla teaches the members to love their nation and put their nation’s interest above anything else. It also states that they must fight for the freedom of the nation.

27. Do you find this decision sound? How are their perspective different and alike? ​


Answer:

Andres Bonifacio's decision to adopt Emilio Jacinto's teachings of the Katipunan instead of his own draft of the Dekalogo ng Katipunan was likely influenced by a variety of factors, including the content of the teachings themselves and the political climate of the time. Jacinto's teachings were more philosophical and idealistic, emphasizing the importance of duty, honor, and self-sacrifice for the cause of independence, whereas the Dekalogo was more practical and focused on the rules and regulations of the organization.

Whether this decision was sound or not is a matter of interpretation, as both perspectives have their strengths and weaknesses. Jacinto's teachings were seen as more inspiring and motivational, and they helped to unify the members of the Katipunan. On the other hand, the Dekalogo provided a clearer set of guidelines and expectations for the members, which could have helped to maintain discipline and order within the organization.

In terms of similarities, both Jacinto's teachings and the Dekalogo emphasized the importance of sacrifice and unity in the pursuit of independence, reflecting the shared goal and ideals of the Katipunan. Ultimately, both perspectives were important in different ways for the Katipunan, and Andres Bonifacio's decision to adopt Jacinto's teachings was likely motivated by a desire to promote the values and ideals that he believed would best serve the cause of independence.
