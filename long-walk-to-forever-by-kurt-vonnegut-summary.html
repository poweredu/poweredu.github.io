1. summary of another universe by Kurt comendador​


"He was about my age and my size. The boy first approached us while we were eating at a cottage at a spring resort."

This is where everything started, when the author saw a boy, about the same age as he is with the same build. The boy he saw is not a normal 'on a vacation' boy just like them, he is a vendor, a champorado vendor.

“Champorado mo, Ma’am. Sir.” he said, “Init pa.”

Asked the boy. He was offered a chocolate porridge by the vendor yet he declined.

He watched the boy as he went on his way selling champorado to other people served in a small pot. He said in his mind that the boy might have better sales if only he have a better container that what he is currently using that time.

After some time, he then soak himself into the water, everything happening in the resort is familiar to him, he said. The laughter, the splashin of water, the singing of people, the mood and the effect of water to him. He believed that the coldnedd of the water cleanses his body and mind from stress. "It's like swimming in your universe," as he described how he felt in the water.

 

rising action

"Then there he was again, walking on top of the wall that separates the water into smaller pools."

He spotted the boy once more still selling his champorado.

After seeing the boy again, he asked his girlfriend if she would like to eat some champorado, but she refused and said, "We still have Siopao in the bag.".

Not getting the chance to buy the chocolate porridge, he then just watched the boy from afar and noticed that tha pot the boy is using is just a small pot which is also already been blackened by so much usage.

"He must’ve used it over open fire frequently." he said.

They swam until twilight, people become fewer yet the boy still peddling on his champorado.

When they decided to stop swimming, they started to queue on changing clothes. He let his girlfriend in first then waited outside. While waiting, sat on a circular bench, he noticed the boy sitting next to him looking so down and miserable. He knew that he was the champorado vendor as he saw a small sooty pot beside the boy. Then he saw that the boy is figuring out how to fix his slippers because it's broken. Seeing this, he felt like crying but he held it back.

 

climax

He watched as the boy tries to fix his slippers, unconsciously watching him until the boy tried to wear it, tried it out, but still not fixed. He then noticed that the boy is wearing different footwear, on his left foot is a slide slipper while on the other is the one that broke, a traditional flip-flop.

He wanted to help him, he thought of giving him money but he doesn't have plenty of it and thought he might need it on the way home, he also thought of giving him his old pair of shoes but also thought that if he did, he might get trouble with the enforces riding a motorcycle barefoot, thn he thought of what he saw before, people putting wires from behind to hold the rubber strap in position but he can't find any wire in the vicinity as the resort is too dark at the time.

He wanted to say something but nothing came out of his mouth.

 

falling action

After a while, the boy went inside the changing room, carrying everything on one hand and his slipper in the other. He noticed that the boy also have a backpack in him which he concluded that he might have his utensils in there.

He's left alone outside, waiting, and felt trapped in another universe.

He is thinking that the boy and him could've been similar in many ways. He thought that the boy might also be the eldest of his siblings, he might also have four younger siblings, or that he might also want to soak himself into the cold water and atleast enjoy. He thought that he was about the same as he is, yet doing something which he doesn't.

He liked to ask him couple of questions like where he lives, was his champorado sold out, what happened to his slippers, or what would he doafter that.

But he also thought of a bigger question, a question for himself, that if the circumstances dictates him to do what the boy does, would he be willing and ready to do it

denouement

His girlfriend now finished changing, it was his turn to change. But he noticed that the boys are still not coming out.

And so he tried to take his mind off of him for a moment and change into his dry clothes. The thought of the boy taking too long inside the changing room still bothered him. And it also bothered him that he doesn't have any way of knowing if the boy was able to fix his slippers.

When he got out, he saw the vendor at a distance, There were little light to see him clearly so he only saw his backpack. He saw that the boy was sort of walking with a drag, and at this moment he hoped that the boy gets home safe and sound.

When they were ready to leave, it was already dark. They can already hear the frogs cheering, and to him it sounded like a woeful dirge.

2. What is the tone and mood of the story "Long walk to forever?" why you did so?


The tone of the story "Long walk to forever" by Kurt Vonnegut is nostalgic, romantic, and introspective. The mood of the story is bittersweet, gentle, and reflective.

The author uses a tender and romantic tone to convey the feelings of the characters, Newt and Catherine, as they reconnect after a long separation. The nostalgia in the tone is reflected in the characters' reflections on their past and the memories they share. The introspective tone is evident in the characters' thoughts and internal monologues about their feelings and relationship.

The mood of the story is bittersweet, as the characters are happy to reconnect but also face the reality that they will soon part ways. The gentle and reflective mood is evoked by the serene setting and the lyrical descriptions of nature. Overall, the tone and mood contribute to the story's themes of love, memory, and the passage of time.
