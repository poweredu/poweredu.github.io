6. Theory of wages and profit by david ricardo


Answer:

The subsistence theory of wages, advanced by David Ricardo and other classical economists, was based on the population theory of Thomas Malthus. It held that the market price of labor would always tend toward the minimum required for subsistence. If the supply of labor increased, wages would fall, eventually causing a decrease in the labor supply. If the wage rose above the subsistence level, the population would increase until the larger labor force would again force wages down.
