14. Anong lalawigan ang katulad ng Bulacan at ano Ang pagkakatulad ng lalawigan at Bulacan at ano Naman Ang pagkakaiba nito sa bulacan


Answer:

Lalawigan na katulad ng Bulacan

Ang lalawigan na naiisip kong katulad ng Bulacan ay ang lalawigan ng Nueva Ecija. Narito ang kanilang pagkakatulad at pagkakaiba:

Pagkakatulad:

Ang lalawigan ng Bulacan at Nueva Ecija ay parehong matatagpuan sa Central Luzon. Bigas ang pangunahing mga produkto ng mga lalawigang ito dahil nakatayo ito sa isang malawak na kapatagan. Parehong dinadaanan ng Sierra Madre ang silangang bahagi ng mga lalawigang ito.

Pagkakaiba:

Ang lalawigan ng Bulacan ay mas maliit ang nasasakupang teritoryo. Ang lalawigan ng Nueva Ecija ay mas malaki ang nasasakupang teritoryo. May bahagi ng karagatan ang nasasakupan ng Bulacan (sa bandang Hagonoy), habang ang Nueva Ecija naman ay landlocked. Malolos ang kabisera ng Bulacan, habang Palayan City naman ang kabisera ng Nueva Ecija.

20. why do people calibrate the pulilan carabao festival (pulilan bulacan)​


Answer:

Celebration of the Pulilan Carabao Festival

People from Pulilan, Bulacan celebrate the Kneeling Carabao Festival every May 14 and 15 because they wanted to give thanks to the patron saint of the town, San Isidro Labrador. He is also the patron saint of the farmers who are using the carabaos for their work in the field.

Explanation:

Every May 14 and 15, the townspeople of Pulilan, Bulacan line up the streets to witness the annual Kneeling Carabao Festival. Many tourists from all over the Philippines also come to the town to see what the celebration is all about. The parade starts at 2 PM, in Barangay Dampol 1st, and the carabaos, along with the dancers and the musicians, would have to walk more than 1.5 kilometers just to reach the San Isidro Labrador Parish Church in Barangay Poblacion.

Feel the heart and soul of the Philippines in Bulacan. Its history and tradition, its land, its culture, and its people, live and breathe Filipino. Truly a melting pot of the past and the present, the old and the new, the countryside and the urbane – that is Bulacan.  

The province is noted as the land of heroes. Bulacan is the home province of some of the nation’s celebrated heroes: Francisco Baltazar (Balagtas), “The Prince of Filipino Poets,” Marcelo H. Del Pilar, “The Great Propagandist,” and Gregorio del Pilar, “The Hero of Tirad Pass.” It is reputed for beautiful women, progressive cooperatives, small and medium scale industries. It is known for excellent craftsmanship as in its jewelries, leather crafts, and garments.  

The province has emerged into a reputable resort haven of Luzon. Just a few minutes north of Manila by car, Bulacan resorts provide an accessible and welcome respite from the pressures of city life.

The province’s name is derived from the Tagalog word “bulak” meaning cotton, which was its former principal product.  

Bulacan started with small fishing settlements along the coast of Manila Bay and expanded into the interior with the coming of the Spaniards. These settlements formed the nuclear of towns that were founded from 1572 (Bulacan and Calumpit) to 1750 (San Rafael). In 1848, the town of San Miguel was annexed to Bulacan from Pampanga.  

Bulacan was one of the first eight provinces to rise against Spanish rule. The first phase of the Philippine Revolution ended with the signing of the Pact of Biak-na-Bato in San Miguel in 1897 between the Filipinos and the Spaniards, after which Aguinaldo was exiled to Hong Kong. The second phase saw the drafting of the constitution of the first Philippine republic by the Malolos Congress at Barasoain Church in 1898. The subsequently established republic had its capital at Malolos until President Emilio Aguinaldo transferred it to San Isidro, Nueva Ecija in 1899 when the Filipino-American war broke out.  

When the Americans established a civil government in the Philippines, they held the first election in the country in the town of Baliuag, Bulacan on May 6, 1899.

25. What makes the art of the people of Bulacan and Pampanga Filipino? Cite example.


Answer:

Bulacan's Art:

The Art of Making Bulacan Pastillas Wrappers (also known as "Pabalat" or "Borlas de Pastillas") Pastillas come in a variety of shapes and sizes in the Philippines. What makes the pastillas of Bulacan different is how they are wrapped in beautifully constructed pastilla wrappers, also known as pabalat or borlas de pastillas.

Pampanga's Art:

The Kuran ampong Pasu, Pukpuk, Dukit, Parul, Burarul, Dase, Kupia, Santos, Gitara, and Gawang pande are among the ten (10) arts that the province is known for. The above-mentioned arts can be found in various locations of Pampanga. Every art form represents the town or city in which it is most often practiced.

What makes Philippine art Filipino?: The concept was that portraying scenes of everyday life and surroundings without idealizing them was the most spiritually related to the Filipino soul and home soil. (The local magic realists' awareness of genre keeps them from being wholly derivative.)

EXAMPLE OF FILIPINO ART:

Folk architecture, marine transport, notable sculpture in the Philippines, weaving, carving, folk performing arts, folk (oral) literature, folk graphic and plastic arts, ornament, pottery, and other artistic representations of traditional culture are among the traditional arts in the Philippines.
