Lesson Plan in Filipino Grade 2

I. Layunin

Nagagamit nang wasto ang pangngalan sa pagbibigay ng pangalan ng tao, lugar, at mga bagay kasarian. F2WG-Ic-e-2

II. Paksang Aralin

A. Sanggunian: CG pahina 23 , Filipino LM 28-30

B. Kagamitan: magic box, tsart

III. Pamamaraan

A. Pagganyak

Sa magic box, kumuha ng isang larawan at tukuyin kung ito ba ay ngalan ng tao, bagay, hayop, lugar o pangyayari.

B. Paglalahad at Pagtalakay

Basahin ang kwento at sagutan ang mga tanong.

Nasaan ka Nena?

Nagising si Nena na wala ang ina sa kaniyang tabi. Nakadama siya ng takot kaya niyakap niya ang unan. Narinig niyang tumatahol ang aso. Bumangon siya para hanapin ang ina. Pumunta siya sa kusina pero wala ang kaniyang nanay. Biglang namatay ang ilaw. Kumulog nang malakas. Isang matalim na kidlat ang kasunod nito. Bumuhos ang malakas na ulan. May kalakasan din ang hangin. Pilit nilabanan ni Nena ang takot na nadarama. Ipinikit niya ang mga mata at nagdasal nang taimtim. Hindi nagtagal, dumating ang kaniyang Ate Nelia. May dalang nakasinding kandila. Sinabi nitong pumunta ang ina sa palengke upang bumili ng bigas.

Mga Tanong

Bakit natakot si Nena?  Bakit kaya biglang nagdilim ang paligid?  Paano ipinakita ni Nelia ang kaniyang pagmamalasakit sa kapatid?  Ano-ano ang ginagawa mo para sa kasapi ng pamilya?  Ano-anong pangngalang tumutukoy sa tao, bagay, hayop, at pook ang iyong napakinggan sa kuwento?

21. reaction paper about cold sandwiches of grade 11 home economics ​


In my reaction paper about the cold sandwiches made in my grade 11 home economics class, I must say that I was thoroughly impressed with the variety and creativity of the sandwiches we were able to make. Not only were the sandwiches delicious, but they were also visually appealing and fun to make.

One of the things that I enjoyed most about the project was the freedom we were given to come up with our own sandwich combinations. This allowed us to get creative and try out different flavor combinations that we might not have thought of otherwise.

I was also pleasantly surprised by the variety of ingredients we were able to use. From different types of bread and spreads to a wide range of meats, cheeses, and vegetables, we had a wide range of options to choose from. This made it possible for everyone in the class to find a sandwich that they enjoyed, regardless of their personal preferences.

Overall, I thought the cold sandwich project was a great success. Not only did it teach us valuable skills in the kitchen, but it also gave us the opportunity to be creative and have fun while learning. I would definitely recommend this project to other home economics classes in the future.
