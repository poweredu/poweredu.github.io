8. integers addition star burst​


Answer:

To add integers, you can use the following rules: if the two integers have the same sign then add their absolute values together and keep the sign; if the two integers have different signs, then subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value and keep the sign of the larger absolute value. For example, to add 3 and -2, you would subtract the smaller absolute value (-2) from the larger absolute value (3) and keep the sign of the larger value, so the result would be 3 + (-2) = 1. You can use this same rule to add more than two integers together. To illustrate this using the Starburst candy, you can think of each flavor as a different integer, and then add them together to get the total number of Starbursts in the mix.

10. what literary device is used in burst into laughter?


Hyperbole

The phrase burst into laughter is an example of a hyperbole.

A hyperbole is a statement of extreme exaggeration. It is used to add more humor and interest in exclaiming an idea and expression.

To burst into laughter does not literally mean to explode. Rather, it is used as an idiom meaning to explode into laughter, to laugh intensely or uncontrollably in a given situation.
