The war on drugs is a global campaign that aims to stop illegal drug use, production, and trade. In the Philippines, the government launched a war on drugs campaign in 2016 to eradicate the drug problem in the country. The campaign involves the use of police force and extra-judicial killings to crack down on drug dealers and users.

Many human rights organizations have criticized the campaign for violating human rights. The campaign has resulted in thousands of deaths, mostly from low-income communities. Human rights advocates argue that the government's approach is denying the basic human rights of citizens, such as the right to due process, the right to a fair trial, and the right to life.

On the other hand, supporters of the campaign argue that it is necessary to combat the drug problem in the country. They believe that the drug trade is a major threat to public safety and that tough measures are needed to eradicate it.

In conclusion, the war on drugs in the Philippines is a complex issue with opposing views. While it is important to address the drug problem in the country, it should not be done at the expense of human rights. The government should find a way to balance the need to eradicate the drug problem while ensuring the protection of citizens' basic human rights
