14. my personal timeline with reflection


My personal timeline captures the academic ups and downs I experienced in elementary and high school. My academics and academic performance in school were the main, if not only, significant events in my life. Given that the majority of my 16 years have been spent in the academic world, if I were to give my life a title, it would be "My Life Academy."

The turning points in my timeline were my love-hate connection with science and my transition from an extroverted introvert to an isolated introvert. Going outside of my comfort zone and trying something new at first was challenging. Although being cautious and apprehensive is acceptable, you shouldn't let them stop you from taking action. These adjustments are done in your best interests and shouldn't be seen as a mistake.

The most important individuals in my life are my friends, parents, and teachers. I am who I am now and have accomplished what I have in life because of this group of people. My tutors recognized my potential and worked with me to shape it into a variety of abilities and skills that I will find useful in my future endeavors. My greatest supports are my parents. Whatever I did, they always had my back as long as it got me somewhere better. They also assisted me in making difficult judgments and tough situations. I couldn't conquer the obstacles I face in life without them. Next to my family, my friends are the individuals I value most in life.

To me, everything that happened had a purpose, so I wouldn't add anything to my timeline or remove anything. All of them are linked. A series of incidents occurred after one incidence, one after the other. It is possible that modifying or adding one incident will change the events that follow. If those occurrences had never transpired, I may be someone else.
