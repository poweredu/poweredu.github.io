1. Tatiana volosozhar and maxim trankov marriage


Tatiana Andreyеvna Volosozhar (Russian: Татья́на Андре́евна Волосожа́р, Ukrainian: Тетяна Андріївна Волосожар; born 22 May 1986) is a Ukrainian-born Russian pair skater. With Maxim Trankov, she is the two-time 2014 Olympic champion in the pairs and in team events, the 2013 World champion, a four-time (2012, 2013, 2014 and 2016) European champion, the 2012 Grand Prix Final champion, and a three-time (2011, 2013 and 2016) Russian national champion. They have also won six events on the Grand Prix series.

Volosozhar competed for Ukraine with Petr Kharchenko in 2000–04 and with Stanislav Morozov in 2004–10. She and Morozov are four-time Ukrainian national champions (2005, 2007, 2008 and 2010) and finished as high as fourth at the World Championships. In December 2010 she was granted expedited Russian citizenship, as both her parents are Russian.

Volosozhar and Trankov are the current World record holders for the combined total, and short program. They are the first figure skaters to win two gold medals at the same Olympics, having taken gold in pairs and in the inaugural team event.

3. Tatiana wants to give friendship bracelets to her 32 classmates. She already has 5 bracelets, and she can buy more bracelets in packages of 4.


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

If she buys 5 packages, she will add 5*4 = 20 to the 5 she already has for a total of 25. That is not enough since she need at least 32.  

She has 5, she needs 32 or 32-5 = 27 more. If n is the number of packages she must buy, then:

4n + 5 ≥ 32

4n ≥ 27

n ≥ 27/4

n ≥ 6.75

The smallest integer greater than or equal to 6.75 is 7.

She must buy 7 more packages of 4 to have enough for all her classmates. 5 packages will not be enough

13. ENRICHMENT • The last Emperor of Russia, Nicolas II, was married to Empress Alexandra, and they had five children, Olga, Tatiana, Maria, Anastasia, and Alexis. Alexis was the only one who was afflicted with hemophilia or the royal bleeding disease; all other members were normal. - Research on this medical condition and determine the mode of inheritance. • If only Prince Alexis was afflicted with the disease, determine his genotype. • What could be the genotypes of the Emperor and Empress? • Is it possible that each daughter could have been a carrier?​


Explanation:

Hemophilia is an X-linked recessive genetic disorder. This means that the gene responsible for hemophilia is located on the X chromosome, and the disease is expressed only when both X chromosomes have the mutation. Since males have only one X chromosome, they will express the disease if they inherit the mutation. Females, on the other hand, have two X chromosomes, and they will be carriers of the disease if they inherit one mutated X chromosome.

If only Prince Alexis was afflicted with the disease, his genotype must have been XhY, where Xh represents the mutant hemophilia allele and Y represents the Y chromosome.

The Emperor Nicolas II must have had a normal X chromosome (XN), and the Empress Alexandra must have been a carrier of the hemophilia gene, meaning she had one normal X chromosome (XN) and one mutated X chromosome (Xh).

It is possible that each daughter could have been a carrier. The daughters would inherit one X chromosome from their father (XN) and one from their mother (either XN or Xh). If a daughter inherited the Xh chromosome from her mother, she would be a carrier, but she would not express the disease. If a daughter inherited the Xh chromosome from both parents, she would express the disease, but this did not happen in the case of the Romanov daughters, as they were all reported to be normal
