7. What celebrated part of the pahiyas festival honors the significant role of carabaos in farming?


Answer:

Colorful decoration of houses made from different fruits, vegetables, and kipings. Carabaos made all of these things possible especially in the bounty of harvest brought by the fields which the carabao helped to plow.

Explanation:

To keep the tradition alive, people agreed to display their harvest in front of their homes where the parish priest would come to bless the harvest during the procession of the image of San Isidro Labrador, the patron saint of farmers and in honor of the festival that is being celebrated. Because of the economic situation, the town decided that every year only assigned houses around the town would participate by means of a competition, the best decorative and creative design of the Pahiyas during the festival. Houses used different fruits, vegetables, hats, baskets, paper-mache and kipings. After the competition, all the decorations will be thrown away to the people.
