The statement is wrong.

King Edward VII was popularly called as "the Peacemaker." He is the son of Queen Victoria and Prince Albert. He reigned as the King of United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and Emperor of India from January 22, 1901 until his death in 1910. He fostered good relationships and did a very great deal to keep peace between Britain and other European countries. His work for maintaining world stability at a time when war seemed to be looming was credited to him and made him increasingly popular with his people. He tried to stop arguments between other foreign rulers.

While he was a prince, he was kept out of politics during his mother’s reign, but was skilled in foreign affairs. As he became king, his main interests lay in the fields of foreign affairs and naval military matters. King Edward is fluent in French and German, that helps him reinvent royal diplomacy by numerous state visits across Europe. He paid visits to most of the rulers of the Europe, and many of them were his guests. Edward only ruled for nine years but made a lasting impression. He died at the age of 68 in 1910. 8. Who is Joseph Henry and the Henry's law?


Henry's law states that at a constant temperature, the amount of a gas that dissolves in a liquid is directly proportional to the partial pressure of that gas in equilibrium with that liquid.
