2. Introduction, body, conclusion mercury and the woodman ​


Introduction:
Once upon a time, there lived a woodman who earned his living by cutting and selling wood. One day, as he was working in the forest, he accidentally dropped his axe into a river. Feeling hopeless, he sat down by the riverbank and started to weep. Suddenly, a miraculous event occurred. The god Mercury appeared before him and offered to retrieve his lost axe. The woodman was overjoyed, but little did he know that the god had a surprise for him.

Body:
Mercury dived into the river and came back with an axe made of pure gold. The woodman, astonished at the sight of the valuable object, claimed that it was not his. Mercury then dived into the river again and came back with an axe made of silver. The woodman again denied it was his. Finally, Mercury brought back the woodman's iron axe, and the woodman gratefully claimed it.

To reward the woodman's honesty, Mercury gifted him with the gold and silver axes as well. The woodman, who was originally heartbroken by the loss of his iron axe, was now overjoyed with his newfound wealth. However, he soon realized the value of honesty and the importance of being content with what one has.

Conclusion:
In this story, we learn the importance of honesty and contentment. The woodman's honesty in claiming only his own axe, even when presented with more valuable alternatives, earned him the god's respect and admiration. We also learn that material possessions do not necessarily bring happiness, as the woodman was initially content with his simple iron axe. It was only after being gifted with the more valuable axes that he learned the value of honesty and contentment. The story of Mercury and the woodman remains a timeless tale that teaches us important life lessons.

Answer:

Introduction:

Mercury and the Woodman is a classic fable that teaches an important lesson about honesty and the consequences of deception. The story has been retold and adapted in different forms over the centuries, but the central message remains relevant today.

Body:

The story begins with a woodman who is struggling to make ends meet. One day, he finds a bag of gold coins in the forest and is overjoyed. However, when he tries to pick it up, he finds that it is stuck to the ground. As he struggles to free it, Mercury, the messenger of the gods, appears before him and offers to help. The woodman agrees, and Mercury uses his powers to free the bag.

Mercury then asks the woodman if he is the rightful owner of the bag. The woodman, realizing that he cannot claim it without getting into trouble, lies and says that he found it in the forest. Mercury, who knows the truth, decides to teach him a lesson. He tells the woodman that he will be punished for his dishonesty and disappears.

Soon after, the woodman tries to buy food and supplies with the gold coins, but the merchants refuse to accept them, suspecting that he stole them. The woodman is arrested and taken to the king, who orders him to be thrown into prison until he reveals the truth about the bag of gold. The woodman realizes his mistake and confesses, but it is too late. He is punished for his deceit, and the bag of gold is returned to its rightful owner.

Conclusion:

The moral of Mercury and the Woodman is clear: honesty is the best policy. The woodman's initial decision to lie ultimately led to his downfall, while his confession and acceptance of responsibility may have saved him from a harsher punishment. The story reminds us that our actions have consequences, and that it is important to be truthful, even when it is difficult. Mercury and the Woodman continues to be a timeless fable that teaches an important lesson about integrity and accountability.
