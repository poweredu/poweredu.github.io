1. how long does it take to digest food?​


Digestion time varies among individuals and between men and women. After you eat, it takes about six to eight hours for food to pass through your stomach and small intestine. Food then enters your large intestine (colon) for further digestion, absorption of water and, finally, elimination of undigested food.

Answer:

Explanation:

Food then enters your large intestine (colon) for further digestion, absorption of water and, finally, elimination of undigested food. It takes about 36 hours for food to move through the entire colon.

Answer:

1. Peristalsis is a rhythmic contraction of muscles that occurs in the walls of the digestive tract, which propels food and other contents through the digestive system. It contributes to the digestive process by helping to move food along the digestive tract and mix it with digestive juices and enzymes.

2. I believe "petistalsis" is a typo and you meant "peristalsis" again. Peristalsis affects the movement of food through the digestive tract by contracting the muscles in the wall of the digestive tract to push food and other contents along the tract, from the esophagus to the anus.

3. Mechanical digestion is the physical breakdown of food into smaller pieces by the teeth, tongue, and muscular contractions of the digestive system. It helps with the digestive process by increasing the surface area of food particles, making them easier for digestive enzymes to break down.

4. The process of mechanical digestion starts in the mouth, where the teeth chew and grind food into smaller pieces. The tongue and saliva help to mix the food into a bolus that can be swallowed. Once in the stomach, the muscular walls of the stomach contract to mix the food with digestive juices and break it down further.

5. Segmentation in the small intestine refers to the rhythmic contraction of circular muscles in the intestinal wall that mix the chyme (partially digested food) with digestive enzymes and bile. This facilitates absorption by bringing the chyme into contact with the intestinal wall, allowing for more efficient absorption of nutrients.

6.During the process of absorption, the nutrients from the food are transported from the lumen of the digestive tract, through the wall of the small intestine, and into the bloodstream. Lipid absorption takes place primarily in the small intestine, where bile from the liver emulsifies fats and lipases break them down into fatty acids and glycerol for absorption.

7. Absorption is the process by which nutrients from food are taken up by the body and transported to the cells that need them. It primarily takes place in the small intestine, where the surface area of the intestinal wall is maximized by finger-like projections called villi and microvilli. Nutrients are absorbed through the walls of the villi and transported to the bloodstream for distribution to the rest of the body.

Answer:

The digestive system -- which can be up to 30 feet in length in adults -- is usually divided into eight parts: the mouth, the esophagus, the stomach, the small intestine (or "small bowel") and the large intestine (also called "large bowel" or "colon") with the liver, pancreas, and gallbladder adding secretions to help digestion. These organs combine to perform six tasks: ingestion, secretion, propulsion, digestion, absorption, and defecation.
