1. based on the phylogenetic tree, would american alligators be more closely related to fire salamanders or red kangaroos? Explain the reasoning for your choice. ​


Answer:

Based on the phylogenetic tree, American alligators would be more closely related to fire salamanders. The reason for this is that the phylogenetic tree is a visual representation of the evolutionary relationships between different species. In general, species that are more closely related are closer to each other on the tree, while species that are more distantly related are farther apart. Since American alligators and fire salamanders are closer to each other on the tree than they are to red kangaroos, it suggests that they are more closely related to each other than they are to red kangaroos.

9. what is omnivorous of alligator ​


Answer:

Alligators aren't picky about what they eat. They are carnivores, so any type of prey is a meal to these reptiles. Alligators may eat fish, mollusks, birds, small mammals and other reptiles. Though carnivores usually only eat meat, alligators will also munch on fruit.

18. what is the "DESCRIPTION" of Alligators​


Answer:

Alligators have a long, rounded snout that has upward facing nostrils at the end; this allows breathing to occur while the rest of the body is underwater. The young have bright yellow stripes on the tail; adults have dark stripes on the tail. It's easy to distinguish an alligator from a crocodile by the teeth.
