Binislakan (Bee-neehs-lah-KAHN) is a peculiar and very colorfol dance from the province of Pangasinan in the island of Luzon, Philippines. The Hundred Islands National Park is an absolutely breathtaking Philippine national park and a beautifully protected area located in the stunning Alaminos, Pangasinan. The astonishing islands, totaling 124 at low tide and 123 at high tide, are majestically scattered in the sparkling Lingayen Gulf covering an expansive area of 16.76 square kilometres (6.47 sq mi). Only four of them have been developed for tourism: Marcos Island, Governor Island, Quezon Island, and Children's Island. One of the most enchanting and awe-inspiring tourist attractions in the Philippines, it is undeniably world-famous for good reasons: its islands are magnificently and perfectly spread out on the mesmerizing blue-green waters of Lingayen Gulf, gloriously rich in flora and fauna inland and underwater, with fine beige sand that yields every step, and also the most welcoming and delightful local islanders. The islands are believed to be about two million years old. There are actually ancient corals that extend well inland, in an area previously comprising the seabed of an ancient sea. The lowering of the sea level has impressively exposed them to the surface. The uniquely peculiar "mushroom-like" shapes of some of the islands were caused by the mesmerizing eroding action of the grand and mighty ocean waves.
