9. It is the visible part of human culture. It may anything that members of society make, use, and share such as food.


The answer is material culture. Material culture is the visible part of human culture. It may anything that members of society make, use, and share such as food. As material culture refers to the visible part of the culture, it involves physical things that can be recognized by the human senses. Material culture involves these physical things to define the human culture.

Definition of Material Culture Material culture refers to the visible part of the culture, hence, it involves physical things that can be recognized by the human senses. Material culture is the visible part of human culture. It may anything that members of society make, use, and share such as food.Examples of Material Culture

There are many examples of material culture that reflect human culture. Here are some of them:

temples as places of worship crowns as a symbol of power, wealth, and authority rings as symbol of marriage or "being taken" schools as places of learning Material Culture vs. Non-material Culture

Aside from the material culture, non-material culture is also related to the human culture. Here is the difference between the two:

Material Culture - Material Culture refers to the visible, physical, actual objects that define a culture. Non-material Culture - Non-material culture is the opposite of Material Culture. Non-material culture refers to the non-visible and nonphysical concepts or ideas that people have regarding the human culture. An example is the belief about God.
