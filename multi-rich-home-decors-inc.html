3. it is a leaf shaped multi-colored rice paste wafers used to decorate house​


Answer:

I believe you are referring to "kolam" or "rangoli" designs made using rice paste or powder. Kolam is a traditional art form originating from South India and is popularly used to decorate the floors of houses, temples, and public spaces during festive occasions.

Explanation:

The designs are typically made using rice flour or paste, which is mixed with water and colored using natural dyes. The paste is then used to create intricate patterns on the floor or on special kolam boards. The designs can be simple or complex, and are often inspired by nature, geometric shapes, or religious symbols.

The practice of making kolam designs is not only a form of decoration, but also a way to promote creativity, mindfulness, and community spirit. It is often seen as a way to bring good luck and prosperity to the home and its inhabitants.

Indigenous materials are materials such as wood, canes, grass, palms, and rattan that are found naturally and locally in a particular location.

Narra

- In a fine house, a beautifully designed piece of furniture will make all the difference. With the country's large abundance of many types of wood, Narra is one particular wooden material that is best used as home furniture. This may not be recognized by non-locals, but Narra is hailed as the national tree of the region. Narra can be used as furniture by exquisite cabinetwork, solid headboards and beds, and even designed into complex details like this piece above.

Rattan

- Rattan consists of a palm that originated in Southeast Asia with thin pliable stems. Rattan is a very good material, primarily because it is lightweight, strong, ideal for outdoor use, and versatile to some degree. It is a well-loved favorite of homeowners who enjoy pieces of furniture and décor that offer their interiors a touch of indigenous and natural appeal.

Palm

- This range of furniture features palm or buri as seat covers for the sofas, showcasing the idea of a 'banig, which is a handwoven mat commonly used for sleeping and sitting in East Asia and the Philippines. Typically mistaken as a textile, this kind of material is actually made of dried leaves that are often dyed and woven into a mat before being cut into strips.

- Husked, cut, scraped, and harvested from the fruit of the coconut palm, in Philippine architecture, interior design and decoration, coconut shells are now introducing a new incredible trend.
