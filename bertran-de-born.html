7. What is Food Borne, Blood Borne, Droplets, Vector Borne And Air Borne?​


Answer:

Food Borne: Foodborne illness is caused by consuming contaminated foods or beverages. Many different disease-causing microbes or pathogens can contaminate foods, so there are many different types of foodborne illnesses. Most foodborne diseases are infections caused by a variety of bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

Blood Borne: Bloodborne disease, any of a group of diseases caused by pathogens such as viruses or bacteria that are carried in and spread through contact with blood. Common bloodborne diseases include hepatitis B, hepatitis C, and human immunodeficiency virus (HIV).

Vector-Borne: Disease that results from an infection transmitted to humans and other animals by blood-feeding anthropods, such as mosquitoes, ticks, and fleas. Examples of vector-borne diseases include Dengue fever, West Nile Virus, Lyme disease, and malaria.

17. A brother and sister were born in summer and winter . the sister was not born in winter . who was born in summer ?​


A brother and sister were born in summer and winter. The sister was not born in winter. Who was born in summer? Answer: The sister

Explanation:

This is a tricky question or a riddle. Since 'the sister' is already mentioned in the second sentence, you will then choose 'the brother' since it was not yet mentioned. It said that the sister was NOT born in winter so she definitely was born in summer, while the brother was born in winter.

Riddle  

A riddle is a statement, question, or phrase that is intended to make the reader think. A riddle has a valid answer, which the person must arrive at afterthought and logical reasoning. A riddle frequently contains a double or hidden meaning in order to intentionally confuse the reader.
