Answer:

It is a Quadratic Equation

Step-by-step explanation:

Tell whether or not each of the following situations illustrates quadratic equations. A motorcycle driver travels 15 kph faster than a bicycle rider. The motorcycle driver covers 60 km in two hours less than the time it takes the bicycle rider to travel the same distance.

First, consider the given.

Let:

x = speed of the motorcycle driver

y = speed of the bicycle rider

A motorcycle driver travels 15 kph faster than a bicycle rider. Hence:

x = y + 15

The motorcycle driver covers 60 km in two hours less than the time it takes the bicycle rider to travel the same distance.

Since the distance is 60km, the rate by which the motorcycle driver traverse the place can be computed by the following equation:

60km x [tex]\frac{1 hr}{y + 15 km}[/tex] = 2 - (60km x [tex]\frac{1 hr}{y km}[/tex])

Following the computation, we would know that the rate by which the bicycle river traverse the place is:

(60km x [tex]\frac{1 hr}{y + 15 km}[/tex]) + (60km x [tex]\frac{1 hr}{y km}[/tex]) = 2

[tex]\frac{60y+60y+900 }{y^{2}+15y } =2[/tex]

Computing it, we would arrive at the following values:

y = 15;

x = y + 15 --> x = 15 + 15 --> x = 30

It is a quadratic equation with a degree equals to 2.
