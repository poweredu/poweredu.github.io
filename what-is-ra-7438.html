8. explain the salient provision of R.A 7438​


Answer:

Explanation:

Republic Act No. 7438, an Act defining certain rights of people who have been arrested, detained, or are under investigation while they are in custody as well as the obligations of the arresting, detaining, and investigating officers and providing penalties for violations thereof, was passed in 1992. Specifically defining the rights of a person who has been arrested, detained, or is the subject of a custodial investigation, Section 7438 also establishes the obligations of arresting, investigating, and detaining authorities and stipulates the sanctions for their infractions. As used in this Act, "custodial investigation" includes the practice of sending a "invitation" to a person being investigated in relation to a crime they are suspected of having committed, without affecting the "inviting" officer's accountability for any legal violations. 2 Page 5 Section 2.
