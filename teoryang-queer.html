5. HOW IS THE COLOR PURPLE BY ALICE WALKER A QUEER BOOK?​


Answer:

The Color Purple by Alice Walker is a novel that has been widely regarded as a queer book due to its themes of gender and sexuality. The novel follows the story of Celie, a black woman living in the rural South, as she navigates complex relationships with other women and men.

One way in which the novel can be seen as queer is in its depiction of same-sex relationships. Celie has a close and intimate relationship with her friend Shug, and the two women are depicted as loving and supportive of each other. The novel also includes a subplot involving the character Sofia, who has a relationship with another woman while in prison. These relationships challenge traditional notions of gender and sexuality, and their portrayal in the novel can be seen as a celebration of queer love and desire.

Additionally, the novel touches on themes of gender performance and non-conformity. Celie and Shug both challenge traditional gender roles and expectations, and their relationships and experiences can be seen as a critique of the ways in which society enforces and polices gender norms.

Overall, The Color Purple is a novel that offers a nuanced and complex portrayal of gender and sexuality, and its portrayal of same-sex relationships and non-conformity to traditional gender roles has contributed to its reputation as a queer book.

The surfaces of the regular polyhedra (Platonic solids) are spherelike. (If they were made of rubber, you could inflate them into spheres.) The regular polyhedra have the following property: there are numbers n and m such that every face is a regular n-gon and every vertex is of order m. 28. what queer theory in theoretical approach?​


Answer:

Queer theory emphasises the fluid and humanly performed nature of sexuality – or better, sexualities. It questions socially established norms and dualistic categories with a special focus on challenging sexual (heterosexual/homosexual), gender (male/female), class (rich/poor), racial (white/non-white) classifications

Answer:

Queer Theory emphasizes the fluid and humanly performed nature of sexuality/sexualities. It questions social establish norms and dualistic categories with a special focus on challenging sexual ( heterosexual and homosexual ), gender ( male and female ), class ( rich or poor ), racial ( white and non-white ) classification.
