9. Korean Culture/DescriptionFoodClothesReligionBeliefs​


Answer:

Korean culture is a unique blend of ancient traditions and modern influences that have evolved over thousands of years. Here are some key aspects of Korean culture:

Food: Korean cuisine is known for its emphasis on fresh and fermented ingredients, and its bold flavors. Some popular Korean dishes include kimchi, bulgogi, bibimbap, and Korean barbecue. Rice is a staple food, and is often accompanied by a variety of side dishes, known as banchan.

Clothes: Traditional Korean clothing includes the hanbok, a colorful, flowing dress for women, and a shorter, simpler version for men. These days, Western-style clothing is more commonly worn, but the hanbok is still worn on special occasions such as weddings, birthdays, and holidays.

Religion: The two major religions in Korea are Buddhism and Christianity, with smaller numbers of adherents to Confucianism and Shamanism. There is also a significant population of people who do not identify with any particular religion.

Beliefs: Traditional Korean beliefs include a strong emphasis on family, respect for elders, and a deep reverence for nature. Confucianism has also had a strong influence on Korean culture, emphasizing the importance of education, moral values, and social order. Korean folklore is rich with stories of gods, ghosts, and mythical creatures, which are still popular today.

Piña (Tagalog pronunciation: [pɪˈnja] pi-NYAH) is a traditional Philippine fiber made from pineapple leaves. 19. what makes the pina cloth important in our culture?​


Piña Cloth

Piña fabric (pronounced pi-NYAH) is an indigenous woven fabric made from pineapple leaves, specifically the Spanish Red pineapple variety, because of its high tensile strength. It is one of the most well-known traditional textiles in the Philippines, and it was primarily used by Filipino aristocrats as early as the nineteenth century when the country was a Spanish colony.

What makes the Piña cloth important in our culture?​It is often used in the Philippines to make barong tagalog, baro't saya, and other traditional formal wear.Due to its natural luster and airy, breathable quality, pia remains the fabric of choice for formal wear and haute couture, such as pina barong, by Filipinos and Filipino designers alike.The finished pina fabric is translucent, stiff, and naturally glossy. Because the fibres are thin, pineapple thread is frequently combined with silk, polyester, or even abaca to create pia cloth garments, scarves, shawls, handkerchiefs, veils, and other items. The majority of pieces made from Philippine pina cloth are embellished with calado, a traditional hand embroidery technique. As a result, an embroidered pina garment is known as a pia calado.

What are the benefits of using piña fabric in clothing?The breathability of pina fabric is one of its distinguishing features. Because the fabric is naturally lightweight, it is ideal for subtropical climates and the accompanying humid weather. Pina's natural softness and translucence add to the ethereal yet regal appearance. The pina fiber also blends well with other fibers such as silk, abaca, and cotton, allowing for the creation of hybrid fabrics that add a layer of character to the finished cloth. Pina-silk, or pineapple silk, is popular among the upper crust of Philippine society.A finished garment made of pure pina cloth is regarded as a testament to the highest quality of Philippine fabrics and workmanship, making it truly valuable and a family heirloom.

29. PHILIPPINE CULTURE/DESCRIPTIONFOODCLOTHESRELIGIONPRACTICES​


Answer:

Philippine culture is a rich and diverse mix of indigenous, Asian, and Western influences, shaped by centuries of colonialism and cultural exchange.

Food plays a central role in Philippine culture, with a wide variety of dishes reflecting the country's diverse regions and ethnic groups. Some popular Filipino dishes include adobo, sinigang, lechon, pancit, and lumpia. Rice is a staple food, and is often served with a variety of meat, fish, and vegetable dishes.

Traditional Philippine clothing includes the barong Tagalog for men and the terno for women. The barong Tagalog is a lightweight, embroidered shirt made of pineapple or abaca fibers, while the terno is a formal dress featuring butterfly sleeves and a distinctive bell-shaped skirt. Modern Filipino fashion is influenced by Western styles, and many Filipinos wear Western-style clothing on a daily basis.

The Philippines is predominantly a Christian country, with Roman Catholicism as the largest religion, followed by Protestantism and other Christian denominations. Islam and Buddhism are also practiced by some communities, particularly in the southern part of the country.

Many traditional practices and beliefs are still observed in the Philippines. These include the bayanihan or communal spirit, where neighbors help each other in times of need, and the fiesta or community celebration, which is held in honor of a patron saint. Other practices include pagmamano or showing respect to elders by taking their hand and placing it on one's forehead, and pagtatawas or using eggs to diagnose illnesses or bad luck. These practices are often intertwined with religious beliefs and cultural traditions, and continue to be an important part of Philippine culture.
