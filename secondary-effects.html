The secondary and tertiary effects of the hazard in a community or family?​

A natural hazard is a risk that a naturally occurring event will cause harm to humanity. A natural disaster is the term for this detrimental outcome. In other words, we term an occurrence a natural catastrophe when a dangerous threat occurs and causes harm to humanity.

Primary, secondary, and tertiary consequences can occur in any hazardous procedure.

Primary Effects are caused by the process itself. For instance, water damage from a flood or building collapse from an earthquake, landslide, or storm.

Secondary effects only occur as a result of a primary effect. Fires started by earthquakes, loss of electrical power and water service as a result of an earthquake, flood, or hurricane, and flooding caused by landslides are just a few examples.

Long-term effects that are triggered as a result of a primary event are known as tertiary effects. These include things like habitat loss due to a flood, permanent changes in the position of a river channel due to a flood, crop failure due to a volcanic eruption, and so on. Geologic processes affect every human on the planet at all times, but they are most noticeable when they result in the loss of life or property. A natural disaster occurs when the process that poses a hazard occurs and damages human lives or property. Earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, tsunamis, landslides, subsidence, floods, droughts, hurricanes, and tornadoes are among the natural risks and potential disasters to consider. Earthquake is one of the natural hazards.

Throughout Earth's history, all of these processes have been at work.

28. primary, secondary, tertiary effects of tsunami​


Answer:

A tsunami, also known as a seismic sea wave, can have several primary, secondary, and tertiary effects.

Primary Effects:

1. Inundation and Flooding: Tsunamis can cause significant inundation and flooding. particularly in low-lying coastal areas, which can lead to loss of life and property damage.

2. Strong Water Currents: The strong currents generated by tsunamis can cause significant damage to infrastructure, such as bridges, ports, and buildings.

3. Erosion: Tsunamis can cause significant erosion of beaches, shorelines, and riverbanks.

Secondary Effects:

1. Fires: Tsunamis can damage gas lines and electrical infrastructure, leading to fires that can exacerbate the initial damage caused by the waves.

2. Contamination: Tsunamis can also lead to the contamination of water and soil with hazardous materials, such as chemicals and oil.

3. Disease: In the aftermath of a tsunami, there is often a risk of disease outbreaks due to contamination of water sources and the displacement of populations.

Tertiary Effects:

1. Economic Impact: Tsunamis can have a significant impact on local and regional economies, particularly in areas that rely heavily on tourism or fishing.

2. Psychological Impact: Tsunamis can cause significant psychological trauma and stress for survivors and their families, which can have long-lasting effects.

3. Environmental Impact: Tsunamis can have significant environmental impacts, such as the destruction of habitats and the displacement of wildlife.
