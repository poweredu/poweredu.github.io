4. exposition train to busan​


Train to Busan

The Film entitled Train to Busan was a popular Action Horror film from South Korea. The story began when a workaholic father, who was played by the actor Gong Yoo, was on his way to Busan together with his young daughter, who played by Kim Su-an, to see his former wife who is currently residing in the province of Busan. Seok-woo and his daughter together with other passengers rode a bullet train going to Busan when a zombie outbreak begins. All passengers were trapped inside a speeding train and people were infected by the virus carried out by the zombies.

5. what is the climax for train to busan


What is the climax for train to busan

Seok-woo starts to fight the zombified Yon-suk and almost falls off the ledge of the vehicle.  Su-an yells on the other side of the car floor, which prompts Yon-suk and tries to attack Seong-kyeong and Su-an. Seok-woo intervenes, however, and his hand is heavily bitten. He manages to get Yon-suk off the motorcycle, but he discovers his injury. Aware that he'll be turning really fast, he'll sit Su-an and Seong-kyeong in the cockpit and tell them how to use the brakes as fast as they get to Busan. He then tries to leave the car, but Su-an begs him to sit there and reveals that on the day  of her recital, she didn't finish singing Aloha 'Oe because she didn't see Seok-woo in the audience—she saved her father's album. They both have a tearful goodbye, and Seok-woo holds Su-an's little  hands in his for a moment, before tossing them aside and locking himself out of the cockpit.  Su-an starts yelling and crying for him to come back.  

When the virus spreads, Seokwoo sobs when he heads towards the end of the train car, clutching his bloodthirsty side. Su-an keeps weeping for him inside the cockpit, while Seong-kyeong is trying to hold her back.  Slowly, he enters the back of the train car, battling the infection, and starts to think of when Su-an was  a boy. Recalling his best memories of cradling her and grasping her little fingers and toes (as he  had done in the cockpit before), the infection takes hold, her eyes glow and her veins darken. Nevertheless, despite being completely zombified, Seok-woo appears to think of Su-an and smiles  happily to the sky before falling off the car and onto the tracks (it remains unclear as to whether this was  due to his body spasm, or whether he jumped off the car in an act of suicide to keep Su-an and Seong-kyeong safe).

Eventually, the train car arrives at the tunnel leading to Busan station. The track is cut off with rubble,  however. The two of them leave the car and start walking down the tunnel, not realizing that the Busan  army is watching them on the other side. One of the snipers mentions their approach to his superior and is ordered to shoot them down, because they can not tell in the dark whether or not they are infected.  Just as the sniper is about to pull the trigger, Su-an begins to grievously sing Aloha 'Oe to rally the tired  Seong-kyeong to the end of the tunnel. Listening to her tune, the soldiers realize that  they're not infected and run to their aid.

19. setting of train to busan

Explanation:

Train to Busan (Korean: 부산행; Hanja: 釜山行; RR: Busanhaeng) is a 2016 South Korean action horror film[4] directed by Yeon Sang-ho and starring Gong Yoo, Jung Yu-mi, and Ma Dong-seok.[5] The film mostly takes place on a train to Busan as a zombie apocalypse suddenly breaks out in the country and threatens the safety of the passengers.

The film premiered in the Midnight Screenings section of the 2016 Cannes Film Festival on 13 May.[6][7][8][9] On 7 August, the film set a record as the first Korean film of 2016 to break the audience record of over 10 million theatergoers.[10][11] The film serves as a reunion for Gong Yoo and Jung Yu-mi, who both starred in the 2011 film The Crucible. A sequel, Peninsula, was released in South Korea on July 15, 2020.

Plot Summary Seok-woo, a fund manager by profession, isn’t able to spend quality time with his daughter, Su-an. On the eve of her birthday he plans to visit his separated wife in Busan to make it up to Su-an. Their co-passengers on board the Korean High-speed rail from Seoul to Busan include people from different walks of life – a CEO, a husband and pregnant wife, a group of high school kids, a couple of elderly sisters and a homeless man.

The train departs and the journey starts with the arrival of a woman who doesn’t seem normal at the first glance. With a bite wound on her leg, she definitely seems infected by the zombies. Immediately this woman turns into a zombie too. Seeing her, a passing female attendant tries to help her but the woman attacks her too. Soon this zombie infection starts spreading over to the other travellers. The first infected of the lot being the baseball team. But two of the baseball players, Yong-guk, his girlfriend Jin-hee along with two other students save themselves by speeding towards a front car. Before long, the news broadcast about the zombies spreads far and wide throughout the cities in Korea.

30. conflict train to busan​


Train To Busan was one of the biggest box office successes in South Korea’s history, over 10 million people saw it. Key to it’s success is the film’s balance of high-grade, unadulterated action, and its sensitive, layered character work that personalises the unfolding drama through they eyes of a 10-year old girl who vies for the love of her father. These two sides of the coin belie a third: a searing social commentary that critiques the South Korean social hierarchy.
