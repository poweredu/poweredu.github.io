16. identify the following words.  1. Drugs 2. Drugs of Abuse 3. Drug Abuse 4. Drug Dependence 5. Drug Tolerance​


Answer:

Drugs- are substances, not eaten or taken as food, that can be ingested, drank, or injected intravenously and in other mediums.  Drugs are used to prevent sickness and treat symptoms of a disease.

Drugs of Abuse: Refers to substances that are being used illegally. Some of these are alcohol, cocaine, opioids, marijuana, nicotine, and misuse of prescription and over-the-counter drugs.

Drug Abuse: The habit of using illegal drugs or addictive substances.

Drug Dependence: The abuse of drugs and addictive substances over a period of time that led to dependence. It is also called a substance use disorder.

Drug Tolerance: When the body adapts to a certain drug or substance is called drug tolerance. The body gets used to a drug that more amount is needed or a change of medicine is required.

19. write a letter to a drug abuser inform him/her about the effects of drugs in his/her health suggest ways to prevent and control the abuse.(minimum of 10 sentence) ​


Answer:

Dear [Name],

I am writing to you today to express my concern about your drug abuse and the effects it is having on your health. I want to remind you that drug abuse can have a wide range of negative effects on your physical and mental well-being, including damage to your heart, lungs, liver, and brain. It can also lead to addiction, which can be extremely difficult to overcome.

I understand that you may be going through a difficult time in your life and that drugs may seem like an easy solution to your problems. However, I want to remind you that there are other ways to cope with stress and emotional pain that are much healthier and more effective in the long run.

One way to prevent and control drug abuse is to seek professional help, such as counseling or therapy. These types of programs can provide you with the support and guidance you need to overcome your addiction and learn new coping mechanisms for dealing with stress and emotional pain.

Another way to prevent and control drug abuse is to surround yourself with positive influences, such as friends and family who support you and encourage healthy behavior. Avoiding situations and people that might tempt you to use drugs is also important.

It's also important to take care of your physical and mental health through exercise and healthy eating habits.

In addition, there are support groups for people who are struggling with drug addiction, such as Narcotics Anonymous, that can provide you with a sense of community and support as you work towards recovery.

I know that overcoming drug addiction is not easy, but with the right support and resources, it is possible. I care about you and want to see you succeed in overcoming this challenge. Please reach out to me or any other support system, we are here to help you.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]

23. What is the importance of knowing the facts and myth and misconception, aboutdrug use, drug abuse and drugs of abuse?​


Answer:

Myth: “All drugs are addictive”

Fact: Some drugs can create addiction or dependence much quicker than others. There is no evidence that people get ‘hooked’ after one or two uses, or that everyone who tries a drug will become addicted. Using a drug even once can cause serious problems. Addiction depends on the what, who, why, where and how of drug use.
