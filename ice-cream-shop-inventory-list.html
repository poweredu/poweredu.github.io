6. An ice cream shop had 315 customers the first weekend it opened. Two out of every 5 customers bought a small ice cream cone. How many small ice cream cones did the shop sell on the first weekend it opened?


Answer:

Ice Cream Sales: 126

Step-by-step explanation:

If two out of every 5 customers bought a small ice cream cone, then the proportion of customers who bought a small ice cream cone is 2/5.

To find the number of customers who bought a small ice cream cone, we can multiply the total number of customers by this proportion:

Number of customers who bought a small ice cream cone = 2/5 x 315

Simplifying the expression:

Number of customers who bought a small ice cream cone = (2 x 315) / 5

Number of customers who bought a small ice cream cone = 630 / 5

Number of customers who bought a small ice cream cone = 126

Therefore, the ice cream shop sold 126 small ice cream cones on the first weekend it opened.

18. An ice cream shop is testing some new flavors of ice cream. They invent 25 new flavors for customer to try,and throw out the 13 least popular flavors. The shop makes 80 cartons of each of the remaining flavors. It takes 2 cartons to get one liter of ice cream . How much total ice cream is in the cartons


Answer:

480 L

Step-by-step explanation:

Since the shop will throw out the 13 least popular flavors, that will leave us 25 - 13 or 12 left for them to produce.

Total Ice Cream = Flavors * Cartons * (1 Liter / 2 Cartons)

= 12 * 80 * 1 / 2

= 480

Therefore, there is a total of 480L of ice cream.

Solution:

Probability:

So we have 3 types of cones and 5 different flavors of ice-cream.

3*5 = 15 possible ice-cream cone combinations.

Cones = {1,2,3}
Flavor of ice-cream = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5}

So,the possible ice-cream cone combinations are {(1,1), (1,2), (1,3), (1,4), (1,5), (2,1), (2, 2), (2, 3), (2,4), (2, 5), (3,1), (3,2), (3,3), (3,4), (3,5)}.

Thus, we have 15 possible ice-cream cone combinations.
