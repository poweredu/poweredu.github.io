3. What are the consequences of removing fossils from paleontological sites, as in the Araripe plateau?​


Answer:

Explanation:

Removing fossils from paleontological sites, such as the Araripe plateau, can have significant consequences. It can lead to the loss of important scientific information and damage to the delicate ecosystem of the site. The removal of fossils can disturb the surrounding geological layers, making it difficult to understand the geological and environmental conditions in which the fossil lived. It can also make it harder to accurately date the fossil and determine its evolutionary relationships. Additionally, the commercial trade of fossils can encourage illegal activities, such as smuggling and looting, which further contribute to the destruction of paleontological sites. Overall, removing fossils from their original site can have severe negative impacts on scientific research, cultural heritage, and the natural environment.

Answer:

One of the major benefits of the removal of fossils is that it gives us a better understanding of the living things that once lived there, which is very significant for us to understand and know the history of a certain location.

Nonetheless, it is important to use caution when working in these locations because irresponsible fossil removal can cause erosion, alter the local ecosystem, and change the morphologies of the fossils in an unnatural way. It is a specialized job as a result.
