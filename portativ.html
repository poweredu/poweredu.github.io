22. Mr. Portacio bought a piece of residential land in the shape of a rhombus, it's height is 97m, while its base is 108.34m. Mr. Portacio paid ₱500.00 for each square meters. How much did she pay in all???​


Step-by-step explanation:

To find the total amount Mr. Portacio paid for the piece of residential land, we need to find its area first. Since the land is in the shape of a rhombus, its area can be calculated using the formula:

Area = (diagonal 1 x diagonal 2) / 2

To find the diagonals, we can use the Pythagorean theorem. Let's call half of the base length as "a" and the height as "b". Then we can write:

a^2 + b^2 = (108.34 / 2)^2

b^2 = (108.34 / 2)^2 - a^2

b^2 = 2916.3776 - a^2

Since the diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular bisectors of each other, they divide the rhombus into four congruent right triangles. The height of each triangle is b/2, and the base is a, so the area of each triangle is:

(1/2) x a x (b/2) = (ab) / 4

Thus, the area of the rhombus is:

Area = 4 x [(ab) / 4] = ab

Substituting the value of b^2 from above, we get:

Area = a x sqrt(2916.3776 - a^2)

Now we can find the area and the total cost:

Area = a x sqrt(2916.3776 - a^2)

Area = a x 66.573

Area = 108.34/2 x 97

Area = 5254.39 sq.m.

Total cost = Area x ₱500.00/sq.m.

Total cost = 5254.39 sq.m. x ₱500.00/sq.m.

Total cost = ₱2,627,195.00

Therefore, Mr. Portacio paid ₱2,627,195.00 in all for the piece of residential land.

Answer:

UDOL

Explanation:

This dance is classified under Tribal dances. It originated from the Tagakaulo tribe

of southern Davao.This is a ceremonial dance which portrays death and revenge. It opens with three women walking in with votive candles, mourning the loss of a relative. They are followed by men playing the udol, a long wooden musical instrument. The woman make eloquent gestures of tenderness and despair such as wielding a spear and pounding the udol in anger, countering the steady rhythms of the musicians. A male priest then dances, begging the spirits to guide the soul of the deceased. Finally, two warriors enter, spears in hand, performing a frenzied dance in a circle, then disappearing off stage “to the woods,” apparently to secure the heads of their enemies.
