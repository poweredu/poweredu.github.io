4. 1. What are the advantages and disadvantages of vintage cars from the olden days.​


Answer:

Advantages of a historic vehicle

- In many locations you do not have to pay road tax for historic vehicles

- They have to go through MOT (vehicule inspection) less often: every two years if they're between 30 and 40 years old, three years if you are between 40 and 45 years old and four years if over 45 years.

- The vehicule inspections are less complete depending on the type of vehicle being inspected. They can normally be exempted from emissions testing or suspension

- Insurance is cheaper

Disadvantages of a historic vehicle

- The DGT may limit the circulation of a historic vehicle. For example, it may restrict the dates or lanes on which it may travel, if the vehicle cannot exceed 80 km/h. They may also prohibit driving at night or in poor visibility conditions, if it does not have an adequate lighting system. In addition, if you cannot exceed 60 km/h you may not be allowed to drive on the motorway or expressway. If you cannot exceed 40 km/h you must always drive on the hard shoulder or as close to the right as possible if there is no hard shoulder.

- Insurance companies usually impose a limit of 5,000 km per year

- The procedures to convert it into a classic vehicle cost about 600 euros.

- The State could request the car for exhibitions if it has a relevant historical value.

In the case of the Subaru commercial, the company was able to appeal to the emotions of parents by showing a realistic portrayal of a father handing over the car keys to his daughter for the first time. Another advantage of using emotional appeals is that it can help to differentiate the company and its products from competitors. By creating an emotional connection with the audience, the company can stand out in a crowded marketplace and create a sense of loyalty among its customers. This can be especially important for companies that sell products that are seen as similar to those of their competitors, as it can help to establish a unique selling proposition and build a strong brand identity.

However, there are also some potential disadvantages to using emotional appeals to reach customers. One disadvantage is that it can be difficult to predict how the audience will respond to the appeal. Some people may be moved by the emotional appeal, while others may be resistant to it or even turned off by it. This can make it hard for companies to gauge the effectiveness of their emotional appeals and to determine whether they are reaching their target audience effectively.

Another disadvantage of using emotional appeals is that it can be seen as manipulative or insincere if not done correctly. If the company does not genuinely care about the issues it is highlighting or does not have a strong track record of addressing those issues, the emotional appeal may come across as contrived or insincere, which can damage the company's reputation and credibility.

Finally, emotional appeals can be expensive to create and may require significant resources to produce and disseminate. This can be a disadvantage for companies with limited budgets or resources, as it can be difficult to compete with larger companies that have the resources to produce high-quality emotional appeals.
