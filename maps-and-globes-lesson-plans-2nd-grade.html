20. make an deductive and inductive reasoning from grade 8 lessons Last pt for 2nd quarter​


Answer:

Deductive reasoning:

Premise: All living things need energy to survive.

Premise: Plants are living things.

Conclusion: Therefore, plants need energy to survive.

This is an example of deductive reasoning because the conclusion is drawn from the premises that all living things need energy to survive and that plants are living things. The conclusion that plants need energy to survive logically follows from these premises.

Inductive reasoning:

Observation: The last five plants that were given sunlight and water have grown taller.

Conclusion: Giving plants sunlight and water will make them grow taller.

This is an example of inductive reasoning because the conclusion is drawn from a pattern observed in a specific set of data (the last five plants that were given sunlight and water have grown taller). The conclusion that giving plants sunlight and water will make them grow taller is a generalization based on these observations.


21. what are the lines that appear on a map and on a globe ​


Explanation:

Maps and globes usually have lines on them to help locate places on Earth. These lines are called latitude and longitude lines. These lines are not actually on the planet, but are imaginary lines used to help us find our way around the curved surface of Earth.
