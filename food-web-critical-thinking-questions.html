19. Critical thinking question​


Answer:

1. Can abiotic and abiotic factors affect one another?

The significance of abiotic and biotic factors comes in their interaction with each other. For a community or an ecosystem to survive, the correct interactions need to be in place. A simple example would be of abiotic interaction in plants. Water, sunlight and carbon dioxide are necessary for plants to grow.

2. What will happen if huge amounts of rain fall on land?

Flooding is a type of extreme weather. Flooding happens when there is heavy rainfall in a short amount of time. A flood happens when water overflows or soaks land that is normally dry.

3. What if there is no rain for many day, what will happen to the organisms living on land even in bodies of water?

When little or no rain falls, soils can dry out and plants can die. When rainfall is less than normal for several weeks, months, or years, the flow of streams and rivers declines, water levels in lakes and reservoirs fall, and the depth to water in wells increases.

4. What human activities are number one contributing factor to pollution and climate change?

Humans are increasingly influencing the climate and the earth's temperature by burning fossil fuels, cutting down forests and farming livestock. This adds enormous amounts of greenhouse gases to those naturally occurring in the atmosphere, increasing the greenhouse effect and global warming.

5. What human activities are number one contributing factor to polluted water?

Around the world, agriculture is the leading cause of water degradation. In the United States, agricultural pollution is the top source of contamination in rivers and streams, the second-biggest source in wetlands, and the third main source in lakes.

6. What can we do to prevent the effects from the changes in the abiotic factors on the ecosystem?

Good care practices, including using proper techniques when watering, fertilizing, pruning, planting, and mowing, can help prevent abiotic problems.
