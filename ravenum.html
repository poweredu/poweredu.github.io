3. If marginal revenue is equal to zero, then * a. total revenue is zero. b. average revenue is zero. c. total revenue is at a maximum or a minimum. d. average revenue is at a maximum or a minimum.​


Answer:

In this situation, total revenue will be at a maximum or a minimum, depending on the initial level of total revenue. If total revenue is initially at a maximum, then it will remain at that maximum as the quantity of the product being sold increases. If total revenue is initially at a minimum, then it will remain at that minimum as the quantity of the product being sold increases.

It is important to note that if marginal revenue is equal to zero, this does not necessarily mean that average revenue (price) is also equal to zero. Average revenue is the total revenue divided by the quantity of the product being sold, and it can still be positive even if marginal revenue is zero.

Answer:

The correct answer among the given listed choices is letter d. Deferred revenue or unearned revenue is not considered revenue. It is a liability account. Money is already received from the customer or client for a service or product that has yet to perform or deliver. In accounting initial entry, cash is normally debited and unearned revenue is credited since it is a liability that the business need to provide yet a service or product. Definition of Revenues

Revenues are fees earned from providing services and amounts from selling merchandise/goods/products. According to the accrual basis of accounting, revenues should be recorded at the time of delivering the service or the merchandise, even if cash is not received at the time of delivery.

Examples of Revenues

SalesRent revenueDividend revenueInterest revenueProfessional RevenueService Revenue

All type of revenues have normal credit balances. The revenue account will be credited once earned. It should be noted that if the revenues earned are a main activity of the business, they are considered to be operating revenues.
