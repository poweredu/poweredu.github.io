1. Structuration theory giddens and education


Answer:

Anthony Giddens' theory of structuration is a theory of social action, which claims that society should be understood in terms of action and structure; a duality rather than two separate entities.

Central to the theory of structuration, is the idea of structuring social relations across time-space, in virtue of the duality of structure. The theory of structuration has implications for education in that it has pointed to a possible resolution of this dualism.

3. what is the main concern of Gidden's about agency​


Answer:

Giddens argues that just as an individual's autonomy is influenced by structure, structures are maintained and adapted through the exercise of agency. The interface at which an actor meets a structure is termed “structuration.”

7. Using your own words, explain your understanding about the three dimensions of globalization proposed by Anthony Giddens.


Answer:

Anthony Giddens, a sociologist, proposed three dimensions of globalization: disembedding, time-space compression, and reflexivity.

Disembedding refers to the breaking down of social relations from specific places and contexts. In other words, social interactions are no longer tied to specific physical locations or social contexts. This is made possible by technological advancements in communication and transportation, which enable people to interact across vast distances and time zones. Disembedding is evident in the growth of online communities and the increased ease of international travel and communication.

Time-space compression refers to the idea that technology has shrunk the world and made distances between places seem shorter. This means that people are more connected to one another than ever before, and it has led to a greater sense of global interconnectedness. For instance, it is now possible to travel from one end of the world to the other in a matter of hours, and we can communicate with people on the other side of the world instantly.

Reflexivity refers to the way in which globalization has led people to reflect on their own cultural and social identities. As people become more aware of other cultures and ways of life, they start to question their own beliefs and values. Reflexivity has led to the growth of multiculturalism and the recognition of the diversity of human experience.

Overall, Giddens' three dimensions of globalization highlight how technology has changed the way we interact with each other and with the world around us. It has broken down barriers between people and places, and has led to a greater awareness of our shared humanity.
