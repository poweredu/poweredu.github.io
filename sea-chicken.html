Classify the following animals according to the mode of reproduction if it is Asexual Reproduction or Sexual Reproduction= Amoeba= Flatworms= Paramecium= Sharks= Barnacles= Jellyfish= Pythons= Snails= Chickens= Koalas= Rays= Sponges= Coral= Lichens= Salmons= Starfish= Euglena= Pangolin= Sea Anemones= Whales


Answer:

Amoeba - asexual reproduction

Flatworms - both sexually and asexually

Paramecium - asexual reproduction

Sharks - sharks breeding asexually is extremely rare.

Barnacles - Such lonely individuals are believed to reproduce by self-fertilization

Jellyfish - Throughout their lifecycle, jellyfish take on two different body forms: medusa and polyps. Polyps can reproduce asexually by budding, while medusae spawn eggs and sperm to reproduce sexually.

Pythons - asexual reproduction

Snails - sexual reproduction

Chicken - sexual reproduction

Koalas - sexual reproduction

Rays - Sharks and rays don't reproduce like other fish. Males have two organs called claspers attached to their anal fins.

Sponge - both asexually and sexually, Asexual reproduction occurs by budding. Figure below shows the sponge life cycle when sexual reproduction is involved.

Corals - Corals can reproduce asexually and sexually. In asexual reproduction, new clonal polyps bud off from parent polyps to expand or begin new colonies. ... Along many reefs, spawning occurs as a mass synchronized event, when all the coral species in an area release their eggs and sperm at about the same time.

Linchens - Lichens reproduce sexually via spores.

Salmon - sexual reproduction

Starfish - Asexual reproduction

Euglena - asexual reproduction

Pangolin - Pangolins are dioecious organisms, meaning male sex organs are found only on males and female sex organs are only found on females. ... To attract a mate, males will mark their location with urine or feces, letting the females use their strong sense of smell to locate them.

Sea Anemones - asexual reproduction

Whales - sexual meiosis.
