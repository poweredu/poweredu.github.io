[tex]____________________________________[/tex]

A daughter going at 15m/s increases its velocity at 25m/s in 4seconds. What is it's acceleration?

Solution

List down the given data:

Initial Velocity (Vi) - 15 m/sFinal Velocity (Vf) - 25 m/sTime (t) - 4 seconds

Fornula to be used:

[tex]\tt A = \frac{vf - vi}{t} [/tex]Solving for:

Acceleration

[tex]\tt A = \frac{vf - vi}{t} [/tex]

[tex]\tt \: A = \frac{25 \: m/s - 15 \: m/s}{4 \: s} [/tex]

[tex]\tt \: A = \frac{10 \: m/s}{4 \: s} [/tex]

[tex]\tt A = \tt \: \boxed{2.5 \: m/s {}^{2} }[/tex]

Answer

- Therefore, the acceleration is 2.5 m/s².

Answer:

"Fishers of men" is a phrase used in the gospels to describe the mandate given by Jesus to his first disciples to commence his preaching ministry.  Fishers of men is someone who is called to help others become followers of Christ or transform them to the truth, the way, and the life. "Fishers" means the preacher and "men" are the followers or Christians.  Therefore, the accurate meaning of the phrase may be described as working to the world to save humanity about their spiritual salvation. Unlike the actual process of fishing, where the fish is taken out of the sea of life, here humanity is saved from the sea of sins and renewed in body, mind, and spirit through the waters of Baptism, which is done by none other than the ‘fishers of men’ who are empowered by none other than Christ Jesus. This is the love of the Lord, for when you decide to follow His will, He blesses you with a gift of transformation, such that it is not only you who sees the light, but you become the light for others to guide them through darkness.

Answer:

The history of cartography traces the development of cartography, or mapmaking technology, in human history. Maps have been one of the most important human inventions for millennia, allowing humans to explain and navigate their way through the world. The earliest surviving maps include cave paintings and etchings on tusk and stone, followed by extensive maps produced by ancient Babylon, Greece and Rome, China, and India. In their most simple form maps are two dimensional constructs, however since the age of Classical Greece maps have also been projected onto a three-dimensional sphere known as a globe. The Mercator Projection, developed by Flemish geographer Gerardus Mercator, was widely used as the standard two-dimensional projection of the earth for world maps until the late 20th century, when more accurate projections were formulated. Mercator was also the first to use and popularise the concept of the atlas as a collection of maps
