The amylase is an ENZYME. The key phrase in your question is increased the rate at which starch is broken down. By nature, enzymes are compounds called catalysts. Catalysts are materials that enhance the rate at which a reaction occurs without using up or incorporating themselves in the products. Amylase, or salivary amylase, is an enzyme found in your saliva. This is the first chemical digestion (via hydrolysis) that occurs in your body. This is why starch is readily and broken once you chew your favorite biscuit or dough.

15. Explain how amylase speeds up hydrolysis of starch. ​


Answer:

In the presence of amylase, a sample of starch will be hydrolyzed to shorter polysaccharides, dextrins, maltose, and glucose. The extent of the hydrolysis depends on how long it is allowed to react – if the starch is hydrolyzed completely, the resulting product is glucose.

Answer:

Explanation:

Starch is classified as a polysaccharide comprised of glucose. 60% of the carbohydrates being consumed by humans are starch. Digestion of starch starts in the mouth where it is broken down by salivary amylase. The second stage of digestion of starch occurs in the intestine.

In case, the starch is not digested or fully hydrolyzed, these are the clinical consequences:

The undigested starch will pass through the large intestine.Gas will be produced which will result in bloated feeling. Some bacteria which can be found in the large intestine will use the carbohydrate to produce carbon dioxide and methane gas. Diarrhea and DehydrationThe gas and water in the large intestine will make you feel pain. You will also feel tired/lethargic because the energy production from starch did not happen.
