"While the Auto Waits" is a short story by O. Henry, and the main action revolves around the character of Jimmy Valentine, a notorious safe-cracker, who has just been released from prison.

Jimmy's objective is to leave his criminal past behind and start afresh, living a normal life. He wants to be a respectable member of society and earn an honest living. However, he finds it difficult to do so, as his reputation precedes him, and people are not willing to trust him.

While he is waiting for his train at a small town, he comes across a young woman, Annabel Adams, who is stranded and unable to start her car. At her home, Jimmy is faced with a new challenge. He discovers that the bank in the town has installed a new safe, and the locals are confident that no one can break into it. However, Jimmy is tempted by the challenge and decides to attempt to crack the safe.

Through Jimmy's attempts to crack the safe, we see his internal conflict between his desire to leave his criminal past behind and his skill and inclination towards safecracking. He wants to prove to himself and others that he can be trusted and live an honest life, but he also feels drawn to his criminal past and the thrill of cracking the safe.

The climax of the story comes when Jimmy succeeds in opening the safe, but he is caught by a local police officer. However, his newfound friendship with Annabel and her family leads to a twist ending, where the police officer agrees to let him go in exchange for his promise to leave town and start anew.

Overall, the main action of the story is Jimmy's attempt to leave his criminal past behind and start afresh, and the internal conflict he faces when he is tempted to use his safecracking skills again. The plot revolves around his character's objectives of earning an honest living and proving himself to be trustworthy, while also being drawn towards his criminal past.
