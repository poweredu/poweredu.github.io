11. coclusion of hermes story​


Answer:

Hermes was the ancient Greek god of trade, wealth, luck, fertility, animal husbandry, sleep, language, thieves, and travel. One of the cleverest and most mischievous of the Olympian gods, he was the patron of shepherds, invented the lyre, and was, above all, the herald and messenger of Mt. Olympus so that he came to symbolise the crossing of boundaries in his role as a guide between the two realms of gods and humanity. To the Romans, the god was known as Mercury. Hermes has a very long history, being mentioned in the Linear B tablets of the Mycenaean civilization, at its height from the 15th to 13th century BCE. Such tablets have been discovered at Pylos, Thebes, and Knossos. With origins, then, as an Arcadian fertility god who had a special love for the Peloponnese, the ancient Greeks believed Hermes was the son of Zeus and the nymph Maia (daughter of the Titan Atlas) and that he was born on Mt. Cyllene in Arcadia. In mythology, Hermes was also the father of the pastoral god Pan and Eudoros (with Polymele), one of the leaders of the Myrmidons, although the god was not given a wife in any Greek myth. The idea that Hermes represented movement is reflected in his role as the leader of both the Nymphs and Graces (Charites). Noted for his impish character and constant search for amusement, Hermes was one of the more colourful gods in Greek mythology. While still a baby, he stole his half-brother Apollo's herd of 50 sacred cattle from Pieria, cleverly reversing their hoofmarks by adding bark shoes to make it difficult to follow their tracks. Hermes, therefore, became associated with thieves and he managed to keep the stolen herd of cattle until the satyrs finally discovered it in a cave in Arcadia. After a hearing before Zeus and the Olympian gods, Hermes was permitted to hold on to the herd (now down to 48 as he had already sacrificed two of them) if he gave Apollo his lyre. The episode illustrates the god's link with both physical and moral boundaries and crossing them, and may have a basis in historical events, as here described by the famed expert on Greek mythology Robert Graves: As messenger and herald, particularly for Zeus, Hermes is involved in many mythological episodes. Perhaps most celebrated was his killing of the many-eyed (some accounts say 100-eyed) monster Argos on the orders of Zeus in order to free Io. Hermes also freed Ares from his year-long imprisonment in a cauldron by the twin Giants Otus and Ephialtes. One of his most famous regular roles was as a leader of souls to the river Styx in the underworld, where the boatman Charon would take them to Hades. Hermes was also known as something of a trickster, stealing at one time or another Poseidon's trident, Artemis' arrows, and Aphrodite's girdle.

24. how did hermes and athena help perseus?


Hermes gave him a sword that was strong enough and hard enough to kill Medusa, the Gorgon.

Athena gave him a shield that was polished to mirror brightness, which would allow him to avoid looking directly at Medusa (which would turn him to stone), and instead use the reflection in the shield to direct his slashes and stabs with the sword, allowing him to behead Medusa without being petrified.

Answer:

mercury

sana makatulong

Answer:B. Mercury

Explanation:

Si Hermes ay tinatawag na Mercury ng mga Romano. Makilala si Hermes o Mercury sa sapatos nitong may pakpak na nakatutulog sa diyos na lumipad. Gamit ang mga sapatos na ito si Hermes ay nagdadala ng mensahe sa iba pang mga diyos at diyosa.

Mars - Si Mars ay tinatawag na Ares ng mga Griyego. Siya ang diyos ng digmaan.

Neptune - Si Neptune ay kilala bilang si Poseidon. Siya ay ang diyos ng tubig at dagat.

Vulcan - Si Vulcan ay tinatawag na Hephaestus ng mga Griyego. Siya ang diyos ng apoy at ang taga-gawa ng mga armas ng diyos.
