It is characterized by imitations of sounds from nature and the environment. It is also highly influenced by the native language of the province. A. Cordillera Music C. Palawan Music B. Mindoro Music D. Visayan Music

The answer is letter C. Palawan Music.

Musical Context The subtle threads between Mankind and Nature include all our senses and, more importantly, our auditory sensibility. Capturing tonalities, patterns, and melodies, we can like a bird come down from the heavenly vault to the Earth, the dwelling-place of the Highlander-islanders known as the Palawan, who live in the southern part of the island with the same name. This aural journey will carry us to the domain of language, poetry, and music as human inventions that the Palawan uses to connect in order to survive in peace in this world.

Music in Ritual - The shaman sings the complicated experience of the journey of his double, kuruduwa, by a particular tune, the lumbaga, whose melody is at all points assimilated to some epic melodic line. And it is precisely the ordeals that the spirit of the shaman overcomes in the course of his journey—the meetings with the Wrong Doers, Länggam or Säqitan, the conversations, the agreements with the Unseen Beings—that constitute a shamanic song.

Music in Ritual- To sing tultul is to be possessed by the Täw Tultulän. These "Epic Heroes" are a form of human being who lives in the median space and intercedes between the people of this earth and the Ämpuq. They are the Compassionate Humanity that preserves the "True Citizens." The act of singing thus doubles with the embodiment, in the very person of the bard, of these heroic and semi-divine creatures. This relationship may be viewed as an act of ownership in which the bard becomes a medium. • Epics are often performed at night, stopping at dawn; it is prohibited to sing while the sun rises and throughout the day.

Music in Ritual-  The Palawan Island is found in the Central West Highlands. TAGBANUAS is one of the tribes in Palawan. The Tagbanuas also preserved their ancient culture. The Party of the Calamians of the Islands who are engaged in funeral festivities. Five days after the interment, the family went to the deceased's home to conduct final rites. Then the participants sang the Batac, a long song recounting the valuable adventures of the amythic individual called DUMARACOL. The singing goes on for three consecutive nights till dawn.
