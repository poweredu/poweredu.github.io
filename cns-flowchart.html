6. What is CNS and PNS?


Answer:

2 Major divisions of Nervous System:

I. Central Nervous System

- which is made up of Brain and Spinal Cord. It's main purpose is integrating the sensory information that the Peripheral System collects from all over the body, and responding to it by coordinating both conscious and unconscious activity.

II. Peripheral Nervous System

- which consists of nerves that run throughout the body. This system itself is made up of 2 subdivisions:

         A. Somatic Nervous System

   - which contains nerves that carry sensory signals from the body to the Central Nervous System and nerves that carry motor signals from the Central Nervous System to skeletal muscles. This is associated with voluntary movement.

         B. Autonomic Nervous System

  - is sometimes called the Involuntary Nervous System, and it is involved in regulating the internal environment of the body. It carries signal from Central Nervous system to the internal organs. In this way, it is involved in regulating things like digestion and heartbeat, which are generally outside the realm of conscious control. The Autonomic Nervous System can be further subdivided into:

                1.  Sympathetic Nervous System

-plays a large role in stimulating and mobilizing energy resources. It is often described as being involved in fight or flight responses.

         2. Parasympathetic Nervous System

- acts to conserve energy. It is described as being involved in rest and digest responses.

30. what is the cns composed of


The central nervous system is composed of the brain and the spinal cord. Nerves and all other ganglia outside of the brain and spinal cord all belong to another division called peripheral nervous system.

The central nervous system, especially the brain, is the main processing unit of the nervous system. This division of the nervous system is responsible for producing the signals related to responses on stimuli and regulation of the bodily processes.

The brain is responsible for processing the signals from the nerves and formulating a response to that stimuli. The signal for the response is relayed by the spinal cord to the PNS to initiate the response to the stimuli. It is important to know though that the spinal cord is not just limited to relaying signals from the nerves to the brain and vice versa. The spinal cord is also capable of processing information without relaying to the brain, this is called a reflex action.
