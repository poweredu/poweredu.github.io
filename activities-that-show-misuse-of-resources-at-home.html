3. Example of activities that show sustainability use of resources at home.Example of activities that show misuse (to use incorrectly) of resources at home.


Few examples of activities that show sustainability use of resources at home are:

1. Conserving water by turning off water after use, minimizing the opening of faucet to minimize the amount of running water and using a glass when brushing teeth to minimize the unused running water.

2. Conserving energy by turning off unused lights, using solar chargeable lights, and unplugging appliances when not used.

3. Using food container in packing food rather than using plastic bags.

4. Using metal or wood straws instead of plastic straws.

5. Collecting used plastic bottles and making as decorative plant pots.

6. Used papers can be soaked in water and mold them into pieces to be used as charcoal.

Few examples of activities that show misuse of resources at home

1. Turning on all (used of unused) lights and appliances.

2. Usage of plastic cups, straws, and disposable container.

Natural resources are those that the planet offers without the need for human intervention. They are essential our survival, but if they are consumed at a faster rate than their natural regeneration, as is currently the case, they can be exhausted. Then, we review the consequences and possible solutions to this problem.

WHAT ARE NATURAL RESOURCES

There are two types of natural resources: renewable and non-renewable. The former are inexhaustible, like solar radiation, or their renewal is relatively rapid, as is the case with biomass. Non-renewable resources are those that exist in nature in a limited way because their regeneration involves the passage of many years, such as minerals and fossil fuels — oil, natural gas and coal —.

Human beings are depleting the planet's natural resources and standards of living will begin to decline by 2030 unless immediate action is taken. The World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF) warns that the current overexploitation of natural resources is generating an enormous deficit, as 20% more than can be regenerated is consumed each year and this percentage is growing steadily.

Thus, if we continue at this rate, we would need 2.5 planets to supply ourselves in 2050, according to the WWF itself. In turn, this organization shows that the world's population of fish, birds, mammals, amphibians and reptiles declined by 58 % between 1970 and 2012 due to human activities and predicts that by 2020 this percentage will soar to 67%.

Activities that show sustainable use of resources:

1. Turning off lights and faucets when not in use.

2. Buying zero-waste products.

3. Using ecobags when buying from the market.

4. Recycling bottles.

5. Walking when my destination is not far away.

Activities that show misuse of resources:

1. Use of too much plastics.

2. Buying stuff that are not recyclable.

3. Ordering from food deliveries.

4. Using of laundry individually, and not for the family (waste of water and electricity).

5. Stocking food which ends up forgotten and will be thrown away.

My Pledge of Commitment:

After seeing the activities that show misuse of resources, I realize that even though I am doing my best to live sustainably, there are still some things that are not suitable for sustainable living. Upon knowing these, I pledge to: avoid using too much plastics and always bring ecobags or paper bags; buy stuff that can be recycled easily; try my best to order food physically to avoid carbon footprint for the delivery; use laundry wisely; and mind the food we buy.

Also, I pledge to continue doing the sustainable activities I did before.
