Eukaryotes are organisms with a nucleus contained in a nuclear envelope. They belong to the domain Eukaryota or Eukarya, and their name originates from the Greek (eu, "well" or "good") and (karyota, "cell") (karyon, "nut" or "kernel"). Eukaryotes is one of the three domains of life, with bacteria and archaea (the prokaryotes) making up the other two. The eukaryotes are now commonly thought to have originated in the Archaea or to be the sister archaea of the now-cultivated Asgard archaea. Eukaryotes make up a small percentage of all creatures, but their global biomass is estimated to be roughly equivalent to that of prokaryotes due to their relatively bigger size. During the Proterozoic eon, eukaryotes first appeared around 2.1–1.7 billion years ago, most likely as flagellated phagotrophs.

13. complex feeding interrelationship between organisms​


Answer:

Interrelationships between living organisms and their environment are complex and interdependent. All organisms are part of complex food webs that include both plants and animals. The predator-prey inter-relationship is common. Some species are highly dependent on others (for example, pandas or koalas can eat only certain species of grasses/trees) while others are so closely linked that their survival may be dependent on just a single species (for example, there exists a species of wasp that nests only in the fruit of a type of fig tree and is the only insect that can pollinate the flowers of the tree).
