Meter

Explanation:

The folksong Sakura has a quadruple meter. The time signature of the folksong is a 4/4. It means that there are 4 beats in a measure and so it is quadruple.

The folksong Mo Li Hua has a duple meter. The time signature of the folk song is a cut time or 2/2. It means that there are 2 beats in a measure and so it is duple.

The folksong Arirang has a triple meter. The time signature of the folksong is a 3/4. It means that there are 3 beats in a measure and so it is triple.

Melody: the main melody of this song begins at 0.24 after a solo by the traditional Japanese Koto. The melodic line is made up of fourteen bars and nine phrases (seen in Image A). The melodic line consists of conjunct notes, as notes typically move by one tone. Aside from the fourth, sixth, and eighth bar when the notes move two tones.

Metre: This piece is in duple metre 4/4, and moves at a very slow tempo, adagio. There are sections in the piece where the rhythms are very simple and times when they can be more complex. In the main melodic line (0:23-1:39 part A in form chart) the notes and rhythms are conjunct and simple, with quarter notes and eighth notes.
