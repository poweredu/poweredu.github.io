a butterfly has a line of symmetry. This means that if you were to draw a line down the center of the butterfly's body, both halves would be nearly identical or mirror images of each other. This line of symmetry can be observed not only in the body but also in the wings of a butterfly. This symmetry is an important feature of the butterfly's anatomy, and it helps it to fly and maneuver effectively.

11. Have an outer covering called exoskeleton​


Insects do not have bones , but instead have a hard outer covering known as an exoskeleton. This exoskeleton protects their organs and gives them support for moving around. Their bodies are divided into three sections: the head, the thorax and the abdomen.


12. Why does the exoskeleton of insects and other arthropods need to be molted periodically?


Answer:Since the exoskeleton is hard and its outer layer is non-living, it cannot grow bigger by small increments as the human skeleton does. Instead, arthropods must go through the delicate process of shedding the old exoskeleton and expanding to a larger size before the new exoskeleton hardens.

Arthropods must shed their rigid exoskeleton in order to grow. A new soft exoskeleton expands before it hardens so the animal within has room to grow.
