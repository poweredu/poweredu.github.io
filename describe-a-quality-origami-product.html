3 Main Types of Origami FoldsValley Fold- The valley fold is the primary of all origami folds. The valley fold derives its name from the fact truth it causes the paper to sink into a river valley. A valley fold is illustrated by hasten lines in a classic origami diagram. Simply fold a portion of the paper forwards you and crease along the rush line to form a valley fold.Mountain Fold- The flipside of the valley drove is the mountain fold. The name "mountain fold" comes from the truth that it causes the paper to rise up like a mountain. A mountain fold is impersonated by a hasten and dot line in a conventional origami outline. An arrow indicating the folding direction is sometimes included as well.Squash Fold- The squash fold is a simple method for "squashing" your paper into place. It's a compound fold, which is a collection of creases that are all done at the same time. You will be pre-creasing the model, as shown in the photo. Then, using your instructions as a guide, collapse it into the shape indicated. From a center point, two layers of paper open up.Who created modern Origami?Akira Yoshizawa- Akira Yoshizawa, known as the Grandmaster of origami, was a Japanese origamist. He is recognized for turning origami into a living art form. He constructed more than 50,000 models, according to his own estimate from 1989, of which only a few hundred were published as diagrams in his 18 books. Throughout his career, Yoshizawa served as an international cultural ambassador for Japan.
