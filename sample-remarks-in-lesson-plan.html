5. Sample opening remarks for linggo ng kabataan doc


Answer:

LINGGO NG KABATAAN

No Comments0

The Sangguniang Kabataan Pederasyon – Palo Chapter Council, celebrated the Linggo ng Kabataan on August 19 to August 24, 2019. The Linggo ng Kabataan celebration serves to commemorate the significance and rights of the young Filipino people, as mandated through Section 30 of the Republic Act No. 10742, better known as the “Sangguniang Kabataan Reform Act of 2015”.

In line with the celebration of the International Youth Day, the Linggo ng Kabataan 2019, with the theme: “Transforming Education”, aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promotion of lifelong learning opportunities for all.

The Sangguninag Kabataan Pederasyon of Palo, in collaboration with the Local Government Unit of Palo, headed by the Municipal Mayor, Hon. Frances Ann B. Petilla, Department of Health, PNP-Palo, and the Rural Health Office spearheaded a School-to-School Caravan with the activity slogan, “I KNOW RIGHT!”. Symposiums were held regarding Dengue Prevention, Sexually Transmitted Infections, HIV and AIDS, Depression and Suicide, Teenage Pregnancy, and the Anti-Hazing Law.

The said symposiums were conducted in several schools in the municipality, namely: Anahaway National High School, San Joaquin National High School, Palo National High School, and Palo National High School – Annex, attended by students from the Grades 11 and 12.

As part of the Pederasyon’s mandate and responsibilities which is to convey legitimate and valid information, dynamic personalities and resource speakers coming from the Rural Health Unit of Palo and the Department of Health were invited and were the ones who gave the lectures at the symposium.

This kind of activity is geared to apprise the youth of their rights and privileges and to instill proper awareness leading to a more safe, harmonious and peaceful community. And making them active members and partners of the municipality.

13. Sample closing remarks for nutrition celebration


Team Care

For healthy women or infants, the minimal team comprises the patient and her health care provider (physician or midwife). The efforts of nurses, dietitians, social workers, and health educators increase the potential to address common problems that interfere with normal nutrition; they can also increase the patient's understanding of healthful diets and how to achieve them. If a dietitian is not a member of the practice or of the health team, the committee recommends that an active, formalized relationship be established with a dietitian for periodic in-service education, consultation about patient care, and information about useful nutrition-related resources.

A coordinated, multidisciplinary health care team is essential to deal with complex nutrition problems. Depending on the nature of the problem, such a team would ordinarily include the physician, nurses, a dietitian, and an assortment of other providers such as a pharmacist, social worker, breastfeeding specialist, and physical therapist. For families that have difficulty dealing with the medical system because of language or cultural barriers, learning disorders, or other reasons, a trained peer counselor may help serve as a bridge between them and the rest of the health care team

If you think that "impromptu" looks like a relative of "prompt," you're right; both are ultimately derived from the Latin promere, meaning "to bring forth, take out." "Impromptu" was borrowed from French, where it meant "extemporaneously," but French speakers picked it up from the Latin phrase in promptu, a "promere" descendant meaning "in readiness" or "at hand." 28. Can you give me a sample of closing remarks for nutrition month celebration with the theme "Ugaliing magtanim sapat na nutrition aanihin.


Good morning everyone. Today is the time that we wrap up another successful celebration of our nutrition month with the theme “Ugaliing magtanim, sapat na nutrisyon ay aanihin.” This topic is very timely, as obtaining the right amount of nutrients especially for children is becoming more and more ardous.


With the advent of technology, comes the busy and fast-pace lifestyle that a modern Juan dela Cruz is experiencing.

People become more and more ensnared with this kind of cycle that they forget which things are more important. Children tend to get more obese, as they prefer fast food rather than fruits and vegetables. Junk foods like chips and soda with lower nutritional values are becoming easier to access. But, we shouldn’t despair as there’s still time to change that. As aligned with the theme that we have this year, we are encouraging families to plant and cultivate their own fruits and vegetables in their backyards. The best part is it is FREE! It will bring not only monetary savings, but we are also sure that what we eat is chemical free and organic. “We are what we eat”, afterall.


In summary, we are well-aware that this will not be easy. This journey is something that should be done with teamwork. But, it will surely be worth it. The benefits that will be reaped will be for the whole family. So again, I encourage everyone to start this wonderful practice. May this serve as an inspiration for all. Thank you very much!
