14. How many moles are in 2.47grams Calcium oxide?


Mole Conversion

There are 0.04 moles in 2.47 grams of Calcium oxide.

Explanation:

Mole is an SI unit that is used to measure the amount of substance. It is comparable to other counting units such as dozen, ream, or pair. It is a convenient way of expressing large numbers of atoms or molecules. In order to convert mass to moles, we need the molar mass of the substance. This will serve as the conversion factor. The molar mass is the mass of a substance in one mole. The unit of molar mass is g/mol.

In the given question, the substance we are trying to solve is calcium oxide. Calcium oxide has a chemical formula of CaO.

To get the molar mass of calcium oxide, we get the sum of the atomic masses  (g/mol) of elements. The atomic masses can be found in the periodic table of elements. For the calcium oxide, below are the atomic masses of the elements:

            Ca - 40.08 g/mol

             O - 16 g/mol

Then, we multiply these atomic masses by the number of atoms present. In the compound calcium oxide, there are only 1 atom of calcium and 1 atom of oxygen, thus:

             Ca - 40.08 g/mol x 1 = 40.08 g/mol

             O - 16 g/mol x 1          = 16 g/mol

We, then get the sum of these to solve for the molar mass:

       40.08 g/mol + 16 g/mol  = 56.08g/mol CaO

The molar mass of calcium oxide will be used as the conversion factor to convert mass to moles. There are two ways to express the conversion factor. These can be:

          [tex]\frac{56.08g}{1 mol}[/tex]   or  [tex]\frac{1 mol}{56.08g}[/tex]

To convert mass to mole, we will be using the second conversion factor above, which is [tex]\frac{1 mol}{56.08g}[/tex]. The reason to use this conversion factor is so that we can cancel out mass (g)  leaving us with mole. The given mass of the calcium oxide is 2.47g. This mass will be multiplied by the compound's molar mass to get the mole. To show computation:

             2.47 g x [tex]\frac{1mol}{56.08g\\}[/tex] = 0.04404422 mol

We can round this off to 0.044 mol of CaO. Thus, based on the computation, there are 0.044 moles in 2.47 g of calcium oxide.

19. what is the oxidation number of Cl in calcium perchlorate?


Answer:

Ca(ClO4)2 is a neutral molecule, therefore has a net oxidation state of zero.

Ca is a group 2 metal, therefore has an oxidation state of (+2)

(ClO4)- has a net oxidation state of (-1), which is the same as its ionic charge. Oxygen (O), for the most part, has an oxidation state of (-2). As there are four oxygen atoms, the net oxidation state is (-2) x 4 = (-8). Therefore chlorine (Cl) has an oxidation state of (-1) - (-8) = (+7).
