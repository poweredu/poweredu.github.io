Answer:

In general, atoms are most stable, least reactive, when their outermost electron shell is full. Most of the elements important in biology need eight electrons in their outermost shell in order to be stable, and this rule of thumb is known as the octet rule.

An atom is stable when:

D. having 8 valence electrons

Explanation:

Atom is a basic unit of matter, consisting of an atomic nucleus and a cloud of negatively charged. The atomic nucleus consists of protons positively charged neutrons (except in the nucleus of the Hydrogen-1, which has no neutrons). The electrons in an atom are bound to the atomic nucleus by electromagnetic forces. Such a group of atoms can also bond to each other, and form a molecule. Atoms containing the same number of protons and electrons are neutral, while those containing a different number of protons and electrons are positive or negative and are called ions.

Atoms are grouped according to the number of protons and neutrons in the atomic nucleus. The number of protons in an atom determines the chemical element of the atom, and the number of neutrons determines isotope of that element. A stable atom is an atom in which all of its orbitals are occupied by electrons so that none of the orbitals are vacant. Fully filled orbitals indicate the stability of an atom because its structure will not change easily.  

Examples of stable atoms are He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, and atoms unstable are Na, Cl, O and others. A stable is an atom that satisfies the octet or duplet rule so that it is difficult to bond like He and Ne. The configuration of an atom is said to be stable if it meets the duplet or octet rule where the atom has electrons .
