14. a meter stick is pivoted at the 0.50m line. A 3.0 kg object is hung from the 0.15m line. Where should a 5.0 kg object be hung to achieve equilibrium (the meter stick oriented horizontal and motionless)?


This topic is about moment of force  or torque applied to an object. Imagine it as a swinging or twisting force (like a seesaw). In our problem, first thing you must do is draw the scenario, which I attached a photo for your reference.

The formula for moment or torque  (T) is equal to force (F) multiplied by the radius or arm (r) .

T = F x r

Based on the drawing I attached, there is a 1m long stick and its fulcrum is at 0.5m of exactly in the middle of it. This fulcrum or pivot is your reference point. In order for us to achieve an equilibrium or zero sum of forces or steady stick, we must balance the stick (like a seesaw). T₁ is the torque from the 5kg object and T₂ is the torque from the 3kg object.

T₁ = T₂
F₁ x r₁ = F₂ x r₂
5kg x r₁ = 3kg x 0.15m
5kg x r₁ = 0.45 >>> divide 5 to both sides of the equation to get the answer
r₁ = 0.45 / 5kg
r₁ = 0.9m
Answer is the 5kg must be placed 0.9m from the pivot line.
