10. what hans christian oersted contributed


What hans christian oersted contributed

A Danish physicist and chemist who discovered that the electrical current in a wire could deflect a  magnetized compass needle, a phenomenon whose value was quickly recognized and which influenced the development of electromagnetic theory.

During the evening lecture in April 1820, Ørsted discovered that a magnetic needle perpendicularly aligns  itself with a current-carrying thread, a definite experimental proof of the relationship between electricity and magnetism.  

In 1824, he founded a society dedicated to  the propagation of scientific knowledge to the general public.  Since 1908, the company has been awarded the  Ørsted Medal for exceptional achievements by Danish physical scientists.  In the early 1930s, the term oerced was adopted for  the physical unit of magnetic field strength in the centimeter-gram-second system.

12. contributions of the scientist ampere,faraday,hertz,maxwell,and oersted


Answer:

a.) Ampere

    André-Marie Ampère was the first to show that a magnetic field is produced when two parallel wires are charged with electricity. He also created the astatic needle, a crucial part of the current astatic galvanometer. He made the ground-breaking finding that an electric current-carrying wire may pull or pull away from an adjacent wire that also carries an electric current. Although the attraction is magnetic, magnets are not required to witness the effect.

b.) Faraday

    He created the electric motor in 1821, and in 1831, after learning about electric current induction, he created the first dynamo, also known as the Faraday disc, which served as a precursor to the modern electrical generator. He also made significant contributions to the electromagnetic theory of light, which may not be as well recognized. The magneto-optical effect, sometimes referred to as the Faraday effect, was first observed by Michael Faraday in 1845 and is a phenomenon that affects polarized light.

c.) Hertz

    James Clerk Maxwell's electromagnetic waves were expected to exist, and Heinrich Hertz, a great German scientist, and experimentalist proved that they do. He made the important discovery of radio waves, which was widely seen as supporting James Clerk Maxwell's electromagnetic theory and opened the door to several developments in communication technology. He published his findings in the physics journal Annalen der Physik in 1887 on the photoelectric effect and the generation and reception of electromagnetic (EM) waves. His receiver was made comprised of a coil and a spark gap so that when EM waves were detected, a spark would be seen.

d.) Maxwell

    One of the finest physicists of the 19th century was James Clerk Maxwell. He is well recognized for developing the theory of electromagnetism and for establishing the relationship between electromagnetic waves and light. Electromagnetic waves and charges were both connected in Maxwell's electromagnetism theory. He observed that a fluctuating electric field, in turn, results in a fluctuating magnetic field due to an oscillating charge. These two dynamic fields will continue to feed off of one another.

e.) Oersted

    A Danish physicist named Hans Christian Oersted discovered that electricity and magnetism are related around 1820. Oersted demonstrated the ability to move electrons to generate a magnetic field by setting up a compass through a wire carrying an electric current. He established a group in 1824 to educate people about science. Along with his production of metallic aluminum in 1825, he made a significant addition to chemistry by discovering pipeline, one of the pepper's pungent ingredients.


13. how do you relate oersted's law to the operation of electric motor?​


Answer:

The operation of electric motors is governed by various laws of electricity and magnetism, including Faraday’s law of induction, Ampère’s circuital law, Lenz’ law, and the Lorentz force. The first two – Faraday’s law and Ampère’s circuital law – are included in Maxwell’s equations. Together with Lenz’ law and the Lorentz force, these principles form the basis of electromagnetism.
