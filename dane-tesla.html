8. SINO SI NIKOLA TESLA?


Nikola Tesla, (born July 9/10, 1856, Smiljan, Austrian Empire [now in Croatia]—died January 7, 1943, New York, New York, U.S.), Serbian American inventor and engineer who discovered and patented the rotating magnetic field, the basis of most alternating-current machinery. He also developed the three-phase system of electric power transmission. He immigrated to the United States in 1884 and sold the patent rights to his system of alternating-current dynamos, transformers, and motors to George Westinghouse. In 1891 he invented the Tesla coil, an induction coil widely used in radio technology.

17. Who is Nikola Tesla?


Nikola Tesla was an engineer and scientist who was known for his experiments and discoveries in electrical and related fields. To this day he is known by many as the father of electricity.

What made him better known:

It was the discovery of alternating current, many in the scientific and academic circles venerate him for this feat. Although he studied a lot on the subject, he was not the one who discovered alternating current. When he was still working as a telephone engineer, transformers were already being manufactured and tested for distributing electricity over long distances. What he truly created was the induction motor that works on alternating current, since at the time there were only direct current ones.

He did not create the X-RAY. The creator of this equipment was the British scientist William Croques.

He was not the creator of radio and wireless communication: Guillermo Marconi was already studying about it at the time. Tesla won the patents for the equipment used and made several demonstrations of the operation of boats and equipment by remote control, but many other scientists had already made several discoveries in these areas.

He was not as ignored as the books claim, as he was well known at the time, and had certain funding on several of his projects. At times he was considered a celebrity!

The idea of frée wireless energy distribution was considered unfeasible and utopian by today's scientists.

They found no evidence of the creation of the death ray that he claimed to have created at the time. Another detail is that he launched these rumors when he was suffering from Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD).

Although not everything that people mystify about Nikola Tesla is true, he remains one of the most important scientists today, as his discoveries revolutionized the areas of electricity and telecommunications, allowing the unleashing of computer systems, networks, telephony, industrial devices, etc.
