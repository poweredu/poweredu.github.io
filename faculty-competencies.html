The critical faculty of the personality​:

a) Choleric (choler is yellow bile)

b) Melancholic (melas and choler are black bile)

c) Phlegmatic (plegma is mucus)

d) Sanguinic (sanguine is blood)

EXPLANATION:

Based on the dominance or strength of a liquid in a person, there are four personality types, namely:

Choleric (choler is yellow bile). Predominant in the person is yellow bile. A Choleric has a quick-tempered, irritable, impatient temperament, and so on.Melancholic (melas and choler are black bile). The dominant in Melancholic people is black bile, he has a gloomy, gloomy temperament, easily sad, pessimistic, and hopeless.Phlegmatic (plegma is mucus). A Phlegmatic who is dominated by mucus in his body, has a sluggish temperament, passive, lazy, and apathetic.Sanguinic (sanguine is blood). What is dominant in this person is blood, he has cheerful, active, dynamic, agile traits.

These fluids exist in the human body in theory in certain proportions. If a fluid is present in the body that exceeds the proportion that should be (dominant) it will result in the existence of distinctive psychological characteristics.

Another type was developed by Spranger, a German philosopher. Spranger classifies individuals on the basis of their predisposition to values ​​in life. There are six personality types, namely:

a) Theoretic or theoretical human, those who base their actions on the basis of theoretical values ​​or knowledge. This type has a great urge to research, seek truth, curiosity, an objective view of himself and the outside world.

b) Economic based on its activities on the basis of economic values, namely the principle of profit and loss. His behavior is always colored by economic impulses, seeing the benefits of an object for life, everything is seen from its benefits or uses, especially for himself.

c) Aesthetics, namely those who make the values ​​of beauty or aesthetics as the basis for their lifestyle. The characteristics of this type are happy with beauty, symmetrical forms, harmonious, everything is seen from the point of view of beauty.

d) Sociatic, namely they prioritize social values ​​or relationships with other people as their pattern of life. Some traits of this type like other people, sympathetic, kind, reviewing the problems of human relationships.

e) Politics, namely those who make political values ​​their pattern of life. He has the drive to dominate others, to be the most important human being in his group.

f) Religious, namely they prioritize spiritual values ​​with God. His behavior is based on religious values, steadfast faith, submission to God.

20. There are 675 female faculty and staff at LSU. If 45% of the faculty and staff in the university are female. How many teachers are there at LSU?​


Let's assume that the total number of faculty and staff at LSU is "x".

If 45% of the faculty and staff in the university are female, then we can set up the following equation to represent the number of female faculty and staff at LSU:

0.45x = 675

To solve for "x", we can divide both sides of the equation by 0.45:

x = 675 ÷ 0.45

x = 1500

Therefore, there are 1500 faculty and staff at LSU.

29. conclusion of a faculty setup​


Answer:

Currently, many states are adopting the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) or are revising their own state standards in ways that reflect the NGSS. For students and schools, the implementation of any science standards rests with teachers. For those teachers, an evolving understanding about how best to teach science represents a significant transition in the way science is currently taught in most classrooms and it will require most science teachers to change how they teach.

That change will require learning opportunities for teachers that reinforce and expand their knowledge of the major ideas and concepts in science, their familiarity with a range of instructional strategies, and the skills to implement those strategies in the classroom. Providing these kinds of learning opportunities in turn will require profound changes to current approaches to supporting teachers' learning across their careers, from their initial training to continuing professional development.

A teacher's capability to improve students' scientific understanding is heavily influenced by the school and district in which they work, the community in which the school is located, and the larger professional communities to which they belong. Science Teachers' Learning provides guidance for schools and districts on how best to support teachers' learning and how to implement successful programs for professional development. This report makes actionable recommendations for science teachers' learning that take a broad view of what is known about science education, how and when teachers learn, and education policies that directly and indirectly shape what teachers are able to learn and teach.

The challenge of developing the expertise teachers need to implement the NGSS presents an opportunity to rethink professional learning for science teachers. Science Teachers' Learning will be a valuable resource for classrooms, departments, schools, districts, and professional organizations as they move to new ways to teach science.

30. what kind of noun is faculty?​


Answer:

Faculty is a mass (collective) noun—one that denotes something uncountable because it refers to an indeterminate aggregation of people. As the subject of a sentence, a mass noun usually takes a singular verb. But in a collective sense, it may take either a singular or a plural verb form.
