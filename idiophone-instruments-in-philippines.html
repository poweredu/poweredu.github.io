12. all idiophone instruments​


Idiophone instruments are musical instruments that produce sound by vibrating the entire instrument without the use of strings or membranes. Here are some examples of idiophone instruments:

Bells - This includes handbells, church bells, and cowbells.

Xylophone - This is a percussion instrument consisting of a set of wooden bars that are struck with mallets.

Maracas - These are small, handheld percussion instruments that produce sound when shaken.

Cymbals - These are percussion instruments made of two circular plates of metal that are clashed together to produce sound.

Gongs - These are large, flat percussion instruments made of metal that produce a deep, resonant sound when struck.

Triangle - This is a small percussion instrument made of a metal rod bent into a triangle shape.

Tambourine - This is a percussion instrument consisting of a small drum with jingles attached to the edge.

Claves - These are small, wooden percussion instruments that produce sound when struck together.

Steel drum - This is a percussion instrument made of a metal drum that has been tuned to produce a specific pitch.

Kalimba - This is a small thumb piano made of metal or wood with metal tines that produce sound when plucked.

25. idiophone instruments


Answer:

An idiophone is any musical instrument that creates sound primarily by the vibration of the instrument itself, without the use of air flow (as is the case with aerophones), strings (chordophones) or membranes (membranophones). It is the first of the four main divisions in the original Hornbostel–Sachs scheme of musical instrument classification (see List of idiophones by Hornbostel–Sachs number). The early classification of Victor-Charles Mahillon called this group of instruments autophones. The most common are struck idiophones, or concussion idiophones, which are made to vibrate by being struck, either directly with a stick or hand (like the wood block, singing bowl, steel tongue drum, triangle or marimba) or indirectly, by way of a scraping or shaking motion (like maracas or flexatone). Various types of bells fall into both categories. A common plucked idiophone is the Jew's harp.
