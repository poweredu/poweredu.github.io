Paedocypris Progenetica, a member of the carp family, has a partially see-through body and a reduced head skeleton which leaves the brain completely unprotected by bone. 22. Discover what is the smallest fish in the world​


What is the smallest fish in the world​?

Paedocypris progenetica fish is considered to be the world's tiniest fish. It was found in 2005 by a group of scientists led by Maurice Kottelat of the National University of Singapore, according to a Royal Society article posted on Eurocean. The fish is found in the seas of the Indonesian islands of Sumatra and Bintan Island, and is endemic to Southeast Asian peat swamp forests. A mature female Paedocypris progenetica fish is barely 7.9 millimeters long, or slightly more than 0.3 inch. The tiny fish was, in fact, the world's smallest documented vertebrate at the time of its discovery; according to LeisurePro.com, this distinction is currently held by Paedophryne amauensis, a minuscule frog from the New Guinean rain forests.
