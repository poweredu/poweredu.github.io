CHARACTERISTICS OF DISCO MUSIC

Answer:

Disco is a dance music genre and a subculture that arose in the 1970s from the metropolitan nightlife scene in the United States. Four-on-the-floor beats, syncopated basslines, string sections, horns, electric piano, synthesizers, and electric rhythm guitars characterize its sound.

During the late 1960s and early 1970s, disco began as a mash-up of music from locations popular with Italian Americans, Hispanic and Latino Americans, and African Americans in Philadelphia and New York City. Disco was a reaction by the 1960s counterculture to both rock music's dominance and dance music's stigmatization at the time. During the height of disco's popularity in the United States, other dance styles emerged, including "the Bump" and "the Hustle."

Disco is a type of uptempo dance music that incorporates the following elements:  

a 4/4 time signature a quick pace Rhythms that are four-on-the-floor Guitar-driven intensity with syncopated bass lines is common. orchestral arrangements that are lush Reverberated vocals Structure of the verses and chorus lyrics about love and dancing that are escapist

The bass drum plays on all four beats of the 4/4 bar, whereas the hi-hat cymbal plays on the off beats in four-on-the-floor drumming.

28. Define what disco is?


During the 1970's, another form and modified pop music has emerged and it is infamously known as disco. This type of pop music is a rock music that can be accompanied by a dance and is usually danceable given its tempo and rhythm. The term disco came from the French word "discotheque" which literally means a library or set of/for phonograph records. More often than not, disco is accompanied by a 4/4 time signature accompanied by strings, horns, electric guitars and electric pianos or synthesizers.
