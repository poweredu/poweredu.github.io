1. who is the founder of all Soul's Day? what is the purpose in stablishing all soul's Day?​


Answer:

All Souls' Day, also known as the Commemoration of All the Faithful Departed and the Day of the Dead, is a day of prayer and remembrance for the souls of those who have died, which is observed by Latin Catholics and other Christian denominations annually on November 2. All Souls' Day is often celebrated in Western Christianity; Saturday of Souls is a related tradition more frequently observed in Eastern Christianity. Practitioners of All Souls' Day traditions often remember deceased loved ones in various ways on the day.[2][3] Beliefs and practices associated with All Souls' Day vary widely among Christian denominations.

The annual celebration is the last day of Allhallowtide, after All Saints' Day and its eve.[4] Prior to the standardization of Catholic observance on 2 November by St. Odlio of Cluny during the 10th century, many Catholic congregations celebrated All Souls Day on various dates during the Easter season as it is still observed in some Eastern Orthodox Churches and associated Eastern Catholic Churches. Churches of the East Syriac Rite (Syro-Malabar Catholic Church, Chaldean Catholic Church, Assyrian Church of the East, Ancient Church of the East) commemorate all the faithful departed on the Friday before Lent.

4. Make an Essay (minimum of 200 words) Title: All Souls Day in the New Normal ​


All Souls Day is a day designated to remember and offer prayers on behalf of all believers who have died. Given the close meaning between the two, it is not surprising that the Church celebrates them in succession. After we celebrate the day of the saints, we pray for our brothers and sisters who have gone before us, in the hope that they too can join the saints in heaven.

Christians have been praying for their dead brothers/sisters since the early days of Christianity. The Church Fathers, namely Tertullian and St. Cyprian also teaches the practice of praying for the souls of the dead. This shows that the early Christians believed that their prayers could have a positive effect on the souls of the deceased. Associated with this practice is the teaching of Purgatory.

The New Testament implicitly teaches that there is a period of purification that believers experience after death. So before preparing for the coming of Christ, we are invited to first reflect on the temporary life on earth; about the end of our lives, so that we may eventually be joined in the number of the saints in heaven.

We are also invited to reflect on the meaning of death, by praying for our brothers and sisters who have gone before us.

Also, this November, the readings of Holy Mass are about the end of the world, namely to remind us of the end of our lives which we must prepare in fellowship with Christ. The hope is that, by contemplating the end of our life on earth, we will be able to appreciate even more the Mystery of the Incarnation of God (at Christmas) which allows us to be able to join in the number of His saints in eternal life in heaven.

24. how do you celebrate all souls day with conjunction ​


Answer:

The feast of All Saints’ Day, celebrated on November 1, honors all of the souls who are in heaven, even those who are not canonized. This feast has its origins in the early church when the Christians celebrated the anniversaries of the martyrdoms of fellow Christians. The number of martyrs became so large during the heavy persecution under the emperor Diocletian that a single day was assigned to celebrate them all. Feast days were later added for holy departed souls who were not martyrs. In the early eighth century, Gregory III dedicated a chapel in St. Peter’s Basilica to all saints. The feast day for this dedication was November 1, and it was extended to the whole church by Gregory IV in the ninth century.
