To find the measures of the angles in quadrilateral KLMN, we can use the fact that the sum of the measures of the angles in a quadrilateral is 360 degrees.

Since angle NML is 68 degrees and angle NLK is 77 degrees, the sum of the measures of these two angles is 68 + 77 = 145 degrees.

The sum of the measures of the other two angles in the quadrilateral must be 360 - 145 = 215 degrees.

Let x be the measure of angle LKM and y be the measure of angle LNK. We can set up the following equation to represent the fact that the sum of the measures of angles LKM and LNK is 215 degrees:

x + y = 215

To find the measures of angles LKM and LNK, we need one more equation.

Since quadrilateral KLMN is inscribed in circle J, the measures of angles LKM and LNK must be supplementary to the measures of arcs LM and NK, respectively.

Since the measure of arc LM is equal to the measure of angle NML and the measure of arc NK is equal to the measure of angle NLK, we have:

x + y = 180 - (68 + 77)

Solving this system of equations, we find that x = 43 and y = 172.

Therefore, the measure of angle LKM is 43 degrees and the measure of angle LNK is 172 degrees.
