26. What is the relationship between distance and time of travel of the rolling tin can


Distance and time of travel is needed to get the speed of an object. In this case, the object is a rolling can.

Speed is computed by dividing the distance the object has traveled by the time of travel.

For example : the rolling can reached 5 feet after rolling for 5 minutes.

Speed of the rolling can = 5 feet / 5 minutes = 1 foot per minute.

OR

The distance of the rolling can is directly proportional to the time of the rolling can, because as the distance of the rolling can increases the time of the rolling can also increases.
