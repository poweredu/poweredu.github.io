Mango Graham Ice Box Ref Cake

Cream Drop

INGREDIENTS

1 pack NESTLÉ All Purpose Cream 250ml

½ cup Nestle® Carnation® Condensada

24 pieces Graham Crackers

2 cups sliced mangoes

Curve

STEPS

STEP 1

Combine NESTLÉ All Purpose Cream and Nestle® Carnation® Condensada. Put 2 tbsp of the mixture on the glass dish.

STEP 2

Arrange 12 pieces of Graham Crackers in the glass dish. Then top with the cream mixture. Cover first layer with mango.

STEP 3

Repeat procedure to form 2 layers. Keep in the refrigerator for 4 hours or until the graham is moist with cream.

STEP 4

Slice into serving portions before servings.

27. Joshua has 4/5 kilograms of mango slices to be put equally on top of 5 tubs of Graham cake . how many kilograms of mango will be put on each tub​


DIVISION OF FRACTIONS

Problem:

Joshua has 4/5 kilograms of mango slices to be put equally on top of 5 tubs of Graham cake. How many kilograms of mango will be put on each tub?

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

In order to determine how much mangoes will be put on each tub, we can use division process. Given that there are 4/5 kg of mango slices that Joshua initially have. Sine he plans to put then equally on 5 tubs, we can compute it by:

4/5 ÷ 5

In the process of the division of fractions, we need to get first the reciprocal of the divisor which is 5. The reciprocal of 5 is -1/5. After reciprocating the divisor, we need to change the division sign to multiplication. So:

4/5 × 1/5

In multiplying fractions, just multiply the numerator to numerator then denominator to denominator directly.

(4×1)/(5×5) = 4/25

Therefore, each tub will be put 4/25 kilograms of mango slices.

28. 6. Mrs. Cabagay wanted to arrange graham cakes, gelatin; fruit salad. on leche plan and puto a round table. In how many possible ways can this be arrange​


Answer:

24 ways

Step-by-step explanation:

Assuming that the arrangement of the desserts on the round table matters, the number of possible ways to arrange the desserts can be calculated using the concept of permutations.

Since there are 5 desserts (graham cakes, gelatin, fruit salad, leche plan, and puto), there are 5! (5 factorial) ways to arrange them without any restrictions. This can be calculated as:

5! = 5 x 4 x 3 x 2 x 1 = 120

However, since the desserts are arranged on a round table, there is circular symmetry, meaning that the same arrangement can be rotated to produce an equivalent arrangement. Therefore, to account for the circular symmetry, we divide the total number of arrangements by the number of desserts (5) to avoid overcounting.

120 / 5 = 24

So, there are 24 possible ways to arrange the desserts (graham cakes, gelatin, fruit salad, leche plan, and puto) on a round table
