2. How to form the comparative of one syllable adjective?​


Answer:

Adjectives with two syllables can form the comparative either by adding -er or by preceeding the adjective with more. These adjectives form the superlative either by adding -est or by preceeding the adjective with most. In many cases, both forms are used, although one usage will be more common than the other.

6. 4. Many adjectives are changed into the comparative form by adding ______________. A. estB. erC. iest5. A long adjective of 3 or more syllables is changed into a comparative adjective by _________________A. adding -er to itB. adding -est to itC. putting "more" or "less" before it


Answer:

4. B. er

5. C. putting "more" or "less" before it.

Explaination:

4. The correct answer is B. When we want to compare two things, we use comparative adjectives. Many adjectives are changed into the comparative form by adding "er" to the end of the adjective. For example, "big" becomes "bigger", "fast" becomes "faster", and "happy" becomes "happier". This is the most common way to form comparative adjectives in English.

5. The correct answer is C. Long adjectives of 3 or more syllables are not usually changed into the comparative form by adding "-er" or "-est" to the end of the adjective. Instead, we use "more" or "less" before the adjective to form the comparative and superlative forms. For example, "interesting" becomes "more interesting", "expensive" becomes "more expensive", and "difficult" becomes "more difficult". This is because adding "-er" or "-est" to long adjectives can sound awkward or incorrect. Therefore, we use "more" or "less" to form the comparative form of long adjectives.

23. Write the comparative and superlative degree of the following adjectives. Do this in your answer sheet. Number 1 is done for you. 1-5 - one syllable adjectives 6-10-two or more syllable adjectives Positive Comparative Superlative 1. thick thicker thickest 2. tall 3. fat 6​


Answer:

1. thick thicker thickest  

2. tall taller tallest

3. fat fatter fattest

Explanation:

Positive, Comparative Superlative

The positive degree of an adjective is the simplest form of that particular adjective.

Comparative degree of an adjective denotes a higher degree when compared to a positive degree. It is used to compare two persons or things. The superlative degree of an adjective denotes the highest degree of the quality. Superlative degree is used to compare more than two persons or things.

Comparative and superlative forms of adjectives are formed by adding r and st or er and est or more and most etc.

Examples

☞ Victor is a tall boy. (Positive Degree)

☞ Victor is taller than Shyam. (Comparative Degree)

☞ Victor is the tallest boy in the class. (Superlative Degree)
