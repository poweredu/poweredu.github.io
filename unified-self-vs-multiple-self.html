7. compare and contrast of man vs. manand man vs. self​


Man vs. man conflict refers to a situation in which two or more individuals or groups are in opposition to one another. This type of conflict can occur in various settings, including personal relationships, social groups, organizations, and even nations.

Man vs. self conflict, on the other hand, refers to the inner struggle or conflict that an individual experiences within themselves. This type of conflict can be emotional, psychological, or moral in nature and may involve making difficult decisions or overcoming personal challenges.

One key difference between man vs. man conflict and man vs. self conflict is the source of the conflict. Man vs. man conflict is external, arising from interactions with others, while man vs. self conflict is internal, arising from an individual's own thoughts, feelings, and desires.

Another difference is the way in which the conflict is resolved. Man vs. man conflict may be resolved through negotiation, compromise, or some other form of resolution, while man vs. self conflict is typically resolved through personal growth, self-reflection, and making changes within oneself.

In summary, man vs. man conflict involves opposition between two or more individuals or groups, while man vs. self conflict involves an individual's internal struggle with their own thoughts, feelings, and desires.
