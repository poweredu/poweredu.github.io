2. Examples of vernacularization​


Examples of vernacularization

Interpretation of the Koran in Bugis (lontara) script

One of the vernacularization of Al-Quran interpretation in Bugis can be seen from the use of Bugis script. The Bugis script (lontara) itself has an old term, namely Sure' Ugi, which is a type of ancient letter form that gives a picture of culture in the past. The Bugis call it the Suratu Sulapa Eppa (Quadrilateral Letter). Bugis literature written in lontara began around the XVI century, before Islam was generally adopted by the people of South Sulawesi.

In the beginning, the written Bugis literature was sacred literature in the form of incantations and mythological beliefs. Gradually experiencing development in accordance with the development of Lontara and the attitude of life of society and culture. Included in this case is also used to write interpretations. Lontara itself, according to its name, is taken from the function of the palm leaves which were used as a place to write.

Vernacularization or simply called the local language process is no longer something new in the scientific tradition of our society. The vernacularization of Al-Quran interpretation in Bugis itself only occurred around the 1940s. This is marked by the use of the Bugis script (lontara) by South Sulawesi scholars in their interpretation. Vernacularization was originally related to religious teachings which originally used Arabic. Then replaced with translation or written using the script owned by the local community. This is then in line with what Azyumardi Azra said that vernacularization is the process of translating key words or concepts from Arabic into local languages.

8. What is Vernacular Literature?


Answer:

Vernacular literature is literature written in the language of the common people.

Explanation:

The word vernacular comes from the Latin verna (home-born) and later vernaculus (domestic/native) and is used to indicate the native language of the people.

In European literature, this usually refers to books not written in Latin though, across the world, it would refer to books written in the language commonly spoken by native people.
