13. determine the type of chemical bond by solving ∆EN of the compound (H2) H= 2.1 H= 2.1 ∆EN


Answer:

In order to determine the type of chemical bond in a compound using electronegativity difference (∆EN), we can use the following guidelines:

If the ∆EN is less than 0.5, the bond is nonpolar covalent.If the ∆EN is between 0.5 and 1.7, the bond is polar covalent.If the ∆EN is greater than 1.7, the bond is ionic.In the case of (H2), both atoms are hydrogen, which have the same electronegativity value of 2.1. Therefore, the ∆EN is:

∆EN = |2.1 - 2.1| = 0

Since the ∆EN is less than 0.5, the bond in (H2) is nonpolar covalent. This means that the electrons in the bond are shared equally between the two hydrogen atoms, resulting in a molecule with no overall dipole moment.
