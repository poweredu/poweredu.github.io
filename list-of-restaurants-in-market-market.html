7. If you have a restaurant, which marketing intermediary will you use and why?​


Answer:

The Importance of Intermediaries

In an age where it is easy for any company to set up shop with an e-commerce website, it may be tempting for a small business to eliminate intermediaries to maximize profit. For a scaling business, however, this can create a lot of work in logistics and customer support.

For example, if 1,000 customers were to buy a product directly from the producer in a single month, this would entail 1,000 separate shipments to 1,000 locations, and with a minimum of 1,000 customer interactions. If you added customer inquiries about the product, returns and after-sale support – and all the customers who initiate a purchase without following through – you would have several thousand interactions with customers for every 1,000 sales. Selling through three or four intermediaries with a weekly shipping schedule, the manufacturer would have only a dozen shipments to schedule each month with a fraction of the interactions.

9. marketing plan for restaurant Product Price Promotion Place Packaging people​


Sure, here's a marketing plan for a restaurant using the 5 P's of marketing:

Product: First things first, define the product—in this case, the drinks and food served at the restaurant. Make adjustments to the menu based on what dishes and beverages are most popular in the area by conducting market research. To generate buzz and excitement, you might want to think about introducing special offers that are only available for a limited time.

Price: Keep competitors' pricing strategies in mind as you set prices for the menu items based on costs. Discounts for groups or regular customers are a great way to keep them coming back. To attract customers during off-peak hours, offer special deals or happy hour.

Promotion: Utilize flyers, social media, and local newspapers to promote the restaurant. Partnering with food bloggers or local celebrities to promote the restaurant makes use of influencer marketing. To attract customers and create a fun atmosphere, host events like trivia nights or live music nights.

Place: Choose a location that is visible to potential customers and easy to reach. If you want to offer a delivery service, think about forming a partnership with delivery apps. This will make it easier for customers to order food from the restaurant.

Packaging: When placing orders for delivery or takeout, invest in high-quality packaging to ensure that the food arrives in good condition. If you want to attract customers who place a high value on environmental responsibility, think about packaging alternatives.

People: Hire and train friendly, helpful employees who are familiar with the menu and are able to provide excellent customer service. In order to create a memorable dining experience, staff should be encouraged to interact with customers and provide individualized recommendations. To increase job satisfaction and decrease staff turnover, provide opportunities for training and development to employees.

22. Identify one (1) chemical you can find in each place listed below. Church Hospital Environment Market Industry School Transportation Restaurant Your bedroom


Examples of chemicals that we can find around us.

Chemicals are pure substances or mixtures with various chemical elements.

In everyday life, we will always be in contact with chemicals.

Here are some examples of chemicals that can be found all around us:

1. In church.

Because the church is a place of worship, maybe we will not find many objects that are chemicals. But during the pandemic, we can easily find hand sanitizer in many places, especially churches, which are places where many people gather during worship. Hand sanitizer contains alcohol which is an example of a chemical substance.

2. In the hospital

Drugs are the most common examples of chemicals that we can find in hospitals. Each medicine contains different chemicals that are used for healing.

3. In Environment

The oxygen we breathe is the easiest chemical we can find. There is also carbon dioxide for photosynthetic plants and also water which is the main human need. These three things are examples of chemicals.

4. In the market

Baking powder, a food ingredient that is usually used to make cakes and sold on the market is one example of a chemical containing sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO₃).

5. In the industry

Boric acid is one of the chemicals in the industrial field. These chemicals can be used to make cement, preservatives, and to process wood.

6. At school

In school, we can find many chemicals in science laboratories. Examples are hydrochloric acid (HCl) and methanol (CH3OH).

7. On transportation

Gasoline, the fuel used by motorized vehicles is one example of a chemical substance in transportation. Gasoline contains many different chemicals, examples of which are alkanes and alkenes.

8. At the restaurant

Hand soap, which is usually available in restaurant toilets, is an example of a chemical. Hand washing soap contains sodium compounds.

9. In the bedroom

Chemicals that can be found in the bedroom are skincare that we usually use and store in the room. Skincare contains a variety of chemicals that function to treat the skin.
