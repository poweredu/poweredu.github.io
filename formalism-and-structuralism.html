1. Differentiate structuralism and formalism?


Answer:

Formalism analyses the structure of a text without focusing on the external factors such as authorship, social and cultural influence. However, structuralism connects the work of a particular author with works of similar structures whereas formalism only analyses one particular work at a time.


2. in formalism What are the structures in the poem?​


Formalism does not consider the author's personal history, cultural influences, and the actual content in the work itself. Instead, it focuses on the form and genre of the writing. For example, formalism is concerned with the use of grammar and syntax, and meter in poetry.

4. In defining a concept, formal definition follows this structure.


Concept, Formal Definition

A concept, formal definition is built upon a clear, logical structure that fits as much information as possible into a small amount of room. Definitions should be used in writing primarily to prevent misunderstandings with readers. A formal definition has three components:

The concept (word, phrase) being definedThe group of things or ideas that the phrase refers to The distinctive qualities that set it apart from all other products in its class

Example:

Water (term) is a liquid (class) made composed of hydrogen and oxygen molecules in a 2 to 1 ratio (differentiating characteristics).Comic books (term) are sequential, narrative works (class) with pictures, captions, and speech bubbles that frequently include superhuman characters (differentiating characteristics).

25. three movements is a typical formal structure for a baroque


Three movements is a typical formal structure for a baroque

The traditional concerto has three movements, traditionally fast, slow and lyrical, and fast.  There are many examples of concertos that do not comply with this plan.

The first known solo concertos are Nos. 6 and 12 of Giuseppe Torelli's Op. 6 of 1698.  These works use both a three-movement cycle and a clear (if diminutive) ritornello form, such as that  of the ripieno concerto, except that the sections for the soloist and the continuum separate the orchestral  ritornellos. Active in Bologna, Torelli would have known the operatic arias and the numerous trumpet and  string sonatas and symphonies produced in Bologna since the 1660s.He himself composed more than a  dozen such trumpet works, two dated back to the early 1690s. Other early violin concertos include four in Tomaso Albinoni's op. 2 (1700) and six in Torelli's important op. 8 (1709—the other six in this set are double concertos for two violins).
