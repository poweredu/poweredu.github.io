7. Sofie has a mass of 50 kg runs to her next classroom for her next subject.If her running speed is 1.5m/s, what is her kinetic energy?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the kinetic energy

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{50 kg})(\text{1.5 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{56.25 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of Sophia is 56.25 J.

20. Sofi saves ₱2,000 at the end of each six-month period in an ordinary annuity earning 6% interest compounded semiannually. How much will Sofi have saved in 4 years?A. ₱16,565.68B. ₱17,784.67C. ₱14,039.38D. ₱20,000​


Given; Principal amount saves by sofi at the end of each six months (P) = ₱ 2,000

Rate of interest (R) = 6%

Time (N) = 4 years

Period (M) = 2 ( for semi-annually).

ㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤ

Solution:

Total amount saved in 4 years is given by

[tex] \tt P\bigg( \frac{(1 + \frac{r}{m} ) {}^{m \times n} - 1}{ \frac{r}{m} } \bigg) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt = 2000 \bigg( \frac{(1 + \frac{0.06}{2} ) {}^{2 \times 4} - 1}{ \frac{0.06}{2} } \bigg) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt = 2000 \bigg( \frac{1 + 0.03) {}^{8} - 1 }{0.03} \bigg) \: = 2000 \bigg( \frac{(1.03) {}^{8} - 1}{0.03} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt = 2000 \bigg( \frac{1.26677008 - 1}{0.03} \bigg) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt = 2000 \bigg( \frac{0.26677008}{0.03} \bigg) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \tt = 2000 \times 8.892336[/tex]

[tex] \tt = ₱ \: 17,784.678[/tex]

Hence the answer is B. ₱17,784.67
