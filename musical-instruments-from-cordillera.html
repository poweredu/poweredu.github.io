6. Different Indigenous Musical Instruments of the Cordillera Gangsa is a single hand-held smooth-surfaced gong with a narrow rim.

7. Kalaleng or Tongali (nose flute) Because the kalaleng is long and has a narrow internal diameter, it is possible to play different harmonics through overblowing.

8. Tongatong is a bamboo percussion instrument used by the people of Kalinga to communicate with spirits during house blessings. It is made of bamboo cut in various lengths.

9. Diwdiw-as is 5 or more different size of slender bamboo that is tied together.

10. Saggeypo it is a bamboo pipe that is closed on one end by a node with the open end held against the lower lip of the player as he blows directly across the top. The pipe can be played individually by one person or in ensembles of three or more.

11. Solibao is hallow wooden Igorot drug topped with pig skin or lizard skin this is played by striking the drum head using the palm of the hand.

12. Bungkaka- bamboo buzzer

13. Kullitong- polychordal bamboo tube zither

14. Ulibaw/Kubing - bamboo jaw’s harp

15. Patangguk– bamboo quill-shaped tube

16. Pateteg - bamboo leg xylophone

17. Paldong - bamboo lip-valley flute
