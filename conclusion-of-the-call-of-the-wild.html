12. summary of "The Call of the Wild" bhy COLLINS CLASSICS​


Answer:

The Call of the Wild is a classic novel by Jack London that tells the story of a domesticated dog named Buck who is forced into the harsh realities of life in the wilds of the Yukon during the Klondike Gold Rush of the 1890s. After being kidnapped and sold into service as a sled dog, Buck must learn to survive in a harsh and unfamiliar environment. With the help of a wise old Native American, John Thornton, Buck learns how to become a wild animal, adapting to the harsh conditions of the Yukon landscape. The novel follows Buck’s journey as he discovers his true nature and learns to live off the land. The Call of the Wild is a timeless story of survival and adaptation that has captivated readers for generations.

18. THE CALL OF THE WILDwhat did they expect to find in africa?​


The African wild dog (Lycaon pictus) is a canine which is a native species to sub-Saharan Africa. It is the largest wild canine in Africa, and the only extant member of the genus Lycaon, which is distinguished from Canis by dentition highly specialised for a hypercarnivorous diet, and a lack of dewclaws. It is estimated that about 6,600 adults (including 1,400 mature individuals) live in 39 subpopulations that are all threatened by habitat fragmentation, human persecution and outbreaks of diseases. As the largest subpopulation probably consists of less than 250 individuals, the African wild dog has been listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List since 1990.
