Idiophones

Greek and Roman idiophones were passed on to post-Classical Europe, their distribution undoubtedly aided by joculatores and civic or court musicians (minstrels). 25. define what is parabolic and hyperbolic​


Answer:

Parabolic and hyperbolic are terms used in mathematics to describe different types of curves.

A parabolic curve is a symmetrical U-shaped curve that is created by plotting points that are equidistant from a fixed point (called the focus) and a fixed straight line (called the directrix). The most common example of a parabolic curve is the trajectory of an object thrown into the air, such as a ball or a rocket.

A hyperbolic curve, on the other hand, is a symmetrical curve that looks like two parabolas facing each other. It is created by plotting points that are equidistant from two fixed points (called the foci) and forming a curve that intersects with both foci. Hyperbolic curves are commonly found in physics, particularly in the study of electromagnetic fields and gravitational forces.

28. What is the essence of the parabolic design


Answer:

A parabola is a conic section created from the intersection of a right circular conical surface and a plane parallel to a generating straight line of that surface. The parabola has many important applications, from the design of automobile headlight reflectors to calculating the paths of ballistic missiles.
