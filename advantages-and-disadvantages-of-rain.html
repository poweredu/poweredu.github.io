Answer:

1. Under constant acceleration, the average velocity of an object is the sum of its initial and final velocities divided by two. Would this still hold true even if the acceleration is not constant? Explain your answer.

Answer:Usually not. This is simple if you understand what acceleration is, and draw a graph of velocity versus time. If acceleration is constant it is a straight line, and the average height of the graph is halfway between the first and last velocities. If the graph is a curve - well it depends on the curve. It COULD just work out to be half the sum of the initial and final velocities, but it is unlikely.

2. if the acceleration of the body is constant does it necessarily mean that its velocity is also constant

Answer:If you travel with a constant acceleration, your velocity is always changing, but it's changing by a consistent amount each second. The acceleration due to gravity on the Earth has the constant value 9.8 m/s2, so you can imagine this like dropping something from a skyscraper

3. Although air resistance is sometimes a disadvantage, it also has several advantages magine that there is no air resistance on the earth's surface and for some reason, it began to rain. Why would it be dangerous to go outside while raining?

Answer:Friction is a force that opposes the motion of objects; friction can cause objects to slow down. Air resistance is a type of friction. Air resistance causes moving objects to slow down. Different physical properties, such as the shape of an object, affect the air resistance on an object.
