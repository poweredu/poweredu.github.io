BIOMOLECULES: LIPIDS AND CARBONS

Lipid comes from the Latin word lipos which means fat. The definition of fat is an organic compound that occurs in nature and is difficult to dissolve in water, but easily soluble in non-polar solvents, such as sure or ether. Lipids are biomolecules that are very important in our dietary needs. One form of lipids is triglycerides and lipoproteins.

Based on their origin, lipids are divided into two types, namely vegetable lipids (fats derived from plants) and animal lipids (fats contained by animals). The differences between animal oil lipids and vegetable oil lipids include:

1. Animal lipids

contains cholesterolfat content is dominated by saturated fatTends to be solid at room temperature

2. Vegetable lipid

phytosterols,higher levels of unsaturated fatty acids than animal lipidsVegetable lipids tend to be liquid.

Lipids have a very important role in the metabolism of living things as a source of energy, are as follows :

Lipids are the most effective energy storage in the body of living things. Lipids provide more or about 2 times more energy than protein and carbohydrates.Thermal/or heat insulation. Mammals have a lipid layer under their skin that serves as thermal insulation. For example whales and seals that live on cold seas.As a protector of the body's organs. Some important organs in the body that are soft, such as the kidneys are protected by a layer of fat.As a source of vitamins (food). Vitamins A, D, and E are only lipid soluble.

Oil is a substance or material that belongs to the lipid group. Lipid is one of the biomolecules that are in the body of living things. Biomolecules are commonly known as macromolecules or larger molecules. This is because it is composed of much smaller organic molecules.

The macromolecules apart from lipids are carbohydrates (glucose, cellulose, fructose, maltose, lactose, starch), proteins (enzymes, hormones, and antibodies), and nucleic acids (DNA and RNA).
