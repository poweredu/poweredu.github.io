25. if a ferrari, with an initial velocity of 10 m/s accelerates at a rate of 50m/s/s for 3 seconds, what is it's final velocity be¿ ¿ ¿ ¿ ¿? ? ? ? ?​


Answer:

To find the final velocity of the Ferrari, we can use the formula:

Final velocity = Initial velocity + (Acceleration x Time)

Given: Initial velocity (u) = 10 m/s, Acceleration (a) = 50 m/s^2, Time (t) = 3 s

Final velocity = 10 m/s + (50 m/s^2 x 3 s)

Final velocity = 10 m/s + 150 m/s

Final velocity = 160 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the Ferrari after accelerating at a rate of 50 m/s^2 for 3 seconds starting from an initial velocity of 10 m/s would be 160 m/s.


26. If a Ferrari, with an initial velocity of 10 m/s, accelerates at a rate of 50 m/s/s for 3 seconds, what will its final velocity be?​


Answer:

50 m/s/s

Explanation:

v_f = v_i + a*t

where:

v_f is the final velocity

v_i is the initial velocity

a is the acceleration

t is the time

Plugging in the given values, we get:

v_f = 10 m/s + (50 m/s/s)*(3 s)

v_f = 10 m/s + 150 m/s

v_f = 160 m/s

Therefore, the Ferrari's final velocity will be 160 m/s after accelerating at a rate of 50 m/s/s for 3 seconds.
