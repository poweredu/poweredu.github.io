3. rrl about Reason that influence incoming senior high school student in choosing accountancy,business and management (ABM) strand


Answer:

The Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM) strand is a popular choice among senior high school (SHS) students in the Philippines. Researchers have conducted studies to identify the reasons that influence incoming senior high school students in choosing this strand.

According to a study conducted by Garcia (2019), one of the primary reasons that influence incoming seniors high school students in choosing the ABM strand is the potential it holds for future job opportunities. The students recognize that the strand will provide a foundation for pursuing higher education in business management, accounting, finance, and entrepreneurship, among others. The students believe that graduating from the ABM strand will prepare them to become successful in the business sector.

Another reason identified in the study is the interest of incoming senior high school students in the subject. Students who have an inclination towards accountancy, business, and management are likely to choose the ABM strand. They believe that the ABM strand will help them develop their skills and achieve career aspirations.

In another study conducted by Gonzales (2018), peer influence was identified as a factor that affects the decisions of incoming senior high school students in choosing the ABM strand. The students tend to follow the recommendations of friends or family members who have had previous experiences in the ABM field.

Furthermore, social media trends and advertisements also play a significant role in the decision-making process of incoming senior high school students. They are influenced by the images of successful businesses, job opportunities, and prestige that the ABM strand represents in the industry.

15. what is ABM strand ? ​


Answer:

The Accountancy, Business, and Management Strand—or ABM strand—seeks to prepare the young business leaders of tomorrow. ABM strand paves the way for business-related college degrees. It teaches the basic concepts of financial management, accounting, and corporate operations.

Answer:

The K-12 program aims to give junior and senior high school students enough time to master skills and absorb basic know-hows for college education or job search. Students of the new system will graduate at the age of 18 and forge their path in their chosen career.

Each strand focuses on a specific skill set to provide a better grasp of concepts. The ABM strand highlights business and finance subjects to prepare the students for the business industry. Mainly, it covers these objectives:

Acquire basic industry skills.

As said earlier, the students who can finish the 18-year K-12 program will receive a diploma, which can be used for higher education or job search. Graduates can smoothly advance to college or find jobs, given that they passed the ABM track programs. Hence, the last two years in high school aims to teach everything that would make the graduates ready for job.

Adopt the right mindset.

Aside from the basic skills and knowledge, taking the ABM track courses will help the students develop confidence in doing their projects and tasks. Eventually, they will be able to adopt critical thinking skills and teamwork, which they can carry over once they partake in their chosen careers. Aside from skills, the ABM track programs could help students develop the right mindset.

Gain business exposure.

Being good in one craft takes practice. So, it’s best to shape young business leaders early on through real business exposure. The ABM track engages high school students in business simulation. This may require research projects and other practical courses. Also, the subjects under the ABM track aims to expose them to the different types of situations that business managers face regularly.

students using laptops during their ABM strand class

Advantages of the ABM Strand

Learn the pros of taking the K-12 ABM strand this school year. The following are the advantages of taking this strand.

1. Fosters Learner-Centered Programs

With the K-12 program, the students have the freedom to choose among the four K-12 tracks. Their active role in choosing the track aims to teach the values of independence and responsibility. Since they are the one who chose the track, it’s also part of their duty to study the required subjects and complete the track programs.

2. Provides Direct Path to Careers

One of the goals of K-12 in the Philippines is to lead more students to their chosen careers. Many Filipinos are not able to pursue college due to various reasons. Based on data from the Department of Education (DepEd), close to 4 million students were not able to enroll for the current school year (PNA, 2021). K-12 seeks to bridge this gap through programs that will produce job-ready graduates each year.

3. Boosts Interest for Tertiary Learning

Aside from preparing them for work, the ABM track programs aim to encourage students to advance their learning through college degrees and skill courses. The ABM track subjects provide a glimpse of the careers ahead of them. Although they know the basics of ABM, gaining a higher degree can open more job opportunities.

4. Helps with Career Decision Making

Before, most high school students would experience a hard time choosing their college courses; with lack of awareness about the courses and tracks among the reasons behind frequent course shifting. With the K-12 program, senior high school students could explore arts, science, technical, and sports courses. Through this, they will have a better grasp of their college course choice and a clearer career path view.

5. Advances Skills in the Job Market.

Part of the K-12 ABM track program’s goals is to increase the education quality in the country. The track subjects aim to shape fresh graduates who are globally competitive and are set to win a spot in today’s job market.

21. rrl about Reason that influence incoming senior high school student in choosing accountancy,business and management (ABM) strand///// pang junior highschool po sanaaa


Answer:

The decision to choose a strand in Senior High School, such as Accountancy, Business, and Management (ABM), is often influenced by several factors, which can be traced back to Junior High School. Generally speaking, students' experiences in their Junior High years can significantly impact their perspective in choosing a career pathway. Here are some of the reasons that influence students in selecting the ABM strand:

Firstly, students who demonstrate good performance in Mathematics, Economics, and Business-related subjects during Junior High are likely to choose the ABM strand. These subjects provide basic knowledge and skills needed in the strand, making it easy for them to excel in ABM's curriculum and help them prepare for their future business career.

Secondly, career awareness activities in Junior High play an essential role in decision-making for the ABM strand. Career talks, workshops, seminars, and counseling sessions provide opportunities for Junior High students to learn more about different careers and industries, including the ABM strand. Exposure to the business industry at this stage can help students solidify their interests in the industry and encourage them to pursue it later on.

Moreover, guidance and advice from parents, relatives, and friends who work in the business industry can influence students in choosing the ABM strand. Experience and knowledge from people who work in the industry provide a practical perspective on the profession's challenges and opportunities, which can help students make informed decisions.

Another factor that affects students' ABM strand decisions is the availability of learning materials and resources. Junior High institutions that offer comprehensive learning resources that cater to ABM-related subjects can positively impact students' interests in the strand. Accessible materials and a supportive learning environment can enhance students' learning experiences and help them develop their skills in the business industry.

Lastly, personal interest and passion are critical factors that influence students' ABM strand decisions. Junior High students who have a natural talent in managing finances or are into entrepreneurship are likely to choose the ABM strand. Such interests and passions can drive them to pursue careers in the business industry.

In conclusion, Junior High School experiences, such as performance in relevant subjects, career awareness activities, guidance, access to learning materials, and personal interest, play an essential role in influencing students' ABM strand decision. These factors can help students determine if the ABM strand is the right fit for their interests, skills, and career aspirations.

Parents who work in the corporate world or who own businesses may encourage their children to pursue a career in accounting, finance, or management. They recognize that the ABM strand provides a solid foundation for pursuing higher education in business management, accounting, finance, and entrepreneurship.
