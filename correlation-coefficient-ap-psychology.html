13. What is the difference between correlation ratio and the point biserial coefficient of correlation?


Answer:

A point-biserial and biserial correlation is used to correlate a dichotomy with an interval scaled variable. The difference is that the point-biserial correlation is used when the dichotomous variable is a true or discrete dichotomy and the biserial correlation is used with an artificial dichotomy.

What is coefficient correlation?

Coefficient Correlation is the measure of linear correlation between two sets of data.

Formula:

- Use the formula (zy)i = (yi – ȳ) / s y and calculate a standardized value for each yi. Add the products from the last step together. Divide the sum from the previous step by n – 1, where n is the total number of points in our set of paired data. The result of all of this is the correlation coefficient.
