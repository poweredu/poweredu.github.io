Answer:

Therefore, the expected net gain for a single bet is 0.22 pesos.

Step-by-step explanation:

E(X) = (1/9) * 58 + (8/9) * (-7)

E(X) = 6.44 - 6.22

E(X) = 0.22

Answer:

In this scenario, the random variable X represents the net gain for a bet on the roulette wheel in a fiesta celebration.

Given the information provided, we can analyze the possible outcomes and corresponding probabilities to calculate the expected value of X, denoted as E(X), which represents the average net gain (or loss) for a single bet.

The possible outcomes for X can be:

If the bet results in a win, the net gain would be the prize worth 65 pesos minus the initial bet of 7 pesos, resulting in a net gain of 65 - 7 = 58 pesos with a probability of 1/9, since there is only one winning number out of the 9 numbers on the wheel.

If the bet results in a loss, the net gain would be the negative of the initial bet of 7 pesos, resulting in a net loss of -7 pesos with a probability of 8/9, since there are 8 losing numbers out of the 9 numbers on the wheel.

Based on this analysis, we can calculate the expected value E(X) as follows:

E(X) = (Net gain for winning outcome) * (Probability of winning) + (Net gain for losing outcome) * (Probability of losing) = (58 pesos) * (1/9) + (-7 pesos) * (8/9) = 58/9 - 56/9 = 2/9

So, the expected value of the net gain for a single bet on the roulette wheel in this fiesta celebration is 2/9 pesos. This means that on average, a bet of 7 pesos would result in a net gain of 2/9 pesos per bet. However, it's important to note that this is just the expected value and the actual outcome for any individual bet may vary

17. What is the net force or resultant force, Fnet?​


Explanation:

Net force is the vector sum of forces acting on a particle or body. The net force is a single force that replaces the effect of the original forces on the particle's motion. It gives the particle the same acceleration as all those actual forces together as described by the Newton's second law of motion

You use “Fnet=” to show the values that you add in order to get the net force.
