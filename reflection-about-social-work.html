Answer:

1. How are women's lives portrayed in the work?

In "Dead Stars," women's lives are portrayed primarily in domestic roles and as objects of men's affections. The story depicts women as taking care of children and managing household tasks, while men are depicted in more professional roles.

2. Is the form and content of the work influenced by the writer's gender?

This is not specified in the critique but one can approach it as that as a woman writer Paz Marquez Benitez could have chosen to represent the women characters differently.

3. How do male and female characters relate to one another? Are these relationships sources of conflict? Are these conflicts resolved?

In "Dead Stars," the male characters often relate to the female characters in a patriarchal and possessive manner. The central relationship in the story, between Alfredo and Julia, is a source of conflict and ultimately unresolved. Alfredo's continuing affection for Julia creates distance in his marriage and prevents him from fully committing to his wife.

4. Does the work challenge or affirm traditional views of women?

The critique suggests that "Dead Stars" reinforces traditional views of women as being relegated to domestic roles and as objects of men's affections.

5. How do the images of women in the story reflect patriarchal social forces that have impeded women's efforts to achieve full equality with men?
