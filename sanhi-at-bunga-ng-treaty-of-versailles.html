5. ano ang mga layunin ng treaty of versailles


TREATY OF VERSAILLES

Layunin ng treaty of versailles

Ang layunin ng Treaty of Versailles ay ang pangunahing kasunduan na ginawa ng Paris Peace Conference sa pagtatapos ng World War I. Ito ay nilagdaan noong Hunyo 28, 1919, ng Allied at mga nauugnay na kapangyarihan at ng Alemanya sa Hall of Mirrors sa Palace of Versailles at nagsimula noong Enero 10, 1920. Ang kasunduan ay nagbigay ng ilang mga teritoryo ng Aleman sa mga kalapit na bansa at inilagay ang iba pang mga teritoryo ng Aleman sa ilalim ng pangangasiwa ng internasyonal.

Explanation:

Bilang karagdagan, ang Alemanya ay tinanggal ng mga kolonya sa ibang bansa, ang mga kakayahan ng militar ay mahigpit na pinaghigpitan, at kinakailangan na magbayad ng mga pag-aayos ng giyera sa mga bansang Allied. Ang kasunduan ay lumikha din ng League of Nations.

11. . The treaty of Versailles was not a good peace treaty


Answer:

Yep pretty much

Explanation:

The Treaty of Versailles was a peace treaty signed on June 28, 1919, between Germany and the Allied Powers to officially end World War I.   However, it was disliked by many parties involved, particularly in Germany.  The Germans were angry that they had not been allowed to negotiate and called Versailles a “diktat” or dictated peace.  They also hated the war-guilt clause (Article 231) and officially denied it in 1927.  There was even a revolution against the treaty in Berlin in 1920.

Other countries also had their criticisms of the treaty. For example, British Prime Minister David Lloyd George thought the treaty was too harsh and said: "We shall have to fight another war again in 25 years time".

27. treaty of Versailles bunga​


Answer:

The Treaty of Versailles (French: Traité de Versailles; German: Versailler Vertrag, pronounced [vɛʁˈzaɪ̯ɐ fɛɐ̯ˈtʁaːk] (About this soundlisten)) was the most important of the peace treaties that brought World War I to an end. The Treaty ended the state of war between Germany and the Allied Powers. It was signed on 28 June 1919 in the Palace of Versailles, exactly five years after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, which had directly led to the war. The other Central Powers on the German side signed separate treaties.[i] Although the armistice, signed on 11 November 1918, ended the actual fighting, it took six months of Allied negotiations at the Paris Peace Conference to conclude the peace treaty.
