1. which part serves as a tunel for the ripe ova egg cells to rich the uterus


The part that serves as a tunnel for the fertile/fertilized ova/egg cell/s to reach the uterus is called the fallopian tube.

The fallopian tube is a hollow, tube-like muscular organ that connects the ovaries to the uterus. It serves as the passageway for the egg cells to cross during:

fertilization. The fertile egg cell lies in wait in the fallopian tube of the female reproductive duct. Once a sperm fertilizes the egg cell, it moves to the uterus where it implants itself. menstrual cycle. If the egg does not become fertilized, it still moves to the uterus via fallopian tube. However, the egg cell does not implant itself. It sloughs off with the rest of the clotted uterine wall during menstruation.
