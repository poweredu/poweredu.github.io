24. what is the opportunity seeking process before launching a product or a service?​


Be strategic.

One, find your core audience. Whether you’re creating the new iPhone or a cupcake stand, find and learn from your core audience. Establish your base and build their confidence in you.

Two, get advice and listen. Most friends will be your biggest cheerleaders, but that doesn’t mean they’re helping. They need to poke holes in your ideas, which you can fix before taking a product to market. Resist the urge to be defensive. And use the opportunity to strengthen your product.

Three, learn from others. There are no more original ideas, just repurposed ones. As you prepare to market your idea, examine your competitors. Take from their good, learn from their bad, and enhance what needs to be improved. Then put your spin on it.

Four, be smart about publicity. You no longer need traditional media to drive ideas, products or messages. Don’t get caught up in creating a PR budget if it won’t drive core business. Instead, spend your time creating messages to buyers.

Five, conditioning your social media audience is critical. Always pushing them to "buy" creates fatigue, making them feel like a number. Build a relationship with your audience at least three months in advance so they can learn your tone and rhythms. Then seek ways for them to interact with your product.

Six, for all your analysis, don’t forget to rely on "feel." Entrepreneurs are risk takers with unwavering belief. Don’t abandon that now that you’re moments away from the launch.

Finally, use the power of broke. I wrote a book about this. My biggest business mistakes came when I tried to buy success. My most successful business experiences came from using limited resources, supported by innovation.

29. what opportunity seeking in entrepreneurship?​


Looking for opportunities that are much needed in entrepreneurship. Business opportunities can simply be interpreted as an opportunity that a person has to achieve business goals. The purpose of this business can be in the form of profit, money to wealth that is achieved by doing certain businesses by utilizing the resources they have.

Robbin & Coulter defines business opportunity as the entire process related to the involvement of individuals or groups in utilizing certain facilities to create added value to meet needs.

Understanding the meaning of business opportunity alone is not enough. The variety of business opportunities that we can find sometimes makes us confused in the selection. We are also required to be able to determine what kind of business opportunities are good and in accordance with the circumstances and the resources we have.

So, in order to be able to choose the right business opportunity, here are some general descriptions of the characteristics or characteristics of a good business opportunity:

- Have a selling point

- Not a seasonal business

- Has sustainable value and can last a long time (sustainable)

- Has the potential where the business scale can grow to be bigger

- Have a real nature and not just ambition

- Not too big business capital

- Has a promising profit.

To find business opportunities there are several strategies or steps that we can follow, which include:

- Determine in advance the source of business opportunities that we will look for. Sources of business opportunities can be from oneself such as hobbies, expertise, to educational background, and so on.

- Determine the right sector for us to be involved in. In addition to looking for sources of business opportunities, we must also be able to determine the right sector for us to be involved in and master. For example, whether to focus on the service sector or products.

- Focus on the potential strength of products that have business opportunities so that later they can have a high selling value.

- Continue to evaluate and take advantage of the opportunities we have in order to become a product.
