18. If Christine walked from his house 60m east and then walked back 30m west.What was Christine's total distance​


• Problem:

If Christine walked from his house 60m east and then walked back 30m west. What was Christine's total distance?

• Solution:

Distance is a scalar quantity, this means that direction is not needed compared to vector in which both magnitudes and directions are required. Example of vector quantity is displacement.

You just need to add the given quantities to get the total distance.

[tex] \tt60 \: m \: + 30 \: m = 90 \: m[/tex]

Thus, the total distance is 90 meters.

If you want to know the displacement, you need to subtract the given quantities and use the direction of the one that has the larger value.

[tex] \tt60 \: m \: East \: - 30 \: m \: West= 30 \: m \: East[/tex]

We can say that displacement is the shortcut of the distance. The displacement is 30 m East.

• Answer:

Christine's total distance is 90 meters.


19. Christine is 41 years old and her daughter is 9.In how many years will Christine will be three times as old as her daughter?with solution


Let x = years when Christine will be 3x as old as her daughter, therefore:

[tex] \frac{(41 + x)}{(9 + x)} = 3[/tex]

Solution:

First, eliminate the denominator:

[tex](9 + x) \times \frac{(41 + x)}{(9 + x)} = 3 \times (9 + x)[/tex]

[tex](41 + x) = 3 \times (9 + x)[/tex]

Then, simplify:

[tex]41 + x = 27 + 3x[/tex]

Finally, solve for x:

[tex]x - 3x = 27 - 41[/tex]

[tex] - 2x = - 14[/tex]

[tex] \frac{ - 2x}{ - 2} = \frac{ - 14}{ - 2} [/tex]

[tex]x = 7[/tex]

Meaning, Christine will be 3x as old as her daugther in the next 7 years.

Checking:

[tex] \frac{(41 + 7)}{(9 + 7)} = 3[/tex]

[tex] \frac{48}{16} = 3[/tex]

So, after 7 years, Christine will be 48 years old, which will be 3 times as old as her 16 years old daughter by then. (16 x 3 = 48)
