26. Ferdie planned to invest his 35,000 for 5 years at 5% compounded semi annually find the future value and interest of this amount ​


Answer:

In order to find the future value of Ferdie's investment, we need to know the principal amount, the interest rate, and the length of time the money will be invested. Based on the information you provided, Ferdie plans to invest $35,000 at a 5% interest rate for 5 years.

To find the future value of this investment, we can use the following formula:

Future Value = Principal x (1 + Interest Rate / Number of Compounding Periods) ^ (Number of Compounding Periods x Length of Time)

In this case, we have a principal of $35,000, an interest rate of 5%, a compounding period of twice per year (semi-annually), and a length of time of 5 years. Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

Future Value = $35,000 x (1 + 0.05 / 2) ^ (2 x 5)

Calculating this, we get a future value of $43,906.94. This means that after 5 years, Ferdie's investment will be worth $43,906.94. The interest earned on this investment will be $8,906.94, which is the difference between the initial investment of $35,000 and the future value of $43,906.94.

It's important to note that this calculation assumes that the interest is compounded semi-annually, meaning that the interest earned on the investment is added to the principal and re-invested twice per year. This has the effect of increasing the amount of interest earned over time, and ultimately results in a higher future value for the investment.
