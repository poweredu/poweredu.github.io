23. If December 25 is the birthday of Jesus, Why was he given birth in winter but didn't die from cold? Was the birthdate wrong or did he just got born in summer? Or the estimated time was April?


Answer:

(see explanation)

Explanation:

(disclaimer: I asked a priest about this)

Remember the Nativity Scene. There are lots of animals in the manger where Joseph and Mary stayed. Cold from winter isn't normally fatal, and if the animals did not die from the cold, then it is possible for a baby to stay alive. Evidence has also shown that despite being winter, sheep can still be grazed out on the field (Luke 2). There are also farming storage techniques that allow sheep to graze out (hay).

December 25 is not really the birthday of Jesus. It is an estimated time, and designated by the Church based on various accounts of scholars from the past. If we were to follow the calendar, it will take one week for the Epiphany (three kings) to happen, despite several accounts of it happening during the birth of Jesus, or years later. It is important to note that feasts are celebrated to remember what happened, and not because it is an anniversary of what happened.

You also have to account that our modern calendar was only made in the 14th century. The church then announced several dates as feast days to commemorate significant events like the birth of Jesus. Back in the time of Jesus, there was no calendar; time was recorded via astronomical events like the time it takes for the phases of the moon (new moon to full), positioning of stars, and even harvest time.

It is also important to remember that the writers of the bible books covered the details of what happened, and put focus on the theological and religious elements rather than historical elements.
