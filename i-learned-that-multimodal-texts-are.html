EXPLANATION:

Multimodal text is text in the form of a combination of written or spoken text and images or animation. Multimodal text is not only a verbal text, but a combination of both written and spoken text with images, audio, or video. Multimodal text combines language and other means of communication such as visual, audible, or spoken which is presented in a single text that is intact and present simultaneously. Multimodal text in the form of language, images, music, gestures, and architecture.

With reading material in the form of multimodal text, students not only gain understanding from reading the text, but also see pictures or listen to audio, and watch animations or videos. Multimodal text used as a medium in learning is a text consisting of various semiotic sources (verbal, motion, visual) which are deployed through various modalities as well as interaction and integration in the text coherence process. Thus, students will more easily understand the contents of the text, both expressed and implied, analyze the text, evaluate the meaning and benefits of the text, and reflect on the content of the text.

17. why is it important to Learn how to create multimodal texts?​


Creating multimodal texts

Multimodal texts incorporate two or more modalities, such as gestural, spatial, auditory, visual (both still and moving image), and written or spoken language (The New London Group, 2000; Cope and Kalantzis, 2009). Communication technologies are used to create digital multimodal texts, although multimodal texts can also be paper-based or live performances.

Further explanation

In modern schools, creating multimodal texts is becoming more and more prevalent. Multimodal texts are simple to create, and examples include posters, storyboards, oral presentations, picture books, brochures, PowerPoint slide shows, blogs, and podcasts. Web pages, digital tales, interactive stories, animation, and cinema are examples of more advanced multimodal text outputs in the digital age.

The ability to accurately, fluently, and imaginatively produce multimodal writings for many audiences and purposes is a must for student authors. Students must understand how different textual modes are utilized to convey meaning as well as how different modes can be used in combination to express a story or a piece of information.

Students must learn how to creatively and deliberately select which different modes could best express a certain meaning at different points in their texts and how to combine multiple modes in different ways across the entire text to best tell their tale (Jewitt, 2009).

30. What is the Definition of Paper-based multimodal text?Live multimodal text? and Digital multimodal text?​


Paper-based multimodal text is text that is composed of multiple modalities (such as text, audio, video, and/or graphics) and is displayed on a single sheet of paper. Live multimodal text is text that is created and displayed simultaneously in multiple modalities. Digital multimodal text is text that is composed of multiple modalities and is stored in digital format.
