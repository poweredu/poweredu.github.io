23. Which of the following is not a step in addition of dissimilar fractions? *A.Copy the common denominator of the addendsB.Change the addends into similar fractionsC.Add the numerators of the addendsD.Add the denominators of the addends​


Which of the following is not a step in addition of dissimilar fractions?

Answer: B. Change the addends into similar fractions

Adding and Subtracting Dissimilar FractionsWe are only concerned with the numerators when adding or subtracting similar fractions.  Meanwhile, dealing with numerators and denominators is required when adding or subtracting dissimilar fractions.

Step-by-stepStep #1All unlike fractions (dissimilar fractions) must be converted into like fractions (similar fractions).  To do so, we must convert the fractions' denominators into a common denominator. Remember that similar fractions have denominators that are the same, common, or similar.The common denominator must be a multiple of the denominators. That is, the common denominator can be divided by all of the denominators without leaving any remainder.For any fraction, there are an infinite number of common denominators, but we will only consider the smallest or lowest one. This is referred to as the least common denominator, or LCD.Finally, using the obtained common denominator, compute the equivalent fractions of those fractions.

Step #2Follow the same steps as when adding or subtracting similar fractions.We'll use the same method for adding or subtracting similar fractions as before:Subtract or add the numerators.Take note of the denominators.Convert the final answer to the shortest term possible.

Example No.1 Find the sum of 1/4 and 1/2.The first step is to find the common denominator of 1/4 and 1/2. It is equivalent to determining the least common multiple of 4 and 2.  As shown above, our least common denominator is 4. The next step is to make 4 our new denominator. Then, using 4 as the denominator, we will find the equivalent fractions of our original fractions. 1/4 will remain unchanged. The denominator is already 4. 2/4 is the equivalent fraction of 1/2 with 4 as the denominator. We now have 1/4 plus 2/4. Because they are similar fractions, we can easily add them now. 3/4 is equal to 1/4 plus 2/4. We simply add the denominators.Since our sum which is equal to 3/4 is already in the lowest term, 3/4 is the final answer.
