1. What protects the eyes of bee's?


Answer:

Bees have five eyes, two large compound eyes and three ocelli. The compound eyes are situated on either side of the bee's head (Figure 3). The compound eyes are composed of thousands of little lenses or facets. Together, the facets help bees see color, movement, and patterns.
