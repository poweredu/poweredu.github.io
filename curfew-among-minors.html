3. Ideas about curfew for minors​


Parents impose curfews to protect their teens, and teens often feel this is an unfair limitation placed upon them. Curfews imposed by state and local governments are even more controversial because it is not only teenagers who disagree with these laws, but many adults as well.

Teen Curfews Are a Traditional Rite of Passage

Teenager curfews have been around for centuries as the adult population has tried to keep adolescents safe. Most adults can remember being annoyed when hearing their parents tell them they had to be home at a particular time. Many adults also experienced being "grounded for life" for violating a curfew. Adhering to a curfew is one of the first ways many people learned to handle the responsibilities that go along with freedom.

Explanation:

Curfews help teens to become used to setting a schedule which they routinely follow, and become able to develop important time management skills which they will carry out into adulthood. Being able to manage time effectively is useful in a variety of situations including school, work, and personal relationships.

Parents have many motivations for imposing curfews on teenagers. The first one is because they care for their teen and want them to be safe. Parents have a responsibility to their children both morally and legally to keep them safe and monitor their activities. It is also a matter of social courtesy to know when to expect a teenager to be home. Parents need to know when their child is coming home so that they can get some sleep.

Opinion about the Legal Curfew for Minors

By assuming that all young people are potential lawbreakers, the presumption of innocence is reversed. They are also subjected to blanket age discrimination, despite the fact that only a small percentage of young people commit criminal offenses and that adults commit crime as well. Furthermore, curfews violate parents' rights to raise their children as they see fit. We should not intervene simply because we disagree with how some parents treat their children.

There are numerous legitimate reasons for children in their mid-teens to be out late at night without adults. Many will work part-time, such as in fast-food restaurants or delivering newspapers. Others may wish to participate in church groups, youth clubs, or school trips. Requiring adults to always drive them to and from such activities is unreasonable, and it will ensure that many of them never take place in the first place, either because adults are unwilling or unable to do so. More sinisterly, some children are abused at home and feel safer out on the streets.

17. advantage and disadvantages of Curfew Hours for Minors (17 years and below)​


Answer:

Advantages of Teenage Curfew

1. It can be a way to give families access to the services they require.

2. Most curfew laws allow teens to be outside after the permitted time for specific reasons.

3. It reduces the threat of human trafficking and child exploitation.

4. Teenage curfew can teach lessons about personal responsibility.

5. Curfews can provide peace of mind for families.

6. It is an opportunity for parents and teens to set healthy boundaries.

7. Curfew laws can work to prevent gang violence.

Disadvantages of Teenage Curfew

1. It provides parents and teens with a false sense of security.

2. Most curfews are set from an autocratic standpoint.

3. Some teenage curfew rules can encourage rebellion.

4. There can be legal consequences for violating curfews.

5. It impacts minority teens more than any other demographic.

6. Teenage curfew laws create unnecessary conflicts with today’s youth and police.

7. The data to support the idea of curfews is lacking.

8. Neighborhoods without curfew sweeps often see less crime.

9. Teenage curfew does not set an example of how the real world works.

10. There are other interventions that reduce youth crime more effectively than curfews.
