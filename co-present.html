20. calculate the number of molecules present in 6.40 moles of CO


Answer:

To calculate the number of molecules present in a given amount of a substance, we can use the Avogadro's number. Avogadro's number is defined as the number of atoms, ions, or molecules in one mole of a substance. The value of Avogadro's number is 6.022 x 10^23.

To calculate the number of molecules present in 6.40 moles of CO, we can use the following formula:

Explanation:

Number of molecules = Avogadro's number x moles of substance

For CO, the number of molecules would be:

6.022 x 10^23 x 6.40 = 3.814 x 10^24

So there are 3.814 x 10^24 CO molecules present in 6.40 moles of CO.

28. If 100 L of a gas solution contains 0.006 L of carbon monoxide (CO), what is the ppm of CO present in the solution?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_{\text{solute}} = \text{0.006 L} \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{100 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the concentration of the solution in ppm.

[tex]\begin{aligned} ppm & = \frac{V_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \times 10^6 \\ & = \frac{\text{0.006 L}}{\text{100 L}} \times 10^6 \\ & = \boxed{\text{60 ppm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the concentration of a solution is 60 ppm.

30. Determine the number of carbon dioxide (CO₂) molecules present in 24.5 grams of the compound.Please make a solution step by step. Thank you. ​


Answer:

5.3 x 10^23

Explanation:

To determine the number of carbon dioxide molecules present in 24.5 grams of the compound, we can use the following steps:

Calculate the molar mass of carbon dioxide by adding the atomic masses of its constituent elements: carbon (12 g/mol) + oxygen (16 g/mol) = 28 g/mol

Divide the mass of the sample (24.5 g) by the molar mass of carbon dioxide (28 g/mol) to calculate the number of moles: 24.5 g / 28 g/mol = 0.879 mol

Multiply the number of moles by Avogadro's constant (6.022 x 10^23 molecules/mol) to calculate the number of carbon dioxide molecules: 0.879 mol * (6.022 x 10^23 molecules/mol) = 5.3 x 10^23 molecules

Therefore, there are approximately 5.3 x 10^23 carbon dioxide molecules present in 24.5 grams of the compound.
