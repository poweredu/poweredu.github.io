First Mass in the Philippines

After the Spanish ship Concepcion caught fire off the coast of Bohol on May 2, 500 years ago, Antonio Pigafetta and Francisco Albo, both survivors of the Magellan-Elcano expedition, stopped including dates in their records of the first circumnavigation of the globe.

Francisco Albo's accountAlbo's story focused solely on the cross's placement atop a mountain from which three islands could be seen to the west and southwest and left out any reference of the first Mass.One is the journal that Magellan's ship, Trinidad's pilot, Francisco Albo, kept. He was one of the 18 survivors who made the voyage around the world with Sebastian Elcano and returned on the ship Victoria.

Antonio Pigafetta's account.Pigafetta mentions in his narrative that the first Mass takes place in Butuan on March 31, which is Easter Sunday.Primo viaggio intorno al mundo (First Voyage around the World). Like Albo, Pigafetta was a part of the Magellan expedition and a first-hand observer of the events, particularly the first Mass.Pigafetta is regarded as a reliable source since famous figures in the west who interpreted the new world, such as William Shakespeare, Michel de Montaigne, and Giambattista Vico, cited his work as a classic. When researching the pre-colonial Philippines, historians frequently referred to the chronicle of Pigafetta.

According to (Republic Act No. 2733), the law and the government state that the first mass was actually celebrated in Masao, Butuan. As a result, the researchers conclude that the first mass was held in Masao, Butuan and not in Limasawa Leyte.
