Answer:

The given sentence, "3 Pig #3 pities big bad wolf so, he helps him get out of the fire," is a part of the falling action of the story.

Explanation:

In a plot, the falling action follows the climax and leads to the resolution. It is the part of the story where the consequences of the climax are revealed, and loose ends are tied up. In this case, the climax of the story would be the moment of highest tension or conflict, which would likely involve the wolf and pigs. The falling action would then follow, showing how the story's conflicts and tensions begin to be resolved. In this case, the falling action involves Pig #3 helping the big bad wolf get out of the fire, which shows that the characters are beginning to overcome their differences and work together.
