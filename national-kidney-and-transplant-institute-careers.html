RESPONSIBILITY OF THE PHILIPPINE HEART CENTER

The PHILIPPINE HEART CENTER is the leader in upholding the highest standards of cardiovascular care, a self-reliant institution responsive to the health needs of the Filipino people. To provide compassionate and expert patient care. To provide world-class education and training.

SPECIALIZATION OF THE PHILIPPINE HEART CENTER

The Philippine Heart Center is a hospital specializing in the treatment of heart ailments. It has rooms for paying patients and charity patients and admits more than 14,000 patients every year, including 3,300 that undergo heart surgery. It holds regular training programs for medical professionals.

RESPONSIBILITY OF THE NATIONAL KIDNEY AND TRANSPLANT INSTITUTE

Our goal is to provide world class health care to all patients and their families and to pursue excellence in developing and establishing the highest level of training and research for physicians and paramedical personnel.

SPECIALIZATION OF THE NATIONAL KIDNEY AND TRANSPLANT INSTITUTE

Each hospital has its own field of specialization and NKTI is presently the only government hospital specialized in renal diseases and organ transplantation. It is the center for referral of kidney patients from various regional hospitals nationwide and is recognized as the lead agency in voluntary blood services.

2. sino ang direktor ng national kidney institute​

Answer:

DR. AILEEN RIEGO - JAVIER

Explanation:

A lady physician is now taking on the cudgels of steering NKTI to continue its vision-mission as a world class renowned transplant Institute. She carries the distinction as the first lady Executive Director who specializes in renal pathology. Dr. Aileen Riego - Javier was sworn in by Health Secretary Enrique T. Ona last November of 2010 making her the fourth director of the Institute.

Dr. Javier is an alumna of the University of the Philippines finishing degrees in Bachelor of Science and in medicine after which taking up post-graduate training in pathology at the Philippine General Hospital (UP-PGH). As a fellow, she trained at the General and Renal Pathology of the University of Singapore and likewise at the Pediatric Pathology at the Babies’ Hospital, Columbia Presbyterian Medical Center in New York.

The pleasant and soft spoken Executive Director pioneered renal pathology in the Philippines. She is the president of the Philippine Blood Coordinating Council and the Philippine Society of Pathologists. Dr. Javier helped set up the Anatomic Pathology Division of three specialty centers to which she has been part of notably Lungsod ng Kabataan, the Lung Center of the Philippines and that of NKTI. The Institute uses an electron microscope that gives accurate diagnosis of biopsies not only for the hospital’s use but for others as well too. Dr. Javier stresses that pathology is decisive and fundamental in many areas of medical care. Doctors are dependent on the pathologist’s diagnosis of biopsies.

Moreover, just this year, Dr. Javier was a finalist in the Government Service Category of the l9th Dr. Jose P. Rizal Memorial Awards. This award selects outstanding Filipino physicians whose life and work embody the ideals of our national hero: in the conduct of their practice, in the way they live life reflective of Rizal’s love for God and country, and in the selfless service to fellowmen, especially the poor and the underprivileged. Dr. Javier received a medallion and a P150,000.00 worth of medicines from Unilab to be donated to a charitable institution of one’s choice. To be commended as a finalist in the search is very significant to her considering how she emulates the character and attitude of our national hero in her dealings with colleagues, employees and patients of the Institute.

In her assumption of the challenges of being the new Executive Director, we wish her all the success in her endeavors in the pursuit of medical excellence for NKTI. Congratulations!

By Ronald S. Lora

Making a Career Choice

Family

In making some decisions, my family plays a vital role in life. They are the ones who guide me and gives opinions and suggestion on what is best for me. Some of their advice and experiences in life benefit a lot, particularly in choosing the right career that I wanted in life. And they support me on what I choose as what they want to be who I am. Family is our number supporter that will guide our decision-making.

Peers or classmates

These people are the ones who can give some information and knowledge to a specific path since they come upon some research. Also, it can give me some insights into what I want to choose, and they will support it no matter what. They become a motivator for me to stick to my mind and choose the right one for myself.

School

Here, it can teach us and develop us to become better people on what track you choose. It can help me improve my skills and see what career I should be. It makes me realize some things in life that I wanted and decide in line with it. And, attending classes at school gives me knowledge on different aspects of choosing my career.

Others

It pertains to some individuals who are not close with us but respect and show concern unto our life. They act as listeners to our stories and make some advice that might help us.

Remember:

One important thing in life is that the help of our family, peers, classmates, school and even other people can make us decide what kind of career we will choose. They act as guides and advisors that benefits us if we struggle in this situation.
