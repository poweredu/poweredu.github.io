9. The height of Tanya's baby at birth is 50 cm. Tanya measures the height of her baby monthly. She noted that on the average, the height of her baby increases by 1 cm monthly.​


Answer:

3. Assuming that the growth of the baby is constant and linear, we can represent it as a linear function:

g(m) = m + 50

where g(m) is the height of the baby in centimeters after m months, and 50 is the initial height of the baby at birth.

Since the growth of the baby is said to be 1 cm per month, this means that the slope of the function is 1. Therefore, for every additional month, the height of the baby increases by 1 cm.

Hence, the function that represents the growth of Tanya's baby is:

g(m) = m + 50.

4. Since there are 12 months in a year, we can substitute m = 12 into the function we obtained earlier:

g(12) = 12 + 50

= 62

Therefore, Tanya's baby would be 62 cm tall after 1 year, assuming that the growth rate remains constant and linear.
