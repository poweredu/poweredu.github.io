24. anu ano ang mga produktong may sangkap na caffeine​


Anu ano ang mga produktong may sangkap na caffeine

Caffeine Foods and Drinks Dark Chocolate Coated Coffee Beans Coffee Energy Drinks Espresso Sodas Green Tea Black Tea Dark Chocolate Coffee Liqueur Chocolate Cake with Frosting

Caffeine is a naturally occurring chemical contained in a variety of plant foods and beverages. Caffeine supplements, both synthetic and natural, are also manufactured and added to foods. Caffeine's calming and addictive properties are causing it to make its way through an increasing number of unhealthy items, like sodas, candies, and energy drinks. Caffeine's health effects and costs are debatable, with several research documenting both benefits and health issues caused by caffeine. Caffeine's upper intake limit (percent UL) is 400mg for adults and less than 85mg for children. Caffeine-rich foods and beverages include chocolate-covered coffee beans, coffee, energy drinks, espresso, sodas, green tea, black tea, dark chocolate, coffee liqueur, and chocolate-flavored baked goods. The caffeine content in all of these foods and beverages is average. Caffeine content varies depending on how intensely tea or coffee is brewed, as well as how much caffeine is added to different colas and energy drinks.

Does Medication Contain Caffeine?

Despite common belief that caffeine may aggravate premenstrual symptoms, newer literature indicates that it might not be as dangerous as commonly assumed. In reality, caffeine, which is used to alleviate bloating and nausea, is used in some drugs sold to relieve premenstrual symptoms. Caffeine is also applied to certain nutritional supplements and pain relievers, such as headache medication.

Should I Avoid Foods with Caffeine?

If you are caffeine sensitive or suffer from insomnia, it is beneficial to be aware of which substances to avoid. To be on the safe side, avoid caffeine within four to six hours of going to bed. Caffeine should be used with caution by children, the elderly, pregnant women, and other vulnerable groups. A few slices of chocolate or a pain killer are unlikely to contain enough caffeine to greatly impact sleep quality for most people. Having said that, eating mindfully may make you sleep well and can also help you from having bad dreams.
