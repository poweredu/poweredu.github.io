1. Shape: A parallelogram is a quadrilateral with opposite sides that are parallel and equal in length. If the wallet has four sides that are parallel to each other, and the opposite sides are of equal length, then it would indeed be a parallelogram.

2. Angles: In a parallelogram, the opposite angles are equal. If the wallet has opposite angles that measure the same, then it would support Elsie's claim.

3. Diagonals: The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other, meaning they intersect at a 90-degree angle and divide each other into equal segments. If Elsie's wallet exhibits this property, it could be evidence that it is indeed a parallelogram.

4. Symmetry: Parallelograms typically exhibit a sense of symmetry, where one half is a mirror image of the other half. If Elsie's wallet displays this kind of symmetry, it could be indicative of a parallelogram shape.

5. Documentation or Design: If Elsie has a receipt, a product label, or any other form of documentation that explicitly states or depicts the wallet as a parallelogram, or if the wallet has a distinct design that resembles a parallelogram, then that would be conclusive evidence supporting her claim.
