21. Biography of filomena calendrino​


Answer:

Filomena Calendrino (1890-1962) was an Italian-American artist and sculptor. She was born in Naples, Italy and immigrated to the United States in 1912. She studied at the Art Students League of New York and later taught at the school for many years. Her work is characterized by its strong sense of movement, texture, and color.

Calendrino's sculptures often featured figures from classical mythology or religious subjects such as angels or saints. She also created abstract works that explored themes of nature, music, and dance. Her most famous sculpture is a bronze bust of Pope Pius XII which she created for St Patrick's Cathedral in New York City in 1950.

Calendrino exhibited her work widely throughout her career including shows at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, Whitney Museum of American Art, Corcoran Gallery of Art in Washington D.C., Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts, Carnegie Institute in Pittsburgh among others. In addition to her sculptures she also painted landscapes and portraits which were exhibited at galleries throughout Europe including Paris' Salon des Independants as well as galleries across America such as The National Academy Galleries in New York City where she had a solo show in 1951

In 1962 Calendrino died suddenly while on vacation with friends near Rome Italy leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire artists today with her unique style that combined elements from both classical art forms with modern techniques creating works that are timeless yet still relevant today.
