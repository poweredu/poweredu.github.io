"I have an imaginary ex" is a poem about the narrator's desire to forget their past love and move on, but finding it difficult to do so as the memories of the relationship linger in their mind. The poem explores the theme of heartbreak and the difficulty of letting go, even when the relationship is no longer viable. It also touches on the idea of imaginary love, where the narrator is still attached to an idea of their ex that is not necessarily reflective of reality. The poem is a reflection on the complexities of love and the lingering effects it can have on a person even after the relationship has ended.

Answer:

The theme/message of "My Imaginary Ex" by Mina V. Esguerra is about finding closure and moving on from past relationships. The story also explores the idea of letting go of the idealized version of an ex and accepting their flaws and mistakes.

The story portrays the protagonist, Camille, as initially fixated on the idea of her ex-boyfriend being perfect, but as the story progresses, she learns to accept his flaws and the reality of their relationship. This is shown through her interactions with her imaginary ex, who gradually becomes less idealized and more realistic. Additionally, Camille's growth is demonstrated through her willingness to pursue a new romantic interest and her ability to let go of her past relationship.

Overall, the story's message of finding closure and accepting reality is relatable and valuable in real-life situations. Whether or not someone agrees with the author ultimately depends on their personal beliefs and experiences.
