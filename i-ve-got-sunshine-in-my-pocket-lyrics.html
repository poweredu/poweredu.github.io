6. I have some chocolates in my pocket. I buy 4 more. I gave 2 to Fred and 5 to Joshua.if I have10 chocolates left how many chocolates did I have in my pocket initially


MATH: INTRODUCTION TO ALGEBRAQuestion

I have some chocolates in my pocket. I buy 4 more. I gave 2 to Fred and 5 to Joshua. If I have 10 chocolates left how many chocolates did I have in my pocket initially?

Answer:13 chocolatesWhat is asked

The initial number of chocolates in the pocket.

What are given

After buying 4 more chocolates, the total number of chocolates becomes 10.

2 chocolates were given to Fred and 5 chocolates were given to Joshua.

What is/are the operation/operations to be used? (Solve initially)

We need to find out the initial number of chocolates in the pocket, so we need to work backwards from the current situation.

First, we know that after giving 2 + 5 = 7 chocolates, there are 10 chocolates left.

So, initially there were 10 + 7 = 17 chocolates in the pocket.

Then, we subtract the 4 chocolates that were bought to get the initial number of chocolates:

17 - 4 = 13.

Solution/Checking

The initial number of chocolates in the pocket was 13.

So we have the following equation:

x + 4 - 2 - 5 = 10

where x is the initial number of chocolates in the pocket. Solving for x, we get:

x + 4 - 2 - 5 = 10

x + 4 - 7 = 10

x - 3 = 10

13 - 3 = 10

Therefore, x = 13

Final answer:13 chocolates
