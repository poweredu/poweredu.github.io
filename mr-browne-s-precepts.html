7. Nine years ago, Mr. Brown was 2 and 1⁄2 times as old as Mr. Agnew. On the other hand, six years from now Mr. Agnew will be only 6/10 as old as Mr. Brown. What are the present age of these two men?


QUESTION

Nine years ago, Mr. Brown was 2 and 1⁄2 times as old as Mr. Agnew. On the other hand, six years from now Mr. Agnew will be only 6/10 as old as Mr. Brown. What are the present age of these two men?

SOLUTION

We will make a table using the given facts about the problem:

Name Past Present Future

Brown 5/2(x-9) x (x+6)

Agnew y-9 y 6/10(y+6)

The equation will be like this: We will add 15 years because if the past is 9 yrs from the present age. So we will add 6 from 9 it will become 15 and the equation will be equal.

Solving for the age of Brown:

5/2(x-9)+15 = x+6

We will multiply 2 to the both sides to remove the rational:

2(5/2(x-9)+15) = 2(x+6)

10/2(x-9)+15 = 2(x+6)

5(x-9)+15 = 2x+12

5x-45+15 = 2x+12

5x-30 = 2x+12

5x-2x = 12+30

3x = 42

x = 42÷3

x = 14

» Present age of Brown is 14

Solving for the age of Agnew:

The equation will be like this: We will add 15 years because if the past is 9 yrs from the present age. So we will add 6 from 9 it will become 15 and the equation will be equal.

(y-9)+15 = 6/10(y+6)

y+6 = 6/10(y+6)

We will multiply 10 to the both sides to remove the rational:

10(y+6) = 10(6/10(y+6))

10(y+6) = 60/10(y+6)

10y+60 = 6(y+6)

10y+60 = 6y+36

10y-6y = 36-60

4y = -24

y = -6

We will get the positive value of y ,

» Present age of agnew is 6

Now , We will complete the table without the conditions:

From this:

Name Past Present Future

Brown x-9 x (x+6)

Agnew y-9 y (y+6)

____________________________

To this:

Name Past Present Future

Agnew -3 6 12

____________________________

ANSWER

Brown's age is 14 years old While Agnew's age is 6 years old in their present ages

11. 1. How do the Five Precepts of Buddhism and Bodhisattva Precepts differ from each other?​


The first precept consists of a prohibition of killing, both humans and all animals. Scholars have interpreted Buddhist texts about the precepts as an opposition to and prohibition of capital punishment,[8] suicide, abortion[9][10] and euthanasia.[11] In practice, however, many Buddhist countries still use the death penalty. With regard to abortion, Buddhist countries take the middle ground, by condemning though not prohibiting it fully. The Buddhist attitude to violence is generally interpreted as opposing all warfare, but some scholars have raised exceptions found in later texts.

The second precept prohibits theft and related activities such as fraud and forgery.

The third precept refers to adultery in all its forms, and has been defined by modern teachers with terms such as sexual responsibility and long-term commitment.

The fourth precept involves falsehood spoken or committed to by action, as well as malicious speech, harsh speech and gossip.

The fifth precept prohibits intoxication through alcohol, drugs or other means.[12][13] Early Buddhist Texts nearly always condemn alcohol, and so do Chinese Buddhist post-canonical texts. Smoking is sometimes also included in here.

17. Define the five precepts of the church?​


Answer:

precepts of the Church, also called the commandments of the Church, are duties that the Catholic Church requires of all the faithful.

Explanation:

1.The Sunday Duty

The first precept of the Church is "You shall attend Mass on Sundays and holy days of obligation and rest from servile labor."

2.Confession

The second precept of the Church is "You shall confess your sins at least once a year."

3.The Easter Duty

The third precept of the Church is "You shall receive the sacrament of the Eucharist at least during the Easter season."

4.Fasting and Abstinence

he fourth precept of the Church is "You shall observe the days of fasting and abstinence established by the Church.

5.Supporting the Church

The fifth precept of the Church is "You shall help to provide for the needs of the Church."
