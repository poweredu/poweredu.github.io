27. why do government agencies conduct research before having a project proposal for the community?​


Answer:

Historically, the public has been involved in identifying and addressing problems in the fields of government agencies. This study focused on individual risk factors rather than social risk factors, separating researchers from public experts and the community.

The growing disparity between rich and poor, as well as between racial groups, demonstrates that social and local environmental factors play a significant role. Furthermore, while this research has improved public knowledge and status, this knowledge and its application frequently diverge.

As a promise of community-based research, this inequity and action gap can be addressed. Public community-based research focused on "social, structural, and environmental physical inequalities through active participation of community members, organizational representatives, and researchers in all aspects of the research process." Partners contribute their expertise to the understanding of a specific phenomenon and to the implementation of knowledge throughout this process. This is the paradigm of community-based research: a comprehensive approach to public affairs with the potential to illuminate local care barriers while allowing the community to participate.

Because many community research studies include surveys, focus groups, and some interactive components, researchers are frequently in a unique position to collaborate with community members and organizations. Indeed, much community-based research aims to identify and build on the community's strengths, resources, and relationships in order to address communal concerns. These may include the abilities and assets of individuals or networks, such as churches and other community institutions. Partnerships may include representatives from health, service, and academic organizations, as well as community organizations and members.

Community research is a collaborative process in which the researcher solves the puzzle and morbidity.

The various societal, cultural, and logistical barriers that stymie the well-intended efforts of numerous public programs necessitate community-based research. In addition to increasing knowledge, this research has the potential to empower communities all over the world through collaboration and partnership. As the field of community-based research grows, so does the opportunity for student researchers.
