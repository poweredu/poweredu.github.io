3. my dream society essay​


Every member of my ideal society has a voice. Everyone has a place to express their ideas, emotions, and opinions. The heart of the place I'd like to live is freedom of speech. In a lot of respects, this is true in the world I live in. The difference between my fantasy world and reality is that everyone can recognize the strength of their own voice. Not only in terms of material, but also in terms of presentation.

In my ideal world, we will teach this to children as part of their daily education so that as adults, they would comfortably and openly contribute to society. Children can do this in the world we live in through extracurricular activities. Many who participate in the artistic and expressive arts are more likely to be comfortable with their own voices.

We free our speech by learning these things. We express our ideas by allowing ourselves to speak freely. We develop and expand our communities by sharing our ideas.

My ideal world isn't as far-fetched as it may seem. Perhaps yours isn't, either.

Explanation:

In my dream society, all members have a voice. Everyone has a space to share their opinions, thoughts and feelings. Freedom of speech is the essence of the place I’d like to inhabit. In most ways, this is in place in the world I inhabit. However, the difference between my dream world and reality is that everyone has the ability to understand the power of their voice. Not just in the content, but in the vocal mechanism itself.

Through teaching for the last ten years, it has become apparent to me, that most people think that individuals are gifted with a beautiful speaking voice. A voice that eases into their listener’s ears and transports us with their stories, awakens us with their ideas and moves us with their view. Maybe some people are lucky to have been born with this skill. However I believe that everyone can unleash this marvellous power and use their voice to their full potential, if they are shown how.
