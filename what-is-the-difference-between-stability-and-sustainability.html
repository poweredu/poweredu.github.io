What is Sustainable Development?

Sustainable development can be understood in a broad sense as activities in the field aimed at meeting current development needs without reducing the carrying capacity of resources for future generations to meet their needs.

The challenge of sustainable development is to find ways to increase well-being while using natural resources wisely.

Development policies should take into account the need to restructure the foundations of property management systems in rural areas. The redesign is more about integration into multiple uses, namely the economy and the environment/ecosystem.

Although understanding the agricultural system is a complex management, its success can be seen and shaped by indicators such as:

- Contribution to local environmental sustainability,

- Contribution to the sustainable use of natural resources,

- Contribution to employment growth,

- Contribution to macroeconomic sustainability, profitability,

- And contribute to technical independence.

Four general aspects are the most important concrete features of the concept of an agricultural system : equity, sustainability (sustainability), stability (stability), and productivity (energy).

Their definition simply:

- Equity is an assessment of the extent to which the outcomes of a resource environment are distributed among its inhabitants.

- Sustainability can be defined as the ability of a resource system to maintain its productivity despite various constraints.

- Stability is a measure of the extent to which resource productivity does not change due to fluctuations in environmental factors.

- Productivity is a measure of the physical or economic production of resources. Within the framework of sustainable rural development, resource management in the village must be carried out with a model that ensures environmental sustainability, maintains biological balance, preserves and even improves the quality of resources so that they can continue to be empowered, and adopts a sustainable and efficient resource use model.

8. Differenciate thestabilityfrom Sustainability​


Stability indicates the balance while sustainability indicates the able to be maintained at a certain rate or level.

EXPLANATION:

Stability

Stability is a condition of a system whose components tend to remain in or return to a relationship that has been established. Stability is the absence of fundamental or chaotic changes in a system or changes that occur within agreed or defined limits. Stability is stable, balance and determination. In an institution stability is the stability of a conducive situation so that a company or an institution can run well.

Sustainability

Sustainability is the durability of a system and process. The organizing principle of sustainability is a sustainable development, which includes four interconnected domains, namely ecology, economy, politics and culture. Sustainability science is the study of sustainable development and environmental science. The term 'sustainability' can be defined as a socio-ecological process characterized by the attainment of the same ideals.

Ideals by definition are unattainable in a certain time and space. However, with a continuous, dynamic approach, the process produces a sustainable system. Healthy ecosystems and environments are necessary for the survival of humans and other organisms. The way to reduce negative human impacts is through environmentally friendly chemical engineering, environmental resource management and environmental protection.

10. 1. What are the two rule of stability of letters ? Describe each stabilityof letters.2. What are the general proportion of letters? When do we use eachgeneral proportion of letters?3. What is the difference between boldface and lightface?​


General Proportions.—Before combining letters into words we must be familiar in detail with the forms and peculiarities of each letter. Letters vary in their proportion of width to height. Not only are the widths of the different letters in the same alphabet very unequal, but different alphabets vary in their "measure," some being tall and narrow, others short and wide. There is a certain proportion or appear­ance as in the ordinary printed or drawn letters which may be called normal or standard. The styles whose widths are less than these in propor­tion are called compressed or condensed, and those whose widths are greater are known as expanded or extended.

There is also in the different styles a wide variation in the proportion of the thickness of the stem or stroke of the letters to their height, ranging all the way from 1/3 to 1/16. Letters with heavy stems are called Bold Face or Black Face, and those with thin stems, Light Face.

There is an optical illusion well known to all design­ers, in which a horizontal line drawn across the middle of a rectangle appears to be below the middle. In order that the divisions may seem to be symmetrical such a line must be drawn above the middle. In the construction of letters this illusion must be provided for in what may be called the "rule of stability." In order to give the appearance of stability such letters as the B E K S X and Z, with the figures 3 and 8 must be drawn smaller at the top than the bottom. To see the effect of this illusion turn a printed page upside down and notice the letters mentioned.
