9. what do you think could be the reasons why Juan Luna, Felix Hidalgo and, Fabian de la Rosa chose to study arts in europe?​


Answer:

In 1877 Manuel and Juan Luna traveled to Europe, where Manuel studied music and Juan painting. Juan entered the Escuela de Bellas Artes de San Fernando, where he befriended the painter Don Alejo Vera. Luna was discontented with the style of teaching in school and decided that it would be much better to work with Vera.

Vera brought him to Rome for some of his commissions, and Luna was exposed to the art of the Renaissance painters. It was in 1878 when his artistic talents was established with the opening of the first art exposition in Madrid which was called the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes (National Demonstration of Fine Arts). From then on, Luna became engrossed in painting and produced a collection of paintings that he exhibited in the 1881 Exposition.

In 1881, his La Muerte de Cleopatra (The Death of Cleopatra)[1][2] won him a silver medal and came in second place. Luna's growing reputation as an artist led to a pensionado (pension) scholarship at 600 pesos annually through the Ayuntamiento of Manila. The condition was that he was obliged to develop a painting which captured the essence of Philippine history which would then become the Ayuntamiento's property.

18. description of marikina valley undated fabian de la rosa​


Answer:

The Marikina Valley

When I write this essay, I thought the instruction was to find an art inside Vargas museum which will captivate you and a reaction about in a descriptive way. Maybe I was thinking of something else while my teacher gives the instruction. well anyway, this is the edited version though I only made some minor adjustment, since time is not a luxury I have for quite a while now. "The Marikina Valley", is a painting made by Fabian De la Rosa.

The scenic painting reminds me of the rural rice fields back at my home province. The rice stalks sway as the wind blows, gives a pleasant sight, like the waves in the ocean. The farmers, who smile at you when you greet them is a perfect representation of the Filipino's positive attitude towards life. This view is often witnessed along the highway in the middle of the fields. Whenever I see a scene like this, a two-pronged nostalgia captures me, as it brings me back to lost childhood I once had, where I spent my youthful days running and playing in a filed like this. It also reminds me that large land area in the Philippines were once blanketed with a patchwork of rice paddies, though little by little they are turned into subdivisions and infrastructure. To think that Marikina valley once was like this, makes me wonder that maybe in the not so distant future, this scenery will be just a myth, preserved and can only be witness in a painting such as this. I may have not been born during the era of this masterpiece of Fabian De la Rosa, But looking back does not need the ability to see backwards in time, just the opportunity to look somewhere else.
