17. 1. Why is the concept of Holy Trinity confusing for some Christians and non-Christians alike?


The concept of Holy Trinity goes like this, when you are looking at a single object, but using three (3) different angles, you are seeing different views of a single thing. It is confusing because, you are seeing three (3) different people or symbols. When we are seeing objects or people, we are giving them characters and recognized them as different entities. Also, confusions are experienced by people who don't read the bible or haven't understood the bible at all. Explanations are indicated in the bible but, it requires deep understanding as some are embedded in scriptures.

The Holy Trinity is pertaining to one God, but he has three (3) forms, God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit. One God, but his 3 personas are united.  

During the Old Testament, most of the prophets and characters in the Bible interact with God, being described as a Supreme Being, with older form. He is God the Father which symbolizes power and justice. He represents Past and normally encountered during the earliest times in the bible.  

The second persona is God the Son, who we known as Jesus Christ. He is interpreted as younger than the Father. He is human-like, kind and he is loved by many. His life began in the New Testament and went to heaven after his Resurrection. He is communicating to the Father by means of talking to him in Spirit.

The last persona of God is the Holy Spirit. After Jesus went to heaven, his Apostles continued his teachings and works. He communicates to most of them in form of Light and a pure white dove, which is the symbol of the Holy Spirit. Since the Father and Son are both already in Heaven, God will continue to communicate to us, his people by the Holy Spirit.
