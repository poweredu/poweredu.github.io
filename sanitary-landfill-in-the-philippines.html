1. meaning of sanitary landfill​


Answer:

Sanitary landfill is a modern engineering landfill where waste is allowed to decompose into biologically and chemically inert materials in a setting isolated from the environment.

Sanitary landfills are sites where waste is isolated from the environment until it is safe. It is considered when it has completely degraded biologically.

Prevents the burning of waste which is the major cause of air pollution.Landfills are an excellent energy sourceModern Landfills are Eco-friendly.Sanitary landfills keep cities, towns, and districts clean.They provide a place to keep more hazardous materials that need to be segregated from the public.

Disadvantages of sanitary landfill:

Landfills are partially responsible for climate change.Landfill sites have negative effects on plants and animals.People living near or prolonged exposure to landfills are most likely to have cancer and respiratory disorders.Landfill can pollute air, water and also the soil. 27. As the matter inside the sanitary landfill breaks down it generates gases including


Answer:

Landfill gas contains many different gases. Methane and carbon dioxide make up 90 to 98% of landfill gas. The remaining 2 to 10% includes nitrogen, oxygen, ammonia, sulfides, hydrogen and various other gases. Landfill gases are produced when bacteria break down organic waste.

Explanation:

As waste breaks down, methane gas is created, and if it escapes from the landfill, it could pollute the air. In addition, methane gas can be dangerous if it builds up in the landfill because it is flammable and could be explosive. Along with air pollution, sanitary landfills also pose a threat to water systems.
