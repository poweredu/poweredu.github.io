2. In 2021, ABC Construction, Inc. entered into a contract with XYZ House Developers, Inc. whereby the former would build for the latter the houses within its subdivision. The cost of each house, labor and materials included, was P1,00,000.00. Four hundred units were to be constructed within five years. In 2023, ABC found that it could no longer continue with the job due to the increase in the price of all commodities, including basic raw materials required for the construction of the houses. The cost of development had risen to unanticipated levels and to such a degree that the conditions and factors which formed the original basis of the contract had been totally changed. ABC brought suit against XYZ Homes praying that the Court relieve it of its obligation. Is ABC Construction entitled to the relief sought?


ABC Construction, Inc. may be entitled to relief if it can prove that the increase in the cost of development constitutes an unforeseeable event that makes the performance of the contract excessively burdensome or impossible, under the doctrine of rebus sic stantibus.

Under this doctrine, a party may be excused from performing its obligations under a contract if the circumstances that existed at the time the contract was entered into have so changed that performance has become impossible or impracticable, or if the value of the performance has been so greatly reduced that it would be inequitable to require the party to perform.

In this case, the increase in the cost of development to unanticipated levels may constitute a change of circumstances that was not foreseen by the parties at the time of the contract. If ABC can prove that this change of circumstances has made the performance of the contract excessively burdensome or impossible, it may be excused from its obligations under the contract.

However, it should be noted that the burden of proof lies with ABC to demonstrate that the increase in the cost of development was an unforeseeable event that makes performance of the contract excessively burdensome or impossible. The Court will carefully examine the evidence presented by both parties before making a decision on the matter.
