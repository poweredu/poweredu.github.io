22. differentiate the following terms:(a) unicellular cells vs. multicellular cells(b) prokaryotic cells vs. eukaryotic cells(c) cyptoplasm vc. karyoplasm(d) rough endoplasmic reticulum vs. smooth endoplasmic reticulum​


Answer:

(a) unicellular cells vs. multicellular cells

- Unicellular organisms are made up of only one cell that carries out all of the functions needed by the organism, while multicellular organisms use many different cells to function.

(b) prokaryotic cells vs. eukaryotic cells

- eukaryotic cells have a membrane-bound nucleus and prokaryotic cells do not.

(c) cyptoplasm vc. karyoplasm

- cytoplasm is (cytology) the contents of a cell except for the nucleus it includes cytosol, organelles, vesicles, and the cytoskeleton while sarcoplasm is (anatomy) the interfibrillar cytoplasm of striated muscle.

(d) rough endoplasmic reticulum vs. smooth endoplasmic reticulum

​- Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum is known for stocking the lipids and proteins. It is not bounded by ribosomes. While, the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum is bounded by the ribosomes and also stores proteins.
