8. causes ng lactose intolerance ​


Answer:

Lactose intolerance is usually the result of your body not producing enough lactase. Lactase is an enzyme (a protein that causes a chemical reaction to occur) normally produced in your small intestine that's used to digest lactose. If you have a lactase deficiency, it means your body does not produce enough lactase.

12. how do you get lactose intolerance​


Answer: When It doesn’t produce enough enzymes.

Explanation:

Lactose intolerance occurs when your small intestine doesn't produce enough of an enzyme (lactase) to digest milk sugar (lactose). Normally, lactase turns milk sugar into two simple sugars — glucose and galactose — which are absorbed into the bloodstream through the intestinal lining.


13. Ano ang intolerable acts?


Intolerable Acts was the term used by American Patriots for a series of punitive laws passed by the British Parliament in 1774 after the Boston Tea Party. The laws were meant to punish the Massachusetts colonists for their defiance in the Boston Tea Party protest in reaction to changes in taxation by the British to the detriment of Colonial goods. In Great Britain, these laws were referred to as the Coercive Acts.

18. what is behavior intolerance


The term "behavior intolerance" is not commonly used in psychological or clinical literature. However, based on its two constituent words, it may refer to a general inability or unwillingness to tolerate or accept certain types of behavior in oneself or others.

In some cases, behavior intolerance may refer to a specific type of intolerance or prejudice towards certain behaviors, such as intolerance towards people who engage in substance abuse, sexual behavior, or other types of behavior that are stigmatized or socially disapproved. In these cases, the intolerance may stem from personal beliefs, cultural norms, or other factors.

Overall, it is important to recognize that intolerance of any kind can lead to negative outcomes, including discrimination, exclusion, and conflict. If you are experiencing behavior intolerance, it may be helpful to seek support from a mental health professional or engage in self-reflection to better understand your beliefs and attitudes.
