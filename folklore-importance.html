8. solar eclipse folklore


Explanation:

In different parts of the world, their folklore is different.

In India, the demon spirit Rahu steals and consumes the nectar of immortality but is beheaded before he can swallow it. His immortal head flies into the heavens. The Sun and Moon had alerted the gods of his theft, so he takes revenge on them: When Rahu swallows an orb, we have an eclipse-but the orb returns to view because Rahu has no body.

In Norse culture, an evil enchanter, Loki, was put into chains by the gods. Loki got revenge by creating wolflike giants, one of which swallowed the Sun thus causing an eclipse. Another of the giant wolves changed the Moon trying to eat it.

17. What is the importance of folklore in the modern world? Do they allow the people to escape from societal consequences? validate their established culture, or transmit their culture's value.


Answer:

Folklore is defined as the community's traditional beliefs, customs and culture passed on to the next generations by word of mouth or through practice. The study of folklore is important in understanding ancient beliefs, traditions and even rituals. Folklore consists of both spiritual and material aspects.

Explanation:

Appreciating our historical origin specifically the folklore of our ancestors help us develop an understanding of their culture from where we originated. One importance of the study of folklore is nationalism, which focuses on the ethnic identity. Secondly, folk culture helps different people living in the same country to be united. Every nation consists of different ethnic groups with their own distinct tradition and culture. However, despite the difference in traditions, people belonging to different ethnic background understand each other because they approach a given situation in the same emotional pattern.

Lastly, folklore is all about human pains which are communicated in the forms of folktales, folk customs, folk arts and so on. Thus, this helps in generating a shared identity for the groups.

Though people have different customs and traditions, this does not mean that they can escape from societal consequences because each country has a law to be followed by the society. However, folklore validates the established culture of a certain ethnic group and the culture's values are being transmitted from one generation to another.

Thus, the study of folklore is important in understanding our past, so we can function in the present and can be improved in the future.

Answer:

Folklore is the expressive body of culture shared by a particular group of people; it encompasses the traditions common to that culture, subculture or group. These include oral traditions such as tales, proverbs and jokes. They include material culture, ranging from traditional building styles to handmade toys common to the group. Folklore also includes customary lore, the forms and rituals of celebrations such as Christmas and weddings, folk dances and initiation rites. Each one of these, either singly or in combination, is considered a folklore artifact.
