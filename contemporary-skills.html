Contemporary art is often looked at with a skeptical eye. The truth about contemporary art is that it uses techniques that most people aren’t quite used to, causing that skepticism to come into play. However, these techniques are actually quite difficult and all deserve our credit. It’s an incredible feat to create a piece of contemporary art, especially using these techniques. They challenge the mind and offer unique perspectives in a way that art has never been able to do before.

One of the artistic movements that has been most relevant throughout the contemporary art movement is minimalism. Minimalism seeks to take away what’s unnecessary and leaving only what’s essential. Minimalism is incredibly difficult and has even made its way into being a huge part of branding and design for companies all over the world. This is probably the most important part of contemporary art, as it has informed so much of society. Have you noticed that branding has gotten more minimalist over the past ten years or so? You can thank the contemporary art movement for doing that.

With every new age of art, there are new unique techniques that define how the movement develops. For contemporary art, among many techniques, you can find techniques such as minimalism, found objects, and large scale paintings. Each of these bring their own unique perspective into the artistic community, and each is valued as a pick for a technique that is most influential in contemporary art.

22. What are the steps in applying artistic skills and techniques in contemporary art​


How to Create Contemporary Art

Step 1. Decide on an inspiration.

Determine the theme to use in the artwork. Find any particular person, scenery, object, idea or issue which interests you. This will help you envision how the artwork will look like.

Step 2. Incorporate your message in it.

Contemporary art is more than what meets the eye. Brainstorm with the inspiration you had in mind to come up with something to tell to your audience. What is the story behind the artwork? What do you intend to do with it?

Step 3. Decide on the medium or form to use.

Will you use paint and use canvas? How about molding and sculpting something? Do not limit yourself with the traditional forms of art making – performance art can also be considered, if it will help you convey your message clearer and stronger.

Step 4. Study and plan your artwork carefully.

Try exploring with the elements and principles of art. Decide whether you like using geometric shapes in dark colors or bright minimalist brush strokes in your painting, for example. Don't be afraid to go wild with combining these techniques together. If there is one thing to remember, it is that contemporary art has no limits–expression of self is always the key for an effective artwork.

Feel free to decide on what style to use. The common styles include:

conceptual or abstract artcubism (use of geometric figures)expressionism (use of symbols)fauvism (aggressive use of colors)futurism (speed art)neoplasticism (use of lines)pop artsurreal or fantasical art

Step 5. Ready your materials.

Have all the materials you need to carry out the artwork. This will also help you feel ready to continue your plan.

Step 6. Start right now.

As soon as you completed the concept, the plan and the materials, start doing it. Don't hold yourself back and don't allow yourself to procrastinate. Live in the moment and make the difference of doing progress.

Step 7. Don't be afraid to make mistakes and do something new.

Mistakes are inevitable. Don't just stick to the plan. If you find yourself bored and troubled, then experiment with what you have. Try using different elements and principles and put them together in the artwork. Explore the possibilities and combinations. Also, don't forget your purpose–try to make your message clear with the the use of your chosen style and technique. If your current ones aren't working out, you can always start over again or create a beautiful mess out of that mess. After all, you are here to create art, and art has no bounds.

Step 8. Feel proud about yourself.

After all is set and done, reward yourself. You just created something out of nothing, out of an intangible idea which only you had thought of. Feel proud to have expressed yourself. Even if it didn't look well in the end, atleast you tried. You can still try again and get better with practice too.

Collage is created by pasting flat elements to a flat surface, such as newspaper or magazine cut-outs, printed text, graphics, photographs, cloth, string, image, and so on, to form a thick layer that resembles a relief sculpture. The practice of applying gouache to paper or glass and then transferring a reverse of that image onto canvas or other flat materials is known as decalcomania. Decoupage is achieved by attaching paper cut-outs and then covering them with one or more layers of varnish or a transparent varnish coating. Frottage is the process of rubbing a crayon over a piece of paper that has been placed over an item or an image. The impression can be made with leaves, wood, wirescreen, or metal embossed with an image or words. Montage is a term used in photography and film to describe the process of juxtaposing or overlapping images to create a new image or design.

29. enumerate the steps in applying artistic skills and techniques contemporary art. ​


ARTISTIC SKILLS AND TECHNIQUES FOR CONTEMPORARY ARTS

1.) Landscape - Landscape refers to a kind of artistic technique, where an artist paints or illustrate a picture that depicts a natural scene of landforms, sunsets and large expanse of vegetations.

2.) Collage - A collage refers to a kind of artistic technique, where an individual paints or creates a collective and mixture of images that represents a particular idea, using cut outs from magazines, books and news papers.

3.) Digital Painting/Drawing - refers to a kind of artistic technique, where an artist paints or creates an illustration or drawing by the help of technology or gadgets.

4.) Mixed-Media - Mixed-Media refers to a kind of artistic technique, where an individual paints, illustrates or creates an artwork using different forms of medium such as pens, color pencils, charcoal pencils or ballpoint pens.

5.) Graffiti - refers to a kind of artistic technique, where an artist paints, writes or creates an illustration, pictures or words on a wall, building, whatsoever.
