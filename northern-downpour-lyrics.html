10. Why do mosquito-borne diseases break out after a flood downpour?​


Answer:

Why is there a danger of waterborne diseases breaking out during floods?

The main cause of waterborne diseases during flooding is the contamination of drinking water supply. Floods transport bacteria, parasites, and viruses into the clean water system thus leading to the outbreak of waterborne diseases

25. Explain the difference of Northern Hemisphere summer and Northern Hemispherewinter.​


The Short Answer:

Throughout the year, different parts of Earth receive the Sun's most direct rays. So, when the North Pole tilts toward the Sun, it's summer in the Northern Hemisphere. And when the South Pole tilts toward the Sun, it's winter in the Northern Hemisphere.
