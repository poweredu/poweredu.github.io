16. The arts and crafts of bicol region in terms of elements of arts observed ​


Answer:

The Bicolano people or the Bikolanos (Bikol: Mga Bikolnon) are the fourth-largest Filipino ethnolinguistic group.[2] Male are usually called as Bicolano and Bicolana for female. Their native region is commonly referred to as Bicolandia, which comprises the entirety of the Bicol Peninsula and neighbouring minor islands, all in the southeast portion of Luzon.


17. Besides arts and crafts in the Bicol Region, what else Bicolanos are better known for?


Answer:

The Bicolano cuisine is primarily noted for the prominent use of chili peppers and gata (coconut milk) in its food. A classic example is the gulay na lada, known outside the region as Bicol Express, a well-loved dish using siling labuyo (native small chillies) and the aforementioned gata.
