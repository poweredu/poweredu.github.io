24. why is there a need to classify front office tools equipment and paraphernalia.


Explanation:

1. Room rack – used for registration records are inserted to serve

as room rack slips. Front desk agents use this equipment and

information to match available rooms with guest needs during

the registration process.

2. Key rack – the key rack is an array of numbered compartments

used to store guestroom keys

3. Reservation racks – a special board or series of pigeonholes

where cards are put to show which rooms have been locked

4. Information rack – An information rack is an index of in-house

guests by both last name and room number

5. Folio trays – a bucket where the guest folios are stored and

arranged by guestroom numbers

6. Account positing machine – An electro-mechanical machine is

used to post, monitor and balance charges and credits to guest

accounts

7. Voucher rack – it is a container for storing vouchers for future

reference and verification during the night audit

8. Cash register – used to record to cash transactions and

maintain cash balances

9. Telephone equipment – telephone equipments consists of call

accounting systems, automatic call dispensing, telephone / room

status system, fax machine and call detection

10. Credit card imprinter – Imprinter presses a credit card voucher

against a guest’s credit card

11. Magnetic strip reader – a magnetic strip reader, reads data

magnetically encoded and stored on the magnetic tape strip on

credit card

12. Time stamp – Time stamp recording is important for

establishment of chronology of events

13. Security Monitor – CCTV cameras for security personnel to

monitor certain areas of hotel

14. Wake-up devices – used by telephone operators to place wake-

up calls
