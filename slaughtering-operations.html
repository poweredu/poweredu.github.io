Answer:

Slaughtering equipment, particularly for smaller-scale operations, need not be elaborate and expensive. The amount of equipment will depend on the slaughtering procedures employed. If possible, all equipment should be made of stainless steel or plastic, be rust resistant and easily cleaned and sanitized. Equipment which does not get in contact with the meat (e.g. overhead rails, working platforms, knocking pen) is usually made of galvanized steel.

Answer and Explanation:

Transporting and slaughtering are two related but distinct processes involved in the production of meat products.

Transporting refers to the transportation of live animals from the farm or feedlot to the slaughterhouse or processing facility. This involves loading the animals onto trucks or trailers and transporting them to their destination. During transportation, animals should be handled with care and provided with food, water, and rest to ensure their welfare and prevent stress and injuries.

Slaughtering, on the other hand, is the process of killing and preparing animals for human consumption. This involves stunning the animal to render it unconscious, followed by bleeding and processing of the carcass into various meat cuts and products. The slaughter process must be carried out in a humane and hygienic manner to ensure animal welfare and food safety.

Both transporting and slaughtering are highly regulated processes that are subject to various laws and regulations to ensure animal welfare and food safety. These regulations may vary depending on the country or region, but they generally require that animals are treated with respect and handled in a manner that minimizes stress, injury, and suffering.

24. summarize the story “Lamb to slaughter”​


Answer:

"Lamb to the Slaughter" is a short story by Roald Dahl. It tells the story of Mary Maloney, a devoted housewife who discovers that her husband, Patrick, wants to leave her. In a fit of rage, she kills him with a frozen leg of lamb and then manipulates the evidence to hide her crime.

When the police arrive to investigate the murder, Mary offers them the lamb to eat, which is the murder weapon. They accept and praise her cooking skills, unaware that they are eating the evidence. Mary acts innocent and devastated by her husband's death, and the police leave without suspecting her.

Later, the detectives come back to the house to look for the murder weapon, but they are unable to find it. As they are leaving, one detective mentions that they should keep an eye out for the murder weapon, suggesting that it could be anything, even the leg of lamb. Mary is relieved and smiles as the story ends.
