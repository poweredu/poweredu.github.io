The equation y = 27 - 3x represents the price of each lollipop in terms of the price of each chocolate candy. To find the price of each lollipop, we need to substitute a value for x into the equation and solve for y.

The equation y = 17 - x represents the price of each chocolate candy in terms of the price of each lollipop. To find the price of each chocolate candy, we need to substitute a value for y into the equation and solve for x.

Let's say we want to find the price of each lollipop and chocolate candy when the price of each chocolate candy is $1.

Substituting x = 1 into the equation y = 27 - 3x we get: y = 27 - 3(1) = 27 - 3 = 24.

So the price of each lollipop when the price of each chocolate candy is $1 is $24.

Substituting y = 24 into the equation y = 17 - x we get: 24 = 17 - x.

We can solve for x by adding x to both sides of the equation: 24 = 17 - x + x.

24 = 17 + x.

So x = 24 - 17 = 7.

So the price of each chocolate candy when the price of each lollipop is $24 is $7.

Another approach is by solving the system of equations given by y = 27 - 3x and y = 17-x.

x = 24 - 17 = 7

y = 27 - 3(7) = 27 - 21 = 6

So, the price of each chocolate candy is $7 and the price of each lollipop is $6.
