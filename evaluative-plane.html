2. what is the semiotic plane, Iconic plane, contextual plane and evaluative plane of the old guitarist by pablo Picasso?​


Answer:

The "Old Guitarist" is a painting by Pablo Picasso that depicts a lone, hunched figure playing a guitar. This painting can be analyzed from various semiotic planes, which are different ways of interpreting the meaning of a work of art.

The iconic plane refers to the visual elements of the painting, such as the figures, objects, and composition. In "The Old Guitarist," the iconic plane includes the figure of the guitarist, the guitar he is playing, and the overall composition of the painting, which features a single figure in the center of the canvas.

The contextual plane refers to the historical and cultural context in which the painting was created. "The Old Guitarist" was painted in 1903, during a period of poverty and social upheaval in Spain. The painting may be seen as reflecting the struggles and hardships of the time, as well as the resilience and determination of the people.

The evaluative plane refers to the viewer's personal reactions and interpretations of the painting. Different viewers may have different reactions to "The Old Guitarist," depending on their own experiences and perspectives. Some may see the painting as a poignant depiction of loneliness and hardship, while others may see it as a celebration of the enduring power of music and art.

Finally, the semiotic plane refers to the meaning of the signs and symbols in the painting. In "The Old Guitarist," the figure of the guitarist and the guitar he is playing may be seen as symbols of the enduring power of music and the human spirit. The hunched posture of the figure may be seen as a sign of his struggles and hardships, while the act of playing the guitar may be seen as a sign of hope and resilience.

3. what is evaluative plane? in art appreciation


Explanation:

In art appreciation, the evaluative plane refers to the level of analysis where the viewer evaluates the artwork based on their personal preferences, cultural background, and other factors. This includes considering aspects such as the artwork's composition, technique, and meaning, and determining whether the artwork is successful in achieving its intended purpose or not. It is an important aspect of art criticism and analysis, as it allows for a deeper understanding of the artwork and its impact on the viewer.
