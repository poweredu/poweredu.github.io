23. How should a media and information literate individual address fellow citizens who are misinformed, hateful or discriminatory?​


A person who is media and information literate should treat their fellow citizens with respect and avoid conflict if they are uninformed, hateful, or bigoted. They can accomplish this by giving them accurate and trustworthy information, motivating them to fact-check material, and educating them on the negative effects of misinformation and hate speech. They can also provide students a deeper comprehension of the relevant problems and the viewpoints of other groups of people. Understanding that changing someone's perspective, views, or attitude takes time and that it's not always simple to do so is crucial. As a result, it's crucial to maintain these conversations in a respectful, patient, and persistent manner.
