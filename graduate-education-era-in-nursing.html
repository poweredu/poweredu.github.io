The given are 168 total number of students, [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] will take technical courses and [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] will take education. We need to know how many students will enroll in nursing.

Step 1: Write the mathematical statement of the problem. Let x be the number of students who will enroll in nursing. So,

[tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] + [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex]  + x = 1

Step 2: Rearrange the equation to solving the unknown variable. Hence,

x = 1 - [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex]  - [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex]

Step 3: Get the least common denominator to perform subtraction. The LCM for the given fractions is 12. Thus,

x = [tex]\frac{12 - 8 - 3}{12}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex] is the fraction of students who enrolled in nursing or

Let us check the answer:

168 x [tex]\frac{2}{3}[/tex] = 112 students  ; 168 x [tex]\frac{1}{4}[/tex] = 42 students

168 - 112 - 42 = 14 students

Therefore, [tex]\frac{1}{12}[/tex] or 14 students will enroll in nursing.

22. What the graduates of an educational institutions are able to do beyond graduation?


Following from an educational institution and receiving recognition for achievement is the pinnacle of any student journey. There are many valuable things that can be done after graduation, among others:

1. Search for a graduate job

Even if you are unsure of the type of work you want to perform once you graduate, it is a good idea to look for a career. You can search for vocations that are somewhat linked to your subject of study or totally unrelated.

2. Gain graduate work experience

With growing knowledge and expertise in an industry that is fiercely competitive, you will be well-positioned to apply. Internships may not always be paid, but if you can afford them, they are frequently a good method to obtain experience that leads to paid employment.

3. Travel after graduating

During this time, you can decide what you want to accomplish and volunteer to help out on the trip. Traveling can teach you a variety of skills that are essential in both work and daily life, such as budgeting, planning, independence, and socializing.
