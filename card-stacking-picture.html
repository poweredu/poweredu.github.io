3. 2. Joelita is stacking game cards. When she has 1 level, she uses 2 cards. Completing the two-level stacks, she had used 7 cards. Then the patter continues. If Joelita used 122 cards for the base of the stack, how many levels of stacked cards did he make?​


Answer:
Therefore, Joelita made approximately 3 levels of stacked cards.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the number of levels of stacked cards Joelita made.

When she has 1 level, she uses 2 cards. Thus, when she has x levels, she will use 2x cards for the base of the stack. For the additional levels, she will use 2 more cards than the previous level.

The total number of cards used for the base and additional levels is given by the sum of the arithmetic series:

122 + (2x + 2) + (2x + 4) + ... + (2x + 2(x-1))

= 122 + 2x(1 + 2 + ... + x) + 2(1 + 2 + ... + x-1)

= 122 + 2x(x+1)/2 + 2(x-1)(x)/2

= 122 + x^2 + 3x - 2x

= x^2 + x + 122

Since Joelita used 122 cards for the base of the stack and a total of 7 + 2x cards for the stack, we have:

122 + 7 + 2x = x^2 + x + 122

2x + 7 = x^2 + x

x^2 - x - 7 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, we get:

x = (-(-1) ± sqrt((-1)^2 - 4(1)(-7))) / (2(1))

x = (1 ± sqrt(29)) / 2

Since x must be a positive integer, we take the positive root:

x = (1 + sqrt(29)) / 2 ≈ 3.19

29. short essay about card-stacking


Answer:

is a propaganda technique that shows the positive qualities of any promoted product over the other

Answer:

Card stacking refers to any time one party creates a setting where they can hold favor over their opponent or of an opposing idea. Card stacking propaganda is when a party shows only the positives associated with their side and omits or severely downplays any negative details that may exist. Card stacking originated in a technique called 'stacking the deck' in card games. Gamblers popularly use deck stacking where they would stack the deck of cards so that they could increase their benefits while simultaneously damaging the position of their opponents. This metaphor coined the phrase stacking the deck fallacy. The stacking the deck fallacy in propaganda means that it is an illegitimate form of advertisement because of the lack of information for the opposing side or of any possible negatives that may exist. A logical fallacy is when there is invalid or biased reasoning that was used during an argument.

There are many card stacking examples today in corporate marketing campaigns and in political campaigns. In political campaigns, propagandists will use a coincidence technique to shed a positive light on their candidate. coincidence technique is when propagandists create a scene that seems totally random and organic. Examples of this are when a politicians show up at a public event and the media is already there. Card stacking ads are used by private businesses to make their products or services look better than their competition. Card stacking ads commonly use testimonials where people use a product and give a review of the product's qualities. Corporations will modify their advertisements to only show positive testimonials.
