6. A bag of jelly beans contains a 10 red, 6 green, 7 yellow, and 5 orange jelly beans, what is the probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, randomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it and then randomly choosing an orange jelly beans


The probability of choosing a red jelly bean on the first draw is 10/28 or 5/14 since there are 10 red jelly beans out of a total of 28 jelly beans in the bag.

Since we are replacing the jelly bean after each draw, the probability of choosing another red jelly bean on the second draw is also 5/14.

The probability of choosing an orange jelly bean on the third draw is 5/28 since there are 5 orange jelly beans remaining out of a total of 28 jelly beans in the bag.

To find the probability of all three events occurring, we need to multiply the probabilities of each event. Therefore, the probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, randomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it, and then randomly choosing an orange jelly bean is:

(5/14) x (5/14) x (5/28) = 125/5488 ≈ 0.02277 or about 2.28%.


7. A bag of jelly beans contains a 10 red, 6 green, 7 yellow, and 5 orange jelly beans, what is the probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, randomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it and then randomly choosing an orange jelly beans


Answer:

The probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, randomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it, and then randomly choosing an orange jelly bean can be calculated as follows:

P(red) = 10/28

P(red) on second draw = 10/28

P(orange) on third draw = 5/28

The probability of all three events happening together is the product of their individual probabilities:

P(red and red and orange) = P(red) x P(red) x P(orange)

P(red and red and orange) = (10/28) x (10/28) x (5/28)

P(red and red and orange) = 0.0068

Therefore, the probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, randomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it, and then randomly choosing an orange jelly bean is 0.0068 or approximately 0.68%.

10. A bag of jelly beans contains 10 re, 6 green, 7 yellow, and 5 orange jelly beans. What is the probability of randomly choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, wandomly choosing another red jelly bean, replacing it, and then randomly choosing an orange jelly bean?


Answer:

[tex]\frac{125}{5488}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

We have multiple events here and one comes after the other. For this problem, we are finding the probability that these events all happen. The Multiplication Rule of probability states that: to find the probability of two events both happening, multiple the probability of the those two events.

We find out first how many possible outcomes can we have when we draw from the bag of jelly beans. There are 10 red, 6 green, 7 yellow, and 5 orange jelly beans. We add all of the beans

[tex]10+6+7+5=28[/tex]

We have 28 possible outcomes.

For our first event, we want to know the probability of choosing a red jelly bean. We have 10 red jelly beans, and those 10 are our favorable outcomes.

[tex]P(red) = \frac{10}{28}[/tex]

For our second event, we return the jelly bean we just drew to the bag. This means that we will again have 28 possible outcomes. We want to know the probability of choosing a red jelly bean. Since we returned the red jelly bean from earlier, there will also be 10 favorable outcomes.

[tex]P(red) = \frac{10}{28}[/tex]

For our third event, we again return the jelly bean we just drew to the bag. This means that we will again have 28 possible outcomes. We want to know the probability of choosing an orange jelly bean. There are 5 orange jelly beans, and those 5 are our favorable outcomes.

[tex]P(Orange) = \frac{5}{28}[/tex]

We multiply all these probabilities together to find out the probability of all of them occurring.

[tex]P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = P(red) * P(red)*P(orange)\\\\P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = \frac{10}{28} *\frac{10}{28}*\frac{5}{28}\\\\P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = \frac{10*10*5}{28*28*28}\\\\P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = \frac{5*2*5*2*5}{7*2*2*28*28}\\\\P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = \frac{5*5*5}{7*28*28}\\\\P(red \ and \ red \ and \ orange) = \frac{125}{5488}\\[/tex]

The probability of choosing a red jelly bean, replacing it, drawing another red jelly bean, replacing it again, and drawing an orange jelly bean is [tex]\frac{125}{5488}[/tex].
