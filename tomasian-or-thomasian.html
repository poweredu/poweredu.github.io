10. What do you think was the greatest legacy of Jose Rizal as a Tomasian?Why?​


Answer:

Rizal wanted Pinoys educated first before dabbling with independence or Politiks. This position is agreeable (by hindsight). So the good doktor wanted more schools, and when you have more schools you will have more boys and girls asking more questions. And Rizal wanted more Rights for the indios. So Rizal wanted this Islas Filipinas be a province of Spain. As a province you have Rights as that of the Home country Spain. Not a bad idea and many schooled Indios and Insulares are sold to this position; of course there are reactionaries, but the conventional wisdom is that the path espoused by Joe is acceptable in the 1890s. That was 130 years ago counted from this year.

Now Andres and his underground group want Independence in one stroke; but the good doktor cautioned against this because Pinas in not ready yet.

You can imagine the debate (take your side). Remember the Castillians look at Rizal with doubt; and also are aware of the underground of Andres.

This was the situation in the 1890s. Remember, there was yet no internet nor texting those years but there was already the telex (cables) and the days are shorter from Europa to Pinas via the Suez so news (and propaganda) travels a bit faster than Noah’s time.

With two opposing ideas (contraries) one path has to be taken. A few shots here and there, a few ambushes and a few explosions occurred (or were caused by who we need not know) and the Castillians blamed poor Doktor Jose and he was court-martialed and executed.

Jose belongs to the educated class, the intelligentsia (not very rich, but definitely not poor), schooled highly by the standards of his day. He has a lot of writings, and two novels describing the situation of the day (you should read the Noli me tangere and the El filibusterismo.

In Rizal’s writings, there was a bent towards education (knowledge) as a key to going forward. And this education can be secured under Spain. Andres Bonifacio (the Phil Army camp is named after Andres) believed that Spain will not grant Jose’s line of reforms; it can be secured only via revolution.

The rest is history; but we Pinoys now see that Education (correct education and not mis-education) is one of the essential keys to national progress.
