A facultative obligation is one where only one prestation has been agreed upon but the obligor may render another in substitution. Examples: (1) I will give you my piano but I may give my television set as a substitute. In this obligation, only the piano is due.

11. What is aerobes, anaerobes and facultative? Kindly give an example for each types of oxygen the aerobes, anaerobes and facultative.


Answer:

Aerobes are microorganisms that need oxygen to live and grow, like most fungi, some bacteria and certain protozoa.

Anaerobes are microorganisms that can't survive in the presence of oxygen, and use different methods to generate energy, like Clostridium tetani or Bacteroides fragilis.

Facultative organisms can live with or without oxygen and can switch between methods depending on what's available, like Escherichia coli or Salmonella enterica. Knowing what type of oxygen microorganisms need helps in understanding their behavior and how they affect us.
