What art in the Visayas is considered a permanent body adornment and a symbol of pride and valor?

Tattooing

Tattooing is considered a permanent form of body adornment. The Visayans considered it a symbols of male pride and valor.

Why did the Spaniards called Visayan as Pintados?

The original Spanish name for the Visayans, Los Pintados ("The Painted Ones") was a reference to the tattoos of the Visayans. Antonio Pigafetta of the Magellan expedition (c. 1521) repeatedly describes the Visayans they encountered as "painted all over".
