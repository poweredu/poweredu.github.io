1. what is kuder Richardson menthod?​


Answer:

The Kuder-Richardson Formula (also known as the Kuder-Richardson 20, or KR-20) is a statistical measure of the reliability of a test or assessment. It is commonly used in educational and psychological testing to evaluate the consistency and stability of test scores.

The KR-20 is calculated by dividing the variance of the test scores by the variance of a perfect test. A perfect test is one in which all of the items are perfectly correlated with one another, resulting in a variance of 1. The KR-20 ranges from 0 to 1, with higher scores indicating a more reliable test.

The KR-20 is generally considered to be more appropriate for tests with a small number of items (20 or fewer) and is less accurate for tests with a larger number of items. There are other measures of reliability, such as the Cronbach's alpha, that are more commonly used for tests with a larger number of items.

16. What were the reforms that Saint Charles proposed and fought for at the Council of Trent?​

– St. Charles Borromeo, Italian San Carlo Borromeo, (born October 2, 1538, Arona, duchy of Milan—died November 3, 1584, Milan; canonized 1610; feast day November 4), cardinal and archbishop who was one of the most important figures of the Counter-Reformation in Italy. He is the patron saint of bishops, cardinals, seminarians, and spiritual leaders.

Borromeo, St. Charles

Borromeo, St. Charles

See all media

Born: October 2, 1538 Italy

Died: November 3, 1584 (aged 46) Milan Italy

Role In: Counter-Reformation

Borromeo received a doctorate in civil and canon law from the university of Pavia in 1559. The following year his uncle, Pope Pius IV, appointed him a cardinal and archbishop of Milan. Chief among his curial functions was heading the Consulta, a position that made him secretary of state to Pius. The pope leaned upon him heavily in directing the third convocation of the Council of Trent (1562–63). When the council closed, Borromeo served in executing its decrees and was largely instrumental in bringing out the Roman catechism in 1566. Also at this time he was actively sponsoring the conversion of Swiss Protestants. Upon the death of his uncle, Borromeo took part in the conclave that elected Pius V (1566).

Thereafter Borromeo resided at Milan, where serious administrative problems confronted him. He regularly visited his more than 1,000 widely scattered parishes, which fell under the jurisdictions of King Philip II of Spain and also of Venice, Genoa, and Novara. Seeking to apply the edicts of the Council of Trent to his own diocese, Borromeo worked diligently to eradicate the sale of indulgences, to reform monasteries, and to simplify the ornate interiors of many of the churches. He fostered clerical education to combat the threat of Protestantism and established seminaries and colleges at Milan and in the Italian cities of Inverigo and Celano. Colleges for lay students also were erected and entrusted to the Jesuits. His last undertaking was the opening of the college at Ascona, Switzerland, in 1584.

Political and other turmoils beset Borromeo. He became embroiled with the Milanese Senate and with the viceroy, Luis de Requesens y Zúñiga, as well as with the rebellious canons of Santa Maria della Scala and the order of the Humiliati (“The Humble Ones”). Borromeo nevertheless had the support of many religious congregations, including his own Oblates of St. Ambrose. In 1569 one of the Humiliati, the priest Girolamo Donato Farina, attempted to assassinate Borromeo. Despite the archbishop’s pleas for leniency, Farina and his accomplices were tortured and executed.

Borromeo’s heroic behaviour during the plague of 1576–78 won him much respect, and he gave away much of his wealth to feed the hungry and care for the sick in Milan. He was canonized by Pope Paul V in 1610.

20. Trent is drawing a diagram of his house for a school project. He decides that every 5 feet in real life should be 2 inches in the diagram. Trent's living room is 15 feet long. How long should Trent's living room be in his diagram?


PROPORTION

》happens when two ratios are equal

PROBLEM

Trent is drawing a diagram of his house for a school project. He decides that every 5 feet in real life should be 2 inches in the diagram. Trent's living room is 15 feet long. How long should Trent's living room be in his diagram?

ANSWERTrent's living room in his diagram shall be 6 inches.

SOLUTION

The first given ratio is 5 feet is to 2 inches, hence,

5 feet : 2 inches

Thus, the second ratio would be,

15 feet : x inches ; where x is the missing value

Getting the value of x we have,

[tex]5 : 2 =15 : x\iff \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{x} \\ \\ \bigg(\frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{x} \bigg)(2x) \\ \\ 5(x) = 15(2) \\ \\ 5x = 30 \\ \\ (5x = 30) \bigg( \frac{1}{5} \bigg) \\ \\ \bold{x = 6}[/tex]

24. Richardson pulls a toy 3.0 m across the floor by string, applying a force of 0.50 N. During the first meter, the string is parallel to the floor. In the next two meters, the string makes an angle of 30 degree with the horizontal direction. What is the total amount of work done by Richardson on the toy?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Solving for the work done at d = 3.0 m (parallel to the floor)

[tex]W_{1} = Fd[/tex]

[tex]W_{1} = (\text{0.50 N})(\text{3.0 m})[/tex]

[tex]W_{1} = \text{1.5 J}[/tex]

Step 2: Solving for the work done at d = 2.0 m (30° from the horizontal)

[tex]W_{2} = Fd \: \cos \theta[/tex]

[tex]W_{2} = (\text{0.50 N})(\text{2.0 m}) \: \cos 30^{\circ}[/tex]

[tex]W_{2} = \text{0.87 J}[/tex]

Step 3: Solving for the total work done

[tex]W_{T} = W_{1} + W_{2}[/tex]

[tex]W_{T} = \text{1.5 J} + \text{0.87 J}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{W_{T} = \text{2.37 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the total work done by Richardson is 2.37 J.
