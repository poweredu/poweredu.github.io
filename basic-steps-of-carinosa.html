4. 1. What is the nature/background of the folk dance "Cariñosa"? 2. What is the nature/background of the folk dance "Polka sa Nayon"? 3. What are the basic skills/steps in "Cariñosa" and "Polka sa Nayon"? 4. How do participation in folk dances help promote physical fitness?


Answer:

1. Nature: Cariñosa (Spanish pronunciation: [kaɾiˈɲosa], meaning the loving or affectionate) is a Philippine dance of colonial era origin from the Maria Clara suite of Philippine folk dances, where the fan or handkerchief plays an instrumental role as it places the couple in romance scenario.

Background: Bicolano Cariñosa

According to the book of Francisca Reyes-Aquino, Philippine Folk Dances, Volume 2, there is a different version of the dance in the region of Bicol. In the Bicol Region Cariñosa, hide and seek movement is different ways. In the original version, the dancers used the Fan and handkerchief as the way to do the hide and seek movement, in Bicol they used two handkerchiefs holding the two corners of the handkerchief and doing the hide and seek movement as they point their foot forward and their hands go upward together with their handkerchiefs following the movement. It is a complicated step however it is still used in Bicol Region during festivals and social gatherings.

6. Ilang hakbang o steps mayrong cariñosa na sayaw?​


Answer:

Ang Cariñosa, na na ang ibig sabihin ay "mapagmahal", ay sayaw na nagmula sa panahon ng mga Kastila, kung saan ang pamaypay o panyo ang nagbibigay panimula sa isang romantikong kuwento o sayaw. Sa sayaw na ito ipinapakita ang kaugalian ng pag-akyat ng ligaw sa mga dalaga.

Ang Cariñosa ay karaniwang sinasayaw ng isang lalaki at isang babae na nakaharap sa isa't-isa kapag sa kanilang pagsayaw.  Ang babae ay dapat magkaroon ng isang pamaypay at isang panyo naman ang para sa lalaki. Ang mga babae ay nagsusuot ng patadyong na may malambot na panhuelo. Ang mga lalaki naman ay nagsusuot ng barong Tagalog o kamisa de chino.

Paano sayawin ang Cariñosa?

Sa Cariñosa, bawal na magdampi ang lalaki at babae. Pero ang kanilang mga galaw ay nagpapakita ng interes ng lalaki sa babae habang ang huli ay nahihiya at nagpapahabol.

Ang pangunahing hakbang ng Cariñosa ay pareho sa waltz, at ang sukat ng musika ay 3/4. Ang babae ay hahawakan ang kanyang palda ng isa o parehong kamay sa pagsimula ng musika, sasayaw patagilid at paharap ang babae, sabay ikot, at uulitin lamang ito.

Step 1: 3 step turn and bow reverse  

Step 2: 3 step and point Right-Left in 16 counts  

Step 3: Click Step-In 2 counts 3 steps forward. 4 Click moves, then change location

 

Step 4: 4 touch steps  Change position and repeat the first two on step 4 above then Touch Move-3 step forward in 2 counts.

Step 5: 3 steps forward, Girl opens her fan and starts fanning herself, 4 steps, Switch position and repeat the first three steps on step 5 above.

Step 6: 3 steps forward, Boy goes forward and then kneels, Girl pushes the boy with her fan and takes 4 steps, Switch posture, Girl will kneel as they look at each other and the boy will do 4 steps.

Step 7:  3 moves forward, Girl gets the boy's handkerchief, Both pass the hanky up and down four times. Switch position and then repeat the first three steps in step 7

Step 8: Do it gracefully

17. 1.What does the movements portray in the Cariñosa dance?2.What are the fundamental steps that you observed in the cariñosa dance?3.What is the characteristics of Cariñosa?4.What personality does the Cariñosa dance give to its performance?NONSENSE -- REPORT​


Answer:

1.)The cariñosa is a Philippine folk dance of Hispanic origin. It is closely associated with the island of Panay and the Visayas region in general. This is a courtship dance that portrays acts of flirtation between a man and a woman. The dancers perform steps resembling hide-and-seek movements.

2.)The basic step of Cariñosa is similar to waltz and the music is 3/4 signature. When the music starts, the female holds her skirt with one or both hands, swaying the fabric of her skirt at the side while moving around the floor by swaying to the side with a left foot, then stepping your right next to the left foot.

3.)The word Cariñosa is from a Spanish word which literally means "she that is loving" (the verb ending indicates it refers to a woman). As such, it is a slow dance where a man and a woman dance together. Its style bears resemblance to a bolero or a waltz. The Spanish first arrived on the island in 1569.

4.)Cariñosa

You might think that most of the Philippine folk dances include women characters that have a shy and Maria Clara personality. While it's true, Cariñosa is a Filipino cultural dance made for flirting, hence it's a courtship dance in the Philippines.
