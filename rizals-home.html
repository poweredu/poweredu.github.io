21. discuss the house and home life of jose rizal​


Answer:

The original Rizal ancestral house was where Jose Rizal was born and where he spent most of his childhood with his 10 siblings and his parents, Francisco Engracio Rizal Mercado y Alejandro and Teodora Realonda y Quintos. The house was subsequently confiscated and owned by the Spanish authorities in 1891. It was reoccupied by Rizal’s older brother, Paciano, during the Philippine Revolution, but was confiscated again by the friars. Years later during World War II, the house was sold, destroyed, and then demolished

24. why did rizal decided to return home in the philipines​


For the following reasons, Rizal was determined about returning to the Philippines:

- To operate his mother's eyes.

- To serve his people, who had been oppressed for many years by Spanish tyrants.

- To discover the reason for Leonor Rivera's silence.

- To see for himself how Noli Me Tangere and his other works affected Filipinos and Spaniards.

-Calamba's financial difficulties.

-His conviction that the Spanish government will not prosecute innocent people.

-Want to demonstrate that returning home need not be feared.

Further Explanation :

The Ilustrado Movement of Filipinos in Europe, which advocated for reforms in the Philippines, was formally led by Rizal. He made the decision to go back to the Philippines in 1885 despite the unanimous advice of his friends and supporters that he should stay home since he would undoubtedly be arrested.

When Jose Rizal returned from his travels in several nations, including Europe, he did so in order to assess the impact of Noli Me Tangere in both Spanish and the Philippines. Another motivation is to discover the cause of Leonor Rivera's cease-fire in correspondence and to assist his fellow countrymen in reclaiming the land that the friars in Bian, Laguna had taken from them. He had his mother's eyes operated on when he returned. Teodora Morales Alonso y Quintos Realonda is her mother's name.

Rizal encountered legal issues as a result of his book, Noli Me Tangere. He was contacted by the governor-general for this. Because he attempted to subvert the beliefs of the state, its government, and the Spanish nation, Noli Me Tangere was outlawed. He also assisted other farmers in their efforts to have the Dominican friars changed. As a result, he was more obedient to his people and cooperative. He stood up for his convictions and never wavered in his affection for his mother and the rest of his family. His return, in my opinion, was a significant event in his life since it increased his sense of patriotism.
