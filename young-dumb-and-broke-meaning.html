21. Idioms. Meaning1.a ray of hope. 2.rain or shine 3.save for rainy day4.broke the ice 5.as fresh as a Daisy​


Explanation

1. A ray of hope-something that holds the promise of hope This new medicine is a ray of hope for/to thousands of people.

2. Rain or shine—used to say that something will happen even it rains The party will be on Tuesday, rain or shine. —sometimes used figuratively for no matter what happens I'll always love you, come rain or (come) shine.

3. Save for rainy day-for a time in the future when something will be needed saving money for a rainy day.

4. Broke the ice-do or say something to relieve tension or get conversation going at the start of a party or when people meet for the first time.

5. As fresh as daisy-to be full of energy and enthusiasm: After a good night's sleep I'll be as fresh as a daisy. SMART Vocabulary: related words and phrases. Energetic and lively. a spring in your step idiom

29. What could be the meaning of "straw that broke the camel's back"?​


Answer:

The idiom "the straw that broke the camel's back", alluding to the proverb "it is the last straw that breaks the camel's back", describes the seemingly minor or routine action that causes an unpredictably large and sudden reaction, because of the cumulative effect of small actions.
