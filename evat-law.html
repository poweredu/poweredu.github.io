3. Ano ang tax evation?​


Answer:

Ano ang Tax Evasion?

Ang pag-iwas sa buwis ay isang iligal na aktibidad kung saan ang isang tao o entity ay sadyang umiwas sa pagbabayad ng isang tunay na pananagutan sa buwis. Ang mga nahuhuling umiiwas na buwis sa pangkalahatan ay napapailalim sa mga kasong kriminal at malalaking parusa. Upang sadyang mabibigo na magbayad ng buwis ay isang pederal na pagkakasala sa ilalim ng code ng buwis sa Internal Revenue Service (IRS).

Explanation:

Answer:

Tax evasion is an illegal activity in which a person or entity deliberately avoids paying a true tax liability. Those caught evading taxes are generally subject to criminal charges and substantial penalties. To willfully fail to pay taxes is a federal offense under the Internal Revenue Service

KEY TAKE WAYS

Tax evasion can be either the illegal non-payment or underpayment of actual tax liabilities due.  

Tax evasion can be determined by the IRS regardless of whether or not tax forms were filed with the agency.

To determine tax evasion, the agency must be able to show that the avoidance of taxes was willful on the part of the taxpayer.

While tax evasion is illegal, tax avoidance includes finding legal ways (within the law) to reduce taxpayer obligations.

16. sino ang nagpatupad ng EVAT SA 12%​


Answer:

President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo

Explanation:

President Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo had signed into law on May 24, 2005 Republic Act No. 9337, also known as the Expanded Value-Added Tax Act of 2005, which will take effect starting July 1, 2005. The said law was enacted to streamline and restructure the present VAT system and to provide additional revenue for the government through the increased tax rates, lifting of exemptions, and subjecting to tax transactions not previously covered by tax, in order to balance the government’s budget and to curb the existing fiscal deficit.
