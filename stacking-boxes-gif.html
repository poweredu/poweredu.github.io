5. a dozen numbered boxes of which three are red, four are green and five are blue are to be stacked one on top of the other. How many ways can they be stacked if the red boxes and the blue boxes are to be separated?​


Answer:

6 * 24 * 5 = 720

Step-by-step explanation:

Since there are three red boxes, there are 3! = 6 possible orders they can be placed in.

Let's first consider the green boxes as a single group, since the order of the green boxes doesn't matter. There are 4! = 24 ways to arrange the green boxes.

We have 5 blue boxes and 4 gaps between the green boxes to place them in. We can think of this as choosing 4 gaps from 5 possibilities, which can be done in 5 choose 4 ways, or 5.

we can stack the red boxes in any of the 6 orders we found earlier, and we can stack the green boxes in their 24 possible orders. So the total number of ways to stack the boxes with the red and blue boxes separated is: 720

21. a stack of soap boxes has 12 boxes in the bottom, 11 boxes in the second row and so on. how many boxes are in the 4th row?


Answer:

Word Problems in Algebra

There are 9 soap boxes in the 4th row.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the answer, let us list down the given items first:

12 – number of boxes on the bottom (the first row) 11 – number of boxes on the second row

Find:

the number of soap boxes on the fourth row

Solution:

n = 12 + (4 – 1) (-1)

n = 12 + 3 (-1)

n = 12 – 3

n = 9

The sequence shows us that for every additional row, the number of boxes is decreased by one. By the time we reached the 4th row, there should be 9 soap boxes left. This is how it was represented:

1st row = 12 boxes 2nd row = 11 boxes 3rd row = 10 boxes 4th row = 9 boxes
