21. Give three principles, out of the 10 principles discussed, on how you can be an effective and active listener. You may give specific examples or situations, whether in school, in the office, or in day to day life, when you can practice these principles. What is empathy and how does it relate to being a good listener ?​


Answer:

Three principles for effective and active listening are:

Pay attention and minimize distractions: Focus on the speaker and avoid multitasking or being distracted by external factors. For example, in a classroom setting, actively listen to the teacher by taking notes and avoiding the temptation to check your phone or engage in side conversations.

Show interest and ask questions: Demonstrate interest in the speaker's message by asking relevant and open-ended questions. For instance, in a job interview, actively listen to the interviewer's questions and provide thoughtful responses that show your knowledge and enthusiasm for the position.

Paraphrase and summarize: Repeat the speaker's message in your own words to confirm your understanding and show that you are actively listening. In a team meeting, for example, summarize the main points of the discussion to ensure that everyone is on the same page and can move forward with a clear understanding of the objectives.

Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others. It relates to being a good listener because it allows you to not only hear the speaker's words but also to understand their emotions and perspective. By practicing empathy, you can create a deeper connection with the speaker and demonstrate that you value their thoughts and feelings. For example, if a friend is going through a difficult time, actively listen to their concerns and offer support by showing empathy and compassion.
