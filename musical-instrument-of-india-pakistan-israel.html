7. what are the differences and similarities of vocal and instrumental music in pakistan, india, and Israel?​


Israel

The singers add Arab-style melismatic ornamentation, (silsulim in Hebrew), and often sing in a nasal tone, similar to Arab music. The melodies are often modal, swinging between major and minor, and diction is guttural.

Pakistan

Qawwali is a kind of Sufi devotional music that is popular in South Asia, especially in parts of Pakistan and India. It has existed for at least 700 years. Although it is often referred to as a meditative and trance-inducing music, qawwali can be equally fast-paced and rapturous.

India

Hindustani music style is mainly found in North India, Pakistan and Bangladesh. It exists in four major forms: Dhrupad, Khyal (or Khayal), Tarana, and the semi-classical Thumri.

Answer:

A. Instrumental music of India

- The two main traditions of Indian classical music are Carnatic music, which is practiced predominantly in the peninsular (southern) regions, and Hindustani music, which is found in the northern, eastern and central regions. Instruments most commonly used in Hindustani classical music are sitar, sarod, tambura, sahnai, sarangi, and tabla. While instruments commonly used in Karnatak classical music include the vina, mrdangam, kanjira, and violin.

B. Instrumental music of Pakistan

- Pakistani music, as diverse as its multiethnic population, ranges from qawwali, a popular brand of music branched from Sufi Islam to good ol' fashioned rock 'n' roll. It includes diverse elements ranging from music from various parts of South Asia as well as Central Asian, Persian, Turkish, Arabic, and modern-day Western popular music influences. With these multiple influences, a distinctive Pakistani sound has been formed. From Pakistan's inception, music was a form of entertainment like anywhere else. But unlike Pakistan's hardline-Islamist image today, society was just a little bit different back then.

C. Instrumental music of Israel

- Israel's musicians have made significant contributions to classical, jazz, pop-rock, and other international music genres. Since the 1970s, there has been a flowering of musical diversity, with Israeli rock, folk, and jazz musicians creating and performing extensively, both locally and abroad. Many of the world's top classical musicians are Israelis or Israeli expatriates. The works of Israeli classical composers have been performed by leading orchestras worldwide.
