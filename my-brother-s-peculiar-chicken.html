The conflict in the story arises when Kiko's wealthy friend wants to buy the chicken from him for a large sum of money. Kiko is torn between the financial benefits of selling the chicken and the traditional values that he and his family hold dear. In the end, Kiko decides to keep the chicken and protect it from outsiders, preserving his family's rural way of life.

The story has been interpreted as a commentary on the tension between modernization and tradition in the Philippines, as well as a celebration of the country's rural culture and the wisdom of its people.
