14. Jessie has a 15 km headstatt on Melvin. How long eill it take Melvin ti catch Jessie if the latter travels at 70 kph and the former at 100 kph


Problems in mathematics involving DISTANCE, TIME, and RATES are generally called DISTANCE PROBLEMS, though many times they ask for time and rates.

These problems oftentimes are difficult to answer due to confusion of the given how they are to be used. But the most important formula to be used with these problems is the DISTANCE FORMULA

distance = rate x time

d = rt

Most likely, all distance problems revolve around this equation, so we need to always remember this.

On the problem above, refer to the attached picture for better understanding.

As stated in the problem, Jessie has 15km head start of Melvin. That means at point B, Jessie started to run at 70 kph. And at the same time, Melvin started running at point A with a speed of 100 kph.

After some time, which is the unknown, Melvin catches Jessie at point C. Take note that we let the variable “d” as the distance travelled by Jessie from point B to C. Since Melvin travelled a longer distance than Jessie, we have (d+15) as his total distance travelled.

Using the equation d=rt, we get

Jessie’s

d=(70kph)(t of jessie)

Melvin’s

d+15= (100kph)(t of melvin)

Recall that if both run at the same time (t=0) and one catches the other, taking a stop to a certain distance, we can say that the time it took Jessie to run before he was caught is equal to the time it took Melvin to catch Jessie.

Thus we equate their time, giving us

t of Jessie = t of Melvin

Or simply we can have it as “t”

(d+15)/100kph = d/70kph

Cross multiplying, we get

70d + 1050 = 100d

Transposing all the variables to the right.

1050 = 100d - 70d

1050 = 30d

d = 35 km

Thus, Jessie ran for 35 km before Melvin caught him. And Melvin run for

35+d = 35+15 = 50km to catch Jessie

Since the problem asks for how the time it took Melvin to catch Jessie, we substitute the value of d to the Melvin’s

t=(35+d)/100

t = (35 + 15)/100

t = (50)/100

t = 0.5 hr

Therefore, it took Melvin 0.5 hour or 30 minutes to catch Jessie.

24. lara’s father is 36. he is 16 years older than four times lara’s age. how old is lara?


Answer:

Lara is 5 years old

Step-by-step explanation:

Evaluate the ages of Lara and Lara's father in an algebraic sentence

Lara's age = x

Lara's father's age = 4x + 16

So the expression would be

4x + 16 = 36, find x

4x + 16 = 36

4x = 36 - 16

4x = 20

4x / 4 = 20 / 4

x = 5

Checking

Let x = 5

4x + 16 = 36

4(5) + 16 = 36

20 + 16 = 36

36 = 36 -- true
