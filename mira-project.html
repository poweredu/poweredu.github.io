9. how does truthful knowledge contribute to or limit mira's freedom?


From this passage, it was clear to the audience that Mira's freedom to sell is not limited by the truth about the company's bad behavior.

Even if she finds out that the products she sells for the company are bad for the customers, she is still willing to sell them. This shows that, in order to stay alive, she stopped caring about other people and only thought about herself. She knew that if she told the police about the company, she would lose her job and have a hard time finding other ways to make money to meet her needs.

She had the right to decide on her own to look for a job that would give her extra money to help pay for her schooling. She has the freedom to decide for herself what she thinks is the best way to solve her problem and meet her own needs.

We can show respect for other people's freedom by letting them know the truth. By knowing the truth, people will be able to see it from a better angle, which will help them make a fair decision about what to do. Just like in this case, the people who buy the product should know the truth about it and how bad it is for their health. By telling them the truth about the product, you give them a better idea of how it might affect their health. In this way, people are treated fairly and their decisions are respected. They can choose not to buy it because they know it is bad and will hurt them if they use it. So, freedom is good if it doesn't hurt people or make them feel bad about their choices in any way.

$(1.80 * 5) = (20*5)% of the total

$9 = 100%
