What is material self?

William James claimed that the things, places, or even persons that carry the label "my" are what make up the material self. These things are seen as extensions of people's identities. For instance, your clothing, which you refer to as "my wardrobe," expresses certain facets of your personality.

Knowing one's material self entails being aware of one's physical attributes, such as size, form, and appearance. Knowing one's intrapersonal self entails being aware of one's feelings, needs, values, opinions, and other inner workings. Knowing our interpersonal selves also entails being aware of how others perceive us.

The sum of all your material goods, including your possessions, home, and body, is your material self. When you look through your list of answers to the query, "Who are you? Take note of any times you make references to your appearance or anything you own.

22. "Confessions of a Shopaholic" (2006) What is the Climax Of the story?​


Confessions of a Shopaholic

When Rebecca Bloomwood discovers the truth behind Flagstaff Life, the narrative reaches its conclusion. Two weeks before to the investors' expected receipt of a 20,000 pound windfall, a letter from Flagstaff Life advised them to move to the company's new unit-linked growth fund. They no longer receive that windfall since they switched.

Further explanation

The first two books in Sophie Kinsella's Shopaholic series served as the basis for the 2009 American romantic comedy Confessions of a Shopaholic. Isla Fisher plays the compulsive shopper journalist in the P. J. Hogan-directed movie, while Hugh Dancy is her boss.

Together with her closest friend Suze, Rebecca "Becky" Bloomwood, a shopping addict, resides in New York City. She is a journalist for a gardening magazine but aspires to work for Alette, a fashion publication. She attempts to buy a green scarf on the way to an interview with Alette, but her credit card is rejected. Rebecca claims that the scarf is a present for her sick aunt and goes to a hot dog stand where she promises to pay for all the hot dogs with a check provided the vendor gives her back change in cash. Another buyer offers her the $20 she needs to buy the scarf when the dealer rejects her request.
