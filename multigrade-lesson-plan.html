3. if you were the multigrade teachers, what are your perspectives toward multigrade teaching, in general, and lesson designing, in particular?​


Answer:

Multigrade teaching involves the instruction of students of different ages and grade levels in the same classroom. The teacher has to design lesson plans that suit the learning needs and abilities of all the students in the class, which can be challenging. However, multigrade teaching offers several benefits, including the promotion of collaborative learning, individualized instruction, and the development of social skills.

In terms of lesson designing, the teacher has to consider the different learning styles and abilities of the students and tailor the lesson plans accordingly. The lesson plans should be flexible, engaging, and interactive to cater to the diverse needs of the learners. The teacher should also incorporate technology and other resources to enhance the learning experience. Furthermore, the teacher should evaluate the effectiveness of the lesson plans regularly and make necessary adjustments to ensure that all students are making progress.

My perspective on multigrade teaching is that it offers an exciting opportunity to design creative and engaging lessons that are tailored to the individual needs of students. When designing lessons, it is important to focus on the specific learning needs of each student, and also pay attention to the different pacing that may be required for individual grade levels. Additionally, when considering lesson design, it's important to remember that multigrade teaching requires both creativity and flexibility to keep the students engaged and ensure that all are learning something new in each lesson.

17. why is there a need to establish multigrade classes?describe a multigrade class.​


Answer:

There is a need to establish multigrade classes in order to address the issue of small and isolated schools, particularly in rural areas, where it is not feasible to have separate classes for each grade level due to lack of resources and student population. Multigrade classes are classes where students of different grade levels are taught together by one teacher. This allows for more efficient use of resources and allows for students in these small communities to have access to education.

A typical multigrade class would have students from different grade levels, such as grades 1-3, in one class. The teacher would be responsible for teaching the curriculum for all of these grade levels, often using different teaching methods and strategies to cater to the diverse needs of the students. The teacher would also have to be proficient in different subjects and have the ability to differentiate instruction to help each student reach their full potential.

18. what is Multigrade class?What isMultigrade classroom?​


Classes with three grade levels conducted in one classroom and handled by a single teacher are called a multigrade or multilevel class. This means that children with different skills and abilities, developmental levels, and needs are mixed in a class and work together under the guidance of one teacher

Multigrade is an education method where a teacher conducts classes to primary students of multiple grade levels in a single classroom. It is applicable to schools located in far-flung or mountainous areas where school teachers are scarce and are forced to teach many grade levels simultaneously

Multigrade and monograde terms need to be used with precision because the conflation of studies from different contexts has possibly led to misleading claims being made for the cognitive effects of multigrade settings. It is particularly important to distinguish between terms referring to mixed age classes formed by administrative necessity and those formed by choice (Mason and Burns, 1997; Veenman, 1995). The latter usually occur within a non-graded context where there may be special curricula, instructional materials, and teachers, while the former occur in graded contexts where there may be no special provisions made.
