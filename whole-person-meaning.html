5. What is meant by integrated personality? Or what is integrity? In mathematics, an integer is a whole number. Does this help in understanding the meaning of integrity? When can we say​


that a person has a strong sense of integrity?

Integrated personality refers to the unification of different aspects of one's personality into a cohesive, consistent whole. It involves a person's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors being in alignment, and their actions being guided by their values and beliefs.

Integrity, on the other hand, is the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles. It is about doing what is right, even when no one is watching. In mathematics, an integer is a whole number, but this does not have any direct connection to the meaning of integrity.

A person can be considered to have a strong sense of integrity when they consistently behave in a moral and ethical way, even in difficult or challenging situations. They have a clear set of values and principles that guide their actions, and are not easily swayed by external pressures. They also take responsibility for their actions and are accountable for their mistakes.
