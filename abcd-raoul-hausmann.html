6. The vertices of the quadrilateral ABCD are A (0,0), B (2,1), C (1,3), and D (-2,4). Prove that ABCD is a trapezoid..​


To prove that ABCD is a trapezoid, we must prove that sides AD and BC are parallel. To do this, we determine the slopes of the lines that go through A and D and B and c.

The slope of the line that goes through A(0,0) and D(-2,4) is

[tex]m = \frac{4 - 0}{ - 2 - 0} = \frac{4}{ - 2} = - 2[/tex]

The slope of the line that goes through B(2,1) and C (1,3) is

[tex]m = \frac{3 - 1}{1 - 2} = \frac{2}{ - 1} = - 2[/tex]

Since the two slopes are equal, the lines are parallel, meaning sides A and BC are parallel. By definition, AB is a trapezoid.

24. in rectangle abcd, ac=7×=47 and bd =5×+29 find the length of the diagonals of the rectangle abcd​


Answer:

the length of the diagonals of the rectangle ABCD is approximately 99.82.

Step-by-step explanation:

In a rectangle, the diagonals are equal in length and can be calculated using the Pythagorean theorem. Let the length of the rectangle be a and the width be b. Then, we have:

a^2 + b^2 = diagonal^2

We are given that AC = 47 and BD = 5x + 29. Since AC and BD are diagonals of the rectangle, they must be equal in length. Therefore, we have:

47^2 + b^2 = (5x + 29)^2 + b^2

Expanding the right-hand side and simplifying, we get:

220x + 900 = 2209

Solving for x, we get:

x = (2209 - 900)/220 = 7

Therefore, the width of the rectangle is:

b = 5x + 29 = 5(7) + 29 = 64

And the length of the rectangle is:

a = AC/2 = 47/2 = 23.5

Finally, the length of the diagonals is given by:

diagonal^2 = a^2 + b^2

diagonal = √(a^2 + b^2)

diagonal = √(23.5^2 + 64^2)

diagonal = √(5876.25 + 4096)

diagonal = √9964.25

diagonal ≈ 99.82

Therefore, the length of the diagonals of the rectangle ABCD is approximately 99.82.
