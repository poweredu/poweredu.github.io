5. How the Wayang kulit puppet theatre of indonesia and the Nang Shadow Puppets Show of Thailand differ from each other?​


Explanation:

Wayang kulit is a traditional form of puppet-shadow play originally found in the cultures of Java and Bali in Indonesia.[1] In a wayang kulit performance, the puppet figures are rear-projected on a taut linen screen with a coconut-oil (or electric) light. The dalang (shadow artist) manipulates carved leather figures between the lamp and the screen to bring the shadows to life. The narratives of wayang kulit often have to do with the major theme of good vs. evil.

27. In the nang shadow puppet show of thailand, what elements of art is least important


Answer

Value is the least important element of art in a Nang Yai performance.

Explanation

Value is an element of art that refers to the tone of colors from light to dark. The color value of Nang Yai is barely noticed because of the nature of the performance. It is a theatrical play that uses strong lights to create silhouettes or form of the puppets. That is why value is the least noticable element in a Nang Yai performance.

Additional InformationNang Yai puppets are made from buffalo skins and are made flat. They are operated by sticks to create movement. The story is narrated through singing, chanting, and music. Texture is created through the use of negative(empty) space.Puppets are barely colored because most of them appear as black silhouettes or form.
