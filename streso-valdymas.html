Answer:

Compression squeezes rocks together, causing rocks to fold or fracture (break) (figure 1). ... Rocks under tension lengthen or break apart. Tension is the major type of stress at divergent plate boundaries. When forces are parallel but moving in opposite directions, the stress is called shear (figure 2).Tensional stress is the stress that tends to pull something apart. Alice E.M. Underwood

· Grammar

A metaphor is a figure of speech that describes an object or action in a way that isn’t literally true, but helps explain an idea or make a comparison.

Here are the basics:

A metaphor states that one thing is another thing

It equates those two things not because they actually are the same, but for the sake of comparison or symbolism

If you take a metaphor literally, it will probably sound very strange (are there actually any sheep, black or otherwise, in your family?)

Metaphors are used in poetry, literature, and anytime someone wants to add some color to their language

23. 1. What is Stress 2 Give 10 examples of Stress 3. Explain each number on how you can manage istress your 4. Is stress all bad? Why? 5. What Strasses you out?​


1. Stress is a physical, mental, or emotional response to a demand, threat, or challenge that exceeds a person's ability to cope with it. It is a natural reaction that prepares the body to respond to danger or pressure, also known as the "fight or flight" response.

2. Examples of stress include:

Financial problems

Work deadlines

Family conflicts

Relationship issues

Health problems

Traffic jams

Academic pressure

Social anxiety

Performance anxiety

Life transitions (moving, divorce, etc.)

3. Here are some ways to manage stress:

Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga

Exercise regularly to release tension and boost endorphins

Get enough sleep and establish a regular sleep routine

Eat a healthy diet and avoid excessive caffeine, alcohol, or drugs

Set realistic goals and prioritize tasks to avoid feeling overwhelmed

Seek social support from friends, family, or a therapist

Practice time management and avoid procrastination

Engage in hobbies or activities that bring joy and relaxation

Learn to say "no" to commitments that cause unnecessary stress

Maintain a positive attitude and practice gratitude and self-compassion

4. Stress is not all bad. It can motivate us to take action, achieve goals, and adapt to new situations. However, chronic or excessive stress can have negative effects on our physical and mental health, including increased risk of heart disease, anxiety, depression, and other illnesses.

30. Which of the three types of stress cause the rock layers to form faults?a.) shearing stress, normal stress, and tension stressb.) compression stress, normal stress, and tension stress c.) deformation stress, tension stress, and shearing stress d.) tension stress, compression stress, and shearing stress ​


Answer: D.

Explanation: Stress is a force acting on a rock per unit area. It has the same units as pressure, but also has a direction (i.e., it is a vector, just like a force). There are three types of stress: compression, tension, and shear.
