10. why ethyl alcohol evaporates faster than water and acetone


Answer:

Because alcohol evaporates at a considerably faster pace than water because to its lower boiling temperature (82 vs. 100 degrees C), it can transport more heat away from the skin. This means that alcohol evaporates much faster than water in a given length of time.

While ethanol has a direct OH link because it is an alcohol. Intermolecular hydrogen bonding exist in ethanol as a result. As a result, ethanol necessitates the destruction of more physical connections than acetone. As a result, despite its higher surface tension, acetone evaporates faster than ethanol.
