23. What does each part of the constructed lung model represent?​


Answer:

The constructed lung model makes use of plastic bottles,straws and balloons to represent the respiratory system. The representation is as follows: -The bottle itself represents the chest cavity. -The upper part of the straw represents the trachea.

Accordingly, how does the lung model work?

The plastic at the bottom works like your diaphragm—a strong muscle that expands and contracts to cause your lungs to fill with air and then empty out again. The movement of the balloon matches your breathing – when you breathe in, your lungs fill with air just like the balloon did. This model just represents one lung.
