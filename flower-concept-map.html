4. using the flower concept map give 4 characteristics of social stratification and explain your answers.​


The characteristics of social stratification:

There are differences in roles and status.There is a distribution of rights and obligations.There is a symbol system to indicate status.There are differences in the form of relationships that occur between groups

Explanation:

Social Stratification is a society that is deliberately separated into certain groups based on the characteristics or criteria needed. The characteristics that exist in social stratification are as follows:

Closed

Nature Stratification with a closed nature limits a person from moving from one layer to another, either moving up or down. The only way to occupy that layer is through birth. Example: In society that adheres to the caste system, feudal society, and society that uses physical characteristics as a measure of an individual's layer.

Open

Nature In an open layering system, every member of society has the opportunity to occupy the top layer. In such a layer, each individual is motivated to achieve his desires according to his abilities. Thus competition will occur and development will run faster.

Mixed

In a mixed system it will be found in society that for certain things it uses a closed system while in other cases it uses an open system. A simple example is the Balinese people who still adhere to the caste system in their socio-cultural life. However, in the economic field anyone can compete.

Diagram of a flower showing the pistil, stigma, style, ovary, stamen, anther, filament, petal, ovule with embryo sac, sepal, receptacle, and peduncle.

Peduncle: The stalk of a flower.

Receptacle: The part of a flower stalk where the parts of the flower are attached.

Sepal: The outer parts of the flower (often green and leaf-like) that enclose a developing bud.

Petal: The parts of a flower that are often conspicuously colored.

Stamen: The pollen producing part of a flower, usually with a slender filament supporting the anther.

Anther: The part of the stamen where pollen is produced.

Pistil: The ovule producing part of a flower. The ovary often supports a long style, topped by a stigma. The mature ovary is a fruit, and the mature ovule is a seed.

Stigma: The part of the pistil where pollen germinates.

Ovary: The enlarged basal portion of the pistil where ovules are produced
