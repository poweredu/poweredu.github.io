21. Why do bats hang upside down


Quick Answer: Bats hang upside down to roost and sleep because the bones in their hind legs are lightweight and cannot support a bat's body weight in an upright stance. While they rest, bats wrap their wings around themselves like a cloak.

Full answer: Bats are the only true mammals that can actually fly. Most bats take off flying by dropping while hanging upside down. Many bats cannot take off while on the ground.

Bats normally land by slowing down until they are stalling, then they grab hold of a branch or some other surface. Some bats will perform a flip and then grasp onto a branch.

Bats usually gather in large social groups and rest in caves, man-made structures or trees.

27. Imaged viewed in the microscope will appear upside down?


Answer:

The optics of a microscope's lenses change the orientation of the image that the user sees. A specimen that is right-side up and facing right on the microscope slide will appear upside-down and facing left when viewed through a microscope, and vice versa.
