11. the intensity of an earthquake gives a qualitative description of the servicing shaking GRADE 6 MERCURY BIST​


Answer:

both intensity and magnitude. However, the two terms are quite different, and they are often confused.

Intensity is based on the observed effects of ground shaking on people, buildings, and natural features. It varies from place to place within the disturbed region depending on the location of the observer with respect to the earthquake epicenter.

Magnitude is related to the amount of seismic energy released at the hypocenter of the earthquake. It is based on the amplitude of the earthquake waves recorded on instruments which have a common calibration. The magnitude of an earthquake is thus represented by a single, instrumentally determined value.

Earthquakes are the result of forces deep within the Earth's interior that continuously affect the surface of the Earth. The energy from these forces is stored in a variety of ways within the rocks. When this energy is released suddenly, for example by shearing movements along faults in the crust of the Earth, an earthquake results. The area of the fault where the sudden rupture takes place is called the focus or hypocenter of the earthquake. The point on the Earth's surface directly above the focus is called the epicenter of the earthquake.

19. What is ardipithicus Rami dus


Answer: Ardipithecus is a genus of an extinct hominine that lived during the Late Miocene and Early Pliocene epochs in the Afar Depression, Ethiopia. Originally described as one of the earliest ancestors of humans after they diverged from the chimpanzees, the relation of this genus to human ancestors and whether it is a hominin is now a matter of debate.Two fossil species are described in the literature: A. ramidus, which lived about 4.4 million years ago during the early Pliocene, and A. kadabba, dated to approximately 5.6 million years ago (late Miocene). Behavioral analysis showed that Ardipithecus could be very similar to chimpanzees, indicating that the early human ancestors were very chimpanzee-like in behavior.

20. Partial Differentation. If u xy/x^2+y^2 + sin^-1 (x/y) evaluate du/dy and x du/dx + du/dy​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{u=\dfrac{xy}{x^2+y^2}+sin^{-1}\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{\partial}{{\partial}x}\left[u=\dfrac{xy}{x^2+y^2}+sin^{-1}\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)\right]}[/tex]

treat y as a constant

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}=\dfrac{(x^2+y^2)y-xy(2x)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)^2}}\left(\dfrac{1}{y}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}=\dfrac{x^2y+y^3-2x^2y}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{1}{y\sqrt{\dfrac{y^2-x^2}{y^2}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}=\dfrac{y^3-x^2y}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{\sqrt{y^2}}{y\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}=\dfrac{y(y^2-x^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{\partial}{{\partial}y}\left[u=\dfrac{xy}{x^2+y^2}+sin^{-1}\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)\right]}[/tex]

treat x as a constant

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{(x^2+y^2)x-xy(2y)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)^2}}\left(-\dfrac{x}{y^2}\right)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{x^3+xy^2-2xy^2}{(x^2+y^2)^2}-\dfrac{x}{y^2\sqrt{\dfrac{y^2-x^2}{y^2}}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{x^3-xy^2}{(x^2+y^2)^2}-\dfrac{x\sqrt{y^2}}{y^2\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{x(x^2-y^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}-\dfrac{x}{y\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}+\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=x\left[\dfrac{y(y^2-x^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}\right]+\left[\dfrac{x(x^2-y^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}-\dfrac{x}{y\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}\right]}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}x}+\dfrac{{\partial}u}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{xy(y^2-x^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}+\dfrac{x}{\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}+\dfrac{x(x^2-y^2)}{(x^2+y^2)^2}-\dfrac{x}{y\sqrt{y^2-x^2}}}[/tex]
