12. we'll know in pangasinan for his mango plantation and a70% supplier of mang to zest-o company​


Answer:

Lito Arenas, a nursing graduate, has been supplying 70 percent of the mango requirements of Zest-O Co. for its mango juice production the past 12 years. He is also a successful exporter to Japan-based companies with his L.A. Fruit brand. He conducts his mango farming and trading in Barangay Baguinay, Manaoag, Pangasinan.

Mang Lito shares his blessings with his fellow farmers in a cooperative he set up in their barangay. With 80 members, the organization provides livelihood assistance to the members and provides rice supply at a more affordable price than what is the prevailing price.

He is also the chairman of the Association of the Seed Growers in Pangasinan and has participated in several biddings for various agricultural products and inputs conducted by the Department of Agriculture. For the DA’s El Nino assistance project, he supplies fertilizer on a 50/50 subsidy scheme for the Quick Turn Around Program. Other business ventures are also within the scope of his expertise.

Mang Lito believes that great things come from small but sincere and humble beginnings. Gifted with a strong business foresight, he sensed that there is an unlimited demand for mango fruits in the market and in the manufacturing sector. Indeed, mango is one of the most in-demand fruits hereabouts and in the global markets — as juice and dried commodity and fresh ripe and eating green.

With a meager capital of P2,500, he engaged in local wholesaling of mangoes in Urdaneta, Pangasinan in 1980. He attended seminars on mango production technology to maximize their mango production in the family owned three-hectare farm. He traveled extensively seeking wider sources of fruit supply and expanding contact for his business.

With his enterprising decisions, business opportunities came around enabling him to establish buying stations in Urdaneta, Pangasinan and later branching up to Bulacan, Isabela and Cagayan during which time, he was supplying quality mangoes to the following companies: KLT Fruits, Pure and Rich and Tres Martires.

Then came his biggest break with Zest-O, one of the leading fruit drink manufacturers in the country. His contract with the company increased his financial resources that enabled him to expand his business. He is now leasing a 17-hectare mango plantation in Bolinao, Pangasinan, with around 1,300 fruit-bearing mango trees contributing a great deal to his expanded business.

With Mang Lito’s success, he inspires and motivates other mango farmers and aspiring entrepreneurs in the province.
