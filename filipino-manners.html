3. Why do Filipinos do this practice of mannerism?​


Answer:

for restpect

Explanation:

sana makatong

Respect for elders is very strong in the Philippines. From youth, Filipinos are taught to respect those who are older than them. Not only have the elders given birth and raised many, but in their age, they have grown wiser, more experienced, and have, by tradition earned the respect of younger generations. Several symbolic ways in which respect is given to elders is in the use of language by calling older Filipinos "Po" and older siblings, cousins, and family friends "Kuya" and "Ate". Perhaps the most fascinating and ritualistic custom of showing respect to elders is the greeting or salutation of Mano. Mano [Spanish for hand] evolved from the traditions of respect for ones elders which comes from Asian cultures coupled with the respect for the clergy during the Spanish occupation of the Philippines.

23. Research on the use of honorifics in japanese conversation and compare it to filipino manner of conversation. cite the differences.​


Answer:

The use of honorifics in Japanese conversation is deeply ingrained in their culture, and is seen as a way to show respect for the person being addressed. There are various honorifics in Japanese that are used based on the level of respect, familiarity, and social status of the person being addressed. Some common honorifics and their meanings are:

San: a general honorific that can be used with anyone regardless of status, similar to "Mr." or "Ms." in EnglishSama: a more respectful honorific, used to address someone of higher status or someone who deserves extra respectChan: an affectionate or childlike honorific, used for children or close friends and family membersKun: a respectful honorific used for young males

In contrast, Filipino conversation does not use as many honorifics as Japanese conversation. However, respect for elders or authority figures is still an important aspect of their culture, and there are certain words and phrases that are used to show respect. For example, "po" is added to the end of a sentence when addressing an elder or someone of higher status, similar to the use of "sir" or "ma'am" in English.

In terms of differences between Japanese and Filipino conversation, Japanese conversation tends to be more formal and hierarchical, with the use of distinct honorifics and language depending on the social status and relationship between the speakers. In contrast, Filipino conversation tends to be more informal and egalitarian, with a focus on building rapport and maintaining harmonious relationships.

Overall, the differences in the use of honorifics between Japanese and Filipino conversation reflect the cultural values and social norms of each country.

24. Why did the Spaniards have to gather the Filipinos in a community in a manner called pueblo?


Answer:

The Spaniards used the system of pueblo to gather the Filipinos into a community in order to better control and manage them. By doing so, the Spanish could more easily impose their laws, collect taxes, and spread their religion throughout the region. The pueblo also helped to facilitate communication between the Spanish and the Filipinos, allowing the two cultures to interact and learn from one another.
