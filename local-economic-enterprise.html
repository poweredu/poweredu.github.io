15. what are the steps you must take upon turning a local business to an international enterprise? pa help po ​


Answer:

However, expanding into other countries doesn’t just happen; in fact, it often comes with significant challenges and risks. While there are numerous factors to consider before deciding if your operation is ready to go global, the following best practices will help you navigate this change.

Leverage Influencer Relationships in Foreign Markets

Look for influencers in the markets you’re interested in and develop a relationship with them well before you begin expansion. Don’t think small when looking for these influencers.

As this relationship grows, consider whether they’ll make for a good partner in your expansion, or if they’ll serve more as mentor, helping you learn how to do business abroad. These relationships will be key when you make the final jump.

Assess Your Capital

In order to be financially successful overseas, it takes a long-term financial plan. If you don’t have enough capital for the initial investment, consider where you’ll get that money and remember that expanding internationally is not something that you can expect to turn a profit from right away. You have to be in it for the long haul.

Hire a Market-Research Firm

One of the first steps when considering which market to enter is to hire an experienced and reputable market-research firm. Just because an overseas market might be hot, it may not be home to the customers you want to reach.

With a market-research firm, you’ll be able to decide whether your business will be well received in that area and whether or not there’s a market for your services and products at all.

Understand Language Barriers and Cultural Challenges

Entering foreign markets presents many obstacles, with cultural differences being one of the most challenging. Not only might you have to adjust your marketing and branding to appeal to consumers in a very different region, but if you’re opening offices, you have to consider office culture. For example, work/life balance in other countries is vastly different from the U.S. In the U.S., professional and personal lives often cross paths, where in some other regions of the world they are kept entirely separate.

Get Employees On Board

In order to successfully expand your business abroad, current employees need to be on board; you’ll need them to help train new employees and build out new processes. Get them on board by asking about their ideas for communication, customer acquisition, marketing and more. These people are the backbone of your company and a large part of the reason why it’s successful enough to expand, so keeping them in the loop will go a long way.

These are just some of the steps you’ll need to take when expanding your business internationally. If you go into expansion with the right boxes checked, you’ll be more successful in the long run.

26. 1. What are the roles of public enterprises?2. Trace the history of GOCCs in the Philippines.3. Mention and explain some of the common public economic enterprises in the Phil.4. What are the implications of the issues of public enterprises in the country?​


Answer:

1. The roles of public enterprises include providing essential services to the public, such as transportation, energy, water and sanitation services; providing employment opportunities; contributing to economic growth and development through investments in infrastructure projects; and acting as a source of revenue for the government.

2. The history of GOCCs in the Philippines dates back to the Spanish colonial period when they were established by royal decree for various purposes such as mining, agriculture, manufacturing and trade. During the American period (1898-1946), GOCCs were used mainly for infrastructure development projects such as roads, bridges and ports. After independence in 1946, GOCCs continued to be used for infrastructure projects but also began to be used more widely for other purposes such as social welfare programs and agricultural production.

3. Some common public economic enterprises in the Philippines include Philippine National Railways (PNR), Philippine Ports Authority (PPA), Manila International Airport Authority (MIAA), National Power Corporation (NPC) and Philippine Amusement & Gaming Corporation (PAGCOR). PNR provides rail transport services throughout Luzon while PPA manages port operations nationwide. MIAA is responsible for managing air transport operations at Manila’s international airport while NPC is responsible for generating electricity throughout the country. Finally, PAGCOR operates casinos throughout the country with profits going towards funding social welfare programs like health care initiatives or educational scholarships.

4. The issues surrounding public enterprises can have significant implications on both economic growth and social welfare outcomes in a country like the Philippines where there are limited resources available from private sector investment or foreign aid sources due to its developing economy status . Issues such as corruption within these organizations can lead to misallocation of resources which can result in inefficient use of funds that could otherwise be used towards more productive activities that would benefit society at large . Additionally , lack of transparency within these organizations can lead to an environment where it is difficult or impossible to hold them accountable which further exacerbates their ability to effectively serve their intended purpose .
