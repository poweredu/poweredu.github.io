Taal Volcano is a large caldera filled by Taal Lake in the Philippines. Located in the province of Bata ngas, the volcano is one of the most active volcanoes in the country, with 34 recorded historical eruptions, all of which were concentrated on Volcano Island, near the middle of Ta al Lake.

Last eruption: 12 January 2020

Elevation: 311 m

Prominence: 306 m

Location: Tal isay and San Nicolas Bata ngas, Philippines

Volcanic arc/belt: Luzon Volcanic Arc

Easiest route: D aa ng Ka stila

Mount Banahaw also spelled as Banahao and Banájao) is an active volcano on Luzon in the Philippines. The three-peaked volcano complex is located at the boundary of Laguna and Quezon provinces. It is the highest mountain in both provinces and Calabarzon region, dominating the landscape for miles around.

he mountain is considered by many as a holy mountain, thus a bundok dambana, and is popular among pilgrims along with mountain climbers. It is located in a protected area known as Mounts Banahaw–San Cristobal Protected Landscape covering 10,901 hectares (26,940 acres) of land

Mount Banahaw has four unconfirmed historical eruptions, with the most severe occurring in 1730, when the crater lake located on top of Mount Banahaw breached its rims, sending torrential floods on the towns below it. Sariaya was totally destroyed due to the flooding brought by the crater breach.

21. which of the following volcanoes are very familiar in term of eruption?A. Taal, Pinatubo and MayonB. Taal, Pinatubo and BulusanC. Taal, Banahaw and PinatuboD. Taal, Kanlaon and Mayon​


Answer:

Active Volcanoes in the Philippines

Of all the choices presented, the most active volcanoes in the Philippines are Taal, Kanlaon, and Mayon. Therefore, the correct answer is letter D.

Explanation:

Since the Spanish began documenting the history of the Philippines, Taal, Mayon, and Kanlaon volcanoes have erupted more than a dozen times. These three volcanoes are the three most active in the country. The fourth most active volcano in the country is Bulusan, found in Sorsogon. Pinatubo and Banahaw are also active volcanoes being observed and monitored by PHIVOLCS, but they only erupt once in a while. The real threat lies with the volcanoes that are known to erupt frequently.
