15. Liam uses 200 regular paces to walk around a square park if one peace of liam measure's 40cm .What is the measure of each side of the park? ​


Answer:

The measure of each side of the park is 2,000 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Total number of paces around the square park = Perimeter of the square parkTotal number of Paces = 2001 pace = 40 cm

Find the Perimeter:

Perimeter = 40 cm × 200

Perimeter = 8,000 cm

Measure of each side of the square park:

Perimeter = 4 (side)

8,000 cm. = 4(side)

side = (8,000 cm)/4

side = 2,000 cm

The measure of each side of the park is 2,000 cm  or  50 paces.

Answer:

2,000 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

It is stated that Liam uses 200 regular paces to walk around a square park. Meaning, this is the perimeter. One pace is equal to 40 cm.

First, let us find the measurement of the perimeter of the square park in cm.

P = 200 × 40

P = 8,000 cm

Now, we will find the measurement of each side. To do that, let's recall the formula of perimeter for a square.

P = 4x

where x is the measurement of a side.

Now, we will substitute the value that we have which is the perimeter.

8,000 = 4x

To isolate x, we will divide both sides by 4.

[tex]\frac{4x}{4} = \frac{8,000}{4}[/tex]

x = 2,000

Therefore, the measure of one side of the park is 2,000 cm.

If the answer you are looking for is in paces, you will simply use 200 as your perimeter.

200 = 4x

[tex]\frac{200}{4} =\frac{4x}{4}[/tex]

x = 50

The answer could also be 50 paces.
