Answer:

This analysis of “Désirée’s Baby,” an 1893 short story by Kate Chopin, explores the narrative of miscegenation and motherhood in the antebellum American South. Chopin is best known for the groundbreaking classic novella The Awakening (1899).

Désirée, a young woman adopted as a child, is married to a plantation owner named Armaud. She is startled when she suddenly realizes that their baby is of a darker complexion than her own and her husband’s.

Chopin uses the setting of Louisiana to create a delicate yet hostile environment for a disillusioned young mother. Motherhood in this era can be deemed sensitive and complicated, since Désirée lives in a time when its treatment is based on ethnicity and social stratification.

10. short story of Desiree's Baby by Kate Chopin1. What is the story all about? (Summary -10 sentences -max) 2. What kind of discrimination is present in the story? 3. How is racism treated in the story of Desiree's baby?4. What lesson did you learn from it? need help po plss​


Explanation:

1. Summary:

Desiree's Baby is a short story by Kate Chopin which tells the story of a young woman of French Creole descent and her life with her husband and their infant son, who is of mixed race. The story begins with the arrival of a baby boy in the home of Desiree and her husband, Armand. The couple is ecstatic and they both take delight in the child’s presence. However, the joy of their child is soon replaced by the discovery that the child is of mixed race. Armand is filled with anger and despair, believing that Desiree is responsible for his child's mixed race. He begins to treat her cruelly and eventually drives her away.

Desiree is eventually revealed to be the child of a former slave, and Armand is the one who is of mixed race. Armand's racist beliefs prevent him from accepting his own heritage and he is ashamed that his own child has the same heritage. As a result, Armand is filled with regret and sorrow for his actions. He is ultimately driven to the brink of insanity and he dies alone.

2. Discrimination:

The story of Desiree's Baby highlights the discrimination against people of mixed race during the time period in which it was written. Armand is filled with anger and shame when he discovers that his own child is of mixed race and he blames Desiree for it. He believes that she is responsible for his child's heritage and he treats her cruelly and eventually drives her away. Armand's racist beliefs prevent him from accepting his own heritage and he is ashamed that his own child has the same heritage.

3. Racism:

Racism is treated in a very negative way in the story of Desiree's Baby. Armand's racism is what ultimately leads to the downfall of the couple's relationship and the downfall of Armand himself. His racism prevents him from accepting his own heritage and he is so ashamed that his own child has the same heritage that he is driven to the brink of insanity and eventually dies alone.

4. Lesson:

Desiree's Baby teaches the lesson that racism is a deeply rooted and destructive force. Armand's racism prevents him from accepting his own heritage and leads to the downfall of his relationship with Desiree and his own death. The story serves as a reminder that racism must be confronted and challenged in order to create a more just and equitable society.

Kate Chopin's story 'Desiree's Baby' is told from a 3rd person omniscient point of view. ... Throughout the story, we are given the thoughts and feelings of several of the characters. This tells us that it is 3rd person omniscient, since in 3rd person limited, we would only have the thoughts of one character.
