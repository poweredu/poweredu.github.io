1. favorite and dislike scene in five feet apart?plot: ( five feet apart)​


Answer:

Favorite Scene:

The favorite scene in Five Feet Apart is when Stella and Will share their first kiss. Will has been struggling with his illness and Stella has been trying to stay away from him to protect her own health, but when they finally come together, it's a powerful moment of connection and hope. The moment is even more special because of the challenges they have faced to get to that moment.

Dislike Scene:

The dislike scene in Five Feet Apart is when Stella's best friend Poe collapses and is rushed to the hospital. The scene is difficult to watch as Poe has been a source of strength and joy for Stella throughout the film. Seeing her friend in such a vulnerable state brings up a lot of fear and sadness for Stella, and it's a difficult moment to watch.

3. introduce the movie (five feet apart)


Answer:

Five feet apart is a movie about Stella Grant (Haley Lu Richardson) is every bit a seventeen-year-old: she’s attached to her laptop and loves her best friends. But unlike most teenagers, she spends much of her time living in a hospital as a cystic fibrosis patient. Her life is full of routines, boundaries and self-control -- all of which is put to the test when she meets an impossibly charming fellow CF patient named Will Newman (Cole Sprouse).

There’s an instant flirtation, though restrictions dictate that they must maintain a safe distance between them. As their connection intensifies, so does the temptation to throw the rules out the window and embrace that attraction. Further complicating matters is Will’s potentially dangerous rebellion against his ongoing medical treatment. Stella gradually inspires Will to live life to the fullest, but can she ultimately save the person she loves when even a single touch is off limits?

21. what is the plot twist in five feet apart?​


The plot twist in Five Feet Apart is that Stella, the main character, discovers that her illness, cystic fibrosis, has been caused by a genetic mutation that makes a particular medication that she’s been taking ineffective. This means that even if she follows the protocols for physical distancing, she is still vulnerable to the virus causing her illness and could potentially die from it.
