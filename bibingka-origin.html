1. what is the origin of bibingka? ​


Answer:

in Indonesia and in the Philippines

Explanation:

Bibingka is a type of baked rice cake from Indonesia and Philippines. It is usually eaten for breakfast, especially during the Christmas season. It is traditionally cooked in clay pots lined with leaves. It is a subtype of kakanin (rice cakes) in Philippine cuisine. Bibingka is also found in East Timor and Christian communities in eastern Indonesia.

19. Ben ate 2/6 of the bibingka while Rachel ate 1/3 of the same bibingka. What parts of the bibingka did they eat altogether?​


ADDING FRACTIONS

A fraction represents part of a whole. When a whole is divided equally into a number of parts, a fraction tells is how many of those parts we have. A fraction has 2 parts called numerator and denominator.

[tex]\qquad\qquad \sf{\dfrac{ \pink{1}}{ \blue{2}} \implies \dfrac{ \pink{Numerator}}{ \blue{Denominator}}}[/tex]

Numerator - The number above the bar in a fraction that tells how many of the equal parts of the whole are being considered.Denominator - The number below the bar in a fraction. It tells the total number of equal parts or groups into which the whole or group has been divided.Three types of fraction:

Proper fractions are fractions whose numerators are less than the denominators. They are fractions less than 1.

[tex]\sf{ \: Examples: \: \dfrac{2}{3}, \: \: \dfrac{4}{5}, \: \: \dfrac{6}{7}, \: \dfrac{50}{100} \: }[/tex]

Improper fractions are fractions whose numerators are greater than or equal to the denominators. They are equal to or greater than 1 whole.

[tex]\sf{ \: Examples: \: \dfrac{8}{5}, \: \: \dfrac{10}{4}, \: \: \dfrac{6}{4}, \: \: \dfrac{9}{9} \: }[/tex]

Mixed numbers have a whole number and a fraction written together.

[tex]\sf{ \: Examples: \: 1\dfrac{5}{8}, \: \: 3\dfrac{1}{4}, \: \: 10\dfrac{3}{9}, \: \: 50\dfrac{9}{9} \: }[/tex]How do we know if a fraction is already in its lowest terms?

A fraction is already in its lowest terms if the numerator and the denominator can no longer be divided by any number except 1. To express fractions in lowest terms, divide the numerator and the numerator by their greates common factor (GCF).

Examples:

[tex]\qquad\sf{\dfrac{4}{8}(GCF \: is \: 4) \implies \dfrac{4 \div 4}{8 \div 4} = \dfrac{1}{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\sf{\dfrac{18}{20}(GCF \: is \: 2) \implies \dfrac{18 \div 2}{20 \div 2} = \dfrac{9}{10}}[/tex]

[tex]\Large\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Ben ate 2/6 of the bibingka while Rachel ate 1/3 of the same bibingka. What parts of the bibingka did they eat altogether?

Let us add [tex]\sf \dfrac{2}{6}[/tex] and [tex]\sf \dfrac{1}{3}[/tex].

Step 1: Set the fractions in vertical form.

[tex]\qquad\qquad\qquad \begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{3}}}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Find the LCD.

[tex]\begin{aligned}\qquad\qquad\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{+ \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{3}}}\end{aligned} \: \: \: = \: \: \: \begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{ \boxed{ \: \: \: }}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{+ \: \: \: \dfrac{ \boxed{ \: \: \: }}{6}}}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Rename as similar fractions.

[tex]\qquad\qquad \begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{3}}}\end{aligned}\: \: \: = \: \: \:\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{2}{6}}}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4:Add the numerators. Write the sum over the common denominator.

[tex]\qquad\qquad\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{3}}}\\\\\end{aligned}\: \: \: = \: \: \:\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{2}{6}}} \\ \sf\dfrac{4}{6}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Simplify the answer.

[tex]\qquad\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ + \: \: \: \dfrac{1}{3}}}\\\\\end{aligned}\: \: \: = \: \: \:\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{2}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ +\: \: \: \dfrac{2}{6}}} \\ \sf\dfrac{4}{6}\end{aligned}\: \: \: = \: \: \:\begin{aligned}\sf{\dfrac{4}{6}} \\ \underline{\sf{ \div \: \: \: \dfrac{2}{2}}} \\ \green{\boxed{\bold{ \purple{\dfrac{2}{3}}}}}\end{aligned} \\[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\qquad\quad\bold{ANSWER:}\quad\green{ \boxed{\bold{ \purple{ \: \: \dfrac{2}{3} \: \: }}}} \\ [/tex]

Therefore, Ben and Rachel ate [tex]\dfrac{2}{3}[/tex] of the bibingka.
