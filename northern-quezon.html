Northern Tier is the Boy Scouts of America’s gateway to adventure in the Great Northwoods. In the summer, Scouts from Northern Tier’s three wilderness canoe bases explore millions of acres of pristine lakes, meandering rivers, dense forests and wetlands in Northern Minnesota, Northwest Ontario and Northeast Manitoba. What is the design of Kalinga?

Kalinga hand woven fabrics are characterized by dominant red stripes and motifs of geometric patterns with nature symbols interlaced with white yellow and black fibers. Originally woven in Lubuagan, the “ka-in” (wrap around skirt) is now more known as the Kalinga.

What is the common pattern of Kalinga textile?

Kalinga weaving, which can be traced back to the 18th century, had an Indonesian influence. Through centuries, it has evolved into its more fashionable fit called Ginamat design. Predominantly styled by the Lubuagan weavers, Ginamat is a twilled pattern decorated with silk embroidery.
