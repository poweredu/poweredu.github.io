3. What is the difference between World cinema and Philippine cinema?​


Answer:

The cinema of the Philippines (Filipino: Pelikulang Pilipino or Sine Pilipino) began with the introduction of the first moving pictures to the country on August 31, 1897 at the Salón de Pertierra in Manila. The following year, local scenes were shot on film for the first time by a Spaniard, Antonio Ramos, using the Lumiere Cinematograph.[5] While most early filmmakers and producers in the country were mostly wealthy enterprising foreigners and expatriates, on September 12, 1919, Dalagang Bukid (Country Maiden), a movie based on a popular musical play, was the first movie made and shown by Filipino filmmaker José Nepomuceno.[6] Dubbed as the "Father of Philippine Cinema," his work marked the start of cinema as an art form in the Philippines.[7]

15. similarities of comics and cinema​


The use of language to construct stories of both comics and film is basicly similar. To help readers and viewers comprehend and follow the plot, both use terminology that is often used in regular discourse.

Let’s discuss about comic and cinema.

What is a cinema?

Cinema derives from the word cinematographie, which also refers to motion, light, and writing (writing, picture, image). As a result, it is possible to understand cinema as manifesting motion through light. use specialized equipment, most frequently a camera, to realize or paint motion with light.

Cinema components :

•Director

- Scenario writer

- Photographer (Cameramen)

• Director of Art (Art Director

• Musician

• Editors

• Stylist and voice-over

• Cast (actress and actor)

What is a comic?

Comics are picture-illustrated stories that are meant to amuse readers.

What are the characteristics of comics?

• Comic books use both words and images to create stories.

• Proportional in nature, where readers are engaged, emotionally interested, and appear to be the major actors.

• The language used in comics is common speech to make it simple for viewers to comprehend and comprehend the contents of the comics.

• Comics are heroic; most comic storylines instill a sense of heroism in the reader.

• The characters in the comics are described simply so that it is simple to comprehend their traits and the message they are trying to get through.

• Because it occurs frequently in everyday life, comics offer humor that is very simple to comprehend.

• Comic strips, specifically comic strips assembled into a brief plot. This kind of comic is typically produced in series.

• Cartoons, specifically cartoons in the form of a single display that include irony, satire, or critique.

• Comic Annuals, which are comics that are released either monthly or annually.

• Web comics, or online comics, which are comics published online

• Comic books, which are narratives with illustrations and text bound into books.
