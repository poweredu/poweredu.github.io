2. what is the classification of instruments ranat ek,khong wong yai,ta phon,ching,and pi nai?


Answer:

Khong wong yai

Explanation:

The khong wong yai (Thai: ฆ้องวงใหญ่, pronounced [kʰɔ́ːŋ woŋ jàj]) is a circle with gongs used in the music of Thailand. It has 16 tuned bossed gongs in a rattan frame and is played with two beaters.The player sits in the center of the circle. It is used in the piphat ensemble to provide the skeletal melody the other instruments of the elaborate ensemble. The gongs are individually tuned with beeswax under the gongs. The khong wong yai can either be played with soft beaters or hard beaters.

5. tempo control and time marking instruments of the Pi Phat Ensemble​


Answer:

Tempo control and time marking instruments of  Pi Phat Ensemble are Ta-phon, and Klong That. Mainly composed of “striking instruments” like Thum, Ranat Thum Lek, Ranat Ek, Wong Yai, Ranat Khong, and Khong Wong Lek; wind instruments - such as Pi, Khlui; and ‘tempo control and time marking instruments’ Pi Phat is a type of Thai musical ensemble.  Pi Phat holds aspects of culture that include the musical instruments used in the ensemble, values, customs, beliefs, norms, traditions, and rules.

Explanation:

Pi Phat ensemble is performed in public festivities in musical concerts or as an accompaniment to the Khon (mask dance drama), and in the royal ceremonies, also in Nang Yai (grand shadow puppet spectacle), Hun Krabok (bamboo puppet theatre), Lakhon (dance drama), and Li-ke (folk dance drama). Pi Phat ensemble has six sub-type which are:

1. Pi Phat Mai Khaeng ensemble - refers to the unpadded wooden mallets for striking on the bars of the Ranat Ek (Ranat - Thai xylophone; Ek - the main or leading instrument in the ensemble).  

2. Pi Phat Se-pha ensemble - created during the reign of King Rama II, this sub-type introduced either Song Na (two-faced) or Luk Poeng drum into the Pi Phat Khrueang Ha (five instruments) ensemble.

3. Pi Phat Mai Nuam ensemble - performs in accompaniment to the Khon and Lakhon.  

4. Pi Phat Duekdamban ensemble - conceived to accommodate the newly emerged type of musical drama - a hybrid of Lakhon Nai and the Western opera - which was devised by Chao Phraya Thewetwongwiwat (Mom Ratchawong Lan Kunchon).

5. Pi Phat Nang Hong ensemble - conceived specifically for woeful occasions which is a combination of the Pi Phat ensemble and the Bua Loi ensemble that features a Pi Chawa, two Klong Malayu, a Khong Meng.  

6. Pi Phat Mon ensemble – this ensemble is a mixture of musical instruments from the Pi Phat Mai Khaeng ensemble - Ranat Ek, Ranat Thum, Ranat Ek Lek, and Ranat Thum Lek - with some of those of the Mon ethnic group - Pi Mon, Khong Mon Wong Yai, Khong Mon Wong Lek, Ta-phon Mon, Poeng Mang Khok, and three Mong. The percussion section comprises Ching, Chap Lek, Chap Yai, and Krap.
