6. POST-LAB QUESTIONS Experiment 1 :TRANSPORT ACROSS THE MEMBRANE 1. Explain the effect of temperature on the rate of diffusion of solute particles.​


Answer:

Temperature has a significant effect on the rate of diffusion of solute particles. As temperature increases, the kinetic energy of the solute particles also increases, leading to more collisions with the solvent particles. This results in a higher rate of diffusion as the solute particles move faster and spread out more quickly in the solvent.

Conversely, when the temperature decreases, the kinetic energy of the solute particles decreases, leading to fewer collisions with the solvent particles. This results in a slower rate of diffusion as the solute particles move slower and take more time to spread out in the solvent.

Therefore, it can be concluded that temperature has a direct impact on the rate of diffusion of solute particles, with higher temperatures leading to faster diffusion rates and lower temperatures leading to slower diffusion rates.

8. Iodination of acetone lab report


Answer:

Kinetics in chemistry deals with the rate at which a chemical reaction occurs. This rate, which is referred to as the reaction rate, is defined as the change in concentration of a reactant or product with time and is measured in M/s.

The rate of a reaction is proportional to the concentration of reactants. An equation called the rate law expresses the relationship of the reaction rate to the rate constant, k, and the concentrations of the reactants raised to some powers, x and y, found experimentally. The rate law is expressed as, rate = k [A]x[B]y. The constant k is equal to the rate divided by the concentration of a certain substance. The purpose of this lab was to experimentally determine the rate constant k, as well as the exponential values of x and y in the rate law.
