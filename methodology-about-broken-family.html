26. identify research methods or methodology of system analysis used to finish the projects. Create a discussion about why did you use this methodology.​


Answer:

System analysis is a crucial step in the development of any software system. It involves examining and identifying the requirements and goals of the system and designing a solution that meets those requirements. There are several research methods or methodologies used in system analysis, including:

Structured Analysis: This methodology is used to identify the different parts of a system and how they relate to each other. It uses graphical tools like flowcharts and data flow diagrams to represent the system's components and their interactions.

Object-Oriented Analysis: This methodology is used to model a system based on objects and their relationships. It is often used in object-oriented programming languages and uses techniques like use case diagrams and class diagrams.

Agile Methodology: This methodology emphasizes collaboration and flexibility in the software development process. It involves breaking down the development process into smaller, iterative cycles, with a focus on customer feedback and constant adaptation.

Waterfall Methodology: This methodology is a linear approach to software development, with each phase of the development process following the previous one. It involves a detailed planning phase, followed by design, implementation, testing, and maintenance.

The choice of methodology used in system analysis depends on several factors, including the project's complexity, the team's skills and experience, and the organization's goals and culture. Each methodology has its strengths and weaknesses, and selecting the right one can have a significant impact on the success of the project.

For example, if the project involves a large, complex system with many interconnected parts, structured analysis might be the best choice as it provides a clear, visual representation of the system's components and interactions. On the other hand, if the project involves a small team working on a relatively simple system, agile methodology might be more appropriate as it allows for more flexibility and faster iteration.

In conclusion, the choice of methodology used in system analysis should be based on a careful evaluation of the project's requirements and goals, as well as the team's skills and experience. By selecting the right methodology, the project team can ensure that they have a clear, well-defined plan for developing a successful software system.

Answer:

Abstract

Problem: Teenage pregnancies are frequently unplanned, the young pregnant woman being often unmarried. These pregnancies occasionally end in induced abortion. Teenage pregnancy, especially when unintended, is correlated with negative effects for the child and the mother. Despite the need to reduce the magnitude of this phenomenon, there is no reliable database on teenage pregnancy. Such a database could help define populations at risk, and could help in designing prevention programs. Birth rates, fetal deaths, and induced abortion are reported, but there is no uniformity or merging of the data.

Objectives: The aims of the paper are to present a methodology for the estimation of pregnancy rates and to use it for the analysis of characteristics and trends in adolescent pregnancies in Israel.

Methods: The pregnancy rate was calculated as the sum of three outcomes: live birth, induced abortion, and fetal loss, at all stages of pregnancy.

Results: Based on these computations, the teenage pregnancy rate in Israel was estimated as 32 per 1,000 adolescent girls. This rate was three times higher among Moslems as compared to Jews. From 1988 through 1998, the rate decreased by 12%. The pregnancy rate in Israel is similar to the rate of most Western-European countries, and is three times lower than the US rate.

Conclusion: There is a need to improve the means for collecting the data on which the computations are based, to build an algorithm specifically adapted to Israel, and to publish detailed pregnancy rates on a regular basis.
