1. what is the action of ravana in Ramayana ​


Answer:

Ravana is the primary antagonist in the Hindu epic Ramayana. He is portrayed as a powerful and intelligent demon king who possesses extraordinary strength and magical powers. Ravana is known for abducting Sita, the wife of Lord Rama, and holding her captive in his kingdom of Lanka. He does this because he desires Sita and wants to make her his wife.

Ravana is also known for his arrogance and pride, which ultimately lead to his downfall. He is warned by his advisers and family members not to harm Sita or Rama, but he refuses to listen and continues to pursue his desires. This leads to a great battle between Ravana's army and Lord Rama's army, in which Ravana is eventually defeated and killed by Lord Rama.

- Lakshmana: Lakshmana is Lord Rama's younger brother and one of his closest allies. He is known for his loyalty and bravery, and accompanies Rama into exile. He is also known for his temper and his fierce protection of Sita.
