18. The radius of a circle has endpoints (-4, 3) and (1, -2). What is the equation that defines the circle if its center is on the second quadrant? A. (x – 4)^2 + (y + 3)^2 = 50 B. (x + 4)^2 + (y – 3)^2 = 50 C. (x – 1)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 50 D. (x + 1)^2 + (y – 2)^2 = 50.


Answer:

B.

Step-by-step explanation:

The Equation of circle is: ( x - h )^2 + ( y - k )^2 = r^2

where ( h, k ) is the center and r is the radius.

So we just need to look for the location of its center.

A. (4,-3) - IV quadrant

B. (-4,3) - II quadrant

C. (1,-2) - IV quadrant

D. (-1,2) - II quadrant

B. (-4,3) is one of the endpoints of the radius and it is located at the second quadrant.
