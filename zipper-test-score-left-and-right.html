26. zipper test procedure


Zipper test procedure

Flexibility refers to the ability of the joints to travel through a full range of motion. Zipper Test – The upper arm and shoulder girdle flexibility test was developed to complement the strength / endurance assessment of the area. Aim – To be able to bring the fingertips together behind the back by going over the  shoulder and under the elbow Equipment Ruler ProcedureFor the student:

a. Stand erect, guy.  

b. To check your right shoulder, lift your right arm, bend your elbow, and stretch your back as far as you can.  

c. At the same time, extend your left arm down and behind your back, fold your elbow over your back, and  attempt to cross your fingers over those of your right hand.  

d. Reach with your right hand over your right shoulder and down your back as though you were  pulling a zip or scratching between your shoulder blades.

e. Repeat the operation (a-d) with the left hand over the left shoulder to check the left shoulder.  

For the tester, a. Observe whether the fingers crossed each other or overlapped.  

b. Measure the angle at which the fingers overlapped with c. Record the centimeter value. Performance – mark zip check to nearest 0.1 cm Norm 0 1 2 3 4 5-do not touch fingertips-just touched fingertips-fingers

overlapped by 1-2 cm. -the fingers overlapped by 3-4 cm. -the fingers overlapped by 5-7 cm. –fingers  overlapped by 8 cm or more.
