11. When was Schramm’s model developed?


When was Schramm’s model developed?  

This Schramm model of communication was originally introduced by Wilbur Schramm in 1954 and later on adapted from the theories of another scientist, Osgood So, it is also called Osgood and Schramm model of communication. This model of communication was developed to know the entire process of communication and to understand the flow. Schramm's model of communication focuses on information being put into words to relay information. Information is encoded and thought is converted to content so communication can be initiated. The speaker initiates the conversation. Once the information has been understood by the receiver, the receiver automatically decodes what the sender is trying to say. This why this model emphasizes coding and decoding as essential processes of effective communication. The flow will not be complete if the sender does not receive feedback from the receiver. Also, this model highlights that the person's knowledge, cultural background, experiences, religion, etc., are vital in relaying information because one word spoken by the speaker may not be the same word the receiver is thinking due to these factors.

Here are four Components of Schramm model of communication.  

Encoder (Sender) is the one who sends the message.  Decoder (Receiver) is the one who receives the message.  Interpreter: is the one who is trying to understand or interpret the message. The message is something that is conveyed from one person to another.  

Features of Schramm model of communication.  

The main feature of this model is that, it is a circular model.  This model presents a comprehensive treatment of fundamental of communication.  This model talks about general explanation of the nature of a communication.

26. Historical development of models of disabilities?​


Answer:

Applications and interpretations

Models of Disability are tools for defining impairment and, ultimately, for providing a basis upon which government and society can devise strategies for meeting the needs of disabled people. They are often treated with scepticism as it is thought they do not reflect a real world, are often incomplete and encourage narrow thinking, and seldom offer detailed guidance for action. However, they are a useful framework in which to gain an understanding of disability issues, and also of the perspective held by those creating and applying the models.
