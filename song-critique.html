15. why do you think is the most appropriate approach to use in critiquing the poem of song of autumn ​


Answer:

Formalist Criticism: This approach regards literature as “a unique form of human knowledge that needs to be examined on its own terms.” All the elements necessary for understanding the work are contained within the work itself. Of particular interest to the formalist critic are the elements of form—style, structure, tone, imagery, etc.—that are found within the text. A primary goal for formalist critics is to determine how such elements work together with the text’s content to shape its effects upon readers.

Favorite Poetry: "The Waste Land" by T.S. Eliot

"The Waste Land" is a modernist masterpiece that explores the disillusionment and despair of post-World War I Europe. The poem is fragmented and complex, with multiple narrators and shifting perspectives that reflect the fractured state of society at the time. Eliot draws on a wide range of literary and cultural references, from classical mythology to popular culture, to create a rich and layered text that rewards careful reading and analysis. The poem's themes of isolation, decay, and spiritual emptiness are expressed through vivid and often disturbing imagery, making it a challenging but rewarding work of art.

Favorite Historical Movie: "Schindler's List" directed by Steven Spielberg

"Schindler's List" is a powerful and moving portrayal of the Holocaust, and the efforts of one man to save the lives of countless Jews during this dark period of history. The film is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit, and the capacity for compassion and heroism in the face of unimaginable evil. Spielberg's direction is masterful, using black and white cinematography and understated performances to create a sense of stark realism that captures the horror and brutality of the Holocaust. The film's central message of hope and redemption, embodied in the character of Oskar Schindler, is a reminder of the importance of individual acts of kindness and bravery in the face of oppression and injustice. Overall, "Schindler's List" is a powerful and unforgettable work of cinema that deserves its place in the canon of great historical movies.

28. Write a Critique about "Song of Autumn"patulongg​


Answer:

In Song of Autumn I, Baudelaire is discussing three of the four seasons, summer, winter, and autumn. In this poem he is describe the emotions that he feels with each of these three seasons. Summer is seen as happiness and joy while winter is seen as dark times that bring pain and death. However, Autumn is seen as nothing but a place in between the two, he even describes autumn as a time where a coffin is made to prepare for when winter comes. The tone that the narrator shows in this poem is the fear of winter and the coming death. This is seen in stanza two when the speaker discusses all the pain and darkness that is brought with winter and he uses words like “hate,” “anger,” “horror,” and “chills” (Baudelaire 473). Stanza three, however, discusses the destruction of man’s spirit, here he uses the metaphor “my spirit is like a tower whose crumbling walls the tireless battering-ram brings to the ground” (Baudelaire 473).
