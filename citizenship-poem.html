His tongue shorn, father confuses

snacks for snakes, kitchen for chicken.

It is 1992. Weekends, we paw at cheap

silverware at yard sales. I am told by mother

to keep our telephone number close,

my beaded coin purse closer. I do this.

The years are slow to pass, heavy-footed.

Because the visits are frequent, we memorize

shame’s numbing stench. I nurse nosebleeds,

run up and down stairways, chew the wind.

Such were the times. All of us nearsighted.

Grandmother prays for fortune

to keep us around and on a short leash.

The new country is ill-fitting, lined

with cheap polyester, soiled at the sleeves.


2. Poem about good citizenship


Cigarette Runs and Traffic Lights

It is night

2 am

I am driving to the store for cigarettes

A short trip

Or it would be

Except the light turns red

The minute I reach it

I slow my car to a crawl

Hoping that I can inch my way forward

That if I just take my time

It will turn green and signal

My departure

It doesn’t turn green

And so I sit

At a red light

With not another car in sight

I sit tight

Just like I was trained to do

A good citizen

A willing slave

Even when it is clear I could move

Even when not a soul would be harmed

By pushing on the gas

And blowing the light

I sit in the dark

At the light

A quiet symbol of control

And wonder what it means to be free

And this is quite possibly

The greatest argument

For not buying cigarettes

At 2 am

If more people sat

In the dark

At lights at 2am

Then the makers of lights and rules

Might tremble

10. Define and discuss global citizenship. What are the roles and importance of global citizenship in Global Development & Progress? ​


Answer:

Global citizenship refers to a concept that emphasizes the responsibilities and rights of individuals, organizations, and governments in promoting a just, peaceful, and sustainable world. It recognizes that we are all interconnected and that we share a common responsibility to address global challenges such as poverty, climate change, inequality, and conflict.

Global citizenship involves developing a sense of belonging to the global community, recognizing and respecting cultural diversity, promoting human rights, and taking action to address global issues. It includes being informed about global issues, engaging in dialogue with people from different backgrounds, and taking action to make a positive impact in the world.

The role of global citizenship in global development and progress is crucial. In today's interconnected world, global challenges require global solutions, and global citizens have a critical role to play in creating a more just and sustainable world. By promoting cultural understanding and respect, global citizens can help to reduce conflict and promote peace. By advocating for human rights, they can help to ensure that everyone has the opportunity to live a dignified life. By taking action to address global challenges, they can help to create a more sustainable and equitable world.

Global citizenship is also important for sustainable development, as it promotes a holistic approach to development that takes into account social, economic, and environmental factors. Global citizens recognize that development must be sustainable to ensure that future generations can also thrive.

In summary, global citizenship is a critical concept for creating a just, peaceful, and sustainable world. It involves developing a sense of belonging to the global community, recognizing and respecting cultural diversity, promoting human rights, and taking action to address global challenges. By promoting global citizenship, we can create a more equitable and sustainable world for all.
