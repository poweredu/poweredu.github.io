7. Give the nuclear reaction of the following elements.Cn — alpha decayAr — alpha decayHg — bombardment of alpha particleCs — beta decay​


Answer:

Cn (copernicium) undergoes alpha decay, where it emits an alpha particle (helium nucleus) from its nucleus, resulting in the formation of a new nucleus. The nuclear reaction for this process is:

Cn → alpha particle + Ds

Here, Ds represents the daughter nucleus formed after the emission of the alpha particle.

Ar (argon) also undergoes alpha decay in certain isotopes. The nuclear reaction for this process is:

Ar → alpha particle + K

Here, K represents the daughter nucleus formed after the emission of the alpha particle.

Hg (mercury) can be bombarded with alpha particles, resulting in the formation of a new nucleus through a nuclear reaction. One possible nuclear reaction is:

alpha particle + Hg → Tl + n

Here, Tl represents thallium, and n represents a neutron that is also produced in the reaction.

Cs (cesium) undergoes beta decay, where a neutron in its nucleus is converted into a proton, and an electron and an antineutrino are emitted. The nuclear reaction for this process is:

Cs → Ba + e- + antineutrino

Here, Ba represents the daughter nucleus formed after the beta decay, and e- and antineutrino represent the electron and antineutrino emitted in the process, respectively.
